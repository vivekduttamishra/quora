Did Arjuna alone defeat Karna in the Virat War (the war before Mahabharata)? If yes, then why were Kauravas not scared of him as he alone defeated all the troops of Hastinapur in the Virat war?

📅 Sep 20, 2020 11:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Yes, if you trust the epic, Arjuna single handedly defeated entire Kaurava army. It's mentioned clearly and in vivid details.

And who said they were not afraid of him? Almost all great general knew that Arjuna was invincible —

  • Krishna himself proclaimed the victory of Pandavas when he went to Hastinapur.
  • Bhagwan Parshuram too announced his invincibility.
  • Karna accepted the projected outcome to Krishna.
  • Bhishma, Drona, Kripacharya and even Shakuni tried to dissuade Duryodhana from the war.
  • Drona didn't form the Chakravyuh in Arjuna's presence.
  • Before Ghatotkatch wadh both Kripacharya and Ashwathama acknowledged Arjuna's strength.
  • Even Karna recognized on multiple occasions.

Knowing Arjuna's capabilities, why did they fight?

Being scared doesn't mean you would runaway. Here we are taking about some of the bravest warriors of the time. Most of them knew they are siding with the wrong, and they would be vanquished, but they had call of duty. They fought knowing the opponent was undefeatable, more so because of the presence of Shri Krishna himself — that's what bravery means. Bravery is not running away despite of knowing death is inevitable.
