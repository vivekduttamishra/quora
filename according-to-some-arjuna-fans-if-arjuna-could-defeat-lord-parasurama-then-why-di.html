According to some Arjuna fans, if Arjuna could defeat Lord Parasurama, then why did Arjuna fail to protect Lord Krishna's wives and run away from ordinary Dwaraka robbers? Why don't they understand Arjuna's power is Lord Krishna?

📅 Jun 16, 2020 05:01 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Let's divide the question in two disjoint parts that exists —

1.Arjuna vs Parshuram

There is no vs between Arjuna and Parshuram. The 6th Vishnu Parshuram himself declared Arjun unparalleled warrior who can't be defeated. Guess what, he too appears to be an Arjuna fan as mentioned in this question. He was supported by Krishna another Vishnu incarnate. So why on earth would they really fight? Bhagwan Parshuram is revered by all and only relationship that could exist between the two is blesser and blessed. Arjuna never cheated him that he would curse him.

2. Arjuna's fight with Robbers

In Musala Parva, Arjuna fights a band of robbers where there is three clear conclusions —

  1. Arjuna manages to force them into retreat.
  2. Arjuna did save most of the women accompanying him including the primary wives of Krishna who finally entered fire.
  3. Robbers managed to abduct few women from the group and Arjuna could do nothing about it.

Why did Arjuna fail and what does it tell about Arjuna and Mahabharat?

  1. It was over three decades of Kurukshetra war. Arjuna was no longer in his prime. Mahabharat points out no one is stronger than time, and age effects everyone.
  2. Arjuna was suffering a psychological trauma, his best friend Krishna gone, his ally Dwarka, all his favorite people utterly devastated. Such tragedy would break anyone. With his friend philosopher and guide gone, Arjuna was vulnerable and he lost!
  3. Most Important! It contradicts all the claims that Mahabharat is a tale glorifying Victor's,that it is biased in favour of Arjuna and Pandavas. The hero of Kurukshetra couldn't fend against a ordinary robber! Krishna died in the hands of hunters. Who else could have defeated such great men other than destiny? Had this been a tale of Victor or biased they may have censored this part of the event.
  4. Mahabharat war and its incident too is true and so is the might of Arjuna during the war when he was in his prime form.
  5. Even in defeat Arjuna was everybit a hero. When Divyastra failed him, when arrows were over, he continued to fight for the protection of that unfortunate women folk who were not his direct responsibility. He rehabilitated even those who fought against them in Kurukshetra. This is so unlike those heros of the Era who would flee leaving their own wives behind and try to strip others.
  6. Divine weapons don't make a hero; a hero is one who fights even when beaten and defeated. That is the difference between brave and just powerful. You can check out my poetry “Arjuna ki Parajaya” in my best-selling non-fictional poetry collection Manas (मानस)
