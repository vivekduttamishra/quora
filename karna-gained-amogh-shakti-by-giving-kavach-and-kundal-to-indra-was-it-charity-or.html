Karna gained Amogh Shakti by giving Kavach and Kundal to Indra, was it charity or business?

📅 Sep 28, 2020 10:01 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi,

Thanks for the A2A. This question is becoming monotonous now. You may check out my other answers including Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s post in The Mahabharata Project.

But in short —

  1. Indra wanted to takeaway Karna's Kavacha.
  2. Surya informed Karna of Indra's intention and suggested he should refuse.
  3. Karna was not ready to refuse, lest such an act would blemish his reputation.
  4. Surya suggested, if Karna can't refuse, at least he should ask a powerful weapon in exchange.
  5. Karna liked the idea and clearly asked Indra for an exchange. He categorically mentioned, he won't give otherwise. Here charity can be comprehensively ruled out.
    1. Indra may have wanted to trick.
    2. It may be Surya's suggestion.
    3. But the moment Karna asked for an exchange, it ceases to be a donation.

Was it a Fair trade?

  • It was a trade. Fair or not can be decided by the two parties.
  • Karna was happy with the exchange — content in his mind.
  • Karna admitted to Indra, that he was scared of Arjuna. So the weapon was important to him.
  • Indra gave a fair warning that the weapon may not work against someone protected by Narayana. That's was a fair disclosure.
  • Weapons power can be verified verified by Krishna's worry. He never showed similar anxiousness for any other weapon.
  • It saved Kaurava's army from Ghatotkatch.

This should help you decide the nature of exchange.
