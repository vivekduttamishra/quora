Some speculate that Duryodhana refused to give five villages to Pandavas because they were prosperous and strategic places, then why didn't Duryodhana offer other less prosperous and strategic villages to them? Why he said he will not give anything?

📅 Jun 03, 2021 11:59 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Answer lies in the question — Some speculate.

There is no basis of those speculation. None.

Duryodhana knew agreeing to give anything to them would be accepting their claims. He knew what had happened when they were given a animal infested waste land Khandava years ago.

He knew now none of his deceit would work and hoped with Karna, Drona, Bhishma and a large number of alliance he forged during thirteen years of Pandavas’ absence — it was his best chance at thwarting Pandavas once and for all. He hoped a victory.

Evils always feel they are invanquishable till they are vanquished. Duryodhana was no different. He had bad advisors who misled him into the wars. And he paid no heed to his true well wishers. It was those terrible advises that led to his doom.
