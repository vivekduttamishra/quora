Was Karna a Vedic scholar?

📅 Mar 30, 2021 01:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short answer: NO.

Karna as a part of gurukul learning curriculum must have learnt Vedas. But there is not a single reference in Mahabharata that points out to the fact that he had either expertise or interest in Vedas. In fact, his only apparent interest was in archery with an ambition to be considered better than Arjuna. Even in this field, there is no reference that Karna strived for higher studies of weaponry after being expelled by Bhagwan Parashurama.

On contrary, there are evidences to suggest, that he lacked the knowledge of Vedas. One such example is when he invokes shastras to make his point that Draupadi is a whore because she married multiple men —

O descendant of the Kuru lineage!213 It has been ordained by the gods that a woman should only have one husband. However, she submits to many and it is therefore certain that she is a courtesan. It is my view that there is nothing surprising in her being brought into the sabha in a single garment, or even if she is naked.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3375-3377).

Now the question is —

  • Which text did Karna referred to here that was unknown to Ved Vyas, who ensured that Draupadi’s marriage to five Pandavas is valid as per Veda? Was Karna a greater scholar than Ved Vyas? Do you know any text that validates Karna’s claim? Do you know any reference where he is said to be greater scholar of Vedas than Ved Vyas?
  • Which God ordained that a woman should have only one husband, when God of Gods Mahadeva, himself blessed Draupadi to have five husbands? Was Karna greater than Mahadeva?

But this isn’t an isolated incident.

Mahabharata also describes different kind of friendships as per the shastras. Ashwatthama explains about those friendship to Duryodhana while urging him to make friendship with Pandavas. Apparently Karna missed that lesson too, and forged a friendship that is between third and fourth grade of friendship, that contains certain degree of selfishness. Krishna offered him option of first type of friendship, which Karna refused.

Learned scholar Vidur also describes the shastras mandate — It is wise to sacrifice individual for the good for family, family for the good of community and community for the good of nation. But Karna let the nation be destroyed for the sake of individuals.

None of Karna’s action gives a hint that he either knew or card about teaching of Vedas. He just strived to be a warrior.
