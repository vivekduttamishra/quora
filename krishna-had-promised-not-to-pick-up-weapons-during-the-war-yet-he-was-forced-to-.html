Krishna had promised not to pick up weapons during the war. Yet he was forced to do so by Bhishma, a human who never ever broke his vows. Is Bhishma greater than Krishna in the matter of personal integrity?

📅 May 03, 2020 01:41 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is one of those wonderful question, that calls for a constructive thinking, and that can be answered at so many levels —

  1. The Most Important Question: What is of greater importance — Dharma or Personal integrity?
    I think, this is one of those great puzzles that baffles only people with honor. Personal integrity, oath vs greater good and Dharma. Can we forgo Dharma for the personal integrity? And there is no easy answer, as the question of personal integrity includes ego (personal). And ego often clouds our judgement — how will people see me leaving my oath? Its about me and my. Krishna as a god and great teacher here teaches, nothing is more important than dharma itself.
  2. Why did Krishna broke his oath?
    Perhaps, the greatest lesson here is, that personal oath can be and should be broken for upholding Dharma. One should act according to the situation and not based on pre-conceived notion or personal biases or glory. It should also be noted that this wasn’t the first time Krishna proved his point. Remember, he is not called Ranchod without a reason. Krishna always valued Dharma over ego. Can there be a greater example for personal integrity when you focus on ‘integrity’ and not ‘personal’?
  3. Did Krishna really broke his oath?
    Krishna undertook the most spectacular role in the Kurukshetra war — that of a guru. He emphasized that lessons of life can be learnt any and everywhere. Even in the battle field. War of Kurukshetra was over 5000 years ago and if it weren’t for Krishna’s teaching, there is not point in talking about it today. When Krishna realized, Arjun is still not ready to do mortal damage to Bhisma, he jumped towards Bhishma with a chariot wheel? While even a piece of straw, in the hand of Krishna, would prove more fatal than any celestial weapon, the important point is intent — He wasn’t attacking Bhisma, he was attacking Arjuna’s lust that was not allowing him to use his full potential while his army continued to Perish. Once successful, he didn’t really attack Bhisma — did he?

Above all it is an important question to each of us — What is more important — Personal glory or Dharma?
