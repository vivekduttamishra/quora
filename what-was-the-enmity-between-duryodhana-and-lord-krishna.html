What was the enmity between Duryodhana and Lord Krishna?

📅 Oct 31, 2021 12:15 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A. It is mentioned in one of the most popular shloka of Bhagwat Gita —

“यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम्।।”

“परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम्।
धर्मसंस्थापनार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे।।”

— Gita 4, 7–8

When ever there is a decline in Dharma and expansion in corrupt and evil practices, O Arjuna, to renew the Dharma I manifest myself. I manifest myself again and again, in every era, to destroy the evils, to protect the good and to establish the prominence of Dharma.

Duryodhana was the icon of those evils and corrupt practices. He was a deceitful conspirator, murderer who had little regard for anything other than his lust, greed and ambition. He was a bad omen since he was born and manifested his criminal tendencies long before reaching adulthood. He was joined by equally evil men like Karna, Shakuni and Duhshashana. There were the opponent of Dharma. And that was the core enimity.

Let me ask you this — How do you feel about Nirbhaya’s rapist? Or for that matter any rapist? How do you feel about terrorists like Ajmal Kasab? How do you feel about your prevailing situation in Afganistan? What is common thread between them? Can I call you an enemy of all those people mentioned here, despite the fact that they did you no personal injury? Why is that?

Any sensible human would revolt against the criminals and devils even if they do you know personal harm. Krishna was not just a sensible human but a manifestation of divinity.

But we must understand Krishna was also a relative to Duryodhana and he tried his best to persuade him accept the peace deal even if it was an unjustifiably low demand. Had Duryodhana accepted the proposal Krishna could have found way to establish Dharma, by separating evil from the person. Alas!
