What if karna also fought from the first day with his kavach?

📅 Jun 11, 2020 12:10 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Karna's Kavach, of late, appears to have been more overrated than Karna himself. A wishful thinking…only if he had the Kavach with him. This question seem to be disrespectful for Karna as a warrior he always strived to prove himself — he wasn't a warrior just because he had the Kavach. Who else had such Kavach? Isn't it disrespectful for Karna to say he could win only with and because of Kavach?

Kavach don't fight a warrior’s battle, it doesn't attack the enemies, it doesn't protect friends, it just can protect one person? For instance, Kavach or no Kavach, Karna was protected for first 10 days of battle, by not participating in it. He didn't abstained from the battle because he had no Kavach, so even if he had his Kavach he would still have been out for the first 10 days.

Now the question is —

What if he had Kavach?

Surely it would have protected his covered body. But would it attack the enemy? Would it help him defeat his enemies? Did it help him in battle against Drupad (Gurudakshina battle), Swaymber battle, Rajsue battle or Gandharva's battle? No Kavach just protects you, doesn't win the war for you.

And Karna, it seems, had realized this fact, and wise as he was, exchanged it for a weapon — the potent Indrastra. That's what a true warrior would do. To win a war you need weapons, protection you can get even by hiding behind a bush.

So let's come back to the hypothetical scenario, if Karna had kept his invincible Kavach —

  • He might have been the only surviving Kaurava man on day 14 against Ghatotkatch. Because no one could defeat him or protect their men. Karna could kill him only using that Indrastra he got in exchange for Kavach. So solve the equation —
    • (Karna + Kavach - Indrastra) / Ghatotkatch => Total destruction
  • Ok, let's say somehow take ghatotkatch out of the equation, question is how much can Kavach protect? Can it protect the head? What if enemy severs your head? Would your protected body still matter? How did Karna finally died?

I think it's unfair towards Karna to say the outcome would have been better for Karna with Kavach — as if his own capacity and wisdom doesn't matter. Karna deserves more respect as a warrior than his Kavach.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —
