Why don't people give the example of friendship between Duryodhana and Karna when they supported each other through thick and thin?

📅 Apr 26, 2020 11:19 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Duryodhana, indeed proved to be a loyal friend throughout his life never questioning and always supporting his friend. It may have started selfish, and he saw him as his biggest asset.

People have often quoted their friendship and this question is a testimony to the fact.

Why shouldn't they be example of true friendship?

A true friend not only supports his friend, but also fights his friend to ensure his wellbeing. He tries his best to see the best for his friend. The duo mentioned here ensured each other downfall. What lesson should one teach from this example?

The problem with learning from a villain is you tend to endorse their misdeeds.

And finally, when we have heros to give us a lesson, why learn from a villain? Why not learn from Krishna Arjuna, Krishna Sudama, Ram Hanuman, Ram Lakshman? We have no dearth of great motivations to pick a bad example.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
