Why didn't Dronacharya accept Eklavya as his student?

📅 Dec 17, 2020 02:05 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Not every question has a simple answer. This one certainly can’t be understood with a simple caste biased views.

Let us first take two contemporary examples —

  1. Almost all jobs, competitions and higher educations have an entrance test and if you don’t qualify for the entrance test, you simply don’t get into the position. And these entrance test doesn’t check only your technical skills of IQ, but also checks your EQ and other psychological conditioning.
  2. There are reservations in India in almost every thing owned by government — jobs, education, opportunities. But if there is one place where there is no such reservation it is Indian Armed Forces. To join Indian armed forces, they test your subject knowledge (and this is the easy part) and also a rigorous personality evaluation including psychological evaluation, team skills, visualization and personal interview by a senior official of the rank of colonel or above. Most important, they never divulge why they rejected you or selected you.

Now what does these two point say — While every walk of life accepts or rejects you own various criteria led down by they society no one takes more seriously than armed forces. They don’t consider your birth, but mantle to wield a weapon. Even today you are evaluated by an experienced veteran from the field to ensure you are worthy to wield a weapon.

Why Dronacharya didn’t accept Eklavya as his student?

Let us first realize Dronacharya accepted a lot of pupil from different kingdoms — Vrishnis, Anhakas and even Karna, who was a Suta. This proves he didn’t pick up students based on the caste. However Drona refused to grant higher weapons not only to Karna but most of his disciples. Once again, caste angle doesn’t come into picture.

Second, when Parshuram sacked Karna, he didn’t do it because he was a suta or because he wasn’t a brahmin. He sacked him because he was deceitful. Drona, too, had earlier refused.

Interestingly when Drona refused to give higher weapons to Karna, few interesting points are mentioned —

  • He rejected because he found Karna to be vengeful.
  • But he gives him a different reason. He says only Brahman and higer Kshatriya should wield it. Why? He hoped Karna accepts is argument and don’t go searching for them somewhere else which may not be beneficial to society.
  • As we see he did succeed in deceiving Parshuram, but what was the final consequence?

So acharyas of the time didn’t reject someone because of the caste, but based on his evaluation of the person in front of him. He may have given any reason for his rejection and we may make any assumption about why he did what he did, there was a reason he refused him because he realized he is not a fit to be a warrior.

And looks like he was right after all. Eklavya supported Jarasandha and was finally killed by Shri Krishna.

So instead of doubting Drona’s intention, we should aske, why he decided what he decided?
