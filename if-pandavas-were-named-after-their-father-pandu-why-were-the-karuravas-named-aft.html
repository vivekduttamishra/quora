If 'Pandavas' were named after their father Pandu, why were the Karuravas named after Kuru but not after Dhritarashtra?

📅 Sep 15, 2020 01:03 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Kaurva was a general term to refer to the dynasty of King Kuru, their ancestors. At many places, even Pandavas have been refered as Kaurva.

Pandu was a distinct term used for the five sons of Pandu to distinguish the two group. Pandu was a great warrior emperor and his name was a matter of pride.

Now, why Duryodhana and his brother didn’t chose to go by their father’s name must be their choice. They are, however, often referred as sons of Gandhari or Dhritrashtra
