Does Draupadi really tell Karna a Sutaputra, or is it just a story created by StarPlus?

📅 May 05, 2020 02:14 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The Story isn't created by Star Plus but exists for more than 200 years now. That said there is ample of contradiction to this story.

Origin of Draupadi denying Karna's participation

  1. The Neelkantha compilation which is one of the most refered version in Northern India is perhaps the one that first mentioned this story.
  2. The Neelkantha also presented a contradiction of the same when it mentioned in the very next chapter that warriors like Karna, Shalya and other Kshatriya failed at Swaymber.
  3. This story is translated by Kisari Mohan Ganguli. There he retains point one and dilutes point 2 by mentioning warriors like Shalya etc failed. Karna's name lay hidden in etc.
  4. Dakshinayat mentions only failure story and not Drawpadi's rejection. Same is adapted by Shri Rajgopalachari in his highly acclaimed abridged adaptation of Dakshinayat.
  5. Bhandarkar Research published a critical edition, popularly known as BORI, compared over 1200 manuscript and weed out inconsistencies. It is regarded as one of the most authentic version. BORI too rejects this story.
  6. The popular Sanskrit Hindi edition of Mahabharat which refers to Neelkanth mostly, also refers to Dakshinayat and BORI mentions both version (like Neelkantha) and rejects the story in the footnote.
  7. To verify the authenticity of a event another technique used is cross referencing. Like Drawpadi's chherharan is mentioned at multiple locations. But this incident is not cross referenced anywhere.

These points proof that popular narrative of Draupadi's rejection is an interpolation.

But StarPlus and other tv shows have shown lot of fictional elements not present in any of these versions like —

  • Draupadi insulting Duryodhan at Indraprastha.

  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
