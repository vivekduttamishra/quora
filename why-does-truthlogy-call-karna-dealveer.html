Why does Truthlogy call Karna dealveer?

📅 May 26, 2022 11:02 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: Karna wasn’t a dealveer.

People who troll him as deelveer, point out that he exchanged Kavach-Kundala for Vasvi Shakti.

To be fair, trading his Kavacha-Kundala wasn’t his idea in the first place, and he never approached Indra for the exchange in the first place. I won’t be surprised, had Suryadeva not suggested him the idea of exchange, he may have given away the Kavacha Kundala without an exchange.

That doesn’t mean Karna didn’t insist on exchange or wasn’t happy with the exchange. But that doesn’t make him dealveer. He just took the advantage of a great proposition presented to him.

And this was not the only favorable deal Karna did in his life. He sold his soul, by cheating his guru, in exchange for divyastra. But that deal failed as guru saw through the facade. He lost his soul and gained nothing. He again tried and fail to strike out a deal with the brahmana who cow he killed — He tried to offer wealth in exchange (to avoid) his curse.

The only bargain I see worked was when he purchased the loyalty of Ashwasena by worshipping him for years so that he could use him to kill Arjuna. Ashwasena did work for him. It’s a different story he too failed.
