Why was karna a better choice for draupadi than the pandavas ?

📅 Mar 13, 2020 09:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is a leading question with the assumption that Karna was a better choice for Draupadi than Pandavas. The basic foundation of this question is flawed for two reasons —

  1. Karna was not a better choice — He was wicked and cheater (as proved by his acts with Parshuram) and later reinforced by Bhisma before the war. He had no respect for women that is evident from his conduct during the Great Gamble. If you don’t respect a woman, you can’t respect a woman — even if she is your wife. There has no proof that he actually ever stood for a woman’s modesty — the alternative proof is clear.
  2. Draupadi never had any emotion about Karna other than contempt — The only interaction that has happened between Karna and Draupadi include the Swyamber where she didn’t choose Karna ( there are two versions, the acceptable one suggests that Karna failed the test, Popular one she denied him the chance). Either it was clear, Karna was not his choice. Second time during the Great Gamble where Karna insulted and humiliated her and there would be no reason why after such a humiliation any lady would have a secret feeling for her tormentor. She had always referred him with contempt always asking for vengeance.

Now someone mentioned the

  1. Karna equaled all Pandavas together → How? He was defeated by Satyaki, Abhimanyu, Ghatotkatch, and Bhim and Arjun indvidually on many occassions. He failed to protect the brothers of Duryodhan from the wrath of Bhima. Bhisma considered him Ardhrathi and not Atirathi like Arjun. He equalled five pandavas together is a wishful thinking of modern story-tellers and it is never once mentioned by Ved Vyas.
  2. The Jambuk story proves Draupadi loved Karna → Right! But first we need to prove Jambuk story. It has no mention in any Sanskrit version of Mahabharat neither in north version, nor south version, nor Bori version nor K.M.Ganguli. The story appears in a Bheel version (tribal edition) which is not considered authentic by a big margin.

And before you pledge your loyalty to Karna, ask yourself what is more important for you — a fabled tale glorifying Karna and de-glorifying the original spirit of Mahabharat or you value ancient epic more which has more relevant description of the indian ancient.
