Why didn't lord Krishna annihilate caste based discrimination in his times despite he is a god?

📅 Jul 07, 2020 04:03 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The Real question should be —

Despite being a God, Krishna didn’t annihilate caste based discrimination. What does it tell?

Or even more important — Why did Krishna said in Gita that I created the caste (varna)?

The main problem is we try to see the Mahabharata era with our contemporary eyes and try to reject every logical answer if it doesn’t match my understanding of society based on the current era.

Did santatan society had caste system?

Of course. Caste system existed. And before you ask me, yes, it was based on birth.

Did caste system discriminated?

Yes, it discriminated. The entire purpose a caste system was to discriminate — group, organize and segregate.

First let us understand What is discrimination? As per oxford dictionary,

Discrimination is to recognize the distinction or differences.

Is it bad? Is it something evil that should be eradicated?

And such division exists in every walk of life everywhere. Don’t organizations have hierarchy? Does everyone in your workplace equal? Do they get same pay? Do they get same privilege? Does everyone have the same say in policy decision?

Isn’t discrimination an evil practice?

You are confusing between caste and racism.

Consider these cases —

  • Do I discriminate between a Dog and a Rabbit? Sure. Is a rabbit weaker than Dog? Sure. Do I like Dog more than Rabbit? Actually no, I adore the rabbit.
  • Should I discriminate Lion and Elephant? Yes. Does that mean one is better and other is abused?

That was our santan culture. Caste was a distinction, not abuse. It was way better than racism that you see in the USA or the hierarchy you see in the organizations. In fact caste turned abusive in India only on after foreign influence and nothing like that was present in Mahabharata or Ramayana age.

Consider important parts from Ramayana and Mabharata to understand it much better —

  • Why did Shantanu married Sayavati, who was a fisherwoman? Did he ever get an objection from any part of the society?
  • Who was the first born of Satyavati? Sage Ved Vyas. Do we ause him? No he is one of the greatest authority on Sanatan Dharma?
  • What was the caste of Sri Krishna? We deem him the incarnation of Vishnu. Is that caste racist?
  • How did Vidur, the son of a maid become the prime minister?
  • Karna became the king of Anga? Did society disallowed him?

Let us now check Ramayana —

  • Ram and Nishadraj studies together and were friend.
  • Ram and Shabri interaction
  • Ram enlisted the support of Vanars who were certainly not high on any caste rank. Ram made Sugriv a friend, and ever gave Hanuman a status lesser than his siblings.
  • When Ravana, a Brahmin became and evil, he was killed, and no one lamented why a high caste brahmin was killed.

Our society had castes, caste was discriminatory but not abusive or racist.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
