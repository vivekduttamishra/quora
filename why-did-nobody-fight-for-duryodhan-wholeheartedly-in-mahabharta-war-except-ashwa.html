Why did nobody fight for Duryodhan wholeheartedly in Mahabharta war except Ashwatthama?

📅 May 16, 2020 04:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is an interesting and important question. Why indeed?

This is one of the greatest teachings of our epic. You can persuade someone to fight on your behalf. They may even pledge their person loyalty to you, but you can’t buy their conscience.

Dharma has its own justice system — it is its own weapon and shield. And no one is out of its influence. Bhisma, Drona, Kripacharya, they all fought for Duryodhana, for Hasitnapur, and for Dhritrashtra. But they knew they are fighting on the wrong side. They knew their victory would be meaningless, nay, distratous. So they discharged their duty, but couldn’t dawn on the ruthlessness required to vanquish their enemy. Bhisma openly declared that he won’t kill Pandavas. Drona tried to imprision Yudhisthir rather than kill him for a swift end of the war.

But then it would be wrong to say nobody fought wholeheartedly for Duryodhana in the war. Few who fought wholeheartedly for Duryodhana include — his brothers, notably Dussasana and Vikarna and his friend Ashwatthama, and may be his uncle Sakuni.

Then there were few who although fought on behalf of Kauravas had their own agenda which, for them, was much greater than Duryodhana and his cause.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
