In my understanding, the important question is —

  • Why should Krishna really help Draupadi or anyone?
  • Why should he behead anyone for playing a game of dice?
  • And what would be the lesson if he actually sever their heads?
  • Isn’t there a real difference between God and a henchman? Kill someone to sort out a struggle?
  • What lesson would be there for Pandavas who baited what they weren’t supposed to?

And most important question —

What lesson would be there for humanity, had Krishna averted the incident by his divine power?

That God will do your biddings and you don’t need to do anything by your own? That the God will spoon feed you?

Another Important question that is again asked too many times is —

Krishna could have averted the war. Why didn’t he?

Yes, Krishna could have averted the war in many ways. But why was it important to avert the war? Didn’t Krishna’s Gita start with an attempt to persuade Aruna to take part in the war? It is another capital mistake to think God is averse to destruction — because seeds of construction lies in the destruction. And Krishna clearly says so in Gita, and that's the reason —

So why did Krishna interfere?

This, too, is imbibed in Hindu philosophy. God intervenes when human justice completely fails. This is what Krishna said in Gita —

yada yada hi dharmasya
glanir bhavati bharata
abhyutthanam adharmasya
tadatmanam srjamy aham

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

Gita Chapter 4 - Sloka 7,8
