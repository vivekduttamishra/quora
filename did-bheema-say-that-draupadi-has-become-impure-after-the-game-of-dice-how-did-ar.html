Did Bheema say that Draupadi has become impure after the game of dice? How did Arjuna react to it?

📅 Aug 26, 2021 08:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Before I venture to answer this question, let us understand Hindu philosophy about what is impure. Impurity isn’t just a physical trait, but also a mental trait. When you are humiliated and mentally hurt you are impure. Most of you would understand when there is a death in the family, worshipping Gods are prohibited for a period of 13 days. Because family members are considered impure for that period. So what does impure really mean?

It may be defined as a physical or mental state where you are not in your perfect condition and is forced to bowed down by adverse condition like humiliations, pain, grief or hurt. In such a situation any venture you take is like to be less than perfect because you are not at your optimal performance.

And this is not just an ancient belief that you can discard by calling mythology. Psychologist and doctors around the world endorse this view today. Why do doctor suggest expecting mothers to remain happy and clean? Because mental stress of the mother affects the child also.

Having laid down the foundation of the context, let us see what Bhima said —

Bhimasena was extremely hurt. In the midst of the Kurus, he said unhappily, “Devala240 has said that there are three stars241 for a man—offspring, deeds and learning, since these are the source of creation. When the body has lost life and is hollow and impure, discarded by relatives, these are the three that survive. But our light has become dark, because our wife has been humiliated. O Dhananjaya! How can offspring from a defiled one serve any purpose?”

—Dyut Parva, BORI CE

Analsysis:

  • Bhima is not talking about physical impurity here.
  • He clearly mentions humiliation
  • His observation is in the same line as doctors. A mother’s stress affects even the child.

Later Bhima also proposes a solution — to kill everyone and avenge the insult!

Now coming to the next part of the question. How did Arjuna respond —

Arjuna replied, “The Bharatas do not talk about words from inferior men, uttered or not uttered. They only converse about those from superior men. They remember good deeds and not enmity that has been shown. They know only the good because they have confidence in their own selves.”

—Dyut Parva, BORI CE

Analysis

Arjuna as usual, exhibits his maturity and tries to calm down Bhimasena by asking him —

  • To ignore the words of inferior people
  • Focus on the good deeds and self confidence to ward of hurting thoughts. Remember Bhima was too hurt at this point.
