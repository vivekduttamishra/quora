What are the similarities between Abhimanyu and Karna?

📅 Oct 17, 2020 11:05 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Let us check out the similarities —

  • Both were contemporary, they existed in Mahabharata era.
  • Both fought Kurukshetra war.
  • Both were archers.

endAnd the similarities end here. There had more contrasts —

  • They didn’t share thoughts or beliefs.
  • They didn't fight for the same cause.
  • They didn’t follow the same ethics.
  • They didn’t share the same experience as child or as youth.

And they didn’t die the same way

Looks like this is the similarity this question is seeking. But don’t be swayed by the fact that both died when they were without their chariot.

Abhimanyu fought severals warriors together, who cut down his weapons, shields and killed him like a pack of hungry hyenas. His chariot was shattered. He was cutoff from his army and was devoid of any support. He was alone. And he never asked anyone to wait before he get reinforcement or chariot. He died fighting multiple Maharathas who are referred as hunters and not as warriors as they killed Abhimanyu —

‘“O king! Thus, in that battle, a single one was slain by many. He had agitated all the soldiers, like an elephant among lotuses. The brave one was as resplendent as a wild elephant killed by hunters.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 891-892).

Unlike Abhimanyu, Karna died fighting a one-to-one battle, when he still had access to his bow and divine weapons like Brahmastra and Varunastra. Neither his charioteer was slain not his chariot was shattered. It had just stuck up in the ground.
