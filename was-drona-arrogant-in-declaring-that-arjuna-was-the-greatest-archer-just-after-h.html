Was Drona arrogant in declaring that Arjuna was the greatest archer just after his education, without knowing the warriors of the whole world?

📅 Feb 06, 2021 11:54 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

There are two aspects of this question which I would answer separately.

1.WAS DRONA QUALIFIED FOR DECLARING ARJUNA AS THE GREATEST ARCHER JUST AFTER HIS EDUCATION OR WAS HE ARROGANT?

In retrospection, I would say Drona was factual and a visionary. Arjuna was the greatest archer not just acclaimed by him, but also by Parashurama, Indra, Krishna and Shiva.

We must understand Drona was the greatest guru of his time excluding Parashurama who was no more active in training new pupil. Drona knew the valour of other princes and Kings most of them were his own disciples.

Among the contemporary warriors Arjuna alone was worthy to be bestowed the knowledge of Brahmasira weapon on his own merit and not by deceit. That made him natually more powerful than anyone else.

2.DID DORNA DECLARE, ARJUNA IS THE GREATEST ARCHER PUBLICALLY?

Sometimes we are so much invested in myths that we misplace the references. While based on his estimates and perception Drona certainly believed Arjuna was the best archer, he didn’t announce it publicly as narrated in popular modern narrative as the main reason, why Karna challnged Arjuna. Here is what Drona said in play arena —

BORI

“Now behold Partha, who is dearer to me than my own son. He is skilled in the use of all weapons. He is Indra’s son himself and is like Indra’s younger brother.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 5162-5163). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

KMG

And Drona himself entering the yard of the arena commanded the musicians to stop, and with a voice deep as that of the clouds addressed these words, 'Behold ye now that Partha who is dearer to me than my own son, the master of all arms, the son of Indra himself, and like unto the younger brother of Indra, (Vishnu)!

—Adi Parva: Sambhava Parva: Section CXXXVII

Niether mention any public announcement of Arjuna as the greatest archer. So the popular narrative that Karna entered the arena because Drona publically announced Arjuna is the greatest archer is inconsistent with the epic. It is a cover story to justify Karna’s undesirable intrusion to the assembly hall.
