Dhritrashtra was the King of Hastinapur so automatically his sons are heir to the throne, then why did Pandavas desire for the power and position?

📅 Jul 27, 2020 10:51 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi thanks for the A2A.

The question makes two suppositions —

  1. Dhritarashtra was the King of Hastinapur.
  2. Son of a King was autometic heir to the throne.

Both are incorrect.

Dhritrastra was a caretaker king, a proxy for Pandu. He was ineligible for being the king. As such it wasn't his to pass on.

As per the tradition of the family, heir may not be the son of the king. Not only King Bharat made an exception by sidestepping his sons, even Shantanu's father sidestepped Bahlika and crowned him.

People liked Yudhisthir as their next king which is apparent from Dhritarashtra Duryodhana talks before they decide to send Pandavas to varnavrat.

Finally the question, why they desired for power and position?

  1. Their father was the real king.
  2. They were capable.
  3. People liked them for King.
