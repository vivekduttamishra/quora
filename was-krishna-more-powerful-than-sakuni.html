Was Krishna more powerful than Sakuni?

📅 Jun 26, 2020 08:42 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Is Sun more bright than an 100W Bulb?

The basis of this narrative is popular pulp fiction which presents Shakuni as the mastermind for all the Kaurva’s wrong-doing — which is factually incorrect. This should be clear from one of the most important phrase from Adi Parva which summarises the roles of all important characters of Mahabharat.

‘Duryodhana is a great tree created out of passion, Karna is its trunk, Shakuni is its branches, Duhshashana is the plentiful fruit and flowers and the mindless Dhritarashtra is its root. Yudhishthira is a great tree created out of righteousness, Arjuna is its trunk, Bhima is its branches, the two sons of Madri27 are its plentiful fruit and flowers, and Krishna, Brahma and the Brahmanas are the root.

— Adiparva

Notice, Shakuni is a mere branch of the the evil tree of passion called Duryodhana; Krishna is the root of Dharma tree along with Brahma (the creator) and Brahmanas (the accumulated wisdom).

Shakun’s role for most part of the Mahabharat has been a minion to Duryodhana — he was part of all the conspiracy, but more like Sambha was to Gabbar Singh. While certainly an evil, he wasn’t the Chanakya of Chandragupta.

His prominent role is seen in Dyutkrida (dice game). It was his suggestion and that too was his way to avoid a battle which even Dhritrashtra agreed was not winnable. Shakuni’s role swiftly ends once Yudhishthir looses Draupadi and the game. He should also be credited for winning the second game which sent Pandava’s to 13 years of exile. But Shakuni had almost no role in Draupadi’s insult that was staged predominantly by Karna and Duhsasasan.

Shakuni even tried to persuade Duryodhana to agree to peace agreement offered by Krishna, which summarily rejects the theory that he engineered everything for some doom of Kuru empire to avenge insult of Gandhari. In fact he never worked against the well being of Dhirtrashtra and his nephews. In fact his family too participated in Kurukshetra alongside Kaurava.

Shakuni, despite being evil, has become a victim of modern narratives that makes him the evilest of all, but vyasji was clear on his interpetation.

Krishna is unparallel!
