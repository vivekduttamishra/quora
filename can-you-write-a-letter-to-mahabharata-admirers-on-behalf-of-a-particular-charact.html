Can you write a letter to Mahabharata Admirers on behalf of a particular character of Mahabharata, confessing the feelings of that particular character to his admirers (short one)? (Request- please don't target any character and don't spread hatred.)

📅 Sep 06, 2020 10:29 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A. I would love to write one. Here it is —

Dear friends from over five thousand years later,

I know I may offend you just by calling you my friend, and I don’t resent your reaction. I know I don’t deserve your friendship.

But to let you in my childhood, like most of you, even my mother told me the stories of great Ramchandra. And do you know which character fascinated me the most? It wasn’t Shri Rama. He couldn’t be my ideal. I wasn’t destined to be Rama or even Bharata. I was destined to be the ever loyal follower of my Rama. I was destined to be the great warrior — Lakshman.

So, I followed my brother Rama like his shadow, like his Lakshman. Never asking a question, never objecting to his actions, never seeking anything in return — no kingdom, no richness, no fame — just the pride of being Lakshman.

Alas! I didn’t realize the folly of my analogy — Lakshman deserves to follow Rama. And my big brother was no Rama.

And honestly, I could feel, my brother was no Rama even as a kid — when I assisted my big brother in poisoning my bully cousin. Rama won’t have done this, but then he had no bully cousin, I consoled myself.

Rama won’t try to burn someone alive, let alone your own family including an elderly woman! It was not my idea, I was horrified. But, alas, I never resented it. Hell! I cheered as I heard they perished — afterall, my cousins were no Bharata, they were trying to usurp my brother’s throne.

Oh! I was thrilled, as my brother crowned that archer as the King. My Ramayana was back on the track. We had got our Hanumana. Or was he Sugriva? Either way, I didn’t resent as my brother sidelined me and followed the advice of his new found council — after all Sugriva needs recognition, he may need his kingdom, Lakshmana just follows his Rama.

And I continued imprisoned to my own role play, till I dragged that hapless queen by her hair to the court room. At that moment, I thought I had become almost Lakshman — cutting the limbs of Shurpanakha. And then the very next moment my illusion broke — for ever. She was no demon, and I was no Lakshman. My brother was no Rama and his friend was no Hanumana or Sugriva. I was an evil, who was trying to blemish the Ramayana and its narrator, my mother.

People may feel, that I fell down that day, scared of Bhima’s terrible oath — one day he would tear me apart. But I never heard him say those terrible words. I wasn’t there. I was fighting my own inner battle — trying to prevent my Ramayana from falling apart.

You may say — I was the big bad villian there — but I wasn’t alone, and I wasn’t big. Her husbands went mute. My grandsire Bhisma, my gurus they fiddled lousy arguments, that great archer provoked me, and yet I became the evil there.

Yes, that day my Ramayana was over. Forever. I was part of Mahabharata. I surrendered. I never objected when my brother on his friend’s suggestion planned to mock my cousins in exile. It backfired and we were sorrounded by Gandharavas. The great warriors fled. But I stayed behind, standing with my brother, for my brother. I half hoped we would be killed, half hoped my brother would come to his senses. Alas! as you know neither happened.

Then came the war. My brother rejected the Krishna’s offer and I seconded his decision. I loathed my being, and Krishna assured, war would put an end to it.

You may not remember me as a great warrior, but I stood alone guarding our grandsire Bhisma as he fought his last battle. Yes, my Rama had fled, but I stayed till the end — yet I was the evil!

After 5000 thousand years, when I see so many fan club for my brother and his friend and none for me, I ask — why me? Was I the only evil? Didn’t I always stood by my brother? Did I ever deserted him? But then I know the answer — I was the evil.

So, please don’t go on forming a fan club for me. If you really want to do something for me — plan a day and burn my effigy like Ravana or crush it like Bhima did. For no one who attempts to disrobe a woman needs anything better. Don’t show this letter of mine to your children, lest they may sympathize or worst idolize me. I deserve neither. Continue to choose some evil looking bad guy to play my role, that they carry no sympathy for me. Your society is better off casting me as a villian.

And remember, be Lakshmana only for Rama — not for some Duryodhana, not even for Yudhishthira. Don’t blindly commit for an unknown future like my grandfather. Whenever you read half and follow a quarter, you would end up with the gory world of Duhssasana.

Destroy this letter of mine and continue to hate me, but remember my life’s folly — I trust person over dharma, I skewed Ramayana in my favour — and you would never know how aspiring to be a Lakshmana you would became Dussassana.

Loving you all, but not expecting you to love me back,

Yours,

Big Bad Dussassana
