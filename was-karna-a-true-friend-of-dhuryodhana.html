Was Karna a true friend of Dhuryodhana?

📅 Aug 06, 2021 11:41 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Before we decide if the two were true friends, let us understand what does ‘true’ friend mean?

  • A true friendship isn’t a trade or obligation.
  • A true friend would care about the welfare of his friend not just his wishes that may eventually destroy him.
  • A true friend would fight his friend to stop him from going on the wrong path.
  • A true friend would fight and die for his friend rather than lying to him and risking his friend’s life.

So, the question is, were Duryodhana and Karna were true friends? Let us recap important milestones of their lives.

  1. Duryodhana and Karna joins Drona gurukul as students where they become friends as their father’s too were friend. This makes them childhood friend which continued life-long. But does that make it true friendship?
  2. Karna supports Duryodhana in in various conspiracies' against Pandavas. Here Karna had no selfish interest against most Pandavas (except Arjuna). That makes his actions selfless and aligned to his friends wishes. Once again Karna proves himself as a friend. But a true friend would have stopped his friend from such evil acts.
  3. Duryodhana makes Karna the King of Anga so that he can challenge Arjuna to a duel. Now calling Duryodhana a generous person based on this would only be half true. First, Duryodhana and Karna were pals for a long time before the incident and Duryodhana wanted to solidify that bound to keep Karna on his side as he thought he was the only competition to Arjuna.
  4. Karna supports Duryodhana in all his conspiracies and spawned some of them on his own. He knew a fair fight with Pandavas wouldn’t work so he suggests they should crush Pandavas before Vrishnis and Panchal come to their aid. A great strategy for hyenas. We can give Karna the credit of being a great strategist but does that make him a true friend? Won’t a true friend ask his friend to do an honorable job?
  5. Karna provokes Duhshasana to strip Draupadi. This act brandished Kauravas evil beyond reproach. What true friend would force his friend to become pure evil? Evils are drawn to each other by their common lust and passion and not by their friendship.
  6. Karna again provokes Duryodhana to go to forest and mock Pandavas. Why? Because Karna felt, more important than family and prosperity in seeing enemies in distress. Seeing Druapadi's and Arjuna in tree bark! He drags Duryodhana to Ghosha yatra were Yaksha’s attack them. And what did Karna do? He fled leaving Duryodhana at the mercy of enemies! What true friend would do that?
  7. Karna promises Kunti that he would spare the lives of all his brothers except one with whom he carried personal grudge. The others included Bhima, he who took an oath to kill all the Kaurava brothers. Karna kept his promise at the expense of his friend and his brothers. What true friend would lie to his friend about the most crucial piece of information in a war? Forget friendship, that would makes Karna’s act a high treason!

Duryodhana proved a better friend than Karna. He beyond doubt lashed with his personal strategy but then he never backed out of his friendship. He continued to ignore Karna’s repeated failures. He never complained about them. He didn’t ask Karna when he ditched him in front of Gandharvas or against Arjuna during Ghosha yatra. He never asked why he spared Bhima who was continuously killing his brothers. But most likely it is because Duryodhana clung to his hope that Karna may change his destiny. Nevertheless, he proved a better friend.
