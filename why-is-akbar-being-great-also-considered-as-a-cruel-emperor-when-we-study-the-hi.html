Why is Akbar, being great, also considered as a cruel emperor when we study the history of Maharana Pratap?

📅 Jun 26, 2020 07:06 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Probably the right question should be —

Why Akbar, the cruel referred as great? In the battle of Chittorgarh, he massacred over 40000 innocent civilians — is this greatness?

Akbar was a ruthless emperor who expanded his empire based on greed, brutality, deceit and divide and rule. He spared none who went against him — neither his own son or son of the woman who cared for him. People may claim some of his acts as a great historic brainwash making him great.

But he can’t be compared with Maharana Pratap — The great warrior, who lived and died for his motherland. He didn’t brutalize anyone. He didn’t fight to expand his empire. And see the difference —

  • People fought for Akbar for greed or fear; People were devoted to Maharana Pratap
  • Akbar looted the wealth; People surrendered their lifetime earning to Pratap.
  • Akbar appreciated Pratap; Pratap desisted Akbar.
  • Akbar’s own son rebelled against him; Pratap’s rebel brother Shakti Singh returned to his fold, not to share victory, but to share the glory of vanquished.

Guess who was great!
