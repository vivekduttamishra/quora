Did Duryodhana use Karna in his lifetime to gain supremacy in Hastinapur (Mahabharata)?

📅 May 05, 2020 03:47 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Karna-Duryodhana friendship is a classic case — A friend is need, is a friend in deed — which I find is a pretty selfish definition of friendship.

Karna was an excellent archer and Duryodhana was aware of his skills since the gurukul days as they both studied under the guidance of Drona and Kripacharya. He and Ashwathama were the best candidates against the guru proclaimed best archer Arjuna.

Duryodhana knew that his coronation won't be devoid of struggle as he is neither as well accepted as Pandavas nor his father’s Kingship is debatable and so is his natural right to the throne. He needed to build a strong alliance to combat Pandavas. Ashwatthama and Karna were natural choices, and Duryodhana was successful in bringing them to their side.

Duryodhana had high hopes with mighty Karna and he undertook missions after missions against Pandavas — Gandharvas incident, Virata incident and finally the war. His expectation and victory relied on Karna — So yes, Duryodhana used him.

But to be fair to Duryodhana, he never abused him despite of their constant failures to yield the expected result.

However, the friendship of need was mutual — Even Karna benefited from Duryodhana’s friendship —

  1. Respect and Recognition
  2. Kingships
  3. Life of luxury
