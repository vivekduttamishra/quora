If Draupadi didn't insult Duryodhana’s saying “andhe ka putra andha”, then why did Duryodhana feel so insulted?

📅 May 23, 2020 10:16 PM PDT  |  🌐 English
  • Why did Duryodhana poisoned Bheem? Was it because Draupadi insulted him?
  • Why did Duryodhana tried to burn Pandavas in Laksha grih? Was it because Draupadi insulted him?
  • Why did he cheat Pandavas of their fair share and gave them Khandava van to rule?
  • Why did he humiliate Pandavas if only Draupadi had insulted him?
  • Why did he go after Pandavas in exile to mock them? Was the debt of his insult not over yet?
  • Had Indraprastha episode not happened, would Duryodhana let Pandavas rule as the empire with splendid Indraprastha as it's capital?

What Duryodhan did can't be painted as rightful or vengeance story — it was another of his deceit and jeaolosy story with following important point —

  1. He wanted Indraprasth for himself. He was jealous.
  2. He wanted a way to usurp it. They debated many way including a war. Note had a war happened, at worst Pandavas brothers may have been killed but Draupadi won't be humiliated! As far as Pandavas losing the war, even Dhritrastra rejected this possibility.
  3. It was Shakuni who takes the lead for the first time and suggest the dice game. He was sure he would win. But could anyone be sure that Yudhistir would bait Draupadi or his brothers? No they just wanted to win the kingdom.
  4. Enslaving Pandavas was a cherry on the top. Enslaving Draupadi was a way to humiliate Pandavas not Draupadi ← One line answer to the question.
  5. I would say based on actual Mahabharat till this point of time. Had Draupadi not resented and objected to being slave and complied to original commands of Duryodhana and presented her self to the chamber, she won’t be dragged. Kauravas would have mocked them and perhaps let them go. It would be the routing of Pandavas— defeated, humiliated and mocked with no further claim to throne. Yudhishthir would never have returned to demand the kingdom.
  6. But she was Draupadi. And she refused. Her refusal gave rise to a debate — Vikarna and Vidur supporting Draupadi, and Karna arguing against it. They exchanged words. Decorum of court was crushed under egos, abuses and arguments. And at this point, Dussasan tried to disrobe Draupadi at the suggestion of Karna. Either Duryodhana or Shakuni not engineered this episode in their original plans.← Another concise answer to this question.
  7. Uncalled for humiliation of Draupadi gave Pandavas a renewed reason and moral high ground to fight back, something they had lost otherwise. Terrified of the escalating situation, Dhritrashtra freed all Pandavas (Draupadi including). He returned their kingdom.
  8. But Duryodhan, naturally, wasn’t happy. He successfully persuaded Dhritrashtra to persuade Pandavas play one final game. What was the term of this final dice game? Was it to enslave Draupadi again? Was it to seek vengeance for his humiliation on her hand? No. It was to claim their kingdom for 13 years with another possible infinite extension. You see, it wasn’t about Draupadi, but as usual— deceit for greed.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
