Who is really responsible for Mahabharata?

📅 Oct 26, 2020 06:06 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Indeed an interesting question. Who was responsible for Mahabharata? Who was the reason for it?

  • Was it Duryodhana who refused to return Pandava’s share after 13 years of agreed exile was over?
  • Or was it Krishna, who failed to perssuade Duryodhana for a treaty with Pandava?
  • Or was it Shakuni, who won the gambling for Duryodhana and forced them to exile?
  • Or was it Yudhishthir, who gambled away his kingdom, his family and his wife and freedom?
  • Or was it the court of Hastinapur who remained moot spectator to the Gambling or let the partition happen?
  • Or was it Bhima who took an oath to kill all Kaurva’s in retaliation to Druapdi’s insult.
  • Or was it Dhritrasthra whose ambition let the rift between Kaurava’s or Pandavas grew wide?
  • Or was it Vidur, whose politics stopped Dhritrashtra from becoming the King and his ambition made him the root of the evil tree as described by Ved Vyas?
  • Or was it the Grand Bhishma, who took an oath of celebacy and let Dhritrashtra destroy Hastinapur
  • Or was it Arjuna who let Krishna sway his resolve of not fighting the Kurukshetra war?
  • Or was it Krishna himself, who perssuaded Arjuna to fight the war he had almost given up, and it was predictible — no Arjuna, no war. Wasn’t it because of Krishna’s role, Gandhari cursed him?
  • Or was it Gandhari, who decided to blind fold her eyes and remained blind to his son’s evil persuits?

Each of these people can be considered as responsible for the war, but calling any of them, except the one, truly responsible for the war would be exaggarating his ability. That would mean these people had control over destiny. It would mean Bhisma had known the futur. If Duryodhana had the abilty to prevent the war he could have done by destroying Pandavas years ago. He who had couldn’t destroy his enemies couldn’t have prevent the war either.

We must understand, Mahabharata wasn’t just a family fued between Kauravas and Pandavas. They were just the the catalys who set a chain of events in motion.

So who was that one, who had the capability to change the course of time. Well we all know — Krishna. But wait, before you jump to the conclusion that I hold him responsible for the war. What makes Krishna absolved of any responsibility was his Niskam Karma. Not being selfishly attached to the consequences. Krishna forsaw the event, but didn’t control his flow, as controlling the flow would have a greater consequence and certainly not for the good of the society. Krishna could have perssuaded Duryodhana or not have perssuaded Arjuna and could have averted the war. But then he would let the evil continue to rule without paying for their crimes.

The fact that Krishna never wanted to prevent the war itself indicates that the war was vital and needed. And preventing what must happen would give rise to a disaster or much greater magnitude tomorrw. So Krishna’s couldn’t be held responsible for a war that was already brewing without him and all he did it to let the destiny take its course of action.

So that leaves the question unanswered. Who was responsible for the war? The beauty of the life is, it doesn’t seek answer to every question. Sometimes an un-answered question keeps her quest for the search of the ultimate truth.
