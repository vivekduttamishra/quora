What if Karna had no Shakti weapon during his fight with Ghatotkacha?

📅 Nov 25, 2021 09:48 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Let us start by recapping what Indra said about the usage of Vasvi Shakti —

But if you unleash this invincible weapon in a fit of fury, when you possess other weapons, there is no doubt that it will descend on you.”

—BORI, Kundala Harana Parva

Karna couldn’t have used the weapon before exhausting all his options. That implies Karna was indeed out of his options. And this is explicitly mentioned in Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva —

His divine weapon had been destroyed by the maya. He began to think about what should be the appropriate course of action. All the Kurus saw Karna and the terrible maya. They said, ‘O Karna! Quickly kill the rakshasa now with your spear. 270 The Kurus and the sons of Dhritarashtra are being destroyed. What can Bhima and Arjuna possibly do to us? Kill the rakshasa who is destroying us in the night. We will be able to fight with the Parthas in the battle only if we escape from this terrible encounter. Therefore, kill the rakshasa, terrible in form, with the spear that Vasava has given you. O Karna! All the Kouravas are like Indra. Let these warriors not be destroyed in the night.’ O king! He saw that the army was being destroyed by the rakshasa in the night. He heard the loud lamentations of the Kouravas. ‘“Karna made up his mind to use the spear. Unable to bear the assaults made in the battle, he was like an angry and intolerant lion. That supreme spear was always victorious and he decided to use it to kill him.

—Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva

Note:

  • Karna’s divine weapons were destroyed by Ghatotkatch.
  • Karna was unable to bear the assaults.
  • There was a serious risk of total annihilation of Kauravas.

With explicit references, there is little left to imagination. Some may carry wishful thinking, today, that Karna could have overpowered Ghatotkatha, no one that night thought it was possible to win. Not even Karna.

WHAT IF KARNA HAD NO VASVI SHAKTI

I take it if Karna had no Vasvi Shakti, the exchange never took place and Karna would have his Kavacha. The divine Kavacha would have protected his life. More than his divine armor, his great capability to flee the battle may have saved his life the way it did on so many other occasion.

Either way only Karna and few others may had been left standing for the moment. But the fate of the war would have been decided that very night. And not just because of Ghatotkatch. He wasn’t alone. He would have consumed majority of the forces leaving the main villains face the dance of justice at the hands of those who swore to end their vile existence.
