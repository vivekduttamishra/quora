Though Arjun killed Karna unfairly, who was fighting in a better position in the 17th day of the war?

📅 Oct 17, 2020 12:29 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Please define unfair. And define the basis of this claim — TV Shows? Novel? Social media? bellowing of widows? Claims of Duryodhana?

Let us understand how unfair was Karna’s death.

Karna was not on his chariot.

True. Karna’s chariot had stuck up in the mud, and he was unable to pull it out. If you go by the epic reference, it was because of a curse of a Brahmin, because of Karna’s doing. But let us leave the curse aside.

Was that unfair? Karna’s chariot was stuck up. It wasn’t destroyed. Karna alighting his chariot was his decision. He could have chosen a different chariot instead of pleading to stop the war. Let us first analyse how many other warriors in Kurukshetra fought without chariot before this day —

  • Abhimanyu — He was killed without chariot. The one difference was, he fought on multiple warriors together making them flee. Karna was one of them.
  • Drona — On Fourteenth day, Bheems broke Drona’s chariot several times. But an experienced and intelligent general that Drona was, he moved to another chariot every time.
  • Bheema — On Fourteenth day, Karna broke the chariot of Bheema and continued to attach him while he was chariotless. What did Bhima do? He threw a dead elephant and continued to fight. He never once asked for a reccess during the fight.
  • Satyaki — He too fought from ground without a chariot. Guess whom was he fighting? Well you guessed it.

If you see here on 3 out of 4 occassions, the warrior who attacked chanotherariotless warrior, was the one for whom you claim he was killed unfairly. What shall I call it irony? hypocracy or poetic justice?

Was Karna without a weapon?

Karna, may have chosen to be out of his chariot and certainly had asked for a reccess, he wasn’t without a weapon. In fact, this was one occassion where he didn’t flee, but fought bravely till the end. That is something worth applauding. Now calling that he couldn’t fight and died haplessly is an insult to few glorious moment Karna can recount.

‘ “On seeing this, Karna again invoked brahmastra against Dhananjaya. He showered down arrows and tried to extricate his chariot. Pandava countered those weapons with his own weapons. Kounteya then released another weapon, beloved of the fire god, towards Karna. It blazed fiercely. Karna pacified the fire through a varuna weapon. He covered all the directions with clouds and it was as dark as a rainy day. Pandaveya was not frightened. While Radheya looked on, the valiant one used the vayavya weapon and dispelled all the clouds.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4960-4963).

So let us now move to the question —

Who was fight better on the 17th day of the war

On this way the two warriors founght with bravely. Karna was at the top of his performance on that day. His valour can be checked from following references —

It was wonderful. Using his weapons, Radheya countered all of Savyasachi’s weapons. At that time, his valour seemed to be greater than that of Partha.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4939-4940).

However, if you look at this statement, it clearly indicates two things —

  • It is mentioned as an assumptions (seemed)
  • It talks about a moment and not about the entire day.

But there are several references as to how bravely he fought. Even Krishna and Bhima mentioned his valours here.

Cheerfully, but fiercely, they attacked each other in the great conflict. In the great conflict, the great-souled Bhimasena became wrathful and intolerant. He squeezed his hands and bit his lips with his teeth. Dancing around like a musician, he asked, ‘O Kiriti! How was it that the son of a suta was able to pierce you first with ten great arrows? Do you remember the fortitude with which you defeated all beings and satisfied Agni in Khandava? Use that fortitude and kill the son of a suta.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4816-4820).

Here is what Krishna said —

‘O Kiriti! How is it that in every way, your weapons today are being countered by Karna’s weapons? O brave one! Why do you look like someone who is confused? The Kurus are cheerfully roaring. All of them are honouring Karna, knowing that all your weapons have been destroyed by his. In yuga after yuga, you have used fortitude to destroy the weapons of darkness and terrible rakshasas. You slew Dambhodbhava and other asuras in encounters.778 Use that fortitude and slay the son of a suta. Or use this sudarshana chakra now and slice off his head.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4821-4825). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

If you checkout these statements you can observe following interesting points —

  1. Karna, no doubt, gave his best performance.
  2. Both Krishna and Bhishma knows Arjuna is capable of killing Karna. They wonder why Arjuna is not giving his best. They are urging him to finish it fast.
  3. Krishna offers Arjuna his Sudarshana chakra to slay Karna. That, as I see, was one of the greatest tale of a true hero. He passed on the offer to get one of the greatest known weapon. This is so unlike his adversary who sought Indra’s vasvi shakti out of his self confessed fear of Arjuna. But Arjuna wouldn’t accept a weapon he hasn’t rightfully earned. That was Arjuna.

But was their valour equal?

First we must understand that everyone knew that Karna’s end was drawing near including Karna himself. This is evident from Karna’s own statement —

I will single-handedly slay the large army of the Pandavas and the two Krishnas, tigers among men. I tell you this truthfully. Without killing those two brave warriors, I will not retreat. Or I will be slain by them and will lie down. Victory in a battle is uncertain. But I will be successful in my objective, whether I kill them, or whether I am killed.

At this, the son of a suta smiled and told Shalya, ‘O friend! If through some means, Partha slays me in the battle today, what will you do after that? Tell me truthfully.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7

Here Karna praises Arjuna whole heartedly

It is said that no one like him has been born in this world. He is supreme among rathas. That is what we have heard. I will fight against the Partha who is like that.

He is firm and accomplished in the use of weapons. He is light in the use of his hands. There is no warrior who is Pandaveya’s equal. He grasps many arrows tufted with the feathers of herons. He shoots them as if they were but one. They descend at the distance of one krosha and do not deviate from their aim.

Where is the warrior on earth who is his equal?

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7

We must also mark the words with which Karna’s performance in the war has been referred. Here are a few reference —

Bala. Arjuna shot ninety arrows at Karna and each of them was like Yama’s staff. Those arrows severely mangled his body, like a mountain shattered by thunder.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7

I don’t see any similar statement mentioned for Arjuna. Rather there is a stark difference in the statments —

Karna again pierced Pandava with terrible arrows and roared loudly. Pandava could not tolerate this joy. He was like Indra in his valour.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4907-4908).

Here Arjuna’s body, though pierced is not mangled nor is he like a mountain shattered by thunder. Rather He roars like Indra.

The story further describes Karna’s conditions in these references —

In an instant, Pandava shattered this into many fragments with his arrows. Having deprived him of his armour with those excellent arrows, he then angrily struck him with four arrows. Distressed and struck by his enemy, he was like a diseased person, suffering from bile, phlegm, wind and wounds. Arjuna spiritedly shot sharp arrows from the great circle of his bow.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4911-4914).

Checkout the descriptions of Karna’s suffering once again. You won’t find a similar mention of Arjuna’s plight here.

Now observe the highlight in this reference which clearly indicates Karna was drawing to his end —

“Karna also shot a large number of arrows from his bow. With those nets of arrows as rays, he looked dazzling. He was like the sun advancing towards sunset, red and with a crimson solar disc.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4921-4923).

As we reach towards the final and much before Karna’s chariot gave away —

He was struck in his inner organs and was incapable of acting. He repeatedly censured dharma.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4929-4930).

Not he begins to censure Dharma for his current agony. If you want to check out his exact words here, when Brahmastra didn’t manifest at his call —

Unable to tolerate this, he whirled his garments and his arms around and lamented, ‘Those who know about dharma have always held that that dharma protects those who place dharma at the forefront. But instead of protecting one who is devoted, it is now bringing me down. I think that dharma does not always protect.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4926-4928). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Here instead of seeing Karna repenting for his misdeeds, he is still blaming Dharma for his condition. No wonder Krishna had to remind him of his wrong doings.

A careful examination of the battle would conclude —

  1. Karna without doubt fought with great valour on the last day of his battle.
  2. But he had an apparent premonition about his approaching death, which he discussed with Shalya and even in front of Arjuna and Krishna.
  3. Krishna proclaimed in no uncertain term, no matter what Karna would never be able to defeat Arjuna.
  4. Krishna enquires Arjuna why is he prolonging the delay of Karna’s death with in Ghatotkatch Parva itself he had proclaimed is certain to happen.
  5. He offered his sudharshana chakra and Arjuna showed his ultimate character by not accepting the weapon as gift.
  6. Karna’s attack while fearce didn’t produce the affects similar to those done by Arjuna where the phrases used are —
    1. Karna’s body was mangled and he trembled.
    2. He looked distressed, diseased like a mountain shattered by the sun.
    3. Karna looked like a setting sun.
    4. He was incapable of acting
  7. All the above description is before his chariot stuck into the earth.
  8. No similar description of tormented Arjuna is available.

Based on authentic references you may decide who fought better on the last day and every other day.
