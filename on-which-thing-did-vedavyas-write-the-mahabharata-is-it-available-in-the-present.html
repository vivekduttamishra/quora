On which thing did Vedavyas write the Mahabharata? Is it available in the present? If yes, where is it?

📅 Oct 02, 2020 06:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi, thanks for the A2A.

The thing on which Vedvyas wrote Mahabharata or Valmiki wrote Ramayana is still present and you possess it — it is called Smriti — What is remembered. And to write on this device you need an instrument, we called Shruti.

We are talking about an incident that occurred over five thousand years ago. No paper, no leaf bark and no ink would survive this long.

Have you heard about storage divices called floppy disk and magnetic tapes? Have you heard about Orkut, ICQ etc? Where are they now? They disappeared in a span of 2 decades. How would any written or stored information survive a long time?

Our ancient society was wide beyond age, and engraved its teaching on one media that would be timeless — Human memory.

They committed the information to the tradition of shruti-smriti and propoagated the information using dna cells of human brain — not one human but an unbroken human tradition. Each generation, heard the information (Shruti), committed it to their memory (Smriti) and transferred to the next generation (Smriti). Unfortunately we are loosing this great tradition in the name of technological advancements.

Good news is, you and I are media. Can we keep it alive and going?
