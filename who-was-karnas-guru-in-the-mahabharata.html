Who was Karna's guru in the Mahabharata?

📅 Mar 08, 2021 08:05 PM PST  |  🌐 English

This question has been raised innumerable times and not without a good reason. Contemporary literature spanning over last hundred years tried to distort the great epic. And in order to build a case Karna-as-a-victim, and made an impression that Drona rejected Karna into his gurukul. Since then every TVShow followed the suite.

But, if we talk about the great epic Mahabharata and any ancient version of it, it clearly mentions that Karna was trained by Dronacharya, Kripacharya and Parshurama.

May this answer become a reference point to quench further question on this line —

REFERENCE#1 ADI PARVA

O bull among the Bharata lineage! Other princes also came to Drona, supreme among Brahmanas, to learn the use of arms—the Vrishnis, the Andhakas, kings from many countries and Radheya, 344 the son of the suta. They made Drona their preceptor. The suta’s son was envious of Partha and always competed with him. With Duryodhana’s support, he showed his contempt for the Pandavas.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5033-5036).

NOTE:

  • Adi parva clearly mentions Radheya the son of Suta amade Drona his perceptor.
  • It also indicates the envy that Karna harbored for Arjuna from an early period, dating long before the famous face-off in play arena.

REFERENCE #2: KUNDALA AHARANA PARVA

In due course, when suta Adhiratha saw that his son had grown up, he sent him to the city of Varanasahrya. 33 There, he went to Drona to learn about weapons. The brave one became Duryodhana’s friend. Having obtained the four kinds of weapons from Drona, Kripa and Rama, he became famous in the world as a great archer. 34 Having allied himself with Dhritarashtra’s son, he was engaged in enmity towards the Parthas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8522-8526).

Note:

  • It again clearly mentions that he learnt from Drona, Kripa and Rama (Parshurama)
  • It also and again mentions, he was engaged in enimity towards Parthas.

REFERENCE #3: SHANTI PARVA

He was energetic as a child and came to be known as the son of a suta. He went to the best of the Angirasa lineage, your preceptor, 9 to learn about the science of war.10 O Indra among kings! He thought of Bhima’s strength, Phalguna’s dexterity, your intelligence, the humility of the twins, the friendship that the wielder of Gandiva has had with Vasudeva since childhood and the devotion of the subjects and was tormented. From childhood, he formed a friendship with King Duryodhana. This is because of the enmity he always bore towards you and natural destiny. He saw that Dhananjaya was superior to everyone in learning about dhanurveda. Karna secretly went to Drona and spoke these words. ‘I wish to know about brahmastra and the secrets of releasing and withdrawing it. It is my view that I should become Arjuna’s equal in battle. It is certain that the affection you bear towards your disciples is equal to what you bear towards your son. Because of your favours, make me accomplished and skilled in the use of weapons.’ Drona was partial towards Phalguna. He also knew about Karna’s wickedness. Having been thus addressed by Karna, he replied, ‘The brahmastra can only be known by a brahmana who is observant of the vows, or by a kshatriya who has performed austerities, and by no one else.’ Having been thus addressed by the best of the Angirasa lineage, he honoured him and took his leave. He then quickly went to Rama11 on Mount Mahendra. Having approached Rama, he lowered his head in obeisance before him and said, ‘O Bhargava! I am a brahmana.’ This earned him respect. Rama welcomed him and asked him everything about his gotra.12 He was extremely delighted at this warm welcome.

Because of this, he was loved by the gods, the gandharvas and the rakshasas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2345-2357).

Note:

  • Once again it is clearly mentioned Drona was his guru.
  • Drona refused him the higher weapon knowledge as he knew he was wicked.
  • Karna knew, Arjuna was his superior.
  • He went to Parshurama and lied to him again.
  • Because of being disciple of Parshurama he was loved by Gods, demons and Asura.

All the references in Mahabharata conclusively points out to the following facts —

  • Karna was taught by Drona, Kripa and Rama.
  • From gurukul days he was envious of Arjuna whom he found superior to himself.
  • Drona refused him higher knowledge because he found him deceitful.
  • Karna went on to Parshruama, where he obtained knowledge and fame by deceit
