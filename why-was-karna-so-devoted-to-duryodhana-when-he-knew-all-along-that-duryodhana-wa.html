Why was Karna so devoted to Duryodhana, when he knew all along that Duryodhana was Adharmi?

📅 Feb 07, 2021 11:35 PM PST  |  🌐 English

I have answered several such questions in the past, one of the recent one is here — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Was Karna not an exemplary man of honor that he promised Kunti that he would not kill any of her sons except Arjuna and kept that promise?

Second part of the question is in fact correct — Duryodhana was evil, and anyone who says otherwise either hasn’t read the epic or don’t know the meaning of what an evil is. Duryodhana has been referred as evil in original Vyas’ work on uncountable occasion throughout the epic.

But I would like to challenge the first part of the question with my question —

WAS KARNA DEVOTED TO DURYODHANA?

  • Karna fled Gandharvas leaving his best friend and women of royal family behind at the mercy of Gandharvas or Pandavas. Was that devotion?
  • Karna promised Kunti, without her even asking, to spare the life of Yudhisthir, who was the contender to the throne and Bhima who had promised to kill him and all his brothers. This doesn’t sound devotion.
  • Karna never informed Duryodhana about the promise he made to Kunti.
  • Karna refused to fight for his friend for the first ten days of the battle for his ego. Guess his ego was greater than his devotion.

WHY DID KARNA TAGGED ALONG DURYODHANA, IF NOT LOYALTY

Karna’s life revolved around a single axis — Arjuna.

Karna never strived to be anything other than better than Arjuna. He cherished no dream greater than defating Arjuna. All his action throughout his life was focused on this single point —

  • How to defeat Arjuna
  • How to kill Arjuna
  • How to mock Arjuna in exile
  • How to vanquish him in Matasya war.

Now to fulfil his mission he had only one path — Duryodhana. Following advises of Krishna or Kunti would have taken his life mission away from him.

More than devotion, Karna-Duryodhana friendship was a relation of mutual benefit and convinience.
