Why did Krishna like Karna in the Mahabharata?

📅 Dec 09, 2021 08:34 AM PST  |  🌐 English

God never reject anyone, we in our ignorance and arrogance reject God. He is always eager and waiting for us to see the light of Dharma. God doesn’t envy or hate anyone. Neither is he biased. He is too big and compassionate to be driven by lowly human emotions. He gives opportunity to everyone — even the lowest of lowly beasts. It is up to us if we chose him or reject him. And one who rejects God is condemned to a vile existence.

— Vivek Dutta Mishra

Krishna, too, gave opportunity to everyone — even vile creatures like Duryodhana and Karna. But blinded by their lust and arrogance they rejected him. And Krishna, who incarnated to destroy evil and protect good had no alternative to rise against evil powers.

Here is what Krishna said about Karna. See, if you can find how much he liked him.

Kairata Parva

Krishna meets Pandavas after they were exiled and humilated in the Dyut Parva. Here is what he said about Karna.

Vasudeva said, “The earth will drink the blood of Duryodhana, Karna, the evil-souled Shakuni and Duhshasana as the fourth. After that, all of us will instate Dharmaraja Yudhishthira. Those deceitful ones deserve to be slain.

—Kairata Parva

Drona Vadha Parva

Krishna is overcome by great delight after Gandharva’s battle. What is the cause of this supreme satisfaction?

‘O Dhananjaya! I am overcome by great delight. Listen to me. I will tell you what will bring supreme satisfaction to your mind. O immensely radiant one! Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain.

— Dorna Vadha Parva

Karna Vadha Parva

Krishna urges Arjuna to kill Karna. He remind him of all his crimes including Lakshagriha, Abhimamyu Vadha and Dyutshabha and leaves no room for doubt about his likings or disliking. Read the extract, especially the highlights —

He does not know about dharma and is extremely evil-minded. Those were the words that he spoke then. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Karna spoke those wicked and sinful words in your hearing. Those were the evil one’s words. Arrows decorated with gold, sharpened on stone and capable of taking away life, released by you, will pacify those words and other wicked deeds that the evil-souled one has done towards you. Let your arrows rob him of his life and pacify those and other things today. Let his limbs be touched by terrible arrows shot from Gandiva. Let the evil-souled Karna remember the words of Bhishma and Drona. Let gold-tufted iron arrows, capable of killing the enemy and with the radiance of lightning, be shot by you. Let them pierce his inner organs and drink his blood. O Arjuna! Let fierce, immensely forceful and sharp arrows be shot by you and let them penetrate his inner organs. Strike Karna with great force and convey him to Yama’s eternal abode. Let all the lords of the earth see Karna brought down from his chariot, oppressed by your arrows. Let them utter woes of lamentation and let them be miserable and distressed. Let all his well-wishers see Karna prostrate, deprived of his weapons. Let them be cheerless on seeing him shattered, drenched in his own blood and lying down on the ground.

—Karna Vadha, Parva

The last highlight is clear to indicate Krishna didn’t count himself as Karna’s well wisher. But here is one of the final quotes, when Arjuna asks Krishna what would happen if he dies in the battle at the hands of Karna?

Krishna laughed and spoke these supreme words to Partha. ‘The sun may fall down from its place. The earth may shatter into many fragments. The fire may become cold. But Karna will not be able to kill Dhananjaya. However, if this does happen, the world will be destroyed. Using my arms in the battle, I will kill Karna and Shalya.’

— Karna Vadha Parva


WHY DID KRISHNA OFFERED KINGDOM TO KARNA?

Krishna did praise Karna on two occasions. The first when he offered him the entire kingdom in exchange for avoiding the approaching apocalyptic war. Krishna was hopeful that he would see the light of dharma and accept him. As I said God is always ready to embrace us. It is our own evil design which prevents this union. Same thing happened with Karna. He rejected Krishna’s extended hand and condemned himself. You see God carry no malice, our own malice prevents us in reaching him. Had Karna accepted Krishna, he would have washed his sins.

But those who think Krishna liked Karna and that is why he approached him, no only miss his remark from Kairata Parva but also Udyoga Parva where Krishna offers a similar proposition to Dhritrashtra and Duryodhana like one he offered to Karna —

O lord of the earth! Reflect on the consequences and then act accordingly. O lord of men! You are capable of uniting the Bharatas. O king! Protected by the Pandavas, you will be established in dharma and artha. O lord of men! However much you try, you will not obtain people like them. When the great-souled Pandavas protect you, not even Indra with the gods can withstand you. How can kings? O bull among the Bharata lineage! Who will wish to fight against a side that has Bhishma, Drona, Kripa, Karna, Vivimshati, Ashvatthama, Vikarna, Somadatta, Bahlika, Saindhava, Kalinga, Kamboja, Sudakshina, Yudhishthira, Bhimasena, Savyasachi, the twins and the immensely energetic maharatha Satyaki? O destroyer of enemies! With the Kurus and the Pandavas, you will become invincible before your enemies and will become the unrivalled lord of the world. O lord of the earth! O scorcher of enemies! Lords of the earth who are your equal and kings who are your superior will seek alliances with you. Protected in every direction by your sons, grandsons, brothers, fathers and well-wishers, you will live happily. O lord of the earth! Give primacy to their interests and treat them well, as you have in the past, and you will enjoy the entire earth.

—Bhagwat Yaan Parva

And then he tells to Duryodhana —

An alliance with the Pandavas is superior to one with the wicked. If you live affectionately with them, you will obtain every object of desire. O supreme among kings! You will enjoy the land conquered by the Pandavas.

—Bhagwat Yaan Parva

As you can see, Krishna tried to bargain with all devils to avoid the complete destruction. It is nothing personal about Karna.

WHY DID KRISHNA PRAISE KARNA IN DRONA VADHA PARVA?

Krishna did praise Karna in Drona vadha parva, right after expressing his extreme delight at the prospect of his death being easier. Here is what Krishna said —

He is devoted to brahmanas. He is truthful. He has engaged in austerities. He is devoted to his vows. He is compassionate towards his enemies. It is for these reasons that Karna is known as Vrisha.

—Drona Parva

This is indeed flattering, and goes on to prove the essence of Krishna. He gave the credit where it was due. Karna indeed donated his reaches to Brahamanas there by performing the austerities. But Krishna doesn’t say he was not evil or he shouldn’t be killed. Rather he immediately explained that in near future we must kill him. And that has been a recurring topic thought out the epic.


SUMMARY

Krishna never showed any special affection for Karna, rather his words carried contempt for him on multiple occasion across many years. He willed him to die and even pronounced that if Arjuna fails he would kill him himself. Yet he also praised Karna on ocassion and even tried to enlist his help. But he did similar praise of Duryodhana and Dhritrashtra as well, with single intention — avoid the devastating war.
