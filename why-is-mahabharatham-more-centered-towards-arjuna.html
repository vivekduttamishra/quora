Why is Mahabharatham more centered towards Arjuna?

📅 Jul 10, 2020 09:28 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

The Grand Epic Mahabharatam is actually centered on Krishna, the 8th Vishnu, who incarnated to establish Dharama.

Arjuna shares this center by being on the side of Krishna — on the side of Dharma.

Krishna is the ultimate guide and he needed an executor. But interestingly he doesn’t choose any warrior — He let the warrior choose him. And that choice makes Arjuna great.

He gives the offer to everyone. He walks down to Duryodhana and pleads for the peace — grant just the five villages. Duryodhana refused. He pleads to Karna for peace — be the King, but eliminate the war. Karna refuses. These great people sidelined Krishna, not realizing, in sidelining Krishna they are actually sidelining themselves for Krishna is the center of Mahabharata.

The best instance is when both Duryodhana and Arjuna seeks Krishna’s help. Duryodhana was content with the might of Krishna’s army when Arjuna selected a weaponless non-combatant Krishna. This choice makes Arjuna the center character of Mahabharata — how many others would have made a similar choice?

What does this tell us? Even if Krishna gives you an opportunity, you must be worthy of it. And Arjuna was worthy of it.

Many believe Arjuna was nothing without the grace of Krishna, Hanumana and Shiva. Many feel offended by this statement. But I fully endorse this view. In fact no one is anything without the grace of almighty. Those who think they are, are fools! But it is not easy to come in the grace of the Shiva. You need to do tapas and you must be pure in heart. So how was Arjuna pure —

  • He was so dedicated a disciple, that Drona became his fan!
  • His devotion made Shiva and Krishna happy
  • Shiva trusted him enough to bestow Pashupatastra on him. And he didn’t disappoint him. He never abused its power.

And most important!

Arjuna was the only warrior to question the relevance of war! When he surrendered his weapon, he surrendered his ego. He wasn’t fighting for personal glory. This gave the world the divine discourse of Gita. But note, Gita is not really a preaching — it’s a dialog between Krishna and Arjuna.

Only a true warrior, who is not out to prove something, can choose not to fight. This made Krishna announce — I am Krishna among Vrishnis and Arjuna among Pandavas.

And yes, Mahabharata is centered around Krishna-Arjuna. Arjuna is the center character because Arjuna chose to be in the center.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
