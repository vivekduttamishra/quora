Was Arjun defeated karn ever in fair battle? In Kurukshetra war and before Kurukshetra was.

📅 Sep 19, 2020 12:27 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

If you go by the authentic epic, Arjuna defeated Karna in every single encounter they had and none of them have been deemed unfair. Karna died fighting as a warrior.

  1. Karna attacks Arjuna after Draupadi’s Swayamvara. Arjuna had no special weapon, not Gandiva. He fought with the weapon he earned in Swayamvara. Yet Karna retreated comparing him to Parshuram, Indra and Vishnu and the personification of knowledge of weapon.
  2. Gandharva war — wasn't a direct encounter. But Karna was supported by a large army and yet he was one of the first to flee the scene. Arjuna saved the day with the help of his four friends.

In both these cases Karna had his invincible Kavacha. Yet he failed and fled. This is an important point to consider. Kavach or celestial weapons are no replacement for real courage and perseverance. And that is where Karna lacked. He was an excellent archer, protected by divine Kavacha but he lack courage and perseverance that led to his repeated failures.

3. MATASYA BATTLE — Arjuna won. Just before the war Karna had declared that he would defeat and kill Arjuna. That said he was prepared. He was also supported by warriors like Drona, Bhisma and Ashwathama. Yet he could not win. Worse than losing, he like always fled.

Now I say people claim Arjuna won because of his weapons. Maybe. But one must remember —

  1. Arjuna wasn't born with one. He earned those weapons by struggling for it. He didn't steal them by deceit or got in exchange. Never looked for the shortcuts, never asked for them without showing worthiness.
  2. He never used more powers than required. He never used Pashupatastra, although it was with him.
  3. If we criticize Arjuna for using the weapons he earned, no one would have any incentive to work hard and earn. It's like a hard working student tops the class and get a great placement and someone says he had a unfair advantage of being the topper.
  4. Arjuna indeed had the support of Krishna and Shiva. Was it an unfair advantage?
  5. Incidently people who shout that Arjuna had unfair advantage lament Karna's loss of Kavach or Vasvi shakti. Ironic. Right?
  6. Krishna tricked Karna into killing Ghatotkatch. But why didn't Karna killed Ghatotkatch with other divine weapon? Was it fair for Ghatotkatch to be killed by Vasvi shakti?
  7. Would it be unfair for India to use their best missiles against Pakistan? Was it unfair if India's Field Marshall Sam Manikshaw was smarter Than Pakistani counterparts?
  8. First someone will cry foul that
    1. You have better weapon
    2. You have better advisor
  9. But people forget, that Arjun has those support, because either Arjuna was a better person or Shiva and Krishna were fools. I will go with former. What do you think?
  10. They also forget, Karna too had a chance — remember he rejected Krishna, to side with evils. His choice led to his downfall.
