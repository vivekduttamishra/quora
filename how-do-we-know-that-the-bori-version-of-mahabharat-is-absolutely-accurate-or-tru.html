How do we know that the BORI version of Mahabharat is absolutely accurate or true? Just because they did years of research on most manuscripts having similar stories doesn't mean that it’s the truth, right?

📅 Sep 01, 2021 11:39 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: No one says BORI version of Mahabharata is absolutely accurate or true. But it is certainly more accurate and trust worthy than any other source.

We must understand that Mahabharata is based on an incident that took place over five thousand years ago and the narrative of that epic era propagated for thousands of years by the means of Shruti-Smriti tradition.

Let us understand what is Shruti-Smriti traidtion. Shruti means ‘what is heard’ and Smriti means what is remembered.

Consider the scenario — My father tells me a story which I memorize and later pass on the same story to my children. May be after years. Do I remember everything exactly the same way I heard? May be not. May be when I narrated the story, I glorified a certain part more than my father did and my children may add his own creative ideas. Thus the description that my children and my brother’s children would hear may be slightly different. THIS CAN’T BE CHANGED.

But see the brighter side — Suppose you heard the narrative from your school teacher with fifty of your class mates. Now two may pass this story to next generation with an stretch of creative imagination on one side and two with creative imagination on other side. Over time those stories too will spread. But those 4 deviations and their propagation won’t be able to surpass the propagation by 46 other students who didn’t alter the narrative. Now say after 50 years if we sit down and compare the versions, hundreds may have heard deviation #1 and another hundred may have heard deviation #2. But there are thousands who have heard the original tale. Now the question is how do I verify which one is accurate? Of course a researcher may trace the origin and isolate the incident on these grounds —

  1. Deviation#1 and Deviation#2 would be contradicting not only the most popular and widespread narrative but also each other.
  2. Those deviation are applied on a small portion of the epic and if we compare the story with rest of the epic, they would be also be self contradictory.

This is exactly what BORI did. Now with thousands of years of non-written propagation may have added some details, but they are easy to weed out as we would see they don’t exist in narratives present in different narratives.

One of the greatest beauty of Shruti-Smriti tradition is the fact that the narrative can neither be suppressed nor controlled because there is no controller and the spread is democratic in nature — propagated by the people.

SUMMARY — You may choose to doubt the accuracy of BORI but can’t offer anything more accurate than that.
