Is Vaisampayana Says "Karna is the best Warrior in Mahabharata"?

📅 Feb 24, 2022 05:14 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

SHORT ANSWER: NO. Even Karna said so.

That said, in the epic different warriors, even a very minor warrior’s valor have be exaggerated. Many warriors have been referred as Indra among men moments before they died without being of any significance whatsoever. So I won’t be surprised if few blind follower of the most evil character of Mahabharata may try to distort the epic by quoting some astray lines. But based on the entire epic narrative if Karna was greatest is something it was in being evil, If Karna was best in something, it was being absolute worst.

I won’t waste my energy in quoting numerous defeats Karna had in his life or the fact that Krishna when Krishna said Karna was invanquishable he wasn’t praising Karna, he was praising the divine artifacts Karna was holding. What I am keen in understanding is how is this question relevant?

WHAT DOES BEST WARRIOR MEAN?

Should a ‘best’ warrior be also a good human or he just need to be a killing machine having eye set straight on victory? Well either way, Karna can’t be considered as a warrior.

Karna was the trunk of the evil tree ‘Duryodhana’. He was one of the worst character of Mahabharata — a wicked, deceitful, coward, traitor. How can we use the word ‘best’ for a person who would like to strip a woman in public or try to burn her alive? How can a person be best if he is not loyal to even his own family, benefactor or friend?

Now you may say, we just wanted to talk about just he ‘warrior’ aspect. So tell me which ‘best’ warrior would keeping running away from the battlefield to save his own life? Which great warrior would let his own brothers die while he runs away? Karna did it twice! Which great warrior wouldn’t care about the honor of his own family woman and let them be captured by enemy and would save his own life?

Does a traitor also qualify as a warrior? In modern phrase, they are called deserters. They are considered war criminals. And here we have a deserter who runs away letting his King taken captured and people are roping him being the best warrior.

S U M M A R Y

Vaisampanya never said Karna was the best warrior of the Mahabharata. NOT ONCE. There is not a single battle in the entire epic that hints that he was the best warrior.

Great people like Krishna, Parshurama, Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama, Indra and above all God of Gods Mahadeva himself explicitly pronounced a warrior as the best warrior of the era. He was NOT Karna.

  • About that other warrior
    • Shiva called this warrior, beyond comparison in the universe.
    • Parshurama dared Kshatriyas to come to their senses before he takes up his bow.
    • Krishna dared that entire Kuru army together cant’ withstand this one warrior.
    • Even Karna called him as a warrior without an equal.
    • He was one warrior who never lost a battle
    • He was the one warrior who faced great Mahadeva and Mahadeva took him as his disciple.
    • He was a warrior who even helped Gods
    • He was a warrior who even defeated army of Gods and Gandharvas.
