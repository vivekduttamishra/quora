Wearing the armour presented by Drona, Duryodhana could not stand before Arjuna. From that point, is the natural kavach and kundal of Karna over hyped?

📅 Dec 09, 2021 05:46 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Short answer: Karna’s armors are not as much overhyped in the epic as they have been afterwards.

While reading the epic text it must be understood that any several points there have been aggregations or metaphors that are superlative. Many warriors of no great importance have been referred as lions or Indra among warriors. At other times statements have been made with no cross reference. Are they interpolations? NO. They are not interpolations. Some of them are simple writing style to make the reading more impressive. Some of them are psychological statements, which you must take into account before reaching any conclusion. And the best way would be to cross-reference those statements to validate them.

WHAT DOES EPIC REFERENCES SAY ABOUT KARNA’S ARMOUR?

There are several reference that suggests that Karna’s armor were divine and invincible. But then Drona, the great guru, mentioned that with proper design armors can be made invicible. He mentioned Abhimanyu’s armor as invincible and gave a similar one to Duryodhana on day 14. That said, it was a matter of technique and not divinity. On the other hand Karna’s armor were divine.

Apart from the armor, his earrings were made up of Amrita that would prevent Karna’s death. No one else had similar divine armors. Karna got this divine armor because of his mother Kunti’s insistence from Suryadeva —

‘Kunti said, “O dispeller of everything that is dark! If I obtain a son through you, may he have earrings and armour. May he be mighty-armed, brave and immensely strong.” ‘Surya replied, “He will be mighty-armed and possess earrings. O fortunate one! He will be clad in divine armour and both25 of these will originate in amrita.” ‘Kunti said, “If my son’s earrings and the excellent armour are created out of amrita, as you have said, you may beget him on me. O god! O illustrious one! Then I will unite with you.

—Kundala Harana Parva

This is repeated by Indra to Karna as he warned him of Indra’s plans —

O Karna! If you give away the beautiful earrings that you were naturally born with, your life will be shortened and you will come under the power of death. O one who is reverent! Arrayed in your armour and earrings, you cannot be slain by your enemies in battle. Remember these words. These two treasures arose from amrita. O Karna! If you desire to live, they must therefore be carefully protected by you.”

— Kundala Harana Parva

Both these statement suggests Karna’s armors and earrings were divine and could have prevented his death.

And then there is a third statement, by Krishna after Ghatotkatch’s death —

It is through good fortune that his armour has gone. It is through good fortune that his earrings have been robbed. It is through good fortune that the invincible spear has been used up on Ghatotkacha. Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals. Vasava, Kubera, Varuna, lord of the waters, and Yama would not have ventured against Karna in an encounter. Had that bull among men possessed those, you with your Gandiva and I with my sudarshana chakra would not have had the capacity to defeat him in a battle.

— Drona Vadha Parva

And it is for this point, I asked you to search for cross-references.

DID ARMOR REALLY MADE KARNA INVINCIBLE?

Krishna said no one could defeat Karna had he possessed the armor. But Karna faced defeats. Again and Again. And yet again.

He got scared and defeated and retreated after Swaymver battle when he fought Arjuna. He had his armor and Arjuna had no Gandiva. Arjuna’s arrow pierced Karna despite of having armor.

He again lost and surrendered to Bhima during Rajsuy yagna battle. He still had his invincible armor and Bhima and not divine weapon like Gandiva or Sudarshana Chakra.

He, yet again, lost to Gandharavas. He was badly injured, his body mangled with arrows. He still had his armor on. He got scared and fled for his life.

Here we see two things —

  1. Despite of armor, it was possible to wound Karna. Some may argue that armors could protect only part of the body and not the who person. They may protect vitals like torso, stomach, and chest while hands and legs may remain exposed. But remember, every exposure leads to injuries that in turn leads to defeat.
  2. Karna didn’t die in those cases. I believe due to earring Karna may have lived on even if someone had severed his head off — just like Rahu. Amrita were that powerful. But could Karna without his hands won any battle, I would leave that to your imagination.

DID KRISHNA LIE WHEN HE SAID NO ONE COULD HAVE DEFEATED KARNA WITH ARMOR ON?

NO. He didn’t. He explained a possibility highlighting the significance of armor so that Arjuna realizes the advantage he now has. But we must understand it is praise of armor and not the wearer of it.

Think for a moment — Some one has a divine armor better than others. You have an artifact made up of Amrita and you couldn’t be killed in a battle, something even Krishna didn’t have. What could have stopped you from being invincible and victorious, if you decide to stand and fight? That is what Krishna mentioned — A possibility. Not a certainty. Not a history of the past. Not a future prediction. Just a theory.

But as we know Karna lost battles, nevertheless. He lost even when opponent didn’t carry Sudharshana Chakra or Gandiva or any divine weapon. How do we comprehend that?

Idea is simple — armors are tools of defense not offense. They don’t attack enemy. They can’t win battles.

Again armors can protect your vitals only from physical attacks. Armors can’t protect someone from his own fear. It can’t protect someone from getting afraid or running away.

And this is exactly what happened with Karna. He lost repeatedly because his armor couldn’t protect him from getting afraid or running away.

KARNA VS ARMOUR

The problem arises when people confuse between warriors and his artifact and confuse one for the other. Someone who lacks spiritual strength in his character would cling to artifacts. His well wishers would wish they could hold on to them. His opponent's would like to strip those artifacts away. But true power or reputation of a warrior is ascertained only when you can separate the two. So let me take you back to Surya and Krishna for this —

O Karna! If you give away the beautiful earrings that you were naturally born with, your life will be shortened and you will come under the power of death. O one who is reverent! Arrayed in your armour and earrings, you cannot be slain by your enemies in battle. Remember these words. These two treasures arose from amrita. O Karna! If you desire to live, they must therefore be carefully protected by you.”

— Kundala Harana Parva

Check out yourself, what is Surya’s opinion about Karna without armour — his survival depended on it. And here is Krishna’s remark often ignored by Karna’s fans —

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain.

It is because he sliced off the armour and the sparkling earrings and gave them to Shakra that Karna came to be known as Vaikartana. 1 Karna is now like an angry snake, whose energy has been sapped through mantras. Therefore, Karna is now like a fire whose flames have been pacified.

—Drona Vadha Parva

This is Krishna’s view about Karna without divine artifacts.

You must also see, that even Karna realized that divine armor couldn’t help him win the battle so he happily exchanged it for Indra’s weapon. Here are two relevant references —

O son! O Karna! Therefore, ask for what you want. With the exception of my vajra, you can ask for whatever else you wish.” At that, Karna happily approached Vasava. ‘Content in his mind, he asked for the invincible shakti. Karna said, “O Vasava! In exchange for my armour and earrings, give me the invincible shakti that kills large numbers of enemies on the field of battle.”

— Kundala Hara Parva

He further expressed his true reason for having this exchange —

Karna replied, “I wish to kill only one enemy37 in a great battle. He roars and blazes and is the source of fear for me.”

— Kundala Hara Parva

And Karna desperately clung to vasvi shakti as his hope for killing his one enemy that was source for fear for him. This is again confirmed by Krishna —

Since the time he obtained it, Vrisha has always thought that you have been killed in the battle. O unblemished one! O tiger among men! However, though that spear has now gone, I swear to you truthfully that he is incapable of being killed by anyone other than you.

— Drona Vadha Parva

Clearly Karna doesn’t overhype his armor. Rather he happily exchanges it for something he thought could help him win against his enemy.


CONCLUSIONS

Karna’s armor weren’t as overhyped in the epic as it is done in modern time. Krishna, Indra and Surya, everyone thought Karna’s armor were a divine protection that could surely delay his death. That proves armors were powerful.

That said, they couldn’t win war. Therefore Karna himself happily exchanged it for something he thought would be more effective for him.

If something is overhyped in modern times it is Karna and not the armor that protected Karna as long as he didn’t part with it.
