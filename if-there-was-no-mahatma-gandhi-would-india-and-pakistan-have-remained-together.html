If there was no Mahatma Gandhi, would India and Pakistan have remained together?

📅 Jul 29, 2022 08:12 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Sure. Who else could have been responsible for India's partition?

Afterall who proposed two nation theory if not him? Who called for the direct action day in 1946 that killed thousands of Hindus? Wasn't he, who demanded for the division? Hadn't he signed the agreements that were to endorse partition? Wasn't he who instigated every Hindu and Muslims to kill each other?

There were innocent victims like Jinnah and Godse who pleaded for a united India. They begged not to break our nation. There was Savarkar who said partition will happen over by deadbody and Hindus and Muslim shall exist in the country together in harmony. But did Gandhi listen to them?

No, Gandhi, with a sword in one hand, and moved from places to places beheading anyone who opposed India's partition. Even Britishers begged him not to divide the nation, Azmal Kasab (of 26/12) fame begged him. But did that man listen?

If Gandhi had not existed, we would have been one nation, we may have been called Pakistan by now, but we would have been one nation. The peace loving Jinnah and Savarkar would never have proposed the two nation theories. They would have preached one nation theory in which people would have lived in harmony. Britishers would never have proposed partition and Patel would never have accepted that or persuaded Nehru and Congress to do so.
