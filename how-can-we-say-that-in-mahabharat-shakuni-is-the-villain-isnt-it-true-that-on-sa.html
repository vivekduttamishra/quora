How can we say that in Mahabharat Shakuni is the villain? Isnt it true that on Satyavatis order Bhishma went to Gandhar with his full strength or maybe and forced the marriage of Dhritrashtra and Gandhari, this irked Shakuni.?

📅 May 04, 2022 06:52 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

No. Nothing irked Shakuni. He never rebelled against Bhishma or Dhritarashtra. He always remained loyal to them and supported Duryodhana with best intentions for his nephew. His family sided with Kauravas and Bhishma even during Kurukshetra war. He wasn't the mastermind of any conspiracy other than dice game which benefitted Duryodhana because of his actions, though backfired by Karna's stupidity.

Now what made Shakuni a villian was his deceitful nature and descision to be the sidekick (not the mastermind) of evil. Neither Pandu nor Pandavas ever harmed Shakuni, but his every action aided in their harm. Is this not being villain?

And finally “maybe” can't be the foundation of any judgement. You don't punish someone because maybe he committed a crimes
