Why did karna suffered the most in Mahabharat?

📅 Mar 21, 2020 11:01 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is not a new question and has been asked in several forms earlier, as such my answer is not likely to be a lot different from the previous one that you can read here — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Was Karna so generous that he had to bear the curses for the sake of others? During Mahabharata’s time, was it bad to be good and generous? Why was fate so cruel towards Karna, but not others?

  1. Why fate was so cruel to Karna but not others —
    1. A couple was thrown in jail where their six children were brutally killed in front of their eyes — they were not Karna.
    2. A boy was poisoned and thrown in river — He was not Karna.
    3. A royal family was attempted to burn alive — five sons and their mother — none of them were Karna — He was among the arsonist.
    4. The family suffered in forest and fought everyday for their life — Not Karna
    5. A family that made prosperity from a waste land was cheated — Karna sat with cheaters.
    6. A woman suffered humiliation in public — A shabha tried to disrobe her — Karna commanded it — yet you say Karna alone suffered fates’ cruelty.
    7. A royal family was sent to 13 years exile — Karna tried to mock them in forest — here fate is not the only thing cruel.
    8. A grand old warrior was brutally pinned to ground by a mesh of arrows alive — He was not Karna.
    9. A young man was disarmed and killed by a pack of hungry hounds calling themselves warriors — Karna was not the victim here but one of the hounds.
    10. Another old man — a guru — was beheaded by his own desiple as he sat lamenting his son's death — can this be defined a cruel fate?

The only difference is unlike Karna, they never lamented their fate but accepted it. Fate tests everyone and what they do defines whose the hero.

How ill-fated was Karna really?

  1. Brought up by so caring family that it is said that the adoptive mother developed lactation.
  2. He was trained by the best Gurus of the time — Drona, Kripa and Parshuram.
  3. He was coronated a king at a very young age and remained a king till he died. He never got exile, never got enslaved, never saw his wife humiliated.
  4. His friend didnt blame him whenever he abandoned him.
  5. Taking his leads today's politicians are becoming daanveer on public money. Remember someone gifted him an entire kingdom in the first place — that someone was never called Danveer.

However, a short summary is

  1. Karna didn’t suffered worst. In fact he gained more than he suffered.
  2. There are other characters who suffered a lot more at people talk a lot less about them.
