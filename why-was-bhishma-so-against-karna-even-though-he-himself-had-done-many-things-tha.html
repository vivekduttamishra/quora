Why was Bhishma so against Karna, even though he himself had done many things that were far worse than anything that Karna ever did?

📅 Aug 22, 2022 11:35 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question bases itself on a self created fabrication in a lame attempt to vindicate one of the worst villains of Indian epic.

Bhishma never did anything remotely as bad as Karna. He never deserted his friend and family to save his own life. He never was deceitful to his guru. He never tried to burn down a family in fire. He never tried to burn down a bride. He never tried to strip a woman in public. He never fled a battle. Forget Bhishma, even Duryodhana and Duhshashana never stooped to the depth of Karna.

But then assuming Bhishma had his own faults, he still had a great reason to be against Karna. And it was NOT personal. Bhishma was trying to protect his Kingdom from a certain doom and Karna was pushing it to the same catastrophic event. Had Bhishma succeed He still would have his family intact. But because Karna prevailed, his entire clan was decimated. Even people with faults won’t like their family destroyed. They would do their best to get rid of those works who are eroding the root of their happiness. That is what Bhishma did. Of all the faults Bhishma, had being coward or war monger was not among them. Little wonder Duryodhana preferred Bhishma over Karna as a warrior and Karna over Bhishma as a conspirator.
