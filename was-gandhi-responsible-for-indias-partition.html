Was Gandhi responsible for India's partition?

📅 Sep 30, 2020 08:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer — He opposed it.

But the answer to such questions can’t be a simple binary yes or no. We need to go in details.

Gandhiji initially and for a very long time continued to oppose partition to the extent that —

  • He said Partition can happen only on his dead body.
  • He even suggested that let Jinnah be the prime minister if that can avoid partition.

But there were oppositions —

  • Britishers wanted to show they cared for the muslim subjects and princly states. They conviniently ignored the fact that even congress represented muslims. If they had their way, India would be devided into hundreds of fragments rather than just two.
  • Even congress leaders like Patel and Nehru were eager to get independence even at the cost of partition. In fact Patel actively supported the idea of partition.
  • Jinnah was a dying man — a well guarded secret. He had less than a year to live. He wanted to ensure paritition happens before he is gone.
  • Gandhiji was practically sidelined. This can be understood by some of the statements from that period —
  • Mount Batten in an interview disclosed —

Only one voice foresaw the dim ensions of th e tr aged y which was about to overwhelm the sub-continent. That was Gandhi’s, and no one in mid-summer 1947 was listening to the prophet of non-violence.

— freedom at midnight by Larry Collins

  • Mahatma Gandhi himself observed —

‘Everybody is eager to garland my photos and statues. But nobody wants to follow my advice.’

—Mahatma Gandhi

  • So as you can see, Gandhiji was sidelined.

Why is Gandhiji blamed for partition of India?

Mahatma Gandhi never endorsed partition. Infact he opposed it tooth and nail. He was not part of the most of the paritition disucssion. In fact he was sidelined by his own people. Yet everyone knew what he was capable of. He wasn’t just a party member.

Gandhiji had greater resources at his command than the leaders of the Party. He had the Party itself. He had the millions of four-anna members who worshipped him and he had, above all, his uncommon skill at galvanizing those masses into action. If he chose to go over the heads of the politicians and appeal directly to India’s masses, he could force a terrible trial of strength between the Viceroy, Nehru and Patel on the one hand, and his own towering spiritual presence on the other.

And yet, when the paritition was announced, he was silent. He didn’t denounce it. Gandhiji isn’t guilty of being a supported of parition, he is deemed guilty of not opposing it.

Why didn’t Gandhiji opposed parition, when he had the will and strength to do it?

As I see, it was because even Gandhiji knew, what he was capable of. Had he denounced paritition, he realized it would have been distraous. Gandhji at the age of 73 was completely forshaken by the consequence of Direct Action Day of 1946. He understood his people better than anyone else. He knew trying to prevent parition would lead into an unprecedented bloodshed, he wasn’t willing to push the country into.

Consider what happened during parition. It happened on two regions — Punjab border (Pakistan) and bengal border (East Pakistan). The total death count ranges between 2.5 Lac to 5 lac or even more. Now imagine, what would have happened, had a riot had broken up in every town, and every village of the country. Parition may have been averted, but probably half the population had perished. And Gandhiji had no courage to see a massacre of this scale.

By the way majority of deaths during the parition happened on punjab border. And bengal that was supposed to be more sensetive was miracally protected from such a roit for at least first 12 days of parition. Interestingly most of the army was deployed on punjab border. And internal media termed it — Miracle of Calcutta. And what was that miracle —

What one unarmed non-violent man was accomplishing in the world’s meanest city, 55,000 heavily arm ed professional soldiers were unable to accomplish in the Punjab.

Gandhiji’s worry of riot came through anyway. But his wisdom prevented a larger scale miracle, albeit couldn’t prevent partition. And those without analysing complete situation would eternally blame Mahatma for India’s parition.

All these details can be verified by official archives.
