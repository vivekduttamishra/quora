Was Gandhiji against modern technology?

📅 May 30, 2022 02:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question is fundamentally flawed. Gandhiji was never against industries nor against modern technology. In fact he used the latest technologies like media to it’s fullest extent in reaching the people. If Mahatma Gandhi was against something it was corporate greed, where machine becomes a tool to push human out of their living and causing widespread inequality in the society. He aspired for a self-reliant India. He advocated individual empowerment and cottage industry. He advocated entrepreneurship where each individual has capability and means for a dignified existence. Alas, many couldn’t understand his lofty ideals because he was way ahead of his time.

Don’t believe? Look at WhatsApp, and you will find an excellent example of cottage industry, where a small group of people can create something more powerful than what biggest corporate could do. That was what Mahatma Gandhi aspired. Today, like never before, India is grasping his concept. Look at the startups around you, entrepreneurs around you or the Zomato delivery guy around you and you would find what human empowerment Mahatma Gandhi was seeking — not destroying industries, but making individual industrialist. No wonder, Steve Job, one of the most reputed technocrat found in Mahatma Gandhi a mascot for ‘think different’ campaign.
