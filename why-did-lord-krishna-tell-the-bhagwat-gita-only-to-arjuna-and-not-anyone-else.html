Why did Lord Krishna tell the Bhagwat Gita only to Arjuna, and not anyone else?

📅 Apr 14, 2020 05:11 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

8Thanks for A2A.

Bhagwat Gita isn't just a discourse, but a question and answer and interestingly doesn't start with Krishna but with Arjuna's question. So you may say Arjuna chose the Gita way.

The almighty Lord Krishna already had answers, but it requires a great question and a great listener to for such knowledge transfer. In Arjuna, Krishna found both.

But probably the greatest reason being, among group of assembled fighters, Arjuna alone was the warrior. He alone questioned the need of war and alone refused to fight. And this conscience made him an ideal candidate to for Gita's knowledge. Same is clear when Krishna says

Among warriors, I am Ram.

Why Ram? Because Ram, too used weapons as the last resort. And Krishna on similar lines says

I am Arjuna among Pandavas.
