Did Kunti have real love for Karna or was it just a fake show off to save her all 5 sons?

📅 Oct 31, 2021 02:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

What a wonderful question! There are many ways to answer this question, but probably the most important is to ask a few counter questions —

Why would a mother not have real love for his child?

What could be the reason that a mother wouldn’t have a real love for her child, specially one who she had to very reluctantly abandon for everyone’s welfare, including the child’s? If a mother could be so emotionless for her first born why would she care her other children? Moreover, why would she care for the twins who were not even her daughter and yet, Kunti was so attached to them that even the questioner forgot they were not her own sons? The care she bestowed on the twins clearly demonstrate the kind of mother she was. And the question fails here itself.

Why did a mother abandon her first child?

Kunti misused the mantra given by Durvasa. She was beyond doubt immature and imprudent. But this is no testimony of what kind of mother she was.

She implored Surya to understand the dilemma of a bachelor girl and release her of the obligation of mantra. But Surya out his ego not only didn’t budge but also threatened Kunti of dire consequences that would befall not only her but her father and kingdom as well. Kunti submits to Surya, reluctant and distressed. This may not show her as a great mother, but certainly a great daughter and a woman of compassionate character. Can we at least agree here, what she did was to protect her family.

She knew she couldn’t keep her son without bringing infamy not only to herself and her father but also her son. What would be his status? Surely no one would have married an unwedded mother. There is not a single reference of such an incident in the epic. Even Satyavati didn’t reveal her first child Ved Vyas to her husband.

Thus she exacts a gift from Surya, something no one ever got before or after — a divine protection. His divine invincible armor to ensure his safety in her life. She laments profusely for abandoning her first child but didn’t throw her to die as people like to believe. She willed her to live. He was careful sailed in a secure box with immense wealth and she ensured he reaches a safe place. Her spies immediately informed Kunti of Karna’s arrival and adoption by Adhiratha. That was the best life a mother could have given her. If she wanted him dead, she could have done, what Ganga did to her newborns.

It is also to be noted, that Kunti didn’t seek any such divine protection for any of her other sons. So what does this reveal about Kunti’s love for her eldest son?

Finally ask yourself—

What did Kunti say to Karna when she finally meets Karna?

Did she ask Karna to spare the life of her other sons? NO. She introduced herself in the hope that Karna would understand her problem and once he sees the truth he would come to the sides of Dharma. She explicitly asked to abandon the evils.

This was his main thought —

He was protected like my son. Why should he not listen to my words, which will be beneficial for the cause of his brothers?”

— Karna Upnivada Parva

And this is what she said —

Today, let the Kurus witness the fraternal union between Karna and Arjuna. Let those who are evil bow down. Let Karna and Arjuna be like Rama53 and Janardana. If the two of you are united in your minds, what can you not achieve in this world?

— Karna

Are these words for the welfare of Arjuna alone? Or does she talk about Dharma?

Think, why would Kunti, who never schemed against the sons of Gandhari, would scheme against her own son?

Were Pandavas in any real danger from Karna?

Krishna didn’t think so. Parshurama didn’t think so. Karna didn’t think so. Facts doesn’t point in that direction.

In every single direct or indirect encounter between Karna and Pandavas, Karna was badly vanquished and was forced to retreat. Even Kripacharya wonders on this questions before Matsya battle — In which battle have you ever defeated any Pandava? Karna had no answer. Does any of you have an answer to this quesiton?

During Bhagwat Yaan Parva not only Krishna but even Parshurama and other categorically mentioned that no one can vanquish Pandavas.

After Bhagwat Yaan Parva, when Krishna and Karna discusses, both agree that Pandavas would be victorious. Even Karna knew this —

Krishna! There is no doubt that there will be a great and terrible war between the Pandavas and the Kurus, with the mud of blood. The kings and the princes who follow Duryodhana’s instructions will be burnt in the battle with the flames of weapons and will go to Yama’s abode.

— Karna Upanivada Parva

So the question is, why would Kunti scheme and seek protection for Pandavas from Karna when she knew they were in protection of none other than Krishna? Karna reiterated his belief even in front of Kunti —

Who does not suffer when Dhananjaya is aided by Krishna? If I now go over to the Parthas, who will not think that I am frightened?

— Karna Upnivada Parva.

Kunti never sought explicit welfare of only five of her sons. She didn’t ask him to spare his brother’s life. Then whence comes the question, that she didn’t love him? It was not Kunti’s demand, but Karna’s offering that he wouldn’t kill her four sons.


I would conclude this answer by answering few common accusations labeled against Kunti. While there is no direct reference in the epic, truth is not difficult to find out —

Why did Kunti abandon Karna?

Of course she was unwed mother, young and wanted to protect herself and her family form disgrace. But she also wanted to protect Karna from the same disgrace. Even if Kunti hadn’t abandoned her, he would still have been called an abandoned child for his father had certainly abandoned her. His mother would have no prospect of honorable wedding. What would have been Karna’s life? Better or worse?

There are occasions where in order to save ones life one has to surgically amputee one of his limbs — hand or leg. Not that you love your hand less or would be happy losing it, but because —

  • That’s the only option for the rest of the body to survive.
  • Even if you decide not to sever your limb, you can’t protect it. It will die causing the death of the rest of the body.

Unfortunately Karna had become that limb, that Kunti had to abandon. But it cause more pain to Kunti than to Karna, who knew nothing about her real mothers. Thousands of child lose their mother due to some reason but few would be as unfortunate enough not to understand her lifelong pain and abandon her.

Why didn’t Kunti approach Karna earlier?

She could have. But then, by the time she encountered her next time, he had already chosen his camp with incorrigible difference between him and her other sons. On the top of it, he was made a King, while her other sons lived in a constant danger of life. There was no reason, why by sharing her secret she should snatch away his peaceful and prosperous life.

The she must have seen with horror how his son stooped so low that he could ask to disrobe a woman in public. She may have been ashamed of his acts and couldn’t have revealed his identity to his other sons.

Why did, then Kunti approach Karna later?

Finally, when the war came. It was Now or never. She like everyone else knew the significance of Krishna’s protection. But one of her son was without that protection. Guess what a mother would do?

Why did Kunti ask Karna to spare her son’s life?

She didn’t initially. It was Karna’s unilateral offer. But which mother wouldn’t jump at the slightest prospect of ensuring her son’s safety? She certainly asked Karna to uphold his promise.
