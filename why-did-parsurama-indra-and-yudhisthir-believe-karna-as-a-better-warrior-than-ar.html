Why did Parsurama, Indra, and Yudhisthir believe Karna as a better warrior than Arjuna?

📅 Jun 12, 2021 12:05 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi,

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: The question is incorrect!

Indra

The reason behind this assumption is the fact that Indra himself came to relieve Karna of his divine armours and ear-rings, and why else Indra would do that unless He feared Karna?

But is there a real foundation behind this assumption? Is there an evidence? Let us explore.

We can understand the true nature of Indra’s action by asking ourselves a simple question — Was Indra worried about Karna as a warrior or was he worried about the divine armour and ear-rings? Why didn’t he use his deceit to kill him? Couldn’t he have simply have asked for his life!

But he wasn’t interested in Karna the warrior! He was interested in divine armours and was trying to level the playing field. Did any other warrior had such a divine armour?

So even if Indra was worried — the worry was Karna would be difficult to kill with divine armour. He wasn’t worried that Karna would kill Arjuna, else he would have lashed Arjuna with divine armour. He wasn’t worried that Arjuna wouldn’t be able to kill Karna, else he would have given the divine spear to Arjuna. He wasn’t worried about Karna the warrior. He was worried about the armours. period.

Surya

Not only Indra, even Surya and Karna knew that Karna without divine armour is good as a dead man. Here is what Surya said —

O Karna! If you give away the beautiful earrings that you were naturally born with, your life will be shortened and you will come under the power of death. O one who is reverent! Arrayed in your armour and earrings, you cannot be slain by your enemies in battle. Remember, these words. These two treasures arose from amrita. O Karna! If you desire to live, they must therefore be carefully protected by you.”

— Kundala Hara Parva

Now the simple question is, did Surya really considered Karna a great warrior?

Karna

Here is what Karna himself said —

O god! If I give you my earrings and armour, I will be liable to be killed. O Shakra! You will become an object of ridicule. O Shakra! Therefore, take my earrings and supreme armour, if you so wish. But take them in exchange. Otherwise, I will not give them.”

—Kundala Hara Parva

He further explains why he needed the supreme spear in return

Karna replied, “I wish to kill only one enemy37 in a great battle. He roars and blazes and is the source of fear for me.”
—Kundala Hara Parva

What do you think is Karna’s own opinion about his comparative strength against Arjuna?

Beyond the fact, that Indra asked Karna’s divine armour, there is not a single mention that Indra considered Karna to be a better warrior than Arjuna and Indra certainly had not such evidence. On his command Gandharvas has routed Karna.

Indra doesn’t seek Karna’s help in killing Nivatakavacha, rather he approaches Arjuna. Why didn’t he approach Karna? There could be only two reasons — First he didn’t find Karna capable, or the second that he knew Karna wasn’t compassionate enough to do something beyond his selfish interest comparising him and his friend.

Parshurama

Parshurama never said Karna was better Arjuna. In fact he clearly mentioned Arjuna superior than everyone in Kaurava’s camp and that included Karna. Here is what he says is Bhagwat yaan Parva —

O king! Therefore, discard your vanity and go to Dhananjaya82 before a weapon has been affixed to Gandiva, the best of bows. He possesses Kakudika, Shuka, Naka, Akshisamtarjana, Santana, Nartana, Ghora and Ajyamodaka as the eighth.83 All men who are pierced by these confront their death, or move around insane, or lose their senses and become unconscious, or go to sleep, or jump around, or vomit, or urinate, or incessantly cry and laugh. Partha’s qualities are innumerable and Janardana is superior to him. You have known him as Kunti’s son, Dhananjaya. O great king! But know that Nara and Narayana are Arjuna and Keshava, brave warriors and bulls among men. If you know this, do not harbour any suspicions about me. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Be an arya in your resolution and make peace with the Pandavas.

—Bhagwat Yaan Parva

Yudhishthira

Among the three, Yudhishthira is the only one to have an exaggerated view about Karna. He always found Karna to be a great archer. He carried such an opinion even during talent showcase event. Here is what Yudhishthira felt —

At that time, Yudhishthira also thought that there was no archer equal to Karna on earth.’

— Jatugrih Parva

Not only this he continued to carry such high opinion till the very end. But we must understand that Yudhishthira wasn’t a warrior at par with Parshurama, Drona, Bhishma, or Indra to understand the true capabilities of the warriors. He just believed the shows presented by Karna that had fooled even Duryodhana into overestimating him.

But that doesn’t mean, he valued Karna higher than Arjuna. He always knew Arjuna would kill Karna eventually. Interestingly many critcs value words more than actions. Here are few instances —

  • During Draupadi Swaymvara, Yudhishira leaves Arjuna and Bhima alone and returns to their house. He knew about Karna’s presence but that didn’t worry him in the least.
  • During Ghosha yatra Kaurava army was routed by Gandharvas. Karna fled for his life. And yet not only Yudhishthira sends Arjuna to confront Gandharvas but also suggests mild use of force! Had he considered Karna a greater warrior, he shouldn’t have sent a lesser warrior to fight would he?
