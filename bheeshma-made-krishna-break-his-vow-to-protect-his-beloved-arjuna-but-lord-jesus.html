This is a leading question hoping to achieve a single goal. The questioner has decided whom would he should worship. Please go ahead with your choice. What I don’t understand is what is the need of malicious distortions?

Let us divide the question in two parts —

DID BHISHMA MAKE KRISHNA BREAK HIS VOW TO PROTECT HIS BELOVED ARJUNA?

This question shows the total ignorance of the questioner about Mahabharata, even at the superficial level. Arjuna didn’t need protection. Arjuna was constantly winning against Bhishma and Kaurava for all these days. He was mere reluctant to kill his beloved old grandsire. He was confused between Dharma and compassion.

And Krishna didn’t break his vow. He didn’t attack Bhishma. He rose to attack. And there is as big a difference as exists between ‘may be’ and ‘done’. The difference is of infinity. Krishna knew he won’t have to break his promise and that both Bhishma and Arjuna would get the subtle message. But I won’t be surprised even if Krishna had attacked.

Krishna seemingly broke his vow to show both Bhishma and Arjuna that personal vows or egos are not greater than Dharma. Bhishma protected Adharma in the name of his vow. Arjuna adored Bhishma and in a way shielded him, who was in the way of Adharma.

Krishna by taking up arms against Bhishma, proclaimed nothing is more worthy than Dharma, your personal egos are certainly not greater than Dharma

He who could have finished the war in a moment, chose to sit out to teach humanity self reliance. God can be your guide and not your gunman!

WHO SHOULD YOU WORSHIP?

Worship a God who disciples don’t feel the need to mock others faith. Worship a God whose so called disciples isn’t looking forward to grow their followers by sending salesperson in the market to spread fake news and bribe people into accepting him.

Worship someone who doesn’t fight your battle for you and just guides you.

But not everyone can follow Krishna. There are four kinds who can’t follow Krishna —

न मां दुष्कृतिनो मूढा: प्रपद्यन्ते नराधमा: |
माययापहृतज्ञाना आसुरं भावमाश्रिता: || 15||

— Bhagwat Gita 7.15

Four kinds of people do not surrender unto Me—

  1. those ignorant of knowledge,
  2. those who lazily follow their lower nature though capable of knowing Me,
  3. those with deluded intellect,
  4. and those with a demoniac nature.

So don’t worry, if you fall in one (or all) of the above category. You may still worship anyone else, you would still be worshipping Krishna indirectly.

WORSHIP ANYONE, YOU STILL WORSHIP KRISHNA

Worship someone who doesn’t force you to chose and can proclaim —

यो यो यां यां तनुं भक्त: श्रद्धयार्चितुमिच्छति |
तस्य तस्याचलां श्रद्धां तामेव विदधाम्यहम् || 21||

स तया श्रद्धया युक्तस्तस्याराधनमीहते |
लभते च तत: कामान्मयैव विहितान्हि तान् || 22||

—Gita Chapter 7 Shloka 21,22

Whatever celestial form a devotee seeks to worship with faith, I (Krishna) steady the faith of such a devotee in that form.

Endowed with faith, the devotee worships a particular celestial god and obtains the objects of desire. But in reality, I alone arrange these benefits.

He has no qualms that you should worship him alone. He doesn’t envy you. He doesn’t make you chose and doesn’t make you belittle others.
