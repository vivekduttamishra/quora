Why didn't Lord Krishna punish Draupadi for humiliating Duryodhan in Maya Sabha and Karna in her Swayamvar? Had Lord Krishna not started establishing righteousness by then?

📅 Apr 20, 2020 02:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

There are many ways to see this question

1.Krishna lived almost 5000 years from now and Draupadi of his era never insulted —

  • Duryodhana in mayabhawan. It is a much later tale.
  • Karna in Swaymber, as clearly stated in Neelkantha, he failed to string the bow. But assuming it to be true, Krishna must have realized it was her prerogative to decline any suiter and it was not to be considered a insult. You should also note that Kshatriya warrior protested against even a Brahmin winning Swaymber as they considered it Kshatriya exclusive. This once again disqualifies Karna.

2. Krishna has taken the task of establishing Dharma, even before his birth. And since, not only he not punished Draupadi but supported her every time she needed; is it not a sufficient enough proof that she was right?
