How does karma revert to Guru Dhrona who made Ekalavya cut his thumb?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 05:55 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

This is an example of a leading question which starts with a preassumption that Drona sinned by asking Ekalavya to cut his thumb and Karma must have punished him.

So what comes next?

  • How did Karma revert to Parshuram for cursing Karna? How are the two different from each other? Didn’t both punished a student?
  • How did Karma revert back to earth for cursing Someone? By a long draught or flood?
  • How would have Drona died had he not asked Eklavya with his thumb? Won’t Dhristdumnya still killed him?
  • How do we know if at all Karma punished Drona, it was for Eklavya and not for Abhimanyu?

Now here is my view on How did Karma revert to Drona —

  • He remained a reputed and acknowledged guru of Aryavart, all his life.
  • He succeeded in his life mission of vindicating against arrogant Drupad. Karma didn’t let him fail.
  • His death made him larger than life —
    • He died unconquered.
    • History tells no one could kill him while he was still fighting
    • Even great Yudhisthir had to lie to him to see his fall. Who else can claim this?

So if Karma reverted this way to Drona, what does this tell about his Karma?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkant Edition of Mahabharat (Geet Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
