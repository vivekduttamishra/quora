I don't know why I am inspired by Karna the most after Lord Krishna. Arjuna doesn't inspire me at all. Why?

📅 Sep 10, 2020 01:43 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi, It maybe a personal choice to be inspired with whomsoever you like, but since you asked, answer is in your question — You don't know.

On quora you may here two extreme views and each group will call the other biased, so at the end of the day, you may still not know. So the only way to be absolutely sure, is to read authentic version of Mahabharata.

But to tell you that if you are inspired by Karna most after Krishna, you aren't alone. In fact, for many Karna is a bigger inspiration than Krishna. But since you are more inspired by Krishna, maybe I can help you see the truth — Whom did Krishna called his friend? Do you think that person may have something inspiring?

Coming to the reason, you may have formed opinion is because of several fake narratives intentionally propagated, few are present in answer to this question as well.

  1. Karna was rejected by Drona… FAKE… Drona taught Karna is mentioned in Adi Parva, Vana Parva and Shanti Parva of Mahabharata. Drona only refused him the knowledge of Brahmastra, that too, because he wanted to defeat a fellow classmate Arjuna.
  2. Parshuram cursed Karna despite of being Dutiful — Parshuram cursed him for his deceit. He acknowledged it in Udyog Parva.
  3. Draupadi insulted him and forbid him in the Swayamvara — Never happened. Contradiction is well documented.
  4. Bhishma forbid him to participate in war under his command — Actually he declined to fight under Bhisma's command.
  5. Karna was unfortunate — He remained a king since the beginning of his career in play arena till the day of his death.
  6. Kunti abandoned infant Karna — unfortunate and true. But this can't have any psychological impact on his life as he was an infant. Karna never forgave her for this. But he never asked for forgiveness for letting her be committed to Lakshagrih fire either, neither ever thanked her for getting Kavach Kundal for him from Surya. Other prominent people abandoned by their mother include Krishna and Ved Vyas.
  7. You must be ignoring Karna's role in dyut shabha
  8. You may not know Gandharva war or on whose suggestion Duryodhana had gone their — suggestion was to mock Pandava in exile.

Why Arjuna don't inspire you?

  1. Do you know Arjuna never cheated his guru, rather saved his life from crocodile? Shouldn't this inspire you more than someone who cheated his guru?
  2. Both were taught by Drona, who considered Arjuna his pride. Don't know why Arjuna should inspire you less.
  3. Bhagwan Parshuram cursed Karna, and declared Arjun a great warrior. Who should inspire you more?
  4. Arjuna was liked by Bhishma, Drona and even enemy like Drupad who was vanquished by Arjuna. Must be something inspiring about him. Right?
  5. Arjuna was a life long learner and was blessed by Mahadeva. Surely, something Mahadeva found worthy in him that you don't know?
  6. Ved Vyas called Arjuna, trunk of the tree of righteousness with Krishna and Brahma as its root. He contrasted Karna as trunk of a tree of lust and passion, with Dhritrastra as its root. Did you know that?
  7. Arjuna saved his worst enemy from Gandharvas, Karna fled leaving his best friend behind. I don't know why Karna inspires you more?
  8. In Gita Krishna compares himself with everything greatest — Vishnu among Aditaya's, Kartikeya among army commander, Ram among warrior… He calls himself Arjuna among Pandavas. Did he ever compared himself to Karna? Can you guess why?

if you don't know, it's time you should read and know. I may get branded as a Arjuna fan but I base my answer on endorsement of —

  • Mahadeva
  • Krishna
  • Parshuram
  • Ved Vyas
  • Bhishma
  • Drona
  • Drupad
  • And KARNA.

Yes you read it right. You may not be inspired by Arjuna, Karna most certainly was. His ultimate ambition in life was to prove himself better than Arjuna —

  1. He asked Drona for Brahmastra so that he could defeat Arjuna
  2. He cheated Parshuram for same Brahmastra. Why?
  3. He entered the arena meant for Drona's disciple only. Why, to prove himself better than Arjuna.
  4. He boasted before Virata war, how better was he from Arjuna .
  5. He promised Kunti, he would spare four of his brothers, all except (make a guess)
