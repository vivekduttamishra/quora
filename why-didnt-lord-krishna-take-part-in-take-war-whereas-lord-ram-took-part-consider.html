Why didn't Lord Krishna take part in take war whereas Lord Ram took part? Considering Draupadi and Lord Krishna were very close and Lord Krishna saved Draupadi from humiliation.

📅 Apr 20, 2020 01:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

That's an excellent question and can be answered at so many levels —

First the Fact: Krishna did participate in the war, although he didn't fight with arms, he did fight with his strategy. Consider 1971 Indo Pak war. The credit if victory goes to Field Marshall Sam Manikshaw. But did he fight with guns?

So let's modify the question

Why didn't Krishna participate in the war as warrior like Arjuna, Bhisma and others?

The answer lies in another popular story (though not present in Vyas Mahabharat) — Why did he got Barbarik killed?

Because Barbarik was too powerful and skewed in his sense of justice and Dharma. Now while being personification of Dharma, he was still too powerful to have a match and that would be Injustice. So while he removed Barbarik, the raw power devoid of any sense of Dharma, he removed his power and participated only for Dharma.

Why did Ram participate in the war.

This also answers, why Krishna didn't. Ram participated as —

  1. Ravan was not human and could be vanquished by anyone but him. Kurukshetra remained a human battle.
  2. Rama fought. It was his battle. His wife abducted. Krishna let Pandavas vindicate themselves.

Above all it was a lesson that under guidance of the God, humans can fight and win their own battle.

Note: My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG), Bibek Debroy and Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
