This question, I believe, comes from the speech of a so called leading mythology expert who cited how Bhishma did injustice of Dhritarashtra and his sins returned inform of such a pathetic end. It may be his personal opinion rather than how Ved Vyas narrated. I believe —

The basic premise of the question is incorrect — Bhisma met a pathetic end in Mahabharata

Bhisma met the most honorable and glorified end any warrior met in battle of Kurukshetra, or could hope to meet.

  • He remained unconquerable throughout the battle and forced even Krishna to break is vow of not taking up the arms.
  • When he fell down, even his opponents lamented his fall and paid their reverence.
  • He chose his own death. How many gets so lucky?
  • He fulfilled his life’s mission to see Hastinapur in safe hands.
  • He gave a parting lesson to new king of Hastinapur — Yudhishthir and chose his date of passing away. Only Lord Rama can truly claim he ended his existence on earth, this way.

Bhisma’s life and death — The sanatana hindu philosophy

Bhisma’s life and death can be seen as an excellent examplification of Krishna’s Karma theory and cycle of Birth and Death —

1. Bhisma’s past sin caused him to live a pathetic human existence

As narrated in Mahabharat, He was one of the eight Vasus — Prabhash. He sinned, by stealing the cow, and his sin earned him a life — A pathetic human existence. Life here is not a boon, but a punishment.

2. His Karma as Bhishma, liberated him from pathetic human existence

Bhishma death wasn’t a punishment, but a rewad to liberate him from the basic human existence. He returns to Devaloka to reclaim his original identity of a Vasu.

Ancient Hindu Philosphy aspires for Moksha, and entry to this Moksha is death. Demons feared it out of lust, Rishi’s welcomed it as Samadhi.

So the main question should be — What were the virtues of Bhisma that freed him from a pathetic human existence and earned him respect, fame and glory in life and in death?
