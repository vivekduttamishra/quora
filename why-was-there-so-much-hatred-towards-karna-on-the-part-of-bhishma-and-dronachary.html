Why was there so much hatred towards Karna on the part of Bhishma and Dronacharya?

📅 Jan 11, 2021 01:40 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Imagine you are the head of a family and you are desperately trying to keep them together. Imagine one part of the family headed is jealous and wants to kill the other part the P’s They tried multiple times. And yet you hope one day all would be well. But then there is young man, who always supports conspiracies that are breaking the family apart. It was his advices and his powers that is prompting the envious group to go against their relatives and they are listening to this outsider more than the old man of the family? What would he do?

That was Bhishma. He loved his family. Wanted them to be together. And yet all his attempts from thwart by Duryodhana who listen to his advisors like Karna and Shakuni who conspired on various attempts to eliminate Pandavas. Bhishma could see, this would take his family to destruction as it finally did. It was Karna who prompted for mocking Pandava in exile and that led to almost the destruction of the Kauravas. Then he supported the idea to attack Matasya and on his advise Duryodhana rejected Krishna peace proposal. Do you think, he was wrong to resenting Karna.

Now consider the guru Drona who spent his life with a group of young princes and trained them into warriors. Now he saw the prospect of his own students fighting each other. He also saw the family of his benfector (Bhishma) getting torned into pieces. And he finds one of his ex-student acting as a catalyst in this entire rift. He knew the kid to be conniving from back in the gurukul days. Now he sees that his students are listening to him more than his advice. And he could see that as long as Karna is with Duryodhana there is no escape from the war. A war where he and his family, too would perish because he can’t leave his benefector. Do you think that guru his unjustified in feeling betrayed and in resenting Karna?
