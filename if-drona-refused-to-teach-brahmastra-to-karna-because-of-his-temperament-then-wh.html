If Drona refused to teach Brahmastra to Karna because of his temperament, then why did Parshuram teach him the secret of celestial weapons, couldn't he read Karna’s nature?

📅 Mar 08, 2021 09:31 PM PST  |  🌐 English

The question is only partially correct.

Both Drona and Parashurama accepted Karna as his disciple, proving those great gurus weren’t biased against anyone and offered sufficient opportunity to Karna.

In front of Drona, Karna had revealed his true nature from the begining. Drona was privy to Karna’s envy towards Pandavas and Karna had openly expressed the true reason he needed a celestial weapon — to fight Arjuna.

Having declined by Drona, Karna had learnt his lesson. He had learnt to mask his genuine emotions behind his deceit. He approached Parshuram disguised as a Brahman. And Parshurama didn’t suspect him.

How was Parshurama, to know that someone would approach a guru with such impure deceitful intention? Even demons of the epics never showed such disrepect to their guru.

But when Parshurama learnt his secret, he smiled. He knew at that very moment, his knowledge of Brahmastra won’t be abused —

However, he smiled and said, ‘You acted in this false way because of your greed for weapons. O stupid one! In a different place, when the time for your death has come, you will be engaged in a fight with someone who is your equal and the brahmastra will not manifest itself before you. The qualities of a brahmana will never remain with someone who is not a brahmana.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2403-2406). (Shanti Parva)

This statement of Parshurama, that was mere a statement of fact about the nature of Brahamstra was declared as curse by Karna.

The fact is, it wasn’t Parshurama, but Karna who himself condemned him by his deceit.

On learning the dark, ugly secret of Karna, Parshurama certainly expelled him from his grace, that was more than what Drona ever did.
