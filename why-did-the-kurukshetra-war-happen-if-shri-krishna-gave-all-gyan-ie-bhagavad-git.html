Why did the Kurukshetra War happen if Shri Krishna gave all gyan, i.e., Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna?

The question is self contradictory.

Kurukshetra war happened because Krishna succeeded in convincing Arjuna that a person’s duty is more important than his emotions or relations.

Kurukshetra war happened because Krishna announced that in every era, to destroy the evil and reestablish the righteousness, God incarnates. Because God doesn’t fear war, they don’t desist war, they desist evil, that must be destroyed to protect what his virtuous.

Kurukshetra war happened because Krishna reminded Arjuna that he is Rama among warriors and he is Kartikeya in army generals. He reminded that being a great warrior and army general is a Godly virtue.

Kurukshetra war happened because Krishna reminded he is punishement among the desciplinary tools, that punishing the wrong doer is necessary.

And finally Kurukshetra war happened because Krishna said, there is no escape. You will act. Even inaction is an action. So act. Act, without expectation of fruit. Act in devotion of God.

Yes, In Gita Krishna explained, doing your duty is paramount. Escapism isn’t a solution.
