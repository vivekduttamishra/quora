Why did Shakuni support kauravas when he knew that Pandavas were strong and wise than Kauravas?

📅 Apr 23, 2020 07:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

A simple and straight forward but incomplete answer would be that he was the brother of Gandhari and thus supported his nephews.

A glorified and highly popular and equally incorrect answer would be — He was taking his revenge for some fictions reason and wanted the doom of entire Hastinapur, and was the mastermind of all the conspiracy leading to ultimate war.

But the truth is Shakuni wasn't really that important — He wasn't the queen of chessboard, a little more than a pawn! He didn't have much role before or after the gamble. And pawns don't choose sides.

He is glorified to shield real a greater evils.

**Note**: My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG), Bibek Debroy and Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
