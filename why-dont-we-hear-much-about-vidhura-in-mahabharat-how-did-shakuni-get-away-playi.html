Why don't we hear much about Vidhura in Mahabharat? How did Shakuni get away playing all psychological tricks on Dhritarashtr and his sons despite his presence and also in powerful position in the court?

Thanks for the A2A.

Shakuni got away playing all those tricks because modern narratives specifically TV show writers allowed him to. Otherwise as per original Ved Vyas narrative, Shakuni was a minor minion in most of the plots — something like famous Sambha with Gabbar Singh — He is as famous as Gabbar Singh. But what had he really done. Now someone will tell you someday that actually he was the brain behind Gabbar Singh’s operation!

Shakuni’s only devilish role is the dice game, which was his brain child. Even that was an attempt to avoid the alternative that was a battle. He had almost no role in Draupadi’s disrobing which was Karna, Duryodhana and Duhshasana and most significantly Dhritrashtra.

After the dicegame, Shakuni’s goes back to insignificant position he had.

Infact he tried to perssuade Duryodhana to agree to Krishna and avoid the war. Here he acted in the same way as Vidur or Bhishma.

Vidur was at a powerful position and still he could do nothing, it has nothing to do with Shakuni. It was not a democratic system and King’s word was the last word and law or at least that is what they thought.
