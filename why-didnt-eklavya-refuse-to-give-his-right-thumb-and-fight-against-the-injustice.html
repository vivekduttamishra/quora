Why didn't Eklavya refuse to give his right thumb and fight against the injustice society?

📅 Nov 07, 2021 01:27 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Let me answer the question in three parts —

Was there any injustice done by the society?

I wonder, what was the injustice of the society? Is it not an injustice to society that you blame it for an individuals action? Would it be justified if we blame entire Nishada clan for the wrong doings of Eklavaya?

Even if you were to blame Drona, without understanding the whole context, it would still be at worse, Drona’s fault and not societies' injustice and we still have to ascertain if Drona was actually wrong here.

How did society did injustice to him? Didn’t he become the King? Was he not presented as an invited guest in Rajsuy Yagana?

Why did Drona asked for his right thumb?

Would you call it an injustice, if NDA (National Defense Academy) refuses you an admission? What would you do to a person, rejected by Indian army, impersonates himself as an army officer passed out of NDA? Would you honor him or punish him? What would the law do? Remember the credibility of the defense institute depends on this one decision.

Being a great archer could be an ambition. It can’t be a necessity. That is why Right to Education (RTE), doesn’t include Right to military Education. Not everyone is allowed to own a gun and there is a small difference between a military officer and a militant — the difference of purpose and ethics.

NDA never discloses the true reason for rejecting a cadet and often happens to be psychological — are they ethical, do they have courage, focus, ability to follow orders, descipline, etc etc.

Drona rejected Ekalavya for his reasons. But Eklavaya not only ignored his advise continued to call himself his disciple. Drona had to take an action. Clearly a polite rejection had failed.

So why did Drona reject him. The answer lies in another question — How did Ekalavya die?

Why didn't Eklavya fight against injustice?

That is another reason Drona asked his thumb. Ekalavya wasn’t desitned to fight against injustice. He was meant to be the hand of injustice. He was supposed to be part of evil empire of Jarasandha. How an arm of injustice, fight against injustice?

Why didn't’ Ekalayva refused to give his thumb?

I daresay, that wasn’t the choice he had. He had openly admitted that Drona was his preceptor and as per the provision he was supposed to pay the desired fee. Had he refused, his knowledge would have been tainted by a similar deceit as that of Karna. Who knows he too may have forgotten his knowledge when he needed that most?
