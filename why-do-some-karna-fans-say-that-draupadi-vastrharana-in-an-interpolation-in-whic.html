Short Answer: Because they are NOT Karna fans.

It is said that a monkey shatters a mirror on seeing his own face, because it is so disagreeable to him.

The so called Karna fans are not the fans of real Karna, but of that fake Karna image they created for themselves and are terrified to let it go. They are too afraid to face the true face of one of the purest evils of our epic. And since they can’t tolerate that ugly face they want to destroy the epic itself. Period.

The Vastraharana is a part of every single narrative — both ancient and critical. It is present in almost all ancient recitation including Neelkantha, southern recitations, and has been included in the critical edition of BORI. It has dozens of cross-references which is yet another technique to verify the authenticity.

So where does interpolation theory come from?

The theory doesn’t have a backing in any of the ancient narratives. It stems from the essay of few scholars who have forwarded their lousy arguments, punctuated with a lot of maybe, it appears and it may be argued. Their best argument includes that while there are sufficient cross-references to Druapadi's humiliations in the court house, there aren’t many that explicitly mentions that she was actually stripped. Now there is a mere flaw in this argument —

Neither the authentic version nor the critical version mention that Vastraharan happened. They all clearly mention that Vastraharana was attempted which was foiled. So I don’t see why cross reference would mention stripping when it was foiled by divine intervention.

And before we move on, let me re-iterate, the interpolation theory is just an opinion of a few scholar and has no backing with any ancient or critical narrative.

No reason for a Karna’s fan to rejoice.

Even if we are not believe in the flawed opinion, it still doesn’t make any room for Karna’s fan to rejoice. Even those scholars in their essays DO NOT vindicate Karna. They do not forward any argument saying gambling never happened or Draupadi was never humiliated or Karna had no role in Draupadi’s humifaction. No, even those scholar's don’t try to shield Karna from his heinous role which was repeated again and again by Drona, Ashwatthama, Krishna and even by Karna. They just debate on one thing — If Draupadi was actually disrobed. And the answer is clear. NO. She wasn’t. A heinous attempt was made at Karna’s behest, which those scholars do not deny, and that had failed.
