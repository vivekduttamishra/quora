Was Yudhisthira punished for making Draupadi as a pledge in the game of dice? If not, why?

📅 Aug 02, 2020 12:17 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Though I have answered a similar question a few times earlier, it is worth answering one more time.

Let us see if Yudhishthira was punished for pledging Draupadi in the game of dice —

  • After the first game of dice, Draupadi had freed all his husbands using a boon from Dhritrashtra. Why do you think the second game of dice started?
  • When Krishna (or some divine power) protected Draupadi from humiliation, why the same power didn’t interfere in the second dice game?
  • Did Yudhishthir got free after the dice game?

Call it cosmic justice, call it one of the greatest teachings of Mahabharata — noone escapes the justice.

Not only Yudhishtir, but all the Pandavas needed punishment. And they were punished —

  • They were exiled for 12 years, out of their kingdom, leaving a wrteched life.
  • They suffered an Agyatvas for another whole year, where they were under the service of another king, everyday terrified if they would be uncovered.

This period was both a punishment and a chance or redemption for Pandavas. And the redemption is clear as Yudhishtir instructs Bheema and Arjuna to get the Kauravas and their wives released from the bondage of Gandharvas —

Through Duryodhana’s forcible capture by the gandharva and through a stranger’s oppression of the women, our lineage has been sullied.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6425-6426).

Now Pandavas were not going to be moot spectators of Draupadi’s humiliation. They killed Keechak for his foolshardy.

So you see Yudhisthir was both punished and he completed his redemption.
