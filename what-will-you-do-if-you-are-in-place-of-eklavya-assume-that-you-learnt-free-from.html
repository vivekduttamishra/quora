What will you do if you are in place of Eklavya, assume that you learnt free from watching video courses from torrent and then in near future you met the teacher and teacher asked thumb as a fee?

📅 May 07, 2020 04:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Looks like this is a new question flooding Quora — comparing Drona’s teaching to a online course and guru asking for the thumb instead of the payment. I have answered a similar question earlier here — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Was it wise for Eklavya to give his thumb. imagine you are learning from internet as distance learning program like Udemy but when payment page comes It ask for your thumb or kidney something like that?

The majory flaw of this debade is three fold —

  1. Down playing the crime by comparing it to some harmless online course whose multiple pirated copies exists. Note here Drona teaching is compared to online course metamorphically, to match current era.
  2. Overhyping the punishment as a ghasty act. Literally. Here unlike point one no metaphor is replaced to match current era — say a large fine or a jail time or getting blacklisted in company.
  3. Considering that someone who is undergoing a course by using priated copy of the course is not really a criminal, because they are not getting punished for robbing the course desiger of their rightful payment.

This question at least proves the guilt of Eklavya as a pirate. Let us now consider the three points in details.

  1. Can Drona’s teaching be compared to an Online pirated course? Can Eklavya be compared to someone watching a pirated online content?
    1. Not everything can be or should be tought online. Not everything be allowed to teach online.
    2. We don’t teach milletery, defence, medical kind of critical segments online, because there practical is critical and distance education can’t provide that — not even if you can replay the same video a hundered times. No think what was Drona teaching?
    3. Drona’s gurukul was a reputed Institution and can be compared to modern day institutue like AIIMS (All India Institute of Medical Science) or NDA (National Defence Academey). Imagine someone didn’t get admission to thse institute and then he bugs the institute. Do you think government would (or should) let it go unpunished?
  2. How ghasty was the punishment?
    1. What would happen if someone is found to have completed the course from AIIMS and found to have forged his way inside? Would be allowed to practice? No He would be jailed and stripped out of his degree. He won’t be allowed to practice medicine. Same rule goes to anyone with a fake degree/fraud degree in almost any descipline.
    2. What should be done if someone tries to steal miletery secret? This would be considered as a matter of national security and treason. What would be the charge?
    3. What did Parshuram did with a similar pirate?
    4. How did Vashishtha’s son cursed Harishchandra’s father — He, too, wanted to crash gate to swarga loka. Right?
  3. Piracy is robbing someone
    1. Piracy is robbing. When someone steals, someone else loose. Has the questioner ever considered his side?
    2. Don’t we justify punishing financial criminals liked — Lalu Prasad Yadava, Harshad Mehta, and those absconding from India after doing financial scams. They all have done similar underbook activity. They didn’t kill anyone.
    3. This question does expose a softcorner for such pirates. Should we promote piracy?
    4. Don’t film industry come heavy against pirates?
    5. How severing thumb of someone be greater punishment than a jail for say 10 years?

Now finally answering the question — What would I be doing…

  1. I won’t be pirating. Period.
  2. I won’t teach my childlren or anyone for that matter to shamelessly pirate.
  3. I won’t endorse someone who is a pirate.
  4. I won’t glorify or justify a pirate
  5. I would read and learn from the ultimate fate of Eklavya.
