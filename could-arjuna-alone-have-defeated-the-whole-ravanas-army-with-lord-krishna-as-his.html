Could Arjuna alone have defeated the whole Ravana's army with Lord Krishna as his charioteer?

📅 Mar 24, 2020 03:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for an A2A answer.

I have recently answered a question on similar lines, however about Krishna instead of Arjuna. My answer is here — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Could Lord Krishna alone have defeated the whole army of Ravana?

But since the question replaces Krishna with Arjuna the answer too shall differ. I think the answer would be No.

  1. Krishna Arjuna duo didn’t defeat Kauravas alone. Why should we expect that Krishna would do it in Ravana battle?
  2. Even Great Ram didn’t do it alone?
  3. Arjuna was not Rama. He didn’t let his wife be insulted but immediately formed an army to fight the mightiest power of the time. He improvised all the army he could. He was Krishna (leader) of his time. You needed someone of such a resolve to win over an evil Ravana.
  4. Ravana too had great warriors who even baffled Ram and Laxman, and Arjuna was no Rama.

The most important part of our epic is to give a lesson — A lesson that war against evil won’t be easy and can be fought by ordinary people. It is to give a social message and that can’t be given by fighting an one man army battle.
