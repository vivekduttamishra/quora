Why was Shakuni allowed to stay in Hastinapur for such a long time?

📅 Sep 05, 2021 09:35 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Simple answer is there was no reason to turn him away. He was close to Duryodhana and it was acceptable for relatives of royal family to stay put and enjoy the royal patronage. A great example would be the brother of Shantanu, Bahlika who was the King of Balkh but was continuously present in Hastinapur court.

This question assumes that Shakuni was a mastermind and had some great influence on the destiny of Mahabharata as depicted in popular pulp fictions — WRONG.

Shakuni’s role in majority part of the Mahabharata had been that of a minor stooge following the royal Prince Duryodhana. He is more like Shambha of Gabbar Singh.

The only time Shakuni acts as the mastermind was during the dice game where his skill as an expert gambler, his capability of deceit and the knowledge of some dark art (maya) allowed him to win against an armature player that was Yudhishthira. It was the only time in the entire epic that he actually planned and executed. There too his role ends after winning and had almost no role in Draupadi’s humiliation.

On all other occasion, Shakuni just followed in Duryodhana’s plan. No wonder Ved Vyas described him just as the branch of evil tree Duryodhana and not its root or trunk.
