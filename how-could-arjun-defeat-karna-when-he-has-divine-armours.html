How could Arjun defeat Karna when he has divine armours?

📅 Jan 16, 2021 10:54 PM PST  |  🌐 English

This question has been raised innumerable times as a means to cast doubt on the epic narrative and give some extra shield to Karna for his failures. But if the divine shield couldn’t protect him from getting defeated, such lame arguments are not likely to do so either.

What an armour can and can’t do?

The role of an armour is to protect you from an attack, protect you from getting killed. An armour can’t kill your enemies and an armour can’t make you braver or more courageous than your are. An armour may protect penetration of an arrow, it can’t prevent the penetration of fear in your head.

There is a difference between being strong and brave, being a great archer and a warrior. Think this way an olympic gold medalist shooter doesn’t automatically qualify to become a great soldier. On the other hand one can be a great soldier, even if he may not be as great a shooter. So, what is the difference between the two? It’s skill vs courage.

No weapon or shield, earthly or divine, can be a substitute for courage and perseverance. Some people have claustrophobia — fear of closed space. The confined space do them no harm, the fear does. They may even die of it.

And lack of courage and fear for his life was the greatest reason of Karna’s failure, and armour wasn’t designed to protect him from either. Karna knew his shield would protect him from dying, but the assurance couldn’t alleviate his fear and he retreated from Arjuna during swyamvara war thinking opponent is invincible and not realizing he too could be invincible. Same happened with Gandharva war. He was overwhelmed. He, in his own words, feared and fled to safety.

On the other hand, take the example of Duryodhana. He had no divine armour to protect him. He could have died. Yet he refused to flee. What does that tell us? Armours don’t make you a warrior. Yes, if you are a warrior, the armour can certainly make you invincible.

Karna lost to Arjuna despite of having a great armour because he lacked courage and perseverance. He was a great archer, but not a warrior. He was strong, but not brave.
