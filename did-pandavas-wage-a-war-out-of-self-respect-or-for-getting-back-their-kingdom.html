Did Pandavas wage a war out of self respect or for getting back their kingdom?

📅 Jul 12, 2020 04:40 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The war was for both the self-respect and getting their kingdom back. But it was for something of far more worth than both. It was for justice and as Krishna himself pronounced for reestablishing the Dharma.

No doubt, It started as a tussle for the throne and the kingdom, with Pandavas being ousted from their kingdom and both Kaurvas and Pandavas committed blunders — one was guilty of cheating, other of foolishness and probably neither worthy to be rulers or the land. This should be clear from the fact that Krishna intervenes only to protect Draupadi and not to protect Pandavas. They were exiled for 13 years, which is no less than sentencing or a punishment, they rightly deserved.

Here a chapter ends, and another begins. One where Pandavas take their exile as a penance. They had learnt their lessons. The same Pandavas who had mocked Duryodhana in Indraprastha palace, jumps to rescue him and his family from a greater humiliation in the hands of Gandharvas, knowing all wickedness of team Kauravas. Arjuna worked hard to gain the blessings of Shiva. Now they had learnt their lessons and not going to forgive the abusers like Jaidradha or Keechaka. In Veerta battle they fought for the welfare of Virata Kingdom, risking their cover getting blown. They proved their might. They were wronged. They had fulfilled their side of bargain. Yet they were denied what was rightful theirs. Despite of all this they tried for peace. They proved they are worthy to be the rulers — compassinate, loyal, justice seeking and peace loving. Yes, they were finally ready.

On the other hand, Kaurvas who were guilty of treacheary, deceit, violance and abuse reigned free. They never did any penance. They devoted the period forging alliance and becoming powerful to fight Pandavas someday. Pandavas rescued them for a humiliating defeat, spared the life of Jaidradh. Yet they learnt nothing They again attacked Virata hoping to phish Pandavas out. They learnt nothing — cared for no justice, or peace. Justice demanded such a vile person shouldn’t rule.

Why did they demand for five village? What if Duryodhana had agreed.

Justice isn’t about proving their might. Pandavas were a better person now and were willing to move ahead. But justice demanded redressal. There would be no justice if they forgo their right. This time they didn’t run away like what they did after Varnavrat. They returned. And the demanded — lesser thean what was theirs, but they demaned nevertheless. Five village was important to exert their right on Hastinapur kingdom which was rightfully their father’s. Five Kingdom was an announcement that the Kings are back. Five kingdom was a becon of hope for people. Remember last time they created Indraprasta out of a barren land. What they couldn’t have done with five village?

Had Duryodhana give up the claims on five village, he too, would have a chance of redemption. The loss of five village would have crushed his ego and he may have become a better person. Maybe, just maybe, justice would have prevailed and Pandava via another Rajsuy yagna gained back their entire kingdom and manage to reestablish Dharma in a more peaceful way. Opportunities were immense, but Kauravas had learn nothing, and they deserved to perish.

As I see it, it was a war for justice and getting the kingdom back was the stepping stone.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
