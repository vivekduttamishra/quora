How do people celebrate Mahashivratri?

📅 Feb 20, 2020 09:39 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Mahashivaratri is one of the most popular festival in India and there have been numerous reasons cited for its origin and benefits of observing - right from his marriage to Parvati to the very birth of the Universe.

But probably there are few subtle philosophy that distinguishes it more than anything. It's a festival celebrated at night - - in darkness, - - to be in unison with Shiva - - not distracted by worldly glitter.

Similarly the benefits of being part of Shiva is being part of Shiva.

There is a popular story of Shivaratri where a debt ridden hunter unknowingly worships shiva, offering Shiva lingam his tears as water and bilba leaf that he was throwing without any intention of worship.

(the full story can be read here : http://shivalaya.vnc.in/en/mahas...)

The popular interpretation is how easy it is to please Shiva. But if you read the story carefully you would find the real essence of Shiva.

The hunter was debt ridden, being haunted by money lender, hungry and terrified about his family. The tear that fell on Shiva was his state of worldly depression. And yet he let his prey a family of 4 deer live. He sacrificed his own self for others just like Neelkanth. That made him Shiva…

And that in my understanding the essence of Mahashivratri.
