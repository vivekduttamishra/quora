Did Krishna not know that Duryodhana would not accept the peace proposal?

📅 Apr 29, 2021 02:13 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: I believe yes.

Krishna knew Duryodhana wouldn’t accept the peace proposal, and yet he deemed it important to give him one final chance — to reshape his destiny.

You see, in Santana philosophy, Gods don’t act as puppeteer or script director where humans have to just enact the script. We are the writer of our own script under certain pre-set rules — Karma! We are free to act based on our gunas, and we pay for our actions. Duryodhana had chance to rewrite his desitiny. He chose not to.

Krishna knew Duryodhana well enough to know he would refuse. And this is something he had announced years ago in Kirata Parva — where he had announced all those deceitful evils shall be slain. The two sides had already made pacts and were ready for the war. It was just a last ditch attempt to bring Duryodhana to his senses — lest not someone say that God doesn’t give opportunity to evils to change there ways.

In all his interactions with Pandava, Hastinapur and Karna he had made it abundantly clear that the war would go in favor of Pandavas without an iota of doubt.

But then as I said… he was there to show the path, not walk the way.
