What would happen if Bhishmapitamah wouldn't have entered the Kurukshetra War?

📅 Jan 09, 2021 02:29 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

This is an interesting question. And I see three important outcomes, had Bhishma not entered the war —

  1. War would be over sooner with less devastation

The war would have been over long before. I would say in less than Three days with much less devastation that that happened during the war.

Bhishma was the longest serving general of Kaurava’s army — Ten days. The other generals lasted 5 days, 2 days and 1 day. So the maximum time Kaurava’s could have fought without Bhishma would be Eight days.

When Bhisma fought, he fought so vigorously that not only Pandava lost hope of a victory, but that Krishna himself had to take up weapon to subdue him. Krishna never did that for anyone else. But had Bhisma been absent from the war, Pandavas Army wouldn’t have suffered the huge loss it suffered as Bhishma slaughtered on. So Pandava would have a much larger army to fight Kauravas.

2. Why three days?

With Bhishma gone, Dronacharya, Kripacharya and Ashwatthama too wouldn’t have fought. Dronacharya made it clear, that he fought the war only because of his loyalty to Bhishma and no one else. Kripacharya and Ashwatthama participated in the war from Kaurva’s side because of Dronacharya.

Had Bhishma not fought the war on Kaurava’s side, Drona had no reason to fight. To think of, he may have sided his favourite student. So do the calculation —

Pandava + Drona + Kripa + Ashwatthama vs Kaurvas - Bhishma

My estimate of three days appears to be bloated.

2. Gita

Yes, Arjuna put off his weapon at the very begining of the battle. Why? Because he didnt’ want to fight his relatives. But which relative? Duryodhana? Dussassana? If you see carefully, the one grand reason for Arjuna’s reluctance was Bhishma and to an extent Drona and Kripacharya. With the three prominent warriors from Kaurava team gone, Arjuna may not be reluctant enough and never needed a discourse from Krishna — Gita. So maybe, just maybe, world would have been poorer by such great knowledge.
