If Arjuna was supported by Lord Hanuman and Krishna, then how is Arjun said to be the greatest archer?

📅 Jan 17, 2021 01:13 AM PST  |  🌐 English

It is an interesting question that need to be answered in two separate phases :.

How was Arjuna greatest archer.

Neither Krishna nor Hanuman did ever shoot an arrow for Arjuna. Arjuna’s archery was Arjuna’s skill, something which was praised not just by his Guru Dronacharya but from the greatest warriors of the time to commoners. What's more, even greatest god of gods, the first archer, Mahadeva praised his skills! Few can boast of such an achievement.

What does Krishna’s and Hanuman Support mean?

The support of Krishna and Hanuman (and Shiva) doesn’t devalue Arjuna as an archer, but elavates his credential as a good human being. The almighty God of all doesn’t praise or support somone out of biase but out of pure merit.

Krishna won’t become the charioteer of just anyone (not matter what TVShows may like you to believe) but only for someone who can be compassinate enough to set aside his weapon and be ready to be killed rather than kill his kins without understanding the divine knowledge.

How can Lord Hanuman support someone who betrays Lord Rama’s philosophy? No he would support only someone who can rush to protect the women of the family of even their tormentors. Only a reminder of Rama could attrack his greatest disciple.

Would Shiva have given his Pashupatastra to anyone who comes asking for it? Shiva tested the perseverance and courage of Arjuna, who kept fighting the Kirata Shiva rather than fleeing the scene. He couldn’t have trusted the mightiest weapon to someone with a lesser resolve and lest he might end up using it at the first great challenge of his life.

So, support of Shiva, Hanuman and Krishna doesn’t devalue Arjuna as an archer, it pronounces what a great human being Arjuna was. Doubting Arjuna would be doubting the wisdom of Shiva, Krishna and Hanuman.
