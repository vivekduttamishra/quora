Karna failed in Draupadi swayamvar as per BORI CE but according to KMG he was rejected, which is true?

📅 Sep 11, 2020 02:10 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question is not dying anytime soon. I have discussed this in details earlier here — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s post in The Mahabharata Project

The summary is as follows —

  1. Draupadi’s rejection to Karna is not mentioned in BORI.
  2. Mr Sukthankar in his editorial preface very clearly rules out Drsupadi’s rejection to Karna.
  3. BORI includes the shloka that clearly mentions Karna failed at the Swayamvara.
  4. Annals of BORI emphasizes Karna's failure.

WHAT ABOUT KMG

KMG has a contradiction. It is based on Neelkantha which is among 5 out of 1259 manuscript that mentions Drawpadi rejecting Karna. But this version also lists Karna's failure. Here is the extract from KMG —

Then Arjuna approached the bow and stood there like a mountain. And walking round that bow, and bending his head unto that giver of boons--the lord Isana--and remembering Krishna also, he took it up. And that bow which Rukma, Sunitha, Vakra, Radha's son, Duryodhana, Salya, and many other kings accomplished in the science and practice of arms, could not even with great exertion, string, Arjuna, the son of Indra, that foremost of all persons endued with energy and like unto the younger brother of Indra (Vishnu) in might, strung in the twinkling of an eye.

Adi Parva: Swayamvara Parva: Section CLXL

So as you can see even KMG mentions Karna's failure.

One of the high voted answer to this question tries to misquote Mr Sukthankar and BORI to suggest Karna never failed. I am responding to those claims here as every comment contradicting that answer has been deleted. And yet answer claims to misquote Sukthankar.

CLAIMS AND CONTRADICTION THAT BORI DOESN'T MENTION KARNA'S FAILURE IN SWAYAMVARAA

  • CLAIM#1 Karna was not a participant, just accompanied Kauravas
  • CONTRADICTION Mr Sukthankar very clearly mentions that Karna was a invited guest.

He had been invited by her father. A t least he was given a seat of honour

-Sukthankar

  • CLAIM 2 Drishtduymna mentions only people with noble birth.
  • CONTRADICTION 2 There are several contradictions —
    • Drishtduymna also mentions handsome. But doesn't mention anyone getting rejected because they were not handsome. Does that mean all were handsome?
    • Drishtduymna also says — O fortunate one! These and many other kings of many regions, famous on earth, have come here for you. These brave ones will try to shoot the excellent target for your hand. O beautiful one! You will choose as a husband the one who hits it.”
    • Note he says king and not Kshatriya. And Karna was a king
  • Sukthankar says

Moreover, it does not appear as if the bride elect had much choice or voice

  • CLAIM 3 — Kings mentioned that shastra says Swayamvara is only for Kshatriya
  • Contradiction — True the kings said that, but they were no scholar of Shastra. They were the same people who tried to throw Draupadi in fire and they said this later and didn't stop Arjuna from participating. How could they have stopped Karna?
  • Sukthankara quotes Yudhisthir to suggest that caste was no bar for Swayamvara. Yudhisthir is undoubtedly refered as an authority and Mr Suthankar's view leaves no room for challenge

She was vīryaśulkā: she was given by her guardian to the highest bidder, the price paid being heroism, or rather proficiency in marksmanship. This is quite evident from the words of Yudhiṣṭhira, addressed later to the Purohita of Drupada (1. 185. 23f.):

प्रदिष्टशुल्का द्रुपदेन राज्ञा; सानेन वीरेण तथानुवृत्ता |न तत्र वर्णेषु कृता विवक्षा; न जीवशिल्पे न कुले न गोत्रे ||२३||

We accordingly find, as a matter of fact, that without murmur or hesitation, she follows an unknown and apparently undistinguished Brahman boy—Arjuna in disguise—who happens to have hit the mark. She does not know him from Adam, but she makes no inquiries about his status or lineage.

CLAIM 4 Drupad was a castist and would never have allowed Karna to participate. They refer to this shloka —

Where has Krishna gone? Who has taken her away? Is it a Shudra or one of low birth? Has a Vaishya who pays taxes placed his feet on my head? Has a garland been thrown away on a cremation ground? O son! Or is it a foremost man from our own varna, or is it one from a higher varna? Or has a lower being placed his foot on my head and defiled Krishna?

CONTRADICTION This shloka is the greatest contradiction to the claim. Note here Drupad was talking about Arjuna. He wasn't sure about the true identity of Arjuna and Arjuna was an undistinguished Brahmin of no noble lineage. Yet neither Drupad nor Drishtduymna stopped him. He wasn't a Kshatriya and yet no king stopped him. Does it not prove no one could have stopped Karna?

THE MOST IMPORTANT REFERENCE, MOST RIDICULOUS CLAIM AND CONTRADICTION.

BORI clearly mentions Karna's failure —

यत्कर्णशल्यप्रमुखैः पार्थिवैर्लोकविश्रुतैः |

नानतं बलवद्भिर्हि धनुर्वेदपरायणैः ||४||

— shloka 4, chapter 179, Adi Parva

This is BORI reference. Now their claim is not based on BORI but translation by Bibek Debroy where he translated the shloka as —

When famous Kshatriyas like Shalya and Karna and other great Kings, who are well known for their strength and archery, couldn’t string the bow…

CLAIM 5 Shloka mentions Kshatriya Karna and Karna was not a Kshatriya. He must have been a different Karna.

CONTRADICTION

  1. BORI DOESN'T MENTION KSHATRIYA, but Parthivah which means Kings. Even Bibek Debroy translated it as King in another shloka —

इदं धनुर्लक्ष्यमिमे च बाणाः; शृण्वन्तु मे पार्थिवाः सर्व एव |

यन्त्रच्छिद्रेणाभ्यतिक्रम्य लक्ष्यं; समर्पयध्वं खगमैर्दशार्धैः ||३४||

—Shloka 34 of chapter 176, Adi Parva (BORI)

O assembled kings! Hear that this is the bow, that is the target and these are the arrows. With these five arrows, shoot the target through the hole in the centre of the machine.

2. The supposed Kshatriya Karna was a famous Archer, but that Karna has not been mentioned anywhere in Mahabharata. Dhritarashtra had a son named Karna but he wasn't a famous Archer.

3. Drishtduymna names all prominent guests including sons of Dhritrastra who attended Swayamvara but only one Karna is named — the famous great archer friend of Duryodhana. No other Karna is mentioned anywhere.

4. On any confusion of names BORI mentions it in footnotes. No such footnote exists here.

5. This shloka is clarified in Annals of BORI clearly suggesting Karna failed.

6. Karna's failure is mentioned in KMG. (reference above)

7. Karna's failure is mentioned in Gita Press which also refers to BORI here.

8. Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat mentions his failure.

9. There is not a single reference that says Karna who came to Swayamvara didn't participate.
