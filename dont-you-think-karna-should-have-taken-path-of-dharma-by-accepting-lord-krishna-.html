Don't you think Karna should have taken path of Dharma by accepting Lord Krishna and Kunti offer? If he had done so, wouldn't he be respected as Yuyutsu?

Thanks for the A2A.

I was going to skip this question, if not for one of the answer which looked so ridiculous that I couldn’t stop but writing an answer.

The answer said,

Had Karna accepted Krishna’s and Kunti’s offer —

  • Then any future Radha wouldn't have dared to adopt any abandoned Karna
  • Any future Radha would have not fed her breast milk to a future Karna
  • Any future Adhiratha wouldn't have performed all the sacred rites for a future Karna

— An answer to this question

And I kept wondering why?

  • Were Radha and Adhiratha would have felt betrayed? Were they too evil like Duryodhana?
  • Were Radha and Adhiratha too war mongers who wanted to have the war?
  • Were they Krishna haters?
  • Didn’t they wanted the rule of peace and Dharma?

Now lets come to the question —

WHAT IF KARNA HAD ACCEPTED THE PATH OF DHARMA AS SHOWN BY KRISHNA, KUNTI AND SURYADEV?

Would he have been more respected? I am not sure. There is no one — not even Gods — who are universally respected. And even devils like Ravana and Hitler’s have followers.

My question is — Is being respected the most important aspect of life?

  • Was his loyalty (if there was one) more important than—
    • Dharma?
    • the millions of lives lost in the war, that could have been avoided?
    • Justice. Even he accepted, Pandava’s have been denied justice.
  • Where was that loyalty, when —
    • He fled Gandharva’s leaving Duryodhana and his brothers behind?
    • He abstained from the battle for 10 days for his pure ego?
    • When Kauravas were killed in front of him by Bheema who he didn’t kill for his own oath even if that means being disloyal to Duryodhana?

When Karna abandoned Duryodhana for his oath to Kunti, his ego getting hurt by Bhishma or his fear of Gandharavas, why didn’t he do it for Dharma, humanity and his justice?

As I said, Karna may not have won everyones heart. For some unfanthomable reason Radha and Adhirath, may have felt betrayed (I still can’t imagine why?), but he would have won one person to his side — Krishna — the only one who mattered. Only one who mattered.

And then Mahabharata would have hailed him as the savior of humanity, rather than the trunk of an evil tree. Alas!
