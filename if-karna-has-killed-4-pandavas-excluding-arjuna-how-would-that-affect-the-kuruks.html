If Karna has killed 4 Pandavas (excluding Arjuna), how would that affect the Kurukshetra war?

📅 Jul 16, 2022 01:04 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Interesting question. Let us divide in few parts.

WHAT IF HE HAD ACTUALLY KILLED FOUR PANDAVAS?

IMPOSSIBLE!

The best case scenario for Karna was to able to kill one of the Pandavas. Maybe the first one he encountered — Sahadeva. That would have been his greatest achievement, moments before the total annihilation of entire Kuru Army along with Karna!

Just think, while Karna would be busy in killing four Pandavas, what Arjuna would be doing? Sitting idle? Lamenting the way Karna lamented while Bhimasena killed Kauravas brother in front of his eyes or the way he wailed by Arjuna and Abhimanyu killed his brothers and sons? Nah! We are talking about Pandavas here.

Remember what happened when Abhimanyu was killed by Kauravas? They saw the wrath of Arjuna in which a sizeable portion of Kauravas army perished.

Remember what happened when Karna merely used abusive language against Bhimasena. Vrishasena paid the price by his life for his father’s arrogance!

Remember Bhimasena almost killed Karna after he insulted Yudhishira on Day 17 and a unconcious Karna was carried away by Shalya. Now, think, what would have Bhimasena done had Karna killed one of his brothers.

So had Karna killed even one of the Pandavas, Karna along with entire Kaurava army would have be decimated even before he had chance to lay eye on the second Pandava. That would have been the worse nightmare of Kaurava which was incidentally expressed by Duryodhana to Drona —

your son joyfully spoke these words. ‘O preceptor! If Kunti’s son is killed by you, I will not be able to obtain victory. If Yudhishthira is slain, there is no doubt that the Parthas will slaughter all of us. They are incapable of being killed in battle, even by all the immortals. The one among them who is left, will destroy us.

—Drona Parva, Chapter 988(11)

Even he knew the answer.

But before going to the possibility of even on Pandava getting killed let as ask ourselves a few more questions.

Did Karna have what it takes to kill Pandavas?

Before we take Karna’s extremely boastful gesture of sparing four Pandava’s life seriously, Let me ask you the same question Ashwatthama asked to Karna during Matasya battle —

In which duel in battle have you vanquished Dhananjaya, Nakula or Sahadeva? Whose riches have you robbed? Has Yudhishthira, or Bhima, supreme among strong ones, ever been defeated by you? And in which battle did you win over Krishna?

— Goharana Parva

When did Karna actually defeat any Pandavas in his life to make such a boastful remark. Bhimasena had certainly defeated Karna during Rajsuy Yagana. He had spared his life then. And again on Day 14 and again on Day 17. Arjuna, well he spared his life in every encounter they had before the last one. Yes, it is a different matter non of them boasted about it.

Now let me take you back to the day when Karna made such a boastful promise and see what else did he say —

Who does not suffer when Dhananjaya is aided by Krishna? If I now go over to the Parthas, who will not think that I am frightened?

— Udyoga Parva

Even he knew, it was not his destiny to win or kill Pandavas. Shall we still discuss that probability, what if he had actually killed them? One of the reasons he wouldn’t switch sides is that people would think he was frightened. It was not about Dharma, but his ego!

Afterall didn't he have the chance to kill them?

Surely. He had a chance. And he recalled his promise to Kunti and didn't kill them. But can we be certain that had he tried to kill, he would have been successful? Didn’t Bhurisrava also think that he would be able to kill Satyaki? Could he? What happened? Didn’t Karna say in Matasya battle that he would kill Arjuna today? Could he?

To say I will kill is one thing. To actually manage to kill is another.

But still had he managed to kill even one Pandava two things I can say for sure —

  1. That Pandava wouldn’t have fled or begged for mercy. He would have accepted his death with grace of a brave warrior, knowing his killer would soon join him.
  2. The entire Kaurava army had joined his rank, with Karna shivering and crying for Mercy!
