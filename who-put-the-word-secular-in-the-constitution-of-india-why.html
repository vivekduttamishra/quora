Who put the word "secular" in the constitution of India? Why?

📅 Feb 25, 2020 06:19 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Secularism has been a the defining trait of Hindu/Indian culture since the beginning of the time. And yes before you take me for some fanatic consider this —

  • Hindu civilization is the most ancient civilization in existence. Other older religions /cultures are long gone.
  • This ancient civilization on its inherent philosophy of mutual respect and tolerance continued not only to coexist but often merge together diverse views and sect. Buddha, one of the greatest critic of prevailing hindu practices was accepted as 9th vishnu, Jainism and Buddhism part of same Hindu civilization.
  • Chandragupta Maurya towards later part of his life tilted towards Jainism, Ashoka towards Buddhism. Gupta period became golden age of India. Underlying philosophy remained same — no religious prosecution, no forced conversation, no malice spread.

When India got independence, it was explicitly decided.

  1. Hindu/Indian tradition will continue. Every religion in India will find a place like always.
  2. The constituent assembly debated and rejected the explicit inclusion of word secular in preamble — it was deemed unnecessary.

The but was forced insert during emergency of 1976 without a parliament debate — it started an era of appeasement and chaos serving the true purpose behind such an inclusion. It was denying the fact that ancient India, and the constitution doesn't add an explicit emphasis.

But key question is — what is secularism?

  1. Tolerance and respect for all faith?
  2. Separation of state and religion?
  3. All religions are equal before law?
  4. Law is not based on religion?

Now consider current Indian law —

  1. There is a Hindu personal law and a Muslim personal law — secularism?
  2. Court interferes in Sabrimala — secularism?
  3. Religious conversation is legalized and known to follow an organized approach including condemning other religions — secularism?

Unfortunately the word has created more chaos than anything :

  1. It tends to differentiate hindu and secular to the extent that hindu gets a tag of anti secular.
  2. Secular today is the facade for appeasement politics.
  3. Democratic processes have been subvert in the name of being secular.
