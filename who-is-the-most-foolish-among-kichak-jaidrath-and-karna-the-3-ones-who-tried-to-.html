Who is the most foolish among Kichak, Jaidrath and Karna, the 3 ones who tried to play with fire, but themselves got burnt (All tried to humiliate Draupadi)?

📅 Sep 14, 2021 09:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I would go with Jaidradh.

Keechak had no idea who was Druapadi or who were Pandavas? He thought he was abusing a lowly house help. He was vile and arrogant but not a fool as he didn’t have the right kind of data. He didn’t underestimate his adversaries as he didn’t know them.

Karna’s folly was not foolishness. He was blinded by his ambition. He wasn’t fool, but wicked. He chose his side wisely right into gurukul. He knew Pandava’s camp won’t need an aide for any conspiracy and with Arjuna in the camp he would never get the glory he desperately desired. Thus he allied with Kauravas, participating in their conspiracies against Pandavas and cementing his bond with them. That paid him well. He was made a King. His attempt to humiliates Draupadi was an extension of the same effort — to be considered closest aide to Duryodhana. To be honest this strategy worked for Karna all his life and he enjoyed a long life full of lust and luxury which he won’t have got otherwise. Another proof of Karna’s wicked nature was his genius in fleeing away from tight spots such as Draupadi’s swyamver battle, Gandharva battle or Matasya battle. He didn’t care if his best friend is captured or his own brother is killed in the process. Some may call it coward, but it was never foolish.

That leaves Jaidradh. He knew Pandava’s and also knew his own strength. He was stupid enough to try his hand, first on Draupadi and then against Pandavas that made him unwitting culprit of Abhimanyu’s gruesome killing. Once again, he exhibited his stupidity by staying back as a bait to Arjuna on day 14 of the battle. Had he been any wiser he would have fled the previous day.

So, I would give the award for being greatest stupid to Jaidradh.
