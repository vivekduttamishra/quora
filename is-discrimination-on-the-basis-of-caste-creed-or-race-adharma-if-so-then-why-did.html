Adding to the excellent answer by Manasi Srivastava , the question becomes an answer if we simply rephrase it —

Lord Krishna, a Vishnu Avatar, who incarnated to establish Dharma, didn’t punish anyone for so called discrimination of Karna, does that mean story of Karna’s absue is an interpolation? Is that why BORI censored it?

The main problem is we try to see the Mahabharata era with our contemporary eyes and try to reject every logical answer if it doesn’t match my understanding of society based on the current era.

Did santatan society had caste system?

Of course. Caste system existed. And before you ask me, it was based on birth.

Did caste system discriminated?

Yes, it discriminated. The entire purpose the caste system was to discriminate — Group, organize and segregate.

First let us understand What is discrimination? As per oxford dictionary, discrimination is to recognize the distinction or differences. Is it bad? Is it something evil that should be eradicated?

And such division exists in every walk of life everywhere. Don’t organizations have hierarchy? Does everyone in your workplace equal? Do they get same pay? Do they get same privilege? Does everyone have same say in policy decision?

Isn’t discrimination an evil practice?

You are confusing between caste and racism.

Consider these cases —

  • Do I discriminate between a Dog and a Rabbit? Sure. Is rabbit weaker than Dog? Sure. Do I like Dog more than Rabbit? Actually no, I adore the rabbit.
  • Should I discriminate Lion and Elephant? Yes. Does that mean one is better than other is abused?

That was our santan culture. Caste was distinction, not abuse. It was way better than racism that you see in the USA or the hierarchy you see in organization. In fact caste turned abusive in India only on after foreign influence and nothing like that was present in Mahabharata or Ramayana age.

I would not repeat the excellent examples put forward my Manasi Srivastava in her answer, so I would present a few more examples from Ramayana —

  • Ram and Nishadraj studies together and were friend.
  • Ram and Shabri interaction
  • Ram enlisted the support of Vanars who were certainly not high on any caste rank. Ram made Sugriv a friend, and ever gave Hanuman a status lesser than his siblings.
  • When Ravana, a Brhamin became and evil, he was killed, and no one lamented why a high caste brahmin was killed.

Our society had castes, caste was discriminatory but not abusive or racist.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
