Please check out this detailed answer to understand the whole context — What made BORI consider Draupadi humiliation as an interpolation?

Summary —

  • KMG is based on Neelkantha, and even Neelkantha mentions Karna failure in two shlokas. It also mentions that Draupadi couldn’t have rejected Karna due to the Swyamber rules led down by Drupad. It doesn’t mention Drupad ever referring Karna as Suta.
  • BORI consulted not only Neelkantha but also 1259 scripts. Out of which only 5 (including Neelkantha, and a Nepali script N2) mentions the story of Draupadi stopping Karna. remaining 1255, that doesn’t include this stroy includes — most ancient Nepali Script N3, entire southern recitation, entire Bengali recitations and Sharda recitations.
  • There is no cross-reference to this incident anywhere in any epic (including that of KMG) where anyone ever recalled this incident, in whatsoever manner.
  • BORI editor Suthankar and then in the annals of BORI there is a lengthy discussion on why this incident can’t be true.
