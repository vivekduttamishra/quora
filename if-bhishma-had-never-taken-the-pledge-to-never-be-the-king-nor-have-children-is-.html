If Bhisma had not taken the pledge, a lot of things would have gone different and I can explore that later, however, it may not have changed the second part of this question which is completely baseless.

Bhishma, Shakuni and Gandhar

Bhisma, indeed proposed the marriage of Gandhari and Dhritrastra. And Gandhar may not have the courage to refuse the proposal. But that was the end of the episode.

Gandhar or Shakuni never plotted against Hastinapur and were no way responsible for the Mahabharat war. Shakuni wasn't the mastermind of anything other than dice game and he has been grossly exaggerated in modern narrations. Shakuni and his family always supported Hastinapur and fought along side Bhisma.

If Bhisma had not taken the pledge…

  1. He would have become the king.
  2. Dhritrastra and Pandava may not have born or may still have born.
  3. Dhritrastra may or may not be blind but not be a King, just his nephew.
  4. King Bhisma may still have approached Gandhar to marry his blind nephew who would never be the king.
  5. Gandhar and Shakuni may have all the more reason to be upset and vengeful against Bhisma.

May be alternative time line would be worse. But in this time line —

  • Shakuni Was not the mastermind of war
  • Didn't plot against Hastinapur, just plotted against Pandava.
  • Entire family always supported Hastinapur under Bhisma's command.

  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and

  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
