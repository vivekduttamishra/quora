Who is better between Abhimanyu and Karna in terms of bravery?

📅 May 08, 2021 07:10 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

As I have mentioned so many times in my answers before there is a difference between being strong and being brave, between being archer and being warrior.

Anyone can appear brave when they are winning, and when a large army is marching with them. Anyone can appear strong if they have great weapons or protection jacket around them.

True bravery is how you react in the face of defeat. How do you face your death — with courage? by trying to plead your way out? or by outright running away. There goes a famous saying —

“A coward dies a thousand times before his death, but the valiant taste of death but once. It seems to me most strange that men should fear, seeing that death, a necessary end, will come when it will come.”

― William Shakespeare, Julius Caesar

Abhimanyu was that valiant warrior, who chose not to die a thousand death. He chose not to fear death. He chose not to run away. He chose not to beg. He chose not to teach others Dharma. He chose not to censure Dharma. He never asked why Dharma is not protecting him.

Abhimanyu could have stayed back and saved his life. But he chose to move forward and face the enemies. He won the day for his army. And he didn’t win the war because of some divine armor or weapon. But because of his seer courage and bravery.

He dwarfed an entire army in front of him. Even in his death he snatched the mask of bravery from faces of cowards who hunted him down in a pack. That is the definition of bravery — knowing you may not win, and yet fighting for what is right. Even in his death, he won the day for Pandavas.

Can we really compare Abhimanyu’s valor to Karna?

Karna was a skilled archer and had obtained many Divyastra by cheating Parshurama. In archery skills some may be claim, he was almost equal to even Arjuna! But bravery? Seriously?

The man who holds the highest record of running away? Refer to the quotes above. They have used another adjective for someone who dies a thousand times before his death.

What is dying a thousand time before his death, if not running away out of fear leaving your best friend, and women of the family at the mercy of your enemies?

Won’t you call it worst than death, when a warrior sees his brothers killed in front of his eyes and the first emotion he has is fear and not vengeance? Would you call some one a warrior who runs away saving his life rather than avenging his brother’s death?

Would you call it bravery when a warrior boasts of great deeds and runs away from his opponent? Would you call it courage if you dare not face you enemy from a front and attack him from behind? When you hunt in pack you still have the hypocrisy of being called an brave or a warrior?

If this is bravery, then I am not sure what would cowardice look like. Oh! bravery needs a new definition!
