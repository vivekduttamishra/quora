If we take out Krishna from Mahabharata, would there have been a war?

📅 May 08, 2020 02:29 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

There is a large number of posts who are talking about war and who should be held responsible for the war, and naturally a lot of them hold Krishna responsible for the war. All these questions converges at a single point — The war is absolutely bad and must be avoided at all cost.

So before identifying whom to blame or credit for war let us understand if the war is absolutely bad or not.

Are war absolutely bad?

  • Why do countries maintain a standing army?
  • If you don’t participate in a war, does that mean that even your enemies will not participate?
  • What should have we done in 1948 - Indo-Pak war — 1948, 1965, 1999 and Indo China war 1961? Krishna wasn’t involved. Did the war happen? Why? Was it possible to avoid the wars? What would have happened if we decided not to fight?
  • In 1971, India played an active role in Bangladesh formation. Good or Bad? Should we ignore the massacre for the fear of war?

Flashback to Mahabharat War — was it avoidable?

  • Pandava’s could have given up their claim to throne and even the five villages. Would that have stopped the war? If yes, at what cost?
    • Was it a noble alternative to war to let those rulers rule who rule by deceit and pervert mindset? Who are ready to molest women in public and burn their own relatives?
    • We can avoid the war with Pakistan by giving them Kashmir — Would that be advisable? Is it not a heavy price for peace? Can we ensure they would not demand entire India next?
  • Even if Pandavas had given up their claim, what was the guarantee that Duryodhana would still not have tried to kill Pandavas. When they were in exile, he tried to humiliate them.
  • What lesson would it have been for a ruler — run away from the welfare of the people, let tyrants rule so that there would be peace!

Mahabharat war without Krishna

  • War wasn’t due to Krishna, it was due to greet, jealously, treachery and ambition, and revenge. How long could that have been avoided.
  • Virat war happened without Krishna.
  • Gandharva war happened without Krishna.

Obviously those raising this question may not have read Gita, where Krishna persuaded Arjuna to fight. He did afer his last attempt at peace had failed. Dharma finds a way to win. It was through Krishna in some age and Chanakya in other. But when Adharma rises, an incarnation will come to suppress it.

This is very well captured by Ramdhari Singh Dinkar in his poetry Kurukshetra — as a dialog between Bhishma and Yudhishthir where Bhismha asks —

क्षमा, दया, तप, त्याग मनोबल सब का लिया सहारा।

पर बोलो तो नर व्याघ्र दुर्योधन तुमसे कब कहाँ और कैसे हारा?

O Yudhisthir you tried everything — forgiveness, compassion, dharma, sacrifice and moral character, but tell me when, where and how you managed to defeat the beastly Duryodhana?

And the most important question —

पापी कौन — मनुष्य से उसका न्याय चुराने वाला?

या न्याय खोजता विघ्न का शीश उड़ाने वाला?

Who is the sinner — One who snatches justice from some one or He who fights for the justice?
