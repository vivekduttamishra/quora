I have answered a very similar question in the recent times and that answers this question as well. Here is the answer — Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Why is Yudhisthira considered as Dharmaraj even though he did not intervene against the unethical act of Draupadi Cheerharan?

To sum it up —

  • Yudhisthir's fault lies in not differentiating between Dharma and protocol(Parampara) , and most people failed this test then. And even now. He failed Draupadi and everyone else because he followed prevalent custom of the era.
  • But he never acted out of selfish motive otherwise he would have —
    • Exposed Duryodhana when he poisoned Bheema.
    • Not accepted Khandavavan.
    • Not protected Duryodhana against Gandharva.
    • Not proposed 5 village instead of his rights.
  • He regretted his mistake and did his part of penance and punishment.
  • And there is no point in doing penance if it can't absolve you from your crimes.

Other than Drawpadi's humiliation, all other blunders of Yudhisthir was his adherence to Dharma.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
