In "Bhagavad Gita as it is" 1.40 and "Krsna" book Chapter-2, by Swami Prabhupāda, why is it that women are categorised as less intelligent?

📅 Sep 29, 2020 06:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I am not sure where you got this idea that women are categorised as less intelligent. Women has always enjoyed a respectable position in our society.

Let us first start by checking the verse you have mentioned — 1.40

कुलक्षये प्रणश्यन्ति कुलधर्मा: सनातना: |
धर्मे नष्टे कुलं कृत्स्नमधर्मोऽभिभवत्युत || 40||

—Gita Chapter 01

When a dynasty is destroyed, its traditions get vanquished, and the rest of the family becomes involved in irreligion.

I don’t see, this specific shloka talks anything specifically about women. It talks about the family and the risk of a dynasty getting destroyed.

But perhaps you mean the next shloka 1.41 —

अधर्माभिभवात्कृष्ण प्रदुष्यन्ति कुलस्त्रिय: |
स्त्रीषु दुष्टासु वार्ष्णेय जायते वर्णसङ्कर: || 41||

—Gita Chapter 01

With the preponderance of vice, O Krishna, the women of the family become immoral; and from the immorality of women, O descendent of Vrishni, unwanted progeny are born.

Now if you read this line carefully, women of the families are not blamed. Women become immoral when the family tradition is destroyed and vices are alreay prevelant due to the destruction of the family tradition.

So why have they discussed woman specifically here? Because, as I said, women enjoy high position in the society. There destruction leads to decline of entire society. See what happened when they tried to destroy Sita or Draupadi —

सङ्करो नरकायैव कुलघ्नानां कुलस्य च |
पतन्ति पितरो ह्येषां लुप्तपिण्डोदकक्रिया: || 42||

—Gita Chapter 01

An increase in unwanted children results in hellish life both for the family and for those who destroy the family. Deprived of the sacrificial offerings, the ancestors of such corrupt families also fall.

When woman of the family is corrupted, the family fall. Because woman are the cementing force of the family — the first guru. If the guru fails, the family fails.

So, I don’t know where in Gita, women have been degraded. As far as individual interpretation or perception is concerned, I can’t answer for them.
