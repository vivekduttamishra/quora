Why do people nowadays tell about BoriCE, and KMG instead of Vyas Bharath?

📅 Aug 01, 2020 07:57 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is a nice question. But first, we need to understand what is Vyas Bharath? Vyas Bharath was originally authored by Maharishi Ved Vyas. But Ved Vyas may not have done a mass printing. In those days the epics and the knowledge were propagated by word of mouth following Shruti-Smriti tradition. A proof of the same is within the epic itself. The entire epic is narrated by Vaipanyadas to King Janmeja.

Fast forward five thousand years. There are many narratives. All are Vyas narratives, none of them is penned by vyas; rather propagated by tradition. Many would pen it down over next centuries. One of the famous written works on Vyas Bharath is by Neelkantha Chatrudhara. This work was later translated in English by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG).

But there are many more narratives — Nepali, Devanagari, Tamil, Malyalam, Sanskrit, Bengali, Maithili… They all are Vyas Bharath, but with subtle differences in the narratives. Someone need to compilie them, and critically reason which of these are most likely the real Vyas Bharath. Thus a critical edition by Bhandarkar Research Oriental Institute — BORI Mahabharata — was conceived.

So as you can see, KMG and BORI are actually Vyas Bharath. One represents a popular ancient narration, the other critically analysed one — They are some of the best versions of Vyas Bharath currently available.
