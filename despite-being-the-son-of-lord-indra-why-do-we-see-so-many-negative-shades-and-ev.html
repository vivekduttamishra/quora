Despite being the son of Lord Indra, why do we see so many negative shades and evil qualities in Arjuna, whereas his brothers came next to godly people in the epic, Mahabharat?

📅 Jan 27, 2021 10:54 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

There is a general saying about shades — Everything looks yellow to a jaundiced eyes.

Are you sure it’s not your perception that is clouded that makes you see Arjuna as with shades of evil qualities? Because I don’t remember the great epic calling Arjuna as evil — I do remember Krishna referring to Arjuna as sinless (निष्पाप).

By the way what evil qualities did you find in Arjuna?

  • Is it, he tried to save is guru from a crocodiles jaws rather than deceiving him?
  • Is it that he kept mum, while evils tried to poison his brother?
  • Is it he rushed to help those evils in distress who had tormented his family and his wife?
  • Is it that he protected Matasya against evil design of Kuru Army, when he wasn’t supposed to?
  • Is it because he let Ashwatthama live, the same person who destroyed his next generation in the middle of the night?
  • Is it that he tried to protect even the family of those who conspired and killed his sons?

Here is a group of people who didn’t find him evil —

  • Ved Vyas — He never called Arjuna evil, but an incarnation of ancient sage Nara.
  • Krishna — He names Arjuna as an inseparable existence from himself and says —He who hates him, hates me. He who loves him, loves me.
  • Parshurama — Praises Arjuna for his valour. Doesn’t call him evil. Same Parshuram called another warrior of the time as deceitful.
  • Bhishma/Drona/Ashwatthama — Always praised Arjuna. Never once mentioned Arjuna as evil.
  • Dhritrashtra — Says he doesn’t know if any Pandava (including Arjuna) ever did anything un-righteous (evil)
  • Karna — Always considered Arjuna as role model and wanted to prove himself better than Arjuna. He never called Arjuna as an evil.
  • Lord Shiva — Graced Arjuna with the most potent weapon world has even known — Pashupatastra — making Arjuna the lone recipient of that weapon. Now Shiva won’t grant such a mighty weapon to an evil. would he?

So the question is which shade of perception are you carrying when you labeled Arjuna as an evil person challenging the opinion of everyone? Can you quote some direct references from Mahabharata that calls him evil?
