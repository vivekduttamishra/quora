Study revealed that Sri Rama was not the incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Then what qualities elevated Sri Rama to the status of the God?

📅 May 05, 2020 04:20 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

An interesting question —

  • Which study revealed Sri Ram was not an incarnation of Lord Vishnu?
  • Who confirmed it — Ram or Vishnu? Did they tweet it?

There are more than ample references that he was indeed an incarnation of Vishnu.

Ok! so let us consider what should be the right question —

What qualities of Ram, would have earned him the status of God, even if he were not an incarnation?

  • Ram was an adherent follower of Dharma, and he never gave an excuse
    • He could have justified being a king instead of going to exile.
    • He could have sided with Bali, the powerful, but chose Sugriv, because Bali was the symbol of Adharma
    • He crossed lanka, but still tried for peace and didn’t start the war immediately
  • His power didn’t corrupt him.
    • He vanquished Parshuram’s ego, but didn’t gain ego. He remained polite.
    • As a king, he sacrificed his own wife and family for the kingdom
      • He could have punished the washermen for talking against his wife
      • He could have ignored public opinion and let Sita suffer the torment in the palace.
      • He could have remarried.
    • He created a wonderful precedence for rulers — subject first.
  • He understood the true Dharma and was never confused with tradition.
    • He killed Bali without guilt or confusing the real dharma with the protocol of not striking at the back
    • He freed Ahalya from her curse, not bothering with whispers sorrounding.
  • He showed the way — how an ordinary man can become God
    • He didn’t differentiate between high and low — between rishis and nishad raj, Sabari and Jatayu.
    • For him, his pain was no greater than others. While searching for Sita he defined his priorities.
      • He tended to dying Jatayu
      • He helped Sugriv get justice
    • He knew how to mobilize an army out of ordinary people. A great leadership.
    • He had the luxury of impatience, yet he was patient enough to build a bridge to Lanka.

These are some qualities. All his qualities is difficult to encompass in a little space and difficult to comprehend by small wisdom of mine.

My answer is based on the story of Ramayan, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers Valmiki Ramayana and Ramcharit Manas apart from Ved Vyas retelling as Part of Mahabharat. I have studied the Sanskrit Hindi Version from Geeta Press
