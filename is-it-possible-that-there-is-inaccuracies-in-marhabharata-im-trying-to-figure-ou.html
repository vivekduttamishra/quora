There is no denying that, there would be several inaccuracies in retelling of everything. Consider the Indo-Pak war 1965. Can you tell who won it? Both Indian and Pakistan history narrates a different story. And we call it a history just above half a century. And Mahabahrata is over 5000 years ago.

That said, those inaccuracies lies in narration of minor incidents to glorify someone or condemn somone. The core of the event is undeniable as it is present in every single retelling as per ancient manuscripts — and your question belongs to this genre.

To answer your question — No there can be no reason for any confusion. I don’t really understand the reason of your confusion — Why should Vishnu not help Pandavas? Is it because of mass destruction?

Shiva, indeed blessed Pandavas, So did Vishnu. Why should that be a confusion? Gods help those who follow Dharama. And Vishnu is the protector of good — Paritranaya Shadunama. And to protect good you need to destroy the evil — Vinaschyash Dushkritam.

Can you suggest any way Vishnu can protect without actually destroying the evils? Why else Vishnu has the Chakra and Gada in his possessation? Why did Vishu incarnate as Ram, if not to destroy the evil Ravan?

I don’t see any reason for contradiction or confusion. And at least in the main part of the epic, there are no inconsistencies or inaccuracies.
