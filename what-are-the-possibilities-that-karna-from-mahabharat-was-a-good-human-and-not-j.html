What are the possibilities that Karna from Mahabharat was a good human and not just exaggerated in books to improve his image (like Radheya, Mrityunjay) ?

📅 May 19, 2020 04:41 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Yes, it is quite possible if —

  • Ved Vyas knew less of Mahabharat than the learned author of Mrityunjay — Vyasji mentions him as the trunk of the evil tree.
  • Parshuram, the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu, cursed him simply because he was not a brahmin. Same Parshuram taught Bhisma, another non Brahim.
  • All the old Sanskrit tales are completely wrong especially when translating the incident related to just one person. And if those texts are wrong, I wonder what is the source narratives of books like Mrityunjay other than wild imagination?
  • Krishna too was biased against Dharma.
  • New facts are being unearthed everyday as with each passing day character and incident related to Karna is drifting more and more away from Vyas recitation —
    • BR Chopra showed Karna failing at Gurudakshina war, star plus decided that he never participated in Gurudakshina war because Drona didn't allow him. Why? Based on another narrative that Drona never taught him. Vyasji mentions Drona being Karna's guru in 3 separate Parvas —Adi , Van and Shanti.
    • BRChopra showed Karna failed Gandharvas war because he was drunk. As if it wasn't fake enough, Star Mahabharat censured the war itself.
    • Same goes for Virat war.
    • By the time we reached Surya Putra Karna, Krishna became Karna's charioteer. Real great progress.
  • Tomorrow we may hear Karna killed evil duo Krishna and Arjuna.

Its really tormenting to see that to glorify one evil, we are ready to distort our own cultural heritage, our thousands of years of civilization. Is this the legacy we want to leave for our next generation that they stop believing Dharma wins? What's the value of one person, if the essense of epic itself is lost?
