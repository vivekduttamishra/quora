If karn never defeated Arjun instead he was defeated by many warriors including Arjun, then why Krishna always warned Arjun that karn has the potential to change the scenario of war, he has potential to even defeat Arjun ?

📅 May 04, 2021 01:05 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

First thing first — there is no room for any ‘If’. Arjuna defeated Karna in every battle. Again and Again.

Second, Krishna never said Karna had potential to defeat Arjuna. In fact he categorically said —

Krishna laughed and spoke these supreme words to Partha. ‘The sun may fall down from its place. The earth may shatter into many fragments. The fire may become cold. But Karna will not be able to kill Dhananjaya.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4736-4738).

But he indeed cautioned Arjuna on multiple occasion against Karna. And he said these words which are often misquoted as Karna’s greatness —

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain. Had Karna possessed the spear in his hand, no man in the world would have been able to stand before him. He would have been stationed like Kartikeya in the battle. It is through good fortune that his armour has gone. It is through good fortune that his earrings have been robbed. It is through good fortune that the invincible spear has been used up on Ghatotkacha. Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals. Vasava, Kubera, Varuna, lord of the waters, and Yama would not have ventured against Karna in an encounter. Had that bull among men possessed those, you with your Gandiva and I with my sudarshana chakra would not have had the capacity to defeat him in a battle.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6415-6421).

And here lies the true answer of this question. Krishna wasn’t worried of Karna’s personal valor. He was worried about the divine armour, ear-rignt and Vasvi Shakti. He declares without those divine artifects know that Karna has already been slain!

Another point to note is

There is a difference between theory and practical

Karna was defeated in Draupadi’s swyamvara battle and again by Gandharvas while he still had his Kavacha and Kundala.

Then, why did Krishna — Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals ?

This is the difference between theory and practical. The divine armors can protect Karna from death. And if you have a guaranteed protection from death, theoretically you could defeat anyone.

But armors don’t kill your enemies. They don’t win the battles for you. They protect you from physical injuries, they can’t make you brave. They can’t protect your from getting scared. They can’t protect you from fleeing the battle.

Karna’s armor had made him technically invincible but he still failed again and again. That was the practical realty.

Why was Krishna worried about Karna’s divine artifacts? Were there superior to that Arjuna possessed?

A weapon of mass destruction is safer in the hands of a brave but is more scary if they are in the hands of a coward. Because you never know, when that person out of fear misuse that weapon. USA is worried of nuclear weapons in the hand of Pakistan. Does Pakistan have greater nuclear weapon than the USA? No. But chances of Pakistan using it is higher and then other nuclear powered country would also unleash that weapon leading to the devastation of entire planet.

No replace Pakistan with Karna. He had a powerful weapon in his hand and he was a shaky person who may easily get tempted to misuse the weapon to ensure his victory. And that would make even a powerful person like Krishna worried.

Take instance of Bhargavastra. It was fired on ordinary soldiers. It was an irresponsible act! Could it have been countered? Of course, with higher weapons. But that would be another irresponsible act. Krishna avoided that by steering Arjuna away. But that should give you a clear picture.

Take another example — Vasvi Shakti. It couldn’t have been fired unless you use up all your divine weapons unsuccessfully. That is a clear proof that Karna used all his divine weapons against Ghatotkatch.

Now Krishna knew Karna’s true nature. He knew he won’t be able to resist his temptation to use divine weapon against Ghatotkatch. And Krishna was right. He tricked Karna in loosing the most potent of his weapon.

The source of worry for Krishna was not his valor, but his divine artifacts and more important his reckless nature. He never said Karna as a warrior can defeat Arjuna!

You need to understand Krishna to understand his statements

Krishna’s statements often contradicted the ground reality. You must understand Krishna didn’t always dwelt in trivial facts people chose to call truth. He often spoke as a motivator and a psychologist ignoring the insignificant facts. He cared for Dharma more than his ego —

  • He was known for his lies as a child — we call it Krishna Leela!
  • He was ready to be called Ranchod
  • He resolved he wont take up arms at Kurukshetra, nevertheless, he rushed to kill Bhishma.
  • He tells Arjuna, Duryodhana is king of all world
  • He also tells Arjuna, Karna is going to kill Yudhishthira, although he knew Karna would do no such things
  • He tells Yudhisthira that Shalaya is more powerful than Bhishma, Karna and Arjuna and only Yudhishthira can kill him. How logical is that? Well, it wasn’t meant to be logical; it was meant to boost Yudhishtir’s morale.
  • He knew Karna’s defeat during Druapadi’s swyamvara battle and again infamous defeat against Gandharva’s. But he still says Karna couldn’t be defeated as long he had his armor.

If you see, you would find Krishna has much deeper meaning and objective in his statements — to motivate, to urge for action and to be careful.

Krishna didn’t want Arjuna to be relaxed. He wanted him to be careful.
