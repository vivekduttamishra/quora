Short Answer: Karna failed to lift the bow in Draupadi’s swaymvara

Please refer to my detailed answer

I will take this opportunity to bust common myths propagated by Karna fan’s in favor of that evil.

Distortion#1 — Karna had not come to participate in Draupadi’s swyamvar

WRONG. Karna was explicitly named by Dhristduymna. I will use the same statement of Dhirsthduymna that they often use to misquote —

‘Dhrishtadyumna said, “Duryodhana, Durvisaha, Durmukha, Dushpradharshana, Vivimshati, Vikarna, Saha, Duhshasana, Sama, Yuyutsu, Vatavega, Bhimavegadhara, Ugrayudha, Balaki, Kanakayu, Virochana, Sukundula, Chitrasena, Suvarcha, Kanakadhvaja, Nandaka, Bahushali, Kunduja and Vikata—these and others are the immensely powerful sons of Dhritarashtra. These warriors have come with Karna for your hand.

—BORI

Those hell bend on distortion try to convey, Karna had just accompanied Kauravas and didn’t intend to participate. This is stupid on so many grounds —

  • If two people have come together doesn’t mean only one intends to participate. There is NOT A SINGLE MENTION that Karna didn’t intend to participate in the swyamver.
  • There is a clear mention of Karna’s failure in BORI, we shall discuss it later.

MOST IMPORTANT: Prof Suthankar, the chief editor of BORI clearly mentioned that Karna was invited, while explaining why Draupadi couldn’t have denied opportunity to Karna. Now this is hilarious to refer to the work of someone and disregard his own version.

Distortion#2: Drishtadyumna clearly tells that only warriors of noble birth can participate and win:

First let us see what Dhristdyumna said —

‘O ruler of the earth! When everything was quiet, Dhrishtadyumna went to the centre of the arena and spoke, in a thundering voice, words that were deep with meaning and sweet. “O assembled kings! Hear that this is the bow, that is the target and these are the arrows. With these five arrows, shoot the target through the hole in the centre of the machine. I truly say that the handsome and strong one, born in a noble family, who accomplishes this great feat today, will obtain as his wife my sister Krishna

—BORI

Here the highlighted text represents Dhristdyumna’s wishes and not a binding term of the swyamver. For example, just imagine —

  • Is there a tangible yardstick to measure handsome? Who would decide who is handsome?
  • Can you reject someone because you think he is not handsome, without inviting the ire of other rulers?

So even if Karna was not handsome (as their fans like to believe) Karna couldn’t have been denied an entry.

As far as noble birth is concerned, let me quote two statements from BORI.

Druapada said —

“Where has Krishna gone? Who has taken her away? Is it a Shudra or one of low birth? Has a Vaishya who pays taxes placed his feet on my head? Has a garland been thrown away on a cremation ground? O son! Or is it a foremost man from our own varna, or is it one from a higher varna? Or has a lower being placed his foot on my head and defiled Krishna?

—BORI

Drupada wasn’t sure about Arjuna’s noble lineage and yet he couldn’t stop him from participation. Why was he allowed? Was his noble lineage ascertained? How could someone have denied Karna?

Yudhishthira

Drupad set a price and this brave one has won her in accordance with that. Therefore, no questions can be asked about his varna, action, intention, means of living, lineage or gotra.

—BORI

IMPORTANT: Prof Suthankar mentions both these points in their preface to highlight the fact that Karna couldn’t have been denied a participation.

Distortion#3: Karna who failed was some other Kshatriya Karna and not o the great evil Karna.

W R O N G

Here is what BORI says —

यत्कर्णशल्यप्रमुखैः पार्थिवैर्लोकविश्रुतैः |
नानतं बलवद्भिर्हि धनुर्वेदपरायणैः ||४||

When the great warriors like Karna and Shalya and other great Kings, who are well known for their strength and archery, couldn’t string the bow, how could a weakling Brahmana, with no knowledge of weapons, succeed?

Now here is the main confusion — Bibek Debroy translated “पार्थिवै” as Kshatriya. The word also translates to “King”. What they fail to realize is —

  • The original BORI Sanskrit verse doesn’t use the word “Kshatriya” but पार्थिवै
  • Bibek Debroy is a translator of BORI and his work is not BORI itself.
  • BORI editor Prof Suthankar clearly mentioned Karna’s participation and rejection
  • There is no other King named Karna who have been named as a participant in the swayver.
  • There was no Karna existing at that point who was so famous to have been named explictly with Shalya and before other warriors like Duryodhana.

IMPORTANT : Apart from BORI — KMG, Neelkantha, southern recitation, sharda edition and all others mentions Karna’s failure. Inteestingly Neelkantha and KMG (English translation of Neelkantha) are the most popular version that includes the part that Draupadi rejected Karna. And both contains Karna’s failure

Distortion#4 — Krishna is mentioned as a participant, does that mean he failed too.

A participant always have option if they want to participate or not. Karna’s name is mentioned among those failed. Important to note is Krishna name is not explicitly mentioned among those who failed.

Distortion#5 — More than 20 authentic source mention Draupadi rejected Karna.

Let me once again quote prof Vishnu Suthankar here —

Unfortunately, this melo­dramatic interlude, to judge by the documentary evidence, appears to be the handiwork of a very late Vyāsaīd, as it is found only in K * Ñ2 Dn D2.4. c, that is, one manuscript of the K group, one Nepali manuscript, and three composite Devanāgarī manuscripts, besides the Nīlakaṇṭha version! And in such cases we must give importance to the chief editor of BORI the unabridged version rather than the words of the translators.

If Karna didn’t participate the swaymver, how did Draupadi reject her? If she rejected her, how was he not allowed to do so?
