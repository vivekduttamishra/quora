How did barbarik become undefeatable in Mahabharata?

📅 Jul 26, 2020 09:44 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Barbarik became undefeatable warrior mostly because of fan fictions. Vyas Mahabharata doesn't have the story of Barbarik, although it is in a few Puranas.

But assuming Barbarik to be a real warrior (which he was not)

Question is what made him undefeatable?

Let us check on facts —

  • Did he win any battle?
  • Had he defeated any warrior of repute?
  • Was his capability endorsed by any guru or other warrior?

No. We just have Barabrik's word for it. We can call it excellent marketing and self projection. Period.

Next, his claim to warriorship is based on a divine weapon. Now what was he without that divine weapon? A single weapon doesn't make a warrior.

In Mahabharata those weapons had ways to diffuse. Ashwathama's Narayanastra did no harm, Krishna made many such weapons ineffective. What makes us think that his weapon was not infallible, when in fact it couldn't destroy one leaf protected under Krishna's foot?

So, honestly speaking what made Barbarik powerful was a projection and a great marketing. There is no real achievement to his credit, no sense of Dharma or justice or loyalty, just brute show of strength. No wonder Krishna removed him.

Ironically he is not the only warrior in Mahabharata who is hailed great based on such projection.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
