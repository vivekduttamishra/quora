Would there be any difference in Mahabharata if Bhishma Pitamah spoke against Duryodhana and Karna in the Dyut Sabha?

📅 Nov 18, 2021 03:12 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is an interesting question, which I shall try to discuss in three section. The first section itself answer this question objectively but not comprehensively, so I would encourage readers to read the complete answer.

1. If Bhishma Pitamah spoke against Duryodhana and Karna in the Dyut Sabha?

I don’t think much would have changed, had Bhishma spoken against Duryodhana or Karna. Dhirtrashtra would have overridden them to carry out the wishes of his son. And this is not a mere assumption. There are sufficient reference to collaborate this fact in the epic itself. It happened just before Anydyuta —

Vaishampayana said, ‘Then Drona, Somadatta, the maharatha Bahlika, Vidura, Drona’s son,6 the valorous son of the vaishya,7 Bhurishrava, Shantanu’s son8 and maharatha Vikarna collectively said, “Don’t have the gamble. Let there be peace.” But Dhritarashtra loved his son and ignored the desires of these well-wishers, all of whom could foresee the consequences. He summoned the Pandavas.

— Anudyuta

Now, as we know, this incident takes place after the fiasco of first gamble, where Bhimasena had already taken an oath to destroy all Kauravas. Everyone was scared. And despite of such a terrible oath, Dhritrashtra overrides Bhishma in favor of his son. Now there was no such foreboding immediately after the initial winning of baits. Kauravas had won squarely and Dhritrashtra, himself was too happy with this victory. Why would he have listened to Bhishma then, when he didn’t listen to him later?

No, I daresay, not much have changed. But other than explicit reference, we must understand the subtle reason as to why this attempt would have failed.

2. Dyut wasn’t the main problem, it was the symptom of a much larger problem

To understand this idea, we must recall three important facts —

  • While everyone debated on technicality of weather Yudhishthira, after losing himself in gamble could have baited Draupadi or not, no one debated if baiting itself was proper or not. Why?
  • Vidura, who was not only learned but wiser among the lot, could feel the approaching disaster and tried hardest to dispel the impending disaster. But he too debated if Draupadi’s baiting of technically correct or not. No one debated on baiting of a human in gambling.
  • Bhishma wasn’t quite. He did speak. But it wasn’t in support of Draupadi. It wasn’t in support of Duryodhana either. He appeared to be have been genuinely confused about what is Dharma? He looked towards Yudhishira, as a subject matter expert, for an answer, that didn’t exist.

Now we must understand Bhishma and Vidura were neither evils like Duryodhana, Karna or Shakuni nor can be considered either as a coward, a fool or a liar. So, why did Bhishma say that ways of Dharma is subtle?

The definition of Dharma is not subtle — Sarve Bhawantu Sukhinaah — Good of everyone.

Honestly the seeds of Mahabharata doesn’t exist in the silence of the court, rather it exists in the spoken words of Bhishma — Ways of Dharma is subtle.

Not only Bhishma, the entire society was confused between Dharma and Paramapara (tradition). They had forgotten the distinction, that led to the war. Remember what Krishna said in Gita — The divine knowledge that he originally imparted to Surya and that was passed to Manu and Ikshavaku is lost over a period of time. And in those times lord incarnates again.

Remember what is the most important shloka of Gita —

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥४-७॥

परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम् ।
धर्मसंस्थापनार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे ॥४-८॥

The great gambling wasn’t the main problem, it was the symptom of a greater problem. Society had been corrupted, the understanding of Dharma was distorted and the society of Aryans had taken non-aryan demonic form where a brother can poison another for wealth, and disciple can back stab their teacher for the same sake of power and fame. There is no mention of any demon, deceiving their guru in the entire epic literature. The society needed a major course correction.

3. Had Bhishma spoken against…

What would have certainly changed the course of history was Bhishma opposing Yudhishthira.

You see, Duryodhana and Karna were petty criminals of no great significance. Such criminals, sometimes on escaping justice fell prey to mobs and other times executed by personal vendetta. An example would be Bhimasena’s oath and execution of all Kauravas or Udhama Singh’s execution of Michael O'Dwyer (not Brigadier General R.E.H. Dyer). Common people like us often rejoice such vengeance and regard executors with great reverence. But what we generally miss, is this is a moment to wait and ponder — Is such vengeance deliver or justice? Or failure of justice? The fact that it came down to personal vendetta is a clear proof of not only the fact that justice failed, but also the sorry fact that we lost hope in such a justice.

But Yudhishthira would have been a different story. He was like the book of constitution people looked forward to. He was the subject matter expert. He was expected to know the Dharma. In the court room Karna shouted many slogans about what is shastra and what does God say (Dharma), but did anyone cared about what he said? On the other hand not only Bhishma but even Duryodhana and Shakuni waited for Yudhishira’s opinion on Dharma.

That day, had Bhishma (or Vidura) challenged Yudhishira, it would have made all the difference in the world. It would have been an attempt to challenge the written constitution with contemporary relevance. It would have resulted in amendment of laws and a ray of hope for the society.

Had Bhishma challenged Yudhishthira and emphasized that no one has any right to stake others like property, things would have been different. Had Bhishma challenged Yudhishthira's authority rather than endorsing it, it would have underlined the fact that the essence of justice is greater than any individual, society may have taken a course very different from Kuruskhetra.

Had Bhishma announced that the baiting was void ab initio, he would have ushered the society into another era.

But as we know, that was not to be. The society didn’t understan void ab initio . They didn’t understand the essence of justice. They forgot the most fundamental traits of Dharma.
