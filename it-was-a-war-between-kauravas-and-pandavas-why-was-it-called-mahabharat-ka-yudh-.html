WHY WAS KURUKSHETRA BATTLE CALLED MAHABHARATA?

This is actually a much easier answer. I expected a more important question, that I would ask and answer later in this post.

Whatever may be the actual reason, and that is by no means insignificant or irrelevant, the battle involved almost all the kingdom and warriors of the era. Even those few who didn’t enter the battle directly couldn’t remain un-affected by the battle.

Imagine if there was no feud between Kauravas and Pandavas. Now think what would have happened to —

  • Amba’s vengeance to Bhishma that occured many years before the birth of even the fathers of Kaurava and Pandavas?
  • Drupad’s vengeance to Drona which predates the rivarly of Kaurava and Pandava?
  • Satyaki and Bhurisrava’s fued that predates the birth of Kaurava and Pandavas?
  • Do you think machineries like Trigartha would have stopped pondering kingdom’s like Matasya?

If you look into the issue, Kaurava and Pandava may have been the immediate cause, the war was inevitable even without them.

And a battle that was fought at such a large scale and that changed the history of the entire region — the only known civilization of the time, can we really call it not great? Hence Mahabharata!

That said, it may surprise you, the original name of the epic was Jaya (victory) and not Mahabharata. Jaya later became Bharata, which over a period of time became Mahabharata. Maha or great calls epic as great and not the war as great.

But as I said, this question isn’t that important as —

WHY IS THE EPIC BATTLE OF KURUKSHETRA CALLED — DHARMAYUDHA

The story of the epic battle of Kurukshetra has been told and retold for ages. Millennia of dust, fables, imaginations — and the epic itself is lost. What remained is the story of a family feud and ambition of Kauravas and Pandavas. But why, then, was this an epic war? Why was it a Dharmayudha? Why entire Aryavart plunged into this first real world-war? Why the echo of this ancient war still resonates after all those centuries?

In true sense, Mahabharata was the first world-war among known extent of civilization and it can’t be brushed aside as a family feud. So the question is what was the true reason?

The message is subtle but clear as we go through many unanswered questions of Mahabharata —

  • Why Ved Vyas refers Bhishma as wise and virtuous when he was trapped in his own oath?
  • What was the true reason of Drona and Drupada feud?
  • Why did entire court of Hastinapur mum as so called Dharmaraja and Dhritrashtra gambled baiting Kingdom and families? Yes, I am calling the gambling between Yudhishthira and Dhritrahstra, and not between Duryodhana or Shakuni.
  • Why, in a court full of scholars, Vikarna’s voice of reason was suppressed by blatant distortions of Dharma by a lustrous man?

And most important —

Why Krishna, incarnated to establish Dharma, persuaded Arjuna to take up arms — Wasn’t Arjuna doing a great favor to humanity?

All these and many more question makes me think that we have The Lost Epic. And that prompted me to get on a journey to re-discover the truth which lay hidden in the ancient wisdom but overshadowed by those not so relevant issues that makes people wonder — Why was it a Mahabharata battle or a Dharmayudha!
