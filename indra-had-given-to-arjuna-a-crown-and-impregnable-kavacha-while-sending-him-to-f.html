Indra had given to Arjuna a crown and impregnable kavacha while sending him to fight with Nivathakavachas. Did Arjuna use them in the battle of Kurukshetra?

📅 Sep 15, 2020 03:48 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi, thanks for the A2A. I don't think this Kavacha finds a mention in Kurukshetra. Neither Pandavas or Kauravas make any reference about it.

And this is an important observation. Whenever Arjuna has been referred by admirers or foe, they talk about him as a warrior. While he is often referred as wielder of Gandeeva, his personal capacity has been the focus. It is no where referred that he is invsnquishable because of some Kavach or gandeev or Pashupatastra —

  • In Swayamvara he doesn't use gandiva.
  • His Kavach is not mentioned anywhere.
  • He never used Pashupatastra.

Arjuna is not a warrior because of a particular artifect. He is a warrior in his own capacity. He doesn't seek to defeat enemies because he has a particular weapon. And that makes him a great warrior who extracts praise even from worst enemies.
