Looks like this question is twisted out of my previous answer here —

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Why did Duryodhana initiate war on Matsya kingdom on the northern front one day after Susarma's attack on southern front? What did he really want to achieve?

However it defies all logic. Let me point out the illogics in this question. First let us check

Arjuna — Trigarth clash

  • How many days did entire Trigarth force could hold Arjuna? Do you recall any single day? Is there any occasion when they actually won over Arjuna?
  • What was the end result of Trigarth — Arjuna clash? In how many days?
  • How many Trigarth warrior fought against a lone Arjuna? What was the end result?

Do you know hundreds of hyenas may attack a lion, and hope to win based on their numerically superior strength; they rarely win. Never with two lions. And even if they win, they don't become mightier.

Keetack Trigarth battle

Trigarth repeatedly lost to Matsya Army headed by Keetack. But is there any mention of —

  • Keetack fighting alone against the entire Army?
  • How many days those war lasted?

Can you draw such baseless conclusion without data?

How powerful was Keechak?

He was powerful enough to defeat Trigarth army but not powerful enough to face a lone Bheemasena. So the inequation looks like

Bheema > Keechak > (Trigarth Army)

Now let us consider another equation.

How powerful was Trigarth?

Trigarth were incapable of defeating Matasya even without Hastinapur power, that's why they sought Hastinapur help.

Matsya - Keechak > Trigarth
Kauravas > Trigarth

Now Kauravas we're defeated by a lone Arjuna. So inequation becomes

Arjuna > Kauravas > Trigarth 
Bheema > Keechak  > Trigarth

There is no data point to compare Arjuna and Keechak, other than the fact that they both were more powerful than Trigarth and two Pandava brothers together more powerful than Trigarth and Kauravas combined.

Arjuna + Bheema > Kauravas + Trigarth + Keechak

Hope this answers the illogical quest. Illogical because —

Raw powers don't make a warrior; the code, ethics and the purpose does.

— Vivek Dutta Mishra

What's the point in proving if Keechak or Trugarth were powerful? Question should be, were they good people? As I see Trigarth were mechinary and looters and Keechak was an abusive devil. Is there a point in proving either of these a worthy warrior, that they were not? Think about it.
