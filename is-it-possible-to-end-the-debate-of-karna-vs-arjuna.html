Is it possible to end the debate of karna vs Arjuna?

📅 Jul 20, 2021 08:33 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Must the debate end?

Debate has been an ancient and important aspect for propagation of knowledge. There were referred as Shastrath. Remember the famous historic debate between Adi Guru Shankaracharya and Mandan Mishra.

Another even more famous debate is Gita.

The only thing missing in our contemporary debate is decency and honesty. People, after realizing that they have lost the debate either start using abusive language or start distorting the core essence of the epic.

It is only through a debate the truth may be preserved else it would be lost like many legacies under the debris of fables and fabrications. It is happening right now!

But if some of us are still inclined to explore the scenario for ending a debate, here are some important aspect to consider.


WHAT WOULD BE AN IDEAL SCENARIO TO END THE DEBATE?

Ideal scenario would be people see the truth. The read the epic and realize that the core values of the epic and stop distorting —

  • Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitaah — It was a fight for the righteousness, where Krishna himself guided one side towards the victory. There must be a reason. Right?
  • Triumph of Dharma over Adharma — One of the most important shlokas of Gita is where Krishna assures the world that whenever there rampant expansion of the evils and decline of Dharma, to destroy the evil and to protect the good, He himself shall incarnate.

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

To protect the good (Sadhu), to destroy the evil and to reestablish the Dharma, I would incarnate in every era!

  • And in that incarnation Lord protected the good and destroyed the evil.

Do we still need more proof of good and evil?


WHAT IS THE CONTEMPORARY DEBATE ABOUT?

The contemporary debate is not between good and evil, it’s between the epic and the fables. It seeds into the fact that few adore and idolize those who are explicitly marked as evil in the epic and for them to win the only option is to distort and question in the integrity of the epic itself.

For them, Mahabharata is not an epic, rather a story of gray characters. To win the debate they are ready to degrade the great epic to a story like fifty shades of gray. And naturally to do that they would go on to portray Krishna as a conniving man and kill the core spirit of the core value of the epic —

यतो धर्मस्य ततो जय:

Where there is Dharma, there is victory

Tell me, should the debate end here?


WHAT IS KRISHNA’S VIEW?

Interestingly this is not a new question. Arjuna too wanted to end the feud at any cost — not just forgoing their rightful claim to their kingdom, but also his respect and honor. He considered peace more important than war, lives more precious than victory.

Krishna, however, steered him towards war. Why? Was Krishna a war monger? It has and continue to vex a lot of peace seekers and philosophers around the world, as like Arjuna, they too miss the two important elements of Krishna’s philosophy —

Peace without justice is just an illusion.

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

and

Dharma is more important the individual lives

— The essence of Gita

Arjuna still sought for peace. And then Krishna taught him one of the ultimate lessons of life, one with which I would like to end my answer —

Karmanye vadhikaraste Ma Phaleshu Kadachana,
Ma Karmaphalaheturbhurma Te Sangostvakarmani

— Bhagwat Gita Chapter 2, Verse 47

We have right (and duty) to do our duty, but we have no right (or control) over our action. So we should do our duty without being motivated (or demotivated) by fruit (consequences) of those actions.

And my duty is to keep upholding the true spirit of the epic without being worried about consequences.

What do you think?
