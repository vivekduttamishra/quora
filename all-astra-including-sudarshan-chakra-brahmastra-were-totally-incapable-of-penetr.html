All astra including Sudarshan chakra, Brahmastra were totally INCAPABLE of penetrating armour ofKarna.Howcould he cut his armour with normal knife?

📅 Aug 30, 2020 11:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Karna's armour were creation of Surya, and to think, that he created something so capable that even superior Gods would fail to destroy is illogical — that makes the second part of the question logical.

One of the answers to this question indicates that Karna had to peal of his skin to remove the armour from his body. This is true as per Ved Vyasa. But that itself proves armour was penetrable — how else his dagger or sword reached beneath the armour to cut the skin or body? This proves —

  1. The armour was stuck to the skin and had to be cut off, no doubt causing great pain to Karna.
  2. It was penetrable, simply because it was penetrated.
  3. If Karna could do it, anyone else could do it.

But Krishna said so…

Oh! Yes. But then you don't understand Krishna. He wasn't some egoistic fool, but a great teacher. He is Ranchod — he would take a step back to ensure victory. He is not bound by mortal words — didn't he attacked Bhisma after promising not to participate in war? He taught Arjuna the knowledge of Karmayoga. But how did he start? If you won't fight people would mock you and bring disgrace to you. Is it niskam karma? No. Krishna knows best what words would prove more effective on Arjuna and he would say what is effective rather than adhering to meaningless truth.

So once armour was gone, there is no harm in glorifying it and motivate Arjuna to out perform.

Is there a proof, that his armour was penetrable?

There are many. Let's take an instance of Drawpadi's Swayamvara —

ततोऽर्जुनः प्रत्यविध्यदापतन्तं त्रिभिः शरैः |
कर्णं वैकर्तनं धीमान्विकृष्य बलवद्धनुः ||९||
तेषां शराणां वेगेन शितानां तिग्मतेजसाम् |
विमुह्यमानो राधेयो यत्नात्तमनुधावति ||१०||

— Adi Parva, Chapter 181, Shloka 9-10

‘Seeing Vaikartana Karna rush towards him, the wise Arjuna stretched his mighty bow and pierced him with three arrows. The impact of these sharp arrows stunned Radheya, and he approached with greater circumspection. Then those two invincible warriors fought violently, each eager to vanquish the other. Such was the speed that they became invisible.

— Bibek Debroy

At this point in time, Karna had his armour and yet Arjuna's arrow pierced him.

Later Karna lost to Gandharvas. He still had his armour. He lost to Bheema in Rajsue battle. He still had his Armour?

But armour doesn't make one a warrior — courage does. And don't forget Armour provided no protection against beheading. Remind me, how did he die?
