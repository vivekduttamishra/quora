Few will disagree that Arjuna is the most evil character ofMahabharat.Myquestion is who is the second most evil character of Mahabharat after Arjuna?

📅 Jan 09, 2021 10:24 PM PST  |  🌐 English

People would keep changing the question generate the hatred for Arjuna or someone else. I am answering for the original question in hope it can quench the hatred —

Few will disagree that Arjuna is the most evil character of Mahabharat. My question is who is the second most evil character of Mahabharat after Arjuna?

Oh! I am really finding it difficult to find the second most evil character of Mahabharata if Arjuna was the most evil. To begin with, anyone who cherishes an evil and finds him good or virtuous should be an evil. That makes everyone except the Kaurva brothers, Shakuni and Karna an evil.

So let us first make a list of everyone who either supported evil Arjuna or called him virtous —

Who disagreed Arjuna was evil, let alone in superlative terms?

Ved Vyas — the original narrator

He mentionins him as the incarnate of sage Nara and starts the epic by a salutation to Nara. He further mentions Arjuna as the trunk of a tree of righteousness. Then he keeps referring Arjuna and other Pandavas as righteous and virtous. I think, someone who calls an evil and virtous may himself be an evil. So Should be aware the position of second greatest evil.

Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama, Kripacharya

Bhishma, although he was the commander of Kaurava’s Army, considered Arjuna great and virtuous.

Drona, Kripacharya and Ashwatthama referred Pandavas and especially Arjuna virtuous and righteous at multiple occassions.

Since these people continously considered Arjuna as not evil, they must also be considered as evil.

Parshuram

Praised Arjuna in superlative term. The same Parshuram who is said to have cursed another warrior of the time. He must be an evil to have praised Arjuna.

Hanuman

Hanuman graced Arjuna by agreeing to sit on his flag. So the great Hanuman must have turned into an evil to consider Arjuna as virtuous enough to support him.

Lord Shiva

Graced Arjuna with blessing and the most powerful weapon ever handled by a human — Pashupatastra. He also opened the door for Arjuna to ascend to heaven. He himself praised Arjuna, blessed him and empowered him. He too must be considered for the position of second greatest evil.

But hold on! Now I think, we have the strongest contender for the post of second greatest evil if not the most evil (a position already reserved for Arjuna)

Krishna

Not only, he supported Arjuna at everytime and also explicitly called him as the trunk of the righteous tree, and drove his chariot, he called Arjuna as an inseparable part of him. This is what Krishna said explicitly —

‘Janardana then spoke to Partha, “You are mine and I am yours. All that is mine is yours too. He who hates you also hates me. He who follows you also follows me. O invincible one! You are Nara and I am Hari Narayana. We are the rishis Nara and Narayana, born from that world in this world. O Partha! O descendant of the Bharata lineage! You are no other than I. I am no other than you. O bull among the Bharata lineage! No one can know any difference that exists between us.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 4341-4345).

And he keeps repeating the idea again and again.

The Pandavas are established in dharma. Who can speak against them for any reason? He who hates them hates me. He who follows them, follows me. Know that I am immersed like a single soul in the Pandavas, who act in accordance with dharma. He who follows desire and anger and, because of his delusion, hates those who possess qualities is known to be the worst among men.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 5914-5916).

And yet again

Tomorrow, the gods, the gandharvas, the pishachas, the serpents, the rakshasas and all the worlds will know me as Savyasachi’s well-wisher. He who hates him, hates me too. He who follows him, follows me too. Use your intelligence to comprehend that Arjuna is half of my body.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 1316-1318).

At one point of time Arjuna got weaker. He refused to fight. At that point of time Krishna motivated him to fight. And in his attempt to motivate Arjuna he says that he incarnated to establish Dharma —

Yada yada hi dharmasya glanirbhavati bharata
Abhythanamadharmasya tadatmanam srijamyaham

Paritranaya sadhunang vinashay cha dushkritam
Dharmasangsthapanarthay sambhabami yuge yuge

—Gita Chapter 4

So, I think, the second most evil person should be Krishna if Arjuna was the most evil person.

But there is a big IF here.

Some might be tempted to ask the rationale for the question — what made them hate Krishna? Because Krishna said — He who hates Arjuna, hates me. Shiva, Hanuman, Parshurama everyone supported Arjuna.

But this question is meaningless as Krishna himself answered this question, as if and certainly he knew that supporters of Kauaravas won’t die anytime soon. I would conclude my answer by re-quoting how Krishna answered this very question —

He who follows desire and anger and, because of his delusion, hates those who possess qualities is known to be the worst among men.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 5914-5916).

I concur the feeling of Krishna on this subject
