Did Shakuni really cheat Yudhistir in the DICE game? If yes or no, how can you prove it?

📅 Dec 22, 2020 08:07 AM PST  |  🌐 English

I am not sure where we are getting so many great fans of the evils these days. The question is as ridiculous as some answers here that claims, there was no cheating mentioned in the original epic.

Short Answer — Shakuni beyond doubt cheated in the dice game and the same is clearly mentioned on multiple occasions in the entire epic.

Let us move to the references one by one. But let us first summaries what we are going to prove beyond reasonable doubt.

  1. Shakuni was no doubt a skilled gambler and he knew Yudhishthir is a noob and easy to defeat.
  2. Shakuni had some magical powers over the dice.
  3. Shakuni used deceit to win the games.

Stage 1 Shakuni Plans the dice game

Shakuni Said

‘Shakuni said, “Kunti’s son loves to gamble with dice, but does not know how to play. If challenged to play, that Indra among kings will not be able to refuse. I am skilled in gambling with dice, there is no one on earth, or in the three worlds, who is my equal. Challenge Kunti’s son to a game of dice. O king! O bull among men! With my skill in dice,21 there is no doubt that I will win for you the kingdom and the blazing prosperity. O Duryodhana! Tell the king22 all this. And if your father permits, there is no doubt that I will vanquish him.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 2754-2758). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Here Shakuni knows both his powers and that of Yudhishthir. Knowing the enemies are weak and still hitting them, is a form of deceit. But this is not the only deceit, I am talking about. So let us move on.

Stage 2 Vidur Invites Yudhishthir for the gambling

Yudhishthir knew about the maya that Shakuni can employee but he also knew, he doesn’t have an option to refuse a King’s invitation as per his own oath as the King.

‘Yudhishthira said, “It seems that some of the most feared rogues122 have assembled there. They are sure to play with the powers of maya. However, everything is under the control of the creator. I will not refuse to play with those rogues. O Vidura! I do not wish to go and gamble on King Dhritarashtra’s command. A father always has a son’s welfare in mind. Therefore, tell me what I should do.123 I have no desire to gamble with Shakuni. But if the confident124 one challenges me in the sabha, I will never refuse, because that has been my eternal vow.”’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3041-3046).

Stage 3. Yudhishtir reminds Shakuni about his knowledge of Shakuni’s maya

‘Yudhishthira replied, “O king! Dishonest gambling is evil. There is no kshatriya valour in that. Nor is there any good policy in it. Why do you then praise playing with the dice? O Shakuni! The learned do not praise deceitful gambling. Like a cruel person, do not defeat us through a crooked path.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3070-3072).

Stage 4. This is how Shakuni answered.

‘Shakuni said, “He who knows the numbers and is knowledgeable about deceptions, is tireless in the art of gambling and is extremely intelligent in gambling, is the one who knows all the techniques. Through handling the dice, one can defeat the enemy. Blaming destiny is pointless. O king! Let us gamble and have no anxiety. Let us immediately decide on the stakes and not tarry.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3072-3075).

Now do I hear a deception here? Shakuni admits to the deception and he flaunts it with pride. So far the deception is just a speculation. But story doesn’t end here, its just the begining…

Stage 5. THE ACTUAL GAME AND ITS DECEPTION

Reference #1

Vaishampayana said, ‘Having heard this, Shakuni used deceit and told Yudhishthira, “I have won.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Location 3105).

Reference #2

Vaishampayana said, ‘Having heard these words, Shakuni resorted to deceit and told Yudhishthira, “I have won.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3119-3120).

Both these references are narrated directly by Vaishampayana, the narrator of the Mahabharata and doesn’t leave any doubt about the deceit. And rest is history.

This is how exactly the deceit happened

Yudhishthir explain this later —

Bhimasena! Shakuni, the dweller of the mountains,225 is knowledgeable in great maya. He hurled out the dice in the middle of the sabha. He used maya against me, who did not know any maya. It is then that I saw the deceit. I saw that the dice would always follow the wishes of Shakuni and favour him in odds and evens.226

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 5214-5216).

Stage 5 — Aftermath

This wasn’t just a supposition by Yudhishthir or Vaishmapayana but everyone knew, even thoseon Kauravas side —

Vidur’s statement in the court room

We all know Soubala’s skills in the game. This one from the mountains161 knows techniques of deceit with dice. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Let Shakuni return whence he came. The one from the mountains fights with the powers of maya.”’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3166-3168).

Vidur’s statement makes it clear, that Shakuni’s deceit was a common knowledge and it collaborates what Yudhishthir said earlier. He further adds —

O descendant of the Bharata lineage! This king is always kind to the sons of Dhritarashtra. Though he has been deprived through deceit, he refuses to open his eyes in anger. ‘I will burn these people down if I look at them through my terrible eyes.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3737-3739).

This is what Dronacharya says on the 15th day of the war —

O son of Gandhari! You are reaping the fruits of that adharma now. This is nothing and greater evils will befall you in the world hereafter. This is because you used deceit to defeat the Pandavas in the gambling match and exiled them to the forest, dressed in the skin of ruru deer.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 4859-4861).

If I just talk about the statistics, Shakuni’s deceit is mentioned over 50 times in Book 2 iteself and it has scattered reference in the entire book. So those who are claimining that Shakuni’s deceit is not mentioned in the epic, have not read the epic.
