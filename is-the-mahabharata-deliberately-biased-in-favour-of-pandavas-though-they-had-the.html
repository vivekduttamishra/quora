Is the Mahabharata deliberately biased in favour of Pandavas though they had their own share of faults?

📅 Jun 07, 2020 12:50 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Did Pandava's had there share of faults? How did you know? You read some other book or time travelled to see their faults? Or may be, just may be this question has no base — you know Pandava's fault because Mahabharata is not biased.

Mahabharat shows unbiased mistakes — they gambling away their kingdom and wife, they fighting and slaying their Gurus and elders, silently suffering Injustice, and the list goes on…

Mahabharata shows, Arjuna failing to protect all Dwarka women against ordinary robbers. Is this being biased towards Pandavas?

But on a serious note, both Mahabharat and Pandavas are biased towards the same element — Dharma. While Pandava made mistakes Kaurava's indulged in crimes; while Pandava's repented for their mistakes and did their penance, Kaurava's remained unmoved till there were no Kauravas left.
