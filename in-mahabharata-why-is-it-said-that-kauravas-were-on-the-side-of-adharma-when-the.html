In Mahabharata, why is it said that Kauravas were on the side of Adharma when they actually won Hastinapur in a gamble?

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Why did Duryodhana from Mahabharata become Adharmi except Cheer Haran? I don't remember his any sins.

If you have heard about just gambling incident in Mahabharata, you haven’t read enough, and hence this confusion may be genuine. But these two questions together at least talks about two different reasons why we can say Kauravas where on the side of Adharma.

Let us start from the begining. The righful King of Hastinpur was Pandu and his son was his heir apparent. He was also better qualified and more popular with the public. Therefore Kauravas conspired on multiple occasion to kill them. Bhima’s poisoning, attempt to drown him in the river and Lakshagrih are some of the early attempts. And none of them is considered Dharma.

On their last attempt, when Pandavas escaped and delayed their return, Duryodhana became heir apperent and when Pandavas resurfaced, the Hastinpaur was partitioned. That was the supposed price for peaceful existence, which was paid exclusively by Pandavas. They were robbed and banished from a prosperous kingdom into a barren land. Just deceit is again not part of Dharma.

Pandavas transformed a barren Khandava to Indraprastha. Duryodhana burnt of jealousy and through a deceitful game of dice, he usurped the kingdom. It was not only gambling, but also deceit that makes it Adharma. Adding to it is the inhumane treated towards Draupadi. Those were sufficient to call Adharma.

But story doesn’t end there. The gambling match was not a tranfer of ownership of the kingdom. It was a lease for thirteen years. And post the agreement period, they wree supposed to return the kingdom. Which they refused. That is again a criminal offence.

So Kauravas were guilty of conspiracy, arseny, attempt to murder, deceit and violation of agreement, grabbing property under false pretext and ultimately waging an uncalled for war. And they paid the price, while you still wonder what wrong they did.
