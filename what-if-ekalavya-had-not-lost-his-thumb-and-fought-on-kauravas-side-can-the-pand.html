What if Ekalavya had not lost his thumb and fought on Kauravas side? Can the Pandavas still win?

📅 Nov 19, 2020 08:41 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer : Most certainly YES.

Eklavya, in my understanding is one of the most overhyped characters of the epic. Granted he showed sparks of brilliance at archery as a young lad. And at that point of time, he was such a great shooter that it invoked jealousy even in Arjuna who was quite young then. In fact, he was only one to have ever made Arjuna jealous.

That said let us understand why his presence would have made no differences to Kaurava's fate —

  • Wars are not won on the basis of Archery skills. There were celestial weapons at play. Now even with his thumbs intact, since Drona had refused to accept him as his student, it is highly unlikely that he would get the knowledge of those celestial weapons. The war was lost for him.
  • People may argu, that just like another warrior of the time, he may have acquired the celstial weapon by deceit from some other guru, but must understand that celestial weapons acquired through deceitful means doesn’t work in favour of the warrior.
  • Arjuna was sarvyasachi — ambidextrous — with capability to shoot with both hands with equal calibre. Eklavya had lost only his right hand thumb. We do not see any great feat of the warrior other than siding with evil Jarasandha in later days.
  • Krishna was determined to destroy evils (Vinaschayata Duskrtam). Eklavya was too insignificant to resist or change the tide against Krishna and Pandavas. There were other great warriors like Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama and Karna who had celestial weapons and yet they failed to protect the evil Duryodhana. Most of them were great archers. Drona was the one whom Eklavya wanted has his guru. So how can anyone expect an extra-ordinary archer with no celestial weapons win.

The only hope of Eklavya’s salvation was to side with Dharma. Be the part of Pandava army and fight Kaurava’s. But Eklavya made his choice. To go against Krishna, to go against Dharma.

Fighting against Dharam when dreaded Ravana, Mahisura, Bali, Tarkasur and others fail, what chance poor Eklavya had against Dharma?
