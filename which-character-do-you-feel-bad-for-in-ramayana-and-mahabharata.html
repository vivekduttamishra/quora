Which character do you feel bad for in Ramayana and Mahabharata?

📅 Apr 19, 2020 04:14 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Mahabharat and Ramayan are wonderful epics without any parrelel in any world literature. We can connect with even the most sidelined characters and connect with them and become their defender. And this is the greatest proof of their authenticity.

Each character is real with its own share of pains and their conduct in the face of those pains make their life a lesson — I find them to be cherished not tragic.

Consider these —

  • There is a dutiful sons (Ram, Bhisma) , who rejects the throne for his father's happiness and live lives with constant miseries. They get a respectable position.
  • But then there is another equally dutiful son, who dies for his father and still labeled greatest villain of all time — Meghnad.
  • There are dutiful wives( Sita and Draupadi) , who follow their husband's to all hardships of life, yet their mighty husband's are unable to protect them against destiny. They suffer all the humiliation and yet remained with their husbands.
  • There are brothers (Lakshman, 4 younger Pandavas) who suffer worse fate because they chose to be with their brother. Lakshman chose a fate which even destiny didn't willed for him.
  • On the other hand there are brothers who became the cause of their own brother's death because of Dharma — Sugriv, Vibhishan
  • There are parents who saw their infants murdered in front of their own eyes and could do nothing about it — Devaki, Vasudev and Shantanu.
  • There are husbands who despite of being mighty failed to prevent suffering of their wives — Ram, Laksham and Pandavas.

Now you would excuse me if I couldn't feel bad for other tragic characters —

  • Whose scewd sense of power overshadows sense of justice and Dharma — Barbarik
  • Who felt his birth right on throne and goes to any depth for their ambition.
  • Who despite of being a king his entire life complaint that society abused him.
  • For whom self glory ranked higher than any Dharma.
