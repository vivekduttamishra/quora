What would have been the result of Mahabharata had Barbareek/Barbarik participated in it?

📅 Apr 24, 2020 07:26 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Barbarik doesn’t exist in Vyas Mahabharat. That makes this question hypothetical. And even if he existed, the question is still hypothetical. But the question has an unambigous answer.

Let us explore the famous story of Barbarik’s three mystic arrows — The legend says that he had three arrows which can mark and destory anything at Barbarik’s wish. And Krishna tested his weapon by asking him to destroy all the leaves of a tree. He intentionally hid one of the leaves under his feet and the weapon waited for Krishna to move his fit away from the leaf and finally destroyed it — such a mighty weapon.

But what is the lesson of this story? Note that the mighty arrow failed to destroy the leaf as long as it was under Krishna’s feet — under his protection.

The war would have gone the Krishna way. Brahmastra and Narayanastra were some of the most powerful divine weapons belonging to the prime diety — Brahma and Vishnu. And they could do nothing against Pandavas. The greatest warriors of the time, the desciples of Bhagwan Parshuram — Bhishma and Drona failed. And we are talking about some mystical warrior with some unknown divine weapon. What chance it had against the wishes of Ninth Vishnu — Lord Krishna?

What is the significance of Barbarik’s story?

It is not how powerful he was. Neither was how powerful his divine weapon was. Krishna got him beheaded not because he was powerful, but because he had a skewed priority — support weak without considering Dharma or justice.

Power without responsibility is a curse. Simply because you are powerful doesn’t mean you can be irrisponsible. Such power must be destroyed.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkanth Edition of Mahabharat (Geeta Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
