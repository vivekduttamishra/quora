Is Karna evil as mentioned in authentic versions of the Mahabharata?

📅 Oct 05, 2020 06:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Karna has been mentioned evil, and yet generous in the epic. His good qualities are also highlighted at many places some of which are —

  • He was a great archer in the superlative term and was acknolwedged as a warrior or repute.
  • He is referred virtuous at several places because of his welknown nature as a great donor.
  • Even Krishna called him invincible as long as he had his Kavach and Indra’s Vasvi shakti.

However, none of these great qualities nullifies his evil acts. As far as the good qualities are concerned they are often over-hyped and have multiple contradictions or parallels. For example —

  • Krishna says he is invinicible with Kavach and Vasvi Shakti
    • Here Krishna is praising the divine artifects rather than Karna alone.
    • There is a contradiction as Karna lost several battles with his kavacha like Draupadi’s swayamvara, Gandharva war and Rajsuy battle.
    • Without using Vasvi Shakti, he failed not only against Arjuna but also against Abhimanyu and Ghatotkatch.
  • Krishna called him both virtious and insolent. He was extremely happy on Ghatotkatch’s death as that meant Karna can be slain. He even offered his Sudarshan Chakra to Arjuna to kill Karna. Why would he do so unless…
  • Yudhishthir and many other Kings through out Mahabharata and epic have possesed the quality of a donor without being evil.

Now returning back to the question, Karna has been refered as evil directly and indirectly in multiple places in the grand epic Mahabhara. Many times words like evil, deceit and wicked have been used for him. At other times, his actions leave no room for doubt. Here I would provide just a few authentic references from BORI to prove my point —

Karna was the trunk of (evil Duryodyana’s) tree ← metaphor

Let us start with an important shloka that appears almost at the beginning of Adi Parva —

दुर्योधनो मन्युमयो महाद्रुमः; स्कन्धः कर्णः शकुनिस्तस्य शाखाः | दुःशासनः पुष्पफले समृद्धे; मूलं राजा धृतराष्ट्रोऽमनीषी ||६५||

युधिष्ठिरो धर्ममयो महाद्रुमः; स्कन्धोऽर्जुनो भीमसेनोऽस्य शाखाः | माद्रीसुतौ पुष्पफले समृद्धे; मूलं कृष्णो ब्रह्म च ब्राह्मणाश्च ||६६||

—Adi Parva

‘Duryodhana is a great tree created out of passion, Karna is its trunk, Shakuni is its branches, Duhshashana is the plentiful fruit and flowers and the mindless Dhritarashtra is its root. Yudhishthira is a great tree created out of righteousness, Arjuna is its trunk, Bhima is its branches, the two sons of Madri27 are its plentiful fruit and flowers, and Krishna, Brahma and the Brahmanas are the root.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 429-432).

But it doesn’t mention evil, it says Passion. Right?

Oh, I know, many would jump at me at remind me the shloka doesn’t refer evil, but passion. But this is important! Let me highlight two points —

  1. The word passion here is contrasted with righteousness, same was as mindless Dhritrashtra contrasted with Krishna. So essentially Karna belong to the same tree that originates with mindless Dhritrashtra.
  2. Important — The tree, whose Karna is referred as trunk, is Duryodhana.

If we prove Duryodhana is evil, it automatically implies Karna was the trunk of an evil tree.

So let us see one by one few references, if Duryodhana and Karna has been referred as evil or no. Please free to counter my reference with valid ones.

Why Krishna’s peace attempt failed.

It was then that the great Krishna, moved by pity and desirous of bringing peace, himself went to Hastinapura to find peace and Krishna’s proposal for peace was rebuffed by King Duryodhana, though this was in the interest of both parties. Then, hearing the evil counsel of Karna, Duryodhana and others, Krishna displayed to the kings his powers of yoga.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 759-761).

  • is it trying to say that while the cousel of Karna was evil, he was not? We shall try to find more refernce.

Janmeyjaya asked about Druapadi’s Molestation

Why did not the pure Krishna Droupadi, oppressed by the evil sons of Dhritarashtra, burn them with her angry eyes, capable though she was of doing it?

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 2400-2401).

  • Now you may see the direct use of the word evil for Duryodhana. And remeber Karna is the trunk of that evil tree (Duryodhana)
  • Later we will check that in this molestation Karna was the prime instigator. And wait for the cross reference before calling it interpolation.

Multiple Conspiracy to kill Pandavas

evil Duryodhana was tormented. Then, Vaikartana Karna and Subala’s son, Shakuni, tried to kill the Pandavas through various means. However, the Pandavas, slayers of enemies, discovered all of them.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5277-5279).

  • Notice Duryodhana is again mentioned as evil, as he tries to kill Pandavas.
  • Karna is an ally in the same crime. Is it possible for the same crime Duryodhana is evil, but Karna is not?
  • Even if you maintain that, Karna is again proven as trunk of evil tree.

Here are some more references. Many say Karna was not involved in poisoning of Bhima. What do you make out after checking this reference —

‘“Thereupon, the evil-minded Duryodhana, with Karna and the son of Subala,13 tried to banish them and oppressed them in various ways. That evil son of Dhritarashtra gave poison to Bhima with his food, but the warrior Vrikodara14 digested it.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 2359-2361). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Note first line clearly says evil minded Duryodhana with Karna and Duryodhana tried various ways to opress Pandavas.
  • The subsequent lines give a couple of examples. So how is Karna not present in Bhima’s poisoning?
  • KMG certainly explicitly places him. But for now, just check BORI reference above.
  • By the way here is only Duryodhana evil minded or others supporting the same cause also evil minded?

The Lakshagrih Conspiracy

While KMG mentions the plot in detail, contrary to popular propaganda that BORI doesn’t mentions Karna’s role in this conspiracy, there is a very explicit mention —

When the Pandavas were not killed through all these means, open and hidden, since they were protected by fate and destiny, he16 consulted his advisers: Vrisha, 17 Duhshasana and the others. With Dhritarashtra’s consent, he had a house of lac built.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 2366-2368).

  • Duryodhana consulted his friend Vrisha and had a house of lac built.
  • Karna knew about the lac house. His involvement in other conspiracy is also mentioned.
  • There is no mention anywhere that he downvoted the proposal.
  • And before someone jumps and say Virsha might me some other person, may be some son of Dhirtrashtra, the footnote 17, clearly mentions Virsha refers to Karna.

What people thought about Karna when they found Pandavas survived Lakshagrih

Duryodhana, Karna and Shakuni, the son of Subala, are evil, foolish and young. —Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Location 7127).

  • Here Karna is directly mentioned as evil along with Duryodhana and Shakuni

Post Lakshagrih incident

When Bhishma and Drona advised Dhritrashtra to welcome Pandavas after swayvara and give their share of land, Karna started absuing the two althought neither had said anything to him. Here is how Drona responded to Karna.

‘Drona said, “We know that you say this out of malice and the reasons for that. You are wicked and to bring injury to the Pandavas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 7094-7095).

  • Oh! here is just mentioned wicked, not evil.

The Dyut Shabha Incident

Karna Said —

However, she submits to many and it is therefore certain that she is a courtesan. It is my view that there is nothing surprising in her being brought into the sabha in a single garment, or even if she is naked.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3376-3377).

Forget being Draupadi, forget being courtesan, any one who considers that she may be brought naked, any person who can utter these words for a woman, if he is not evil then we must redebate the definition of evil. But alas, she wasn’t naked. So what would be the advise of a gentleman?

Karna said — Strip away the garments from the Pandavas and Droupadi.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3379-3380).

Surely an honorable suggestion by an honorable person. But before we move away from this debate, it would be important to note Vyas has mentioned the exact expression of Karna during this incident —

Radheya, who was almost senseless with anger, gripped his lustrous arms212 and uttered these words,

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Location 3365).

I wonder why people don’t call him evil?

Goharan Parva

Here is what Kripacharya said to Karna. I must remind you all, Kripacharya is Karna’s Guru —

‘Kripa said, “O Radheya! Your mind is always wicked and you are inclined towards war. You do not understand natural objectives, nor do you consider the consequences.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1665-1666). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Cross reference for Draupadi’s molestation

In the Goharna Parva Ashwatthama rebukes Karna with these words, which serve two purposes. While it shows his opinion about Karna, it also provides cross-reference to Druapadi’s molestation which many are hell-bent in calling interpolation for they feel it is not cross-referenced. It also highlights Karna’s role, which too, is cross-referenced.

O performer of evil deeds! She was dragged into the assembly hall in a single garment, when she was in season. In search of gain, you have severed the great root of a sandalwood tree. 109 O brave one! You made them perform tasks and what did Vidura have to say then? 110 We have seen that men exhibit conciliation, to the best of their ability. So do other beings, even insects and ants. The Pandavas are incapable of pardoning Droupadi’s molestation.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1700-1704). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Here Karna never denies his involvement in Druapadi’s molestation.

Conclusion

There are many more references where Karna has been referred as evil, insolent. But I think this is sufficient a reference to prove —

  1. Duryodhana has been referred as evil on un-countable occassions. It was even predicted by Brahmanas and Vidur at the time of his birth — On witnessing these terrible and ominous signs, all the Brahmanas and the immensely intelligent Vidura said, “It is clear that this son of yours will bring about the destruction of this lineage. There is peace in abandoning him and great disaster in nurturing him. —Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 4499-4501).
  2. Since Karna has been referred as the trunk of a tree called Duryodhana — he is the trunk of an evil tree.
  3. Karna himself as been referred as evil and parter in crime for Duryodhana, always willing to fight.
  4. Karna while possessing noble qualities, was more possessed with vices than virtues making him more inclined towards evil traits — jealousy, boasting, abusive, coniving.
  5. He was more of a war monger than a warrior.

All the references provided are from BORI and can be verfied.
