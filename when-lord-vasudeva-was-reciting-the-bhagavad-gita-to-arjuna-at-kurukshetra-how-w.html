When Lord Vasudeva was reciting the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna at Kurukshetra, how were the other warriors spending their time till the Bhagavad Gita was completed? Or was Vasudeva directly blocking time?

📅 Apr 27, 2020 05:03 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is one of those very common questions that have no relevance in understanding Gita or Mahabharata —

  1. Battle hadn't started yet. The army had gathered.
  2. The section starts with not Krishna or Arjuna, but with Duryodhana moving around and talking to Drona. What were others doing then? Well as I said battle had not started yet.
  3. Arjuna had given up his arms, not ready to fight. It presented a grand opportunity that war may not happen after all. So people around may be waiting — hopeful and curious.

But did the time freeze?

I am not sure about literally and I doubt, Lord Krishna will temper with the time flow.

The Mighty Bhisma was commanding Kuru Army, and he hadn't signaled for the war yet. So yes Kaurava army had to wait. With Krishna and Arjuna not prepared to fight naturally Pandava’s army too waited. Many of them might we wondering same way as we do — not sure why they are waiting, unable to comprehend what's transpiring between Krishna and Drona, they had to wait nonetheless. For them time froze in confusion and inaction.

Metamorphically, time froze as Krishna Arjuna created Gita through their dialogs.
