Short Answer: Because they are Karna fans.

And it is simple —

If you can’t rise to someone else’s greatness make them fall to your level and then win by their extensive experience at that level

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

The first chapter of the epic represents two trees like this —

‘Duryodhana is a great tree created out of passion, Karna is its trunk, Shakuni is its branches, Duhshashana is the plentiful fruit and flowers and the mindless Dhritarashtra is its root.

Yudhishthira is a great tree created out of righteousness, Arjuna is its trunk, Bhima is its branches, the two sons of Madri27 are its plentiful fruit and flowers, and Krishna, Brahma and the Brahmanas are the root.

— Adi Parva

As you can see Pandavas are referred as righteous with Krishna and Brahma at it’s route. Not only Krishna but God of Gods, Shiva willed them to be victorious. Duryodhana admitted people think they are more qualified and favoured, Gandhari pronounced Pandavas right on the throne and Karna admitted that earth would be destroyed because of him and his friends.

And if someone finds them unqualified or adharmi, they clearly belonged to the other tree governed by passion, devoid of righteousness and governed my mindless root. How else do you explain someone saying Krishna made adharmi win and thereby calling Krishna a party to sinners? How do you explain those people who fail to understand the key lesson of the epic and fail to understand who is a hero and who is villain?
