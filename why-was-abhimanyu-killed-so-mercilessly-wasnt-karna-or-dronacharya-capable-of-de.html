Why was Abhimanyu killed so mercilessly? Wasn't Karna or Dronacharya capable of defeating Abhimanyu single-handedly? Karna was as skilled as Arjuna, which puts him in the front foot against Abhimanyu.

📅 Mar 15, 2020 11:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The fact that neither Karna nor Drona could defeat Abhimanyu simply suggests that they weren't capable to defeat him single handedly.

Abhimanyu was a proven great warrior and it was accepted by Karna even before the battle stared in the conversation where Krishna was trying to win over Karna to Pandava side. Then Abhimanyu proved himself through out the first 13 days of his battle. Even Bhisma admired him.


Edit #1 12th Sept 2021

But since the question is related to the capabilities of Karna and Duryodhana, let me quote directly from what they said about Abhimanyu on that day —

Duryodhana. ‘Let us unite and grind him down. Otherwise, he will kill all of us single-handed.’

Vrisha Vaikartana Karna then spoke to Drona.111 ‘Before he destroys all of us, tell us how we can swiftly kill him.’

Drona, the great archer, told all of them, ‘Have you been able to detect any weakness in this young one? He is roaming around in all the directions. You had better search out your forefathers now. Behold this son of Pandava. He is quick and is a lion among men. The path followed by his chariot and the circle of his bow can be seen. He affixes and releases arrows extremely swiftly. His arrows confound me and afflict my breath of life. But I am delighted with Subhadra’s son, the destroyer of enemy heroes. When Subhadra’s son roams around on the field of battle, I am extremely gratified. The angry maharathas cannot detect a weakness in him. He is dexterous in the use of his hands and releases great arrows in all the directions. In the battle, I do not see any difference between him and the wielder of Gandiva.112 At this, oppressed by the arrows of Arjuna’s son, Karna again spoke to Drona. ‘I am oppressed by the extremely terrible and energetic arrows of the young Abhimanyu. Nevertheless, I remain stationed here. His arrows are terrible and possess the energy of the fire. They are sapping my heart now.’

— Abhimanyu Vadha Parva

Duryodhana and Karna realizes that they need to unite to kill Abhimanyu. They requested Drona for guidelines. But Drona was afflicted for his life, Karna’s heart was sapped and he was oppressed. And this was after Karna had already lost is brother and fled multiple times from Abhimanyu unable to confront him. It is at this point Drona suggests that he should cut down his bow.


Now as of Drona or Karna, both are overestimated and under performing fighters. Karna on numerous occasions was defeated by Arjuna, Bhim, Ghatotkatch, Satyaki and Abhimanyu.

We haven't really seen Drona fighting any battle other than Virata where he was defeated by Arjuna with others. In Kurukshetra he not only failed against Abhimanyu but also Ghatotkatch — why else Karna used Indra's weapon. Next day Bhim defeated him on numerous occasions.

So the question (as raised in one of the answers below) is —

Why Arjuna failed to defeat him during the Kurukshetra battle in first 11 days?

Despite of Krishna's Gita, Arjun still had too much respect for Guru Drona and Bhisma and was not giving his best when fighting them. That is why Krishna had to attack Bhisma to provoke him. Same goes true for Drona.

Even on day 14, even after the death of Abhimanyu, He was reluctant to fight Drona and Bhima had to confront him.

So why he defeated Drona in Virata battle?

I have my theory but I would leave this question for readers to guess.


EDIT #1 12 September 2021

Few people who can’t see their icons sink to the depth of deceit and cowardice attempt to malign every warrior who thrashed their ICON. Naturally there aren’t any authentic reference to back their claim, so they try to distort the facts by doing extremely selective reference and their own interpretation that are often in direct contradiction to the actual epic. Here are a few of them —

Critic Claim #1 Abhimanyu wasn’t alone. He had his army

This is a common reference given —

At this, Karna became enraged. Wishing to ensure the welfare of your son, he covered the unassailable Abhimanyu with sharp arrows. In the field of battle, he pierced his followers with sharp and supreme arrows and was contemptuous of Subhadra’s brave son.

—Abhimanyu Vadha Parva

This reference is correct, just misplaced. Chakravyuha included almost all Kuru generals. Naturally All Pandavas tried to breach it with Abhimanyu at it’s front. Abhimanyu had already caused a huge carnage. In the battle that is referred above, Abhimanyu killed Karna’s brother and forced Karna to do what he was best at — retreat.

It was after this point Jaidradh due to Shiva’s boon managed to stop all Pandava’s warrior from entering the Chakravyuha and there after Abhimanyu was all alone which is clear in the following statement—

‘Dhritarashtra said, “O suta! You have told me about the battle between the one and the many. It was extremely terrible and the great-souled one triumphed. The valour of Subhadra’s son was extraordinary and impossible to believe. But this wonderful deed is possible for those who resort to dharma. When the one hundred princes were slain, Duryodhana retreated.

Critic Claim #2 Group attack was a common thing.

It is true. Group attacks were common during the Kurukshetra war. That said all the group battles were most many to many attack. Two army clashed with many generals and their army fighting each other. They weren’t directed against a single person.

Critic Claim #3 Abhimanyu had impenetrable armor (Kavacha)

This again is a claim born out of reading in a haste and not understanding the meaning. Some even claimed Abhimany had celestial armors. Here is the reference —

The preceptor smiled and spoke gently to Karna. ‘His armour is impenetrable. He is young, swift and valiant. His father has been instructed by me on the art of donning armour. It is certain that this destroyer of enemy cities knows everything about that.

Blinded by their hate for Pandavas and Abhimanyu the read just the first line and tend to ignore Drona’s further explanation. Wearing armor as impenetrable was an art that was taught by Drona himself to Arjuna. This can be clearly understood that at least Drona must have deployed the same art of his armor. Yet, that didn’t stop him from getting afflicted.

Critic Claim #4 Karna didn’t cut his bow from behind.

They perhaps take the idea in literal sense. Let us understand the context. Karna was already defeated, and tormented. His brother was slain in front of his eyes and all he could do was to retreat. He was willing to unite his effort and kill Abhimanyu together. He was too terrified to face him face to face.

At this point, Duryodhana, Karna and Drona started a strategic discussion which we discussed in parts, above. At this point Drona says —

with well-aimed arrows, you are capable of slicing down his bow, his bowstring, the reins of his horses and the two charioteers who guard his flanks.114 O great archer! O Radheya! If you are capable, do this. Make him retreat and strike him subsequently. If he wields the bow, the gods and the asuras are incapable of vanquishing him. If you so wish, deprive him of his chariot and his bow.’ Having heard the preceptor’s words, Vaikartana Karna used his arrows to swiftly slice down the bow of the light-handed one. Bhoja115 killed his horses and Goutama116 the charioteers who guarded his flanks. Once his bow had been severed, the others enveloped him with a shower of arrows.

So a coward who was too terrified to fight Abhimanyu alone despite of the fact that he killed his brother, cut down his bow so that others can start attacking him. If you prefer to believe it was just a cowardly act but not an attack from behind, I may even agree.

Critic Claim #3 Abhimanyu died fairly

That is one of the most ludicrous claim based on the last hit. It is like giving the entire winning credit to one batsman who scored the last run. Let us see what one statement made those critic proclaim such an absurdity —

‘“He then advanced against the chariot of Duhshasana’s son and used the club to slay his horses. O venerable one! At this, Duhshasana’s son was enraged and raised his club. Asking Subhadra’s son to wait, he attacked him. Those two brave brothers wished to kill each other and raised their clubs against each other. They struck, like the destroyer Tryambaka126 in earlier times. Having struck each other with the ends of their clubs, they both fell down on the ground. In the midst of that battle, those two scorchers of enemies were like Indra’s standards that had been uprooted. The ratha who was Duhshasana’s son, the extender of the deeds of the Kuru lineage, arose first and struck Subhadra’s son on the head with the club, as he was about to get up. Because of exhaustion and because of the great force of the club, Subhadra’s son, the destroyer of enemy heroes, lost his senses and fell down on the ground, unconscious.

Look! Duhshasana’s son, who was too inisiginficant to have his name mentioned, killed Abhimanyu. In their urge to prove themselves right, they neither read the text before or after. They miss the first phrase “He then…”. Clearly something happened before this point. What?

‘“At that time, when he was bereft of his chariot, the six maharathas117 quickly and ruthlessly showered him with arrows, while the child fought single-handed. His bow had been severed and he was without a chariot. However, he was engaged in following his own dharma. The handsome one grasped a sword and a shield and leapt up into the sky. Arjuna’s son displayed his dexterity and strength and roamed around in the sky, like a terrible king of the birds. He showed kaishika and other motions. 118 The great archers pierced him in the battle, searching for a weakness, glancing upwards and wondering, ‘He might descend on me with the sword in his hand.’ Drona severed the sword, decorated with a bejewelled handle, from his hand. Radheya used sharp arrows to cut down his supreme shield. Deprived of his sword and shield, but still whole in his limbs, he again descended from the sky onto the ground. He picked up a chariot wheel and angrily rushed at Drona. His limbs blazed because of the dust raised by the wheels. He was radiant, with the chariot wheel raised high in his arms. In that battle, for a short while, Abhimanyu looked beautiful and seemed to replicate the deeds of Vasudeva.119 Blood flowed from his body.

But our modern critic try to prove, Abhimanyu died only by the blow of Duhshashana as if these six Maharatha were too weak to cause any mortal injury to Abhimany whose blood flowed from his body. They would tend to ignore the fact that Abhimanyu foremost died of exhaustion caused by these war and they would ignore what Dhritrashtra and Sanjaya said at this point, what Krishna said later, what Gandhari repeated much later —

‘“O king! Thus, in that battle, a single one was slain by many. He had agitated all the soldiers, like an elephant among lotuses. The brave one was as resplendent as a wild elephant killed by hunters. The brave one fell down and was surrounded by all those on your side.

They would ignore all these statements to prove their point. Bravo!
