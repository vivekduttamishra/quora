In Mahabharata, was the great warrior Arjuna blessed with some extra superpowers as he never got defeated in any war?

📅 Feb 26, 2021 07:16 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Yes, Arjuna was indeed blessed with the greatest superpower that exists, and that made him Vijaya — the victorious.

That superpower was DHARMA!

It was Arjuna’s adherence to Dharma that made —

  • Drona not only declare him equal to Indra but also bestow him with Brahmshira weapon. He never cheated or tricked his guru into giving him the weapon.
  • Krishna confer Arjuna the status of his inseparable existence. Krishna always sided with Arjuna to the extent that he offered even his sudarshan chakra to him.
  • Shiva himself bestowed him with Pashupatastra, the most potent weapon of the universe!

But those weapons didn’t made Arjuna great. He never used any of those weapons. And yet Arjuna was victorious. He was victorious because, he always adhered to Dharma —

  • He protected his guru from a crocodile.
  • He protected Drupada and his family from senseless attacks by a bunch of miserable kings who had failed at swyamvara.
  • Best, he protected even those evil beings who had done nothing but treacherous deeds against him and his family — devilish creatures whom even their frend left at mercy of enemy and fled.

And only such compassion and righteous deed brings the power that can vanquish the evil, not matter how mighty the evil may be — and that was the secret super power of Arjuna.
