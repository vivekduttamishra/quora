Where did Pitamaha Bhishma make a mistake for which he suffered for days on an arrow bed until he got salvation?

📅 Apr 29, 2021 11:15 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Please don’t get confused by the words of some pseudo scholar. Bhishma didn’t suffer bed of arrows because of his bad deeds, not as per the grand epic.

I know a so called epic expert believes that he suffered such a fate because of the justice he did by not letting a handicap man ascend to the throne. He also believes that if Duryodhana was evil that doesn’t mean, Dhritrashtra too was evil. Little did he know that epic itself calls Dhirtrashtra as the root of the tree that was evil Duryodhana. And he was a silent partner of every misdeeds of Duryodhana. Bhishma did a great favour to Hastinapur by not letting him being the king. Only if Pandu had lived long enough… A wishful thinking!

So why did Bhishma suffer?

Let me ask you this — What sin did Shiva commit that he had to swallow the poison while others got Amrita? Nothing. He was the only one capable of swallowing the poison. Period.

Did Bhishma suffered more than Dadhichi, who donated his own bones? What was his crime? Well it was his greatness!

That is ancient Shiva Sutra — Only he who has power can swallow the poison!

People often suffer what may appear cruel fate, even injustice — Alas! we could see the whole picture with our mortal eyes. Remember the horrific Pulwama incident? So many of our brave hearts were martyred. Were the criminals? No they were patriots and good people. So why did they suffer that cruel fate?

Alas! I must be honest. I don’t know the answer. Because I don’t have the divine vision. But I can tell they didn’t die because they were sinners. They died for their motherland. If there is some solace in their sacrifice is the heroic actions of fellow armed soldiers who avenged their deaths.

So, we must understand that in the grand scheme of the things people suffer not always because of their mistake of faults, but also because they are small cogs or wheel in the great design of destiny.

Coming back to Bhisma. He was the old guard of Hastinapur and he needed to be removed from the path of the destiny, and he was no lesser warrior who can be beheaded by a single arrow. He could have died instantly, if he so desired. But his zeal to see Hastinapur in safe hand kept him alive. It wasn’t the suffering but the courage and determination that dwarfed his suffering.
