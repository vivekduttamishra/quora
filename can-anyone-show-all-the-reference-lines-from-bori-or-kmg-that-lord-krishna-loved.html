No. I can’t show a single reference from any authentic version of Mahabharata, where Karna even remotely competed with Arjuna, when it comes to Krishna’s grace or favour.

Krishna categorically mentioned more than once —

  • Arjuna is an inseparable existence to him.
  • He loves Arjuna more than anyone else.
  • He who loves Arjuna, loves him. He who hates Arjuna hates him.
  • He often called Arjuna even in Gita as Nishpap (Sinless) Arjuna.

Compared to this, he had only few encounters with Karna documented in epic.

  • Draupadi’s swyamvara — There was no direct interaction with either Arjuna or Karna. But Krishna was glad to see Arjuna alive. He later meets Pandavas.
  • Bhagwatyan Parva — Krishna attempts at final peace negotiation. But Druyodhana prompted by Karna foils this attempt. Doesn’t look like it would won any favour.
  • Krishna-Karna Dialog — Krishna tries to convince Karna to join him and be part of Dharma. Karna refused. Krishna projects the dire consequence of the forthcoming battle, which wasn’t in favour of Karna.
  • The War — Where Krishna reminds both Arjuna and Karna about the evil deeds of Karna and persuades Arjuna to kill Karna. He even says, should Arjuna fail, he would himself kill Karna. He offered even his Sudarshan Chakra to Arjuna.

There can’t be more contrast in the feeling of anyone as Krishna had for the two mentioned characters.

But I can quote reference from TV Shows like Suryaputra Karna where Krishna had admiration for Karna a tad more than Arjuna and Krishna first became charioteer to Karna. Is that reference acceptable?
