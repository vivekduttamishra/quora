In the Mahabharata, who was Gandhari?

📅 Feb 24, 2020 05:51 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Who was Gandhari in Mahabharat?

Well the answer is known to everyone who know anything about Mahabharat — Princess of Gandhar, wife of Dhritrashtra, the mother of Kauravas…

So I believe the question is not seeking the obvious answer. So let us ask again — who was Gandhari in Mahabharat?

Gandhari was the lady who could have chosen to complement her husband, become his eyes, guide their children to the light and instead chose to blindfold herself!

Gandhari is the icon of that set of society that takes the comfort in the fact that they can't be responsible if they can't see it.

Gandhari curses Krishna for the destruction of her family. Gandhari is the root of common mindset that takes the comfort in blaming others for one's own actions and failure.

Gandhari could have removed her blindfold to guide his sons, prevent a catastrophe, yet she chose to remain in her blind world — sad, suffering, and playing helpless. She represents our inner inertia opposing every external force to bring change even for better.

Gandhari is a satire on that social mindset that values sacrifices no matter how irrational, how devastating they may be — yes Gandhari is respected and applauded for her actions and absolved for being irresponsible.

Gandhari is a common noun for every parent whose self ego and personal goals takes a precedence over their children's welfare.

And ultimately Gandhari is a case study, and a lesson —

1. our blindfold have a cascading effect on our circle of influence — larger the circle, worse the influence.

2. Worse than physical disability is the one caused by egos.

3. Sacrifice becomes the fruit of a poisoned tree if driven by irrationality and ego.

Good question — who is Gandhari?
