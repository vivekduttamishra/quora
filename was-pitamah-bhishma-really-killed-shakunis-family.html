Was Pitamah Bhishma really killed Shakuni's family?

📅 May 05, 2020 12:29 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

No. This is a flight of fantasy to justify and glorify Shakuni and his action.

Not only this story doesn’t find any mention in Mahabharat, abundant contradiction to this story exists. They find clear mention at several places.

  1. Shakuni and his brother’s were present in Draupadi Swyamber

Sakuni, Sauvala, Vrisaka, and Vrihadvala,--these sons of the king Gandhara--have also come.

—Swyamber Parva, Mahabharat (KMG SECTION CLXXXVIII)

2. Shakuni’s father even participated in the battle of Mahabharat from Kaurava’s side. It finds a clear mention in Shalya Parva

Rishyasringa's son and the Rakshasa Alayudha, and the mighty-armed Alambusa and the great car-warrior Subala--these and numerous other kings, O best of monarchs, have taken up arms for my sake, prepared to cast away their very lives in great battle, stationed on the field amidst these, and surrounded by my brothers, I will fight against all the Parthas and the Pancalas and the Cedis, O tiger among kings, and the sons of Draupadi and Satyaki and Kunti-Bhoja and the rakshasa Ghatotkaca.

—Salya Parva, Mahabharat (Kisari Mohan Ganguli Translation)

This reference tell us few things clearly —

  1. Shakuni and his family were well, alive and supported Dhirtrashtra and Kaurvas. They had no qualm against Kauravas or Bhisma.
  2. Shakun’s dice was not made up of his father’s bone and had no desire for revenge against Kuru clan. He always fought with them against Pandavas, who had no role in Gandhari-Dhritrashtra marriage

So who was Shakuni?

contrary to a popular depiction, Shakuni wasn’t the mastermind of any conspiracy other than the dice game. He was little more than a follower who was privy to various crimes of Kauravas with little to add. He, however, must be credited for his deciet in dice game.
