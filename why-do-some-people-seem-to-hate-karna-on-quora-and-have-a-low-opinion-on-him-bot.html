Why do some people seem to hate Karna on Quora and have a low opinion on him both as a warrior and a person?

📅 May 21, 2020 12:31 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

I believe this question is born with Karna's Kavach — it refuses to die. Honestly speaking I don't hate Karna more than what Vyasji suggests. I also feel many (not some) hate exaggeration of the character in recent times more than the character itself.

Some believe people dislike him because they are fans of Arjuna, Draupadi or someone — I believe it to be the intent of the epic. Some think epic, like history, is written by winners. Again wrong!

But I can sum this some people to have these common traits —

1. Those who have read Mahabharat, cross reference facts and based on those study form the opinion.

2. Who are fans of great epic and Indian culture. While agreeing Karna is a part of the epic, it shouldn't be forgotten that epic didn't make him hero. Making him hero is discrediting the epic.

3. Those people who trust Krishna and Vedvyas and his contemporary statement — Karna is the trunk of evil tree. And along with them collate the views of Bhisma, Parshuram, Drona.

4. Those who cherish the teaching of Gita and Mahabharat more than individual characters and hero worshipping.

5. Those who truly believed in Krishna, the incarnation of Vishnu.

6. Those who bow to Shiva and Hanuman and believe if they sided with someone, Dharma must be on their side.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),

Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and

Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.

Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
