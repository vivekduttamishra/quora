Which book is the exact translation of Veda Vyasa's Mahabharata in English?

📅 Jan 09, 2021 06:47 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

To understand the quest for this question, you must first understand the concept and significance of Shruti-Smriti tradition for Sanatan civilization.

Our civilization is tens of thousands of years old. Mahabharata itself is over five thousand years old. Ramayana is older and Vedas predates even Ramayana.

Now the most important question is, how do you expect to record and preserve the narratives dating back thousands of years? No written media — leaves, papers, tree bark — are likely to survive the wrath of nature over so many years. If we think information we are currently storing on clouds is going to last forever, we are leaving fool’s paradise.

Our visionary teachers from ancient times had a solution for this dellima. They utilized the one original neural network that existed all these while — the human brain. Every generation heard (Shruti) the narrative, memorized (Smriti) and passed it to the next generation creating an endless chain of neural network. It was only in recent centuries they were commited to some written media and there was a gap of over several thousand years between the actual incident and it’s written work.

So, the short answer of the question there is no exact translation of Ved Vyas work, because there were thousands of nodes (societies) containing the narrative albeit a slight variation which is natural in any narrative — written or otherwise.

Every narrative dating back at leas few centuries and that are not corrupted by modern political views, carry the same broad narrative — How Pandavas under the guidance of Krishna represented the forces of good that thrawt the evil side represented by Kauravas and Karna and how in such a fight between the good and evil, even those sitting on the sidelines, and those that are not evils but side with them gets wiped out. And on that parameter, I would say most ancient narrative is true to Ved Vyas’s narrative.

As we all know, BORI devoted over 40 years, comparing and analysing over 1259 scripts to bring out the Critical Edition (CE) of Mahabharata. This can be considered nearest to Ved Vyas’s narrative. Few may differ because it is a critical edition and has critically removed multiple incidents prevelant in several of those version while missing on many other. But remember, no single written version can be considered the original one; together they represent the authentic original version
