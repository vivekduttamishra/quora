Was Arjuna respectful towards women as some are calling him a womaniser?

📅 Jan 23, 2021 01:52 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Before trying to answer the question let us understand the meaning of the word womaniser —

Womaniser — pursue casual sexual relationships with multiple women

— Definition of WOMANIZER

Was Arjuna an Womanizer

No. Arjuna had multiple wives. And polygamy was a prevelant and acceptable tradition of those times. Having multiple wives wasn’t considered as adultry or womenizing.

Take example Arjuna liked Shubhadra. But he didn’t go one for just sexual advances, but aksed for her hand from his brother and married her with her family’s conscent. He on the suggestion of Krishna abducted smiling Shubhadra. But it wasn’t for a casual sex.

Take the example of Ulupi. It was she, who approached Arjuna. She said that if he doesn’t love her back, she would die. Does this make Arjuna a womanizer? At Ulupi, didn’t think so. In her words, it was a dharma.

He refused to make love with Urvashi. Does this show Arjuna as a womanizer?

And it was so different from some of the other people of the time, who wanted to see other’s wife naked in a chamber and later enjoy seeing them suffer in tree bark in exile.

Arjuna’s conduct towards women

Arjuna’s conduct towards women is clear throughout the epic. We must understand that Pandavas were one, when it comes to respect the women.

  • He always respected her mother and that is explicitly mentioned in the book.
  • They spared Hidimba, although his brother had tried to kill them all.
  • They rushed to protect women of Duryodhana’s camp when the Gandharvas vanquished Kauravas and forced Karna to flee. The incident takes place after Draupadi had been humilated by same folks. But Yudhishthir’s instructions where clear. Women of the family can’t be humilated by the enemies. Do we need a greater proof of their conduct towards women?
  • Arjuna remained for 1 year in agyatvas in woman quarter teadching dance to the princess of Matasya. There is no mention of any ill-conduct by him.
  • After the victory in Matasya battle, out of graditude, King of Matasya offered her young dauther to Arjuna. But Arjuna instead of marrying a young princess, requested her to be married off to his son Abhimanyu. Does this incident give you any clue about Arjuna’s conduct towards women?

THE GREATEST PROOF

Lord Krishna had left for his heavenly abode. Dwarka’s was destroyed in a civil war, leaving behind a population of unfortunate wailing women. Arjuna was devastated loosing his greatest friend, philosopher and guide. His divine weapons, his inexhaustible quiver all had gone.

What did Arjuna do in this case? Did he pounce upon those poor women as a womanizer? Did he enslave them? Did he ask them to be stripped like some other great heros? Did he ignore them and went on his way? NO.

Arjuna appointed himself as their guardian, took them to his guardianship and started his journey back to Hastinapur. His path was blocked by a band of bandits. Arjuna’s weapon failed him. Then what did he do? Did he flee the scene like some other warriors in the past who fled leaving their family and friend behind? Again, No.

He fought the bandits. When his arrows were gone, he used Gandiva as stick to hit them. Robbers fled with several women, whom Arjuna failed to protect. Some went on their own accord. But Arjuna did manage to save a lot of them which he couldn’t have, had he fled. What Arjuna did next was something remarkable. Lost and devastated as he was, he devoted his energy in rehabilitating those women and their family at safe places. Those family weren’t all of his friends. It included the family of Kritvarma, one who not only faught from Kauravas side but also conspired with Ashwatthama to kill all up-pandavas.
