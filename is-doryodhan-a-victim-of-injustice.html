Is Doryodhan a victim of injustice?

📅 May 08, 2021 06:47 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Wow, what a question.

Name one criminal in the universe who doesn’t say, he is a victim of injustice. Name one criminal whose family, friends, and well wishers, don’t cry that he was wronged.

Like every other criminal of the world, Duryodhana too was victim of injustice.

Let us see some of the injustice done towards him. This is how Duryodhana would plead his case —

Ok I always knew, my father wasn’t a real King, but a caretaker. So what? Does that mean I can have no ambition? That I shouldn’t be the king? So what if my cousins are better than me! After all they don’t have to suffer pangs of jealousy! They have the right to the throne. So I had to take the matter in my hand and device ways to destroy them. I tried to poison Bhima. And he survived! Why couldn’t he simply die? I guess I was given a fake poison vile by the shopkeeper! Can you see, I am not just a victim of injustice but also corruption. Alas! I can’t complain against. I tried multiple attempt to kill them. My friend helped me, and so did my uncle and brothers. Don’t you see I am the victim of injustice. Destiny sided with my enemies!

I again consulted my best friend, brothers and uncle. Then I confided in my father and hatched an indigenous plan to burn my cousins and their mother in a house of lac. They would have died and I would have become King. But that treacherous Vidura foiled my best led plan! He protected Pandavas from their sure death. Isn’t that an injustice? My father’s own minister plotting against my conspiracy! Shouldn’t Vidura have been tried for treason? Yes, someone foiled my plans and he had no business in poking his nose into. And see the injustice, I couldn’t even punish that evil Vidura for his treachary.

I participated in Drauapadi’s swyamvara. So waht if I failed to win the swyamver term? It was an archery competition not hatch a conspiracy. So what if I was no good in archary. How can they insult me? Other Kings were right in trying to teach that arrogant Drupada a lesson. We were about to throw his daughter in fire. That would have been a lesson. And had we succeeded we would have created a legacy. Don’t you see our legacy lives on? Look around you. You call them acid thrower. Those are true lovers who got failed to win the girl of their choice and following our suite they just want to deface those girls. Had we succeeded that day, today you would be honoring those acid throwers. But that evil duo — Bhima and Arjuna came forward to stop us and our legacy. My friend, the great archer tried to counter Arjuna and while I attacked Bhima along with Shalya. So what we were pushed back and we got scared. We were never warriors but conspirators!

But why did Pandavas have to come in our way? Why couldn’t they just die or stayed low? First they saved themselves from fire and now they protected Draupadi! What would you call this if not injustice? But I alone didn’t suffer this injustice and whole generation of future lovers would be condemned as a criminal if they try to follow my suite. Had I succeed…

My father and I tricked Pandavas during partition and gave them barren land of Khandava infested with snakes and deadly wild lives. Wasn’t that the ultimate justice? Didn’t Pandavas loved forests? After all they were born in forest and took shelter there after Lakshagrih conspiracy? Why did they transformed the barren land to Indraprastha? Why did they show their valour? Why did they perform Rajsuy yagna? Had they remained poor and miserable, I may have left them alone. Or may have ventured out to mock them from time to time. But that would have been the end of it.

But that evil Yudhishthira became the emperor and I was still a King in waiting? Why fate had to favor Pandavas? How was it my fault if I am greedy, jealous and deceitful? All I wanted was Pandavas to suffer.

My uncle helped me won Indraprastha by deceit. By hook or crook, I robbed my cousins of all their wealth. All now all I wanted was a little celeberation, a little fun. I had my chance to make my legacy again. Draupadi was the same woman I tried to burn down years ago. Now all my best friend wanted was, that she be stripped in public. Was that such a big deal? Why Dharma needed to interfere? Grave Injustice! Bhima swore to kill him and all my brothers for this silly prank! Shouldn’t he be called evil? Even my father did injustice to me. He returned all my winnings back to Pandavas. Ok. He corrected his mistake by forcing them to rematch.

I won again and exiled Pandavas for thirteen years. That was a fun time. What was the harm in my friend’s suggestion that let us go out on a picnic and mock Pandavas in their misrey. Why did that evil Gandharva interfere? And that too with such a grand force that even my best friend got scared and ran away. Why did fate had to mock me? And Pandavas protected me from Gandharvas. So what? Do they expect me to repent? Ha… Ha… Ha… They must complete their exile. Or better perish during their exile.

And I thought they perished! Why couldn’t they simply die in peace? It was a simple robbing expedition. We were trying to rob Matasya when their general Keechak died. After all, a man need to survive. With Pandavas gone, I needed a new target. Someone else to rob. But damn! that Arjuna. Did he really always need to interfere? He protected Mastya. Aone! And shouldn’t he be called a robber? He stripped us all from our cloths! Oh! gambling was so much better. I had a winning chance.

And if they were hiding in Matasya, couldn’t they have stayed their forever? Didn’t they already have jobs there. Why did they become greedy? They demanded five villages! Can you imagine! Last time they converted a barren land to Indraprastha. What would they not do with five village? Nah! Why should I keep fighting them hoping they won’t come back! Let us kill them once an for all. So I opted for war. For once, I decided, no conspiracy. And see, I lost that one chance. That is called injustice.

Had Pandavas not adhered to Dharma, they would have lost. We had such great warriors. Why did they adhere to Dharma? I never did. Why couldn’t they stoop down to my level? Why I got cousins who were so righteous that I became villian? Don’t you see cousins fighting for property everyday — both parties equally evil? Why my greed had to become the epic?

Don’t you see, I am the victim here?


Disclaimer: The answer is a satire against those who mock our epic and try to portray villian as hero or victims. It reflects the view point of a villian who things he was a victim.
