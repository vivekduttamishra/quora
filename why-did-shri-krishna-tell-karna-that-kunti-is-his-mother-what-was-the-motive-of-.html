Why did Shri Krishna tell Karna that Kunti is his mother? What was the motive of Shri Krishan behind it?

📅 Oct 09, 2020 04:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi,

Thanks for the A2A.

A visionary that Krishna was, could see the devastating consequences of the approaching war. Even Pandavas tried their best to avoid the war.

But insensitive and arrogant Duryodhana was adment on wedging the war on the advice of his counselors that included Karna.

Krishna knew that Karna was the trunk of Duryodhana's evil gambit and if he could persuade him at any cost, he might prevent the disaster. He thought knowing the true identity of his mother and brothers who were good people, Karna would feel motivated to do the right thing for everyone —

  • He would have done a great service to the society by preventing the war.
  • He would have done a great job of saving Duryodhana and his brothers and thus fulfilling his true duty as friend. Even Karna knew, as he confessed to Krishna, war was not winnable for Kauravas.
  • He could have been a bridge between Kauravas and Pandavas.

But that would also mean end of Karna Arjuna rivalry, something not acceptable to him. Coupled with his skewed sense of loyalty — skewed because he actually rejected the chance of any salvation for his friend.

So Krishna's attempt failed. Even identity of his true mother and brothers didn't deter Karna from the path of certain destruction. It, in fact, made him more bitter towards his mother — the one who had got her the divine protection he so cherished.

But Krishna motive of revealing the name was out of his compassion and overall well-being. That said, Krishna was aware of the outcome. Nevertheless he tried, hoping against hope…
