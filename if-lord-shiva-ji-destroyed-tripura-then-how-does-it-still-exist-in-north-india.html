If Lord Shiva Ji destroyed Tripura, then how does it still exist in North India?

📅 Mar 29, 2021 01:23 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This problem had to come, eventually. The writing in the sky was clear. And guess what we ourselves dug the grave of our great civilization.

What would you expect from a culture that has nor just acceptet, but takes pride in using the distorted pronunciation for the words that belong to their culture.

In modern vocabulary, शिव is now pronounced Shiva (शिवा), राम has become Rama (रामा), कृष्ण becomes Krishna (कृष्णा), अर्जुन becomes Arjuna (अर्जुना) and then there are some worst. Thakoor (ठाकूर) becomes Tagore. This is a very, very long list.

Do you realize the absurdity and stupidity of this question now? Figure it yourself. Let me give on a few hints. Do you know the whole story? The three sons of Tarkasura — Taraksha, Kamalaksha and Vidyunmali — after his death prayed to Brahma to get three fortified cities (पुर)। That is Tripur (त्रिपुर)। The demons of these three cities were called Tripurasura (त्रिपुरासुर)

Here we are talking about three (त्री) cities (पुर) and their demons (असुर)। There is no Tripura here. Tripura is a beuatiful Indian state known for its eternal beauty and ancient hertiage and tradition. The only relationship of Tripura with Shiva, it is his infinite blessing on the land.


CALL FOR ACTION

A humble but urgent request. If we want to reverse the process, it must start with us and today. Let us stop calling names that invaders gave to our culture. Let us use the names शिव, राम, अर्जुन and not the suffix them with a distorting aaaa syllable.

We must be the change we wish to see in the world

—Mahatma Gandhi


EDIT #1

I felt the need of adding this section based on a couple of comments I received. They are wondering if I condemn the ‘a’ suffix in names, how come I used the suffix ‘a’ in three names Tarkasura — Taraksha, Kamalaksha. One gentleman even asked how come I have ‘a’ in my names Vivek Dutta Mishra.

Looks like I need to expand the answer to make better understanding. Letter ‘a’ suffix, doesn’t have problem. Problem is when letter ‘a’ is made to sound ‘आ’

The letter ‘a’ represents the swara ‘अ’ and not ‘आ’ . Without the letter ‘a’ the word is considered incomplete.

Consider the word shakti. What does letter ‘a’ represent?

  • sha → श . Here ‘a’ completes the word
  • Notice there is no vowel between ‘kt’ That makes the joined word.
  • Letter ‘i’ represents vower इ

Consider another scenario. How would you represent शिवा the name of Parvati? How would it be different from शिव?

  • Shiva → शिव
  • Shivaa → शिवा

Similarly, the suffix ‘a’ in names like Rama, Krishna, Yoga, Shiva is the vowel sound अ and not आ

Hope that clarifies the doubt. Remember its not letter ‘a’ but the wrong sound आ that is wrong and should be avoided.
