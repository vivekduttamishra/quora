Do you think Krishna in the Mahabharata did commit adharma multiple times but escaped the criticism by giving convincing arguments and proving it as dharma?

📅 Apr 24, 2020 11:58 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

The question belongs to a category that can be said — Questioned and Answered.

Let us consider the second part of the question first —

Krishna escaped the criticism by giving convincing arguments and proving it as dharma?

Now here is a few follow up questions —

  • Who criticized Krishna? What is their credentials? What are their arguments?
  • Who decided which arguments are correct?
  • When Krishna’s provided convincing argument proving his act as Dharama, Where does the first part of question come from?

Krishna in the Mahabharata did commit adharma multiple times…

  • Who says this? Ved Vyas never did.
  • When did Krishna commit Adharma?

Krishna is the incarnation of God who clearly mentions the prime reason of his manifestation —

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

—Gita Chaper 4 Sloka 8

To establish Dharma! And now some great minds here argu he, who incarnated to establish Dharma committed Adharma.

To be fair, this contradiction is not completely unwarranted. Such confusions are quite rationale. Why else do you think Krishra had to recite Gita to Arjuna? Krishna mentions this reason two and clearly when he says —

evaṁ paramparā-prāptam imaṁ rājarṣhayo viduḥ
sa kāleneha mahatā yogo naṣhṭaḥ parantapa

—Gita Chapter 4 Sloka 2

The great wisdom of yog (which includes Dharma) is lost over a period of time. And it needs to be re-established.

Over a long period many local traditions, cultutres and protocols become so prevelent that they tend to defocus the essense of Dharma itself. And since the defintion of Dharma gets polluted people may mistake an act of compassion as Adharma. But that would be a misinterpretation —

  • Why Krishna saving Draupadi wasn’t a Adharma? Didn’t Krishna interfered into the court proceedings of a soverign state?
  • Didn’t Krishna committed Adharma by showing his Virata swaroop in Hastinapur court? Was it not against court protocol to show your power. Cant Vishwaroom be considered a weapon and a threat?
  • Wasn’t Krishna teaching of Gita an Adharma as he provoked Arjuna to fight?

Well if these aren’t adharma, as we know they aren’t, I don’t see what action of Krishna can be argued as Adharma.

To reestablish dharma Krishna hinted he incarnates again and again (sambhavami yuge yuge). Why again and again?

Because society keeps misinterpreting the true meaning of Dharma by arguments just like this — their own belief becomes Dharma, those objecting becomes Adharma. Then you need a Krishna to reincarnate again and again.

How to re-establish

By reminding the society that Dharma and traditions are different and protocol is not Dharma and often contradicts Dharma. The purification involves contraditing many ideas which have been mistaken as Dharma. And that is where people get consued about Krishnas action.
