Who was the greater warrior, Karna or Arjuna, according to original phrases from the Mahabharata?

📅 Feb 22, 2020 12:59 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Mahabharat text has a very clear answer on this question, which has been emphasized by Bhisma, Drone, Ashwathama among others.

But before we delve in there we must define who is a warrior? He who is trigger happy? He who lusts for war? Or he who questions the need of war and puts his weapon down? I guess you see a definitive narrative here from Mahabharat.

There were few Mahabharat incidents that sheds more light on this —

  1. During vanvas, Karna and Duryodhan follows Pandavas to mock them. Gandharvas capture Duryodhan, Karna fled the scene; Arjun frees Duryodhan after a victorious fight against Gandharvas.
  2. In viraat battle Arjun bested Karna twice.
  3. Before killing Abhimanyu as a part of pack of hounds, Karna was bested again.

Karna may have defeated Arjun if…

But that remained a speculation not once turned reality.

But even if he had defeated Arjun — had he become a warrior or a warmonger — that's the question.
