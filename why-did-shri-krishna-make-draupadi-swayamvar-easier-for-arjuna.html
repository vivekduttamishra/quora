Why did Shri Krishna make Draupadi Swayamvar easier for Arjuna?

📅 Sep 21, 2020 01:20 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks Praveen for A2A.

I don’t know what is the source of this question; I believe must be the TVShows people are watching these days. None of the original epic say anything about Krishna’s role in Draupadi’s swyamvara other than the fact that —

  • He was present there
  • He recognized Arjuna
  • He was happy to see them alive and winning.

I don’t know which of these facts suggests that Krishna made Draupadi’s swyamvara easy for Arjuna. As per original references both BORI and KMG —

  • Krishna was not sitting with Draupadi.
  • He never advised Draupadi to reject Karna. Draupadi never rejected Karna
  • He did no magic to help Arjuna aim the bow.
  • He didn’t get involved in the battle that follwed — neither for nor against Arjuna.

In the swayamvara —

  • Arjuna won the swyamvara on his own merit.
  • Arjuna wasn’t carrying any of his weapons. Arjuna had not come in possession of Gandiva as of then. He fought with the bow he had just won in Swyamvara.
  • When King attacked Bhima and Arjuna they fought them on their own.
  • Bhima vanquished Shalya.
  • Arjuna Karna fought violently to vanquish each other. Finally (as per the epic verses) Karna scared away after comparing Arjuna with Rama, the personification of the art of weapons, Indra and Vishnu. He ignored the standing challenge of Arjuna.
  • Seeing Karna scared away and Shalya thrown on the ground, other Kings decided to stop the fight.

Now tell me where exactly Krishna made the life easy for Arjuna during or after the Swyamvara?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to

  • The English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG)
  • Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition
  • Neelkanth Edition of Mahabharat (Geeta Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
  • Mahabharat adaptation by Shri Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
