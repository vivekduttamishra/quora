If Karna has brahmastra, then why didn't he use it against Bheem's son? Was it necessary to use Indra's weapons?

📅 Dec 22, 2020 02:27 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Before I attempt to answer this question, let us see two references —

Indra’s instruction to Karna while handing over the Vasvi Shakti

O Karna! You will again possess the complexion and energy of your father. Your complexion will again become like his. But if you unleash this invincible weapon in a fit of fury, when you possess other weapons, there is no doubt that it will descend on you.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 , Section 42

Karna couldn’t have used Vasvi Shakti except as the last resort. If he had any other weapon, he couldn’t use it without it back firing. What does that indicate?

If there is still some doubt lef let us move to Ghatotkatch Vadh Parva.

His divine weapon had been destroyed by the maya. He began to think about what should be the appropriate course of action. All the Kurus saw Karna and the terrible maya. They said, ‘O Karna! Quickly kill the rakshasa now with your spear.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 6370-6372). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Karna’s divine weapons were destroyed and he was thinking of what options were left to him. It is important to remember once again, he couldn’t have used the Vasvi Shakti as long he had another weapon left to fire.

‘“Karna made up his mind to use the spear. Unable to bear the assaults made in the battle, he was like an angry and intolerant lion. That supreme spear was always victorious and he decided to use it to kill him. 271 O king! He had preserved it for many years, worshipping it for the sake of Phalguna. Shakra had given that supreme spear to Karna in exchange for the earrings. That spear was resplendent and flaming. It was tied with ropes and in the night, looked like Death. Flaming like a meteor, it was like Death’s sister. Vaikartana released it towards the rakshasa.

Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 6376-6380). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

You may see, Karna was unable to bear the assault and thus he hurled the spear which he had preserved for Arjuna. And the spear successfully destroyed the target is a proof that he wasn’t left with any powerful weapon.

What about Karna’s Brahmastra

We must understand, divine weapons like Brahmastra weren’t like a gun or a nuclear weapon — you trigger and they would destroy. They required invocation using right mantras which was confirmed in Mahabharata again and again. Karna certainly had learnt it from Parshuram. But it was through deceit. Parshurama had predicted it won’t be of much use in the hours of need —

The qualities of a brahmana will never remain with someone who is not a brahmana. Leave this place, since this is not meant for an untruthful one like you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2405-2406). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Now was that because Karna wasn’t a Brahmana? But Brahmastras worked in the hands on non-brahmanas. So why not for Karna? Parshurama answered that too — ‘You acted in this false way because of your greed for weapons.

And we don’t see any of the Karna’s weapons (other than Vasvi Shakti) killing any warrior of repute, though they certainly massacredlot of common soldiers.
