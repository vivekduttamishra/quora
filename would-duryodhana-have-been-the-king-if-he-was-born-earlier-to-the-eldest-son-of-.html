Would Duryodhana have been the king if he was born earlier to the eldest son of Panduraja, despite Pandurajas being crowned as a king by disqualifying Dhritarashtra?

📅 Sep 30, 2020 12:03 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi thanks for the A2A

In an alternative narrative —

  • Duryodhana may have been a kind hearted person.
  • Dhritrashtra may not have been blind.
  • Bhisma may not have taken the oath of celibacy.
  • Pandu may not have died
  • Pandavas may have been killed in Lakshagrih.

But that is an alternative narrative.

Keeping all the equation constants and changing only what this quesiton is expecting, there are two ways to look for it —

  1. The eldest son of King becomes the King — Dhritrasthra wasn’t really the king, but a caretaker. So no matter, what the age he was not entitled to the throne. But that is not the real case. At least this is not represented in Mahabharata as such.
  2. The ablest successor should be the Kin — As per the tradition of Bharata, only the ablest person should be the successor. Bharata himself denoucned his sons for the throne. Even Shantanu wasn’t the eldest son of his father and yet he was crowned as the king. So age won’t really matter. Now Duryodhana himself expressed his fear to his king father in Lakshagrih Parva, that Yudhishtir his more preferred for the king by the public because of his ability so much that people may install him as the king removing Dhritrashtra

So under the current narrative, Duryodhana won’t have been the king even if were elder than Yudhishtir.
