What is difference between Pashupastra and MahaPashupastra?

📅 Oct 31, 2021 08:05 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

MahaPashupata isn’t a name, it is an emphasis on the ‘greatness’ of Pashupata weapon. ‘Maha’ in Sanskrit means great.

The favorite and the terrible weapon of Mahabharata is the most potent weapon mentioned in all the great Indian epics and great literatures. This is the weapon that consumes the universe at the end of the Kalpa.

Krishna, Arjuna, Bhishma, Dhritrashtra and Karna, everyone at some point in time emphasizes the greatness of this weapon.

WHY FEW PREFER TO CALL PASHUPATA AS MAHAPASHUPATA

While the famous weapon of Mahadeva is called just Pashupata, there is absolutely no harm in calling it Mahapashpata which based on Sanskrit and Hindi grammar can be split into Maha+Pashupata, that is The Great Pashupata. I repeat there is absolutely nothing wrong in this term.

Now what necessitated the coining of this term is a grave confusion which has been spread unnecessarily by a segment of people challenging the words of Mahadeva.

While granting Pashupata to Arjuna, Shiva himself confirms in no unclear term that no humans of god have the knowledge of this great weapon. Here is what Shiva said —

‘The illustrious god said, “O Pandava! I will give you the great pashupata weapon, dear to me. You will be capable of bearing, releasing and withdrawing it. The great Indra, Yama, the king of the yakshas, 292 Varuna and Vayu do not know it. How can it be known to humans?

Even Bhishma mentions it to Dhrirashtra with these words —

‘“Shantanu’s son glanced at him and said, ‘O king! Transcend your anger and listen to my words. O Duryodhana! You have seen how the intelligent Partha created a stream of water that is cool and bears the fragrance of amrita. There is no one else in this world capable of accomplishing this feat. Agneya, Varuna, Soumya, Vayavya, Vaishnava, Aindra, Pashupata, Brahma, Parameshtha, Prajapatya, Dhatu, Tvashtu, Savitu470 and all the other divine weapons—among all the men on earth, Dhananjaya alone is the one who knows them. So does Krishna, Devaki’s son. But no one else knows them. O son! 471 It is impossible to vanquish Pandava in battle. The deeds of the great-souled one are superhuman. He is spirited in battle. The brave one is the ornament of any battle. He is accomplished in battle.

— Bhishma Vadha Parva

And despite of a clear pronouncement by Shiva, few people disregarding his words, try to emphasize that other warriors of the era too had Pashupata. I must emphasize those people are fabricating the story, simply mis-representing it.

WHAT IS THE REASON BEHIND THESE CLAIMS?

The claim takes the basis of two narratives that we should we looking at.

First let me take you to the second claim. Krishna says following words to Arjuna on the seventeenth day of the battle —

He(Yudhishthira) is established in the strength of the brahman, but he does not possess a great deal of strength of the kshatriya variety. O Partha! I do not think that Yudhishthira, the great king, will remain alive, even though Bhimasena is with him and is roaring intolerantly, like a lion. O scorcher of enemies! The sons of Dhritarashtra are roaring repeatedly. Desiring victory in the battle, they are blowing on their giant conch shells. O bull among the Bharata lineage! Pandaveya Yudhishthira will be killed. Karna will urge the immensely strong sons of Dhritarashtra towards Partha. He will shroud the king with sthunakarna, indrajala and pashupata and the maharathas will follow him.539

— Karna Vadha Parva

Many people take Krishna’s word here as a gospel of truth that Karna had a Pashupata weapon, instead of realizing that it was Krishna’s maneuver to motivate Arjuna. A careful examination would prove it beyond doubt —

  1. Krishna tells Arjuna that Karna would kill Yudhisthira, knowing well that Karna had taken a pledge that he would not harm Yudhisthira. The name of those weapons were to motivate Arjuna. Krishna often used those techniques.
  2. Also see, here he emphasizes that Yudhishthira doesn’t possess a great deal of Kshatirya strength and Arjuna must protect him. It was to make Arjuna responsible for protecting Yudhishira. Later to motivate Yudhishira in killing Shalya Krishna says —

He also possesses dexterity. In an encounter, he is like Bhishma, Drona and Karna. The king of Madra may even be superior to them.

I do not see any warrior who can fight against him. He is like a tiger in his bravery. Barring you, there is no other man in all of heaven or earth. O descendant of the Kuru lineage! In a battle, there is no one else who can kill the angry king of Madra.

—Shaya Vadha Parva

So is Krishna lying? No simply making each warrior believe that should do their best as only they can handle the current situation. It is boosting the confidence.

Now the first reference is Drona using Pashupata in Drona Vadha Parva —

Drona could not establish his superiority over Pandava in any way. The one who was skilled about the motions of weapons then invoked special weapons—aindra, pashupata, tvashtra, vayavya and varuna.67 However, as soon as these were released from Drona’s bow, Dhananjaya destroyed them. When his weapons were duly destroyed by Pandava’s weapons, Drona released supreme and divine weapons towards Partha. However, every weapon that was invoked with a desire to vanquish Partha, was duly destroyed by Arjuna with another weapon.

— Dorna Vadha Parva

Drona invokes a Pashupata which was easily countered by Partha. But how is that possible?

There is actually a very simple explanation to these references. One of the names of Shiva is Pashupati. As such any weapon of Shiva can be called as Rudrastra or Pashupata. It is possible that Karna or Drona may have possessed a lower level weapon and call is Pashupata.


WHY NO ONE COULD HAVE THE GREAT PASHUPATA?

Is there any reference to prove the fact that no one else had great Pashupata, or is it just my biased assertions without any backing evidence.

First and foremost reference is Shiva’s own words which is conclusive and beyond reproach. But then there are other logical evidences to the fact.

  1. It must also be observed that it wasn’t an easy task for even Arjuna to obtain this weapon. He obtained the weapon only after years of penance followed by a severe test by none other than Mahadeva. And, yet, it wasn’t the end of the journey. Shiva himself taught Arjuna the use of this weapon. Now how can anyone without such a sever penance or Shiva’s grace obtain that weapon? There is no such reference in case of either Karna or Drona.
  2. Now assuming Drona obtained this weapon somehow, there was no reason for Arjuna to do any penance or please Shiva, as being the favorite pupil of Drona he would have received it from him the same way he got Brahmsira. But he still goes through penance. Why?
  3. Why did Bhishma say that no one other than Arjuna had Pashupata?
  4. When Karna expends his Vasvi dart, Krishna was too happy and said now consider Karna as good as dead. Why wasn’t he worried about Karna’s Pashupata?
  5. Perhaps one of the most siginficant of references, Karna himself admits he doesn’t have the Pasuhpata like the one Arjuna has —

He faced the immensely generous Mahadeva himself in a battle and satisfied him.683 He thus obtained the extremely terrible pashupata, the great weapon that can destroy the three worlds. The various guardians of the world assembled and gave him weapons that have no measure.

— Karna Vadha Parva

Two Pashupata Theory

Two explain all these points, what we can safely agree is the possibility of two distinct Pashupata weapons —

  1. One the great Pashupata weapon which Shiva announces was available only to Arjuna and Shiva himself granted not only the weapon but also taught Arjuna as to how to use it. This was the most terrible weapon that Bhishma, Arjuna, Krishna and Karna all agree was immensely powerful.
  2. Some other weapon of Pashupati Shiva, that gained the name of Pashupata, perhaps in the same way as even Satyaki was called Madhava and Arjuna and Draupadi were known as Krishna.

Then the question arises how do we distinguish between the two?

Hail MAHAPASHUPATA!

If we really take up the two Pashupata theory to explain the entire situation, how do we differentiate between the two? Naturally by emphasizing the first as “The Great Pashupata.” But then, how do you call the great Pashupata in Hindi or Sansrkit — Mahapashupata!

So, hail Mahapashupata, the great weapon of Mahadeva.
