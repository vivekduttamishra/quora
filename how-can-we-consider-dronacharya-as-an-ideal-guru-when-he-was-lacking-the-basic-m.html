How can we consider Dronacharya as an ideal Guru when he was lacking the basic moral judgement that made him to support the wrong, why there should be an award named after him?

📅 Feb 08, 2021 06:57 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Let me rephrase the question and that may answer your question — We have a Drona award because we deem him as a magnificent coach. Are you sure you are not letting your perception overshadow the wisdom of the epic?

Ok, while you ponder over the incident, let us analyse the man in two parts.

Drona — the perceptor

The goal of a great teacher is to produce worthy students. Did Drona produce worthy students?

Of course. And I am not talking about just Arjuna. He created a generation of great warriors. He created warrior of such calibre that they can vanquish an entire army. Didn’t Arjuna vanquished Panchala and paid his Gurudakshina?

A great teacher re-invents himself to provide innovative teaching aids to the students. Drona times and again proved it. Remeber the famous bird story. But then there is story of how Arjuna became expert in night fighting and some more.

I saw a few answers who would like a guru to give all knowledge to everyone. The fundamental idea is flawed and distratous when it comes to milletary education. Remember, there is a thin line gap between an army officer and a terrorist.

It would be the responsibility of a great teacher to ensure he is producing warriors and not terrorists without bother if someone calls if partial. Look at Arjuna, he was entrusted with Brahmashira and Pashupatastra. Did he use them in his personal fight? No. Look at Karna, when he got Vasvi Shakti. Could he resist using it? So Did Drona again qualify as a great teacher.

If he wanted to produce just one great student, he would have rejected everyone. But he taught most of them. Rejected one for higher weapon and other for any education. Can you guess why? Look where they ended up and you will have an answer to Drona’s grand vision.

Drona — The person

Let us meet the person Drona. Drona had high hopes from his gurukul friend. His best friend. He betrayed him. He insulted him for being a poor Brahmin and not reconized him for the person he was.

Stung with insult Drona, with the agony of having failed his child to procure even a glass of milk for him, he liven in agony. For many years. Then Bhishma hires him to train the princes of Hastinapur. He gave him a comfortable and dignified living. He offered his a strature.

Years later Drona found himself on the cross road. On one side was his favorite desciple, dearer than his son, whom he knew was righteous. On the other hand was Bhishma, his benefector. He had a tough choice to make. What is his Dharma —

Support his favourite student or repay his debts? Could he have betrayed Bhishma, the way Drupad betrayed him? No. loyalty was the chief trait of Dronacharya. He wasn’t going to abandon Bhishma. And he wasn’t going to abandon his students that included even the evil Duryodhana. He times and again tried to make him see err of his way, make him see the right path. But no one could.

Once Drona decided to side with his benefector Bhishma, there was no turning. His hand never wavered. He never betrayed his army. He never gave anyone a lifeline even if that meant killing the son of his most favourite desiciple.

Who would be a better example for being a great teacher other than a great honorable man?

Drona’s action didn’t lack moral judgement, it defined what a moral judgement really mean.
