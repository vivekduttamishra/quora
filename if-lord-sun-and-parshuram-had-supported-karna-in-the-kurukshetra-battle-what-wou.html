If Lord Sun and Parshuram had supported Karna in the Kurukshetra battle, what would have happened?

📅 Jan 17, 2021 01:26 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Interesting Question.

If Parshurama had supported Karna, then Kaurava would have been vanquished the battle much earlier marking 22nd inahliation of evil by Parshurama.

Confused?

You see, the only equation in which Parshuram would support Karna, was when he sided with righteousness and give up his evil design.

Parshurama was the vanquisher of evil twenty-one times over. In no alternate theory he would have sided with evils. Didn’t he himself proclaimed, no one could defeat Arjuna and Krishna in a battle?

Same goes true even for Surya. He was the father of Karna. But he didn’t support him in the battle. Did he? The best he did was to —

  • Protect his life by warning him about the expected arival of Indra
  • Advising him to listen to Kunti and follow her advice.

He certainly wanted to save the life of his son like any father. But didn’t support him in the war — he didn’t give him any divine artifects, didn’t support him on his chariot and didn’t protect him. Why? Because Gods don’t protect evil. They continously strive to set us in the right course. Even Krishna tried. But when you defy such calls, you are alone to face the dance of justice..

The righteous people won’t change their ways. To win their grace patronage, you need to be worthy of it. And if Karna had become worthy of their grace, he won’t be fighting of evil side.
