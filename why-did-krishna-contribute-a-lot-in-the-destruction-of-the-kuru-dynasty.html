Why did Krishna contribute a lot in the destruction of the Kuru dynasty?

📅 May 11, 2020 12:02 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Krishna himself answered this question —

yada yada hi dharmasya
glanir bhavati bharata
abhyutthanam adharmasya
tadatmanam srjamy aham

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

—Gita Chapter 4, Sloka 7,8

Every time, whenever, there is a decline of Dharma, and rise of Adharma, I will incarnate — to preserve the good ones, to vanquish the evils, and to reestablish the Dharma. I shall incarnate again and again.

But then the question is —

Did Krishna really contribute to the destruction of Kuru dynasty?

  • Did Krishna poison Bheem?
  • Did Krishna conspired to burn Pandavas alive?
  • Was it Krishna who cheated in dice game?
  • Did Krishna tried to molest Draupadi?
  • Was Krishna a moot spectator to Drawpadi's humiliation?
  • Didn't Krishna offered peace deal to Duryodhan?
  • Didn't Krishna tried to show the right path to Karna?

No. Krishna didn't destroy Kuru dynasty, their own karma destroyed them.

Based on a comment below that Krishna could have prevented the war and didn't and that's why Gandhari cursed him, here is my observation —

There are two parts here —

  • Gandhari cursed as a grieving mother who lost all his sons and as someone having no understanding or interest in big picture — Dharma. She also regretted her curse upon realization.
  • A common misconception here is that war was something worth avoiding, and this is questioning the wisdom of Krishna and not understanding Gita. Preventing the war was letting Adharma prevail.
  • If Krishna was supposed to avoid the war, then no country should war against terrorism, or invasion.
