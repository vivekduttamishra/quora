Did Karna in the Mahabharata have Bharmashira? If yes, where is it written in the bori cricital edition?

📅 Mar 24, 2021 10:55 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: No.

WHAT DOES BORI SAY?

There is not a single explicit reference in entire BORI edition that Karna possessed Brahmasira and if you look through the entire epic in chronology, you would know why it was unlikely for Karna to have it.

First, we must understand Karna had three gurus who could have provided him with the knowledge of Divyastra. We don’t have any mention of Kripacharya giving the weapon to Karna. Dronacharya flatly refused to provide those weapons to Karna, and that is the source of a widespread rumor that Drona didn’t teach Karna.

That leaves Parashurama and it is from here comes the wishful thinking that Parashurama gave all the weapons to Karna, and that must have included Brahmsira. Now there are two points I must make absolutely clear —

  • No single shloka in entire BORI anywhere explicitly mentions Brahmsira as one of the weapons possessed by Karna. This leaves room just for speculations like
    • Since Parashurama gave all the knowledge, he must have…
    • Since he is mentioned as the knower of Brahma weapon…
    • Since they say he knew four kinds of Dhanurveda…
  • What is important to understand that even if Karan obtained divine weapons from Parshurama, he obtained it under the false pretext and Parshurama made it abanduntly clear that those weapons won’t manifest for you when the need arises.
  • Since Brahmasira was a higher Brahma weapons and Parashurama says that the weapons he got by deceit wouldn’t work for him, there is no point of debate — He had something that he won't be able to use, because he would forget to use them when the time comes. Do you call this a possession?

After leaving Parshurama, Karna never ventured into further learning path — at least none is mentioned in the epic. He didn’t please any God or any other teacher unlike Arjuna, who never really quit learning. So there is no possibility of anyone else providing him with Brahmsira knowledge — against at least none is mentioned in the epic.

One of their favourite quotes is from Yudhishthir in Kairata Parva —

भीष्मे द्रोणे कृपे कर्णे द्रोणपुत्रे च भारत |

धनुर्वेदश्चतुष्पाद एतेष्वद्य प्रतिष्ठितः ||४||

—BORI CE, Kairata Parva

The Shloka translates to —

The four parts of dhanurveda 250 are today established in Bhishma, Drona, Kripa, Karna, and Drona’s son. 251 They know all types of brahma, divine and asura weapons, how to release them and how to counter them. They know the use of all weapons and how to counter them. All of them have been pacified by Dhirtarashtra’s son.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Edited Translation)

Note in the English translation by Bibek Debroy the name of Karna is missing and I would consider it a mistake or an omission. But since Mr Debroy is merely a translator and not the authority, we should stick to BORI, so I took the liberty of including Karna’s name in the translation.

Now, let us focus what does Yudhishthir say? He says Karna is among those who know four parts of Dhanurveda. But does that include Brahmsira? Again there is no explicit reference to it.

Yudhisthir further says, they know the use of all weapons and their counter. But we know three things for the fact —

  1. Shiva himself proclaimed no one knows Pashupata. That means there are weapons excluded from all kind of weapons. It just includes all common kinds.
  2. Yudhistir says they know all about weapons and their counters, but we know Ashwatthama wasn’t wel-versed with Brahmastra. He didn’t knew how to retract it.

So was Yudhishthir lying? If you believe something to be true, say so in good faith, it is not a lie. Pashupata was a weapon not in existence with any mortal or normal Gods. Yudhshithir couldn’t have known about it to include with anyone. Again he wasn’t one of the greatest archer to know those intrinsic details.

WHAT DOES KARNA’S CHARACTER SAY

Karna wasn’t a warrior who knew much restraint. He exhausted up all his great weapons in the battle including Vasvishakti and Bhargavastra. On 17th day, he knew it would be his last day, so there was no reason of not using Brahmsira if he possessed one. But most important thing to note is Karna’s own statement, which being an expert archer is more valuable —

He faced the immensely generous Mahadeva himself in a battle and satisfied him.683 He thus obtained the extremely terrible pashupata, the great weapon that can destroy the three worlds. The various guardians of the world assembled and gave him weapons that have no measure.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4372-4374).

Karna admits, he has no measure for the weapons possessed by Arjuna. Do we really need some indirect proves, when an explicit direct prove exists?

Let us also understand, Karna sought out Vasvishakti from Indra in exchange for his divine armour and worhsipped Ugrasena (the serpent), both with a single goal — Kill Arjuna? Why he needed Vasvis Shakti or the help of a serpant to kill a human if he had godly weapons with him? Because he knew Brahmastra won’t help him, he having achieved using deceit.


TAKEAWAY

  • There is not a single reference in the entire epic to make explicitly attribute Brahmsira to Karna.
    • Common references provided in any answer below or else where are just trying to argue on a possibility.
    • They are projections, not references.
  • The only possibility of Karna to have Brahmsira is from Parshurama. And Parshurama himself declared the weapon would be rendered useless for Karna. So if Karna got Brahmsira from Parshurama it was as good as he didn’t have it. And there is no other possibility.
  • Karna used all his powerful weapons like Bharagava, Vasvi and Nagastra. He never used Brahmsira.
  • He himself admitted he as no measure (to counter) weapons of Arjuna.
  • Krishna, who was worried about Karna’s Vasvishakti didn’t really bother about Brahmasira. He knew Karna is a pacified fire, and as good as dead. You can’t claim something for the wearer of a Brahmasira.
  • The pre-condition of using Vasvishakti was to have expended all other option. I said that in my other answer related to a similar question if Karna had Pashupata . Karna manages to fire Vasvishakti successfully and that means he met precondition. Are we to believe Brahmsira (and Pashupata) failed against Ghatotkatch? (credit: Rakesh Basak who asked me to add this point).
  • Sometimes the simplest explanation is the fact. If hundreds things clearly point out to something, it doesn’t really leave ground for suppositions.
