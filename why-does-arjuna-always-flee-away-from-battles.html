Why does Arjuna always flee away from battles?

📅 Feb 26, 2021 09:35 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I am sure you know the answer. Interesting thing is, you even know the absurdity of your question. So let me humour you.

No one can stand in battle field forever, they eventually leave — specially the victors; vanquished often stay there dead. And some how you mistook, returning back from the battle-field as fleeing.

Here are important incidents, when Arjuna fought and retreated —

  1. Gurudakshina battle — He went to Panchal, but eventually retreated back to Hastinapur.
  2. Swayamvara battle — He went ahead to defend Drupada and Panchala against hostile guests. What happened next? Arjuna retreated to the forest.
  3. Khandava battle — He fought at Khandava against the enemies of God. Then he retreated back to Indraprastha.
  4. Rajsuy battle — He engaged in the battles and the retreated back to Indraprastha.
  5. Indraloka battle — He fought millions of demons and then retreated back to earth.
  6. Gandharva battle — He fought Gandharvas to free evil Duryodhana and his team. Then he retreated back to his camps.
  7. Matasya battle — He fought against entire Kuru army and then again retreated to Matasya.
  8. Kurukshetra war — He retreated eighteen times at the end of each day of the battle.
  9. Ashwamegha battle — He once again retreated finishing his campaign.

Do you know, what is common in all these stories? He was victorious each time. But even in victory, you need to retreat back. But you may be surprised to know this victorious retreat is not known as fleeing.

Fleeing is when you run away in fear, desperate to save yourself even if it means leaving your loved ones, even your best friend and family behind. It is bartering life for values. And I don’t recall one scenario where Arjuna actual fled on this parameter.

But this story won’t be complete without mention of two battles where Arjuna retreated with no physical victory, and I consider the two as greater than any other victory.

  1. Brahmastra Battle against Ashwatthama — Ashwatthama murdered the sleeping people in Pandava’s camp — their sons, princes of Panchal. When Arjuna confronted he wielded Brahmastra. Arjuna to wielded the Brahamastra. And finally Arjuna retracted his Brahmastra and retreated. Why? Because he was the only one capable of retracting the Brahmastra. Ashwatthama didn’t because he couldn’t. He could have killed Ashwatthama, but he still retreated. Why? Because he had the power to forgive.
  2. Battle against Dwarka Robbers — Arjuna had lost his best friend. He had lost his divine weapons. Desparate and dejected, when he was retreating from Dwarka towards Hastinapur, a band of robbers attacked him. Arjuna was with thousands of Dwarka women — Krishna’s wives and other Dwarka woman along with non-combatant old soldiers. He alone fought against them. Robbers took away few women with them and Arjuna couldn’t protect them all. But Arjuna continued to fight till robbers retreated. People say Arjuna lost the battle — lost because he failed to protect every single women. But they miss two important point —
    1. Had Arjuna fled, he won’t have protected anyone, but he did protect most of the dwarka women including wives of Krishna.
    2. He weapons failed him, but his valour didn’t. Even in that dejected condition he ensured rehabliation of every family who were not his responsibility — even the family of Kritvarma who accompanied Ashwatthama in the night masscare of Arjuna’s family. That’s what I would call a valour.

Thanks for allowing me to elaborate why Arjuna fled all the battles.
