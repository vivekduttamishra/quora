Despite knowing that Ashoka caused so many deaths, why does the Indian flag have the Ashoka Chakra to show so much respect to him? Do you think the chakra should be replaced with some other emblem?

📅 Aug 25, 2020 07:33 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

The Game of thrones has never been a bed of roses. The deaths you refer to are the side effects of the wars. As long as there is war, there is greed, there would be deaths. And when all those wars and those deaths cease, there would still be deaths. Death is inevitable. What you should see in Ashoka, the great, is a capable general powerful enough to defend the country and its boundary. Tell me why that shouldn't be cherished?

Ashoka is also a symbol of transformation — from ruthless violence to benevolent spreader of peace, nonviolence and Dharma. It is probably the greatest redemption story of all time. What could be a better symbol of progress than the Ashoka Chakra?

But the symbol you see doesn't belong to Ashoka the ruthless conquerer of Kalinga, it belongs to Ashoka Priyadarshi — one who conquered a larger part of globe by spreading Buddha's message, one whose conquest is significant even today, and not just for Indian nation but beyond. A nation's flag is also its identity to the world. What can be a better symbol than to remind the world of that nonviolent cultural conquest of India from a time when many of modern religions didn't even exist?

Do you know what is more dangerous for a nation? It is to cast a doubt on its national identity. It is a proposal to distort her national emblem. It is to disrespect your own flag. Do you know how many brave souls died with the pride that they were wrapped in Indian national flag? Do you know how many bravehearts wear this symbol proudly on their head, shoulder and chest?

Jai Hind!
