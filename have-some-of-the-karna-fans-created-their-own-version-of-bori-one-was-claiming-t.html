The problem is not BORI footnote, many cling to a few scholarly articles, which based on their own skewed logic and not on reference make such tall claims. Before going forward some of those baseless claims include —

  • Pandava may be just two, not five — only Yudhishthir and Bheem.
  • Maa Durga was Mahisasura's…
  • Sita was…by many

They have no references. Just theories and illogic.

Coming to the current context —

WHY DRAWPADI'S STRIPPING ATTEMPT IS NOT AN INTERPOLATION

  • Every single ancient script mentions the attempt to disrobe Draupadi. A CE's goal is to weed out inconsistencies and not write its own story. Contrast this with Draupadi rejecting Karna — just 5 out of 1259 ancient script mentioned it. That's were BORI CE acted and removed it.
  • BORI clearly includes it in the main text. Footnote raises question on Krishna's role and justifies its stand on deviation from ancient narration. It doesn't remove stripping.

This should settle the matter, but…

WHY FEW SCHOLARS CLAIM DRAUPADI CHEERHARAN AS INTERPOLATION

  • They claim (also claimed by a fellow answerer) that there is not a single claim of Cheerharan and as such this must be an interpolation.
  • The claim says, while on several places, Krishna, Draupadi and Pandavas have mentioned the incident of Draupadi being dragged to court by Duhsassan, there is no cross reference of the stripping.

FACTS

  • We have the cross-reference in every single ancient reference. This itself is one of the greatest cross-reference.
  • Most of the incidents, indeed, didn’t mention stripping. This shouldn’t be a suprise because of two reasons —
    • Stripping didn’t really succeed. No point in mentioning it.
    • It is hard to spell out the most tormenting incident.
  • There is actually a direct cross reference, shattering the claim that not a single cross-reference exists. It is in Shalya Parva, soon after Duryodhana was punished for his crimes. Here it is —

‘Droupadi was in her season and they disrespected her. They deprived her of her garment there. Behold. Through Yajnaseni’s39 austerities, in the battle, the sons of Dhritarashtra have been slain by the Pandavas.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 8346-8348).

  • Further, the molestation has also been referred in Udyoga Parva, where Ulluk, the son of Shakuni addresses to Pandavas —

Remember Droupadi’s molestation and be a man. O Pandava! Though impotent, Bhimasena took an oath. If he is capable, let him drink Duhshasana’s blood.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 8100-8101).

  • Here the word used is molestation.
  • Many claim that, Stripping was a common practice for slave. There is no cross-reference to this fact. We see Matasya episode and don’t see any such reference there as well. Refer to Bheema’s statement in sabha bhawan —

Gamblers have many courtesans in their country. But they are kind even towards those, and do not stake them in gambling.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3340-3341). Penguin

  • Kindness was the culture of the era, even for couresans. No one protested as Bheema killed Keechak for his advances towards a servant (Draupadi).

BOTTOM LINE

  • Stripping never succeeded. There is no second doubt about it.
  • Even if they manage to argue that stripping didn’t happen (which is difficult against stack of evidence which BORI considered and approved) —
    • Does it really matter? Draupadi was molested. She was dragged in a single garment to the court of Hastinapur.
    • Bheema took a terrible oath to kill Duhsassana, break Duryodhana’s thigh and kill all Kaurva’s

“O kshatriyas! O those who live in this world! Hear these words of mine, never before uttered by any man and never to be uttered in the future. O lords of the earth! Having uttered these words, if I do not act accordingly, may I never tread on the path followed by my forefathers. In battle, I will forcibly tear asunder the breast of this evil and misguided one, 215 wretch among the Bharatas, and drink his blood.”

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Penguin Translated Texts)

  • Bheema finally fulfills his oath. Is that not a sufficient cross -reference?
  • Scholars, claiming cheerharan didn’t happen, they do not rule out Karna’s role in the cheerharana. So even if they rule out the stripping attempt of Draupadi, they can’t provide any justification about Karna’s role —
    • There are several cross references of Karna’s role in the chamber.
    • Drauapdi is constantly tormented not only because of Duhsassana but also Karna.
    • Arjuna takes a vow to kill Karna.

This is just an attempt of few scholoar, to build a narrative based on a highly flimsy argument, which is devoid of references. These claims are neither made by BORI nor my ancient epic references. Even those flimsy arguments, DO NOT exonarate Karna from his misdeeds.
