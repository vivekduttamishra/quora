Who were the true winners in the war between the Pandavas and Kauravas in the Mahabharata?

📅 May 02, 2022 12:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: Dharma

Both sides of the war suffered huge losses in military terms. Both sides lost many near and dear ones with few left to lament on the outcome. And if it were a normal war on any other day we can believe there were no winners and if we consider war as an isolated incident it goes without saying —

Wars have no winners — utterly demolished and greatly demolished.

But then, it is not just about the war and the economics alone. It was about something much bigger — Dharma.

WHAT WAS THE ALTERNATIVE OF THE WAR?

The alternative of the war was to let evils rule the land. Those evils, in whose rule, even their family women weren’t safe. Not just Draupadi was humiliated in the open court, their foolhardy also put the fate of Kuru women in jeopardy during Ghosha Yatra.

What kind of society would you expect from a council of evils who find more pleasure in seeing their enemy tormented than having their own prosperity or even having sons? What kind of society do you think would have existed where rulers conspire to kill their distracters by burning alive and their relative don’t hesitate to abduct a lone women (Draupadi Harana).

To understand who won, ask a simple question — What was the life in Kaurava’s rule? A child poisoned. Attempt to burn down a family. Attempt to burn down a bride. Deceit. Attempt to molest. Mock their enemy in disguise. Try to loot a kingdom in distress. Now imagine, what would have happened had Kauravas won?

Now let us see the glimpse of Pandavas rule. A prince was ready to die to protect a village from a demon. Another prince was ready to defend a princess in distress where monsters tried to burn them alive. A prince willing to accept exile to protect the cows a poor brahmin. Even in exile protecting the honor of that family that not only deceitfully grabbed their wealth but dishonored their wife. A family ready to fight a battle that wasn’t theirs to defend and kingdom that wasn’t their.

Do you still need to ask if there was an alternative of this war?

DID DHARMA REALLY WIN THE WAR?

The true answer to this question can be seen in the aftermath of the war. Dhritrahshtra always sided with his son. He silently endorsed all his crimes. In fact in the crimes of Duryodhana true criminal was Dhritrahshtra. He was the King. War was fought in his name. Was Dhritrahshtra ever tried in the court as prisoner of war? No he was given due love and respect (with exception to Bhimasena) and Dhritrashtra experience pleasure that he never experienced while his sons were alive. Can there be a greater evidence of the fact that Dharma won?

HOW CAN THERE BE A WINNER WHEN ALMOST EVERYONE DIED?

Wrong notion.

Not everyone died. There were heavy losses. Many warriors died. Most of the important generals died. But world didn’t consist of only army. Many died so that people can live in peace and justice. There was all round prosperity thereafter. And that is not something worthless. Had there been no one left, Pandavas couldn’t have performed Ashwamegha yagna latter.

WHY SO MANY PEOPLE NEEDED TO DIE?

Power corrupts. Absolute power corrupts absolutely. There were too many powerful people and they were threats to society. No one forced them to fight. But there egos got better of them. Let us take a few example —

Rukmi offers his support first to Pandavas and then to Kauravas. Both reject him. But ask yourself, what was the reason for Rukmi to fight? Did he plan to fight for a cause or for the sake of fighting for his ego?

Karna had an opportunity to stop the battle by accepting Krishna’s proposal. He knew then, and we know know, it was even in the best interest of his friend. He was to inherit the throne. If he wanted, he could have given the kingdom to Duryodhana. But he chose to fight. Why? For his friend? Think again. He chose not to fight for first ten days of the battle for his own ego.

Trigarth was mostly like a missionary or hire for money.

Narayani Sena was tasked to fight for Kauravas. But Satyaki doesn’t join them. Other too must have similar choices. Why did they fight for Kauravas? Why did Kritvarma support Ashwatthama when he attacked sleeping Pandava camp?

Just like today we talk about nuclear disarmament, a lost of time the only solution to prevent rampant corruption is to destroy the power center.

After the war, Yadavas became a reckoning force in Aryavart. And what did they do? They fought among themselves destroying themselves. That is the outcome of irresponsible power.
