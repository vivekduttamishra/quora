Is Lord Krishna the backbone of Pandavas?

📅 Apr 09, 2021 01:04 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The answer is YES and NO.

Krishna supported Pandavas, and his support was crucial for more than one reason. But we must understand Krishna had to support Pandavas, and it was as important to him as for Pandavas. Why? Same reason Shiva supported Pandavas or Hanuman supported them.

The real backbone of Pandavas was their adherence to Dharma. And always remember —

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah!

So don’t trust a fool who says — instead of protecting one who is devoted, it is now bringing me down. I think that dharma does not always protect. Perhaps those fools don’t understand the true essence of Dharma, and don’t understand the fact that you may even have to pass through fire (hardship) to prove your worth. And to quote from my own book and belief —

“How do you bless a seeker, Muniwar? How did you bless me this morning?”

“Do your duty. Protect dharma.” Sadananda was smiling at such a fierce opponent; he also knew if it’s a trick, it won’t last long. Dharma, like a serpent, would coil and strangle anyone trying to play with it.

—The Accursed God

Dharma is never partial. And it spares no one. Even those who follow Dharma need to prove by going through many trials. Even they are not spared — how many people suffered in their life more than Pandavas?

DID KRISHNA ALWAYS PROTECT PANDAS?

Krishna did protect Bhima from getting poisoned. Krishna didn’t protect Pandavas from Lakshagrih. Krishna didn’t interfere in Draupadi’s swyamvara. Krishna didn’t make their life easy during exile. Krishna didn't fight along side Arjuna with Gandharvas or during Matasya battle.

In fact, Pandavas were on their own for most of their struggle before Kurukshetra war. Krishna indeed advised them on occasions, but never fought their battle — not even in Kurukshetra.

That said, Krishna had to help them. He was duty bound to. He had incarnate to reestablish righeousness. And the path or righteousness required him to support Pandavas.

How many people would constantly ignore conspiracy against them and their family right from the childhood? While Duryodhana and his team consipired and invented new depths of deceit, Pandavas practices to make themselves better humans. Who do you think Krishna would support?

While few were being deceitful to their gurus, few were eager to do anything for them even blindly, be it saving their life or fulfilling their wishes without knowing what they are? Whom do you think Dharma would support?

Some one try to burn you and your family. What would you do? Won’t you expose them? Or would you ignore them and go running trying to make the world a better place by getting rid of cannibals like Hidimba and Bakasura? Would you share your prosperity with them?

You are exiled by deceit, your wife is humiliated in public. What would you do, when you find out, now that person is in peril. His wives are at the mercy of a powerful enemy? Would you run to help them and protect your worst enemy? Or would you run away, leaving your best friend behind? Or would you watch as a spectator as your enemies perish? Well, you perhaps know the answer.

Your enemies once again attack you and you vanquish them. You have a chance to imprison them and kill them while they are unconscious. What would you do?

You suffered all your life. Yet you decide to fulfil the promises you made after a deceitful game. And after all your obligations are over, the enemy has no intention to fulfil their end of bargain. But now you got all the powers of the world and support of the mightiest of the mighty. What would you do? Destroy them? or seek peace for the welfare of humanity?

Who do you think would support Dharma? What option did Krishna have? What option did Shiva have? They can’t break those cosmic rules based on Dharma that they themselves decreed. Remeber —

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitaah
