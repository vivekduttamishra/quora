What is the algorithm used for this counting?

(Human Votes)* B = (Shiva’s Vote)

(Human Votes) * C = (Parshuram’s Vote)

(Modern Author’s Votes)* D = (Ved Vyaa’s Vote)

(Evil’s Votes)* E = (Dharma Vote)

(Contemporary Votes)* D = (Epic Characters Vote)

Ok, once you have calculated these values, here are how they span.

Karna’s Fan: Duryodhana, Dev Dutt Pattanayak, Shivaji Sawant, Ramdhari Singh Dinkar, and few modern authors.

Arjuna’s Fan:

  1. Mahadeva Shiva, Krishna — Both not only admired Arjuna but Also graced him by endorsing him. Krishna considered Arjuna his greatest friend, and Shiva bestowed him with his blessing and Pashupatastra.
  2. The epic era who’s who — Parshurama, Ved Vyas, Bhishma, Drona, Chitrasena or Yakshas, Bhagdatta, Kripacharya, Satyaki, the common folks including the armies of Duryodhana, and Duryodhana who although jealous and scared, was still a fan of Arjuna’s powers.
  3. But the greatest fan of Arjuna was none other than Karna. He lived and died with a single desire to prove he was a better archer than Arjuna. So fascinated was he from Arjuna, from the early days of childhood, that he strived to be like him, nay better than him. Why did he want Brahmastra? To win against Arjuna. Why did he lied to Parshurama? To get Brahmastra, so that he can defeat Arjuna. Why did he made an exception for Arjuna while granting his mother boon of 4 lives? So that he can prove that he is better than Arjuna.
  4. Everyone had many things to look for — Duryodhana was eyeing for throne. Arjuna eyeing for Shiva’s blessing, Krisna’s support and never-ending quest for more knowledge, Yudhishthira lived for his Dharma, other Pandavas had to look for each other and then for their revenge. But for Karna it was Arjuna. Fight Arjuna. Kill Arjuna. Prove to the world he his better than Arjuna. He never wanted to prove himself better than Bhishma or Drona. He never wanted any kingdom and he even rejected Krishna’s offer. Because he wanted…

Tell me who do you think had the greatest follower?

In my counting I have excluded mortals like you and I, who are countless and have heard the whispers of the incidents that occured more than five thousand years ago. Our number is great and their is no official census to prove either way.

That said, this shouldn’t be the question — Who has more followers. The right question should be —

  • Who was on the side of Dharma?
  • Why Ved Vyas called one as the trunk of an righteous tree, while other as tree of lust and passion contrasting with the word righteousness?
  • Whom Shiva and Krishna supported and why?
  • What is the lesson of the story?
  • Who should we followed by fans?

WHY IS THIS NOT A RELEVANT QUESTION?
