Is it true that had Bhishma Pitamah or Danvir Karna not taken side of Duryodhana, the Mahabharata War could not happen?

📅 Apr 03, 2021 10:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

We can’t have definitive answer to any what if situation? But yes, that would be the most probable outcome. But I can see other possibility as well.

Duryodhana depended on the powers Bhishma and Karna. Presence of Bhishma also ensured the presence of three other great warriors — Dronacharya, Ashwatthama and Kripacharya. While Drona was loyal to Bhishma, the other two were connected to Dronacharya.

Duryodhana was powerless without Bhishma. Had Bhishma refused to fight, Duryodhana wouldn’t have dared to go to the war.

On the other hand, Duryodhana had blind faith on Karna’s loyalty and his powers and really believed he could win the war for him. While there was no verifiable evidence to support his faith, he had faith nevertheless. And that is why Krishna tried his best to convince Karna to switch side and give humanity a chance to survive. Krishna knew while Karna couldn’t win the war, he could prevent bloodshed.

As I said, I could see another bleak possibility as well. Duryodhana going to war even without these warriors. While this is quite unlikely, we must understand Duryodhana had run out of his options to hold on to the kingdom. He had tried every low-level deceit and conspiracy. He wasn’t going to let the kingdom go. And don’t forget a desperate mind like a brilliant craftsman, creates wonderful mirage. If not Karna, Duryodhana would have made another mirage and clung to hope that victory is possible. And he may have gone to fight, anyway.

But yes, with Karna, Bhishma and Drona out of the equation, the battle would have been far less bloody and may have finished in a day or two.

But it wasn’t to happen — Bhishma was bound by his oath and Karna by his ambition — There is no alternate timeline here.
