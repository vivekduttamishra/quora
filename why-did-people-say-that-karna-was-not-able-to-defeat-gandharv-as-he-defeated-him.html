Why did people say that Karna was not able to defeat Gandharv as he defeated him in his digvajay and Chitrasena never used any illusion against Arjun in their fight with him?

📅 Mar 20, 2021 10:18 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

There are two parts of this question. Let us tackle them one by one.

KARNA VS GANDHARVAS

It is not a question if Karna ever defeated Gandharvas. The question is if Karna defeated and fled Gandharvas in the Ghosha Parva or not. And here are two important references —

Reference 1

With a desire to kill Karna in battle, hundreds and thousands of gandharvas rushed collectively towards him. With a desire to kill the son of the suta, the immensely strong ones surrounded him from all sides, with swords, javelins, spears and maces. Some sliced off the yoke, 38 others brought down the flagpole. Some brought down the shafts, the horses and the charioteer. Some sliced down the umbrella, others the fenders39 and the diadem. In many thousands, the gandharvas shattered the chariot. Holding a sword and a shield in his hand, the son of the suta jumped down from the chariot. He leapt onto Vikarna’s chariot and whipped the horses, so that he might escape.’ 528(231) Vaishampayana said, ‘O great king! When maharatha Karna was routed by the gandharvas, in the sight of the sons of Dhritarashtra, the entire army fled. O great king! On seeing all the sons of Dhritarashtra flee, Duryodhana refused to run away.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6393-6400).

And if anyone carry a wishful thinking that it was a strategic retreat and Karna was returning to rescue Duryodhana, it was contradicted by Karna himself.

Reference#2

When night was over, Karna came to Duryodhana and spoke to him. “O son of Gandhari! It is fortunate that you are alive and fortunate that we have met again. It is fortunate that you have vanquished the gandharvas, who are capable of assuming any form at will. O descendant of the Kuru lineage! It is fortunate that I see all of your brothers. They are maharathas and have been victorious, triumphing over the enemies in battle. While you looked on, I was worsted by all those gandharvas. My own army was routed and I was incapable of ensuring that they remained there. I was sorely wounded by the arrows and hard-pressed. I ran away. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! I think it is extraordinary that I see you here, without harm and without injury, and with all your wives, riches and vehicles. You have freed yourself from that superhuman battle. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! O great king! There is no other man in this world who is like you, or can achieve what you have done in battle with your brothers.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6525-6532).

Note:

  • Duryodhana release was secured by Pandavas the previous day after a terrible fight.
  • Yudhishthir granted him to leave. Duryodhana camped for the night.
  • When the night was over, Karna came. It was too long a time for a rescue mission. Had Pandavas been not there, there would be no one to rescue.
  • Karna accepts he ran away.
  • He says it is fortunate he is seeing them safe. Does that suggest he was trying to rescue them?
  • Karna says, no other man in the world could have achieved the victory. Does it sound like someone who would try to fight them back?
  • There is no explicit reference anywhere that Karna ever intended to save Duryodhana or his family that included royal and other women.
  • Also, contrary to popular misconception spread by BRC Mahabharata, there is not a single reference that suggests Karna was drunk during Gandharva war.

This is the precise reason, people including Karna said, he was routed and he ran away. Whether he defeated them later or not is not relvant. By the way you may be knowing BORI doesn’t have Karna’s Digvijaya Parva included.


ARJUNA VS CHITRASENA

I don’t see, how this part of the question is related to the first part unless someone really wants to deman Karna further. The assertions made here is as flawed as execuses made to cover Karna’s cowardise.

Here is the relevant reference from BORI —

On seeing the gandharvas terrified by Kunti’s intelligent son, Chitrasena grasped a mace and rushed towards Savyasachi. In that battle, he held the iron mace in his hand. But with his arrows, Partha sliced the mace into seven pieces. On seeing that the mace had been cut into many pieces by the arrows of the nimble one, he57 made himself invisible and began to fight with Pandava. Established in the sky, he repulsed those celestial weapons. The powerful king of the gandharvas hid himself through his powers of maya. Arjuna saw that he was invisible and was striking. He therefore attacked him with divine weapons that could travel in the sky, invoked with mantras. Arjuna was angered. Dhananjaya used the weapon known as shabdabheda 58 and restrained his disappearance into many different forms. Pierced by the weapons of the great-souled Arjuna, the gandharva revealed himself to his beloved friend. 59 On seeing his friend Chitrasena, fighting feebly in battle, 60 the bull among the Pandavas withdrew the weapon he had released.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6480-6489).

  • Busting the fake claim made by this question, Chitrasen used all illusion (maya) against Arjuna. He made himself invisible and attacked Arjuna.
  • Arjuna by his shabdheda weapon, destroyed his illusions, something others couldn’t.
  • Again contrary to popular narrative Chitrasen was fighting feebly (not mildly). Feeble is a sign of weakness; mildly a sign of exhibiting lesser power than one is capable of.
  • It was Arjuna who stopped his weapon on realizing Chitrasena has lost.

The two part of the question had simple aim —

  1. Provide a cover for Karna’s cowardise.
  2. Tone down the victory of Arjuna.

Guess the question fails on both grounds.
