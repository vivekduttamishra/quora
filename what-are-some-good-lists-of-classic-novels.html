What are some good lists of classic novels?

📅 Feb 09, 2021 09:32 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Suggesting just one book would be an injustice to the universe. No one book, no matter of great a classic, can quench the thirst of everyone. Then what about the language and genre boundary?

So, I assume that by classic we are talking about English classic fictions. There is a treasury of great works in this section including the famous War and Peace, Pride and Prejudice, A Tale of Two Cities, almost the entire work on Sherlock Holmes and the list goes on…

But if I have to pick up just one book, I would say none other than —

THE COUNT OF MONTE CRISTO by ALEXANDRE DUMAS

This book has continuously inspired not only millions of readers across globe and genre but many eminent authors like Jeffery Archer and many more books and movies.

I must warn, it’s not an easy read. Over 1500 pages in small print and you may have to wait for a couple of chapters to jump into the roller and coaster that continues thereafter, though at places the narrative becomes slow.

Rarely I have seen a book that has done justice to so many emotions in one book — A journey from betrayal to revenge, from despair to riches, from ignorant innocence to master of deception, from impatient love to chilling patience and from history to fiction. There are occasions where you are aware of what would happen next and yet you would jump out of your seat while reading the book.

Sadly and understandably, none of the many movies created on the book could reach even remotely close to the greatness of this master piece. Understandably, because of grand tale of this length can’t be converted to a single movie. A movie series or TV shows, maybe.

Once there was a young and naïve sailor Edmon Dantes. Fortune was knocking at his door. A promising carrier as a ship captain, marriage to his beloved. And then…

The journey of simpleton Edmond Dantes to that of The Count of Monte Cristo would be a fascinating read for everyone. It is fascinating for me even after four complete reads.
