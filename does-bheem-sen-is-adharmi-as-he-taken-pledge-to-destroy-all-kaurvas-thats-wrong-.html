Not only are the hunters sinners, those who watch from the sideline too would be tried for their apathy!

Unfortunately other Kaurava brothers weren’t just bystanders, they were co-conspirators, they committed the sin of following a sinner. They always had a chance to unlink their destiny with that of the sinner, yet, they chose not to. They were thick as thieves. And they paid the price for their decision.

Bhima hasn’t been called a sinner — neither for his oath nor for the fact that he fulfilled it.

We must also understand that Bhima didn’t kill his enemies like a murderer but as a warrior. He didn’t try to poison them. He didn’t try to burn them alive. He gave them a fair chance on the battle field to protect themselves — in fact it was eleven to seven in their favour.

Another important point, that the questioner apparently forgot is, Bhimasena not only killed them, but also protected them when they were busted by Gandharvas. He could have left them to perish. But he did protect them. That was honorable. Bhimasena also tried for a peaceful agreement with Kaurava’s indicating that he could have let them live forgoing his pledge. So who was really responsible for the death of Kauravas — Bhimasena or Duryodhana? Who brought them to the battle field? Who wanted to defer the battle?
