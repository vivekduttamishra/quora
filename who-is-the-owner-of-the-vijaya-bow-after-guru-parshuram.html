Who is the owner of the Vijaya bow after Guru Parshuram?

📅 Jul 16, 2023 08:38 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

As per the chain of custody, Vijay bow NEVER came in possession of Bhagwan Parshurama. NEVER!

Here is a direct reference from Bhishma Avishechana Parva chapter 818 where Vaisampanya tells about the Vijaya bow along with Saranaga and Gandiva and who has it —

Those who reside in heaven possess three divine bows—Varuna’s Gandiva, the great Indra’s bow Vijaya and Vishnu’s Sharnga, which is said to be a celestial bow that is full of energy. Krishna wields it and strikes terror in the soldiers of enemies. The son of the chastiser of Paka19 obtained Gandiva from the fire god in Khandava. The immensely energetic Rukmi obtained Vijaya from Druma. Hrishikesha obtained the supreme bow Sharnga after destroying the nooses of Mura, killing the energetic Mura, killing Naraka, the son of the earth, and obtaining sixteen thousand women and many jewels. 20 ‘Rukmi obtained the bow Vijaya, which roared like a cloud and was capable of terrifying the universe.

—Bhishma Avishechana Parva chapter 818

As is clear, the bow never came to Parshurama’s possesion.

What If Parshurama had Vijay Bow?

If Parshurama had such an excellent bow, capable of terrifying the universe, he must have had used it against his famous fight 23 day long fight against Bhishma. But he didn’t. Not only the bow finds no mention in there but it also clearly mentions that Parshurama had to change several bows. He wasn’t really averse of using divine weapons as he used guhyaka and Varuna weapon. So question is why didn’t he use Vijay bow against Bhishma? Or Did Bhishma destroy it? Or the only possible outcome would be the fact mentioned by Vaisampanya earlier — Parshurama didn’t have the custody.

What is Parshurama had Vijay bow?

If you still wish to be wishful and consider that Vaisampanya lied and Parshurama foolishly didn’t use it against Bhishma, he would have given it to Drona when he donated all his weapons to him. Here is the reference for the same —

Bharadvaja’s son went to him and said, ‘O bull among Brahmanas! I am Drona and I have come to you for some riches.’ Rama said, ‘O Brahmana! Now I have only my body left. Ask for either my body or my weapons.’ Drona said, ‘O illustrious one! Give me all your weapons, together with the knowledge of releasing them and recalling them.’ The descendant of Bhrigu13 agreed and gave those to him. On receiving them, Drona concluded that he had become successful.

— Chapter 154, Adi Parva (BORI)

Parshurama gave all his weapons to Drona. All. Now had Parshurama really had Vijay bow, it would be included in this “all” weapon.

So, now Drona would have been in possession of Vijaya bow. But no where in Mahabharata, Drona has ever used this bow. Neither anyone mentioend about it.

Could it be that Parshurama gave it to someone else?

Yes, I think this was the answer questioner was seeking. It might have gone to Karna. Before we embark on this journey let us quickly see three facts —

  1. Vaisampanya clearly mentions that Vijay bow belonged to Rukmi
  2. Parshurama didn’t mention owning this bow. He didn’t use it against Bhishma
  3. Parshurama gave away all his weapons to Drona. Now he couldn’t have given it to Karna

Why people think Karna received the bow from Parshurama

Because he himself proclaimed it in front of Duryodhana before the seventeenth day of the battle. He said —

My bow, Vijaya, is supreme among all weapons. Vishvakarma294 constructed it for Indra’s sake. It was with this that Shatakratu vanquished large numbers of daityas. In the ten directions, the daityas were confounded because of its roar. Shakra gave that revered weapon to Bhargava and Bhargava gave that divine and supreme bow to me.295 With this, I will fight against the mighty-armed Arjuna, foremost among victorious ones, like Indra fighting in a battle against all the assembled daityas. Rama296 gave me this terrible bow and it is superior to Gandiva. It was with this bow that he297 conquered the earth twenty-one times. Bhargava told me about this bow’s divine deeds and Rama gave it to me.

I also possess a supreme, great and divine bow known as Vijaya. O king! Therefore, in the matter of bows, I am superior to Partha. Listen to how that brave Pandava is superior to me. His reins are held by Dasharha, who is revered by all the worlds. His divine chariot is decorated with gold and was given to him by Agni. O brave one! It is impenetrable in every way and his horses are as swift as thought. His standard is divine and, with the resplendent ape atop it, causes wonder. Krishna, the creator of the universe, protects that chariot. Though I am inferior to him in these respects, I still wish to fight with Pandava. But this brave Shalya, the ornament of assemblies, is his298 equal. If he acts as my charioteer, there will certainly be victory.

— Chapter 1172, Karna Vadha Parva

Why should I NOT trust Karna words?

There are several reasons —

  1. Karna has a constant habbit of lying through out Mahabharata. He introduced himself as “Bharava Brahamana” to Parshurama to obtains the science of weapon. He mis-interprets Shashtra without reference to abuse Draupadi and Vikarna. He never reveals to Duryodhana that he won’t kill four Pandavas and that perhaps was his greatest lie. He knows how to manipulate people in his own interest. Here his interest was to obtain Shalya as charioteer
  2. Vijay was Indra’s bow. And yet he wasn’t worried about Karna having it. Why? Why didn’t he ask his bow along with Karna’s armour?
  3. Parshurama tralks about the valour of Partha and his excellent bow Gandiva. But fails to mention that he gave a more powerful bow to Karna. Why ?
  4. Krishna was worried about Karna’s armour and Vasvi weapon, but considers his as good as dead once he loses those two artifects. But why was he not worried about him having Gandiva?

A little introspection will reveal the truth.
