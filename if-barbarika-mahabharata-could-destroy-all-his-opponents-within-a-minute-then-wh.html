Thanks for the A2A.

There are few confusions —

  • There is no mention at what interval Barbarik would decide to switch sides — every half minute, every second, every 100ms.
  • Barbarik doesn't really exist to show his Valor in authentic Vyas’ Mahabharat.
  • Barbarik, most probably, overrated himself when he thought he could vanquish either side under a minute. Both sides have great warriors armed with powerful celestial weapons — Think what would have happened if K
    • Karna would have countered his weapon with Indrastra which is defined as never failing?
    • Arjuna would have fired the Pashupatastra, the most powerful weapon of the time?
  • What do you think Barbarik or his weapon achieved against Lord Krishna, when they couldn't hurt a leaf under lords feet?

What is the lesson of Barabrik's story —

Power doesn't create warrior — ethics and conviction to the cause does. Lord doesn't allow senseless and brute display of power.
