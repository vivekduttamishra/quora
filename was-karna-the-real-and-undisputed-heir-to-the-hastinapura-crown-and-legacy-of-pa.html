Was Karna the real and undisputed heir to the Hastinapura crown and legacy of Panduraj being the eldest son of Panduraja’s eldest wife? Was it the reason why people like Bhishma, Dhritarashtra did not reveal it though they knew it?

📅 Sep 14, 2021 08:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: Karna was neither real nor undisputed heir of Hastinapur crown.

Karna wasn’t the son of Pandu. Pandu never knew about him, never adopted him. That is why he can’t be called the eldest Pandava or heir to the throne.

Let me explain this by siting another example. After the death of Vichitraveer, what was the need of Niyoga for birth of any heir to the throne? Why wasn’t Ved Vyas invited to take on the throne? He had as much claim to the throne as Karna had, which actually amounted to NIL. Just like Karna, Shantanu never knew about the existence of Ved Vyas and never adopted him either as his son or the heir to the throne. Just like after the death of Shantanu, the question cease to exist, so with the death of Pandu, Karna’s any claim cease to exist.

So may ask, why then, Krishna offered him the crown? Krishna wanted to avert the apocalyptic war. At any cost. He also knew that Yudhishthira would be in his right to pass on his throne to whosoever he like. He also knew that once Yudhishthira knows his identity he would not hesitate in relenting his throne in Karna’s favor. But that would have been a charity like one Duryodhana did and not his legal right.

Think, Yudhishthira was known and real heir of Hastinapur’s crown. But does that fact made life any easier for Pandavas or Hastinapur? No. when you want to create dispute, there would be multiple reasons. I believe Bhishma was wise not to kick the hornet’s nest.
