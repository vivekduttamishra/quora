Were Pandav warriors helpless in front of Karna in the Mahabharata War at any time?

📅 Jan 07, 2022 07:29 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The war is a tricky thing and more importantly unpredictable. The formation may push people against the walls and even the best warriors may find themselves helpless. That said, Pandavas were never more helpless in the war than seeing Abhimanyu die. And in that battle too Karna was more helpless than Pandavas. He saw his own brother killed. There was no Jarasandha stopping his way. But he still chose to flee.

But then Karna had his rare moments of glory — the ones where he captured and released four Pandavas keeping his promise to Kunti. You may count those situation as one Pandavas being helpless at a time. But were they in any real peril may be a question that can have no satisfactory answer. On day 14 after losing to Bhimasena for several times Karna finally overpowered Bhimasena. But then what happened was interesting. Arjuna arrived at the scene and Karna, like always, helplessly fled for his life, rescued by Ashwatthama.

Some Karna fan relish Bhargavastra episode as the only occasion where Arjuna appeared helpless to counter the weapon. However, a little reflection of the situation will reveal few points of interest —

  • Arjuna was worried about Bhargavastra and could see no way to counter it. Now question arises was Bhargavastra more powerful than Brahmsira and Pashupata? If so, why it didn’t win the war for Kaurava? If not, why was Arjuna worried about it?
  • Even a more interesting question — Why was Krishna not worried? Why did he want to convince Yudhishthira that today Arjuna would kill Karna? How can Arjuna kill Karna if he was unable to face Bhargavastra? And why did Krishna say that Bhargavastra would tire Karna?

If you understand the nature of divyastra, you would find that there is a single explanation that can tie of all the facts. Divyastras were not supposed to be fired on a weaker opponent. And Karna had fired it on ordinary soldiers of Pandavas side. Arjuna was worried, how can he counter a divine weapon without initiating a higher weapon something he was reluctant to use. He had clearly said that to Yudhishira before the start of the battle and even recalled one he fired on Ashwatthama. So Arjuna main worry was to avoid collision of divine weapons and he saw no escape from it. On the other hand, Krishna was sure, his irresponsible actions would tire him. He wasn’t worried. He didn’t see the need of Arjuna engaging in this tussle. He asks him to let us go and ensure Yudhishthira of his decisive victory.

I see no other explanation that fits all the elements in this discussion. If you read the text Krishna asks Arjuna to leave and there is no mention or context that calls fleeing.
