Why did Bhishma allow Shakuni to stay in Hastinapur even when he knew the cunning strategies of Shakuni?

📅 Dec 05, 2020 08:30 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This question assumes (based on TV shows) that Shakuni was the mastermind from Kaurava's side and for some reason held grudge against Bhishma and Kuru dynasty and wanted its destruction. Every part of this fabbled construction is a lie.

Shakuni wasn't as cunning or mastermind or as important as shown.

Shakuni never had a grudge against Kuru dynasty. In fact he always acted in the welfare of Kaurava's. He wasn't the mastermind for any but one of the conspiracy against Pandavas, albeit the greatest one that brought him to fame.

For most of the time, Shakuni is a little minon following Duryodhana, more like Shambha follows Gabbar Singh and just because Gabbar Singh asks Shambha how many bullets are there, doesn't make Shamba great mastermind.

Shakuni was a part of all of Duryodhana's crime. But he was a side kick not the chief strategist. Lakshagrih was Duryodhana's own idea. To go and mock Pandavas in exile was Karna idea.

The only claim to fame Shakuni had, was his active role in the game of dice. From planning, to execution. And his role promptly ended once the game was over. It was Duryodhana who ordered Draupadi to be dragged into the assembly, it was Karna who ordered to disrobe her. Not Shakuni.

In fact Shakuni never wanted war and advised Duryodhana to make peace with Pandavas. Even dice game was his technique to avoid full scale war.

By the way Shakuni, in the authentic epic is shown as a worthy warrior, something that doesn't go well with modern depiction.

No wonder he is referred to just as branch of the tree that is evil Duryodhana — not the root and not the trunk.
