Why did Prabal Kamra convert to Arjuna fan and betray Karna fans?

📅 Mar 08, 2021 08:39 PM PST  |  🌐 English

No one can or should answer this question on the behalf of someone else. But the question is not related to an individual but to a phenomenon, so I would dare to answer. And I would do so also because, I am qualified to answer this question.

I am qualified to answer this question because I regret long back, almost three decades, I too admired Karna.

I had read Rashmirathi by national poet Ramdhari Singh ‘Dinkar’, and anyone who read one of the greatest master piece in Hindi poetry is bound to be influenced by it. I had believed the story where Karna pushed Arjuna's chariot by three steps back and Krishna showered his praises. I had read, how Drona refused to teach Karna and the emotional Karna Parshuram incident. Then I also learnt, how Karna donated his golden tooth to Krishna on his death bed.

They were awe inspiring stories. But that is all they were — epic violating, praise of an arrogant, selfish abusive coward, whom the epic and Krishna called selfish more times than praised him.

I realized this when I read the original epic. And in that original epic —

  1. Karna was taught by Drona.
  2. Karna was envious of Arjuna and this envy was the prime reason, Drona refused him the knowledge of Brahmastra.
  3. He lied to Parshuram, and got cursed.
  4. A Brahmin cursed him, and hinted the reason of curse as his malice envy towards someone, who would finally kill him.
  5. Karna wasn't denied entry to showcase his skills during play arena because of caste or any reason. He was stopped from wedging an uncalled for, unprovoked duel.
  6. Karna was part of every Duryodhan's conspiracy against Pandavas since gurukul days, including Lakshagrih.
  7. Karna was part of those kings who tried to burn Draupadi alive.
  8. He was the chief instigator of Draupadi's chirharan attempt.
  9. While he rejected Krishna's and Kunti plea to switch sides and prevent millions from dying, in the name of loyalty to Duryodhana, he didn't enter the war for first 10 days for his ego — both loyalty and Dharma took a back seat.
  10. Abhimanyu wadh, once again proved what Karna really was.

As, I said, knowing all the truth, it is embarrassing to think long ago, I admired this trunk of the evil tree. As I said, this is not just about me, but its a phenomenan. I have seen so many making this transition, many on quora itself.

Afterall, whats the point in keeping your eyes shut and continue to stay put knowing the truth? We are humans and it is the nature of humans to evolve. We are not dumb stones, inert speicies who dare not accept our mistakes.

So why many continue to be a Karna’s fan?

Same reason many continue to support Rahul Gandhi. They know the truth. But one, it is easier to stay in your comfort zone rather than face the harsh reality.

But the most important reason why people are afraid of making the transition is the Harassement like this question. People who are too weak and ignorant to see the truth, are harrassing people who could see the truth, they dare not see. Because they aren’t willing to change, they are afraid to those who dare to.

I answered this question in the hope that few find courage from my admission of guilt and gather courage to see, what is right —

Krishna calls Arjuna his inseparable existence — He who hates him, hates me. He who loves him, loves me. He finds Karna evil and persuades Arjuna to kill him. Must be some reason, right?

NOW, MY QUESTION TO THE QUESTIONER?

  • How can you continue to be loyal to someone who knew no loyalty?
  • How can you admire the conniving, abusive trunk of the evil tree?
  • All Karna fans, want to prove was he was a great warrior — stronger than Arjuna. Say he was. Say he was stronger than Rama, Krishna and even the God of Gods, Mahadeva. So what? Does that make him good? All that would prove, he was a strong evil man. Is that sufficient for you to admire him?
  • Raavan, Hrianya Kashyapa, Karna and Hitler were strong. Very Strong. But they were also evil. Great Evil. And they were destroyed.

I would conclude with a warning — You can’t prevent the phenomenon. You can’t reverse it. Anyone who begin admiring Karna and comes to a debate is bound to read the authentic text. And once you know the truth, you would do a transition. You may argue anything you like, you may provide any justification you like. But deep within, you would know the reality. You may dare not accept it, but that doesn’t mean you don’t know it yourself. So all that is lacking is courage (same as Karna) to admit the truth and make a transition.

Anyone recognizing the truth is braver and wiser than before and should we welcomed to Krishna consciousness.
