Do you think Draupadi should have taken divorce from her five husbands after they gambled her and didn't save her?

📅 Apr 26, 2022 01:40 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Honestly speaking, it doesn’t matter in the least, what you are I think about the matter. The only opinion that mattered was that of Draupadi herself. And she never expressed her view of separating with her husbands. No once. Not even immediately after the gambling as she was dragged into the court. Not only did she not blame Yudhishthira, she strongly advocated his helplessness in that matter in front of the court. Looks like she trusted her husbands more than the question is willing to give them the credits. Why was that? Couldn’t it be that she realized that her husbands gambled her as the last resort only after losing their own freedom?

Did she never resent their inactions. She certainly did. Who wouldn’t. But then she didn’t express them in front of deceitful evils. She expressed them to Krishna. She cried on her helplessness. That is what makes you human. But she also understood the helpless of her husbands and trusted their terrible oaths. That tells something about their bonds that wasn’t so weak that one incident would crack it. She shared their exile although she might have stayed in the comforts along with Subhadra. That was Draupadi. She knew next time some Jaidradh or Keechak would set an eye on her, their husbands won’t let them go unpunished.
