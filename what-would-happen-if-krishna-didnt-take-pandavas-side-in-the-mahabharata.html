What would happen if Krishna didn't take Pandavas side in the Mahabharata?

📅 Jul 29, 2022 04:23 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Interesting question. And it is often asked with a wishful thinking. Could there be any alternate timeline in which Pandavas may have been destroyed by the evil forces? I would like to counter this question with another question —

Why would Krishna not have supported Pandavas?

Do you think Krishna supported Pandavas as some kind of undue favour? Are you sure you are not belittling Krishna? Krishna had a reason for his manifestation — Paritranaya sadhunam vinashaya cha dushkritam?

Now tell me what would have happened to this ultimate reason of his manifestation had he not supported Pandavas. Think again and you would find out Krishna as much needed to support Pandavas and Pandvas needed his support. Krishna said this in as many words in the begining of the Drona Vadha Parva —

O tiger among men! I have been born to slay him and also all the others who hate the gods, with your help, and desiring the welfare of the worlds.

— Drona vadha Parva

So think, what could have been the circumstances that Krishna would have abandoned Pandavas? The only one I see is even Pandavas were equally evil haters of God. And if that were the case, we too should abandon them and let them fade in history. But that was not to be.

So let me humor you by assuming —

What if Krishna had not supported Pandavas?

A clear answer exists, even to this quesiton, in Mahabharata. Why did Kauravas tried to kill Pandavas many times by deceit? Because they knew Pandavs were superior to him in every respect. Arjuna was the best student of Drona as Vidura introduced him to Dhritrashtra as the protector of Hastinapur. Bhimasena’s powers were a source of terror for Kauravas even from the young age. All without Krishna taking their side.

Kauravas did try to burn them alive and Krishna didn't save them. They survived nevertheless. How? We shall come to this latter.

Bhima killed demons like Hidimb and Bakasura, something never done by any Kaurava. Arjuna defeated Chitraratha. Again no Krishna here.

Arjuna wins Draupadi's Swyamvar and along with Bhima subdues all the kings including Kauravas. Krishna just watched on.

Bhima defeats Karna and Madri's son Eklavya during Rajsuy battle on their own. Again no Krishna here.

Now let us now move on to Gandharva battle. Kaurava and their army was routed, the so called loyalist fled saving their own life. They were captured. They would have languished in prison had Pandavas not secured their release.

Finally, the semifinal — Matasya battle. All the Kaurava generals were there including warriors like Drona, Ashwatthama and Bhishma along with a large army and regular losers like Duryodhana and Karna. They all were vanquished by a lone Arjuna. No Krishna.

In fact there exists NOT A SINGLE REFERENCE contradicting the above facts to even hint that Kaurava had even a winning chance with or without Krishna. They never won against Pandavas. Not even once. Even Duryodhana never believed that he had a winning chance against them and that is why he always tried to vanquish them by deceit.

DOES THAT MEAN KRISHNA HAD NO ROLE IN THEIR VICTORY?

Krishna was Pandava’s friend, philosopher and guide. Because of him we have Gita. But telling that without Krishna, Pandavas wouldn’t have won is not just belittling Pandavas but also Krishna. Pandavas managed to war for 18 days. Does that mean Krishna wasn’t capable of winning against them before the period? Is that not belittling Krishna? The fact is Krishna mostly observed the battle and didn’t interfered it. And on many occasions his presence didn’t made it easier. Abhimanyu died despite of Krishna’s presence at Kurukshetra. Krishna couldn’t motivate Arjuna enough to kill Bhishma for first nine days and he had to break is oath, not once but twice, to make him oust Bhishma. What does these incidents reflect? That Kauravas were powerful enough to survive against Krishna? Wrong. Think Again. It simply reflects the truth, Krishna didn’t participate in the battle. He just observed and guided.

Few often wonder why did Arjuna take eighteen days to finish a battle, he finished in a single day in Matasya. And there is a logical answer present in the epic itself — Arjuna’s compassion. He could have finished Kauravas in Matasya battle itself. He didn’t. He willed them to live. But Kurukshetra battle was different. Here there would be no conclusion till Bhishma and Drona die. And Arjuna was reluctant to kill either. He refused to use his higher weapons in the battle which could have finished the battle in an instant. No other warrior could resist that temptation. It took nine days for Krishna to motivate Arjuna to remove Bhishma from battle (not kill) and even Abhimanyu’s death couldn’t make Arjuna angry enough to go against Drona. He vented his anger on the entire Kuru force, Jaidradh died. But he didn’t even fight Drona who was later and easily defeated my not so compassionate Bhimasena.

The problem with such meaningless if is, it is applied only at one point and not at the other points. But remember every “if” is a start for a new story. If Krishna would not have supported Pandavas can we say that Arjuna may not have used is higher weapons? Maybe he would have used some and battle may have ended early. Afterall it did during earlier times. Or maybe the battle had lasted longer. But there was no one in Kuru army who didn’t explicitly said that they couldn’t stand against Arjuna. There was no one in the Kuru army that was not already defeated by pandavas on earlier occasion. And there was no great warrior of that era including Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama or Parshurama who had any doubt about who would win a direct confrontation. In fact even evils like Duryodhana and Karna knew it.
