If not Karna, who would be the most tragic character in the Mahabharata?

📅 May 09, 2020 12:46 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Good Question! Who would be the most tragic character in Mahabharat. I will give you candidates and you chose 1.

  • A King
    • He was an emperor who married a beautiful woman and promised her freedom of action. The wife killed 7 of his newborns by drowning them in river. and He could do nothing about it. He suffered seeing his sons die.
  • A couple
    • A sister was jailed along with her husband by her brother.
    • The couple spend more than 16 years in jail
    • They saw six of their son’s brutally murdered infront of their own eyes.
  • An unfortunate Prince
    • A highly capable prince who was set to be a great King.
    • He gave up his kingship for the happiness of his father.
    • He suffered all his life seeing hyenas tear that crown and kingdom that was supposed to be his.
    • His oath bound him to those hyenas, and he could do nothing but suffer and support them.
    • He was condemned to live all his life waiting for his death.
  • Another unfortunate King
    • Once upon a time there was a capable ruler, who left his kingdom to his blind brother to pursue the path of penance for one of his mistake.
    • He trusted his brother.
    • His wives suffered exile with him. His sons were born and spent their early childhood in forest.
    • He died seeing his family suffer for his mistake.
  • A Family
    • When the unfortunate king died and his family returned to Hastinapur they realized the late king had trusted a brother who wasn’t just physically blind but…
    • A brother was poisoned in their own kingdom.
    • The entire family was attempted to be burnt alive.
    • They suffered in exile not sure whom can they trust in their family
    • They returned to their kingdom to be given a piece of wasteland for the share of their father’s kingdom.
    • They transformed the wasteland into a beautiful kingdom but…
    • Their cousins by the means of deceit not only snatched away their kingdom but also humiliated them and tried to disrobe their wife.
    • They suffered another exile for 12 years followed by humiliation of being a slave to another king.
  • And a caste away
    • A boy was born out of wedlock and his mother had to abandon him.
    • She did abandon him but not before extracting a unmatched protection from his divine father.
    • The boy grew up in a loving and caring family
    • They boy had an education from the best gurus of the time — Kripa, Drona and Parshuram.
    • He was rejected by one guru and curshed by another because of his own ambition and deciet.
    • The boy was crowned at an early age and remained the king till his death.
    • He was a respectable member of Hastinapur court where even King talked to him politely and he had a say in the matters of the court.
    • He was never exiled.
    • No one tried to burn him alive.
    • His friend never doubted or rejected him for any of his failure.
    • He died as the army in chief of Kaurava’s army
    • Both friends and foes mourned his death.

Now you can choose who should be considered most tragic of them all?

Do you know why the other names in the list don’t appear in the list of tragics — because they never contested for this position. They never lamented their fate, accepted it and went on.
