What are the logical points for those who think the Mahabharata is not real?

📅 Mar 09, 2020 01:29 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

My answer is more to refute Mr Piyush Jain the atheist expert on non existence of God. And because he is an expert on non existence of something, in his paradox he forgot the question was about whether Mahabharat existed or not and not if Krishna was scientifically correct or not. Consider this — if someone doesn't know solar system does that prove that solar system doesn't exist or that person doesn't exist? You will be surprised to know that even if that person claims himself to be an expert on non existence of solar system, in reality they both coexist.

Now without needing to be an expert let us illuminate the 6 points discussed (note: by illumination I don't mean physics light theory)

  1. Yada Yada… How do you know that there is no incarnation? What this line signifies is a trust in Devine intervention when evil forces become strong. So why do you think sati pratha was abolished and widow remarriage started? How do you think evil dictators met their masters? If you fail to see the connections it is unlikely you will comprehend Virat vishwaroop.
  2. I am sure you must have heard the phrase — things going south? Does that really mean going south? By train or plane? It's a metaphor for going down or bad. Now once you understand this apply on heaven and hell and you will see the idea fly defying rotation, revolution and gravity.
  3. Do you realize Krishna was not teaching astronomy or geography to Arjun when he explained sun, moon and illumination. He was more like a psychological guru using his base understanding motivating him to work. A study in scarlet the debut novel of Sherlock Holmes would be a great read to understand this part.

3b. Varna system. Do you really think it exist only in Hinduism? Look deeper and you would realize such hierarchical categorization not only exist in all civilization and nations but also in non humans. They may name it differently but it exists —

Brahmins the thinkers that originate in brain eg researchers, teachers, scientists

Kshatriya the protector the weapon weilders and protectors. Whose duty is to protect — police, armies

Vaishya the revenue generators

Shudra the service class and muscle power to carry out the visions.

Think and name one society where Krishna's varna doesn't apply.

4. Why didn't Dhritrastra stopped Duryodhana — it simply shows lack of knowledge about the epic. Can't help here.

5. Please don't read a single line in isolation of what Krishna says about killing. He also said you do your duty as a Kshatriya (think an army officer talking about how precious a life is). He also talked about karma or duty and do by surrendering consequence to me (to a greater cause) and not for the selfish interest. Does that make sense against your notion of senseless killing? Without a holistic approach its easy to distort the meaning.

6. What were the army doing during the surmon — sir it's all mentioned there. War was about to start. And rules were not it broken. Arjun was unarmed. Need I say more.

What is most curious is not one these 6 point either way proved Mahabharat wasn't real.
