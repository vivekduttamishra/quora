Was Karna not an exemplary man of honor that he promised Kunti that he would not kill any of her sons except Arjuna and kept that promise?

📅 Feb 07, 2021 10:35 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Exemplary man of honor is a big word and would lose it shine the moment you apply it to some abuser of woman. One example can’t define someone as a man of honour. But let us consider the incident, on which this question is based, to find out how honorable was Karna’s conduct?

1.What was Kunti’s request?

Kunti wanted Karna to support his brothers and Dharma. Even Surya supported Kunti on this and suggested Karna to follow Kunti’s directives. She didn’t ask to spare the life of her four sons.

Why only four sons? Did Kunti ask him to spare the life of only four sons? Did he not explicitly announced in front of a mother that he would kill one of her son, if possible? Does this sound honorable to you? I find it cruel.

Arjuna never knew the identity of his brother Karna. But Karna knew the identity of Arjuna. Still he was determined to kill him. Is this honorable?

Why was Karna determined to Kill Arjuna?

2.Was Karna’s action in favour of Duryodhana?

Karna said that he is under debt of Duryodhana and can’t leave his side. If he were earnest, he would have been a man of honour. But let us consider a few points —

  • Everyone knew the main contender of throne of Hastinapur was Yudhishthir. Had he died, Duryodhana would have better chance to claim the throne. But Karna spared Yudhishthir in his oath. Does this make Karna a man of honor?
  • Bhima had taken an oath to kill every single Kaurava. Karna could have killed Bhima and protected his friend and his brothers. Yet Karna spared Bhima in his oath and saw most of the Kaurava brothers getting killed in front of his eyes — many were killed while trying to protect Karna from same Bhima. Does this make Karna a man of honor?
  • Did Karna ever inform Duryodhana that he would not be killing four Pandavas? Never. Does that make him honorable?

3.Was Karna loyal to Duryodhana in general?

Karna certainly said, he was loyal to Duryodhana and can’t switch sides. He said the same to Krishna and Kunti. But if only words had wing, demons would be worshipped. Let us examine Karna actions towards Duryodhana.

  • A true friend would extend those advice that are in the best interest of his friend. All Karna did was to instigate him on various occasions and advised Duryodhana to reject Krishna’s peace proposal. He could have prevented the war, but he chose not to, knowing it would bring doom to Duryodhana and his clans.
  • If Karna were loyal to Duryodhana he wouldn’t have spared the life of Yudhisthira and Bhima in his oath. At the least, he would have the integrity to inform about his oath to Duryodhana.
  • Karna fled from Gandharavas leaving his best friend behind to be imprisioned. Pandavas had to rescue them. Not only Duryodhana, but women of the royal family too were at the risk of capture, had noble Pandavas not intervened. Is this act of Karna honorable?
  • Karna refused to fight the war of Kurukshetra for first 10 days (more than half the battle) for his selfish ego. Ironically, he would not leave the war for Dharma and family. But he had no moral qualms, no remembrance of loyalty when it came to his selfish ego. What man of honor would abandon his friend for his ego but not for Dharma?

4.Why was Arjuna an exception to his oath?

You must understand Karna’s life revolved around an single axis — Arjuna. He was obsessed to prove himself better than Arjuna from as long as Karna could remember.

  • He was jealous of Arjuna in Drona’s gurukul and wanted guru to give Brahmastra.
  • When Drona refused to give him knowledge of Brahmastra he tried to obtain the same from Parshuram by deceitful means.
  • He gate crashed the play arena specifically meant to showcase the skills of the princess of Hastinapur, just to prove himself better than Arjuna.
  • He instigated Duryodhana to come and mock Arjuna and Draupadi in exile, a scheme which backfired in the worst possible manner.
  • He boasted of vanquishing Arjuna in matasya battle although once again he was bested.

This one obsession of Karna kept him going from bad to worse. If you look carefully here is the real reason of every action of Karna —

  • He refused to heed Krishna’s advise that would have averted the war and saved millions of life. He could have accepted the kingship and if he were so loyal he could have handed over the kingship to Duryodhana. Can you guess what was one variable here that made Karna refuse the proposal? In this situation there would have been no battle between Karna and Arjuna and no one would have ever known Karna was better.
  • He spared the lives of those Pandavas that were important for Duryodhana’s safety but insisted on killing Arjuna. Once again, his obsession for Arjuna was greater than his loyalty for his friend or respect for his mother or even humanity.

Karna’s life, his wars, his decisions, everything orbited around his hate and jealousy of Arjuna. Remove Arjuna, and Karna becomes a big void.

WHAT WOULD HAVE MADE KARNA HONORABLE?

There are many things that Karna could have done to make him a man of honor. I would list them in order of greatness.

  1. He would have tried to be a better warrior rather than better than Arjuna. As a friend he should have tried to bring Duryodhana to right path rather than supporting him in his conspiracy and instigating in many more. The war could have been averted.
  2. He should have heeded to Krishna’s advice and prevent the war in which millions perished. Pandava’s wouldn’t have taken Hastianpur. Duryodhana would not have fought against his friend, who would have been the king of Indraprastha. There could have been a peaceful society. Justice would have been served.
  3. He should have heeded to Krishna’s advised and after taking the throne handed it to Duryodhana. This way he would have proved his loyalty to his friend.
  4. He shouldn’t have given any boon to Kunti suggesting that he is totally dedicated to Duryodhana and sparing his brothers life would be a treachery to Duryodhana.
  5. He should have spared the life of all his younger brothers forgoing his egos. He should have informed his friend and let him decide if he wanted Karna to fight for him. Duryodhana, realizing the truth may have called of the war.
  6. He should have fought the war from the beginning and not excused himself for his selfish ego. His ego shouldn’t have greater value than his loyalty for his friends.
  7. He could have imprisoned Yudhishthir and Bhima in the battle. He had promised to spare their life, not aid them to their victory. Battle may have ended much less bloody.

There were so many things Karna could have done differently, and I would have considered him honorable. But Karna as we know him is the trunk of a tree of lust and passion that was evil Duryodhana. Such people are exemplary of what you shouldn’t be.
