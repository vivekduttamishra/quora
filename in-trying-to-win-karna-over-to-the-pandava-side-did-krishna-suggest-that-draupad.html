In trying to win Karna over to the Pandava side, did Krishna suggest that Draupadi would accept him as her sixth husband?

📅 Mar 13, 2020 09:30 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Krishna Never offered Draupadi as wife.

  • Krishna had initially explained why Draupadi got 5 husbands. In the legend, there was no scope for a sixth husband.
  • Krishna knew Draupadi loathed Karna, and he had too much respect for her to offer him to the same man.

The English translation by K.M.Ganguli appears flawed here (and at few other points). But if you look into other translations such as Sanskrit Hindi Translation by Gitapress Gorakhpur 15the edition it is more than apparent what Krishna offered to him —

Udyog Parva Chapter 140

Sloka 8–11 → Krishna shares the secret that Karna is the first born of Kunti and as per Shastra’s has right to be considered son of Pandu and elder brother of Yudhisthir.

Sloka 12 → He mentions that on his return his 5 brothers along with Draupadi and Abhimanyu will bow before him. (Note Draupadi doesn’t find any special mention)

Sloka 14–16 → Mentions the details of his coronation.

Sloka 17–29 → Mentions the support he obedience he would get after being the King.

That puts an end to the assumptions made about the offer to Karna for being the 6th husband.

Now coming to two answers shared in this thread and my views on them

  1. Krishna had no intention of winning over Karna, and it was all a facade
    • Krisha knew very well that Karna would refuse, and it was late, but that doesn’t make Krishna attempt any less sincere. It is an attempt similar to Lord Ram’s attempt to send Angad as emissary advising Ravan to refrain from the war. Because Ram and Krishna were bound to teach the mankind that war is the last resort and one should try his best to avoid war even in hopeless situations and hopeless people like Ravan, Duryodhana or Karna.
  1. Krishna was afraid of a warrior as powerful as Karna
    • Consider two aspect before you reach on this conclusion
      1. Arjun had already bested Karna on several occasions Draupadi Swaymber (direct), Gandharva episode (indirectly), Drupad capture (indirectly), Virat yudh (twice directly). During the war it was again proved beyond doubt that Karna was not as fearsome as he would be depicted in TV shows 5000 years later. He was bested by Satyaki, Bhim, Abhimanyu, Ghatotkatch and finally Arjun.
      2. Next 2 chapters are important ant as well and it would us understand why Krishna or Pandavas were not afraid of Karna. On the other hand Karna knew the adversary he was about to face.

Chapter 141 → Karna Refused to switch side and accepts the power of Bhim, Abhimanyu and others and their capability to defeat the opponent.

Chapter 142 → Krishna gives him a war projection announcing a confirmed victory for Pandavas.

Hope the answer sattles all the three confusions!
