How was the character of Krishna in Mahabharat?

📅 Mar 14, 2020 10:04 PM PDT  |  🌐 English
  • Krishna is the main hero of Mahabharat — in an epic full of Heros!
  • Krishna’s influence truly extends further and beyond the epic itself.
  • Krishna shows how separate family, society and greater good. His decisions were based on greater good and often ignored family.
  • We can backtrack his teaching to Ramayan and becomes a core of Hindu philosophy.
  • Krishna taught that anything can be sacrificed at the alter of dharma yudh.
  • Krishna taught the difference between hypocrisy and dharma!
  • Krishna taught no work is insignificant as he took the mantle of a charioteer.
  • Krishna taught individuals are not insignificant and didn’t hesitate to show his divine self to protect Draupadi.
  • Krishna taught us to shed your egos — accepted the slang of Ranchod (one who runs away from the war) as a medal. Shifted his kingdom to remote Dwarka to protect his people.
  • Krishna taught us to an important point about the war —
    • Prevent is as long as you can, even at the cost of personal egos.
    • Don’t hesitate in a war if it can’t be prevented and fight against adharma at all cost — even if you need to fight your own.
