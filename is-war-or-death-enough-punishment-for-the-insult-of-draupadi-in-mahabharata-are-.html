Is war or death enough punishment for the insult of Draupadi in Mahabharata? Are Pandavas not to be blamed for not protecting her?

📅 May 09, 2020 02:45 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is yet another question that appears so many times in various forms, however, unlike many other questions, this one appears a genuine question in entirety. Let us answer both parts separately.

  1. Is death enough punishment for the insult of Draupadi in Mahabharata?
    1. Death wasn’t the only punishment the perpetrators received. Death may never be sufficient a punishment.
    2. After over 5000 years, barring few exceptionally progressive thinkers, we still condemn those perpetrators are still. Don’t you think this was a great punishment enough?
    3. Consider Duryodhana and his team — they were the prime perpetrators. They not only died in Kurukshetra, they lost all their respect. No one came forward to help them.
      1. Duhsasana always supported Duryodhana loyally, but when he died Duryodhana couldn’t save him? Was Dussasana’s death only Dussasana’s punishment? Or even Duryodhana’s?
      2. Karna, the great archer, died in the hand of his arch rival. He was armed (contrary to popular depiction) but couldn’t save himself or his dream. He was Suryaputra, a great daanveer, and yet he died as trunk of an evil tree. It’s more than death.
      3. Duryodhana died, but not without agony of the death of 5 up-pandavas in his hand, though the deed to carried out by Ashwathama.
      4. Ashwathama lived on with a cursed life.
    4. Consider those who remained silent —
      1. Bhishma, Drona, Kripacharya — What could have been a bigger punishment for them but to fight on the side of Adharma. An unconqurable warrior like Bhisma, was pinned to ground. But then he accepted that as a penance and not punishment! His punishment was Lord Krishna’s that contempt with which he took the wheel to attack him. Drona died a death of a failure and Kripacharya lived on with no one to remember him in good light. What a punishment.
      2. Dhritrashtra — Played dumb and helpless on every devil act of Duryodhana. And it can be called a divine comedy, that he became dumb and helpless to protect his sons and the blind man had to see (through Sanjay’s eye) every details as his sons and kingdom perished. He died with each one of them, guilt-ridden and helpless.
  2. Are Pandavas not to be blamed for not protecting Draupadi?
    1. When have Pandavas ever escaped the blame?
    2. Didn’t Bhimsen blamed Yudhishthir right in the chamber?
    3. Aren’t they punished sufficiently —
      1. Don’t society blame them for their mistake even today after 5000 years?
      2. Didn’t they were exiled for 12 years and had to accept slavery for another whole year?
      3. Didn’t they too witnessed their loved one perished in their own hand?
      4. Didn’t Yudhishthir had to break his lifelong credibility and had to lie in the battle field?

The one key difference between the two groups Pandavas and Kauravas was — Pandavas repented for their sins, Kauravas had to be punished for not only their crime but also because they never repented.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
