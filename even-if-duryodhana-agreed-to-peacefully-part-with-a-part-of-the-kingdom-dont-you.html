Even if Duryodhana agreed to peacefully part with a part of the kingdom, don't you think that Pandavas would have waged a war later to avenge the insult caused to Draupadi?

📅 Jan 17, 2021 03:35 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I believe war would have happened and the war would have eventually avenged Draupadi’s insult.

That said, the war wouldn’t have been waged by Pandavas, but by Kauravas. And it has been a recurring theme.

Why Pandavas wouldn’t have waged the war

Pandavas adhered to dharma more than they should have —

  • Long before agyatvas, long before Gandharvas incident, Pandavas had already grown mighty and were graced by Lord Hanuman, Mahadeva Shiva and Lord Krishna. They could have used the newly acquired power to vanquish Kauravas rather than fulfilling their promise of exile and agyatvas, everyone knew was the result of Shakuni’s deceit.
  • Pandavas could have let Kauravas perish at the hand of Gandharavas. Draupadi was already humilated. It wasn’t their business, it wasn’t their duty to protect them. Yet they protected them. Because they still valued family ties.
  • They completed the agyatvas. As per the term they were entitled to get Indraprastha back. They could have demanded Indraprastha and still waged a war of vengeance. But they said, they strived for peace.
  • Each Pandava with exception to noble Sahdev asked Krishna for peace.
  • Had they wished to wage a war, they had the power to do it and history is a testimony to this.

Why war would have still happened?

  • Remember why dice game started in the first place. Not because someone insulted Duryodhana or Dhritrashtra. It was because Pandavas managed to transform a barren Khandava into magnficient and prosperous Indraprastha and Duryodhana was unable to contain his envy at the prosperity of Pandava.
  • This time Pandava had demaned five village. Soon they would have rebuild it, probably more prosperous than even Indraprastha. Question is, could Duryodhna have contained his jealousy?
  • With no war, would Karna have been content? He who explictly says the greatest joy is to see your enemy suffer, to see Arjuna and Draupadi in tree bark? How would he have reacted to see prosperous Pandavas? How would he have proved himself greater than Arjuna without another war? He had already been vanquished in earlier battles. How would he have managed to contain himself knowing every warrior knew who is better between the two?
  • So no matter how much Pandavas have tried to strive for peace sooner or later there would have been a war.

What would have been the outcome of such a war?

Here I daresay, things may been been a lot better.

  • With Yudhishtir already becoming a King, he may have had more allies and that would mean lesser allies for Kauravas.
  • Seeing Hastinapur factions finally at peace, Bhishma may have left his human life.
  • With Bhishma gone, the war couldn’t have lasted more than 8 days it lasted in reality.
  • With Bhishma gone, there was no reason for Drona to support Kauravas. He may chosen to side with his favourite Arjuna or may have chosen to stay neutral. Same would go for Kripacharya and Ashwatthama.
  • With Drona being neutral, the war would have ended in 3 days at worst.
  • Drona, Ahwatthama and Kripacharya siding with Pandava and war may have ended in a single day.

In all permutations, the war would have been shorter and much less bloody.
