Why did Karna spare Arjuna (Bramhin) in Draupadi Swayamvar Battle?

📅 Sep 20, 2020 11:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I have disucssed this question is a great detail in my post — First Arjuna Karna Battle

All the evidence points out without any doubt that Karna lost, and retreated from the battle. The article debunks those claims that suggests that Karna retreated because he didn’t want to kill a Brahman. The short version of the story is as follows —

  1. Assembled Kings, initially decided not to kill Brahmana.
  2. They wanted to kill Drupada and his sons and burn Druapadi and return back to their kingdom — simple kind hearted people.
  3. When they attacked, Drupad saught protection from Bhima and Arjuna.
  4. At this point Kings decided it is ok to fight and kill Brhamana willing to fight. Thus the Arjuna being a Brahmana excuse is shattered.
  5. Karna attacks Arjuna (knowing he is Brhamana), Shalya attacks Bhima.
  6. Other Kings attacked other Brahmanas present there who were unarmed, powerless and caught in cross fire. These kings fought carelessly. People try to suggest Karna to fought carelessless, which is not mentioned.
  7. On seeing Karna attacking, Arjuna shot Karna with three arrows which pearced him and he was shocked. Then he moved forward with great care — circumspect.
  8. Then Arjuna and Karna attacked each other violently each trying to vanquish the other.
  9. The best feat of Karna which other kings cheered for was when he repulsed Arjuna’s arrow. Many mistake it as Karna’s victory, althought it was just repulsting Arjuna’s arrow. None of the Karna’s arrow reached Arjuna. He wasn't pearced. He wasn't reluctant to fight.
  10. Then Karna stops and compares Arjuna with — Rama, the personification of knowledge of weapons, Indra and Vishnu. No warrior in his right mind would compare a losing opponent with his guru, Indra, Vishnu and personification of knoweledge of weapons. Not Karna, who used abusive languages after defeating Pandava brothers in Kurukshetra.
  11. Arjuna says he is none of them, but a great warrior nevertheless. He asks Karna to wait so that Arjuna can defeat him. Why would a warrior on the verge of losing ask a Victor to keep fighting?
  12. Karna knowing Brhamana (Arjuna) is invincible retreated. Not because he was Brahmana, but because he was invincible.
  13. Bhima defeated and spared Shalyas life.
  14. Other Kings on seeing Shalya thrown on ground and Karna scared away decided not to fight any further.

Have you heard that a victor got scared away and a loser took the trophy for which they fought? If Karna spared someone’s life here, it was his own.
