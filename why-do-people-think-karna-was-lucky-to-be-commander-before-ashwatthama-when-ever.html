Why do people think Karna was lucky to be commander before Ashwatthama, when everyone from Duryodhan, to Shakuni, and everyone and even Bhishma Pitama wanted Karna to be commander, even Drona was ok with it, but it was Karna who suggested Drona?

📅 May 19, 2022 11:44 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

There are rarely, that a question can be so wrong on each part of it.

First things first, I don’t see why Karna can be called lucky only for becoming the commander of Kuru Army. Karna was lucky all his life, lucky enough to get what he didn’t deserve. He didn’t deserve to be King of Anga. He had just one great quality — He was an excellent salesman. He projected himself as someone who could compete against Arjuna, and all his fortune was due to one single fact that he could sell his pitch. He constantly failed against Arjuna and others, often ditched his friends and family. But where is skills as warrior failed his inter-personal skill namely flattery and misrepresentation of facts kept him in the good book of Duryodhana. Till the very end. No wonder he was appointed the commander.

Second glaring mistake — Bhishma didn’t want him to be the commander. He never said so. In fact he called him Ardharathi, something that he substantiated with evidence and his statement was backed by Dronacharya. Some may say, Bhishma praised Karna after he was out of the battle. That was the strategy of a commander to boost the confidence of someone whom he can’t stop from entering the battle. He didn’t recommend that Karna be made the commander. He asked him to lead his army and protect Duryodhana. But it must be understood that this war had many generals leading different fronts of the battle but there can be only one commander of the army. And Bhishma didn’t suggest Karna’s name for this position.

In fact no one suggested Karna’s name for being the second commander. Duryodhana asked Karna’s advise as to who should be the commander —

‘“Duryodhana said, ‘Bhishma was our commander. He was senior and valiant. He was well-endowed with learning, possessed knowledge of weapons and had all the qualities. O Karna! He obtained great fame by slaying large numbers of the enemy. The great-souled one fought well for ten days and protected us. He performed extremely difficult deeds and is ascending to heaven. Who do you think should be our commander after him?

—BORI

Neelkantha (KMG) has a similar description. And yes, Karna recommended the name of Dronacharya and not without giving a great reason which was unanimously accepted. No one actually named Karna to be the commander. Not Duryodhana and not Shakuni.

But I wouldn’t have been surprised had Duryodhana chosen him as the commander over Dronacharya. Duryodhana had a habits of committing blunders and following Karna’s advise most of which resulted into disaster. Recall it was the advise of great Karna that had led them to Gosha Yatra and Gandharva fiasco, where on the time of need Karna conveniently escaped abandoning Duryodhana. But then he returned with a new sales pitch that transforms Pandavas heroics in saving Kaurvas as their duty. It suited humiliated Duryodhana and the two friend got readied for another misadventure Goharana. This time he abandoned his brother and ran away while he was killed.

What made Karna extremely useful to Duryodhana was the fact that he was the only person who conveniently lied to him that he would kill the Pandavas for him. All Pandavas. Despite and even after promising Kunti that he won’t. No other warrior were willing to lie to Duryodhana. And Duryodhana believed in those lies more than the sane and honest advise of his well-wishers, because in those lies he got a hope of that dream which according to others were both achievable and forbidden. Indeed Duryodhana would have made him commander.

The third flaw of this question is a statement that Drona was okay with Karna becoming the commander. Drona evidently had no reason to agree with a decision that was never made.

After Drona and Bhishma gone, among contemporaries Duryodhana certainly trusted Karna more than other and he was made the commander.
