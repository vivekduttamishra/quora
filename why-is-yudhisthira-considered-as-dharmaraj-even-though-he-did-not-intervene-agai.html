Why is Yudhisthira considered as Dharmaraj even though he did not intervene against the unethical act of Draupadi Cheerharan?

📅 May 05, 2020 02:38 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question truly vexes me and I believe troubles any one with a conscience in our times. And without any intention to justify Yudhisthir inaction (I don't think there is one), I am trying to understand the saga from the perspective of its period — five thousand years ago.

It was a time with different traditions that may not make much sense to us today —

  • Polygamy was an common practice then.
  • Gambling was a Royale protocol.
  • There was nothing like UNO to resolve disputes. Kings words were the law. A king who backs from his words was worthless.
  • Very often we get confused between practices, protocol, and Dharma.
  • Yudhisthir followed what he perceived is Dharma. His interpretation was most likely incorrect. But his sincerity and adherence to his belief was unshakeable. He could have easily ordered Bhim and Arjuna to intervene. That was easy. He didn't do it. Because his belief was firm.
  • Not only Yudhisthir, entire council of wise people were guilty of inactions as they too debated on Dharma. It wasn’t a personal problem, but a social issue.
  • One incident doesn't define a person. His decision to rescue Duryodhana from Gandharvas, adhere to terms of a deceitful game, and and desperate attempt to prevent a war has contributed to his identification of Dharmaraj

Above all Mahabharat is a story with a moral.

  • Pandavas suffered severe punishment for their inaction.
    • They remain in exile for 12 years.
    • They suffered a slave's life for a year.
  • The did their penance sincerely. What's the point of a penance if it doesn't absolve someone from his sins?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
