Krishna in Gita says that anger is harmful & leads to veiling of judgement. (Bhagavad Gita 2.63). But he himself is not able to control anger & attack Bhishma with chariot wheel. How can we follow Krishna's words when he himself don't follow them?

📅 Jan 10, 2021 11:58 PM PST  |  🌐 English

The question fails to follow both the words and actions Krishna.

Let us check the shloka 2.62 and 2.64 in conjunction with 2.63

ध्यायतो विषयान्पुंस: सङ्गस्तेषूपजायते |
सङ्गात्सञ्जायते काम: कामात्क्रोधोऽभिजायते || 62||

क्रोधाद्भवति सम्मोह: सम्मोहात्स्मृतिविभ्रम: |
स्मृतिभ्रंशाद् बुद्धिनाशो बुद्धिनाशात्प्रणश्यति || 63||

While contemplating on the objects of the senses, one develops attachment to them. Attachment leads to desire, and from desire arises anger. [62]

Anger leads to clouding of judgment, which results in bewilderment of the memory. When the memory is bewildered, the intellect gets destroyed; and when the intellect is destroyed, one is ruined.[63]

But one who controls the mind, and is free from attachment and aversion, even while using the objects of the senses, attains the Grace of God.

Shloka 2.62 clearly mentions anger is a symptom not the cause. The root cause is attachment and desire. It is like when you have fever, it is not the diseases but an indicator of some disease. And people jump to treat fever like disease.

Sholka 2.64 also mentions that one who controls mind and is free from attachment, attains the grace of God.

Now you must see, Krishna was that detached being. What applies on you doesn’t apply on Krishna, and not just because he was Krishna.

Did Krishna really attack Bhishma?

Had Krishna really been angry and his memory was in bewilderment, Bhishma would be dead. But he didn’t. And Krishna didn’t really attacked him. Do you call this anger? Krishna’s was motivating Arjuna to do what is necessary. He was reminding Arjuna what he had said earlier.

You read just one like and drew your conclusion, that suggests you didn’t really understood Gita. And you need not follow Krishna’s word, since you don’t really understand it.
