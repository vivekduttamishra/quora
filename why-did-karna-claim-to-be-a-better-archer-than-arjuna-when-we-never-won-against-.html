Why did Karna claim to be a better archer than Arjuna when we never won against him?

📅 May 31, 2020 09:14 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question is simiple — Is their a single Karna Arjuna direct or indirect confrontation that Karna won? So where comes this cliam from?

I would say Karna got lucky — after many centuries of his death. No doubt he was a great archer. He was trained by Dronacharya, Kripacharya and Bhagwan Parshuram. His own inclination and ambition to be the best warrior played no less role in him becoming a recognized archer. And this resonates in his Digvijay yatra. But…

It is undeniable to Karna got lucky to have drawn the fascination of modern writers who fished him out as a sidelined character and built their narrative on few stray quotes of Vyasji which are more in Krishna proving Arjun to outdo himself. I believe these narratives were made to highlight how society differentiated between caste during that period of time. Unfortunately these superb writers used their era’s customs to visualize Mahabharat period.

But as the entire narrative is based on few incidents which are well contradicted at else where in Mahabharat several stories were censored, several added and still left many glaring loopholes in the story.

Important censored elements about Karna in modern retelling

  1. Drona never refused Karna a teaching based on his caste. He denied him only the knowledge of Divyastra seeing his nature and urge to fight Arjuna (reference: Shanti Parva Mahabharata)
  2. Suta was not a low caste — It was a hibrid between Brahim and Kshatriya. Karna himself explained this to Parshuram as he revealed his identity before being cursed.
  3. The Draupadi Swyambera — Arjuna defeated Karna with his Kavach and Kundala on and Karna retreated by comparing Arjuna with the manifestation of art of war, Rama, Indra and Vishnu. But it also censors the cause of the war, that was an attempt to kill Draupadi who chose a Brahmin the swaymber. (Reference: Swyamber Parva)
  4. Rajsuy Yagna — Bheem defeats Karna in Rajsuy yagna. He had his Kavacha and Kundala with him.
  5. The Gandharva war — Here Karna fights bravely until he can fight no more and escapes of Vikarna chariot. He had his Kavach and Kundala intact. This along with point 2. provies that the Kavach and Kundala wasn’t invincible after all. (—Reference: Vana Parva, Mahabharata)
  6. His role in Draupadi’s Swaymber. It was brute. No other words (—Reference: Shbha Parva, Mahabharata)
  7. What did all the contemporary warriors thought about Karna-
    1. Bhisma called him ardhrathi, selfish, arrogant and boasting. As a retaliation Karna refused to fight first 10 days of battle.
    2. Kripacharya used similar words in Virata war, endorsed by Dronacharya. It was proved at that very point in Virat war.

Daanveer Karna

  1. The word Danveer is not once mentioned in Vyasji narration. Karna started to donate after his defeat in Gandharva war and with a clear purpose. He said he would continue to do so till the time he doesn’t slay Arjuna.

“O elephant among kings! So long as I do not slay Arjuna, I shall not allow any one to wash my feet, nor shall I taste meat. And I shall observe the Asura vow and whoever may solicit me (for any thing), I never shall say, 'I have it not.'
—Reference: Vana Parva: Ghosha-yatra Parva: Section CCLV

2. Indra takes away his Kavach and Kundal — Indra doesn’t take away his Kavach or Kundal by divine power. He asks for it. And Karna refused to give him the Kavach and Kundal unless he gives him the celetial weapon. But let us keep this story aside. No matter what the narrative, Karna had to part with his shield. Question is

  • Was he a great warrior only because of his shield?
  • No other warriors had a shield in the battle?
  • Did his shield help him win Gurudakshina war, Swyamber war, Gandharva war?
  • He refused to part the weapon unless… Then why did he part unless he recognized it was a better bargain?

Stories added to glorify Karna (Not part of Vyas Mahabharat)

  • He struggled in his life because Drona refused to teach him. Drona teaching him is mentioned in Adiparva, Vanparva and Shantiparva
  • Draupadi refused him in Swaymber. This is refuted even by the originator of this story — Neelkantha commentary, where it is mentioned along with the fact that Karna failed at Swyamber. The same is available in Gita Press, BORI, Dakshinayat and its abridged translation by Rajgopalachari.
  • Karna pushed Krishna’s chariot few inches and Krishna praised him for his valour
  • Karna was disarmed when Arjuna killed him— Karna was chariot less when he was killed, but he wasn’t weaponless. He died like a brave warrior, fighting. In making him weaponless, you not only snatch the glory of Arjuna but also of Karna.
  • Karna donated is tooth to Krishna on dying bed and Krishna showed him his Viraat swaroop —
    • Karna’s head was shivered by Arjuna’s bow. The foundation of this narration is beyond understanding.
    • Krishna had shown is Viratswaroop in Karna’s presence in Hastinapur Shabha. He couldn’t see. Why? Krishna mentions in Gita— Without true devotion no one can discover his true self.

As you can see there are so many loopholes in suggestion that while he was a brave, being better archer is not mentioned or intent of Vyasji

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
