Did Arjuna countered nagaastra realesed from karna?

Thanks for the A2A.

Before I answer the question let us understand one important thing about this so called Nagastra —

Nagastra wasn’t a divyastra

Nagastra wasn’t a divyastra — a divine weapon that works on the energy of divine positive powers. It was a evil serpant sitting on an arrow. If we have to name it, it would be an Asurastra — opposite of Divastra. Let us first check some references. —

Having said this, he (Karna) released that arrow, the serpent which he had worshipped for many years. He said, ‘O Phalguna! You have been slain,’ and swiftly shot the arrow. On seeing that Karna had affixed the serpent, Madhava, supreme among strong ones, used his strength to press down on the chariot with his feet. The chariot sank down on the ground and the horses sank down on their knees. ‘ “The arrow struck down the intelligent one’s diadem.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4877-4880).

Karna worshipped this serpent for many years. Who was this serpent? Some God? Why did Karna worship this particular serpant. We would talk about it a bit later.

There is no reference that it was a divine weapon and the same is clear from Krishna’s observations and actions. Krishna saw Karna affix the serpent. And all he did was to push the chariot down and save Arjuna from it’s trajectory. Have you seen such a simple solution for any Divyastra?

Think, why didn’t Krishna simply asked Uttara to move to another bed and she would be protected from Ashwatthama’s Brahmastra. I think you get the point.

But let us now explore who that serpent was with whom Karna had colluded. Here is direct reference —

It told him, 792 ‘Know who I am. I am firm in my enmity of the two Krishnas, because they slew my mother.’793 In the battle, Krishna then spoke to Partha. ‘The giant serpent is firm in its enmity. Slay it.’ Having been thus addressed by Madhusudana, the wielder of Gandiva, fierce in using the bow and arrow against enemies, asked, ‘Who is the serpent who is advancing against me of his own accord, as if into Garuda’s gaping mouth?’ Krishna replied, ‘In Khandava, you satisfied the blazing one794 with the bow in your hand. You killed his mother, taking her to be a single snake. However, though her body was destroyed by the arrows, he was in the sky, covered by her.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4894-4899).

In simplest terms, It was Karna’s joint venture with a serpent to kill Arjuna. Both knew alone they don’t have a chance against Arjuna. Together? Well they still lost their chance.

Now comes the answer —

Did Arjuna counter the serpent?

The serpent was falling down from the sky. Jishnu severed the serpent with six sharp arrows. Mangled in its body, it fell down on the ground.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4899-4900).

SUMMARY

Some may repent Krishna thwart the conspiracy of Karna and the serpent, those who understand Dharma would understand that Karna worshipped a snake who was a sworn enemy of two Krishnas because deep within his heart he knew that neither he alone can’t beat Arjuna nor could he defeat Dharma with divine weapons that are meant to protect Dharma.
