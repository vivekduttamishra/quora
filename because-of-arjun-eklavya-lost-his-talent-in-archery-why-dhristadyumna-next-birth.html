Because of Arjun, Eklavya lost his talent in archery. Why Dhristadyumna (next birth of Eklavya to kill Drona) fought on Arjun's side?

📅 Apr 27, 2021 08:22 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is rare that a question has two parts and both are factually incorrect. Kudos to the questioner.

WHY DID EKLAVYA LOST HIS THUMB?

Arjuna was certainly low on finding the great archery skill that Eklavya exhibited and as a natural reaction he asked Dronacharya about it. But he never asked Drona to handicap Eklavya. His question to his guru was in a polite tone and was more concerned about himself —

He went to Drona and affectionately told him, “In your affection, you embraced me and told me that no pupil of yours would ever be my equal. How is it that you have another valorous pupil in this world, the son of the nishada king, who is better than everyone else?”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1(Kindle Locations 5060-5062).

But how could Drona ask Eklavya to offer his thumb? There is no explicit description provided. But rationale is not hard to follow. Eklavya, impersonated himself as Drona’s guru, despite his explicit rejection. He misused his name. Had Drona let him off the hook, he would have been responsible for all his actions.

It must also be seen Drona’s punishment was on similar lines as Parshurama to Karna — preventing the misuse of the knowledge in the wrong hand?

Was Drona justified? How did Eklavya died?

The two questions are related. Eklavya was killed by Krishna when he attacked on Krishna as a part of Jarasandha’s army. Now how are the two questions related? Ask yourself, what was the allegiance of Eklavya? Jarasandha one of the greatest evil of that time. And Eklavya sided with him.

Do you think, Drona was wrong when he attempted to sap his talent?

When Did Eklavya died?

This is not explicit. But he was present as a guest in Yudhishthir’s Rajsuy yagna. And this yagna took place years after Pandava’s wedding to Draupadi. And Draupadi and Drishtduymna were born at the same time.

Can you follow the timeline? There is no way, Ekalavya could have been reborn as Dhristdumyna if the were contemporary.

Now would someone be interested in asking — If Krishna killed Ekalavya, why did he bless him to be reborn as Drona’s slayer?

This is a glaring example of how epic is distorted without rhyme and reason.
