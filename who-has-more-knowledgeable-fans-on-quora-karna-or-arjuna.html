Who has more ‘knowledgeable fans’ on Quora, Karna or Arjuna?

📅 Sep 26, 2020 11:13 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I believe, more important than knowledge, is what we do with that knowledge. And there is a difference between information and knowledge.

The information can be obtained by even a simple Google search. But it requires wisdom and honesty to convert information to knowledge. Unfortunately remove one of the two and you loose knowledge and reduce information to misinformation and epic to myths.

So we often find selective quote with applied perception often contradicting the actual meaning the epic want to convey. And sometimes those perception becomes so strong that few start to challenge the authenticity of epic itself. The debate challenges impartiality of Krishna, Shiva and Ved Vyasa. Can we call such debate knowledgeable?

Most debate I came across recently, quotes epic even if selectively and out of context. So we see information exist. But what about knowledge? What worth is such a information that aims at distorting the very epic it debates about?

I believe the goal of knowledge should be to understand the underlying message of the epic rather than cultivate Fandom. So true knowledge is going beyond that Fandom.
