If Krishna was "dharmaatmi" then why didn't he made Ekalavya on his side and fixed his thumb after Karna’s disagreement on joining him?

📅 Jun 05, 2020 08:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

First thing first — Krishna was not some Dharmaatmi — He is the definition of Dhamra. What he says is Dharma, What he does defines Dharma.

So let us rephrase the question by removing the un-necessary “if” —

Why didn’t he made Eklavya on his side and fixed his thumb after Karna’s disagreement on joining him?

Good now we are making progress. So lets break another part of this question —

Time Line

  • The question assumes
    1. Eklavya was live when Karna refused to side with Pandavas in Mahabharat.
    2. Pandavas needed Eklavaya to win the war to such an extent that Krishna may fix his thumb.
  • Both these assumptions are wrong.
    • Eklavya was dead long before this time frame, killed by Krishna himself.
    • The consequence of war clearly tells that Pandavas never needed Eklavya.

Why Krishna didn’t fixed Eklavya’s thumb and brought him to his side at any point of time?

  • To be on Krishna’s side, you need to be on Dharma’s side. Eklavya had sided with Jarasandha — the manifestation of Adharma.
  • Krishna wasn’t a magician or a doctor who would go on fixing broken limbs unless it is for that greater cause known as Dharma.

So now let’s bring your “if” back at the right place —

If Krishna, the Dharma, made no attempt to bring Eklavya to his side and never fixed his thumb, what does it tell about Eklavya?

Hope you got the answer.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
