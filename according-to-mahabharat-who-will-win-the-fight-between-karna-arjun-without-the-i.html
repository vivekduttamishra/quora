According to Mahabharat, who will win the fight between Karna & Arjun without the interference of lord Krishna and why so?

📅 Aug 26, 2021 08:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is not a question of speculation. There are sufficient evidence to answer this question.

Arjuna won every single battle against Karna. Without exception. And Krishna wasn’t part of any Karna-Arjuna battle before Kurukshetra.

There first faceoff was when Karna marched forward to throw Draupadi to yagna fire after they failed to win her in Swyamvara. Arjuna confronts Karna who retreated, scared.

Second indirect face off was during the Gandharva battle. Gandharvas routed Karna so badly that he fled leaving his best friend behind and didn’t bother to return till he was out of harms way. Arjuna defeated those Gandharvas and freed Kauravas. Krishna wasn’t present in this battle.

Third face-off was during Matasya battle. Here Karna boasts of his divine weapons and powers. He says he would kill Arjuna today and would bring down his ape banner. But when the battle beings we see Karna fleeing even as Arjuna killed his brother! Karna returns back only to flee again. And next time he was rendered unconscious.

There was not a single battle where Karna could defeat Arjuna, with our without Krishna’s aid. He was supported by large armies but was still bested not only by Arjuna but also by Gandharva, Bhima, Satyaki and Abhimanyu to name a few.

To summarize here we are comparing the greatest warrior of the era with an ordinary to above average warrior who lacked courage to stay put.
