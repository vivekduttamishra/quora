Was Karna a danveer or dealveer?

📅 Mar 14, 2021 11:21 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer : Neither.

Karna regularly donated to Brahmans as per his oath, till the time he can defeat Arjuna. It was this well-known habbit of his, that Indra tried to exploit into demanding his Kavach and Kundala. While that makes him a renowned doner, that doesn't make him greatest donor or daanveer. What he did was common for most kings including Yudhisthir. I would say Duryodhana was a greater donor who donated kingship to Karna.

Calling him Danveer in the land of Shivi and Dadhichi would be an exaggeration. They donated their flesh and bones expecting no personal gain in return.

But he wasn't a dealveer either. True, he traded his Kavach and Kundala for Vasvishakti and absolutely refused to part away with his Kavacha as donation. While this trade degraded him as a donor, it must be understood it was Indra who approached Karna and not vice versa. Even the idea of this trade was not his. He took the trade because he could. He donated what was available to him as a king. One thing that was his, he couldn't donate but ended up trading.

Had Karna been either a Daanveer or a dealveer he would have grabbed the opportunity offered by Krishna. It was one thing that would have elavated him to both daanveer and dealveer — A deal to ensure world peace and avoid a gruesome war. He may have become daanveer by sacrificing his friends trust and gained friend's welfare in return. He would have donated his ambition of besting Arjuna in exchange of ensuring there is no war. That would have made him daanveer and dealveer.

But the Karna of our epic was neither. He was a mideocre doner and dealer.
