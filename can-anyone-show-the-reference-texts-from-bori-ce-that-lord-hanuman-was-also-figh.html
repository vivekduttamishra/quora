Short Answer : It never happened. And Bori CE has no reference for something that didn't happen.

Hanuman never fought along side Arjuna. He had indeed assured Bhima that he would be with Arjuna, never promised to fight for him.

The monkey banner that emitted loud roar —

  1. Was gifted by Varundev during Khandava incident. Now naturally Varundev can't be gifting Hanumanji to Arjuna.
  2. Mahabharata doesn't explicitly mention Hanumanji anywhere in Virata.
  3. Karna too mentioned it as monkey banner and his capacity to cut it off. Now that leads to two conclusion.
    1. Karna was aware of existence of this banner, but couldn't have know Hanumanji's assurance to Bhima.
    2. Karna felt confident he could destroy it. Now he couldn't have claimed to destroy Hanumanji.

But I would like to believe that Hanumanji, indeed, graced Arjuna and his flag as per his assurance. But he never fought for him. Did his blessing protected Arjuna? Of course. Isnt that unfair? No more than some warrior having special weapon or skill, better than others. Arjuna earned it.
