How many warriors defeated Radheye and How many warriors Radheye defeated not in the Kurukshetra only but in his whole life span?

📅 Jan 08, 2022 11:31 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Interesting question. But not really important. What do you think is more important? Winning? Defeating? Or the purpose of the war? The cause for which you fight?

Imagine a warrior entering into a battle with the intention of burning a bride alive! Would you want him to win? Would a success like that worth celebrating? Or would you rather celebrate a warrior who may even die defeated but in the process of protecting other? Who would you call more honorable? Whom would you call really victorious?

Again imagine, a strong warrior riding with a large army to mock an exiled family. For fun. He encounters a stronger un expected foe. He fights the battle for a long time. And then he flees leaving behind his best friend and family! Would you celebrate that period where he gave a tough fight or bow in shame for thinking he was a brave warrior? Would you now consider him braver if he stood by his friend even in defeat?

Now imagine, a strong warrior, with a large army marches to plunder a kingdom in distress. Would you really want a plunderer to win? A plunderer who was keen to flee leaving his own brother to die! Would you admire even if he emerges victorious later? Think what is the worth of such a victory, not that such victory ever came.

What really suprises me it that someone wouldn’t admire a warrior who stood alone against such evils.

None of Karna’s battles, in victory or defeat, were for any social cause. He never stood to protect someone in distress. He never stood beyond his own selfish ego, or the selfish interest and evil design of his friend. And whenever he had to choose between himself and anyone else, he always chose himself. As such all his achievements were tainted and not worth relishing.

The greatest warrior the evil Karna ever defeated, nay demolished, was good Karna that existed because of the great parent he had. Karna defeated every single goodness. He was a great donor, but tainted his victory by asking exchange. He may have been a great archer, but his fear of death made him flee every single battle where he appeared to be loosing. He was born to be a great man, but evil Karna defeated him and transformed him into the trunk of an tree that was the evil Duryodhana. Destiny gave several opportunity to Karna’s good side, to become the salvation of humanity, but they were all foiled by the evil Karna who never allowed good to prevail. Krishna, Kunti, Surya all appealed to his good side, but evil side always dominated.

I see this is the greatest victory and greatest defeat of Karna — Karna vs Karna. His fight with other warriors didn’t matter.
