Interesting question. But it has got a quite simple answer — Dhritrashtra had no power to grant any boon. Not everyone can do it. Evil minded selfish soul with nothing good in them certainly can’t grand boon.

You need to have spiritual powers that comes from penance or good deeds. Jaidradh’s father did the penance for years to have earned the right to grant boon. Shantanu was so righteous a king that he was chosen to father Vasu’s.

I daresay King Pandu could have given the boon. He gave up his kingdom and did penance for his mistakes and not a crime. But he chose to educate his son with right values rather unlike Dhritarashtra who only offered his blind ambition in succession.

Dhritrashtra on the other hand had neither the power of penance nor any good did in his account to bless his son. All he had was a desire for the kingdom.

But it would be wrong to say, he didn’t bless his son. He used all the mechinary delegated to him by his brother’s right against his brother’s sons. He supported his son misadventures like lakshagrih, the gambling match. He also misused his power to grant half the kingdom to his sons. Who says thats not a boon?

But he could do only what a conving human can do, he had no spiritual power to offer any divine boon to his son.
