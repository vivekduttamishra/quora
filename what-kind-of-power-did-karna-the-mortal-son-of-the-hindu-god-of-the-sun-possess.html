What kind of power did Karna, the mortal son of the Hindu god of the Sun, possess?

📅 May 29, 2022 06:36 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Before I try to answer this question, let us understand one point where question wrongly emphasizes — that Karna was the son of the Sun God. I am not saying Karna wasn’t a Sun God. He was. But as per the sanatana philosophy we all are parts of the same divine Paramabrahma. There is also an underlying lesson — A person is sum of his actions and the legacy of his parentage while playing an important role is no conclusive testimony to either his character or his destiny.

Let us take a few examples. Ravana was the great grandson of Brahma, the creator of all beings. He was also one of the greatest evils. Then there was Vali, the son of Indra, who too was powerful and crooked. Both were destroyed by Shri Rama. Then there was the other son of Surya, Sugriv, who was too powerless to face the other son of Indra. But he was supported by Rama, because he was a good person.

That tells that the real cash with which you trade your destiny is not your DNA, but you character and adherence to Dharma and every evil is powerless against Dharma. Before they acquire the power destiny creates a weapon or shield to neutralize it. Recall Ravana was immune to death from super powers likes Gods, demons and Yakshas. He was killed by a human incarnate. Hiranya Kashyap thought of every possible way to die and shield himself from all of them. He still died by an impossible being.

Let us now understand the powers of Karna. He was born to an excellent mother, who forced his father to provide him with an unprecedented protection making him virtually immortal. He was trained by the greatest gurus of the era, a distinction not many can claim. But then all these greatness was negated for one simple reason — Karna was a extremely wicked soul (as mentioned by Krishna). His powers were destined to fail. He was destined to fail.

Karna had a divine armor and a Kundala made up of Amrita. He was un-killable. But that didn’t make him invincible. That could have prevented his death. It didn’t make him fearless. And victories don’t come to someone too terrified to stand up and fight. Karna continued to flee, battles after battles, leaving his family, friend and women behind. That fear in heart neutralized all the greatness armor could have offered.

Karna had divine weapons. But they weren’t earned. They were stolen. And the moment, stealing is discovered the guru pronounced that those weapon shall be useless for him. And they certainly didn’t work for him.

Karna was the son of Surya, but he guiltlessly traded the divine gift for a weapon with Indra. At that point he lost that part of Surya he was born with. But it must not be forgotten that Karna would have donated his armor even without exchange had not Surya himself filled him with that idea. That counted for something, if not honest charity, at least the thought. And it is evident from the fact that it was the only weapon that worked in his favor.

You can’t grow big because you are the son of someone great. You have to earn your place in this world. That is the simple rule.

S U M M A R Y

All the external power that Karna possessed were neutralized by his wicked and weak character. There was nothing left to fight with the external world.
