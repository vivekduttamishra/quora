The premise of the question is incorrect on multiple fronts.

1. Throne was never Dhritarashtra’s by right, to begin with.

Long before the birth of Dhritarashtra, King Bharat had decreed that succession would be based on capabilities and not on the birth. That tradition didn’t end with Bharat denouncing his sons for Kingship. Years later and yet ages before the birth of Dhritarashtra, King Pratipa sidestepped his elder son Bahlika and handed over the throne to King Shantanu. Later, after the death of Shantanu, Bahlika or his sons were not considered as a successor of the throne. It was Shantanu’s sons, who laid claim on the throne.

Why was Dhritrashtra side-stepped? Not just because he wasn’t physically fit to rule the kingdom, but mor important, their was a better and more qualified person available to rule. A King is expected to take care of his subjects rather than look after his own selfish interests. It is not toy or a property that you own, it’s a duty that you discharge.

2. Dhritrashtra wasn’t a good choice for the King

Look at Pandu. He expands his kingdom far and wide. He made Hastinapur propsperous. And yet he sentenced himself to exile for his mistake. One mistake. Not a crime. He couldn’t have let himself go. Yet as a King, he discharged his duty. And he set a precident.

On the other hand, Dhritrashtra got to become a king soon after and remained a king all his life. But did he ever considered Kingship as a duty? No he considered it as a property, his right. And see where that led the Aryaravart? He covered the conspiracies by his sons, turned a blind eye to Dharma. No wonder, Vyasa and Krishna called him root of the tree, that was evil Duryodhana.

3. Shouldn’t the throne have gone to Duryodhana by Dharma?

No. How can, Dharma support Adharma? Here are some the important reason why it shouldn’t have gone to Dhritrashtra

  1. As already explained with evidence, in Hastinapur succession was not by birth, but by capabilities. Poisoning someone, burning some one or forming conspiracies are not those qualities that you seek in a King.
  2. Dhritrashtra wasn’t the real King, but a caretaker. And everyone including the blind father and the evil son knew it. Duryodhana feared that people may remove his father and establish Yudhishthir as the King.
  3. Had Duryodhana got any real right to the throne, Dhritrashtra wouldn’t have offered half of Hastinapur to Pandava to establish their own empire.
  4. Pandavas transformed Khandava into Indraprastha. They destroyed Jarasandha and performed Rajsuy Yagna and established themselves as the over lord of Aryavart. This not only proves their might and merit, but concludes for ever, any dispute about their claim on the throne.
  5. Dhritrashtra and Duryodhana not only brought doom for Hastinapur by continously conspiring and thrawting every peace attempt made by Pandava, but constantly proved that they didn’t deserve to be the rulers.

4. If Pandavas had right, why didn’t they assert it rather accepteded unjustified propositions?

That could be one question, by which fans of Duryodhana could try to corner Pandavas. But the answer is really straight forward —

Pandavas were the sons of Pandu. They knew, they have claims to throne, not right to it, They have a duty towards the people and not jsut for their own selfish interest. They followed their father. They decided not to confront even after people tried to burn them alive. Even in exile, without crown or any right they strived for people welfare — Bhima killed Bakasura.

They accepted the barren Khandava and transformed it into an empire. They protected their evil foe Duryodhana and his family from Gandharavas after that vilest of creature had tried to molest his wife and was out in the jungle to mock their miseries that he had brougt onto them.

They had the most potent weapons, and most powerful ally and yet chose to strive for peace and conceded for just five village! Why? Because they cared for people more than themselves. And that made them worthy of the throne.
