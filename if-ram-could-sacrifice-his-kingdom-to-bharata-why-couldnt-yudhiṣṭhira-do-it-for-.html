If Ram could sacrifice his kingdom to Bharata, why couldn't Yudhiṣṭhira do it for Duryodhana?

📅 Mar 07, 2021 09:31 AM PST  |  🌐 English

And the award to the most absurd question of the year goes to…

This question won’t get the award; it would certainly be a candidate.

You may compare Yudhisthir to great Shri Rama, but can you really dare to compare Duryodhana to Bharata?!!!

Both Shri Rama and Yudhishthir were wise and compassionate. Both knew a kingdom is not a property to inherit, but a responsibility to discharge. Even Rama won’t have entrusted his kingdom to someone like Duryodhana, and I daresay, Yudhishthir would have gladly offered kingdom to Duryodhana had he been a fraction of Bharata.

Bharata deserved the kingdom, because he didn’t wanted to be the king. He wasn’t selfish. He didn’t try to murder Rama or his mother. All he wanted was to serve. Ayodhya was blessed to have regent like Bharata. Can you say the same about Duryodhana?

Now consider Yudhishthir. He delayed confrontation as much as possible. He gave Duryodhana endless opportunity to redress his ways. Had Duryodhana, in the process, ever showed the spark of what is needed out of a King, Yudhishthir would have let him rule. His final offer too was generous. Only if Duryodhana’s had evolved. Remeber Yudhishthir chose Shadev over Bhima and Arjuna when Yaksha offered the life of one of his brothers. There Yudhisthir shows his great heart.

To find Rama in front of you, you need to elavate yourself to the level of Bharata.
