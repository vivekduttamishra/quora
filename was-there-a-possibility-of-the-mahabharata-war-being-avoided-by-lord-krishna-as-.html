Was there a possibility of the Mahabharata War being avoided by Lord Krishna as he was God himself?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 04:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

It’s not a question of possibility — Without doubt Krishna could have avoided the war.

Remember, Arjuna had already renounced the war at the very beginning and it took Krisha a Gita level convincing to ask him to fight. He had a great impact of all Pandavas and he could have persuaded them not to fight, and they would have agreed.

Why did Krishna didn’t prevent the war?

Because he is the God. And some misconception related to God and War as apparent from this question is —

  1. God prefers non-violance
  2. God would be against war
  3. The evil that people do is caused by another parellel power called saitan.
  4. God tries to protect everyone.

In Hindu philosophy —

  1. God is one without a comparision ( अद्वैत) — there is no saitan or any other power parallel to him.
  2. God created Deer and Tiger. He is not a preacher of violance or non-violance. he created both.
  3. Evil that men do, is not controlled by any parallel power such as saitan, but by the three gunas (sato, rajo, tamas). Since with proper training these gunas can be controlled, your sins are your doing.
  4. God doesn’t interfere into every of your action — He is not some kind of micro manager for you activity. He created the law of gravitation and when you jump you fall. He doesn’t come to throw you down. This is as simple.

But the most significant saying attributed to Krishna is —

yada yada hi dharmasya
glanir bhavati bharata
abhyutthanam adharmasya
tadatmanam srjamy aham

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

Reference: Bhagwat Gita 4.7, 4.8

Whenever there is a decline in Dharma, O Bharat (Arjuna), to uplift or restabish the Dharma (rightious actions), by protecting the good, and destroy the evil, I shall incarnate.

Now think, what would be achieved by preventing the war. The evil practices, were on the rise, people with devilish mindset controlling the destiny of masses and how was he supposed to restablish Dharma?

In my understanding — why was the war supposed to be avoided? To Protect those who were destroying Dharma?

Many say Gandhari cursed him. She did. Because his sons died. But does that change the fact that his sons were evil and were supposed to be destroyed? That Krishna intentionally didn’t extend his protection to them?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the grand Indian Epic. I have taken reference from English translation of Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG), Bibek Devroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkantha Mahabharat (Gita Press). I have also quoted Bhagwat Gita Chapter 4, Sloka 7 and 8.
