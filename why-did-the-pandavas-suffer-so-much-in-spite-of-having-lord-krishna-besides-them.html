Why did the Pandavas suffer so much in spite of having Lord Krishna besides them?

📅 Jun 14, 2020 08:27 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the request to answer.

This is an interesting question and undeniable fact that suffered all their life.

The great epic is not just some fairy tale with a happy ending, it is something that happened over 5000 years ago. And so many civilizations, empires, and dynasties evolved and destroyed to dust. So what makes Mahabharat stand out? It is the underlying struggle and message.

What is the mission of our epic?

  1. Walk of Dharma The path to seek justice wouldn’t be an easy walk. You may take shortcuts, may win wars, may even rule for years. But that won’t be a legend worth repeating for thousands of generations.
  2. Moral Character Pandava’s suffered injustice after injustice — Bheema poisoned, Lakshagrih, Dyutshabha, Vanvas, and Agyatvas. Yet they remained steadfeat on their moral values —
    1. They forgave them for Bheema’s poisoning and Lakshagrih
    2. They abided by the terms of dyut gambling knowing they were cheated.
    3. Despite of acquiring or divine blessings and powers over the years, instead of breaking the terms of exile, or staying neutral in a war that was certain to destroy Duryodhana, they aided them against Gandharvas. That showed their moral strength.
    4. They suffered and yet preferred a peaceful resolution over a bloody war, to prove it wasn’t just some property dispute our kingdom they were after.
  3. Delliama between Dharma and Tradition
    I think this is another fine lesson that makes the epic stand out. Pandava’s suffered all their life because —
    1. They failed to differentiate between Dharma and Tradition. They failed to realize, that common court decorum and protocol constitute a tradition and while may impersonate Dharama, is not the same thing.
    2. They failed to realize that justice seeks punishment even for the near ones. Forgiving an evil is nurturing an evil, and Pandava’s were guilty of nurturing the evil Duryodhana.
  4. The Most Important — What is God
    Our epics clarifies one of the most important misconception about the role of God in our life — not that of an assistant or a over protective parent spoon feeding us, pulling us out from minor problems, saving our cars from meeting an accident or preventing any scratch to us. He is not some kind of genie, but a mentor and the ultimate guiding light. Like a lighthouse, he shows the way, but not walk for us.
    1. Krishna the almighty, all knowing, the all powerful friends of Pandavas could have prevented every mishaps and made their life a bed of roses. Why didn’t he? What lesson would Krishna leave behind?
    2. He could have destroyed the entire Kaurava’s army without blinking. So why didn’t he?
  5. Role of Krishna
    It is difficult to be powerful and not act when people expect you would. That made Krishna divine and not just some other warrior. Krishna could have aided Pandavas, but he didn’t, and the reasons I believe —
    1. Krishna didn’t interfere in Dyutkrida as he wanted Pandavas to take the call on dharma and tradition.
    2. Yudhishthir’s decision must have disappointed Krishna. He perhaps, realized that Pandavas deserve their share of punishment. He let them struggle.
    3. Krishna also knew those struggles make heros. It was during this exile, while Arjuna received Pashupatastra from Mahadev, Bheema got in the good grace of Hanuman. Had they remained in the luxary of palace they may never have realized what are the pains of ordinary people and the true mistakes they made… and finally Yudhishthir was ready for war.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
