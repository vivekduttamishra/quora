Why was Gandhi jealous of Bhagat Singh?

📅 Mar 15, 2020 08:14 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I think that would be an overstatement!

Bhagat Singh was a young revolutionary whose main reason to surrender was to propagate his ideas to masses which were either ignorant about their great motivation or have completely misunderstood them. HSRA often raised the funds by raiding rich people. While the commander of HSRA like Chandrashekhar Azad and Ramprasad Bismil ensured that the entire money is used only for the revolution and not for any personal goal, people often confused their deeds. Several thugs had also propped up robbing in the false name of HSRA. They were working from the shadows and it was difficult for them to come out in open and explain their ideas to the masses. Bhagat Singh succeeded in educating the mass only after he surrendered — it was a noble plan.

Gandhiji was already one of the most prominent faces of India’s freedom movement and everybody was listening to him. Their ideals differed greatly and Gandhiji often rebuked them for like a father not happy with the path his sons are taking — there was absolutely no reason or ground for jealousy.

Bhagat Singh himself, while differing with Gandhiji, was never disrespectful the great leader. Gandhiji had written a letter to viceroy pleading against hanging of Bhagat Singh.
