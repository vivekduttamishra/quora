Is there a misconception that Gandhi is admired by everyone in India? I used to think almost everyone there probably admired him but I have spoken to some people from India and they dislike him for the partition of India.

📅 Feb 08, 2021 11:56 PM PST  |  🌐 English

It would be a misconception to believe anyone can be admired by everyone. People find fault with even Great Rama and Krishna! And same people also glorify the evils like Ravana.

We do so because we are not designed to agree — we don’t grow in same atmosphere; we don’t get the same education, and we don’t see the same rationality. But in case of Mahatma Gandhi, it is also because of two mor reasons —

  1. Not everyone who write against him has actually read anything trust-worthy.
  2. In condemning Mahatma Gandhi, they think they are condemning the contemporary Gandhi family — they are wrong. Current Gandhi family has no relevance and they are ideological, as different from each other as two beings can be.

Since it is a univeral fact that nobody can be admired by everybody, let us try to list few names which we can trust and who admired Mahatma Gandhi —

  • Netaji Subhash Chandra admired him enough to call him The Father of the Nation. Naturally you won’t give someone this strature lightly!
  • Dr Rajendra Prasad, the first president of India called Gandhi and personification of Ganga, the purifier. It may not be out of place to point out Dr Rajendra Prasad had political differences with Jawahar Lal Nehru and there was no reason for him to praise Gandhiji for furthering so called Nehru’s political agenda.
  • Prime Minister Narendra Modi admires him enough to invoke his name and ideology. Surely Narendra Modi is not a congress agent.
  • Mohan Bhagwat recently called Gandhiji as staunch Hindu, that he was, giving a slap on the face of those who prefer calling him anti-Hindu.
  • Lord Mount Batten compared Gandhiji with Jeasus Christ and Gautam Buddha.
  • Albert Einstien, the eminent scientist admired Mahatma Gandhi saying generations to come shall scarsely believe that a man of flesh and blood ever walked on this planet.
  • Barak Obama, Dalali Lama, Martin Luthar King Junior, Steve Jobs are other some prominent name who have openly admired Mahatma Gandhi.
  • BBC named him the greatest man of the century.

People often claim, Gandhiji is overhyped by sponsored history books. They don’t realize few important thing while pushing forward this lousy claim —

  1. The entire world who came love and admire Gandhiji didn'tt read Indian books of history whom they can call sponsored. Neither do they have political reasons to be aligned with current congress party.
  2. The Gandhian era was also the era of Video, Radio and News Papers. The world had become global enough to fight two world-wars. It was no more an isolated piece of land. What you call history today was a current affair for people like Albert Einstien and George Berand Shaw. And they weren’t reading Indian books of history, rather news paper and article written by their trust worthy journalists and authors.
  3. Leaders, historians, authors and journalists from around the world, who have no affiliations with Indian politics admired him and continue to do so.

SUMMARY

It is useless to assume that anyone can be admired by everyone. So we shouldn’t be bothered by baseless critics. We should form our opinion based on —

  • Proper reading which excludes social media opinions (including mine). There are many great books written by eminent authors both Indian and foreigner that can be cross-referenced.
  • Trust the views of contemporary eminent personalities like — Shubhash Chandra Bose, Albert Einstier, Lord Mount Batten, Dr Rajendra Prasad.
  • Trust the views of great world leaders with sufficient influences like — Narendra Modi, Dalali Lama, Barak Obama, Nelson Mandela, Martin Luthar King Jr, Steve Jobs to name a few.
  • Ask why the entire world admires him. Watch Americal TV Shows to see Gandhi has become a metaphor for saintly, non-violent ideology.
  • Ask yourself, who are those people who denounce him — what are their credentials, study and followship. How many world leaders or prominent personalities you know and can trust critisize Mahatma Gandhi.

At the end of the day form your own opinion, based on rationale thinking and research and not just based on propagandas on social media.
