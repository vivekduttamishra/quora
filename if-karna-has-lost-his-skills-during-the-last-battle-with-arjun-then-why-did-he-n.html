If Karna has lost his skills during the last battle with Arjun, then why did he need to be killed when disarmed condition?

📅 Oct 23, 2020 08:57 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

The most important part of this question is “if“ and makes answer unneeded. Answer is also unneeded because it has been asked and answered multiple times.

Had Karna lost his skills?

No. He was supposed to loose his knowledge of Brahmastra. But in his last battle, we see him invoking Brahmastra and Varunastra. Maybe, he couldn't use the higher Brahmastra, but he didn't loose his core skills. Now this may be debated what core skills he possessed.

Was he killed unarmed?

Oh, didn't I already answered the question? Karna didn't die unarmed. Rather he was killed while he was fighting. So he went down fighting. He had no chariot, but he had his weapons.

Why he needed to be killed?

It was never about his skills, it was about good and evil, it was about Dharma. Tell me how many great warriors did Karna kill with his skill? Any prominent name to his credit? But the war would have been lost if evil forces were not iradicated. Remember, it was Krishna's dictate to kill Karna, who had pronounced his crimes before asking Arjuna to kill him.
