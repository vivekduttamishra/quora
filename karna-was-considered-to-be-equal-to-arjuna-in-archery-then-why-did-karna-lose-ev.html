Karna was considered to be equal to Arjuna in archery then why did Karna lose every duel with Arjuna badly?

📅 Apr 01, 2021 12:06 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

First, the basic premise of the question is wrong. None of the warriors really called them equal —

  • Shiva himself proclaimed Arjuna to be the greatest warrior.
  • Parshuram too announced that Arjuna in invincible and no one can battle with him.
  • Bhisma called him atirathi
  • Drona compared him with Indra
  • Krishna challenged entire Kuru assembly that no one can defeat Arjuna.

But the best comes from Karna himself. On multiple occasion Karna himself claimed Arjuna to be better than him —

  • He approaches Drona to say he need Brahmasira because he thinks he should be equal to Arjuna. Earlier he found Arjuna to be better than everyone.
  • After Draupadi Swyamvara, Karna compared Arjuna with Indra, Vishnu and Parshurama.
  • After Gandharva war, unknowingly that Arjuna won it, announced that he doesn’t think anyone can achieve the feat that he had performed.
  • In front of Indra he confesses that he is terrified of Arjuna.
  • Before his death of day 17, he again confessed that there is no warrior or archer who is Arjuna’s equal.

Most important, why did Karna lost every single battle against Arjuna.

While not comparable to Arjuna, Karna was still a great and reputed archer. To add to that he had his earning from the best of the bests — Drona, Kripa and Parshurama. He was born with divine kavach and kundal, curtsey to his mother. With those kavach and kundal, Karna was supposed to be invincible. Yet. He lost. Again and again. That makes the second part of the question relevant.

We must understand that there is a huge difference between an archer and a warrior. An Olympic gold-medalist is a great archer. But that doesn’t make them great warrior. So what is the difference between an archer and a warrior? It is courage and perseverance. An archer who can do marvel in a play arena where there is no mortal danger to his life won’t perform well in a battlefield where he needs to protect himself and his team.

Do you know, what was the only occasion where Karna performed at par with Arjuna? That was the play arena where he tried to out perform Arjuna. His performance won him a kingdom, no less. But then time for play was over.

Then the great archer met warriors in the battle field. It was not to showcase his skills but fight the real target who too shot back. And in such cases you need courage more than your ability to aim the target.

The kavach and kundal could have protected Karna from wounds and death. They weren’t meant to kill enemies. They were no compensation to the lack of inner strength. They can’t stop a warrior from fleeing out of fear. And that's exactly what happened again and again. Had Karna succeeded in defeating his own fear, he may have been invincible.

He was born to win, but conditioned himself to lose.
