Lord Krishna was after Lord Rama according to the timeline. Does Lord Krishna ever mention Lord Rama? How, where and why?

📅 May 01, 2020 11:45 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

You don't have to search for an obscure reference. One of the most important is in Gita, chapter 10.

पवनः पवतामस्मि रामः शस्त्रभृतामहम्।

झषाणां मकरश्चास्मि स्रोतसामस्मि जाह्नवी।।10.31।।

pavanaḥ pavatām asmi rāmaḥ śhastra-bhṛitām aham

jhaṣhāṇāṁ makaraśh chāsmi srotasām asmi jāhnavī

"Among the purifiers (or the speeders) I am the wind; Rama among the warriors am I; among the fishes I am the shark; among the streams I am the Ganga."

—Gita Chapter 10, Sloka 31

Here not only Krishna mentions Ram, but hails him the greatest among warriors (by comparing to himself)

Apart from this Van Parva of Mahabharat contains Vyas version of Ramkatha, proving both existence of Ram Katha and his glory.
