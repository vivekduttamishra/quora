Your confusion is justified if you ignore time, context, intent and speaker and try to focus on just a few words.

Think —

  • Which is likely to be more accurate — An incident report from the crime scene or one recalled after 13 years in an extremely stressful situation?
  • Who is more trust worthy narrator — A rishi that observes, detached of emotions or a frustrated king who had lost all his family and hopes for victory and knows he stood on a path of no return?
  • What is more important — A crime committed against a woman in public or the accuracy and graphical description of how much humilty she faced?

Now let me take you to the crime scene — the court room of Hastinapur. Duryodhana’s wickedness was at the peak. He had won. And he decided to push his luck further. The lustful Duryodhana asks his brother to drag Draupadi into the chamber. His equally deceitful friend decides to take the humiliation to the next level. He Dussassana to strip Draupadi.

At this point something happens — something no one could have guessed. Some one know one had predicted. A Miracle. Draupadi was saved. They not only failed to disrobe her, they also lost whatever they had won by deceit.

Flash forward by over 13 years. Time for redumption. Duryodhana is remembering all his sins. one by one. He remembers his intentions. He remembers his intention was to make Draupadi nude. He had done his part. Does it matter it succeeded or not? Does that reduce his crime? Does that reduce his guilt? Well that is what you see in the fourth chapter of Shalya Parva. Just don’t read a single line. Read what comes before and after those para.

Let us also understand. You brought a woman dragged in a single garment. In the open court. Such humiliation is a metaphor for making her naked. She may not have been more humiliated? And in the dirty mind of Duryodhana, she was already nakeD. If he had his way, he had made her naked. And he and his clan had to pay the exact price they would have paid to make her naked. Duryodhana was counting that debt, he had to pay.


TAKEAWAY

As per the actual incident Dussassana failed to strip Draupadi naked. The divine intervention that protected Draupadi, didn’t extend its protection to Duryodhana. Draupadi was offered extended garment, Duryodhana was stripped of his hope for salvation. Duryodhana and his evil supports paid for making Draupadi naked.
