Is Karna a hero or a villain?

📅 Nov 08, 2021 03:32 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is one question that can be answered with utmost conviction. Karna was a villain. There are few for whom the same can be said with this much conviction. And I would say so, even if I include a few good deeds that he did in his life.

TRUNK OF THE EVIL TREE

And it is not just my opinion, but a stated fact in Mahabharata. In the beginning of Mahabharata, he has been referred as the trunk of an evil tree that was Duryodhana. The same is repeated by Krishna in Udyoga Parva. Now some may say he was called as the trunk of the tree of passion and not an evil tree. To them I would remind the tree in context is metaphor for Duryodhana who has been called evil in the entire epic, repeatedly.

AMBITION AND DECEIT

Karna had been a part of every conspiracy of Duryodhana from the very beginning which has been mentioned first by Vaisampanya Das, then again by not only Krishna but Karna himself. And during the gurukul period he had no real reason for personal grievance other than jealousy and ambition.

Ambition may not be a bad thing, unless it begins to dominate every other characteristics of one’s life. He deserted his first guru because he declined to offer him divine weapons and obtained it from his other guru by deceit. He would go to any extent to fulfil his dream — to prove he is a better archer than Arjuna. And he left no stones unturned.

Some may attempt to exonerate Karna of his misdoing as his obligation towards his evil friend Duryodhana. But a careful examination would show it wasn’t true. In some of the worst cases, it was Karna and not Duryodhana who initiated evil deeds —

  • It wasn’t Duryodhana, Duhsasana or Shakuni who was directly responsible for the attempt to molest Draupadi. It was Karna’s idea. He not only rebuked Vikarna, lest sanitiy may prevail, but commanded Duhsasana to dirobe her. His villainy didn’t stop even when the attempt was foiled. He continued to abuse her with choicest words. This incident alone is sufficient to prove him a villain beyond reasonable doubts. I call even a one time molester a villain and am not inclined to pardon because he is one time molester. What about you?
  • It was Karna who masterminded the expedition known has Ghosha Yatra an attempt to seek pleasure from the miseries and suffering of his enemies. Just imagine, someone who seeks pleasure in other’s suffering. How can you call him a hero?
  • Matasya battle wasn’t a battle. It was a looting expedition. The idea was coined by Susarma of Trigartha. But Karna was the first one to have endorsed the idea of this expedition of looting a kingdom in distress.
  • Karna along with Shakuni, Duryodhana and Duhasana masterminded the capture of Krishna to thwart all attempts at peace.

COMMON MYTH OF HIS GREATNESS

1. He was a great friend who sacrificed the Kingdom to support his friend.

Karna indeed declined the offer of Krishna to become the King. Krishna’s goal was to avert a disastrous war that was to plunge humanity in sorrow. It wasn’t for the sake of Dharma and not selfishness. Karna refused saying he can’t abandon his friend in the hour of distress. Wasn’t that a noble gesture?

If you want to say, preferring the selfish interest of a friend greater than concerns for humanity, and believe just the words and not the actions, then yes, it was a noble act. Karna was a faithful friend like Sambha of Gabbar Singh. Was Sambha a hero or a villain? Was Kumbhkarna a hero or a villain? You take the call.

But unfortunately there are other incidents which when you consider together it busts the myth that Karna rejected the Kingship just for his dear friend.

We must understand each of us have different ambition — Not everyone wants to become the Prime Minister. Few wants to become great actors, others well known cricketer. They may not be keen in running for Prime Minster’s office. Right? Karna had no ambition to become a King. He said himself. So can we call his refusal to become something he never wanted to be, as a great sacrifice?

If Karna really was a great friend of Duryodhana, he would have accepted the Kingship and transferred it to Duryodhana. Period. He knew his options but refused. He knew without a doubt the consequences of the battle — death of his friend and his clan. Yet he opted for the war. Now the question is, in whose favor did he opt for the war? Duryodhana? Humanity? Think! Or wait for the next section.

If he really was a great friend ready to die for his friend —

  • Why did he fled Gandharva’s abandoning his best friend and his family that included women of the family also?
  • Why did he not fight for first ten days in Kurkukshetra? Some may say Bhishma insulted him. Even if it were true, it only proves his ego was greater than his loyalty. But if you read the epic you would realize it wasn’t just the insult, but the ambition of getting the credit of his achievements that kept him out of the battle.

2. He spared the life of his four brothers.

Superficially it appears as a great charity, even a great sacrifice. But wait for a while. Who paid the price of this sacrifice? Was it Karna? No he earned a lot of goodwill to last for five thousand years. But who paid the real cost of this pledge?

To understand this, let us ask, who would have benefitted had Karna killed Yudhishtira or Bhima? The answer is Duryodhana.

Now ask, who suffered the most because Karna refused to kill Bhima or Durydoyhana? The answer is again Duryodhana and all his brothers who trusted Karna. Duryodhana sacrificed his 31 brothers to directly save Karna and others because Karna didn’t nothing to save them.

So the question is again, who paid the price of this pledge?

Remember, he had no obligation to spare any life. Kunti didn’t ask for it in the first place. He could have spared no one. Or all. Or three out of five.

So the next question — Why did Karna not made an exception for Bhima in his oath?

Had Karna not spared Bhima, Kauravas had a surviving chance. But Karna generously spared Bhima for no reason, certainly not convey his loyalty for his friend. He certainly got a glory.

Worse, he never shared his hidden pact with one person who was worse affected by his treachary.

But a more important question — Why did he made an exception for Arjuna?

Was Arjuna not his brother? Was he not Kunti’s son? Was Arjuna the one who had pledged to kill his best friend? No. Then why did he made an exception for Arjuna. I won’t answer this question. I would let you reach your own conclusion. But I can tell you this — answer to this question is the key to understanding the true character of Karna — who is ruthless enough to spare the life of the nemesis of his friend but not of a person, whom he knew as his younger brother.

Karna also had a abusive nature and while he spared the life of his brothers, it was never without humiliating them with his abusive words. I don’t realize how it was part of his pledge.

3. He was a daanveer who donated to anyone who approached him.

Yes Karna was a renowned donor who regularly donated riches to whoever applied to him. But then most Kings of that era donated wealth. Even Yudhishthira did it.

Question is what are some famous donations or charity by Karna?

The Most Famous — He donated his Kavacha and Kundala to Indra

Well he may have donated. But if you charge something in exchange, we can’t call it a donation. And Karna flatly refused to donate without an exchange. In explicit terms. Does that make Karna a villain? Absolutely NOT. It makes Karna a strategist. Not evil. Not villain. But no hero either.

There are few well documented instances when people approached Karna for significant donations and he declined each of those times —

  • He didn’t donated his Kavacha, but exchanged it. He refused to donate otherwise.
  • He refused to accept Krishna’s offer, that was for the welfare of humanity.
  • He refused to fulfil the request of his mother. He may have had his reasons. But then, we can’t call him an unconditional donor.

Other traits of Karna character

Next to ambition and lust, the greatest trait of Karna’s character was a total lack of courage. He was powerful and a great archer, but lacked perseverance. He constantly fled the battle field again and again. Does that make him a villain? NO. coward doesn’t automatically become villain. But they never make a hero. And when you are ready to pay any price for preserving your life, you do despicable things that makes you villain.

  • Karna fled Gandharvas abandoning his best friend and his family behind. He got scared and that emotion eclipsed his loyalty. He didn’t care if his benefactor would live or die. Would you call heroic? Would you cherish it? Remember, it was his expedition
  • Karna fled Arjuna leaving his brother to be slaughtered. It was once again his expedition.
  • He fled Abhimanyu as the later slaughter yet another brother of his.

So, for a person, whose self-preservation tops all other emotion, I fail to find a hero in him.
