If the Nagastra shot by Karna on Arjuna was super powerful and deadly even without Ashwasena, why didn't Karna use this Nagastra on Ghatotkacha while Karna could save Indra's Vasavi power by doing so for Arjuna?

Thanks for the A2A.

This question has two parts.

A. WAS NAGASTRA POWERFUL AND DEADLY WITHOUT ASHWASENA?

Nagastra was called nagastra because of Ashwasena. It wasn’t a divine weapon of the Gods, but a vengeful serpent sitting on the arrow —

He released that arrow, the serpent which he had worshipped for many years. He said, ‘O Phalguna! You have been slain,’ and swiftly shot the arrow. On seeing that Karna had affixed the serpent, Madhava, supreme among strong ones, used his strength to press down on the chariot with his feet. The chariot sank down on the ground and the horses sank down on their knees.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4877-4880).

  • The serpent was the arrow. That serpent he had worshipped for years
  • Krishna saw, Karna putting a serpent on the arrow.
  • Karna had appeased that serpent for several years. Why worship a firm enemy of Arjuna? Is it a difficult guess?

Now why there may be a confusion, that the two were different is the way this part is narrated going in present and past. But a careful reading removes any reason to doubt —

The serpent released from Karna’s arms was extremely radiant, like the rays of the sun. That giant serpent was firm in its enmity of Arjuna. It struck the diadem and fell down. It told him, 792 ‘Know who I am. I am firm in my enmity of the two Krishnas, because they slew my mother.’793 In the battle, Krishna then spoke to Partha. ‘The giant serpent is firm in its enmity. Slay it.’ Having been thus addressed by Madhusudana, the wielder of Gandiva, fierce in using the bow and arrow against enemies, asked, ‘Who is the serpent who is advancing against me of his own accord, as if into Garuda’s gaping mouth?’ Krishna replied, ‘In Khandava, you satisfied the blazing one794 with the bow in your hand. You killed his mother, taking her to be a single snake. However, though her body was destroyed by the arrows, he was in the sky, covered by her.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4893-4899).

  • Karna fired his weapon affixing Ashwasena who had firm enimty with Arjuna.
  • There is no difference shown.

B. WHY KARNA DIDN'T USE THIS WEAPON AGAINST GHATOTKATCH?

When Indra offered his Vasvi shakti to Karna, he made it clear. You can't use it unless you are left with no other powerful weapon. If you do so, it would back fire. Now invocation of Vasvi shakti was a success. That means the required conditions were met, and Karna had used all the other weapons on Ghatotkatch. Remembers divine weapons (with exception to Vasvi shakti) weren't one time use.

Question is did Karna try Nagastra? It is not explicitly mentioned. But it is certainly mentioned Karna used all divine weapons. Now this gives rise to two possibilities —

First, and the less probable one, Karna used it against Ghatotkatch, and it failed like other weapons, which questions it's powers. Was it really potent enough. Although I really doubt this.

Second, and the more logical, Karna never used it as it wasn't a divine weapon but an alliance of convenience. Ashwasena had an enimty with Arjuna and he had no reason to exeert himself against Ghatotkatch and risk his lifetime mission and give up the element of surprise.

No matter which of the above two alternatives you go with, two points are abundantly clear in

Arjuna vs (Karna + Ashwasena) face-off conclusion

First, Ashwasena was dead moments after his foiled attempt to kill Arjuna by deceit. He, however, did strartle both Krishnas for a moment as neither has expected Vrisha Karna to stoop so low as to use a vile creature for a weapon. Afterall didn't Krishna told Arjuna, that Karna's virtuous nature earned him name Vrisha? But well, we can see, Krishna often exaggerated people like Karna and Shalya to motivate Pandavas out perform.

Second, Karna accepted his defeat the moment, he forged this alliance. It was crying out loud — I can't defeat Arjuna. Please help! I have no hope to win. I just want to kill him. And this moment occurred years before Karna deployed the plan. He was praying the serpent for years. Why? Was it just a coincidence that he worshipped one serpent who was firm enemy or Arjuna?
