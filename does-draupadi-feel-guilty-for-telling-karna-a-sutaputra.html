Does Draupadi feel guilty for telling Karna a 'Sutaputra'?

📅 May 09, 2020 11:57 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

No Draupadi never felt guilty, and that is the biggest proof, that the said incident is an later addon. In Mahabharat apart from matching various manuscript, we also use a technique called cross referencing — is certain incident referred back elsewhere in the story. Draupadi insulting Karna doesn't figure out anywhere in the epic —

  • No one reminds her of her mistake anywhere in the epic — neither Krishna nor Karna.
  • She never regrets it — because perhaps she was unaware of the incident.

References —

  • The incident doesn't find any mention in Dakshinayat and BORI edition.
  • The Neelkanth recitation includes this incident but not only contradicts it in the footnote but also in very next chapter indicating that warriors like Shalya and Karna failed… How would someone fail if he was denied?
  • Gita Press Hindi Sanskrit Version mentions same it the same way as Neelkantha.
  • Rajgopalachari's highly acclaimed Mahabharat retelling also doesn't mention it.
  • Kisari Mohan Ganguli mentions this story and dilutes the next one by mentioning Shalya and others fail…

The overwhelming references suggest in no clear term that Draupadi never insulted great Karna and had no reason to regret. An naturally she never regretted when she had no idea as to what to regret about.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
