If Draupadi rejecting Karna is mentioned in 4 manuscripts only, why do KMG and Gita press used that?

📅 Dec 31, 2021 07:15 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: Both KMG and Geeta Press are based on a single common source. So it’s not about four manuscript, but just one.

NEELKANTHA COMMENTARY

Draupadi’s rejecting Karna is mentioned in five manuscripts. One of the prominent among them is commentary by Neelkantha Chaturdhara. This script includes both incidents —

  • Draupadi objected Karna’s participation
  • Karna’s failure to lift the bow — In fact Neelkhantha includes two reference of failure against one shloka where Draupadi objected Karna’s participation. It also includes other incidents that proves Draupadi couldn’t have objected —
    • Arjuna was allowed to participate despite his credentials were unknown. Drupada wasn’t sure if Arjuna was a Brahmana or a Vaisya or Shudra.
    • Yudhishthira makes Drupada’s priest speechless by arguing that King has no right to ask the lineage or varna of participants as per swyamvar norms.

Considering all the points Neelkhanta, despite of including a lone shloka where Draupadi forbids Karna from participation provide several passages which contradcits it.

KMG MAHABHARATA

The Neelkantha commentary gained prominence when Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) used is as the primary source of translation. Since it is based on the works of Neelkantha it is logical that it would include the rejection. But it so appears that KMG has censured one of the Karna’s failure by removing his name from the Shloka. But there still exist one shloka where Karna’s failure is explicitly mentioned. Not only that the other two references mentioned above, which prof. Suthankar considers vital, is also included in KMG.

Conclusion: KMG not only includes Draupadi objecting Karna but also Karna’s failure. This is clearly documented.

GEETA PRESS

Geeta Press is also based on Neelkantha commentary. Naturally it too includes the shloka where Draupadi objected to Karna’s participation. But there are few important points to note here —

  • Geeta Press includes both references explicitly where Karna is mentioned among those failed at Swaymvar.
  • It also includes important references that proves Karna couldn’t have been forbidden as per the norms of swaymvar.

We must also note that Geeta Press version explicitly mentions in the footnote that Draupadi’s forbidding Karna is considered as an interpolation. It derives it’s reference from the cross-reference and BORI.

SUMMARY

  • There is not if or but — There are only five edition that includes Drapadi’s objection to Karna’s participation.
  • Geeta Press and KMG include this incident not because based on how many versions included it but because of the one version they refer — Neelkantha.
  • Both KMG and Geeta Press also mentions Karna failure. Neelkantha and Geeta Press includes two references of Karna’s failure. KMG censored one but the other exists.
  • All the three versions include important references that makes it obvious that Draupadi couldn’t have rejected Karna
