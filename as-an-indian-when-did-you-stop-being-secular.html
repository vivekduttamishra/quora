As an Indian, when did you stop being secular?

📅 Apr 18, 2020 12:42 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

As an Indian, I was never secular. Infact India was never meant to be secular, and that is why our constitution makers didn't add the word secular to it — it’s meaning is too narrow and inappropriate to be used for India.

Secular word originally means as separation between state (governance) and religion (church). This may work for country with single religion or faith. But how do you apply it in India with a million color rainbow culture? Would you separate state and religion? Or religion and religion? And what about connecting culture? Secular word was too narrow for India's culture and India already had a much wider relevant word — Hindu.

Following true hindu spirit

Ekam sat Vipra Bahudha Vadanti

Mahatma Gandhi followed age old tradition of Srava dharma Sambhav

Unfortunately at a much later date when our constitution was suspended, it was distorted and word secular was added which was originally debated and not included by original makers of the constitution. It is added and used to demean ancient Indian (hindu) culture and create political capital and divide and appeasement.

Now the dream of ancient India and Gandhiji stands shattered.
