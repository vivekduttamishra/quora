Which Mahabharat book is most authentic with deep and detailed story, which is convenient to buy and read?

📅 Oct 07, 2020 08:29 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

You are asked something that legend says Draupadi asked — An impossible choice for a husband. You want deep, and convenient both — not going to happen. So let me grant you a boon like Shiva, Your desire won't feet in one book, but multiple —

  1. Start with a simple, authentic and unabridged edition like Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari.
  2. Read Gita a few times. Great start, and you know whom to trust while reading the epic and you will be saved from the traps of distorted tale and fake heros. Many of our friends have become editcted to them.

Having understood the story and philosophy, here you have your next three choices —

3. BORI edition of Mahabharata — It is the critical edition of Mahabharata, which has done a great work of weeding out interpolation and avoiding myths. But it has modified a certain parts despite the fact that they existed in most authentic versions. So we continue our search.

4. Gita Press Mahabharat — based on the Neelkantha edition with cross reference to BORI, is quite authentic and well written 9000 pages divided into 6 volumes.

5. KMG Mahabharata — I listed the famous and the first ever English translation of Mahabharata by Kisari Mohan Ganguli at the end of my list because of the convince of read. An 1881 style of writing is not an easy read. So we warned.

I have done a detailed analysis with a podcast over here — Mahabharata — Begining and milestone of an endless journey

A word on fictions

I am big fan of fictions as well and read a lot of fictions based on Mahabharata. When you do so, don’t look for authenticity but perspective, and two know the differences, you must again go and read authentic versions.

Most popular fictional narratives in form of TV shows or book about Mahabharata often distort the epic, not just in favour of a particular character but also against the epic and the Indian culture at the time of epic.

  2. The Accursed God — This is the part 1 of my epic fiction on Mahabharata.
