Had Jayadradha been killed at the time of his attempt to abduct Draupadi and he fell on the spot on the ground would the man who caused his final fall to the ground could have broken his head? Did Yudhistara know this and freed the man?

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah!

Jaidradh had the boon, or more precisely, the curse for his killer, from the time of his birth. So had Pandava killed him, most likely the curse would have come into effect. But, there is a but…

Pandavas didn’t really kill Jaidradh

To answer the last part of the question, there is no evidence Yudhisthir knew about this curse. He let him go, the same way Arjuna let go all the Kuru warriors after vanquishing them in the Matasya war or the way Arjuna gracefully let Bhagdatta go with his dignity during Rajsuy battle and on countless other occasions.

Pandavas avoided bloodshed as much as possible, specifically for personal benefits — for greater good, they certainly vanquished Hidimb, Bakasura and enemies of God at Khandava. They tried to avoid the devastating Mahabharata war despite of all the treachery by Kauravas.

It was there interensic nature that saved him from the curse.

WHAT IF PANDAVA KNEW

The curse would have come into effect against him, who would make his head touch the ground. If they were aware of the secret, they could have still killed him ensuring his head doesn’t touch the ground, at least not by them.

Was that possible? Ask Karna, Drona, Duryodhana and entire Kaurava crew. They were all witness to the feat. Jaidradh was killed, Partha wasn’t. This feat could have taken place thirteen years earlier, if only they wanted. But they weren’t bloodthirsty.
