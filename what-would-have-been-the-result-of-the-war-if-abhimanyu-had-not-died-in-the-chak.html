What would have been the result of the war if Abhimanyu had not died in the Chakravyuh due to unrighteousness?

Behind everything creative lies a great question

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

This question calls for a projective narrative. But projections too should be such that justifies that grand epic culture. But before we dive in the realms of creative imagination, let's first revisit the undeniable facts.

WHAT DID REALLY HAPPEN?

Abhimanyu didn't die in the 'army formation' called chakravyuh, but in a chakravyuh of deceit. He died because 'so called' warriors realized their reality. And that reality was really ugly. They killed him because they were too terrified to face him like a warrior. And even in dying, he destroyed their dream of winning, not just for that day but for any and every other day.

What followed was the wrath of Ghatotkach, Bhimasena and above all Arjuna. Next day, in a single day, more than half the army perished along with them any hope of winning the battle!

WHAT HAPPENED AFTER ABHIMANYU'S DEATH

Ask yourself, what happened to Ghatotkach who was in the battlefield for several days but was never that deadly? As I see, earlier he faught just as duty. But after Abhimanyu’s death his reason became more personal. He could see more clearly, the need of destroying the treacherous Kauravas. Perhaps he saw what they really were — cowards!

Now consider Arjuna. He wasn't willing to kill Bhishma, despite of Krishna's Gita. Krishna had to break his vow and wield the weapon. After Bhishma, Arjuna again assumed the same laxed approach towards Drona. Why? He had too much reverence for his guru and grandfather. But Abhimanyu's death changed it all.

WHAT WOULD HAVE HAPPENED HAD ABHIMANYU NOT BEEN KILLED MERCILESS BY DECEIT

It could mean one of the two possibilities –

  1. Abhimanyu lived and returned victorious.
  2. Abhimanyu was killed righteously (less likely)

But then, as I see, the consequences of both possibilities wouldn't have been much different. And interesting it would have favoured Kauravas rather than Pandavas. Both victory or fair death would have been just another day of battle.

Arjuna would not have much reason to show his wrath. He would have been more careful to spoil future strategy of Drona who may not have dared to form another chakravyuh. War would have progressed slowly and Kauravas may have had a little more time to think –survive or surrender?

Drona may have planned rationally and may not have fought a night battle. Ghatotkach may have fought like any other day and Karna might not have needed to unleash Vasvi Shakti on Ghatotkach making Krishna uncomfortable for a longer period as to how to waste Karna's Vasvi Shakti.

But… Abhimanyu did die a treacherous death and Kauravas paid a price much heavier.
