Do you consider Gandhiji calling off the Non-Cooperation Movement after the Chauri Chaura incident a genius move or a big blunder?

📅 Apr 04, 2021 07:03 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Remebmer Jalianwala Bag Massacre? Even after a hundred years, the scar remains, not only in the hearts of those who lost their love ones, but on the conscience of them who enacted it. Even after a hundred years, British government sounds apologetic for the barbarian. Who of us want to be change our seats with British government and General Dyer on this issue?

None co-operation movement was a response to mindless violence at Jalianwala Bag and it had made British government toothless. It is easy to crush an armed reballian with more power arms in larger number, something that has happened across the world. But fighting someone who attacks your conscience and not you physically, was something no government of the world was equipped to handle.

British government couldn’t have suppressed the movement with force without inviting the ire from all corners of the world, not excluding their own home. And the movement because successful beyond imagination — not only in making Britishers uncomfortable, but also uniting India like never before. We felt we could achieve everything.

Then Chauri Chaura incident happened. It was an unexpected gift landed in British government’s lap. Now they had their opportunity to crush the rebellion once and for all. And it would have been — like the struggle of 1857, like the fate of Hitler or Japan after Perl Harbor.

And again the question is what would be the difference between us and a barbarian like General Dyer? Ignoring Chauri Chaura would have following undeniable repercussions —

  • Gen Dyer would have been vindicated. He would have been hailed visionary rather than Barbarian.
  • Britisher Governement would have retaliated. And retaliated hard. There would have been several more Jalianwala Bag and Chauri Chaura — more Jalianwal Bag than Chauri Chaura as they had more guns.
  • The movement would have been crushed anyway, with British force and lack of moral discipline.
  • There would be no apology.
  • Non-violent movement would have been dead forever.

There is another aspect applicable to every public movement — It involves people — who are not paid to be part of movement (excluding recent paid political movements in India). People still need to work to earn a living. They can’t indefinitely take part in a movement, and at some point the movement will die down like everything else.

Gandhiji called off the movement on his own terms, keeping high moral ground. And as I said, it was already successful in uniting people. It had formed the ground for next nation-wide movement. The national was waking up.

The important take away is such movement should have a finite period and there should be sufficient gap between two such movements giving people opportunity to relax, rejunviniate and bask in the glory of previous movement. Tell the tale of the movement and make it epic.

Take the recent movement by Anna Hazare. It was a grand success. But he started the season II of his movement after a few months. What happened to the second movement? Was it as great a success as the first one? Did it get the same support from the people?

You would realize even paid propaganda movements which we have seen in recent years too can run for ever. And once they die on their own or is crushed it leaves no legacy behind.

What Gandhiji did was not only an act of moral high standard, but also a political genius move that should be in the handbook of do’s and dont’s for public movement.

So what do you think — blunder or genius?
