What Did Krishna say

It is through good fortune that his armour has gone. It is through good fortune that his earrings have been robbed. It is through good fortune that the invincible spear has been used up on Ghatotkacha. Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals. Vasava, Kubera, Varuna, lord of the waters, and Yama would not have ventured against Karna in an encounter. Had that bull among men possessed those, you with your Gandiva and I with my sudarshana chakra would not have had the capacity to defeat him in a battle.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6416-6421).

and

Karna obtained a spear from the great-souled Vasava, the one that has now been used against Ghatotkacha. This was obtained in exchange for the earrings and the celestial armour. Since the time he obtained it, Vrisha has always thought that you have been killed in the battle. O unblemished one! O tiger among men! However, though that spear has now gone, I swear to you truthfully that he is incapable of being killed by anyone other than you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6424-6427).

If you watch carefully, you would find two important thing. I would explain them them one by one. First, he isn’t really praising Karna. He is praising the divine armour and the weapon of Indra. But what did Krishna say about Karna without those divine artifects?

O immensely radiant one! Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain. Had Karna possessed the spear in his hand, no man in the world would have been able to stand before him.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6414-6416).

Krishna says, that without those divine artifects, you can consider Karna already slain. That was Karna without the divine artifiects as Krishna described him.

But then there is another quesion —

Was Karna really invincible with divine armour and the spear of Indra?

Karna fought several battles with his divine armors —

  • Digvijaya battle, where Bhim defeated him.
  • Swyamvara battle against Arjuna where he retreated scared.
  • Gandharava battle where he fled against Gandharvas.

So fact says otherwise, which leads to another question —

Why did Krishna said so?

We must understand Krishna isn’t Yudhishthir. He is not fixiated on trivial truths. He is a great teacher who is ready to be called randchod. For him dharma is important, truth is dispensible. And he knew about battles that Karna lost even with those divine artifiects. Yet he says with those artifects Karna would be invincible something even Indra himself didn’t claim —

Indra said, “You kill one roaring and powerful enemy in battle. But the one you seek to kill is protected by a great-souled one who is known as the unvanquished boar,38 Hari and the inconceivable Narayana by those who are learned in the Vedas.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8555-8557).

Even Indra who knew is weapon best, knew it may not harm Arjuna. So why did Krishna, knowing Karna’s repeated failure and power of those weapons said what he said? The idea is simple. He was motivating Arjuna and trying to make him understand the importance of divine artifects and the reason of Ghatotkatch death. The idea was to make Arjuna realize the ultimate truth about dharma —

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah.
