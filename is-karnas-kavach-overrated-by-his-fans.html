Is Karna's Kavach overrated by his fans?

📅 Sep 24, 2020 12:30 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi,

Karna Kavacha is not overrated — if it is, at least not by the fans of Karna. It has been glorified in Mahabharata authentic texts at multiple places —

  • Surya announced that it was made up on Amrita and Karna can’t be slain as long as he has the Kavacha.

O Karna! If you give away the beautiful earrings that you were naturally born with, your life will be shortened and you will come under the power of death. O one who is reverent! Arrayed in your armour and earrings, you cannot be slain by your enemies in battle. Remember, these words. These two treasures arose from amrita.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata (Kindle Locations 8284-8286).

  • Even Krishna mentions the capacity of Kavacha —

Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6418-6419).

Kavacha may appear exaggerated in the epic itself

I am using the word may, because people often misquote from the epic without understanding the context. Notice the above quote where Krishna says that no one would have been able to defeat Karna, if he had the Kavacha and Kundala. But Karna had been defeated on a number of occassion according to all the available sources —

  • Karna was scared away in Draupadi’s swyamvara.
  • Bhima defeats Karna in Rajsuy yagna
  • Gandharvas defeated and made Karna flee

Even Krishna knew about Karna’s battles lost. So why did he say with Kavacha no one could defeat Karna?

Technically a bearer of the Kavacha made up of Amrita was supposed to be invincible. Karna was a great archer, with a shield that was inpenetrable, and he was virtually imortal. This is what Krishna said.

But any weapon or shield is only as powerful as its bearer. A Kavacha that is made up of Amrita could prevent you from dying, could save you from an arrow wound, but can’t infuse courage in you. So Karna didn’t die in the battles mentioned above.

But he did flee and Kavacha can’t stop a warrior from fleeing, because none of these things are substitute of courage. And one thing Kavacha can’t protect you against is your internal fear. A fear that Karna expresses in front of Indra —

Karna replied, “I wish to kill only one enemy37 in a great battle. He roars and blazes and is the source of fear for me.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8554-8555).

So despite of invincible Kavacha, Karna continued to loose battles.

The kavacha is not overrated, but Karna is.

Let us relook at what Krishna and Surya said about Karna without Kavacha —

O Karna! If you give away the beautiful earrings that you were naturally born with, your life will be shortened and you will come under the power of death.

  • Note, Surya knows Karna without Kavacha doesn’t stand a chance and would die.

Now let us re-look what Krishna said about Karna without Kavacha or Indra’s spear —

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6414-6415).

  • You see, with Kavacha and spear gone, Karna is as good as slain.
  • So is Krishna really worried about the personal valour of Karna?
