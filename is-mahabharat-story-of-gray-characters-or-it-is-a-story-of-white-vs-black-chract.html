Is Mahabharat story of gray characters or it is a story of white Vs black chracters like Ramayana?

📅 Oct 30, 2020 10:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi

Thanks for the A2A.

I don’t think our epics as black and white or grey. They are colourful with all shades of every color — white of purity and saffron or valour, blackness or evil heart and pinkness or romance, gray of confusion, rainbow of emotions like love, anger, hate, hue of loyalty and contrast of treachery.

Both Ramayana and Mahabharata are colourful with different colours denoting different characters.

Ramayana

  • Take sage like Vashistha — He represents purity and wisedom. Can the two have same shade?
  • Vishwamitra — Is a spectrum from courage, arrogance and royalty and jealousy to devotion, penances and ultimate wisdom and courage. His older darker shades were polished by his penance and gets a brighter aura.
  • Take the protaganists Rama and Lakshamana — they show shades of courage, valour, devotion, leadership, compassion. Can you represent everything black-white-gray?
  • Take Ravana — courage, wisdom were smeared by the prevailing blackness of his character — lust, arrogance, selfishness and brutality. His others colors were lost under his blackness.
  • Then there is Vibhishan. He has compassion for his people and wisdom to take right actions for them. But the right action leads him to rebel against his brother. Some may call it treachary.

Mahabharata

  • Bhisma — Colors of devotion, courage and wisdom makes him colourful, but his adherence to his oath at all cost makes him lose shine.
  • Dhritrasthra — He was blind. But that doesn’t stop him from having a colorful dream, where he was supposed to be the King and his son’s were to follow him. He rubbed those colors of jealosy, treachary to his sons as well.
  • Karna — Tried to paint the world in his own color, where he was a victim and the greatest archer of all time, where he was loyal with him own priorities.
  • Duryodhana — With ambition and jealousy taking a deeper and darker root, he too showed colors of determination and persistence. Both Karna and Duryodhana rubbed their color on each other.
  • Arjuna — Courage, valour, charm, loyality, leadership, determination would surely make a vibrant color, won’t you agree?

To add to their natural colors, are the colors of the glasses we wear — perceptions.

And when I see people prefer calling such a colorful account as black, white or grey, they apparently live in the era of black-and-white television/film where every colour appears just as a shade of gray.

So let us spare a moment on this grey scale also. Greay has hundreds of shades (not just fifty). And the the two exreme end of this spectrum is black and white. So A grey closer to white is almost white and gray nearer to black appears black. So when you see someone white, they are closest to perfection and when you see black they are the grey closes to evil.

So neither is Ramayana black&white, nor is Mahabharata grey — they are full of colors of lives, each color giving live example that survived thousands of years.
