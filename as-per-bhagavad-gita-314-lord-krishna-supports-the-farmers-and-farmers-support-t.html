As per Bhagavad Gita 3.14 Lord Krishna supports the farmers, and farmers support the whole planet. Then how can greedy capitalists and opportunistic politicians defeat them? Isn't it obvious that farmers have God in support and no one can stop them?

📅 Sep 26, 2021 05:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Let us start by looking at Gita 3.14.

अन्नाद्भवन्ति भूतानि पर्जन्यादन्नसम्भव: |
यज्ञाद्भवति पर्जन्यो यज्ञ: कर्मसमुद्भव: || 14||

All living beings subsist on food, and food is produced by rains. Rains come from the performance of sacrifice, and sacrifice is produced by the performance of prescribed duties.

Now first, I fail to see even a single reference about farmers. Here Krishna talks about food, rain and sacrifice.

So where did farmer come from. It comes from the deceit and misinterpretation that makes people distort our epic. To understand the extent of distortions people are ready to carry out, let us check next two shlokas.

कर्म ब्रह्मोद्भवं विद्धि ब्रह्माक्षरसमुद्भवम् |
तस्मात्सर्वगतं ब्रह्म नित्यं यज्ञे प्रतिष्ठितम् || 15||

एवं प्रवर्तितं चक्रं नानुवर्तयतीह य: |
अघायुरिन्द्रियारामो मोघं पार्थ स जीवति || 16||

The duties for human beings are described in the Vedas, and the Vedas are manifested by God himself. Therefore, the all-pervading Lord is eternally present in acts of sacrifice.

O Parth, those who do not accept their responsibility in the cycle of sacrifice established by the Vedas are sinful. They live only for the delight of their senses; indeed their lives are in vain.

These shloka’s makes it abundantly clear, that Krishna not only never showed any biased favour towards the farmers, but strictly warns that those who don’t accept their responsibilities are sinful.

As I see, the relevant question is not, whom Krishna preferred, but whom did he label sinful. What is the prescribed duties of a farmer? To be involved in agriculture? Or give up your duty and disrupt the life of the nation? What is the true sin?

Krishna sought peace. He attempted for a peaceful resolution of the matter and offered unthinkable favors to wicked people in the name of peace. But those wicked people were adamant in conceding nothing. They wanted their own selfish interest prevail even if it can destroy the nation.

Krishna remained silent still hoping for peace. But then those greedy power hungry people misunderstood Krishna’s peace attempt as his weakness. And those fools dared to hold him on ransom — capture him. Ha Ha Ha!

Do you know what Krishna did? He ensured that wicked deceitful people are destroyed and rules of justice prevail. The commoners who were misled didn’t die. They finally prospered in the rule of justice, but few wicked beings who misled them were destroyed.

And yes, this really gives a great hope. People have seen Krishna’s peace effort, they may even see his viraat swaroop someday, though that may not be pleasing to those deceitful at heart.
