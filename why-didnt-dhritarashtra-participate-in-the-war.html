Why didn't Dhritarashtra participate in the war?

📅 Dec 28, 2020 04:36 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Dhritarashtra indeed participated in the war, he was just not physically present in the battle field. He was the ruler of Hastinapur during Kurukshetra battle. And Kings are not required to be present in the battlefield.

But I guess, this is not the answer, this question is seeking. Probably it is to know, why was he not physically fighting?

Probably the most obvious answer would be that he was blind and won't be able to fight with the greatest warriors of the period. But that leaves one question unanswered —

Why was Dhritrastra sitting far off Kurukshetra into the safety of his palace?

He may not have been able to fight with weapons, but then even Krishna didn't fight with weapon. He became Pandavas advisor. Bhishma continued to advise even from his bed of arrows. Dhritrastra could have been present with his army, boosting their morales, participating in strategies and taking responsibility for his decisions and failures. But then he didn't do any of this, because he was Dhritrastra.

Dhritarashtra is rightly referred to as the root of tree that is the evil Duryodhana and like any tree root, it nourished the tree while remained hidden. He was one of those shrewd people who worked from behind the scene never taking responsibility for his wrong doings. He supported Duryodhana in his Lakshagrih conspiracy, enjoyed as Pandavas lost their everything in dice game, that everything that they made out of waste land he had offered them as part of the partition. And why partition? Because he wanted to usurp the rightful claims of Yudhishthir.

In the war he remained confined. He wasn't sure where the war would take them. But once it was over he continued to stay put with same Pandavas who killed his sons who acted at his behest. And that was some victory.

And Finally

Dhritrashtra had actually participated in the war

May not be at Kurukshetra, but Dhritrashtra actually tried to kill Bhima when later came to pay his homage and seek his blessings. Dhritrashtra tried to crush Bhima. He once again was defeated by Krishna when Krishna offered a Bhima statue to Dhritrashtra in place and that was crushed exposing the last evil design of him, who was the root of the evil tree.

Footnote

Dhritrashtra being absent from the battle where his entire army and family perished for whatever reason — his blindness or shrewdness or selfishness — is yet another prove why he wasn’t worthy to be a king and why he wasn’t crowned as one.
