Why is Arjun not a hero of the Mahabharata?

📅 Nov 26, 2021 08:24 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Arjuna is mentioned, in the very first chapter of Mahabharata, as the trunk of tree of Dharma rooted in Krishna, and Brahma. If this is not the definition of hero, I don’t know what is.

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

But yes, Arjuna is not a hero for everyone. There are people who desist him.

Arjuna is not a hero to Mahabharata to those who have fallen prey to evil worship. He is even a villain to those who carry wishful thinking of crowning their evil idol as the hero of the epic. For these poor victims of gross misconception, even Krishna is a conniving villain whose sense of justice is portrayed as adharma because he destroyed evils who had burdened the earth so long. These people, who try to justify the act of molestation, conspiracy of murder, deceit to your own guru, gasp at the thought that those evil fall to their own design and lifetime of crime. For those people Arjuna is no hero.

Arjuna is not a hero of Mahabharata, who despise him because he represents everything they wanted to see in their idol but couldn’t find. Hell, he was everything, their idol strived to be all his life in vein. He was everything unlike their idol.

Arjuna couldn’t not be a hero to those who seek heroes in molesters and deceitful people and criminals. For them Arjuna has no shine.

Arjuna isn’t a hero of Mahabahrata in modern TV Shows who want to build new narrative around anti-heroes and would destroy epic for their whims without a moment’s thought.

But beyond them, those who still follow the epic by Vyasji and bother to read the very first chapter of Mahabharata would find it begins by saluation to Nara (Arjuna) and Narayana (Krishna).
