How did the name of Suyodhana became Duryodhana?

📅 Jun 23, 2022 08:03 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is one of the confusion of recent creation. People assumed “Su” means good and “Dur” means bad. Then they started to assume that the two words must be antonym of each other. This naturally led to the believe that since no one would name their son negative the original name of eldest Gandhari son must be Duryodhana which over a period of time became Duryodhana. NOT TRUE.

The simple answer is, eldest Gandhari son always had two both these names — Suyodhana and Duryodhana. Now the question arises who would give their son a name with negative vibe and why would someone have two names contradicting each other? Short answer, this confusion arises from the lack of understanding of Hindi. Actually the two names are not contradictory but complementary to each other.

First let us take the simpler of the two that has no confusion —

Suyodhana (सुयोधन) → The “सु” prefix often represents a positive vibe in Hindi language. examples could be सुयोग्य (with good quality), सुगंध (good smell), सुसंस्कारी (with good cultural). Similarly सुयोधन means one who is good at wars or a good warrior. This one is simple and understandable.

दुर... or दु...

Now the main source of confusion is fact that in Hindi, “दुर” prefix to a word often represents (bad). An example would be दुर्गंध (foul smell), दुरात्मा (bad character), दुर्भाग्य (bad fortune) or दुर्जन (bad people). But the story doesn’t end here…

The ‘दुर’ prefix doesn’t always represent “bad negative”. It has got another use also. It often represents “difficult”. Let us take a few examples —

दुर्गम → Difficult path NOT bad path

दुर्जय → Difficult to win over NOT bad victory

Also note, “दुर” or “दु” doesn’t always used as antonym of “सु”. The antonym of सुसंस्कारी is कुसंस्कारी and antonym of दुर्जन is सज्जन ।
