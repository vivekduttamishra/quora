Why did Lord Parshuram get angry on Karna for lying to him and learning warfare, when it was clear he won't teach him had he told the truth?

📅 Apr 14, 2020 08:00 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Why should narration always ignore the guru who was cheated and whose knowledge was stolen against his wish? What about Bhagwan Parshuram, the 6th Vishnu? Had he no right? Or wisdom?

There are several ways to understand Bhagwan Parshram's action —

  1. See it is like this. You have a rare invaluable product on which you have Copyright or patent. It's the outcome of a huge investment. And you have set certain price. Mr X can't afford the price so decided to steal. Is it unfare for you to get angry? And the product is not a life saving medicine that someone needs to save his dying mother. It's a fancy gun, which when misused will be traced back to you.
  2. IIT/IIM today have admission criteria and someone fails to secure admission, so he forges his way into institutions and gets caught. What will IIT/IIM do? Now map the idea to some institutions that manages nuclear weapons?
  3. A girl doesn't like Mr X (may be his caste or temperament) , and Mr X knows she would reject his proposal, so he abducts her under false pretext. How should girl react?
