Who is better in Karna and Arjuna if Kawach Kundal and Brahmastra are kept aside?

📅 Dec 17, 2021 05:47 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Redundant Question. We must compare apple with apple. Here we have no real comparison.

Let us cast away not only weapons and armors, but also victory and defeats. After all victories and defeat don’t make you better or worse. Right?

There are two pupil — One is so devoted to his guru that not only protects his life as a child but also tries to protect him knowing that Guru got his son killed mercilessly. Other cheated his guru to get divine weapons by hook and crook. Who is better?

There are two men — One tries who tries to burn down a bride for failing to win the swyamver or a man who tries to protect her? Who do you think is a better person?

There are two warriors — One who protected his worst enemy and his family as the other ditched his best friend to save his own life. Who is better?

There was a kingdom in distress with his general’s death. One attempts to plunder the kingdom, other rises to protect it against a powerful army. Who is better?

There are two fighters — One who severed the hand of enemy to protect his friend and vowed no one within his reach could harm his friend and family. Other let his own brothers die and fled to protect his miserable self. Who is better?

There are two epic character — One Krishna called his inseparable existence on multiple occasion, the other Krishna condemned to die. And Krishna had manifested to destroy evils and protect good — paritranaya sadhunam vinashaya cha dushkritam. Who do you think must have been better?

This is an endless story. But when it comes to be better, we need to be really blind or evil not to see the reasoning.
