If Arjun was stronger than karna then why did he needed the help of many devas to slay karna?

📅 Jun 27, 2020 07:29 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah

How many Deva's helped him slay Karna?

Did Krishna lend him Chakra or Hanuman striked him with Gada (mace)? Or did he use Pashupatastra given to him by Devadidev Mahadev?

But it is an important point to understand is —

Deva's did support Arjuna

  • All Devas supported Arjuna. There must be a reason.
  • None of the Devas fought for him — Hanuman, Shiva — blessed Arjuna but didn’t fight his battle.
  • Krishna supported him as a charioteer. He indeed saved him on occasion, but that is what charioteers do.

Why did Deva supported Arjuna and not Karna?

  • Deva’s don’t support Adharma.
  • There support to Arjuna was an endorsement that Arjuna was on the right side.
  • They didn’t fight his war as they knew Arjuna doesn’t need their help but blessing.
  • There blessing ensured Arjuna’s victory. And don’t take this point lightly. Arjuna had earned their blessing and it was more powerful than any potent weapons. You must understand celestial weapons too are blessings of the God.
  • Why no God supported Karna — not even his father Surya. Why was he not on Karna’s chariot? Why didn’t he protect his son? Because he is a Deva and Deva’s don’t support Adharma.

It is unfortunate that people mis construct story distort its true meaning. They think they can even manipulate Dharma and Deva’s. They want to prove that Arjuna was nothing without Deva’s blessing, and completely forget, in the process that, no one is anything without Deva’s blessings. And you need to follow Dharma to be blessed.

If people in their fictious world may want to believe Karna to be more powerful, keep believing, but remember —

Dharma supports him, who supports Dharma

So we must be careful. Are we on the Dharma side? Would Dharma protect me? Even Surya couldn’t protect his son when he sided with Adharma.

It pains to see people in their blind hero worshipping are ready to go against the Devas, the culture and the very epic, of whom their hero is a mere part.
