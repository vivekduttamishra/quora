Was Karna a Dalit?

📅 Mar 12, 2021 08:02 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: NO.

The most straightforward reason — there was no Dalit in Mahabharata era.

The Hindi word Dalit means down-trodden and oppressed (दबा कुचला हुआ). It doesn’t represent any caste or varna. Karna was a born Kshatriya, raised by an influential and prosperous suta, who was a close friend of Dhirashtra. He attained his education from the best gurus of Aryavart and was crowned as the King of Anga at a very young age. He remained a king till the end of his days. If that is the definition of Dalit, I would love to be one!

In fact, the concept of ‘Dalit’ was alien in that great civilization where the son of a fisherwoman was accorded the status of a Maharishi and son of a maid servant could become the Prime Minister of the most powerful kingdom of Aryavart.

But if you are confusing Dalit to be Sudra or lower caste.

And yes, you are confusing it. Sudra was no caste. It was a varna defining people doing manual labours and lower strata of work such a physical labour.

Karna was not a Sudra. He was a suta. And Suta, was a mixed race of Kshatirya and Brahmina. Suta’s of the era held prominent positions —

  • Keechak, a suta was the Army Chief of Matasya.
  • Sanjay, another Suta, was given diving vision to provide commentary of war to Dhritrashtra. He was also like an ambassador of Hastinapur.

Karna’s foster father, Adhiratha, was a suta. He was a charioteer. It is important to remind, to be a charioteer wasn’t a lower strata job during that era. It wasn’t like a taxi driver, but the operator of an Army Tank. A charioteer needed to have clear understanding of battle formations and how to maneuver them. Today these positions are held by officers of Captain and Major ranks. It would be apparent if you realize —

  • Krishna became a charioteer to Arjuna.
  • Even Shalya a proud King, accepted the role of a charioteer to Karna
  • Legend says, Brahama took the role of a charioteer for Shiva in his battle against Tripur.
  • Sanjay, a charioteer, was an ambassador for Hastinapur on occasions.

So by no means either Karna or Suta were dalit or downtrodden, nor their occupations accorded them the Sudra varna.

ASIDE

One of the answers to this question mentions, Karna was a Suta-Putra and that is a rank inferior to Suta. This is one of the most ludicrous statement I have heard in some time —

  • Karna is not just referred as Sutaputra, he is often referred as Suta.
  • It is like saying Dashratha-Putra Rama was inferior to Dashratha!
