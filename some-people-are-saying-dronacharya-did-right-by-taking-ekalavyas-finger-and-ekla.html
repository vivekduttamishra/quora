Some people are saying Dronacharya did right by taking Ekalavya's finger, and Eklavya is adharmi because he had stolen knowledge. How to refute this misconception?

📅 Mar 16, 2021 12:37 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I regret I won’t be refuting this, as I don’t see it a misconception.

Let us go through the incidence once more.

PHASE 1 Eklavya Approaches Dronacharya

Eklavya approaches Dronacharya intending to learn the science of weapons. Dronacharya refused him. Why? We don’t know. But he certainly didn’t refuse knowledge to other non-royalties like Karna. So why did he refuse him? We must understand, weapons are serious stuffs whose knowledge can’t be granted to everyone.

Not even modern society does that anywhere in the world — there is no reservation, no caste prejudice. Everyone goes through stringent psychological evaluation to ensure that means of potent destruction should be entrusted to right people only. Otherwise how do you differentiate between an NDA (National Defence Academy) Cadet and a nuxal, who take the arms almost at the same age? You don’t only teach to aim and shoot, but also to restrain and when not to shoot. There are sufficient example to prove, what Drona taught was not a lesson in archary, but in the war protocols as well.

PHASE 2 Dronacharya demands his thumb.

Drona was horrified to find, not only ignoring is explicit denial, not only Eklavya continued to practice archery, he is also using his name for his guru, something he explicitly forbade.

Did Eklavya learnt from Drona? We can’t be sure. But circumstantial evidence certainly points in that direction. Why else would he deem Drona as his guru? Why was he staying close by to Drona’s gurukul?

Still, and even assuming that he didn’t learn from him, he was still misusing his name for his own guru. That made Drona responsible for his conducts. He would be known as Drona’s disciple.

Drona asked for his thumb. And while it may sound, too harsh a punishment, losing a limb was lesser harsh than putting in prison, or curshing him for the whole life. And you can’t let such misdeeds go unpunished.

There are two questions here —

Q1 — Was Drona entitled for gurudakshina, when he refused to teach Eklavaya?Ans — Eklavya himself gave him the privilege by using his name as his guru. What Drona asked can be seen as a compensation for using his name.

Q2 — Was the price asked justified or too harsh?

ANS — Apart from Drona, only time could have answered this question. And time did answer it. Drona tried to deter Eklavya from becoming a warrior. But looks he failed. Looks like the punishment milder and Eklavya refused to take the lesson. Why am I saying so?

PHASE 3 — What did fate take Eklavya?

ANS — Eklavya was killed by Krishna. It happened sometimes between Yudhishthir’s Rajsuy Yagna ( where he was present) and Kurukshetra war. Now why did Krishna kill him? He says he incarnated to establish Dharama! What could have been the situation, that he met the fate of Jarasandha, Shishupapla, Kansa and Narkasura? What was the common cord?

If you observe the entire episode closely and with cross-reference, it would be easy to understand —

  • Knowledge of weapon is not for everyone. It is not a necessary education, that is covered under the idea like Right to Education, in any society — modern or ancient.
  • Dronacharya had a valid reason to deny education to Eklavya.
  • Eklavya proved him right by his actions that led Krishna to slay him.
  • Dronacharya’s punishment, though apprently harsh, was still protecting his life.
