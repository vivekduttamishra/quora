Why did Gandhiji choose pro-Congress Nehru over pro-BJP Patel for the PM post?

📅 May 30, 2022 02:33 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

One of the most flawed question, I ever saw.

Sardar Ballav Bhai Patel wasn’t pro-BJP, BJP is promoting itself as pro Patel.

BJP didn’t exist then, and then congress has ceased to exist now. Current congress is a renamed entity that was originally Congress(I), one formed by Indira Gandhi and not the one that existed during independence and fought for our independence. Perhaps that is why they value so little for our freedom.

Mahatma Gandhi chose Pandit Nehru, a dynamic leader aged fifty-eight over another dynamic veteran leader, Sardar Patel, aged seventy-three, suffering from TB, and not in the best of his health to lead the nation. Moreover Nehruji had a international repute and already presumed to be the Prime Minister of India and was in best position to advocate for India’s need on international platform. Interestingly Sardar Patel understood the ideas when he withdrew his nomination, on the suggestion of Mahatma Gandhi and the two leaders (Nehru and Patel) had a great synergy as the two worked together towards the early crucial years of India’s independence. The role of both the leaders are, beyond doubt, beyond measure.

When a building is created, what people see is the upper façade, the splendid dome, the bright and shining color and exterior. But an architect would tell you the greatest effort goes in something that remain invisible but bears burden of the entire gloss that you see and is something far more greater, the foundation of the building. Unfortunately modern generation is busy seeing the height and the gloss and buying the story that nothing happened in last 70 years and fail to realize that foundation of a strong nation was laid at that point of time. Just ask yourself a simple question — Where would India have been without IIT, IIM, AIIMS, Dams, and nuclear projects. And then ask another question, when was the foundation of this institutions laid. Then ask a third question where India would have been today had these works not done in the early years of Independent India.

Today we may sell anything, say anything as absurd as calling Patel and pro BJP, but these leaders, Patel, Nehru and Mahatma Gandhi, they were neither pro today’s congress, nor today’s BJP. They always were pro India. And that is a lesson for today’s BJP, congress or any party — Be Pro India.

My answer is based on the facts.
