Bhishma had finally explained why he was belittling Karna though he knew Karna to be a great warrior. However, why did Drona always sided with Bhishma?

📅 Jun 01, 2022 07:46 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The question can be better answered by swapping the two parts of the question.

1. Why Drona always sided with Bhishma in belittling Karna?

The answer to this question is simple and straight forward. Drona was a great teacher and not equally great diplomat. He didn’t really change his version about Karna ever. And every time he spoke about Karna, he talked with sufficient data points as evidence. He knew Karna couldn’t compete with Arjuna. He said that during Matasya battle. His assessment proved accurate. He sided with Bhishma, but not blindly. He gave his own reasons. He said he had seen Karna running from battles after battle. Was that incorrect? Even Kripacharya and Ashwatthama challenged Karna asking if he had ever defeated any Pandava, to which Karna had no reply.

Drona also blames him, and not without a reason, that he was responsible for humiliation of Draupadi.

In all his conduct, few things are clear —

  • Drona always backed his statements with verifiable incidents which have sufficient cross references.
  • Drona’s statement was also echoed in the words of Kripacharya and Aswhatthama.
  • Bhishma’s earlier statements co-incided with Drona’s assessment of Karna.
  • Drona didn’t change his views about Karna.

2. Did Bhishma really think Karna to be a great warrior.

In earlier cases, Bhishma made following statement about Karna.

  • “He always speaks about killing the Pandavas. But he is not a sixteenth part59 of the great-souled Pandavas.
  • What extremely difficult task has he61 accomplished in the past that can rival any one deed accomplished by any one of the Pandavas earlier?
  • In Virata’s city, he saw his own beloved brother killed through Dhananjaya’s valour.62 What did he do then?
  • In the expedition over the cattle, your son was captured by the gandharvas. 63 The son of a suta is behaving like a bull now. Where was he then?
  • Be a man. Fight with Partha in battle, whom you seek to challenge. O extremely evil-minded one! I will watch you escape from that fight.’”

As we see in most cases Bhishma’s statement were based on verifiable incidents. And Bhishma’s words were in concurrence with Drona, Kripa, Ashwatthama, Parshurama, Shri Krishna and even Karna.

No warrior of any experience of repute would call such a warrior would seriously consider him Arjuna’s contender. If Bhishma really considered him great, he must have been stupid. But I seriously doubt.

3. What is the relevance of final statement of Bhishma

I would say that was Bhishma’s diplomatic maneuver as a brilliant strategist that he was. He knew about Karna’s arrogant and boastful nature and his knew Karna may enter a war or abandon a war for his false pride. Wasn’t that the reason why Karna abstained for the war for the first ten days?

Once Bhishma realized that he could no longer protect Kauravas he saw Karna as the best bait to stop the war. He didn’t really wanted him to fight the war. He wanted him to bring Duryodhana to his senses and stop the war. But he knew Karna wouldn’t listen to him unless, he plays with Karna’s ego in the right way. He praises him. He apologies him for belittling him. He praises his valor. But all these was with one crucial goal —

O destroyer of enemies! The brave Pandavas are your brothers. O mighty-armed one! If you wish to please me, go to them. O son of the sun! With my end, let all the enmity be over. Let all the kings on earth be free from all danger.’

— Bhisma Vadha Parva

Now with this goal in mind, he tried to appease Karna. He even called him invincible, which we all know is far from the truth. He naturally failed.
