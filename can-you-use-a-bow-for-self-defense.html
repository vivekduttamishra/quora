Can you use a bow for self-defense?

📅 Mar 05, 2021 10:31 PM PST  |  🌐 English

That is an interesting question and a welcome change from useless repetitive controversies of comparing heros and villains and attempt to make heros out villains.

Can we use bow for self defense? I would say only when you follow the mantra — attack is the best defense and use the strategy of cover fire — keep attacking so that enemies can’t.

But yes, bows and arrows certainly can’t be used the way they depict in our TV Shows. It is stupid and childish to think —

  • Someone fire and arrow from few hundred meters. The other waits, thinks and then draws an arrow to take it head-on. And other arrow waits suspended midair to be countered!
  • Two opponents fire at such a precession that the arrows hit each other tip. Nothing else!

That was one of the important question in my mind when I started to write down my own epic re-telling — The Accursed God. Many suggested I should continue to depict the bow-arrow fight as the popular depiction from the TVShows. But both my protaganist warrior and I wonder — How do you duel with a bow? When you both hands are engaged in controlling it, how would you defend?

TVShows, in my understanding, has done more dis-service to the epic that the value add. Their depiction of battle has reduced Divastras to some sort of nuclear weapon — when in reality they were lot different from traditional sense of weapons. It wouldn’t be wrong to say, they were weapons of salvation, rather than devastation.
