Was Karn the greatest sacrificing warrior in the Mahabharata?

📅 Oct 30, 2020 12:15 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Definition of sacrifice — give up (something valued) for the sake of other considerations.

Let's look at few characters of Mahabharata who, too, made sacrifice —

  1. There was a young prince, well learned, highly qualified and destined to be the ruler of Aryavart. He sacrificed his kingship, his family prospects and became a servant to the throne. He sacrificed for his father's happiness and made no exception to his oath. Does his sacrifice qualify as great?
  2. There was another great young King. He sacrificed his throne and accepted the exile for having killed a sage by mistake. He could have escaped the punishment. But he chooses ethics and social values over his personal happiness. Does his sacrifice count?
  3. There was a father of a newly born beautiful girl. He sacrificed his baby for the sake of his friend and future hope of many people against a tyrant ruler. He never regretted but gave his best in the upbringing of the greatest man of the era, nay an incarnation. Few remember his sacrifice.
  4. There was a King in exile whose brothers were killed by a divine power. He pleaded to return the life of his half brothers over his own, who were the greatest warrior of the time and loosing them was loosing any hope to become the King. Was that a sacrifice?
  5. A family suffered the trechary of their kins. They suffered death conspiracy and exile. And yet to avoid mass devastation, they agreed for a bargain far lesser than what was due to them. Is this sacrifice?

Now let us turn to that great warrior whose name is mentioned as the greatest one here. Let me ask what did he really sacrificed?

  • He was a great donor. What did he donated? Gold? Richness? But they weren’t his. He had got a Kingdom in donation. He donated from there. Didn’t he? Let’s accept it was a donation. But was it a sacrifice?
  • Did he sacrificed his friend for the welfare of the society, when Krishna and Kunti asked him to make distance from Duryodhana’s evil project that was to push entire Aryavart to war. You may call it he did is as a loyalty to his friend.
  • Did he at least sacrifice his personal egos for his friend? Alas, No He who refused to save humanity for his friend loyalty, again refused to fight the same war for the same friend. Do you know why? It was for his personal ego and glory.

I truly fail to see, where is the sacrifices made by this great person and how is it greater than those I mentioned. Feel free to enlighten me.
