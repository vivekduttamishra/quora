While reviving Parikshita, Krishna says he has never told a lie, but in childhood he has told lies. How is it possible?

📅 May 08, 2021 10:49 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I would like to answer this question in two parts —

1.Krishna never said that he never lied.

That is something you see in star plus Mahabharata, not in the authentic epic. In the authentic epic, Krishna didn’t need to invoke his truthfulness to grant Parikshita his life. Here is what Krishna said as per BORI —

‘Vasudeva replied, “This supreme weapon is invincible and will indeed descend. The foetus will be born dead. However, it will revive and live till a long age.

—BORI, Astika Parva

He further adds to it —

The brave one will obtain knowledge of all the weapons from Kripa Sharadvata. He will know about all the supreme weapons and base himself on the dharma of kshatriyas. He will have dharma in his soul and protect the earth for sixty years. On top of this, the mighty-armed one will be the king of the Kurus. O extremely evil-minded one! That king will be known by the name of Parikshit. O worst of men! You will look on.

—BORI, Astika Parva

KRISHNA AND HIS TRYST WITH TRUTH AND LIES

Krishna is a divinity, a great teacher, philosopher and psychologist, but he is not a person to dwell on irrelevant facts that many prefer to call truth. He is not egoistic about his knowledge like Yudhishthira or rigid on his vows like Bhishma or Karna.

He is devoid of ego and as such his truth is Dharma and adherence to it. And some may be surprised to realize that truth is multi-dimensional.

There was a time people thought world is flat. That was their truth. And if anyone would tell them world is round, people would call that person a liar, as it contradicts the truth that lesser people see it.

Krishna didn’t bother about those truths that cloud the judgement of lesser mortals like Yudhishthira —

He promised he wouldn’t take up weapons and yet he stood to attack Bhishma.

He told Yudhishthira — Shalya was stronger than Karna and Bhishma and even Arjuna and only Yudhishthira can slay him — Don’t search for facts here. It was a truth Krishna wanted Yudhishthira to believe.
