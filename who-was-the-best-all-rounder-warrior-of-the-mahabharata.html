Who was the best all-rounder warrior of the Mahabharata?

📅 Mar 04, 2020 08:19 PM PST  |  🌐 English

I would rather start by reiterating my own answer from this post

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Who is best warrior from Hindu epics?

There is a distinctions between a warrior and a warmonger and that's what Krishna hails Ram as the greatest among warrior — he knew when not to be a warriors!

Now let's apply it to Mahabharat —

  1. Bhisma: He was the warrior of the highest order. He defeated even Bhagwan Parshuram and he was humble in his victory. He wreck havoc on Pandavas for first 10 days and yet honored his own war ethics and bow down to Shikandi at great personal pain. Rarely you would find a person so powerful and so humble at the same time — he was the greatest warrior of his time. (Disclaimer: I may sound biased as he is in a pivotal role in my epic fiction — The Accursed God. You can check about it on The Lost Epic Book 1, but with or without biase he is the best)
  2. Arjun : Who was the favorite of Krishna, Bhisma and Drona? Whom Shiva bestowed his boon? Who was the only warrior in whole Mahabharat to question the relevance of war? These qualities alone put him to the top of the list. He was always focused and dedicated. Despite not being from the favorite section of the royal family he made his way to the top. As a warrior he vanquished all warriors in Virat battle. Talking about his humility he carried on as charioteer to young Virat Prince without ego. The day he killed Jaidradth, he didn't won Drona by weapon but by his humble gesture. Above all he never fought for personal glory but as a disciplined soldier.
  3. Parshuram Though putting the 6th Vishnu in a single epic would be an Injustice to both him and the epic. Need I say more 3 most fearsome warriors in the battle of Kurukshetra were his creations? Again being an epic author on the subject may deem my views clouded (I stand firm on the choice)

And above all

Krishna: The almighty, the invincible, he who could have finished the war single handedly in a single moment chose to sit out the greatest war of all-time. Could Achilles (battle of Troy) have done this? But to ensure fairness he became a part of the war without warring. Could there be a greater warrior?

Honorable Mentions

  1. Bhima
  2. Drona
