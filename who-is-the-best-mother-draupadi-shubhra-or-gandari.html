Who is the best mother, Draupadi, Shubhra, or Gandari?

📅 Apr 24, 2022 09:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

What defines a good mother? A mother who blindfolds herself and let her children go astray or a mother whose sons die upholding Dharma?

One one hand we have a mother whose sons were the evil beasts who would not hesitate for a moment in poisoning a kid or burning a family alive or stripping a woman in public. What kind of parenting do you think, such evil creature got? And should I add, the mother knew about her son’s evil nature. She knew her son’s greed for a throne, which even she knew didn’t belong to him. And yet she never took a concrete step to stop her sons' crimes. And finally she blames Krishna for what was squarely her fault. Can you call her a good mother, forget being a candidate for ‘best’? Gandhari was a failure for being a mother.

There isn’t much said about Draupadi and Shubhadra as a mother. But if a child is a credible testimony to his mother, both of them must have been great mother for they raised children who fought for Dharma. There isn’t a single line about the sons of Draupadi and Shubhadra that says anything ill against them. The fought with greatness and died shaming their killers, those wretched creaturs. But that would be another story.

I am not sure, if it is by omission or intention, the question misses out one, who can be truly considered as the greatest mother of epic — Kunti.

The life of Kunti was one of unending struggle started when Surya imposed himself on her to save his own honor. Kunti was forced to bear a child simply because in her childish whim she used a mantra without understanding the consequences. Some call her a criminal for letting her first born go. What they don’t realize is the fact that was the best and hardest decision and unwed mother could have taken and it wasn’t just to save her own dignity but also that of his family and his son. Yes, pause here and take a moment to consider — What if Kunti hadn’t let her son go? Do you think any respectable King would have married her? Is there a known precedence? You may say, it was Kunti’s selfish motive. But as I said, take a moment and think, what would be the status of Karna in the society? An illegitimate son of an unwed mother! What honor would he be expecting? But if you still think, Kunti was selfish, try answering another question — Why didn’t Kunti simply drowned him in the river rather than dispatching him to the safety of anonymity? What? Do you think, she wanted her dead? Think Again. Why seek divine protection for a child whom you would want to be drowned? Why dispatch him with such huge wealth when you know he would be drowned. No. either you take reference or common sense, a great mother willed his son to live.

Now let us talk about Parthas. Two of the five Parthas weren’t even the biological son of Pritha and yet what a marvelous sons did she get? She didn’t teach her to be complaining and cribbing. She didn’t teach them to conspire. She taught them to be brave. She taught them Dharma. She taught them to be readily to sacrifice themselves to help the needy. She made them so worthy that even Gods bestow they grace on them. Her sons pleased Dharma, Hanuman, Krishna and even God of Gods, Mahadeva Shiva. And need I remind, if Partha’s grew up to be great, it was because of that single mother, for they had lost their father at a very young age. Kunti raised the greatest heroes of the era they were the testimony of her upbringing. It is surprising to see her name missing for this list.
