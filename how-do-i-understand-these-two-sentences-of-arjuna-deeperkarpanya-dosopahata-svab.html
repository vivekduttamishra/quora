How do I understand these two sentences of Arjuna Deeper?"karpanya-dosopahata-svabhavah" and "dharma-sammudha-cetah"

📅 Aug 29, 2021 10:44 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

My first recommendation — don’t try to read phrases in isolation. You won’t understand the true meaning without understanding the context. The two phrases comes from Bhagwat Gita, Chapter 2, Shloka 7. And Arjuna is feeling the weakness. Why? To understand the context you must go back to previous shloka.

Arjuna is reluctant to fight his own kins. He is ready to spend the rest his life begging rather than killing them. He feels destruction and devastation would cause the family and society to collapse. But Krishna doesn’t agree with him. He says as a warrior it your duty to fight.

Now Arjuna is conflicted between what is his Dharma? To kill? Or get killed? To follow Krishna or to spare his beloved guru and grandsire? And that is what he repeatedly asks.

Here is what he said in the preceeding Shloka —

gurun ahatva hi mahanubhavan
sreyo bhoktum bhaiksyam apiha loke
hatvartha-kamams tu gurun ihaiva
bhunjiya bhogan rudhira-pradigdhan

na caitad vidmah kataran no gariyo
yad va jayema yadi va no jayeyuh
yan eva hatva na jijivisamas
te 'vasthitah pramukhe dhartarastrah

—Gita 2.5,6

It is better to live in this world by begging than to live at the cost of the lives of great souls who are my teachers. Even though they are avaricious, they are nonetheless superiors. If they are killed, our spoils will be tainted with blood.

I am not sure what is better — conquer them or get conquered by them. If we kill the sons of Dhritrashtra, who are now standing before us, we shouldn’t care to live.

Now let us understand the continuation in the current shloka —

karpanya-dosopahata-svabhavah
prcchami tvam dharma-sammudha-cetah
yac chreyah syan niscitam bruhi tan me
sisyas te 'ham sadhi mam tvam prapannam

—Gita 2.7

Karpanya stands for miserly. Now the problem with Arjuna is, he is getting weak because of his selfish interest of saving (miserly) his favorite guru and grandsire and because of this weakness he is running away from Dharma. That is what is referred in second phase — His duty (Dharma) is becoming weak (Sammudha) in his conciousness (chetah). And here he seeks Krishna’s support to guide him.
