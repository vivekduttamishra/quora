In the Mahabharata War, what would have happened if Krishna was replaced by Ram?

📅 Mar 21, 2020 12:25 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A.

I can say with certainty there would have been no difference — Ram and Krishna are one and the same. There action differed based on the yuga they belonged, their belief remained same — Dharma.

  • Was Ram easy to manipulate?
    • No persuasion by entire Awadh including his favorite Bharat could change his decision and give up his 14 years of exile. How can Bhisma or anyone changed his resolve when his father and brothers couldn't?
    • He defeated Asuras known for mayayudh.
  • Both Rama and Krishna knew their divinity and expressed them when needed
    • Ram presented himself to Parshuram
    • Krishna presented himself at multiple occasions.
  • Ram was firm on being the side of the dharma
    • He chose Sugriv over Bali knowing Bali could have been better ally against Ravan.
    • How can we assume Duryodhan would have won him over?
  • Both Ram and Krishna knew the virtue of constraints.
    • Ram prayed to ocean instead of showing is powers. he took up the arms against arrogance of ocean to mellow him down and not destroy him.
    • Krishna sat through Mahabharat war instead of taking up arms. He too took up arms and then cooled down after mellowing down Bhisma.

Remember what Ram said to Bali why he killed him —

अनुज बधू भगिनी सुत नारी सुनु सठ कन्या सम ए चारी। इन्हिह कुदृष्टि बिलोकइ जोई ताहि बधें कछु पाप न होई।।

brothers wife, sister, and daughter in law should be treated as daughter and he who abuses them should be killed and there is no sin in killing them.

Imagine whom Ram would side?

Both Ram and Krishna showed the path of Dharma and were beyond manipulation. Remember what Krishna said —

naham vedair na tapasa
na danena na cejyaya
sakya evam-vidho drastum
drstavan asi mam yatha

The form which you are seeing with your transcendental eyes cannot be understood simply by studying the Vedas, nor by undergoing serious penances, nor by charity, nor by worship. It is not by these means that one can see Me as I am.

bhaktya tv ananyaya sakya
aham evam-vidho 'rjuna
jnatum drastum ca tattvena
pravestum ca parantapa

My dear Arjuna, only by undivided devotional service can I be understood as I am, standing before you, and can thus be seen directly. Only in this way can you enter into the mysteries of My understanding.

There was no way any thing other utter devotion could have won Ram or Krishna, something Kaurava's were incapable of. Only he who can give up glory and arms (ego) can attain them because Ram and Krishna are same.
