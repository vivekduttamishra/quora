Who would win in a battle between Shri Ram and Arjun?

📅 Dec 12, 2020 04:14 AM PST  |  🌐 English

I don’t know who imagines such a weird question? Isn’t the answer obvious?

But first ask yourself — Why would two good people fight? Do you think Rama would side with Kaurava? Or that Arjuna would fight for Ravana?

The only reason they would fight is if — Prabhu Shri Rama (a.k.a. Krishna) decides to test the devotion of his disciple the same way Mahadeva Shiva put him to test. If Shri Rama wanted to test the calibre and perseverance of Arjuna before blessing him, there might be a battle.

And in any such battle, Shri Rama, without exerting himself a bit, would vanquish Arjuna. In a moment. Every time. If he wishes he may even kill him. Arjuna would be sure to be defeated and thrashed. But Arjuna would never flee the battle despite of known his opponent.

And just like what happened after Shiva-Arjuna fight. Arjuna would finally win! No. Arjuna would not win the battle, he would win the Lords heart and his blessings. And Rama would hand over his own great bow with his blessing to a devotee, in whom he would find a true warrior.

And I can say this with conviction, not with supposition, that Arjuna would win Rama’s blessing. He did win Rama’s blessing and heart, when prabhu incarnated as Krishna. And didn’t merciful Rama blessed his other desciple Hanuman? Why won’t he bless Arjuna who was blessed by his favorite Hanuman.
