The very premise of the question is flawed and exposes the ignorance of questioner.

India didn’t come in existence in 1947, as this question claims, but has been eternal — as ancient as the human existence itself, and naturally all history of the subcontinent comes from that ancient civilization. What happened in 1947 was tearing of that great nation in parts and those parts got their new name, but not India.

That rich heritage

Long before the birth of most of the contemporary relegions of the world, when only one ancient santan culture existed in this part of the world, no Pakistan or Bangaladesh existed.

When Chandragupta Maurya uprooted the last remains of Alexandar’s brutality, there was just one unified nation — born out of Indus civilization — from where we got the name India.

What invaders like Changez Khan, Taimur Lang, Mohammad Gori or Babur invaded was India. The land where Akbar established his empire was also India.

When Maharana Pratap showed how a patriot can stand firm against an empire, it was still the saga or Indian heritiage.

When Rani Laksmi Bai or Mangal Pandey fought the great war against Britisher, that was an Indian heritage.

Ram Prasad Bismil and Asfaq Ullah Khan kissed the gallows, for the freedom of India and not for Pakistan. Chandra Shekhar Azad tried to avenge the death of Lala Lajpat rai in Lahore (Now in Pakistan), but that Lahore was also India. Even Jinnah lived majority of his life in India.

And all these freedom fighters, they didn’t fight for Delhi or Mumbai, but the entire extent of the land that was still India before 1947. And that is the reason Indian Army rushed to protect brutality against those people where are now called Bangladesh, but were then known as East Pakistani but were actually of Indian origin, many born in India.
