Thanks for the A2A.

There are few questions here which are so absurd that every part of it is incorrect. This is one of those.

There are two parts —

  1. Shakuni destroyed Duryodhana.
  2. Shakuni knew truth and Dharma.

Both are wrong and contradicts each other. A deceit doesn't know Dharma and Shakuni was a deceitful menion of Dhritarashtra and Duryodhana.

And Shakuni never destroyed Duryodhana — in fact all he ever did was to help him. He was no master strategist and even tried to dissuade him from taking on the war — someone with intention to destroy Duryodhana would never do.
