Who wrote Ramayan? Where?

📅 Sep 16, 2021 09:16 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

SHORT ANSWER: DESTINY

The reason I mentioned destiny to be author of the epic, is to underline the fact that you can be the author of a particular narrative, you can’t be an author of some incident that occurred all those thousands of years ago. The life of Rama was redirected in the direction where it went by several subtle pushes. What made Rama hero in his accord was how he reacted to those challenges when they were thrown upon him. That way he can be considered as the co-author.

Since it is not a work of fiction, we needed a composer like a journalist to chronicle the life’s incident. That said, when someone chronicles even a true incident, the words belong to them, the creativity belongs to them and they anyone include certain degree of imagination to make their narrative more creative and more engaging and not to mention more complete, for there is no way anyone could have known or remembered all the great moments and Shri Rama really never sat down with an autobiographer to suggest his point of view.

This exact question vexed actually the first composer of this great epic — Maharishi Valmiki — the first biographer that ever existed. He wondered what right he has to create a narrative around someone else’s life? Legend says Brahma himself encouraged him to do so. He mentions this incident at the beginning of his chronicle. In the traditional sense, the original author of the first narrative of Rama’s life was Maharishi Valmiki.

His chronicle was narrated across continent for tens of thousands of years. And much later when those recitations were penned down, obviously credit was given where it was due.

Beyond Valmiki, there exist multiple narratives, which though started at different point in time, continued to be inspired by the same original incident — the lives and adventures of Shri Rama, his wife Sita, his brother Lakshmana and his greatest follower Hanumana. These other narratives are credited to saints like Rishi Kamban, Tulsidas, Rishi Ranganathana and even Rishi Ved Vyasa.

But Ramayana narratives aren’t confined to Indian subcontinent or limited to being an Hindu sanatana epic. There is a Jain version of Ramayana, there is versions of Ramayana in Indonesia, Malasya and so on.

Ramayana continues to fire the imagination of multitude of authors, not excluding me, even till date and I dare say would continue to do so for all the times to come making it truly timeless. In that sense there would me million more authors of the Ramayana.

If you dare to take on the task of reciting Ramayana to some one and carry it to the next generation, I would say you are a author of Ramayana. I, certainly am going to be an author of Ramayana. Are you going to be one?
