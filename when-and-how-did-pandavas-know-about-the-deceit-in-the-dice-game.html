When and how did Pandavas know about the deceit in the dice game?

📅 Dec 25, 2020 02:18 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Pandavas knew about the deceit of the dice game from the beginning. It so happened that despite of knowing it as a protocol for the king, oath bound Yudhishthir couldn’t refuse the play.

‘Yudhishthira said, “It seems that some of the most feared rogues122 have assembled there. They are sure to play with the powers of maya. However, everything is under the control of the creator. I will not refuse to play with those rogues. O Vidura! I do not wish to go and gamble on King Dhritarashtra’s command. A father always has a son’s welfare in mind. Therefore, tell me what I should do.123 I have no desire to gamble with Shakuni. But if the confident124 one challenges me in the sabha, I will never refuse, because that has been my eternal vow.”’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3045-3046).

As we can see, Yudhisthir knew about the maya of Shakuni, which was a common knowledge. But Yudhishthir decided to play on because —

  1. Having taken a vow as the king, he couldn’t refuse the invitation.
  2. He trusted everything is control of almighty god.

Before starting the game, Yudhishthir warns Shakuni against deceit.

‘Yudhishthira replied, “O king! Dishonest gambling is evil. There is no kshatriya valour in that. Nor is there any good policy in it. Why do you then praise playing with the dice?

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3070-3071).

Yudhishthir also realizes that Shakuni is cheating during the game.

‘Yudhishthira said, “O Shakuni! You have won this gamble from me by using deceit. Let us now grasp the dice and play a thousand times.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3097-3098).

But since Yudhisthir continued to play on, he doesn’t blame Shakuni further. That is pointed by Bhishma later to Draupadi to which Draupadi gives a befitting reply. But that was after Yudhishthir had already pointed out deceit of Shakuni.

Bhishma said —

Shakuni has no equal in dice. Kunti’s son has voluntarily played with him. The great-souled one does not think he207 has resorted to deceit. Therefore, I cannot answer the question.”

Droupadi replied, “Though he is himself unskilled, the king was challenged in this sabha by those who are skilled, evil-minded and deceitful, those who love the game of dice. How can it be said he chose voluntarily? The pure-hearted and foremost one among the Kurus and the Pandavas was robbed of his senses by inclinations towards deceit. He has understood everything only after he has been won and after the gambling is over.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3333-3336).

It must be noted, that Bhishma’s suggestion that Yudhishthir doesn’t think Shakuni has cheated is incorrect, as Yudhishthir had already blamed Shakuni of cheating earlier.

Draupadi wasn’t present in the court when Yudhishthir claimed about Shakuni’s cheating. Therefore she didn’t contradict Bhishma rather she pointed out pure-hearted Yudhishtir didn’t realize cheating till it was late. However, this statement of Draupadi was based on Bhishma’s statement doesn’t reflect the fact that Yudhishthir had already blamed Shakuni of wrong-doing.

Apart from Yudhishthir everyone else knew about Shakuni’s deceit and Volume 2 itself includes over fifty mention of the word deceit in this context.
