Had Karna not asked Drona for Brahmastra to become better than Arjuna, would he have got it in the normal course?

📅 May 31, 2022 10:50 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The question expects us to go the realm of imagination as the answer can’t be obtained from any authentic retelling. So before we take the flight of creative imagination, let us prepare the runway on the solid ground which can be verified by authentic epic. And here are few relevant point.

  • Drona accepted Karna as his disciple. He was given the same opportunity as other princes. There is no reason why Karna couldn’t have risen to his highest potential.
  • Drona wasn’t favorably partial towards Arjuna from the beginning. He had no reason to. In fact there are incidents where Drona actually tried to reduce the effectiveness of Arjuna trying to ensure he doesn’t become superior warrior who can fight in darkness. Other incident from Neelkantha included where he intentionally delayed all students to give extra lessons to Ashwatthama. This again proves that Arjuna made his way to guru’s grace by hard work and not trying to find a shortcut.
  • Karna realized Arjuna was better than him in everything and he approached Drona with a request to provide him Brahmastra so that he can be equal to Arjuna in a battle. Remember Karna was eyeing for a battle, without any apparent reason, while still a student! Drona realized Karna’s wickedness and refused him the knowledge of Brahmastra.

Now having made a solid ground, let us return to the question

Had Karna not asked Drona for Brahmastra, would he have got it in the normal course?

Theoretically YES. Realistically, I doubt.

Since Drona had accepted him as a student without any apparent biase, theoretically Karna had as much chance of obtaining the knowledge of Brahmastra as anyone else had. Drona didn’t initially favored Arjuna, but seeing is dedication and devotion, he gave him Brahmastra. This proves Drona as a good judge of character. Had Karna worked with similar dedication and devotion, he too would have received the knowledge. Another proof is he received the knowledge from another guru Parshurama when he faked a similar devotion and dedication. So in fictional alternative reality, had Karna been a kind hearted, dedicated student, he would have received the divine knowledge eventually.

But…

(There is a popular saying, anything you say before but is meaningless)

Karna was already a friend of Duryodhana. Both friends were dragging each other to their mutual fall. They were wicked people. And wicked greedy people always try to find a shortcut. They prefer fraudulent schemes which they find much easier than actual hard work. Karna was already a subscriber to this view. In order to obtain the knowledge with Drona in as a natural achievement, he needed to change his character. Karna, as we know, would never have earned guru’s trust sufficiently to have achieved the divine knowledge from him. Drona would never have given such sacred knowledge to a wicked person.
