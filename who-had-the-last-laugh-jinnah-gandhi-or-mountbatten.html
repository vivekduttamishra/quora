Who had the last laugh, Jinnah, Gandhi, or Mountbatten?

📅 Feb 11, 2022 07:58 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: NO OF THE ABOVE.

The people named here were all weeping. All trying hard to contain the frenzy that and engulfed the sub-continent.

Pakistan was Jinnah’s pet project endorsed by British government represented by Lord Mountbatten. Even senior congress leaders like Sardar Patel had understood Partition was inevitable. And he had managed to convince congress leadership including Nehru and Maulana Azad to accept partition. Among them they had decided that with their grand leadership they could breeze through partition plan, or at least as smoothly as you can sail in the rough sea. They thought they knew the people. They didn’t.

Mountbatten recalled, there was only one man in the mid-summer of 1947 who exactly knew the magnitude of disaster and no one was willing to listen to that prophet of non-violence, who was without any political standing and his disciples were not listening to him.

But Mahatma had a direct reach to peoples heart and it was true, had he opposed the partition, he might have succeeded. But one great burden every visionary leader has to face is when is NOT the time to go for war. Had he put his foot down, the magnitude of devastation would have been ten times higher. He had a choice to make, a choice a people in fatal disaster has to make — lose a limb to protect the life. Each limb is precious and no one wants to lose any part of his body. But we have to let a limb go to survive. Even Chankya said that — to protect a nation, loss of a village is acceptable in the same way as to protect a family a person should be sacrificed. Gandhi had taken the lesson from Direct Action Day 1946. But he was on the journey of salvaging the situation. As a result in the midnight of August 15, 1947 as India became independent, he wasn’t basking in the glory of victory, he was far from the nations capital trying to control the situation. He managed something that entire armed strength of British force couldn’t. Peace prevailed in Calcutta for the first vital 10 days post independence, while Punjab burnt during the same period.

Gandhi died before completing his mission. He wanted to go to Pakistan, trying to preach peace and in the process protected millions of strangled Hindus across the border. Had he been successful, millions of lives could have been saved. Alas!

Had Jinnah lived long enough, he would have seen the folly of his plan. He would have repented the creation of Pakistan bitterly. We are still fighting a unwinnable war across borders where together, we could have been a world force.

The riot during the partition remains a scar on the face of British government. It was squarely their failure. They lost the face of any righteousness. What could be a glaring example other than the fact that Mountbatten finds Gandhi at par with Jesus and strived to have a similar meaningful end to his own life.

SO WHO HAD THE FINAL LAUGH?

Who always have the last laugh at the end of any war? Hyenas and Vultures. And such Hyenas and vultures always find their way to tear into the flesh of not only the dead but also the weak and injured. And yes, there were vultures and hyenas roaming wild — looting, rioting, raping and killing everyone who could bring sanity to the situation.

And their biggest enemy wasn’t Jinnah, who forced the partition. Not even Mountbatten who engineered the partition. They weren’t relevant, because they were incapable of stopping their hunt party. They only person they really feared was Mahatma, who spoke to soul of India. He alone could have brought the sanity. And not only vultures knew that, even Jinnah knew that only Gandhi can heal the wound, his idea had made. Therefore he eagerly agreed when Mahatma proposed to visit Pakistan.

But his acts worried the evil looters who were having the party, as wild beasts don’t fit in a civil society. So they attacked Mahatma. They thought, kill the man, and rule the land of dead. But then they didn’t realize then, Gandhi wasn’t just a man of flesh and blood. He was the soul of India. His physical death, brought Indian’s back from the frenzy trans. Gandhi achieved in death, what he couldn’t in life — the end of the riots.

But story doesn’t end here. It was the round one of vultures vs human civilization. Now vultures had learnt the lessons. They knew killing the man is no good. So now they are out trying to hunt down the soul. They are still a fringe group. But they are expanding. Meanwhile Gandhi too has expanded, beyond the border of political maps, language, culture. The noun Gandhi has become an adjective for peace. People from all walks and parts of life like George Bernard Shaw, Albert Einstein, Dalali Lama, Steve Jobs, Barak Obama salute Gandhi. Alas! the vultures are most active in the land of Mahatma himself. And I would not be surprised if one day they write their own history book and try to erase Gandhi from India. They won’t be able to destroy his legacy. They are not powerful enough to control the narrative world wide. But then who knows.

I would leave you with a vital question here — Who would you like to have the last laugh? Humans or Vultures? Civilization or Anarchy?

Choose wisely. Your choice would define, how the world would see India — another Taliban? Or the land of Mahatma?
