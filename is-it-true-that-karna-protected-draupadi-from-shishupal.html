Is it true that Karna protected Draupadi from Shishupal?

📅 Mar 24, 2020 10:41 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Oh Didn't you see Karna protecting Draupadi from Shishupal in Suryaputra Karna? Don't you know it was on the basis of this great series Ved Vyas wrote a distorted version of Mahabharat?

Blame me! But I read and regarded those outdated script more authentic than modern graphical tale with such a great graphics. How outdated me!

But in that outdated script Vyasji never mentioned such a thing as Shishupal trying to molest Draupadi and Great Karna being her protector! Why do you think great Karna would care for the molestation of an unchaste woman who, in his esteemed opinion, can even be brought naked in a public court!

On a serious note, I am socked at the mindset of modern progressive authors and script writers who try to not only glorify a villain but also degrade a woman by narrating a love story between an abuser and the victim. Is this progressive thought? Is this creative? Is this out of box? Is this a society we want to portray?

It is even more shocking to find proponents of such ideology often include Lady authors — can you imagine a rape victim falling in love with rapist? It was much worst.

Please don't waste your time with pathetic narrations like Suryaputra Karna who have knowingly maligned our epics and polluted thoughts of a generation.

As an author, I understand and appreciate creativity and addition of fictional element in epic to create a fiction and it is even a part of epic tradition. But most important part is responsible writing. A great fiction can be created without breaching the soul of epic, without making villains like Duryodhana and Karna a hero. Our epic is so great that infinite tales can be spawned without corrupting its soul.
