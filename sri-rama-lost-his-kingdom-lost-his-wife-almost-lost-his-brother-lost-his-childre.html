Sri Rama lost his kingdom, lost his wife, almost lost his brother, lost his children, lost his happiness and then what not, what is the learning point at the end of it?

📅 Feb 12, 2021 05:22 AM PST  |  🌐 English

An interesting question. And the answer is easy. And an important one — He became Sri Rama, the king of millions of hearts across the millennia.

There have been millions of Kings across globe, who didn’t lose their kingdom, they didn’t lose their wives or brother. They lived their life happily and what not, and yet we don’t know or care about them. In many cases their name died before they did. There was neither a value in their life or in their death.

But Rama…

He didn’t lost is kingdom. He gave it up, because it wasn’t more important than family for him. He could have contested for it, may have won it. But he let it go and gained a devotional brothers like Lakshaman and Bharata who adored and idolized him. For them, too, luxury and throne turned more important than some kingdom.

He could have sent another precedent. Fought for the throne, and maybe Bharat had followed the suite and we may have been looking a bloody history like one between Aurangzeb and Dara Sikhoh in any order. But Rama rejecting the kingdom created a precedence that not only made us proud and many others envious.

Rama paid a great price for setting up the moral and cultural benchmark. He had been selflessly getting rid of the evil forces to bring in the peace to region. A petty evil abducted his wife in retaliation. But he had earned his share to goodwill. His greatness had energized the mass, so much that an old Jatayu rose to the occasion trying to protect Sita from abduction. He immortalized himself by his brave deed.

Rama searched for Sita franatically and met Sugriv on the way. He too was exiled and separated from his wife. He could have bargained that he helped him get his wife before he helps Sugriva. But he was Rama. Not a trader. He killed evil Bali not for barter, but for establishing the Dharma. And do you see what he got in return — Hanuman — The eternal devotee of Rama!

He through his selfless and charismatic leadership energized the army so much that they performed the seemingly impossible feet — a bridge across ocean. But was his life a smooth sail?

His brother was fatally injured and needed a miracle medicine. But he had earned enough goodwill for the lifetime. His brother recovered.

His life had not been a smooth journey, it was never about comfort or selfish gains. If there was one thing his life had abundant of, it was — learnings and lessons.
