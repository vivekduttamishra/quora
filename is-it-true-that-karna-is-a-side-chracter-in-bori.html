Is it true that Karna is a side chracter in Bori?

📅 Nov 17, 2020 11:02 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Hi Thanks for the A2A.

First of all let us we need to understand that BORI is just the critical edition of Mahabahrata and aims to collate the authentic shlokas from 1259 different available manuscript. This is to emphasize that my answer would be about the epic by Vyas and not just BORI.

Now comping to Mahabharata, it is the greatest epic tale based on an incident happened over 5000 years ago. It is the longest narrative in the world history to date. Naturally, it has more characters than you would find anywhere else. Those characters are well developed and many of those characters are so well developed that independent stories can be written keeping them in the center. Karna is one of those prominent characters. Many notable fictions, poetry and TVShows have been created around the character of Karna.

So who is Karna?

He is one of the important antagonist character of Mahabharata, the right-hand man of Duryodhana the big bad evil (as mentioned in Vyas Mahabharata). Karna has been mentioned as the turnk of the tree called Duryodhana. And

Who is Duryodhana?

Well he is the prime antagonist character of Mahabharata — the big bad evil as mentioned by Ved Vyas over and again throught out the narrative. And Karna is the trunk of that tree. It was his valour and power that is the source of confedence for Duryodhana and all his evil dids. Karna is not just an active participant in most of Duryodhana’s conspiracy, he is often the instigator (Gandharva War) and prime supporter of Duryohanas action. It was on his suggestion, Karna ignored everyone else and rejected Krishna’s peace proposal.

Does that mean Karna is the Central character of Mahabharata?

No. While without any doubt, Karna is one of the prominent charcters of Mahabharata but not the centeral character. The story mentions Nara (Arjuna) and Narayana (Krishna) at the center. The story circles around the throne of Hastinapur with two prime contentders Yudhishthir and Duryodhana and follows the conspiracies done by Duryodhana to capture the throne with his supporters. Karna is one of them. So while he is part of the core story and has an important role, neither is he prime protagonist nor the prime antagonist.

He is as said, just the right-hand man of Duryodhana. Despite of the fact that he had his fair share of defeat and punishment for innumerable misdeeds and conspiracy he participated along with Duryodhana, mordern narratives show him almost at par and sometimes even greater than invanquishable Arjuna. And it is because of these narratives, people develop an false perception. When that erroneous collides with authentic reference, questions like these are born.

BORI captures the essense of Mahabharata neither underrating nor overrating Karna. But those who would refer to Karna centric fan fictions and TV Show may find Karna sidelined in the epic, without realizing that the epic is the main source and not the fan fictions.
