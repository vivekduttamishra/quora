The quote "if the Muslims want to kill us, we must face death bravely", has been attributed to MK Gandhi. Can someone confirm it's authenticity?

📅 Mar 22, 2020 12:26 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Disclaimer This answer is a reproduction of an answer I gave for this question. Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to An excerpt from Nathuram Godse's book was about Gandhi saying this, "We Hindus should never be angry even if Muslims go violent and want to erase their existence. If they decide to use sword and kill us, we have to face death bravely." Is this true?

What is worst than a lie is a misquoted truth. While I am not sure about the authenticity, it sounds on the line of something he would say. Himself a practicener of Hindu belief with deep rooted faith in Hindu culture he valued self sacrifice more than being violent to others. He believed on lines of Shivi and Dadhichi. Ram had all the powers of the world yet he prayed for a passage from the ocean — why? He crossed the ocean and decided to give Ravan on more chance at peace. Was he weak? Forgiving Ravan for kidnapping his wife? Rams restraint against abuse of power makes him the greatest warrior of all time. But if we don't understood deep rooted hindu philosophy of Neelkanth Mahadev are bound to misunderstand this remark or become defensive about it.
