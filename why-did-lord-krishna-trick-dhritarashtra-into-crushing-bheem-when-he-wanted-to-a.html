Why did Lord Krishna trick Dhritarashtra into crushing Bheem when he wanted to avenge his son’s Kauravas death? If Pandavas can take revenge of Draupadi, why can't he?

📅 Feb 12, 2021 06:51 AM PST  |  🌐 English

I must say, this is one of the more absurd question I have seen in recent times. There are so many obvious answers for it —

  1. Krishna didn’t trick Dhritarashtra, he thwarts his deceitful tactic to kill unsuspecting Bheem, and that is a big difference.
  2. A judge sentences a criminal to death, but judge doesn’t face a murder sentence for that.
  3. Duryodhana’s death was a punishment for his crimes. You don’t punish the punisher.
  4. Dhritarashtra was a deceitful from the beginning and was a party of every heinous act of Duryodhana. His punishment too was due. His living with his last failed coup, with all his son’s dead was his punishment. He always wanted his son to succeed him as the king. Now he would live knowing he doesn’t have a son left to follow him. It is he who would follow him in death — but not anytime soon. He would have to wait for a very long time.
