Is it true that Karna and Eklavya were denied of learning weapons because they were low-born?

📅 Jan 14, 2022 09:54 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I have answered this question so many times…

Karna was NEVER denied any education. He was declined knowledge of higher weapons. Twice. Both guru found him wicked and deceitful. Drona, who knew him from quite young age, outright reject him for higher weapons only. Parshurama who didn’t know him well expelled him after giving him the knowledge but also proclaiming that he wouldn’t be able to use the higher weapon because he isn’t worthy of it. Both case it was deceit and not a case of high or low born.

Now coming to Ekalavya. He isn’t referred as low born anywhere. He was the son of King of Nishada and he was welcomed even in Rajsuy Yagna of Yudhisthira. The exact reason as to why Drona refused him isn’t clear. All that is mentioned is —

  1. Guru Knew Dharma. It is emphasized here.
  2. He considered for other students.

Now, Nishada weren’t friendly to Hastinapur. Their allegiance lied with Magadha. So rejection of Eklavaya was most likely a political descision based on Dharma.

Drona’s decision in fully vindicated when Eklavaya met his end at the hand of Krishna who had incarnated to destroy evils.
