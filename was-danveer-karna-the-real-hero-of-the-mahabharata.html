Was Danveer Karna the real hero of the Mahabharata?

📅 Apr 06, 2021 11:37 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

First thing first, there is no Danveer mentioned in Mahabharata.

About Karna, without doubt he is one of the prominent characters of Mahabharata and a reputed warrior. But he is no hero, forget being the real hero of Mahabharata.

At the beginning of the epic tale, the prominent characters of Mahabharata has been grouped metaphorically in two trees — evil Duryodhana and righteous Yudhishthir. Same is again emphasized by Krishna in Udyoga Parva. In both these explanations, Karna is the trunk of tree Duryodhana who is mentioned evil on uncountable occasions.

But even if we get past the one line definition, there isn’t anything in Karna’s life that can be called heroic. True, he has often been praised for his charities. But then many corrupt businessmen and politicians are also well known for their charity. Charity isn’t an absolute justification of a good character.

As per the authentic versions of Mahabharata

  • Karna, back in Dronacharya gurukul carried envy and enmity against Pandavas, who were his classmates and didn’t reciprocate his enmity. He was part of several conspiracies against Pandavas along with his friend Duryodhana.
  • Even at gurukul he had a goal to defeat Arjuna and therefore sought Divyastra from Drona who turned him down seeing his wicked mindset.
  • He earned the celestial weapons from Parshurama by deceit and was expelled by Bhagwan Parshurama.
  • He participated in Lakshagrih conspiracy to burn down Pandavas along with their mother. This is clearly mentioned in ADI Parva and Karna vadh parva or BORI without any contradiction.
  • He was part of the hoard who tried to burn down Draupadi after her swyamvara. Of course they failed.
  • He spoke loudest during great gamble and provoked Dussassna to strip Draupadi in public.
  • He wanted to see Draupadi in tree bark and planned Gosha yatara which again backfired.

Here I am not counting multiple defeats or few victories which can be credited to Karna. Victories don’t define a hero. How you use the powers vested on you define who you are.

Karna was certainly someone reputed and powerful. But did he ever use his power for the good of the society? There is not a single reference. Did he try to protect Draupadi from mad crowd who were trying to burn her alive after swyamvara? No, he participated in the mad rush. Did he try to protect her from being dragged in swaymavara? No, he ordered Dussassana to strip her. Did Karna try to prevent the great war that killed many? No. When destiny gave him an opportunity which is rejected.

Karna’s life is full of deceit, conspiracy and evil acts. All his actions were for selfish goals for himself, his own egos, and few for his evil friend. Nothing for society. Nothing for Dharma.

I just wonder how can someone call him a hero who always sided with evil and whom Krishna and Shiva decreed to be killed.
