Is Drona's ignorance about Karna's real high parentage and his belief that he was a Sutaputra partly a reason for his being harsh towards Karna?

📅 Jun 05, 2022 06:23 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short answer: No

Dronacharya was well aware about the parentage of all Pandavas and yet not all of them were bestowed with the rare honour of knowing the divyastra. We don't see any instance during the Gurukul days where Dronacharya has been harsh with Karna not even when he denied him the knowledge of divyastra. In fact that point in time, Karna himself gives the testimony that Dronacharya had always been impartial. The real reason of Drona’s denial was the wicked attitude of Karna which is explicitly mentioned.

Later we see that Karna is crowned as the king of Anga. We don't see either Dronacharya or Kripacharya objecting his coronation. He does not stop him from participating in Guru dakshina battle against Drupada

Letter during the evaluation of Karna as a warrior of rank he does not elude to his birth at soot Putra but his inability as a warrior

Most important of all, questions like these carry delusion that sutas where low born, which while pronounced in modern narrative is almost non exist in ancient texts, with no reference of caste, no reference of prejudice based on caste, and clear contradiction with the elevation of people from lower economic or otherwise strata as the prime ministers learned authors and the army generals.

Suta was a name given to a race born out of the wedlock of a Brahmin and Kshatriaya, incidentally as per the modern society to of the highest castes, ruling out all the factors of birth prejudice.
