If Arjun is the conventional hero of the epic, do you agree that Karna is a tragic hero?

📅 Jan 24, 2022 10:02 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I would agree to what Ved Vyas and Lord Krishna said — He was the trunk of an evil tree Duryodhana, that he was an extremely evil soul. Krishna who incarnated to destroy evils urged Arjuna to kill that “extremely evil souled”. Such people are not called “hero”.

There is nothing “heroic” about Karna.

I failed to find a single reference in the epic that can categories Karna as a hero. I don’t see him ever doing anything that was not aligned to his egos or his selfish interest or that of his friend. There is no mention of anything good he did for the society, or helped a woman being disrobed or burnt, or a village being under threat of a demon or a kingdom being plundered by a hoard of army. NOTHING.

What about “tragic” life?

So was Karna a villain grasped in tragedy and was way led by it. Once again NO. There is nothing tragic about Karna’s life. Sure his mother abandoned him as a baby. So did the mothers of Krishna and Ved Vyas. But we never count that as tragedy. Do we? From all conceivable logic, that was a blessing in disguise. Karna could hope to have a respectable life with an unwed mother. He grew up in a prosperous family whose prosperity also rose with wealth that Karna brought.

Karna never had to struggle. He was trained by the best of the bests gurus available in Aryavart. He consciously chose his friends among the sons of his father’s friend. During gurukul he had just two motto — find shortcut for gaining knowledge, even if it means deceiving the guru and being part of criminal conspiracies of his friend.

Out of college he soon was elevated to the ranks of a King and remained a King through out his life. He never suffered in his life which he admitted himself. He plundered, conspired and lived a life of luxury. His wasn’t exiled. His family wasn’t humiliated. No one conspired against him or tried to burn him alive. What’s more even his friend never blamed him for his numerous failure or act of desertion.

Some people may find the curses of Karna tragic. It is like saying that it was tragic that the rapists of Nirbhyaya were caught. Should we call them too — tragic heroes? Because Karna was no better. He was part of the crowd that wanted to throw a new bride Druapadi's in fire. He was the person who tried to molest Draupadi. He was cursed for his misdeeds. It was a punishment.

The only thing tragic about Karna, is the fact that he destroyed the potential of good he was born with and became such an extreme evil soul that even God wanted him destroyed and relished the fact when his destruction became eminent.

—Vivek Dutta Mishra
