Was Adiratha economically poor as per Mahabharata? Was he an ill paid charioteer without any ancestral property and a large family to support?

📅 Sep 29, 2020 06:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi Thanks for the A2A

An airplane pilot is technically a driver. But are they poor?

Do you know the head of President’s bodyguards is an officer of the rank of Brigadier or a Colonel?

Do you know Kurian Varghese was a milkman who sold milk products under the brand name (Amul)?

Adhiratha was no ordinary charioteer. He was the charioteer and friend of the King of Hastinapur. Do you think Dhritrashtra was paying poorly to his charioteer friend? Duryodhana never ill-treated his friend. And the other charioteer of Dhritrashtra, Sanjay, was given the responsibility to be official ambassador of the King.

All these things clearly point that Adhiratha should be compared to high ranking official like a Brigadier, Colonel or the flight pilot and not ordinary Ola and Uber drivers.

By the way, even few Ola/Uber drivers earn a handsome income of around 1 lac per month. I talked to few of them in the past. They had demaned a minimum guranteed income of 1.25L and had gone on strike a couple of years back.

And yes, I don’t remember any reference in Mahabharata that suggested that Adhiratha was a poor charioteer but as the kings friend — Vaishampayana said, ‘Dhritarashtra had a friend and he was a suta named Adhiratha. At that time, he went to the Jahnavi, 31 together with his wife. O king! His wife was unrivalled on earth in her beauty. The immensely fortunate one was named Radha and she had no son, though she had undertaken special efforts to obtain offspring. She saw the basket floating along, as it willed. It was protected well and was beautiful with ribbons and a handle. The waves of the Jahnavi carried it near her. Driven by curiosity, the beautiful one grasped it.

If you see the highlights, you would realize there is no possibility of them being poor.
