In Ramayana, who was Manthara? What was her contribution to the great epic of Ramayana?

📅 Mar 20, 2020 10:49 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I believe the question is not as simple as it sounds. Everyone who knows Ramayan knows Msnthara, so I will try to discover Msnthara beyond —

  • The Aide of Kekai
  • One who coaxed Kekai against Ramas coronation.

Manthara is essentially the name of the destiny

  • She is a reminder that destiny is so powerful that it can nullify the wishes of kings and court. That when destiny plays its hand, a simple nobody can bow down kings.
  • She is the destiny of Ram — she paved his way to greatness.

Manthara is source of some of the greatest lessons —

  • She is a proof of Karma rule of Gita — you can act but can't control the outcome. Neither Dasharath could make Ram the King nor Msnthara succeeded in making Bharat the King.
  • She is a proof that bad intentions too can have good consequences — Manthara had no good intentions for Rama but she was pivotal to Ram's journey to greatness.
  • She represents the fact that no matter how good you are, not everyone is going to like you and majority may not always have their way.
  • She represents the fact that even evil can be selfless — Manthara had no personal gain, yet she acted against Ram. And an evil act can't absolve its doer even if consequences are great.
  • She is a reminder that evil followers can blemish even their great leaders. Bharat suffered public ire not because of his misdeeds but because of Manthara. Great people should also be careful of who follows them.
