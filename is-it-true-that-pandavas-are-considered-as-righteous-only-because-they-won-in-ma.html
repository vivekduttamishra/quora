Is it true that Pandavas are considered as righteous only because they won in Mahabharata? How would they have been portrayed had Kauravas won?

📅 May 13, 2020 07:13 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Actually its the other way round. They were righteous and that's why the won Mahabharata.

This question often applies on history not on epics. History is often considered to be written by victorious. But think about Mahabharat and you will get the answer.

  1. Mahabharat retelling is done by Maharishi Ved Vyas, who was the common sire for both Dhritarashtra and Pandavas. Why would he be partial? Why would he condemn part of his own clan as evil?
  2. Dhritarashtra ruled the empire for a long time and they sidelined Pandavas almost the entire period. If someone had the capability and means to influence and buy loyalty it was him, and which is clear from the fact that Drona, Kripa and Ashwathama fought on their side. If he couldn’t win the narrative despite of all this, how can you not trust it?
  3. If you read any history of the victors, they are righteous all the way. Why would they allow their depictions such as —
    1. They baited their own kingdom and wife in a game of dice.
    2. They killed Drona while he sat lamenting on the fake news of their son’s death, which was spread by them.
  4. They could have censored everything against them.

Let us know move to the most important clue that contradicts this theory — Krishna sided with Pandavas. And Krishna’s credentials were beyond doubt. Why did he side with Pandavas?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
