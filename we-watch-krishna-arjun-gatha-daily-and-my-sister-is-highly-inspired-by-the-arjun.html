Arjuna-Subhadra Story

While not explicitly mentioned (neither contradicted) in Mahabharata, according to Bhagwatam Subhadra actually loved Arjuna. Social network is full of skewed narratives flashing unknown screenshots by someone who doesn’t even know proper English-Sanskrit translation. If you scan other answers, you would find Subhadra Harana is translated as Rape of Subhadra. There can be only two answers — malicious intention or stupid translation.

As mentioned Bhagwatam clearly mentions Subhadra loved Arjuna. And why shouldn’t she? Arjuna was the greatest warrior of his time, closest friend to her brother Krishna. In an era predating social network, why won’t a girl be attracted to someone admired by entire Aryavart including the great Krishna? Do you think she may not have heard the story of Draupadi Swyamvara or Khandava Dahan?

SUBHADRA ABDUCTION AS PER BORI

But let us not go on supposition and directly check the exact reference of Subhadra harana from BORI CE. After Krishna suggested and Yudhisthir endorsed, here Arjuna abducts Subhadra —

Kunti’s son rushed at her and forced her onto his chariot. Abducting the sweet-smiling one, that tiger among men set out for his own city on his chariot, as if on air.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 711-712).

This is BORI description. Read the highlighted text. Subhadra had a sweet smile when she was abducted. Do you think, a girl would smile if abducted against her wish? I would invite you to cross-reference this narrative against Sita Harana and Draupadi Harana by Jaidradh. Do you find either of them smiling?

But we may ask the question — can any woman be really happy when abducted? In fact I have two popular incident supporting this part also. Rukmini too was abducted. And she too was happy. Another such incident takes places after thousands of years as Prithvi Raj Chauhan abducts Sanyogita.

Now I have shared the story of four abductions. Now, re-read the text of Subhadra harana from the BORI CE and decide for yourself, under which category of abduction — with her consent or without her consent.

Next, to verify the truth, we have a technique called cross-references. So let us check if there is any cross-reference which indicates Subhadra was abducted against her wishes. THERE IS NONE.

Is there a cross-reference that can suggest Subhadra was happy.

  1. Balarama gets to know about the abduction. While other yadavas were agitated, he wasn’t. He based on Krishna’s suggestion invites Arjuna back for marriage. Why? Was Balarama afraid of Arjuna that he decided not to punish the abductor of her sister?
  2. Arjuna-Subhadra gets married and stays over a year in Dwarka. Did Subhadra ever complained against her abduction? Is there a single reference that suggests that she wasn’t happy even for a moment? None.
  3. Subhadra supports Arjuna in trying to pacify Draupadi’s anger. Why would a woman abducted against her wish would try to do that? Won’t she try to extract her vengeance against her abductor and split the family?
  4. Is there any mention that Subhadra or Abhimanyu were mistreated or carried malice against Arjuna.
  5. Was Krishna stupid or so cruel that he would forget the agony of his sister and side with her tormentor?
