Arjuna didn't use nearly 70% of his weapons like pashupatastra because the opposition was not worthy of these weapons, but by making Sri Krishna his charioteer, Arjuna had used his biggest and victorious weapon before the war. How much do you agree?

📅 Sep 06, 2020 12:37 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Arjuna didn’t use most of his divine weapons, but it wasn’t because there was not a worthy adversary — Bhishma was the most formidable adversary.

As I see, Arjuna wasn’t desperate and was confident he could subdue his enemies without those weapons. And there is no denying, Arjuna had Krishna in his support.

Now as per as the celestial weapons are concerned, on multiple occasions it has been highlighted — Don’t use it against an inferior opponent. So naturally Arjuna had to use it with care.

But I disagree, when you say — Krishna was the biggest weapon of Arjuna.

Krishna wasn’t a weapon. He was the shield. And not everyone who gets an opportunity to choose, can actually make the right decision — Their ignorance, their egos, their arrogance and above all their lack of dedication for Dharma prevent them from making the choice.

Didn’t Krishna offered support to Karna and Duryodhana as well? Did they benefit from his gesture?

The biggest victorious weapon of Arjuna was his Dharma, dedication and focus — The won him the grace of Mahadeva, Krishna, Bhishma, Drona and admiration of everyone else.

Remember the most important theme — Dharma Rakshati Rakshitah.
