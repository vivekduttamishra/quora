Who was the most powerful warrior in the Mahabharata? Krishna was the most skilled at war strategy; but who tipped the scales when it came to might, Bheeshma, Arjuna, Karna or someone else?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 03:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

In Mahabharat multiple warriors participated and many of them tipped the war at different point of time. But if we are talking about the top influencers of the war that had a mighty impact on the war I would say there were three main and a few other warriors who can be counted upon. Here is a list in no particular order.

  1. Bhisma: Bhisma had probably the longest impact on the outcome of the war for the first ten days of Mahabharat. A sizeable portion of Pandava’s army perished. Situation had gone to an extent that Krishna setting aside his promise of not taking part in the war rose to attack Bhishma. This was no small an incident to reflect the true influence of Bhisma.
  2. Bhima: Single handedly killed every single Kauravas including Duryodhana and Dusassana. He aided Arjun on day 14 defeating Karna and Drona as Arjuna sought Jaidradh. Even before Mahabharat he killed Jarasandh who would have been a potential ally for Kauravas. I am not including Keechak, Hidimb or Bakasur as they were not likely to have an impact on Mahabharat war.
  3. Arjuna: Arjun was the champion archer of Pandavas and he tilted the winning proposition in Pandava’s favour by defeating Bhisma. He was the only one which truly terrified Drona, who didn’t form chakravyuh until Arjuna was out of way. And who can forget he killed Ardharathi Karna.
  4. Abhimanyu: While Abhimanyu continued to dazzle warriors like Bhisma from the first day of the war, he rellay influenced the war big way on his ultimate war on day 13. Without Abhimanyu and with Arjuna and Krishna fighting on a far off corner, the battle would have been conclusively lost that day. He single-handedly changed the destiny of the war.
  5. Ghatotkatch: Again the great warrior was fighting the battle as early as day 4 and earned a lot of admiration from Grand Bhisma. On Day 14, no one could contain him in the battled and finally Karna managed to kill him only my using Indrastra. Even in his death he turned the tide in Pandavas favour by robbing Karna of only celetial weapon, he could hope to kill Arjuna.
  6. Ashwatthama: He had no real influencing role till day 18. But he did change the course of history in a big way. He killed most of the Pandavas surviving general including Dhristdumna, Shikandi and Uppandavas. His action stole all the joy of victory from Pandavas. However, his action can hardly be considered that of a warrior. He acted as a beast who hunts in the night.

There were other warriors who had influence on the war. However here are some, in my opinion,

Warriors who had little or no impact on the war.

  1. Barbarik — The much hyped warrior doesn’t find a mention in Vyas Mahabharat. But considering the folk tales to be true had a skewed sense of war, fighting for weaker side and not that of Dharma. If such warrior, indeed existed, Krishna got rid of him much before war. With Krishna around, such warrior had no chance.
  2. Drona — Fought for 15 days but could claim to kill only one great general from Pandava’s side — Drupad. This was certainly not a game changer. He became the chief of Kaurava Army on 11th day. He planned to capture Yudhishtir. He failed to do so in Arjuna’s presence on Day 11 and 12. His plan was foiled on day 13 by young Abhimanyu and despite managing to keep Arjuna away, he lost to meet his goal. Abhimanyu’s brutal death however can be attributed to his command. He again failed to protect Jaidradh on day 14. Neither could he prevent Bhima from slaughtering Kaurava brothers one by one.
  3. Karna — Didn’t fight for first 10 days of the war, resenting what he felt humiliation by Bhisma. Shared the common failure with Drona till day 15, the day Drona Died. He took the command of Kaurava’s army on day 16 and died on day 17. His failure on day 14, shattered the confidence of Duryodhana and routing on day 16 made Dhritrashtra realize that the war is truly lost. His medals include death of Ghatotkatch using Indra’s vasvishakti and being part of a pack of eight warriors, each having defeated by Abhimanyu, joined to kill him unarmed.
