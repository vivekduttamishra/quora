Are nivatakavachas are weak demons? Did Arjuna killed because of his boon or because of his skill?

📅 Aug 23, 2022 11:38 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for asking the question, although I do wonder what would even make up for such a question? But let me try to break the question in parts—

Did Arjuna kill Nivatakavacha because of his boon or skill?

Arjuna had no boon that he would be able to kill Nivatakavacha. So we can safely rule out boon part. That leaves the “or” part that would be your answer.

Were Nivatakavachas weak demons?

Now this is the part that makes me wonder… what on earth (or heaven) would make someone wonder that Nivatakavachas were weak? Is there any single reference (other than quora) that even hint at them being weak? If you think, anyone with thirty million count, even ants or honeybees would be typically unconquerable and fatal to an individual. And we are talking about mighty demons. Here are few very hard to miss references —

‘Today, the masses of gods are incapable of vanquishing you in battle, not to speak of humans of the world who have not perfected their souls. You are immeasurable and unassailable. You are also incomparable in battle.’ Then, with his body hair standing up, the god again said, ‘O brave one! There will be no one who will be your match in fighting with weapons. You are never distracted. You are skilled.

Some may say that by boon they were unkillable by Gods, but still there are two important points to note —

  • They were also invanquishable. Now that certainly makes them NOT weak.
  • Here they are clearly mentioned more capable than any human warrior. So even if they were weak, as some may want to say, they were more powerful than humans.

The battle in details mentions their powerful weapons and Maya clearly indicating they were neither weak not dumb like some zombies.

And here is how they are described after their death —

O immensely radiant one! Matali recounted my deeds in detail to the Indra of the gods, completely and exactly as it had happened—the destruction of Hiranyapura, the use of maya, the way it was repulsed and the slaying of the immensely powerful nivatakavachas in battle. On hearing this, the illustrious Purandara, the one with the thousand eyes, was delighted. In the midst of the Maruts, he spoke words like, ‘Wonderful’ and ‘Well done’. Together with the other gods, the king of the gods repeatedly congratulated me and spoke these gentle words. ‘In battle, you have accomplished a deed that was impossible for the gods and the asuras.

Does that sound weak?

Let us now see the views of contemporary reputed warriors of the era —

Kripacharya

he alone defeated the Nivatakavacha and Kalakhanja danavas, whom even the gods could not slay. 102

Bhishma

He penetrated Hiranyapura, which was on the other side of the ocean, after vanquishing sixty thousand Nivatakavachas in battle. This scorcher of enemy cities has vanquished the gods, together with Indra. The mighty-armed Arjuna satisfied the god of fire. 53 In the same way, Narayana killed many others in that battle. Behold! These two immensely valorous ones have arrived as the maharatha warriors Vasudeva and Arjuna. It is said that they are the ancient gods Nara and Narayana. They are invincible in the world of men, and even against Indra, the gods and the asuras.

  • Note the context of Nivatakavacha

and again

Partha vanquished the Nivatakavachas in battle, whom even Vasava found to be invincible in an encounter. That is proof. Who is capable of vanquishing Pandava, who prides himself in an encounter, in battle?

Narada

Yatudhanas were created from Brahma’s altar.112 They had teeth and terrible forms and were valorous, with the speed of the wind. They were endowed with the prowess of maya. They lived here and protected themselves. The danavas named Nivatakavachas were invincible in battle.

Sanjay

Even the gods praise his terrible deeds. The valiant one slaughtered the nivatakavacha s and the kalakeya s, the ones who were the enemies of the great Indra and resided in Hiranyapura. He killed the Poulamas and their followers in the twinkling of an eye.138

Drona

The nivatakavachas were the enemies of the gods and could not be killed by the gods in battle. They were defeated by that brave one. There were thousands of danavas who lived in the city of Hiranyapura. That tiger among men vanquished them.41 How can humans withstand him?

Dhritrashtra

‘Dhritarashtra said, “Arjuna killed all of us easily, as he wished. I don’t think Yama would have escaped, had he attacked him in a battle. O fortunate one! Partha robbed single-handed. Single-handed, he satisfied Agni.280 He vanquished the earth single-handed and made all the powerful kings offer tribute.281 Single-handedly, using his divine bow, he killed the nivatakavacha s.282

Now if you look at the seer number of references and the superlative references you would get the answer of power of Nivatakavacha and how greatly honour Arjuna was accorded for the almost impossible task.
