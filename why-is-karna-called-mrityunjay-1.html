Why is Karna called Mrityunjay?

📅 Jul 26, 2021 01:17 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: Karna is not known as Mrityunjaya.

There is not a single mention of this salutation for Arjuna anywhere in the ancient or authentic texts. The only place he has been called Mrityunjay is in the fictional epic by the same name written by Shivaji Sawant.

It was the name of the novel and in that novel this word referred to Karna.

But this fiction can’t be considered as a replacement of authentic and ancient texts. In your writing your are free to call anyone Mrityunjaya or King of the world or greater than the greatest. No one can object.

But if we are talking about the great epic Mahabharata, Karna was never referred as Mrityunjay, neither had he given any reason to be called by that name. Here is just one statement to contradict this claim —

When night was over, Karna came to Duryodhana and spoke to him. “O son of Gandhari! It is fortunate that you are alive and fortunate that we have met again. It is fortunate that you have vanquished the gandharvas, who are capable of assuming any form at will. O descendant of the Kuru lineage! It is fortunate that I see all of your brothers. They are maharathas and have been victorious, triumphing over the enemies in battle. While you looked on, I was worsted by all those gandharvas. My own army was routed and I was incapable of ensuring that they remained there. I was sorely wounded by the arrows and hard-pressed. I ran away.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6525-6529).

Do you think, a warrior who runs away leaving behind his best friend and family at the mercy of enemy can be called Mrityunjaya?

It may not be out of place to mention that this was not the first time Karna fled scared. Nor was this the last time. His brothers, son, and patrons were killed in front of his eyes and all he did was to run away.

But I see here we have people who are fascinated by fantasy and prefers to call him Mrityunjaya based on several more fables which do not find any mention in the grand epic itself. Some instances which are fictionalized to glorify Karna includes —

  • Fable: They feel Karna was without astra when he died.
    • Fact:
      • Karna was without chariot but not without weapon. He was fighting when he died.
      • Karna himself attached several warriors including Abhimanyu and Bhima while they were without chariot or weapon. Dharma is not one sided.
  • Fable: Parshuram cursed Karna because he was not a Brahmana
    • Fact: Parshurama cursed Karna because of his deceit.
  • Fable: Kunti came to Karna only to protect Pandavas and forced him to spare the life of four of her sons.
    • Fact: She asked Karna to part ways with evils. She never asked to spare his four sons life. Think! why would she ask for four and not all five? Karna gave that boon on his own and Kunti certainly asked Karna to keep his promise.
  • Fable: Karna pushed Krishna chariot few steps back
    • Fact: Never happened in authentic versions
  • Fable: Karna donated his tooth on his death bed.
    • Fact: Arjuna severed Karna head in one clean shot. Karna was dead before he could have donated anything to anyone.

As you can see the extent of distortion you must add to the epic to make a coward evil into something like Mrityunjaya?
