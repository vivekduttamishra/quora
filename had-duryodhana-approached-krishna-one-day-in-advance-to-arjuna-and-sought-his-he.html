Short Answer : Krishna would have given him exactly what he got. No more and no less.

Question assumes Krishna was bound by certain circumstances to divide himself and his army among the two sides. Not true. Nothing could really bind him other than adherence to Dharma and absolute devotion. Duryodhana was incapable of both.

Promises, oaths and trivial truths can never define someone who is himself the defination of truth and Dharma.

He wouldn't fight Kalyavan because he wants to fight. He won't let ordinary citizens suffer to prove he is powerful. He is Ranchod.

He is devoid of egos that made Yudhisthir lose his kingdom, and Karna lose his Kavach as per popular opinion.

He won't handover his Sudarshan Chakra because Ashwathama asks as a Brahman. He won't refuse it either. But he would make the circumstances that he wants. And the end of the story — what matters is what he wanted.

He would still have given the same choice to Duryodhana and he still would have made exactly the same choice. Happily. In his wicked brain he couldn't have comprehend the power of weapon less, non combatant Krishna over an akchohini of reputed warriors, not unless Krishna desired.
