Could Ashoka or Chandragupta Maurya have defeated Alexander the Great had he invaded the Mauryan Empire? How do their skills compare?

📅 Aug 01, 2020 09:27 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Consider the time line.

  • Alexander invades India, and was given not only a clean pass but support from Takshika.
  • His army now also included several kingdom's troop who were either vanquished or surrendered.
  • Then comes the battle with Parvateshwar (Porus). History says Alexander won this battle? But aftermath suggests a altogether different story.
    • An army that marched for thousands of kilometers stopped just sort of reaching their goal — the Eastern end.
    • Army was suddenly so homesick they wanted to run away.
    • What lied ahead?
  • To proceed, they had to fight Magadh — with a standing troop of 2lac, including dreaded elephant. It wasn't a minor kingdom like Porus’.
  • At this point Magadh was ruled by corrupt Nanda dynasty and was in a terrible chaos. Yes Alexander couldn't dare to fight and retreated.
  • Same Magadh was later captured by Chandragupta Maurya, who forced the remains of Alexander's army into a treaty.
  • Chandragupta strengthened the empire greatly ensuring no other Alexander dares to invade it again.
  • His grandson, Ashoka the great, expanded the empire multifold. Not only did he tried war but also won hearts across borders with his message of Buddhist peace.

Alexander invaded India. But what happened in Alexander — Porus war is unclear.

In any case Alexander is irrelevant for history. His conquest has no effect on history. Forget having any long lasting effort on Indian, culture and mindset, they couldn't change even fashion and dressing.

Ashoka's expansion is of a different nature. Not only his artifacts are national symbols, he changed Civilizations and their beliefs as we know — Buddhism.
