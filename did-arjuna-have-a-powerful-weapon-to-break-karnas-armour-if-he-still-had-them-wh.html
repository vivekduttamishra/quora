Did Arjuna have a powerful weapon to break Karna's armour if he still had them? What metal was the armour of Karna made of?

📅 May 14, 2022 01:04 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Karna’s armor was divine. His Kundala was made up of Amrita which would have made his killing difficult.

That said, Karan was repeatedly defeated even with his armors. He was defeated by Bhimasena, by Gandharvaas, by Arjuna and most likely by Drupada. And here lies the secret. Armors may protect you from physical harm, but it can protect your from fear. Karna fought many wars where he didn’t die. But then he fled many of them. Even in his last battle he cared more about his chariot wheel than the fact that he still had weapons left to fight. Can you make a guess why?

But now let us take on this question. Can Arjuna’s weapon penetrate Karna’s armor. Without doubt Pashupata weapon which had potential to annihilate entire universe could have destroyed both Amrita and any metal. That is clearly mentioned as well. But equally clearly mentioned is the fact that Pashupata shouldn’t we used on an inferior opponent who lacks energy. As such Arjuna would never have used Pashupata against Karna. And he didn’t. But then do you recall how Karna was killed?

Karna was killed with an ordinary Anjalika arrow. The arrow severed his head. And his throat and neck wasn’t covered by any divine armor. Do you see a parallel of this story in the story of Rahu, who had consumed Amrita and Lord Vishnu severed his head. Because of the existence of Amrita the head lived on but the body was dead. Now imagine Karna had his armors. Arjuna could still have severed his head. The body would be dead as armors didn’t have Amrita. The head with Kundala would have lived on.
