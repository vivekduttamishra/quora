If Draupadi was so modest, why didn't she protest when Kunti distributed her among Pandavas when she was free?

📅 Jul 28, 2020 09:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A Mohit Kumar Dalal

Let us first understand being modest and being submissive is not mutually exclusive, rather often complementary —

Definition of modest (Marriam Websters)

a: placing a moderate estimate on one's abilities or worth

b: neither bold nor self-assertive tending toward diffidence

There are more than sufficient evidences to prove that Draupadi was submissive, at least till the time of her humiliation during the dice game —

  • She had no say in the samovar terms and conditions. Dhristdumna informs her in no uncertain terms that — “You shall choose the winner as your husband”
  • When Arjuna hits the target, she marries him and follows him without seeking his true identity. What was he? Just a begging Brahmin from no noble family. But she didn’t ask.

Being submissive is a synonym of modesty.

But perhaps by modesty you mean decent or good character or moral. So here is the most important part of your question —

DOES MARRYING PANDAVAS MAKES DRAUPADI IMMORAL?

  • While polyandry, without doubt, was unconventional during those days, it wasn’t one of the unheard. Even today many cultures have the concept of polyandry.
  • Both Krishna and Vedavyas endorsed the marriage with a valid reference from Shastras.
  • Lord Shiva ordained it.
  • She wed all the five pandavas as per vedic rituals.
  • And being modest, she agreed.

Now, it my turn to ask question —

  • Why should marriage be termed distribution?
    • Kunti said that when she was unaware of Draupadi’s identity.
    • But did they really distribute her?
    • No, they all married her, and no matter how unconventional the proposal may sound, it doesn’t change the fact that they proposed marriage and not distribution.
  • And yes, because Draupadi was modest, she couldn’t refuse.
