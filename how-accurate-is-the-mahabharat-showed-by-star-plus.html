How accurate is the Mahabharat showed by Star plus?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 10:01 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Looking at Mahabharat by Star Plus, one thing is very, very apparent — they took their disclaimer seriously. They had no intention of making show authentic or accurate — just dramatic and glamorous.

The story has so many loopholes that it is difficult to count. So let me start with a few good things the show has, in my opinion —

  1. The actor playing Bhisma and Krishna looked impressive. This was something I originally had thought would be impossible after watching Mukesh Khanna and Nitish Bharadwaj
  2. Actor playing Arjun has also done decent.
  3. Amba Swyamber and Bhishma Parshuram samvad was a treat.

Well now that good is over lets concentrate on damaging interpolations —

  1. Most of the lady characters are potrayed in a extremely bad light — arrogant, selfish and vengeful for no reason — Satyavati, Amba, Madri and Draupadi. This has no bearing with original story.
  2. Shakuni is given too much importance and villainous look. While this started with BRChopra’s show with one of their directors playing the role, it heightened this in this series.
  3. Karna has been over glorified by removing episodes like Gandharava war and diluting Chirharan and Virat war.
  4. Krishna commentary in the initial part is not just boring but also way out of sync from the Gita commentary.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkant Edition of Mahabharat (Geet Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
