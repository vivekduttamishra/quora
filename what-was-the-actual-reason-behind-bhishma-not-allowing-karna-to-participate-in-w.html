What was the actual reason behind Bhishma not allowing Karna to participate in war?

📅 Oct 30, 2020 11:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The actual reason was the fact that Bhishma never stopped Karna from participating in the war. In modern narratives it has become a trend to blame others for Karna's flaws, mistakes and failures. There are few ready-made scapegoats — Draupadi, Shakuni, Duryodhana, Bhishma, Drona and Kripacharya to take the blame —

This story refers to the Udyoga Parva. Karna felt offended that Bhisma called him boasting, and selfish, and graded him an Ardharathi. Karna had a right to resent what he found his personal insult. His ego was hurt. But it wasn’t Bhisma who asked him not to participate in the war.

So Why didn’t Karna fight? Let us hear what Karna has to say in this regard —

O tiger among kings! In an excellent fight, there is no doubt that I will kill the Pandavas alone, though the fame for this will accrue to Bhishma. O lord of men! You have appointed Bhishma your commander and the commander is credited with qualities, never the warriors. O king! I will never fight as long as Gangeya is alive. But once Bhishma has been slain, I will fight with all the maharathas.’”’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 8331-8334).

Now some may feel that because Karna’s ego was hurt, he had right to resent and not participate in the war. They may blame Bhishma for creating such a situation for Karna. To understand it let us take two more case studies —

Was Bhishma biased?

During Virat war, Karna abuses Krispacharya who too was fighting for Duryodhana. This angered Ashwatthama and created a rift within their ranks — Drona, Kripacharya and Ashwatthama vs Karna. But as an expert milletary general, Bhisma boils down the situation by supporting both parties —

‘Bhishma said, “Drona’s son sees it well. Kripa sees it well. Karna wishes to fight, in accordance with the dharma of kshatriyas.

What Karna spoke, was for the purpose of inciting enterprise in us. The preceptor’s son121 should pardon us, because a great task has to be undertaken.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4

This proves that Bhishma always supported the cause of the war rather than forcing personal favour or biased. Similarly, Bhishma as a commander spoke what he felt as the prevailing trait of Karna. He had right to judge people who were supposed to be under his command and his accessments were endorsed by Dronacharya and Kripacharya.

Dronacharya

Dronacharya, Kripacharya and Bhisma were abused by Karna on multiple occassions. They, too, could have walked away from Duryodhana stating a similarly hurt ego. But Dronacharya loyalty was a notch higher than his ego or personal insults. Here is what he says —

Uniting with thy brothers (the Pandavas) enjoy all objects of enjoyment. O king, I do not say this to you from cowardice, nor for the sake of wealth. I am enjoying the wealth that Bhishma gave me and not thou,, O best of kings. I do not desire, O king, to have from thee my means of sustenance. Where Bhishma is,there Drona must be.

You see, neither his love for Arjuna and Pandava nor abuses by Karna dettered him from his loyalty.

Conclusion

  • Bhisma never forbid Karna from participation.
  • Karna refused out of his ego and lust for glory.
  • In a similar ego situation warrior like Dronacharya showed their loyalty.
