If Arjuna never used Pashupatastra, then why did he need it in the first place?

📅 May 17, 2020 09:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

I would like to add a few points to the brilliant answer by Lakshmi Telidevara —

  1. Remember Aug 6 and 9 of the year 1945. USA launched nuclear strike against two Japanese cities. That was the first and the only attack of its kind to date. Now no one thinks of using Nuclear weapons against each other. Yet many countries have invest fortune in nuclear weapons. Why? Is this a contradiction? No it is a detterent. The existence of a nuclear weapon on both sides ensures no one uses it against each other.
  2. Unfortunately, divyastras were more potent weapons and multiple irresponsible warriors, too possessed them at that time. How do you ensure that they don’t fire their weapon or in the worst case their weapons can be countered and subdued? Of course by possessing a much potent weapon.
  3. Only very few and very brave remember — With great power comes great responsibility. That's the reason obtaining Pashupatastra was no easy job. Arjuna did severe tapa to prove his worth. Tapa was not to please Shiva, it was to satisfy that person is worthy of wielding it. And what does worthy mean — That he will not misuse it. And Arjuna proved his worth.
  4. Arjuna could have countered Naryanastra or Indrastra with the greatest weapon of all the time. But did he?
  5. If someone achieves a weapon or any divine gift without real hard work, they use it with little responsibility or respect. They may easily barter their gift for something else or may use it to solve their minor problem that they may face. Such people feel powerful only because of the weapon they have or shield they have. Once that weapon or that shield is gone they become vernurable.
  6. Arjuna possessed the Divyastra, as a proof of his capability, as a detterent, as a certificate of him being a great warrior — Because he was a great and responsible warrior Shiva granted him Pashupatastra, he didn’t become a great warrior because of it.

To sum up —

Because Shiva knew, that Arjuna wouldn’t misuse Pashupatastra, he blessed him. It was never meant to be invoked.
