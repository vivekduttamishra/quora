Would you have loved Karna if he had not fought for Duryodhana?

📅 Sep 30, 2020 11:35 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi, Thanks for the A2A.

First of all, nothing would make me love an evil who laughs and attempt to molest a woman. Appreciation is another thing.

And yes, I wouldn’t have appreciated Karna for ditching Duryodhana. But surely, I would have appreciated him for doing something for the betterment of the society rather than acting on selfish interest. Didn’t Arjuna gave up his weapons, forgoing his selfish interest?

And I would have still appreciated if Karna had truly refused Krishna for the sake of Duryodhana and Duryodhana alone. But as you can see, even this was not the case.

Did Karna refused Krishna only because of Duryodhana’s interest?

  • He proactively spared the life of 4 pandavas specifically
    • Yudhishthir who was his main rival to the throne.
    • Bhima who is his personal rival and had sworn to kill all Kaurva brothers. Incidently he fulfilled his oath.
    • Karna’s oath didn’t even allow him to protect his best friend and his brothers. Not that if he had tried, he would have succeeded. But intention matters.
    • Also note, Kunti hadn’t even asked for sparing his sons lives, although she naturally gladly hold Karna to his promise.
  • He refused to participate the battle till Bhisma is in command.
    • This was certainly not a descision in favour of Duryodhana.
    • The explicit reason was, Karna felt his valours credit would go to Bhisma. He was running after personal ego and credit rather than Duryodhana.

Why did Karna refused to Krishna’s proposal?

There were several reasons for it —

  • He owed Duryodhana, and wanted to loyal to his friend. Duryodhana had counted on Karna when he declared a war against Pandava and ditching him at this stage would have been a treachary. This is probably the reason fo this question.
    • As a good friend, Karna should have counseled his friend to the right path and preventing his disaster.
    • But in his defense, he did what he thought is best.
  • He was concious about his image. What would the world think, if he switched sides at this stage? Won’t people talk bad about him?
    • Again this is not a bad thing. Many a times we avoid doing bad because of our image in other’s eyes.
  • The most important reason, however, was his life long ambition to fight Arjuna and prove that he is a better warrior. Accepting Krishna’s proposal would have taken that ambition away forever. Now why do I say that was the most important reason, it was for this single goal —
    • Karna wanted Brahmastra in the first place.
    • Karna lied to Parshuram
    • Karna challenged Arjuna in the arena
    • Karna accepted the kingdom of Anga
    • Karna asked Indra for Vasvi Shakti
    • Karna spared the lives of 4 pandavas but not Arjuna.
