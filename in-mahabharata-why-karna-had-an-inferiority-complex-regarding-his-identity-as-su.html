In Mahabharata why Karna had an inferiority complex regarding his identity as suta putra (while Sutas are closest to the king) but on the other hand Krishna always proudly associated himself with the cattle herders lineage?

📅 Feb 06, 2021 10:28 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

In Mahabharata, the reason of Karna’s inferiority complex wasn’t his identity as a suta.

Let us first understand that there was no taboo associated with suta (or any varna) in Mahabharata period. People were respected for who they were and given proper value —

  • Satyavati, a fisherwoman, was the queen of Hastinapur.
  • Ved Vyas, the son of fisherwoman, was the renouned rishi.
  • Vidur, the son of a maid servant the Prime Minister of Hastinapur.
  • Keechak, a suta, was the general of Matasya Army
  • Salya, a kshatriya, agreed to become charioteer to Karna, a suta.
  • Krishna himself take up the job of a charioteer.

And there is no mention of humiliation because of the varna to any of those people. So there was no reason Karna should have been humiliated for his varna. And he wasn’t

  • Karna was taught by Dronacharya, Kripacharya knowing he was a suta.
  • Parshurama cursed him, not because he was a suta, but because he was deceitful.
  • He was crowned as the king of Anga and remained as a King all his life. Does this show inferiority complex?
  • Karna was a respected member of Hastinapur court who was accorded a place next to Duryodhana.
  • Karna was made commander-in-chief of Kaurava’s army with no one objecting.

WASN’T KARNA HUMILIATED BY BHIM AND KRIPACHARYA FOR HIS VARNA DURING PLAY ARENA WHERE KARNA CHALLENGED ARJUNA?

We must understand the context to pass on such a verdict which is reflective to the entire society.

1.It was a restricted ceromony arranged to showcase the learnings of Dronacharya’s students, mostly the princes of Hastinapur. It wasn’t a public or open even as clarified in Mahabharata.

Drona spoke to King Dhritarashtra, lord of men, in the presence of Kripa, Somadatta, Bahlika, the wise son of Ganga,1 Vyasa and Vidura, “O king! O supreme among the Kurus! The princes have now completed their learning. O king! With your permission, let them now display what they have learned.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5121-5123). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

2.Karna gate crashes the event and demands to showcase his skills. Despite of being a restricted even Dronacharya graciously allows him to show his skills. This proves no biased.

Before the eyes of these people, I will perform feats that will surpass everything that you have done. Don’t be too amazed at what you have done.” O supreme among those who are eloquent! No sooner had he uttered these words, than the spectators quickly stood up all at once, as if raised up by a single machine. O tiger among men! Duryodhana was greatly delighted. Bibhatsu was suddenly filled with anger and a sense of disgrace.

‘With Drona’s permission, Karna, always eager to do battle and immensely strong, exhibited all that Partha had displayed before. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Duryodhana and his brothers delightedly embraced Karna and said, “O mighty-armed hero! Welcome. Good fortune has brought you here.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5199-5204). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Side Note: Kauravas welcome Karna even before his so called humilation.

3.Then Karna challenges Arjuna for a duel. It was uncalled for and it was not the occassion for such a demand. Naturally Kripacharya intervened.

Kripa, well versed in all aspects of dharma and skilled in the rules of duels, said, “This son of Pandu is Kunti’s youngest son. He is a Kuru and will fight a duel with you. O mighty-armed hero! You should also tell us your mother, father and lineage and the royal dynasty of which you are the ornament. On knowing this, Partha will fight with you. Or he may not fight. ”22 At these words, Karna’s face was flushed with shame. It looked as if a lotus had been faded and torn by showers of rain.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5220-5224). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Karna was challenging a prince for a duel. Kripacharya asks his identity. Note he asks about his father, mother, leneage and royalty. He doesn’t ask his varna.
  • Kripacharya reminded Karna the protocol of duel and not his varna.
  • Why did Karna flushed with shame? Was that inferiority complex? It is not clear. But most likely it is because he didn’t have the answer to this question — Why should Arjuna duel with him?

4.Duryodhana crowns him as the King, to ensure he fights with Arjuna which was in line with his long standing ambition. They were already good friends from Guru Drona’s gurukul. He appoined him as the King.

5.Now Bhima reacts and his reaction includes reference to his lineage (again not varna)

“O son of a charioteer! You don’t have the right to be killed by Partha in battle. You had better take up a whip, more befitting of your lineage. O worst of men! You have no right to enjoy the kingdom of Anga, just as a dog has no right to eat the cake that is offered at a sacrificial fire.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5237-5239). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Bhim refers to his lineage. Lineage is not same as varna. Lineage is genology and is specific to a family.
  • Bhima was not doubt abusive. But we must understand his reaction was due to
    • Uncalled for intrusion and unjust demand of Karna
    • Duryodhana’s act of handing over the kingship of Anga to Karna. Its not out of place to mention that Anga was won by Bhima’s father Pandu and Duryodhana misused his powers to hand it to someone who never proved his worth to be a king.
  • Bhima abuse is directed to person Karna and not to any varna.
    • He calls him worst of men. Remember, he conspired with Duryodhana on several occasion to kill Pandavas and specifically him.
  • Many say Bhima called him a Dog. Those people don’t understand the meaning of simily and metaphors. Read carefully, Bhima called him unworthy for being a King in the same way as a Dog is unworthy of consuming scrificial rice cake. Now some animal activists may also blame Bhima for being animal hater and may ask why Dog doesn’t have the right!

There is no other mention in the epic that Karna was abused for his varna.

HAD KARNA INFERIORITY COMPLEX?

Without doubt YES. This is explicitly mentioned in Mahabharata. The first and most undeniable symptom of inferiority complex is jealousy. You are jealous of someone because he has something and you have not.

Other princes also came to Drona, supreme among Brahmanas, to learn the use of arms—the Vrishnis, the Andhakas, kings from many countries and Radheya, 344 the son of the suta. They made Drona their preceptor. The suta’s son was envious of Partha and always competed with him. With Duryodhana’s support, he showed his contempt for the Pandavas.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5034-5036). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

But clearly Arjuna was the champion of gurukul. The guru grants him Brahmsira astra. Karna too wants the same weapon. Why? Here is the reason.

He (Karna) saw that Dhananjaya was superior to everyone in learning about dhanurveda. Karna secretly went to Drona and spoke these words. ‘I wish to know about brahmastra and the secrets of releasing and withdrawing it. It is my view that I should become Arjuna’s equal in battle. It is certain that the affection you bear towards your disciples is equal to what you bear towards your son. Because of your favours, make me accomplished and skilled in the use of weapons.’ Drona was partial towards Phalguna. He also knew about Karna’s wickedness. Having been thus addressed by Karna, he replied, ‘The brahmastra can only be known by a brahmana who is observant of the vows, or by a kshatriya who has performed austerities, and by no one else.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2349-2353). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Karna realized Dhananjaya was superior to everyone.
  • He approaches guru because he wants to become Arjuna’s equal in battle. He knows he isn’t equal. That is inferiority complex.
  • While in gurukul, he is still eyeing for a war. Why?
  • Drona was partial towards Arjuna his best desciple who saved his life. He saw Karna was wicked. And wickedness is another sign of inferiority complex.

Karna goes to Parshurama. Here is exhibits his wickedness more clearly than before. He announces himself as Bhargava Brahmin — same gotra as Parshuram. Naturally on knowing the deceit Parshurama expelled him from his gurukul.

He constantly competes Arjuna, trying to prove himself better than Arjuna. He wanted to prove himself, because he felt himself inferior and need to prove himself.

Here onward everything Karna did was because of his inferiority complex that turned into jealousy and rage —

  • He tries to burn Draupadi after swyamvara, having failed to win her.
  • He abuses her during the dice game.
  • He wanted to mock Pandavas in exile.
  • He boasts how he can vanquish Arjuna but could never do.

Another proof of his inferiority complex is his constant act of fleeing from battle after battle —

  • He retreated from Arjuna after swyamvara battle — scared.
  • He fled from Gandharvas.
  • He fled multiple times is Matasya battle before he was finally vanquished by Arjuna.

He asked Indra for vasvishakti in exchange of his divine kavach and Kundala. What was the reason he gave there?

Karna replied, “I wish to kill only one enemy37 in a great battle. He roars and blazes and is the source of fear for me.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8554-8555). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.


WHY KRISHNA HAD NO INFERIORITY COMPLEX

As already mentioned Varna wasn’t a source for complex — inferiority or superiority. Krishna was a man with divinity. He never needed to compete with anyone. There wasn’t anyone superior to him. There was no reason for inferiority complex.


Summary

  • There is no mention for Varna system working against Karna. In fact he was made a king, and was well regarded in the court of Hastinapur and was never denied an opportunity as a warrior.
  • The root cause of Karna’s inferiority complex was Arjuna, who was always superior no only in others opinion, but also in Karna’s personal opinion.
