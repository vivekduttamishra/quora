In Hinduism who are the 330 million gods referred by many today, are these all the devas included, if so who else are included apart from devas, trimurthi and Devi?

📅 Aug 19, 2020 11:23 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I have heard the modern interpretation that in 33 koti, it refers to 33 types and not 33 crores.

This however is a misconception caused by those Hindu's who feel embarrassed by the idea of so many Gods or fear being ridiculed by those following other faiths.

But those who understand the true essence of Hindu Sanatan philosophy would not be wayled by such ridiculous claims — how is 33 types better than 33 crore? Where do you fit devis in it? What about Indra, Ganesh or Kartikeya?

Sanatan philosophy knows there is on God — Adwait (without any equal) , Anadi(without origin) , Anant (or end) , Sarvagya(all knowing) , and Sarvavyapak (all prevailing)

He isn't one person or at one place — rather he exists everywhere — सर्वव्यापी महेश्वर: — in living and in non living, in trees, in river, in animals and even in stones, in sun, moon and planets, in existence and even in non existence — there isn't a thing where he doesn't prevail.

Realizing the presence, we worshipped God everywhere, and in everything — thus such a huge number.

But I must say 33 crore is an old census. Today we have infinite Gods for there isn't a thing devoid of him.
