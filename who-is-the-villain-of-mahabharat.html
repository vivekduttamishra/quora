Who is the villain of Mahabharat?

📅 Mar 11, 2020 10:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

First, I must thank Shubham Singh for inviting me to answer this question.

Next I would thank Pratik Bhake (प्रतिक भाके) whose answer I am going to partial refute and he expected no less when he says “I know nobody agrees with me” As the answer is controversial, as expected, there are many upvotes.

See the main problem in the premise of Mr Pratik’s theory is the assumption he added as the last line — Karna, a Hero.

Vyasji never mentioned Karna as a hero, a powerful archer may be, but not a hero. And when you want to make someone hero out of turn, you need to make adjustments and one of these adjustments was making a villain out of Krishna. Controversy today surely gets you vote.

Few assumptions in most of the existing answers are —

  • Mahabharat was just about a war for the throne of Hastinapur or about some Draupadi (victim) or some Karna (a hero) or some villain. Now based on your analysis the villains might be —
    • Duryodhana
    • Shakuni
    • Satyavati
    • Krishna
    • Kunti
    • (list goes on — but note assumption remains same)
  • War must be bad. As Mr Pratik mentions, Krishna could have prevented the war. Probably you forgot Krishna tried to prevent the war, and he approached Hastinapur with the final peace proposal. While some may argue that his efforts may be a facade and not sincere, it was an effort nevertheless. Same you can’t be said about Karna — who never once tried to avert the war in entire epic — infact he always encouraged.
  • Krisna could have prevented so many things like averting the great gamble. He is also accused of manipulating the poor Karna. Question is was Karna manipulated? Did Karna sided the right side? Most important question manipulating an enemy is strategy or villany?

Ok enough of preface. So who was the greatest villain of Mahabharata —

In my understanding, No single individual or group that can be condemned as the villain of a Mahabharat.

Krishna declared the battle of Mahabharat as Dharmayudha — a battle to establish Dharma — and in Gita he asked Arjun to take part in war as a duty. It does not differ from the Indian army goes to war to defend its territory.

Thus war isn’t evil and you can’t make Krishna a villain for not stopping the war.

So who was the main villain?

The collapsing social order was the main villain of the time.

It was a social order that —

  • Considered gambling your kingdom and family an allowable conduct.
  • That considered wives as property.
  • That can remain moot spectate on disrobing of a woman in public.
  • When a puppet (to his son’s whim) could be a King.
  • Where a disciple (Drishtduymna) aspires to kill his own guru (Drona)

I must insist it was not about an individual, it was about a collapsing society that needed a much wider correction that a small patch like preventing a single gamble a day and it would take place some other day.
