What is the role of Barbrik ji in Mhabharta?

📅 Jul 08, 2020 11:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Barbarik plays a very important role, not only in Mahabharat but in our life as it gives many important lessons —

  1. If you have power people take note of you and it doesn't matter if you have any vision, value or morality
    1. Barbarik vows to always support the weak.
    2. Now who is weak?
    3. Is it sufficient criteria to use your power.
    4. What if evil is weak? Should we support it.
  2. If you market yourself well, people believe you no matter what you say.
    1. Barbarik announced he had three infallible arrows and many hailed him greatest, without reasoning.
    2. Was his arrows really infallible? We will find out soon.
    3. Was arrows given by some local diety more powerful than Indrastra, Brahnmstra or Pashupatastra?
    4. Can you be powerful just because of a weapon.

Now coming to the two most important lessons.

No power can harm him, who is in lords protection

You may have heard the story of three infallible arrows of Barbarik — first can mark ones that are to be destroyed, second to mark those that are to be protected and last to finish the task. Lord Krishna asked for a demonstration on a tree. As the arrows hit the mark Krishna put his feet on one of the leaf. And that leaf was destroyed when Krishna removed his feet. What does the story tell? No power can harm you, if you are under lords protection.

And second important lesson — a good story can make even the nonexistent mightier than everyone else —
