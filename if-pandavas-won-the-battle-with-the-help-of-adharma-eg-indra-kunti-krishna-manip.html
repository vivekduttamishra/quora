If Pandavas won the battle with the help of Adharma (e.g. Indra, Kunti, Krishna manipulating and cheating with Karna, etc.), did Dharma actually win in the Mahabharata?

📅 Dec 12, 2020 09:39 AM PST  |  🌐 English

If… is a big word, with potential to make the rest of the question irrelevant, as in the current context. And before I go on to explain why Pandavas didn’t win by Adharma, let us first focus on the basic definition of dharma, which incidiently is another word in this question that has a potential similar to that of if.

Justice vs Law

These two terms, that are closely related, are often confused to be a synonym for each other, which they are not.

Achieving justice is most closely related to the word Dharma, — impartial and just to everyone. It is the foundation of human civilization. But it is an abstract idea that needs to be enforced with a set of rules and protocol. This set of rules and protocol is law. Law is a set of established principle to ensure that justice is served.

However, the laws are often rigid and people can find the loopholes thus subverting justice. Due to the rigidity of the law, it sometimes fails to serve it’s purpose — deliver justice.

Let us take an example of infamous Nirbhaya rape case. One of the brutal rapist got away because he was juvinial —

  • Was justice served? NO.
  • Was law upheld? YES.

Now imagine a shrewd lawyer, would have proved him adult. He would have got the punishment for his crimes. Then, again we have to tackle the same question —

  • Would justice have been served? YES.
  • Would that be considered lawful? NO.

So, now I have two questions —

  1. Here when we are talking about Adharma, are we talking about breaking of law, or justice? Remember they are different.
  2. Was justice delivered? Because if justice is delivered, as Krishna proclaimed, Dharma actually won.

Can we consider the acts of Kunti, Krishna and Indra as Adharma?

In the first part of my answer, I explained the difference between law and justice and try to impress, that weather or now law is followed, justice is served. And justice is Dharma. In this section we would evaluate the acts of Krishna, Kunti and Indra to find out if any law was broken or can be considered Adharma on either parameter.

Krishna

Krishna at the behest of Pandavas approached Duryodhana to avoid a full-fledged war that was to consume a major part of human civilization. Now Duryodhana was guilty of several deciet and conspiracy and he should be punished was the demand fo both law and justice. Yet, Duryodhana refused to buzz.

He refused to honour his part of agreement. By violating the agreement, he violated the law. It was then, Krishna approached Karna, trying to enlist his support, not just for law and for justice but also for preventing approaching apploclyptic war.

Any man of honour should have considered it his duty to see justice and law is served. He should do it without a price attached. Yet, Krishna offered him a price to do his duty. He offered him what was denied to him all his life — truth, hoping that would motivate him to do the what is right for society. But Karna chose his friend over law, over justice and over humanity.

Now tell me —

  • How Krishna’s truth Adharma or a violation of law?
  • How Kirshna’s attempt to prevent a such a devastating war Adharma?
  • Had Karna agreed to Krishna, Duryodhana could have been brought to justice. Would that be considered Adharma?

Kunti

Krishna’s attempt has failed. Karna had refused to follow his advise that was in the benefit of justice, Kunti approached Karna hoping to convience him to switch sides. She appealed — Let those who are evil bow down.

Even his father Surya suggested Karna to follow her advice. Yet Karna refused.

Now would you call it Kunti cheated Karna? How? Can you guess why she had approached him in the first place? It was not protect her five sons, but the sixth one. Even Karna knew that as he himself confessed to Krishna earlier and in all the past incident Karna had not won against Arjuna or any Pandava brother. Not once.

It was not Kunti’s request to spare his five son’s life but Karna own offer, which she certainly accepted. And why should she not?

So my turn to question —

  • How did Kunti cheated Karna?
  • Who did she rob of justice by her act?

Indra

Ah! thats a special case, so I saved it for the last. Indra surely wanted to take away the Kavach from Karna by pretending to be a Brahmin and that would have been cheating. But Surya Dev already informed him about Indra’s plan. And since Karna was aware of the plan, it can’t be considered a cheating.

So, let us first understand, how did Karna got the Kavach on the first place? Kunti fought for the Kavach for him from his father Surya. It was one of a kind, bestowed to him by the merit of Kunti and not on Karna’s own merit. But now Karna was going to subvert justice with the aid of this Kavach, so Indra decided to take the divine blessing, that was not something he had earned.

But Karna, just didn’t give away the Kavach as a charity or because he was deceived. He refused to part with it unless it was an exchange for a weapon. And he got a weapon so powerful that made even Krishna worried.

So now the question here is —

  • Only Karna had a impentrable Kavach. If he was allowed to fight with such a Kavach, won’t that have been injustice or adharma agains the other warrior?
  • If Karna got a great weapon in exchange as per his own wish, how was it Adharma?

I would like to conclude my answer with a wonderful conversastion between Bhishma and Yudhishtir in great poetic tale by nation poet of India, Shri Ramdhari Singh ‘Dinkar’. The poetic tale is titled Kurukshetra. Here after the war, Yudhishthir asks a question which is similar to this question —

Yudhishthir to Bhishma —

जहाँ छल से क्षद्म से गिराए गए वरण्य वीर आपसे।
सुयोधन वध, अभिमन्यु वध, हाए! हम से बचा कौन यहाँ पाप से?

When respectable warrior like you was pinned down by deciet, and then the slaying of Duryodhana and Abhimanyu, it makes me wonder, if anyone is anymore sinless.

As you can see this quesiton closely relates to Yudhisthir’s grief. And my answer would mimic Bhishma’s response —

Bhishma to Yudhishthir —

पापी कौन? मनुष्य से उसका न्याय चुराने वाला?
अथवा न्याय खोजता, विघ्न का शीश उड़ाने वाला?

Who is a sinner? He who snatches justice from someone else, or he who destroys obstacle for the justice.

And that is the million dollar question — Would that be a justice, if the criminals had got scott free? Would that be a justice if war mongers who didn’t hesitate to kill their family for properity roamed free? Think about it.
