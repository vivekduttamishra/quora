What happens if Karna had a charioteer like Vidura or Shakuni instead of Shalya?

📅 Dec 16, 2020 09:11 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

If you are thinking that either would have changed the course of action, it would be a wishful thinking.

You must consider two aspects first —

1. To be a charioteer, you need to have the required skill.

Vidur and Shakuni were not known for the charioteer skills. Karna acknowledged Shalya to be a great charioteer. So if you give you the reign of your horses in the hand of a wise person who doesn’t have the skill, you are inviting the disaster.

2. The role of a charioteer cum advisor

If you consider charioteer to be someone with wisdom, strategy who can act as an advisor, then let me review the two potential candidate for the job profile.

A. Shakuni

Shakuni has been way overrated for his cunning by modern narratives in TVShows and fictions. He wasn’t the mastermind from Kaurava side. Authentic script depicts him as the mastermind of the dice game. He is also shown as a good warrior. But other than that he was just a stooge following Duryodhana in his crimes, more like a Shambha followed Gabbar Singh and on one occassion Gabbar Singh did asked his advise — Kitni golian hain…

Shakuni would have been uneffective either as charioteer or as advisor.

B.Vidur

Vidur was never a warrior which was a pre-requisite to be a charioteer. Yes, you are not a bus driver driving from a national highway. You are the driver of a armored veichle through the war zone. How effective can someone be, without any experience of the war?

And as a advisor, Vidur had been ineffective all his life, as he was trying to explain the virtue of righteousness to people who always sided with Adharama.

Karna had zero tolerance policy for such talks — He attacked Vikarna verbally when the later talked about dharma to protect Druapadi. He always countered Vidur and other advisors of Dhritrashtra whenever they tried to talk sense. What do you think would have happened had Vidur taken the reigns of Karna’s horse in his hands?

Do you think Vidur had become an evil? Do you think he wouldn’t have tried to talk sense to Karna? Do you think Karna would have taken it any differently than he took the advise of Shalya? Don’t you think Karna would have thaought, Vidur is trying to sap his energy?

The point is, Vidur was no charioteer and he was no evil streategist which is evident from his actions.

3. Krishna Factor

Now keeping both the above points aside, when you select any charioteer for Karna — be int Shakuni from modern fables, or an unimaginable situation of Vidur turning to an evil strategist, they would still have to compete with Krishna.

  • Shakuni is cunning from the fables, but Krishna was the greatest strategist and master mind.
  • Vidur had the knowledge of Dharma, Krishna is the very definition of Dharama.
  • Krishna wasn’t just a charioteer, or a strategist, he was the greatest warrior of the era, whose reputation as a warrior echoes through out the book. With such a war veteran at the helm who would have competed him.
  • Krishna was on a mission —

Yada yada hi dharmasya glanirbhavati bharata
Abhythanamadharmasya tadatmanam srijamyaham
Paritranaya sadhunang vinashay cha dushkritam
Dharmasangsthapanarthay sambhabami yuge yuge

Now think, who had the capacity or dare to stand between Krishna and his mission?
