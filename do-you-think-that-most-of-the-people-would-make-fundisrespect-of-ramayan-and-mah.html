Hi, Thanks for A2A.

I would disagree with the term most and I would refrain from blaming social media alone. It includes the likes of you and I as well, who feel hurt at abusive language and expression against our grand heritage.

Social media is just a reflection of the social views. Unfortunately there are several under currents I see as the real cause of this problem.

  1. Most people haven't read anything close to the authentic original text. They are easily influenced by not only rumor mongers but even elites hell bent in distorted interpretation. Such attempts and texts have existed for much longer than social media itself. TV shows and scholarly literature and painters like MFH all contributed to it.
  2. Our history was distorted badly by Britishers like Lord Macaulay and Mughals like Bakthiar Khilji who burnt entire library of Vikramshika University.
  3. It has become fashionable to condemn Indian Epics, Indian History and India's national icons like Gandhiji. So people feel proud to condemn them openly.

Unfortunately very often we too unknowingly support them —

  1. We accepted their demarcation of Shruti (श्रुति) and smriti (स्मृति) as divider of our knowledge bank rather than complementary to each other. We often join them in condemning —
    1. Smriti as less authentic and valuable then Shruti, while they should be considered complementary.
    2. Utterkand as interpolation. This again is the direct effect of not understanding the fact that Ramayan too is a Shruti.
  2. We often try to defend every single sloka of our written epic, without realizing that this is against the basic idea of Shruti. It often gives ammunition to critic to condemn entire epic. Our focus should be on the message. When knowledge passes as Shruti, it is bound to have certain interpolation. We needed neither defend it in entirity or throw it away (like many do to Utterkand)
  3. Most important — we engage with those stupids to counter them, in turn, encourage them to be even more stupid. (I, too, am guilty of this) Yes this is frustrating.
