What happens if Karna did not fight in the Kurukshetra War?

📅 Dec 21, 2020 12:23 AM PST  |  🌐 English

What was the Karna effect?

Sometime a mirage or an illusion becomes so powerful that it eclipses the reality, and people ignore what is front of them and stick to those illusions. Karna was that illusion for Duryodhana.

For Duryodhana, Karna was that desert mirage. He couldn’t see failures after failure, ignored all the advice by the well-wishers like Bhishma and Drona and gambled his kingdom, his life and the life of his brothers on one factor — Karna.

His mirage was broken on the 14th day of the war with the death of Jaidradh. It was a little too late, too late to back off. But essentially it was a mirage broken.

To answer this question let us first change the question slightly and ask —

What happened, when Karna fought in Kurukshetra war? Did it change the fate of the war? Did he win the war for Kauravas? No.

Kauravas were evil and were destined to loose, no matter what. They had 4 acchohini in excess and had warriors like Bhishma and Drona. But they were not supposed to win. And the last hope of Kauravas were lost with the fall of Bhishma on the 10th day of the battle. It was Bhishma who was so ferocious that Krishna had to take up arms against his original resolve and motivate Arjuna to fight.

Karna entered the battle on 11th day.

So, with or without Karna battles final outcome would have remained the same.

We must realize that only warrior of repute that Karna vanquished was Ghatotkatch, that, too, with Vasvi Shakti. All he manged thereafter was to massacre ordinary soldiers. He couldn’t stop Bhima from annihaliating all the Kaurava brother or Arjuna from vanquishing Jaidradh or even his own sons or brothers.

What if Karna had not participated in the war?

Kauravas may have won, without actually fighting. Duryodhana may not have dared to wage a war with that illusion of Karna’s power, he was holding on to. He may have agreed to Krishnas proposal and war, that day would have been avoided. And an evil like Duryodhana might have gone scott free of his crime, till such a time, he wicket mind or that of his advisors construed another conspiracy.

Even if they would have fought without Karna, they would be a weaker without him and a lot of innocent lives would have been saved that were killed during the war.

So the result would have been much better for Kaurvas and many other soldiers who died on both sides. Pandavas who were not war mongers would have been on the loosing side but would still have been happier to avert the full fledged devastation of the human race.

Krishna would have found another way to bring the killers to justice.

So without Karna, the Kauravas may have ended with slightly better luck, but there would have been lot less massacre than what happened in the war.
