Why was evil Parshurama jealous of Karna and destroyed his life by not giving his axe?

📅 Jun 22, 2020 08:46 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question is insincere —

  1. The questioner knows the fact and as such raised it anonymously.
  2. Calls Parshuram, the sixth incarnate of Vishnu an evil.

But nevertheless it's an interesting question. Even foulest intention may give birth to something useful.

This question is also disrespectful to Karna, in whose favor, the question is asked as if Karna was nothing without a particular weapon. The logic is simple — If you are nothing without a particular weapon, you yourself is worthless, and if you are worthless no weapon can help you.

Did Parshuram destroyed Karna by not giving his axe?

  • Why would Parshuram give away his axe (Parshu) to Karna? Did he ever ask for it?
  • How would an archer like Karna use this weapon?
  • He did give him many other potent warfare knowledge. Did they work for him?
  • Karna wasn't his first disciple. By that logic he may have parted with this weapon long before Karna arrived in the scene.

Parshu vs Divyastra

Shiva gave Arjuna Pashupatastra but not his Trishul? Why? To destroy him? Krishna never offered his Sudarshan Chakra to his favorite Arjuna or anyone else. Arjuna didn't gave away his Gandiv to Satyaki. Is there a common cord?

  • We must understand there is a fundamental difference between these physical weapons like Parshu, Trishul and Chakra and Divyastra like Brahmastra and Narayanastra — similar to hardware and software.
  • Divyastra isn't some nuclear weapon, nor are they some physical hardware. The are invoked by right mantras that manifest them. Giving someone Divyastra is essentially giving them a special knowledge. And same knowledge can be granted to many people. It's like a software that powers the hardware.
  • Trishul, Chakra, Gandiva and Parshu, represented physical or hardware part of the system like a hardware that can channel the software (Divyastra) Shiva may invoke Pashupatastra using Trishul and Arjuna using Gandiva.
  • Parshu is great in Ram's hand else it's a piece of Axe.

Was Parshuram evil?

  • Parshuram is 6th Vishnu and is revered in hindu culture. No one ever called Bhagwan Parshuram an evil.
  • He didn't hate Kshatriya, he destroyed evil, incidently they were Kshatriya. But he did bless Ram and Bhishma despite of both being Kshatriya.
  • He was a guru and it's duty of Guru to punishing those who make mistake. His curse to Karna was a lesson, alas! Karna never really learnt it.
