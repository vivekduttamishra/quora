Was Eklavya's nature rude? Is this the reason why Acharya Drona didn't let him teach Dhanurvidya?

📅 Apr 24, 2020 10:30 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

There are two parts of this question, and I believe the two are not completely related.

Was Eklavya’s nature rude?

I am not sure. As a kid probably no.

Then why did Drona refused him to teach?

This is an important question. So, let me start by re-emphasizing that the two aren’t necessarily related.

You don’t reject a student only on criteria of being or not being rude. There are other factors for rejecting a student.

Consider this — many students fail to get admission to India’s current premium institutes like IIT and IIM. Are those rejected rude?

Most of the institutions not only conducts written tests, but also interviews. Written tests validate candidates for academic background and IQ. I know many bright students who cleared IAS mains, but failed at interview. What is the use of interviews?

The interviews checks the overall attitude of the candidate. Why don’t we test it also using some test paper? Some series of Objective questions?

You can learn the objective answers and take up the training to appear polite in front of an interview board. You can even learn the model answers to soft skill question. And their lies the capabilities of a Guru. He doesn’t just concentrate on the spoken word, but he looks in the eyes, he gauzes the vibration in the sound and knows the right candidate.

Now instead of IIM and IIT, consider national defence service or institutions dealing with nuclear material. Now that the risk is high, room for selector taking chance is low.

We see both Drona and Parshuram selecting and rejecting students. And a closer look will reveal contrary to a popular propaganda it wasn’t birth based — Parshuram took Bhishma of Kshatriya clan, Drona took Karna a known Suta.

Is it so difficult to digest that the Guru had the basic experience and vision to identify and reject a young candidate who failed to convince him of his merit despite of being a promising archer and not rude?

If you are still not convienced you may look where future took Eklavya and you may have an answer.
