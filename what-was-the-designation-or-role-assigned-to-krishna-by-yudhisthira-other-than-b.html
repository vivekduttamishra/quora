What was the designation or role assigned to Krishna by Yudhisthira other than being the chariotereer of Arjuna in the Mahabharata War?

Few people are born leaders. Their persona simply commands trust, honor and people follow. Period. They don’t need any position to exert their command, for even the leader and people on position look forward to them for their approval and simply follow them. That is their aura, which is most often not mystical. It is based on their strength of character, and past track records.

A more recent example would be Mahatma Gandhi. He held no public position after 1934. Yet from the Indian leaders to Britishers everyone looked forward to him not just for guidelines, but also for endorsement of their official stand. And that is the reason few criticize him for his decisions. While that may be a matter of a different debate, what is relevant here that he was taking decisions despite of having no designation.

Krishna having no official position in Pandava’s camp was the de facto strategist and guide throughout the war.

And it would be incorrect to assume that he reached their simply because people considered him God. He had a track record as a successful general, strategists and administrator apart from being an invincible warrior. It was his brilliance that made people realize he is God. Arjuna himself, says in Gita that he often mistook Krishna just as his friend and not some divinity. That was till he experienced his Virata swaroop.

sakheti matvā prasabhaṁ yad uktaṁ
he kṛiṣhṇa he yādava he sakheti
ajānatā mahimānaṁ tavedaṁ
mayā pramādāt praṇayena vāpi [41]
yach chāvahāsārtham asat-kṛito ’si
vihāra-śhayyāsana-bhojaneṣhu
eko ’tha vāpy achyuta tat-samakṣhaṁ
tat kṣhāmaye tvām aham aprameyam [42]

pitāsi lokasya charācharasya
tvam asya pūjyaśh cha gurur garīyān
na tvat-samo ’sty abhyadhikaḥ kuto ’nyo
loka-traye ’py apratima-prabhāva[43]

tasmāt praṇamya praṇidhāya kāyaṁ
prasādaye tvām aham īśham īḍyam
piteva putrasya sakheva sakhyuḥ
priyaḥ priyāyārhasi deva soḍhum [44]

—Bhagwat Gita Chapter 11 (Sloka 41–44)

Thinking of you as my friend, I presumptuously addressed you as, “O Krishna,” “O Yadav,” “O my dear mate.” I was ignorant of your majesty, showing negligence and undue affection. And if, jestfully, I treated you with disrespect, while playing, resting, sitting, eating, when alone, or before others—for all that I crave forgiveness.

You are the father of the entire universe, of all moving and non-moving beings. You are the most deserving of worship and the supreme spiritual master. When there is none equal to you in all the three worlds, then who can possibly be greater than you, O possessor of incomparable power?

Therefore, O adorable Lord, bowing deeply and prostrating before you, I implore you for your grace. As a father tolerates his son, a friend forgives his friend, and a lover pardons the beloved, please forgive me for my offences.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkanth Edition of Mahabharat (Geeta Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
