The Mahabharata War cannot be a feud just between a family in North India, as the name suggests it may have involved most of the great kingdoms of Bharat. Do you agree?

📅 Jun 25, 2022 07:01 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

And yes, I agree. The great war of Mahabharata wasn’t a family feud between two families; Krishna won’t have persuaded Arjuna to fight a family feud alone. He won’t have proclaimed that he manifested to protect good and destroy evils. Perhaps with a narrow perspective, we have lost the epic.

And that was my motivation for authoring my Amazon#1 best-seller epic fiction series The Lost Epic, that started with book#1 detailing the journey of The Accursed God. You may want to check out the series.

But to understand why it can’t be just a family feud, ask yourself a simple question — Would there have been no war if we remove Kauravas and Pandavas from the equation? Why everyone else jumped into the battle? Do you think the Drupada would have forgiven Dronacharya for his insult? Or do you think Satyaki and Bhurisrava wouldn’t have settled their scores? Or do you think mindless Karna would have let go of his ambition to prove himself better than Arjuna? If you analyse the whole scenario you would find there were a lot of small battles which were collated to become the epic war.

Then there were several more underlying scenario and you need to uncover before you reach to the depth of Kurukshetra war.
