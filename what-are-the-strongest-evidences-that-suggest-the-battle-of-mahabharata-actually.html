What are the strongest evidences that suggest the battle of Mahabharata actually happened?

📅 Mar 10, 2020 12:15 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I won’t try to answer this question in its current format — I won't give you scientific evidence. There are several well-written answer going after those scientific evidences — no point in adding another replication.

The problem in going after such a meaningless debate is some on will drag you deeper to a point to find a contradiction and then go on proving it never existed.

Take example one of the highly voted answer by Madhur Sharma — his sole basis of disproving the time line is archeology survey and Indus valley civilization and he dates it to 1000 BC. This question raises two simple questions —

  1. What existed before 1000 BC?
  2. There was a time when Indus valley was not discovered. What was the belief of archelogist about the most ancient period civilization existed? They based in to few hundered BC. So isn’t the simplest explanation is we haven’t dug deep enough to explore beyond 1000BC
  3. Civilizations are born on the ruins of a more ancient civilization and that is also a recurring theme and wisdom of our epic.

I can quote discovery of submerged Ram Setu and Dwarka that more than conclusively prove the civilization beyond 1000 years. But that's not the point.

Scientific evidences are not sufficient to prove for the authenticity of grand epics like Mahabharat and Ramayan — they keep correcting themselves and haven't reached a time to sufficiently prove anything.

To understand what kind of evidence, we need, we need to understand what value they carry. Mahabharat and Ramayan like grand epics are like beacons — they are guiding light of wisdom. And those wisdom are sufficient evidence to prove their reality — archaeology and other scientific methods will just reconfirm the same well established reality and till the time they fail (and the keep failing), you need to give them time to get mature.

On a related note, and as an observation to another very highly voted answer, I feel one problem thats the root of all these problem is —

There are two groups, one that want to prove everything wrong, the other trying to prove everything right — truth as naturally balancing force lies in between. No writing can ever be without a Biase for that is a natural human trait and we are talking about writings that have been appended and expanded over last 5000 years. And yes here I would like to emphasize that our epics have indeed undergone a lot of changes over 1000s of years and there is nothing to be defensive about it — it should be a matter of pride. It follows the Krishna’s principle Yada yada hi Dharmasya…

Indian epics are pre-historic and dates back to a period much longer than scientific inventions like mass printing press. We propagated the knowledge by word of mouth for centuries before it was written and there are several evidence to suggest. But what makes it great is we adapted it to remain relevant even after so many thousands of years and few other pieces of wisdom known to humanity can claim that.

Unfortunately some of us try to defend every word of the epic is against the key spirit of the epic — that is when we try to seek evidence rather than relevance.
