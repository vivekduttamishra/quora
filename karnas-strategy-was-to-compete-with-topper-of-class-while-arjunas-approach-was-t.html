Thanks for the A2A.

Not only did I apply this approach, but as a trainer I saw many people going on both part and I can say as an expert — not only Arjuna’s approach bring out best of skills in you, it also makes you a better person.

Every person in this world is unique in his own way and it is their duty to sculpt out the best self they can be — that is the crux of human existence.

Following others can make you follower and in the process you lose your own identity and sometimes even your soul. The best you can hope to achieve is becoming someone’s duplicate, still kill yourself in the process.

When you compare yourself with others, not only you are forgoing the best possibility you had of your own, but also inviting frustrations and high expectations which brings on further stress to you.

I wasn’t my school topper. But I can say after years that the toppers who were and are my great friends after all these years, still respect me for what I am today and the respect is mutual. A class rank or a novel skill is of infinitely lesser significance than the human values. In comparing with other you lose the human being you were suppose to be.
