Were all the Pandavas 100 % perfect as we get to see?

📅 Apr 17, 2020 04:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I don't see any depiction of Pandavas as 100% perfect. No where it is either mentioned or depicted. And this is the beauty of our epic over history. We dared to point out imperfection.

A hero is not someone without imperfection, he is a hero inspite of his imperfection. Our epic allows questioning even heros including Great Ram.

Moreover perfection is not an objective attribute to be measured in percentage. It is a subjective idea based on interpretation.

Consider following points —

  1. Arjuna refused to fight the war, and Krishna had counsel him? Bhima was determined. Who was perfect? Only one can be. Right?
  2. Arjuna, after the counsel, fought. When was he more perfect — before or after?

So no. No one was perfect — just better among the lot — and better by a large margin. That's why Lord Krishna was with them. He made them even better.

That said, there is another dimension to this discussion that we must consider.

Just like perfection, even justice is neither objective nor universal. And that's why society is governed by law and not justice.

We often tend to seek justification for a 5000 year old event or actions with contemporary perspective and that would lead to flawed conclusions.

We can't justify or censor polygamy based on current law or tradition.

Let's see few more points —

  1. Arjuna burnt Khandav Van — Currently era after thousands of years of deforestation, global warming and environmental crisis would find it brutal and punishable. But what about 5000 years ago where history and archeology haven't even reached? Are we sure that was an terrible act then?
  2. Killing deer and lion is punishable offense today, but killing chicken and goat is not. Why? Are we sure lions are deers were not over populated 5000 years ago to make them a fair game?
  3. Today chickens are culled in 1000s even if one of them is infected by bird flu, and no one raise an eyebrow. Why? Because their infection may spread in humans. Why do we think clearing a forest and wild animals near a civilization was a crime then? How do you think any city of the world grew? On a barren land?
  4. Grambling is illegal today in parts of the world and legal in other parts. Even today. Is it difficult to visualize that 5000 years ago, it was part of the tradition and kings were bound by the protocol? Remember people debated if Shakuni employed unfair means but no one questioned gamble. Today it makes me furious and height of absurdity. But then I live in a different time. Don't we?
  5. At certain point we need to understand the definition of ethics as mentioned in the epic and can't judge by applying today's law or morality.
  6. Son of wife was acknowledged as his husband's son even if not biological. What is current law about it?

Things that were questionable even then —

  • Polyandry — Draupadi marrying 5 men. Everyone opposed it. But then the law was consulted even then and they managed to find some reference. Can we call Pandavas imperfect for this?

To conclude they weren't perfect, but perfection can't be judged based on contemporary standards.
