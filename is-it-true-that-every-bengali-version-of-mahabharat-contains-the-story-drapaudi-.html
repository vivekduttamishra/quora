Is it true that every Bengali version of Mahabharat contains the story Drapaudi rejected Karna for being Suta? Is it true that Karna belonged from lower class according to V.S. Suthankar and Iravati Karwe?

📅 Mar 22, 2021 10:07 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

No.

As per prof Sukthankar, the interpolated story of Draupadi rejecting Karna is missing from entire Bengali edition. In fact it is present in only 5 out of 1259 edition including famous Neelkantha which also includes the contradiction. Here are the exact words of Sukthankar —

Unfortunately, this melo­dramatic interlude, to judge by the documentary evidence, appears to be the handiwork of a very late Vyāsaīd, as it is found only in K * Ñ2 Dn D2.4. c, that is, one manuscript of the K group, one Nepali manuscript, and three composite Devanāgarī manuscripts, besides the Nīlakaṇṭha version! All of these are late and inferior or conflated manuscripts. It is missing, on the other hand, not only in the Śāradā version and the Southern recension (as in the case of many of the interpolations of the Vulgate ), but for once, also in the entire Bengali version.

— Page LXI, Prologomena of Vishnu S. Suthankar

Prof Sukthankar further and clearly hints that Karna was a respected guest. And the fact that BORI mentions Karna's failure also proves he wasn't considered lower caste. BORI mentions Sutas as a intermix of Brahmin and Kshatriyas and were known to hold prominent positions.
