Was the Mahabharata warrior Karna's Vijaya bow superior to the Arjuna's Gandiva bow?

📅 Nov 10, 2020 06:08 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Hi Thanks for the A2A.

Before I jump into comparison of the two bow, let me tell you why this comparison is pointless?

A bow is a just a device and not a replacement of a warrior. Think like this a balanced bat would help Sachin Tendulkar strike better and make score more runs. The same bat would also work great in the hands of Virat Kohli. But if I play with Tendulkar’s bat, It won’t do any miracle. And it wouldn’t be the bats fault. Let us take this argument a bit further. Tendulkar and Kohli would perform infinitely better than me even if they play with ordinary bat and I play with the greatest bat ever made. What is the lesson here —

An artifact like a bat or a bow if crafted skillfully can enhance the performance of a champion, but —

  1. They are no replacement of champions own calibre.
  2. They won’t perform the same magic in ordinary hands.

Having said that let us now compare the two bows in question —

Vijay Bow —

It was constructed by Vishwakarma and had a proven track records in the hands of Indra and Parshurama. Here is what Karna said about it.

My bow, Vijaya, is supreme among all weapons. Vishvakarma294 constructed it for Indra’s sake. It was with this that Shatakratu vanquished large numbers of daityas. In the ten directions, the daityas were confounded because of its roar. Shakra gave that revered weapon to Bhargava and Bhargava gave that divine and supreme bow to me.295 With this, I will fight against the mighty-armed Arjuna, foremost among victorious ones, like Indra fighting in a battle against all the assembled daityas. Rama296 gave me this terrible bow and it is superior to Gandiva. It was with this bow that he297 conquered the earth twenty-one times. Bhargava told me about this bow’s divine deeds and Rama gave it to me.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 1967-1973).

Now, while the credential of the bow is undeniable in the hands of Indra and Parshurama, there are few things we must consider —

  1. Karna was no Indra or Parshurama, and a bow is just as good as the warrior is.
  2. It is Karna who estimated that Vijaya bow is superior to Gandiva. It is his personal opinion which is often boastful and without any evidence. Consider Karna’s statement just before the above statement —

Savyasachi is not my equal in fighting against great warriors, dexterity, shooting from a distance, skill and the use of weapons.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 1967-1973).

As we see this is Karna’s statement which is completely devoid of evidence or justification. In every single battle, be it Draupadi Swyamvara, Gandharva war and Virata war, Arjuna turned out to be a superior warrior. Yet he boasts being superior to Arjuna. So Karna’s statement can’t be considered as an authority on superiority of weapons.

Gandiva Bow

Arjuna was extremely delighted when be grasped the Gandiva, the divine and supreme of bows, created by Brahma in ancient times.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Location 898).

The bow was created by Brahma, and has been praised though out the epic by various people who have been expert of Weapons. Here is what Shiva himself said as he referred to Gandiva —

The immensely wise Girisha, Shiva the husband of Uma, gave that supreme of men the great bow known as Gandiva, the destroyer of demons and pishachas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 5495-5496). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

But the above two reference do not suggest which one was better. So let us have direct quote from one of the greatest expert of warfare as acknowledged through out the epic — one of the original owners of Vijaya bow — Bhagwan Parshuram. Here is what Parshuram says about Gandiva —

O king! Therefore, discard your vanity and go to Dhananjaya82 before a weapon has been affixed to Gandiva, the best of bows. He possesses Kakudika, Shuka, Naka, Akshisamtarjana, Santana, Nartana, Ghora and Ajyamodaka as the eighth.83 All men who are pierced by these confront their death, or move around insane, or lose their senses and become unconscious, or go to sleep, or jump around, or vomit, or urinate, or incessantly cry and laugh. Partha’s qualities are innumerable and Janardana is superior to him. You have known him as Kunti’s son, Dhananjaya. O great king! But know that Nara and Narayana are Arjuna and Keshava, brave warriors and bulls among men.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 6139-6144).

Now without doubt, Parshuram knows Vijaya bow and his capabilities better than anyone else. And great Parshurama declares Gandiva as the best of bows.

I don’t think, it leaves any further room for debate.

We may also argu that finally the bearer of Gandiva defeated the bearer of Vijay bow and hence Gandiva must be better, but that would be a wrong conclusion, because as I said, a bow can enhance the capability of a great warrior, it can’t make you great.
