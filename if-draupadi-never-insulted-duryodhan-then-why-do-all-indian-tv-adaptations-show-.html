If Draupadi never insulted Duryodhan, then why do all Indian TV adaptations show it? There must have been something, such a huge detail couldn’t have come from thin air.

📅 May 11, 2020 08:30 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Wow what a wonderful argument. To understand the idea, let us rephrase the question —

If Draupadi really insulted Duryodhana as depicted in all TV adaptations, why it fails to find a reference in all ancient versions of Mahabharat — Needkantha edition, KMG, Bori, Geeta Press, etc? Why didn’t they watch the TV show and updated their story?

Well you will be surprised to know that our ancient adapters were not referring to the TV shows, but to an incident that happened over 5000 years ago. And Draupadi, all those years ago didn’t insult Duryodhana. And you will get the answer as by referring to the authentic versions, however, I must admit referring those texts is more laborious than watching a TV show, and posting questions here is easier.

Why can’t huge details come from thin air?

  • Every TV show depicts Shakuni limping, Why? Well because Gufi Pental decided to make Shakuni limping. And every TV show presented the same idea a bit exaggerated? Someday someone will ask If Shakuni was never limping why every TV Show…
  • If Shakuni’s dice was made up on his father’s bone, why authentic Mahabharat shows his father fighting in Kurukshetra war? Such details can’t come from thin air!

Well what you refer as thin air is know as a figment of imagination, and imagination has no bounds — that's why art of story telling is almost as old as huminity itself.

This particular story most probably finds its origin in a play published by Shri Dharmveer Bharti where the phrase Andhe ka putra andha was used. This is called imagination!

I, as an author, use many fictional scenarios to make the story interesting. But I don’t use stories to degrade or glorify the epic.

This was not the only thing added of thin air.
