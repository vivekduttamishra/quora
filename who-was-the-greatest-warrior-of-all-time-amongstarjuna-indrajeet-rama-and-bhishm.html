Who was the greatest warrior of all time amongst"Arjuna, indrajeet, Rama and bhishma? Pls answer…

📅 Oct 29, 2020 09:46 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

When you list the name of Shri Ram, the matter ends there and then. No one tops Rama, and Krishna himself pronounced this in Bhagwat Gita — Among warriors, I am Rama.

Krishna wanted Arjuna to strive to become Rama. So before we remove Rama from the list of contestants, let us understand, what makes Shri Rama the greatest warrior of all times.

But let us be clear, Raw power doesn’t make Rama great. Even demons like Hirankashyap, and Tarkasur had unrivalled powers, yet they don't make it to this list. Take Rama — he failed to prevent his wife’s abduction, fell a prey to Nagpash, and yet Krishna had no doubt, Rama is the greatest warrior.

What makes Ram a warrior, without any parallel, was the strength of his character. He is almighty and yet he doesn’t boast of them. He has the power to dry down the entire ocean, and yet he prays to ocean. He crosses Lanka and Lanka’s destruction was just a wish away. And what does he do? He sends Angad as his emissary. What was the motive? To prevent the devastation of Lanka. He vanquishes Ravaan, but doesn’t annex Lanka to his kingdom but let it remain independent.

What makes Rama the greatest warrior is not his ability of destruction, but to prevent the devastation, not his ruthlessness but compassion. Imagine, someone wife is kidnapped and he takes time to pay last respect to departed Jatayu. He is pained with the agony his wife is suffering, but that pain doesn’t make him selfish rather compassinate to understand the pains of Surgriv.

So having seen what the highest warrior looks like let us see the other warriors on the list —

Look closer, Who was a warrior who never used his higest weapon for his goal? Who was the warrior who despite of being powerful, followed the footstep of great Rama and strived for peace? Who was the great warrior who tried to protect the women folk of his arch rival, one who had tried to molest his wife? Who was a warrior who was blessed by Shiva just like great Rama was blessed?

And that warrior deserves a remote second position in the list of greatest warriors of altime.

Then we have yet another warrior — someone, who just like great Rama accepted an exile for his father — an exile longer than Rama’s, never to be king again. A warrior capable of defeating one the greatest legend of all time and yet humble enough to discard his bow and embrace his fate without compromising with his principles. He certainly deserved a place in the greatest warrior.

And I won’t include Indrajeet in a list of great warriors. He was powerful. Very powerful. But not a warrior. He was a beast, and beasts are best hunted, lest the haunt.
