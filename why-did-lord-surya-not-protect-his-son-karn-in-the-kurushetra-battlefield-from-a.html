Why did Lord Surya not protect his son Karn in the Kurushetra battlefield from Arjun?

📅 Aug 15, 2020 08:43 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This is not just another question, it's one of the greatest answers and clue to one of the biggest mystery of Mahabharata — Karna.

Why did Lord Sun not protect his son? Why didn't he offered him another infallible shield or a celestial weapon? The answer, is same as why all Gods supported Arjuna — Dharma.

Karna had sided with Adharma to such an extent that he was called trunk of a tree of anger and lust. This is in contrast with Arjuna who is trunk of a tree of righteousness.

Now what lesson would it be for humanity if Surya supported his son despite he siding with the evil?

There is a sense of cosmic justice — born by the grace of God, Karna had inherited greatness. He got to become King and lived a life of luxary.

But he exhausted all his fortune in pursuit of his prime vices — blind-ambition and jealous. This added to secondary vices — deceit, foul-mouthed and hypocrisy.

Lord Surya watched his son as he rushed to his downfall. He, bound by that cosmic law couldn't help.
