Why did Duryodhana go ahead with war even after knowing that Sheesha and Guru Dronacharya half-heartedly supported his victory over Pandavas?

📅 Jun 25, 2020 11:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The premise of the question is incorrect and that is reflected in few answers below.

Why did anyone fought half hearted?

No one wants to fight for evil, unless they are forced to. Bhishma was bound by his oath which was taken keeping great Shantanu in mind. Alas, he had to fight for an evil who never respected him or anyone.

Drona fought because of his own son and allegiance to throne. No one wants to fight for Adharma other than other evil minded people.

Half hearted doesn't mean half effort, full effort doesn't guarantee success.

  • Despite being half hearted, Bhishma and Drona fought with all their might. Bhishma fought for 10 days and fought so hard that Krishna had to break is vow and take on weapon. He stayed for 10 out of 18 days and as long as he lived Pandavas were no closer to victory!
  • Drona headed Kauravas for 5 days and fought vigorously forming Chakravyuh, trapping Abhimanyu and slaying warrior like Drupad.
  • They died fighting for him.

Now coming to main part of question —

Why did Duryodhana go ahead with fight?

  1. He was an arrogant fool. He refused to see the signs —
    1. Arjuna alone routed his army and generals in Virat war.
    2. Krishna showed his virat swaroop and announced his doom.
  2. He knew half hearted or not, Bhisma would protect him, which Bhisma did. He had announced his decision that he won't kill Pandavas well before the battle.
  3. He misplaced his trust on Karna despite of fair warning from Bhishma and Drona. Contrary to popular belie, it Karna who decided not to participate in war under Bhisma's command; Bhisma didn't keep him out.
    1. It is better a half-hearted warrior fights for you rather than a trustworthy who would not fight over ego trip.
    2. The two half-hearted warriors hold the forte for 15 days. How many days did his trusted lieutenant managed — 2 days.
  4. Duryodhana thought he would win all rounds with his deceit and didn't realize, evils don't win the final round. If an evil is winning, it's not the final round.
