It is certain that Duryodhana had a great belief in Karna's abilities though this was apparently not shared by Bhishma, Drona, Krupa, etc., but did Dhritarashtra share that belief too or not?

📅 Jan 17, 2021 03:05 AM PST  |  🌐 English

For those who cling to irrational hope, they believe whatever suits their irrational beliefs.

Drona, Bhishma and Kripacharya were learned people with extensive understanding of military and politics. Each while having considerable poweress, they also had a fair estimate of strength of the adversary, and they always objected to foolshardy of Duryodhanana. They talked about the welfare of the kingdom. And it so happened the welfare of the Kingdom and King and his first family weren’t aligned.

Dhritrashtra and Duryodhana had two options. First, and unacceptable was to listen to the sage advise of everyone who was worth listening — Bhishma, Drona, Kripa, Vidur, and Parshuram.

The second and only other option was to listen to one who gives you a talks what suits your ambition — a promise of vanquishing your enemy and ensuring you the throne. No matter how hollow such a promise is, when it is the only one that suits you, you cling to it. That is what Dhritrashtra did.

But there was a difference between Duryodhana and Dhritrashtra. While Duryodhana was an ambitious fool, Dhritrashtra while ambition was conniving and cold-blooded. He played safe and diplomatic. So while he was as keen in Pandavas loosing the gambling and Draupadi, he was first to make U turn. And whenever it came to take a descision against their well-wishers Dhirtrashtra played hapless. In fact he remained at the comfort of the palace during and even after the war that was fought under his banner. Such people don’t always speak their mind freely.

But if you look at the entire chain of incident, you would realize that Dhritrashtra too believed in Karna’s ability otherwise he won’t have given a green signal for the war. Remember, he exerted himself against his son and returned Pandava’s kingdom after the gamble.

During the war he several times said, Duryodhana believed in Karna but, Karna isn’t performing as per his expectation. But if you read those lines and regret you it wouldn’t be difficult to see that even his dreams were shattered. He resented Duryodhana’s trust of Karna which now failed. So the question is, why would he resent unless he too carried the same hope and why would he go to war unless he didn’t believed so?
