Aside from the necessity of having to take part in the Virata War that might have disturbed it, what was the original timeline Pandavas intended to reveal themselves as we get the indication from the epic?

📅 Nov 25, 2020 11:34 AM PST  |  🌐 English

It is an interesting question and a refreshing diversion from stereotype or stupid questions.

However, without being an expert of timeline, I have heard once a timeline in altered it is beyond repair.

Virat war was one such hurdle. The timeline was altered and it is difficult to reconstruct and suggest this would have been the exact timeline.

But let us first understand Hastinapur’s attack was not the first or only incident that affected the timeline. It was altered with the arrival and killing of Kitchak.

Second, Mahabharat seem to be in sync with Bhishma that agyatvas had overstretched by over five months. But this doesn’t seem like a huge time period specially considering the era we are talking about. Methods of communication were slower. It would take days to travel and communication, specially for someone who is incongnito and doesn’t have a machinary at his disposal.

Pandavas were in a territory which wasn’t strictly a home turf. They needed to inform their allies like Dwarka and Panchal. Years of exile had taught them patience and they were better prepared to meet their adversaries. This is clear from their later actions also as they waited for options of peaceful negotiations to be over before opting for the war.

I would guess, if the Virat was not attacked, they may have waited to hear from their advisors, gathering them before revealing themselves. That would have been a great strategy and in sync with Yudhishthir’s earlier strategy when they had god incognior after Lakshagrih and were in no hurry to reveal themselves.
