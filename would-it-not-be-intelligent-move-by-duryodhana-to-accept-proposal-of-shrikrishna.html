Would it not be intelligent move by Duryodhana to accept proposal of Shrikrishna to give five villages to Pandava and avoid war and lead peaceful royal life? Or did he see some catch in this proposal?

📅 Nov 17, 2021 08:19 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Yes, it would have been an intelligent move by Duryodhana. Not just intelligent, but brilliant. He could have claimed to have washed his old sins and further attack by Pandava’s would have been an act of injustice.

But there was a big catch. For this to happen Duryodhana needed to be intelligent and needed good friends and advisor. Neither was true. Just like him, his three advisors were greedy, evil and hardened criminals.

To a fools mind, any attempt at non-violence and peace, appears to be a show of weakness. Duryodhana may have thought Pandavas have weakened. He was blinded by his own lust and was worsen by his companions. He sought death and war, where he should have accepted the peace offer.

That said, in all like hood, war could have been delayed not averted. Last time Dhritrashtra offered them a barren place infested with wild animals and haunted creatures. They transformed it to Indraprastha. Then they sent them to exile of twelve years and they returned lot more stronger and blessed by Gods and above all Lord Shiva.

Imagine, what Pandavas might have done with five villages? They would have transformed it to a mighty empire in no time making Duryodhana jealous of it again. And this time he may not have won them in some stupid dice game. So from Duryodhana’s perspective it was easier to destroy nomads than allow him to become an emperor first. But remember if they war would have happened late, once again it was because of Duryodhana’s own greed and that of his commarades.
