Did Bhagat Singh approve Gandhiji's way of independence struggle?

📅 Mar 23, 2020 12:29 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Before we attempt to answer this question let us understand one thing — during the era, Gandhiji was the face of India’s freedom movement while Bhagat Singh was a youth. And as very often happens, there is a difference of opinion due to difference of maturity. While Bhagat Singh was trying his best to get his revolutionary ideas heard and endorsed (He surrendered for this very purpose), Gandhi’s idea was well known.

That said this question can’t be answered in a single word — No or Yes.

Like rest of India, Gandhi’s idea of Non Cooperation Movement too mesmerised Bhagat Singh and Chandrashekhar Azad. Chandrashekhar Azad even went to jail. But they couldn’t appreciate why Gandhiji called off the movement (that is a wonderful story for some other question) and Azad parted ways. Since Bhagat Singh sided with Azad it is clear he too didn’t follow (I am not using the word endorsed) Gandhian thought anymore.

But story doesn’t end here.

Bhagat Singh never once insulted Gandhiji, and despite of ideological difference always respected Gandhiji. HSRA always looked for Gandhiji’s endorsement (that never came forth).

It is documented evidence that Gandhiji appealed to viceroy both verbally and written to repeal Bhagat Singh’s death sentence.

The problem with our current generation is we try to make our respect for these great freedom fighter mutually exclusive — you either honor Bhagat Singh or Gandhi or Bose.
