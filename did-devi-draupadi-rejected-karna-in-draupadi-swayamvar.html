Short Answer: NO.

Long Answer: https://www.quora.com/q/dqqdyatpbbobowgd/Had-Draupadi-really-stopped-Karna-from-Participating-in-her-Swyamber-Source-Reference-The-Mahabharata-Project-Main-Ar?ch=10&share=66bc3219

Here I have provided detailed explanation with authentic references showing why this story is fake.

A Few simple points —

  • Out of 1259 versions that BORI evaluated, only 5 contains this story.
  • The five versions doesn’t include the oldest Nepali version, entire sourther recitation, Sarda version, Bengali version etc.
  • The most prominent version to include this story is Neelkantha commentry (KMG is based on this version). This script also includes Karna’s failure to string the bow, thus is self contradicting.
  • There is absolutely no cross-reference of this incident anywhere in the entire epic which is unlikely.
  • There are a number of cross-reference to prove —
    • case was no bar.
    • Karna is especially mentioned among invited guests.
  • Prof Suthankar, the chief editor of BORI has explained in great detail, why this was completely impossible.
  • The docile nature of Draupadi as we see, again contradicts any likehood of Draupadi raising voice in the swaymvara much less rejecting Karna.
