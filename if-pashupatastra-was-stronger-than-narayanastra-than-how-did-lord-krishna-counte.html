If pashupatastra was stronger than narayanastra than how did Lord Krishna countered Lord Shiva's pashupatastra with his narayanastra in banasur war?

📅 Feb 06, 2021 11:29 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Pashupatastra is the strongest weapon in the universe. But divyastras don’t follow the general laws of physics. A bullet fired with same gun will have same effect. In fact even bullets behave differently in different atmosphere.

A divyastra works on the moral high ground of its bearer. And that is why there was a statutory warning against its use/misuse mentioned thought out the epic. So a weapon in Mahadev’s hand would be more potent than same weapon in someone else’s hand.

Now coming to the current question —

  1. It wasn’t just a fight between Narayanastra and Pashupatastra. It was a fight between Krishna and Banasura. Shiva was an unwilling participant.
  2. It was a similar situation when Vishnu was an unwilling protector to Daksha and failed to protect Daksha from Shivaganas.
  3. Shiva himself and given a boon to Vishnu that he won’t be defeated by him. Naturally Pashupatastra can’t go against the boon of Pashupati Shiva

Thanks Lakshmi Telidevara for providing exact reference to the boon in her answer. Here is a replication —

The great God said,

'O Narayana, through my grace, amongst men, gods, and Gandharvas, thou shalt be of immeasurable might and soul. Neither gods, nor Asuras, nor great Uragas, nor Pisachas, nor Gandharvas, nor men, nor Rakshasas, nor birds, nor Nagas, nor any creatures in the Universe, shall ever be able to bear thy prowess. No one amongst even the celestials shall be able to vanquish thee in battle. Through my grace, none shall ever be able to cause thee pain by the weapon of thunderbolt or with any object that is wet or dry, or with any mobile or immobile thing. Thou shalt be superior to myself if thou ever goest to battle against me.' Thus were these boons acquired by Sauri in days of yore.
