How powerful was Eklavya?

📅 Apr 20, 2021 01:13 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Eklavya was powerful enough to shut a dog’s mouth with seven arrow and impudent enough to call Drona his guru, despite his explicit rejection.

Ignoring every other aspect, that is the extent of the power of Eklavya as we know it. He was an excellent archer. But was he a great warrior? Could he have been lethal? Answer to both these questions is a resounding NO.

In an era where warriors possessed great Divyastras capable of launching a hundred thousand arrows at a time, would seven or a dozen arrows have helped? Eklavya did not know of Divyastras, and Drona didn’t provide him one.

But was Eklavya a great warrior? How did he die? Krishna slayed him. And should I remind you, Krishna’s incarnated, to destroy evil and protect good? So what does it tell you about Eklavya? And what does it tell you about Drona’s rejection? Just a biased old man, who despite of loving his son most, still made nurtured Arjuna to become great, or a wise man who could see the traits of Eklavya — someone who can shot arrows on a dog just to keep him shut!

He was a great archer. But was he responsible enough to be a warrior. As I see, he was one of the lesser significant characters of Mahabharata.
