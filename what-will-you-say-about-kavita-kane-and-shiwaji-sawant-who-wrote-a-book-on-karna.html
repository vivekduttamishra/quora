What will you say about Kavita Kane and Shiwaji Sawant who wrote a book on Karna?

📅 Oct 02, 2020 11:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

What can anyone say? They have written fictions. There have their creative rights, just like MF Hussain had when he painted Indian goddesses nude and called it creative freedom. Such abuses to our ancient culture is not new.

But Karna is an interesting case study and a paradox. His character originally developed as that of a unfortunate abused lowetcaste who suffered all odds and became paragon of virtue. Unfortunately most of these are eager perception not actually in contrast with epic references and facts.

But then perceptions becomes much stronger than facts, and legally they have copyright on their fictions and facts have no copyright. If there was one, it expired long before the birth of the notion of copyright.

Unfortunately we are keenly aware of our rights, but not our responsibility and just like Kalidas we are cutting the same branch we are sitting on — our own civilization and culture.

That said, in her book Karna's wife, Kavita Kane has narrated the failures and vices of Karna more clearly than most other book with similar theme.
