Would Ashwatthama dare to enter the Pandava camp if he did not know that Krishna, Satyaki, and Pandavas were not there?

📅 Oct 22, 2021 07:04 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Ashwatthama did enter Pandava’s camp thinking that everyone was there. His didn’t mean to kill Uppandavas. Pandavas were his real target. And it was hard to imagine that he had any reason to assume Krishna or Satyaki won’t be around. Then arises the question —

How did Ashwatthama dared to enter Pandava’s camp

Lust and revenge often destroys reasoning and fear. And that is the basic reason for all crimes in the world. A rapist or a murder knows he would pay dearly for his crimes and yet, the fear of punishment doesn’t reduce the crime rates. The vile facets of the ones character invent reasonings that they would succeed in both their crime and evading the justice.

Ashwatthama was blinded by his anger and desire to revenge his father’s death that was aggravated by the fatal injury of Duryodhana. He had lost is reasons and the fear of death. Or he may have been excessive confident that once he decimated all the Pandavas no one would be left to deliver justice to him. He was going to get only chance — one swift blow when no one is looking or expecting. He attacked. And failed.

And yet lies another proof of his dare. He invoked Brahmastra in presence of Krishna and Pandavas. His brain was still conjuring permutations where he would be able to exact his vengeance and escape justice.

But this trait is not limited to Ashwatthama. Think —

  • Why did Duryodhana waged the war despite seeing the Krishna’s virata swaroop? Had he any real hope of winning the war? No. His brain was conjuring possibilities.
  • Karna too knew he is on the wrong side and the cause of devastations of earth. Did that stop him from imagining that he would be able to kill Arjuna? Why did he made an exception for Arjuna in his oath?
  • Duryodhana kept watching Karna’s repeated failures against Pandavas. Yet he kept sacrificing his brother to save Karna in the hope that at some point he can turn the tides of the war.

The lusts make you imagine things.
