Thanks for the A2A.

You need to make your mind first. You need a reliable translation which says what came from the legacy of Ved Vyas or you want a fiction which in trying to perform balancing act deviates from the epic narrative.

And what do you mean by whitewashing soecific character? Do you want a narrative that doesn't say good people good and fight between good and evil as no one is good or bad? Then sorry to bust the bubble, there is no such reliable version that provides such an unreliable narrative.

But if you are interested in a reliable narrative that dares to speak the truth, that proclaims how evils were vanquished by good, then you are in luck. There are epic narratives that begins with the salutation to Nara and Narayana, and explains how Krishna incarnated to protect the good and destroy evil and restablish Dharma.

You may read Gita press translations of Neelkantha commentary or it's English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguly (also know as PC Roy edition) or MN Dutta edition (again based on KMG).

If you want more researched and critical editions then you may read Hindi or English translation of BORI critical edition.

On the other hand if you want heavily biased perceptions glorifying evils, I may recommend Mrityunjaya by Shivaji Sawant or any work of Devdutt Patnayak or Anant Neelkantha.
