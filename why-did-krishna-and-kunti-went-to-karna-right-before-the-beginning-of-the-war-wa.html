Why did Krishna and Kunti went to Karna right before the beginning of the war? Was it part of there psychological warfare or concern about him?

📅 Jul 02, 2020 12:58 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

I believe both of them went to Karna with slightly different reasons, and yet with the same goal in mind which differs greatly from common perception of psychological warfare — for the benefit of Karna.

Krishana’s visit to Karna

Krishna’s visit to Karna was for his redemption. Karna had sided with evils for wrong reasons and what he claimed is his undying loyalty for the evil prince of Hastinapur. Duryodhana too trusted him so much that he would fight rather than to accept a peace deal. Krishna wanted Karna to switch sides which has multiple benefits —

  • Duryodhana would be left out and may accept the peace deal. The war would be avoided. Duryodhana’s treachery would be put in check.
  • Karna-Arjuna conflict would be over. Once the meaningless competition would be out, we could have seen better parts of Karna. That would have been Karna redemption.

Why was it not a psycological war?

  • With Krishna siding Pandavas, there victory was ensured, with our without Karna.
  • Krishna vividly described Karna what would be the outcome of the war. Karna agreed with Krishna’s description.
  • Since Karna knew the outcome and it was outlined by Krishna, there was no question of psycological war.
  • Karna had been defeated so many times that his presence wasn’t as important as that of Bhisma.
  • A psycological war would be to try winning Bhisma to your side.

Kunti’s visit to Karna

  • It took place much later.
  • War was decided.
  • So far Kunti had her reason to stay away from Karna
  • She had a consolation that Karna is safe and happy.
  • This war was going to be the most devastating war of their lifetime.
  • Now Kunti was worried about her safety
  • She approached Karna
  • She never asked her to spare her sons.
  • She wanted her to return.
  • Karna’s assurance to Kunti that he would spare four of his sons was one of the crulest mocking of Mahabharat — telling a mother that I will kill one of your sons.
  • But Kunti approached for Karna not for other Pandavas.
