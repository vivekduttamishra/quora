When Panchali died, why did Yudhistira replied to the Pandavas that she loved Arjuna more than the other Pandavas? I heard that Draupadi loved all the pandavas equally.

📅 Feb 16, 2022 12:34 AM PST  |  🌐 English

There can never be an exact answer to this question. You see feeling is a intangible entity and can’t be quantified. And if someone can qualify it, it is the one having the feeling, in this case Draupadi. And Draupadi never explicitly said anything to this effect.

What Yudhishira said, was his opinion. He may have felt and his feeling can be considered more accurate than my arguments. But it may also be just his opinion and can’t truly reflect Draupadi’s feeling.

That said, even if Draupadi loved Arjuna more, neither this should be suprising, nor it requires a rationale reason for it. You see, love can be irrational. You may love someone more than other — even your own siblings or children. And you may not be able to attribute any reason for it. We can’t rationalize of quantify love.

That again said, there seem rationale explanation of why Draupadi should love Arjuna more than others. His father wanted her to be married to Arjuna in the first place. She must have heard the ripples of the fame of the greatest archer of the time. And all those legends came true when not only Arjuna won the swaymvar but also protected her and her family from the evil design of the sore losers of the swaymvar who tried to massacre their families. Which woman wouldn’t love such a husband?

Finally, as I said, Yudhishira’s opinion was is opinion and can’ be considered a 100% accurate fact. And if you say something you believe sincerely, you are no lying even though what you are saying is not correct. There is another example of this thing. Yudhishthira said that Arjuna promised that he would finish the battle immediately. But the fact is Arjuna said he can finish it with the use of Pashupata but he wouldn’t use it against human army and would let the war run it’s course. Again, he misunderstood Arjuna. And he said what he remembered as truth. That is not a lie, but that doesn’t change the fact.
