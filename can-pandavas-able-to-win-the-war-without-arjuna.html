Can Pandavas able to win the war without Arjuna?

📅 Nov 09, 2021 10:13 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: YES.

Arjuna was no doubt the greatest warrior of the era which has been confirmed by almost every one in Mahabharata whose words mattered — Drona, Bhishma, Ashwatthama, Karna, Indra, Parshurama, Krishna and above all, Devadhidev Mahadeva Shiva.

But then the question starts on a flawed and unclear hypothesis

Why would Pandava’s fight without Arjuna? Do you want to imagine Arjuna didn’t exist? Or Arjuna didn’t participate in this battle? Or Arjuna didn’t possess the power he did?

But no matter what the hypothesis may be, question missed one important point, I would say on of highest importance — Krishna.

Krishna Factor

With Krishna around, Pandava’s would still have won the battle. Naraynastra, Vaishnavastra, Vasvishakti would still have missed their marks. The deadly asura weapon ashwasena would still have failed to vanquish Dharama.

With Arjuna out, no one would be their to stop Krishna from destroying Bhishma on day 3 or day 9. And if once Krishna lashed out on to the war who would have survived? No with Krishna around the war was still in favor of Pandavas.

Karna Factor

Many may consider with Arjuna out, Karna would have finished the Pandavas army in no time. But that is a hypothesis that has as much chance of going wrong as any other. As I see, with Arjuna not around Karna had no real reason to fight against Pandavas. Hell, he may have seen Dharma and switched sides. The only true reason he fought against Pandavas was his deep rooted desire to prove himself better than Arjuna. This was core to his existence —

  • He wanted Brahmastra from Drona because he wanted to beat Arjuna
  • He lied to Parshurama because he wanted the Brahmastra denied by Drona.
  • He gatecrashed the Kridasthala because he wanted to prove himself better than Arjuna.
  • He traded off his kavacha for vasvi shakti because he was scared of one roaring enemy (Arjuna)
  • He refused the peace deal offered by Krishna. While one explicit reason he mentioned for this is his friendship to Duryodhana, the other that he too mentioned in a more elaborate way was his destiny to fight Arjuna. He thought he was chosen to fight Arjuna. He knew Kauravas would be destroyed. He knew he had the power to stop it. But he didn’t do it even for the welfare of Duryodhana. Now imagine a world without Arjuna. Why would Karna really fight the battle on behalf of Duryodhana?
  • He may not have been against Pandava from day #1.

Bhima Factor

  • Bhima was one of the greatest warrior of the time who destroyed many powerful beings like Hidimba, Vakasura, Jarasandha, Keechaka and all Kaurava brothers.
  • Has Arjuna (now gone missing) not there he may have ended the war long before Kurukshetra, right in the Kuru sabha.
  • People pointed out Karna defeated Bhima. But long before he did, Bhima almost killed him and Duryodhana had to sacrifice his 31 brothers to protect him.
  • Now that Karna wouldn’t have killed Bhima anyway, what were his own chance of survival against a powerful warrior whom he would not kill?

X Factor

Let us now eliminate both Arjuna and Bhima. And go back to day 13. A lone young warrior defeated entire Kuru army and thwart their plan to capture Yudhishthira and end the war.

On the night of 14th day, another warrior fought so hard that Kauravas lost all enterprise and Karna had to invoke his most potent weapon.

Had Arjuna not been around, Pandavas may have formulated a different strategy. Krishna may have guided some Abhimanyu to take the role of Arjuna. The power that was vested in Kriti may have been spread in the twins or Satyaki.

What would have stopped some Kairata joining Pandava’s forces? And who had won against a unknown Kairata?

Not every righteous war of the world has been fought with Arjuna at the helm. But what guided all of them was —

Dharmo rakshati rakshitaah!

Dharma would always manifest heros to guide humanity. Name may be different form may be different, but they would be there. Not just to win a war but to inspire humanity to follow Dharma

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

When an evil power raises their head, there would be someone to cursh it. Dharma may transform a Brahmin Rama into Parshurama, an army of apes can destroy the greatest demon army of the world, a single one can wreck havoc beyond their imagination. Some Chanakaya will manifest some Chandragupta and win the war without Arjuna. Some Vikramaditya will destroy huns of their time. Some Maharana Pratap would keep fighting mugals and destroy their design.

Some hero may win, without holding any weapon in their hand. And that is the power of Dharma.

HAD ARJUNA NO SIGNIFICANCE AT ALL?

Only fools would venture into calling Arjuna insignificant. Arjuna was the greatest warrior, who didn’t use any of his divine weapons. So what was his real power? It was his adherence to Dharma? It was his eagerness to jump to help even his worst enemies. All guardians of the world bestowed their grace on him. What made Arjuna more powerful beyond any realm of weapons was the goodness of his character. Krishna called him his inseparable existence and he chose Krishna over a powerful army. Duryodhana rejected Krishna. Karna rejected Krishna. And this is the key difference. What makes a warrior really great is he never loses the sight of Dharma for personal ambition or glory. It was Dharma that gave him the strength.

S U M M A R Y

  • Arjuna was the greatest warrior of the era and most important factor for Pandavas victory.
  • But his greatness lies in his adherence to Dharma.
  • Dharma always triumphs — that is why Kukrukshetra war is called Dharma Yudha and not Partha war. Arjuna was a means to attain the goal.
  • Dharma always triumps — it finds a way. It manifests it’s heros to do the deeds. In every era
