Which warrior was the most successful conspirator in the Mahabharata?

📅 Apr 16, 2020 07:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A.

My answer would be none. I believe these two words — warrior and conspirator. to be Antonyms. Warriors are not conspirators. They may be strategists.

A conspirator would conspire to win over their enemies without any battle. They may try to burn them alive, loot or plunder them by the unfair means. They typically fear their opponent and never enter a fair fight. By that definition the biggest conspirator would be Team Duryodhana.

But I would refrain from calling them successful or warrior. They were conspirator so essentially can be called fighters, but not warriors. And their conspiracy failed ultimately.

Now if we talk about warrior, they take the war only as the last resort and they are compassionate even for their opponent and their subjects to the extent of forgoing their revenge. Take Ram, who offered Ravan a chance to back off even after reaching Lanka.

And when these warriors fight, it's for Dharma. They try to minimize the fatalities. They exploit the weakness of their enemy and force them to make a bad move. And the best and most successful strategist is of course Krishna. Let's see how it's a strategy —

  1. He moves his kingdom to Dwarka, to save his people from suffering uncalled for war. An egoistic warrior could have destroyed Jarasandha and his kingdom and inturn his own soldiers.
  2. He asks Bhima to challenge Jarasandh and is killed in a fair fight. He reveals to Bhima the secret of Jarassndh's death, that is a strategy. A conspiracy would be instead of fighting, trying to poison an unsuspecting Jarasandh.
  3. Note killing Jarasandh was weakening opponent force and an attempt to avoid full blown war. That's what we call a strategy.
  4. Krishna broke his oath and attacked Bhisma on the ninth day. This was a twin strategy —
    1. Provoke Arjun to out perform.
    2. Convince Bhisma to give up fight.This worked like a charm, and Bhisma himself shared the secret of his defeat.
    3. Krishna really never attacked — strategy.
  5. Bhisma defeat and death was a strategy and not conspiracy. Bhisma was protected by a book of iksha mrityu (इक्षा मृत्यु). Krishna made him volunteer. It was a fair fight where Arjuna, Shikandi weren't two fighting against Bhisma alone but all Kaurav general protecting him.
  6. Krishna is often criticized of getting Ghatotkatch killed to snatch Indra's celestial weapon. Can you call it a foul play? He never asked Karna to use the weapon. Only because Kaurava's fail to defeat him by normal means they used a divine weapon? So who should be calling a foul play? Also remember that wasn't first time Ghatotkatch participated in war.
  7. Similarly Krishna knew the weakness of Drona — his son. He tricked him in believing his son is dead. He didn't ask him to start meditation admist the ongoing war. The day wasn't over and any astray weapon could have killed him. Of course he knew that Dhristdyumn will surely avenge his father. He died in wa. Unarmed. Can you complain, after what they did to Abhimanyu?
  8. Krishna saved Arjun on number of occasions. This is what charioteer is supposed to do. No conspiracy.
  9. He never revealed Karna's true identity to Arjun. He knew what would happen. Conspiracy or strategy? It wasn't his truth or responsibility to share that info.

Hope that clears the issue.
