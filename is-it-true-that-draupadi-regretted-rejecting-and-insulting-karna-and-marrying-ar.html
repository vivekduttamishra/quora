Is it true that Draupadi regretted rejecting and insulting Karna and marrying Arjuna, who failed to take care of her during the dice game?

📅 Mar 24, 2020 12:58 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

There are two Important part of this question

  1. Did Draupadi rejected and insulted Karna?

This question is has been very well answered by @Lakshmi Telidevara so I would not repeat that answer. Here I would just add additional reference as one of the comment to @Lakshmi Telidevara's answer apparently quotes Gita Press, which is an authentic compilation of Northern Mahabharat but also includes critical footnotes. There are 3 important points to understand.

  1. Like KMG Gita Press Mahabharat (edition 15, book1, Adiparva chapter 186 page 624-25) mentions Karna succeeding in arming the bow and subsequently Draupadi rejecting him.
  2. The footnote, however contradicts by providing reference indicating Karna never managed to put arrows in the bow. It refers to Dakshinayat, Bori, and Neelkantha.
  3. Chapter 187 sloka 4 (page 625) clearly mentions that Karna failed to arm the bow. This clearly mentions Karna failed. Why would Draupadi reject him?

2 Did Draupadi ever regret it?

Should she regret it? Would any one regret not marrying someone who wants to strip her naked in public?

She on multiple occasions remembers Karna and the insult more as a trauma, call for justice and vengeance.
