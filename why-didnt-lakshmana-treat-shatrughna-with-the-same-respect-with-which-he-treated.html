Why didn't Lakshmana treat Shatrughna with the same respect with which he treated and cared for Rama despite being a real brother to him and only half brother to Rama?

There is no evidence to prove the basic premise of the question — Laksman didn’t treat Shatrughna with same respect.

Ramayana narrates that the all the brothers had great love and admiration for each other. As Laksman and Shatrughna were twins, naturally it was a question of love and admiration rather than respect. So why is his respect for Ram evident?

  1. Ram is an elder brother, well respected and soon to be King. When he accepts exile instead of throne he becomes even a greater legend. How do you not respect such a person?
  2. You don’t thurst respect on anyone, they simply command by the strength of their character. Ram was one such person. It was impossible to not respect him.
  3. In our family, while we love everyone, we tend to pay more attention and attendance to one who is pain or difficult time. Don’t we? How is it different?
  4. Not only Ram, but Bharat and Shatrugna to respected him more than anyone else.

My answer is based on the story of Ramayana the grand epic of India. I have taken referene from Balmiki Ramayana and Tulsidas Ramcharit Manas
