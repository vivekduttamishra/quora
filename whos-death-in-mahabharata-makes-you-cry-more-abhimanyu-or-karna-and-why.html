Who's death in Mahabharata makes you cry more, Abhimanyu or Karna, and why?

📅 Aug 19, 2021 09:42 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

My answer would be neither. You should cry for the death of neither a warrior nor a coward.

ABHIMANYU

Abhimanyu was a warrior of the highest order. He fought a whole herd of so called warriors. Many died on his hand. Many fled. Everyone lost the hope of victory. They together killed a warrior whom they couldn’t face singly. They deprived him of his chariot, armors and weapons. He was alone. His army couldn’t reach him. But did he beg for a recess? Did he request the enemy to wait till he gets back on a chariot? Did he preach Dharma to his enemies? No. He knew they had no knowledge of Dharma. He continued to fight, till he was dead. He died a valiant death. Should we mourn the death of such a hero?

His death changed the course of the war forever. The next saw the wrath of a father and forced Arjuna to get involved in the battle whole heartedly. Should we cry for his sacrifice?

If you really want to cry and mourn, direct your disgust towards to spineless heinous creatures who called themselves warriors and yet too terrified to fight the a lone warrior. Yes, I can certainly cry for them, but not for the brave Abhimanyu.

KARNA

Karna lived his entire life in lust, blind ambition and luxury, plotting conspiracies with his best friend. He was part of the that spineless pack, I mentioned earlier, who killed the brave Abhimanyu.

Some may say Karna’s death was a poetic justice to what he did to Abhimanyu. Alas! Karna wasn’t destined for a glorious death. You may blame Arjuna for killing him while he was without chariot. We may agree or disagree on this subject. But it doesn’t change the fact that Karna begged Arjuna to stop till he gets his chariot out. It was so very unlike Abhimanyu’s valor.

Also remember Karna wasn’t alone because his army couldn’t reach him. They deserted him. It may also be called poetic justice as Karna deserted his people so many time in the battles earlier. But once again it doesn’t glorify Karna’s death. He died begging. Should we mourn for such a cowardly death?

If you really want to mourn, you should mourn for his life — a series of wasted opportunity. Karna was born to be a hero, but his lust and ambition degraded him to a villain and Karna paid the price of his lifetime of misadventure.

I would rather mourn for his life than cry for his death.
