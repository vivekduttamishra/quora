Why did Karna keep quiet when Draupadi was being disrobed?

📅 May 10, 2021 12:32 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

I have answered this question a number of times.

Short Answer: Karna didn’t keep quiet. He was the loudest.

It was at his behest Duhshasha tried to disrobe Draupadi. And this narrative is not only present in ancient narratives like Neelkantha, Geeta Press and KMG but also in the BORI critical edition. Here is the exact phrase —

It has been ordained by the gods that a woman should only have one husband. However, she submits to many and it is therefore certain that she is a courtesan. It is my view that there is nothing surprising in her being brought into the sabha in a single garment, or even if she is naked. In accordance with dharma, Soubala has won all the riches the Pandavas possessed, including her and themselves. O Duhshasana! This Vikarna is only a child, though he speaks words of wisdom. Strip away the garments from the Pandavas and Droupadi.” O descendant of the Bharata lineage! On hearing these words, the Pandavas took off their upper garments and sat down in the sabha. O king! Then Duhshasana forcibly tugged at Droupadi’s garments.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3375-3380).

He didn’t stop here and further continued —

‘Karna said, “There are three who can own no property—a slave, a student and a woman. O fortunate one! You are the wife of a slave and have nothing of your own. You have no lord and are like the property of slaves. Enter226 and serve us. That is the task for you in this household. O Princess! All the sons of Dhritarashtra are now your masters and not the sons of Pritha. O beautiful one! Choose another one for your husband, one who will not make you a slave through gambling. Remember the eternal rule among slaves. Sexual acts with one’s masters are never censured. Nakula, Bhimasena, Yudhishthira, Sahadeva and Arjuna have been won over. O Yajnaseni! Enter as a slave.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 3468-3472). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.


Wait isn’t Vastraharana and interpolation?

Many admirers of Karna carry this wishful thinking that Vastraharan was an interpolation and they try to base their opinion on following two points —

  1. Few BORI scholars published their own opinions in annals of BORI that Vastraharana may be an interpolation.
  2. Their argument is there isn’t sufficient cross-reference for vastraharana.

Now there is three important problem in their assertions.

  1. Annals of BORI is not same as BORI CE. It is individual scholarly articles and doesn’t debase the actual BORI CE. It doesn’t amend the actual BORI CE which was created after over forty years of research. Views can’t replace facts.
  2. BORI CE’s chief editor Prof Vishnu Suthankar NEVER said Vastraharana is an interpolation.

MOST IMPORTANT POINTS

  1. None of the Annals exonarate Karna from his role in provoking Duhsasana.
  2. There are multiple cross-references to prove Karna’s role.

The official stand of BORI CE is that Vastraharana happened and annals that are mere scholary article have no locus-standi against the official BORI CE


WHAT CROSS REFERENCES EXIST FOR KARNA’S ACT

There are many. I would give a few here —

Kairata Parva

Krishna meets Pandava for the first time after their exile and here is what he announces —

Vasudeva said, “The earth will drink the blood of Duryodhana, Karna, the evil-souled Shakuni and Duhshasana as the fourth. After that, all of us will instate Dharmaraja Yudhishthira. Those deceitful ones deserve to be slain.

—Kairata Parva

Now the question is why would Krishna say Karna deserved to die if he had no role? But story doesn’t end here. So read on

Go Harana Parva

During Goharana Parva this is what Ashwatthama said —

O performer of evil deeds! She was dragged into the assembly hall in a single garment, when she was in season. In search of gain, you have severed the great root of a sandalwood tree. 109 O brave one! You made them perform tasks and what did Vidura have to say then? 110 We have seen that men exhibit conciliation, to the best of their ability. So do other beings, even insects and ants. The Pandavas are incapable of pardoning Droupadi’s molestation.

—Goharana Parva, BORI CE

Now question is what was the task that Karna made them perform? Playing the dice? dragging to the court? Karna had no role there. He asked them to strip Draupadi.

Bhagwat Yana Parva

Here is Karna’s own confession

‘“‘O Krishna! I regret the harsh words I used against the Pandavas to please Dhritarashtra’s son. I am now tormented by that deed. O Krishna! When you see me killed by Savyasachi, that will be the punashchiti25 of their sacrifice. When Pandava26 roars loudly and drinks Duhshasana’s blood, that will be the drinking of soma. O Janardana! When the two from Panchala27 bring down Drona and Bhishma, that will be the conclusion of the sacrifice.

He further said —

Krishna Madhusudana31 and spoke these words. ‘O mighty-armed one! Knowing everything, why did you wish to confuse me? The destruction of the entire earth is imminent. Shakuni, Duhshasana and Dhritarashtra’s son, King Duryodhana, and I are responsible.

Why did Karna include himself as responsible for destruction of earth!

Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva

Here is what Drona said —

In the assembly of the Kurus, with Shakuni and with Dhritrarashtra’s sanction, you excited the wrath of the Pandavas. Duhshasana supported you and Karna increased it.

—BORI CE, Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva

Karnavadha Parva

Here is what Krishna himself said —

In front of the Pandaveyas and the Kurus, Karna spoke harsh and cruel words to Krishna649 in the assembly hall. “O Krishna! The Pandavas have been destroyed and have gone to eternal hell. O wide-hipped one! O one who is sweet in speech! Choose another one as a husband. Enter Dhritarashtra’s abode as a servant-maid. O one with long eyelashes!

Your husbands are no longer there.” He does not know about dharma and is extremely evil-minded.

With so many cross-references no annals or CE can exonerate Karna for his henious acts.
