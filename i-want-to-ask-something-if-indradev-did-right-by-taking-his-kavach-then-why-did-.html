I Want to ask something if indradev did right by taking his kavach then why did indradev come in disguise to take it. If he would have asked directly and explained him why he have to take it then karn would never said no and happily give him?

📅 Sep 21, 2021 09:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

First things first — Indra taking Kavacha wasn’t a question of being right or wrong. It was an exchange. While Indra certainly came to take away Karna’s Kavacha, Karna was no less happy with the prospect of exchange. This also answers the second question what if Indra had come directly.

Did Indra have any convincing reason to suggest why Karna should give Kavacha and Kundala. I believe NO. Indra may have thought Karna would value his divine armor with more respect and wouldn’t part with it otherwise. Thus Indra may have planned to test Karna’s well acclaimed generosity. Had he come undisguised Karna had no obligation to donate.

But on retrospection, Karna would have anyway agreed to exchange his Kavacha and I must add gladly. This may be something Indra hadn’t expected.

EDIT 22-SEP-2021

This expanded answer is based on yet another answer to this question that toes the popular perception that depicts Indra as a selfish, conniving and barbaric person who was devoid of any courtesy, consideration or etiquette and back-stabbed Karna —

And yet, when you read the epic, during Mahabharata era, Indra was one of the highest revered God. His name is used in terms of superlative terms and not as some mean person.

So is it a possibility that we after all those years got the context wrong? Let me give you a hint — Karna had refused to donate his kavacha and was too pleased with the exchange he got. And that tells that Indra’s disguise had failed.

Some believe, it is like requesting somebody -“ kindly die please for my sake!”

Do we want to imply, any sans armor was akin to “dead”? In that case that anyone included everyone. Right? Or was it that Karna was the weakest warrior who would be dead without the protection of a Kavacha? Mayb. Krishna said the same.

Coming to Indra showing no courtesy ,etiquette , respect, consideration, stabbing from back nature.

Indra approached as a Brahmin seeking alms, right at the time when Karna used to donate. That was following the etiquette.

He didn’t use his divine power to snatch his armor. Rather he gave him an option. That is courtsey and respect. It is strange that you should call a seeker as robber. Do you expect Karna to be a great donor by donating Kingdom’s wealth and gold coins?

Karna tried to offer substitutes. Indra refused. Karna demanded his weapon in exchange. Indra agreed — that was consideration. Can you imagine if Indra had refused the exchange and insisted in donation, what would have been the scenario?

  • One Karna would have lost Kavacha without getting a weapon. But we can rule out that scenario as Karna flatly refused.
  • The other scenario would be Indra won’t get the Kavacha, and Karna’s real face would have been unmasked as hypocrite.
