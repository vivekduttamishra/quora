Did Bhishma betray Hastinapur by prohibiting Kauravas to remove arrows from his body so that he could continue to fight?

📅 Apr 17, 2021 05:14 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Interesting question that sent me asking — Didn’t Kauravas betray Bhishma and Hastinapur?

Bhishma fought for Kauravas, despite of them being wrong and backing out from their own commitments. He fought for them despite his own advice repeatedly ignored. He fought for them despite knowing that the war was not winnable. He fought despite all these. Uncountable people died. And he realized his victory, that is not to be, would still not bring the rule of righteousness. His victory would do Hastinapur no good. His absence…

As long as Bhishma fought, he fought with all his forces. But now he was badly injured. His inner strength could keep his death away, but his body was no more fit to to fight even if the arrows were removed.

Perhaps mid-way to the war (9th day), Bhishma realized that the ultimate and sustainable protection he could provide to Hastinapur is by moving away from the path of its destiny. The ultimate protector of Hastinapur gave up his arms, knowing that’s his absence would finish the war sooner and Hastinapur could breathe easy in under the rule of his rightful king Yudhishthira. And he was right. Post war, when Yudhistir became the ruler, even Dhritarashtra felt a calm and happiness like he never felt earlier. Now you can imagine the condition of ordinary people of Hastinapur. Perhaps Bhishma realized that he is in the way of Hastinapur’s destiny.
