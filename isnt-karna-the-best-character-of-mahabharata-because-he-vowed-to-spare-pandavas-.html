Isn't Karna the best character of Mahabharata, because he vowed to spare Pandavas for the sake of Kunti, who abandoned him in his infancy itself and never treated him with motherly affection?

📅 Feb 07, 2022 01:03 AM PST  |  🌐 English

This question makes too many presumptions and ignores too many factual details to be relevant. It tries to become a leading question with one conclusion, however due to it’s factual blunders fails to become so.

Here are few important factual blunders —

1.Was Kunti Unaffectionate to Karna

  • It was Kunti’s motherly affection at par with Devaki, that made her separate her son from herself. It was to protect her from infamy of being son of an unwed child. The proof of her motherly affection was the divine Kavacha and Kundala he got for Surya. It was Kunti’s insistence that Surya offered Karna those artifacts. Kunti never asked such a boon for any of her other sons. His arrival to Adhiratha was carefully executed and Kunti was informed immediately after Karna reached Adhiratha. If Kunti wanted her dead, he would have died.
  • Kunti was too happy to see Karna becoming the King of Anga.
  • It was once again Kunti’s motherly affection that took her to Karna. She had not gone to beg for the lives for her other sons. She never asked for it. Not once. She asked her to come to the side of Dharma and fight side by side to Arjuna. It was her last attempt to protect Karna, because even Karna knew beyond doubt that he would die in the battle. He confessed it to both Krishna and Kunti.

2.Does Karna’s vow make him the best character of Mahabharata

Karna’s vow was Karna’s vow. It wasn’t for Kunti. Kunti never asked for it. Kunti never said it is ok that you can kill Arjuna. Which mother would accept one of his son killing the other?

Karna’s oath may be the outcome of his guilt or ego or both. But does that make Karna the best character?

Think why didn’t Karna followed Kunti’s advice? Was it because he wanted to prove his loyalty to Duryodhana? He certainly said so. But tell me how was sparing the life of the nemesis of Duryodhana proves is loyalty? What right he had to take a vow that directly hurt the interest of his friend, benefactor and the King? Did Karna ever tell Duryodhana that he wouldn’t kill his enemies? NO. He hid his secret pact with the enemies. And if this is not treason, what is?

And no single deed of a an evil, wicked, conspiring, molester and traitor can make him qualify as a good person, forget best person.
