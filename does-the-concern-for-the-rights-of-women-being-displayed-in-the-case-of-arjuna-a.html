Does the concern for the rights of women being displayed in the case of Arjuna abducting Subhadra indicate that the present generation of Quorans do not mind critisising even the past heroes if the honour of a woman is compromised?

📅 Aug 19, 2021 10:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The premise of the question is wrong.

Present generation of Quorans don’t mind criticizing even the past heroes if the honor of a woman is compromised.

Criticism is the cheapest and easiest thing to do. I wish it is done responsibly. Unfortunately the criticism in this case on Quora has less to do with any intention of defending the right of the woman and more for the sake of condemning the heroes.

If those critics were really sincere they would have redirected their condemnation towards Karna’s abduction of Kashi Princess (populary known as Bhanumati) for Duryodhana, with equal vigor if not more, who was abducted against her wishes. But do we ever see such accusations by those Qurans who openly condemn Arjuna and Krishna for Shubhadras’s abduction?

If these critics were really honest in their intention, they would have read the epic. And had they read the epic they would have surely come across this line —

Kunti’s son rushed at her and forced her onto his chariot. Abducting the sweet-smiling one, that tiger among men set out for his own city on his chariot, as if on air. On seeing Subhadra thus carried away, all the armed soldiers who were with her raised an alarm and dashed towards the city of Dvaraka.

—Shubhadra Harana

Now what does this sweet-smile reflect? Was abduction against her wishes? Was Sita smiling during her abduction? Was Draupadi smiling during her abduction? Was Duryodhana’s wife smiling? She had certainly expressed her lack of interest in Duryodhana, which is mentioned here.

While the names of the kings were being recounted, the beautiful maiden passed by the son of Dhritarashtra.27 Kouravya Duryodhana could not tolerate that he had been passed by. Ignoring all the kings, he asked the maiden to stop. Protected by Bhishma and Drona, he was intoxicated by his valour. He lifted the maiden up onto his chariot and challenged the kings. O bull among men! Karna wielded a sword and had donned arm-guards and finger-guards. Riding on a chariot, the best among all wielders of weapons guarded him28

—Shanti Parva, BORI

So you may wonder, why those defenders of woman right and dignity either remain mum or praise this act as Karna’s valor?

Let me reinforce the idea. If those critics were honest in their intent, they would have tried to find more detailed reference about the relationship of Arjuna and Subhadra. Bhagwatam clearly depicts Shubhadra’s affection for Arjuna. But even if we choose to concentrate only on Mahabharata (BORI), even a casual perusal would reveal the following facts —

  • Subhadra was mentioned smiling during her abduction, which is unlikely if it was against her will.
  • Not only Krishna, even Balarama or Vasudeva doesn’t resent his action.
  • Shubhadra never complained against Arjuna.
  • Subhadra supported Arjuna throughout her life. This was confirmed even by Duryodhana.
  • Subhadra was respected and honored by all.

So what is real complaint of the defender of woman right? On whose behalf are they really complaining when Subhadra by all account was integral part of Arjuna’s life and that of Pandavas?

So do you really call these critics defenders of woman right or dignity when the create ruckus without valid reference? Can we really call them critics?

Criticism, without honest intent and valid reference is not criticism — it’s slander!

— Vivek Dutta Mishra
