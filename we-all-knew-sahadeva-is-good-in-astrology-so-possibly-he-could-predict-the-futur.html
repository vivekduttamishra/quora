We all knew Sahadeva is good in astrology. So possibly he could predict the future even kuruchetra war too. But why he was not disclosed about the same before to stop the war? What was reason behind his silence?

📅 Jun 24, 2020 08:16 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Sahdev wasn't a astrologer is very well explained by @Lakshmi Telidevara and reference to its origin Astrology by Hari Krishnan.

But despite the fact that first part of the question is irrelevant, second interestingly remains relevant if we substitute Krishna for Sahadev —

  • Krishna predicted the outcome more than once.
    • Warned Hastinapur court and projected his Virat swaroop.
    • While talking to Karna he gave detailed projection of the fate of war.
  • Karna, too understood the fate as was clear from his response to Karna.
  • In Virat was Arjun single hsndededly routed Hastinapur troops and champions and you don't need an astrologer to predict next outcome.
  • Arjuna realized it, and refused to fight.

Why everyone was silent and did nothing to prevent the war?

  • Pandavas did their best to avoid the war. They agreed to just five village? Was it a silence?
  • Krishna showed his Virat swaroop to bring Kauravas to peace talk? How loud can one go?
  • Aruna gave up fight.

So why did battle happen?

The major problem with a questions like this is an assumption that war is bad and must be avoided.

What are we supposed to do against aggressive neighbors like China and Pakistan? What should have we done in Kargil or Mumbai attack? Was surgical strike unjustified?

War was inevitable; A more disastrous alternative was not really an alternative — Let Adharma rule. That would be victory for deceit, brutal ambition, and a failing society.

Destiny has a balancing nature. Even Kauravas were unable to live in a world of their greet and brutality. After Krishna's visit to Hastinapur, Duryodhan sends a message to the Pandavas via Shakuni's son Ulluk. He reminds Pandavas every injustice done to them and provokes them to fight even reminding Arjuna and Bheem their oaths.

The war was inevitable and needed for

  1. Establishing justice in that period.
  2. A lesson for all times to come.

Those who feel all war for any reason must be avoided should read Gita.
