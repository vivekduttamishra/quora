Mahatma Gandhi said 'Industrialise and perish'. Sir M. Vishweshwarayya said 'Industrialise or perish'. Who proved right?

📅 Apr 26, 2022 02:04 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Before we can answer the question, we need to fix to important flaws of the question —

  1. It assumes that two statement must be mutually contradictory and one must necessarily be absolute truth and other absolute false. Well surprise. Human civilization doesn’t stand on boolean.
  2. The question assumes, half truth can prove a point and quotes Mahatma Gandhi’s view in a flawed way.

Was Mahatma Gandhi against industrialization?

The most straight forward answer is NO. He was against a particular form of industrialization, a blind rush towards European model, which was totally based on the exploitation of other nations and killing the competitions. In such a mad rush, he saw no wisdom and what may apply to a small developed country like Engliand or underpopulated country like Russia, wouldn’t necessarily be beneficial to large, underdeveloped, over populated and badly exploited nation like India. India needed a different model of industrialization — A distributed model where power and wealth is not concentrated at the hand of a few mighty and powerful corporates.

Now even Sir M. Vishweshwarayya, didn’t advocate exploitation based industry. He was a brilliant Engineer and focused on how engineering could help a countries growth and how it can be adopted in building industry. But he wasn’t a busineman and industry as we know is part engineering, part management, part finance and a lot about the ethics of business. Sri Vishweshwarayya didn’t talk about those aspects.

But visionary, as he was, Mahatma Gandhi had a blueprint for an alternative for industrialization — Cottage industry. This was his way for a distributed growth, co-existence, human empowerment and entrepreneurship. This industry is not mutually exclusive to the visions of Vishweshwarayya. This too would need machines, but will try to vest power with individuals?

So who won? Well the good news is, one need not lose for the other to win. Apply the two visions together and world is a better place.

So let us see, where Gandhiji’s vision takes us. Have you heard about Whatsapp? or Facebook? They are the examples of cottage industry. You don’t need to be a giant industry to like Google, Microsoft or Yahoo to rule the world. You can be a team of college left outs and you can still dream big to form a new industry. Do you know about modern term ‘startups’. They are the realization of Gandhiji’s dream — Where you can start from scratch and grow big without exploiting or getting exploited by others. Empowering the masses. Have you seen a uber driver or a zomato delivery guy? They don’t beg. They don’t survive on alms. They are industry in themselves. They can earn their dignified living. And that is Gandhiji’s vision of industrialization.

Hope that clears the doubts.
