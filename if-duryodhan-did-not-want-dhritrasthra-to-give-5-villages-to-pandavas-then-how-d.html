If Duryodhan did not want Dhritrasthra to give 5 villages to Pandavas, then how did he allow him to give Indraprasth to Pandavas in dyut Sabha after winning every properties of Pandavas?

📅 Dec 15, 2020 11:31 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Have you heard the term — cornered cat? Well, interestingly, that is the answer to both instances.

Incident 1 — Dhritrashtra returns Indraprastha to Pandavas

It was a momentary decision of Dhritarashtra to pacify Pandavas. There attempt to disrobe Draupadi had failed. Bhima was furious and too the ferocious oath to annihilate all Karuava brothers with brutal deaths for Duryodhana and Dussassana. Court had started censuring their act. A terrefied Dhirtrashtra decided to return the kingdom. An equally stupified and terrified Duryodhana couldn’t object immediately. He was like a cornered cat. A terrified one.

But that was the spur of the moment. As it is welknown, their really didn’t return Indraprastha rather put forward another dyut game in which Pandavas lost everything and were exiled.

Incident 2 — Duryodhana refuses to give even five village.

Now Pandavas had nothing to loose. The thirteen years of exile had made them stronger and Duryodhana had multiple taste of their power — Gandharva War, Keechak’s death, Virata war. He knew he had no deceit left in his arsenal against Pandavas. He can’t win by cheating.

But then Pandavas sent a proposal for five villages only. In my understanding here he made two grave calculation mistake —

  • He mistook Pandava’s genorsity for their weakness and may have thought he could get away be giving them nothing.
  • He overestimated the valour of Karna and though he would win the war for him, little knowing that Karna’s arrogance was so great that he wont even participate in more than half the war.

Once again he was a cornered cat. Out of any option of any further defeat and stuck on his own web of miscalculations, he refused. And rest is… Mahabharata
