Why did Arjuna have to kill Karna while he was unarmed when he could have easily defeated him being a better warrior?

📅 May 08, 2021 07:33 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Now matter what Arjuna would have done, Karna’s death would always have been an unfair one.

It was an unfair and unequal fight. There was no match.

Arjuna was a brave powerful warrior who had even satisfied Mahadeva with his valor and had never tasted defeat even once till that day other than at the hands of mighty Shiva. And that defeat brought greater honor than any victory ever would.

On the other hand, Karna was a midcore fighter, who not only knew the taste of defeat well, but also had a knack of finding escape routes from impossible situation. He tried the same during his last battle — asked Arjuna to adhere to Dharam. And he might as well has succeeded in exploiting the gentle nature of Arjuna, had Krishna, the manifestation of Dharma itself not intervened.

As per authentic epic references, Karna was without chariot but not unarmed. And anyone with even basic understanding of epic or Divyastras could understand that Karna couldn’t have been unarmed.

But it doesn’t matter. People would find fault with Arjuna. People would call Karna’s death unfair. And I can’t disagree —

No matter how Arjuna would have killed Karna it would still have been unfair, because the two warriors were of different league. It was unfair of Arjuna to have fought a mediocre warrior like Karna.

Questions like these may give some solace to those who find Karna a tragic hero rather than an evil person he really was. They may even feel that question like these caste suspicion on Arjuna’s credibly as a warrior. But look carefully, once again —

Arjuna defeated Karna in every direct and indirect face-offs. Did he still need a prove that he could defeat Karna? Won’t that be his arrogance if he had let the battle prolong for few more days to convince few people that he was righteous? Can anyone give assurance that next time they would have faced each other, Karna won’t be disarmed? Can some one say be confidence, next time he won’t have asked Arjuna to stop?

Important question is, did Arjuna disarm Karna by deceit? Did he break his bow from behind? Did he attack a lone Karna in a pack?

Interestingly no one asks why Karna attacked unarmed Bhima? They count it a victory of Karna! They don’t much care if Karna attacked Abhimanyu in pack. It didn’t matter.

Honestly, I too don’t care what Karna did to Bhima or Abhimanyu. I never expected anything ethical from Karna. That was his character.

True, Arjuna fought Karna alone and he disarmed him fairly, and it wasn’t Arjuna fault that Karna’s chariot got stuck up in mud and neither was it Arjuna’s responsibility to provide weapon or chariot to Karna. True, warriors including Arjuna fought even from the ground never asking intermission from their enemies.

Despite of all these being true, and despite knowing that no matter what Arjuna would have done, people would still call it unfair, I too sometimes feel, Arjuna could have been even more fairer in the final duel with Karna. Not because Karna deserved that, but because it is Arjuna we are talking about.
