Was Bhima's vow that he would break Duryodhana's thighs is a farsighted plan as this was the only way DuryODhana can be bested by him in a duel with maces?

📅 Sep 09, 2021 01:42 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: NO

Bhima’s oath was not an example of his farsightedness or seer good fortune, it was meant to be a punishment for Duryodhana’s crime — He dared to ask Draupadi to come and sit on his thigh. He deserved his punishment.

Now coming to the next part —

WAS THERE ONLY WAY TO KILL DURYODHANA?

  1. Unlike popular perception, Duryodhana had no boon from Gandhari that made him indestructible.
  2. Duryodhana on multiple occasion accepted that he can’t beat Bhima or Arjuna. He saw his brothers killed and could do nothing.
  3. Bhima easily defeated Shalya and Duryodhana in a battle following Draupadi’s swaymver.
  4. There is no mention of Duryodhana’s great feat anywhere expect his last day on which he really fought with all his strength.

Duryodhana may have died with his head smashed or ribs bone broken. He wasn’t that great a warrior as no victory is recorded in his name. But he had to die with his thigh smashed and he met his death in the same way.

WHY WAS HE PRAISED AFTER HIS DEATH?

Who is not praised after his death? That is called condolence. You praise even those whom you never liked while living. How many times have we heard glorification of passed away politician and no significant actors as being referred as — end of an era or a unrecoverable loss?

Duryodhana fought with great effort on the last day. He was praised with glorified superlatives. But nothing changes the facts.
