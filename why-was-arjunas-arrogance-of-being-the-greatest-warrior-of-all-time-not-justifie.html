Why was Arjuna's arrogance of being the greatest Warrior of all time not justified in the Mahabharata?

📅 Aug 26, 2021 09:33 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

I think the question is a classical example of two negatives cancel out each other. It is rare that in a two parts of a question, both part can be so wrong—

What Arjuna arrogant?

There is not a single mention of Arjuna being arrogant in the entire epic. He never said he was the greatest warrior of the era. He never tried to prove himself better than everyone. He always showed respect and humility even in the face of victory.

  • During Rajsuy yagna when Bhagdatta accepted that he couldn’t continue to fight him, he gracefully stopped the battle.
  • After swaymvara battle, he let scared Karna retreat although he could have followed him and forced a battle if he so wanted.
  • During Gandharva battle when Chitrasen became feeble and presented himself as a friend, Arjuna immediately stopped the battle.
  • After Gandharva battle neither he nor any Pandava rubbed humiliations of defeat on Kaurava’s and let them retreat.
  • During Matasya battle Arjuna had entire Hastinapur army and generals under his control. Unconscious. He could have them imprisoned or killed. But once again he was gentle in his behavior.
  • Which arrogant person would lay down his weapon and prospect of victory for the welfare of their crooked relatives? At least we don’t have any parallels in the epic.

Who was the greatest warrior of the era?

I would suggest ask this question to Karna or to Ashwatthama or Drona or Bhishma — they would give the same answer — one that was proclaimed by Parshurama, Krishna and above all God of Gods Mahadeva Shiva!

Do you need more hints — He remained unvanquished all his life. Krishna called him his inseparable existence and Shiva bestowed him with his blessings. He who dispersed the array of arrogant warriors after Draupadi’s swyamvara. Yes, the same person who singly wrecked havoc on a large army led by Bhishma, Drona, Duryodhana, Ashwatthama, Kripacharya and Karna.

I hope that should settle the confusion.
