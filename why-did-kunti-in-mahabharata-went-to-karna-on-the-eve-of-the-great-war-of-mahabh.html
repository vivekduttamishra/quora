Why did Kunti in Mahabharata went to Karna on the eve of the great war of Mahabharata?

📅 Jan 24, 2021 11:01 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Before we understand the answer let us understand Kunti and circumstances of Karna’s birth.

  • Kunti was a role model for a virtuous lady — and obedient and caring daughter, and an incredible host. Her virtues earned him a special boon from rishi Durvasa. She can summon any God to get her a child.
  • Now she was an unmarried young woman. And as goes the saying curiosity killed the cat, she makes a blunder. She invokes the mantra out of curiosity. She shouldn’t have done this, but she did it anyway.
  • Suryadev comes to Kunti. Kunti explains her mistake and requests him again and again to return. Not only Surya was not ready to return, he threatened to curse Kunti and his family.
  • Kunti pleads, she can’t become un-wed mother as it would bring disgrace to her family as per the prevailing custom. You must recall, even Satyavati never shared identity of her son (Ved Vyas) with her husband before or after the marriage.
  • Suryadev still didn’t budge. Now she had to choose between her family and herself (dignity). She chooses her family over herself and for their sake surrenders herself to Suryadev.
  • She knew she couldn’t keep her son without bringing disgrace to both his family and his son. Had she kept his son, it would have disgraced his family and no king may have married her.
  • To ensure his unborn son’s safety, she asks for a divine armour and ear-ring to protect him from any danger. His son would get a unique gift, all due to her insistence.
  • Kunti had to let her son go — not for her own dignity but for his family and even his son. But she didn’t just abandon him. The chest was carefully designed for his saftey and stuffed with riches. In fact this richness got Karna his first name — Vasusen. Then again, Kunti had her spies follow the basket who informed her that Adhiratha, a close friend of Prince Dhritrashtra had adopted his son.

If you carefully check the entire chain of events you would realize that in the entire episode, Kunti’s actions were to seek the welfare of her family — her father and her son. She surrendered herself for his family, but didn’t surrender his son’s safety. Had she been selfish she could have killed him rather than ensuring his safety.

Now let us come to the main question —

WHO WAS WINNING THE WAR?

Now even before the war began, almost everyone who knew anything about military and warfare knew Pandavas were winning the war —

  • Arjuna had defeated all the elite warriors together and single-handedly.
  • Krishna was supporting Pandavas and had pronounced their victory openly in Hastinapur’s court room.
  • Parshuram had declared that Pandavas would win the war.
  • Bhishma and Dronacharya too were convinced Pandavas would win the war.
  • Even Karna knew Pandavas are winning the war. He admitted the same to Krishna during Krishna-Karna meeting.
  • Unknown to most of them Shiva willed Pandavas victory.

If Pandavas were winning the war, why did Kunti approached Karna?

A war even in victory causes devastations. Kunti knew Karna hated his own brothers because of evil delusions. She was tormented by this — brothers fighting brothers. She wanted to change the situation..

Because of his evil delusions, he has always hated the Pandavas. He is immensely strong in his resolution and he is also powerful. Karna has always been against the Pandavas and I am now tormented by that. Today, I hope to turn Karna’s mind towards the Pandavas.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 7551-7553).

She approaches Karna to disclose his true identity so that he comes out of his delusions and do what is right —

Let those who are evil bow down. Let Karna and Arjuna be like Rama53 and Janardana.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: (Kindle Locations 7577-7578).

Even Surya recommended Karna to follow her sage advise.

‘Then Karna heard a voice that issued from the sun. It was spoken affectionately like a father and came from the sun god himself. “O Karna! Pritha has spoken the truth. Act in accordance with your mother’s words. O tiger among men! You will obtain the greatest benefit, if you act entirely in accordance with her words.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 7581-7583).

Wasn’t she afraid for the lives of Parthas?

Yes. She was of course worried about the life of Parthas. Not just for five Parthas but for six Parthas. Perhaps most for the 6th Partha — Karna.

You see war always brings devastations. And it wasn’t fair that one brother kills another for no reason. Karna was strong and he could have killed some of his brothers.

But to be honest all the past encounter between Karna and Arjuna have see Arjuna thrashing Karna and the same pattern repeated even in Kurukshetra later. With Krishna on his side, her worry was more for Karna was, she knew, is delusioned and siding with evil. Natually she wished well for him as well as her other sons.

Why didn’t she approached her sooner?

Being a Partha wasn’t a bed or roses. Partha’s suffered in exile most of their lives. Karna on the other hand enjoyed Kingship most of his life. It would have been selfish of Kunti to disclose the secret sooner and rob Karna from his comforts. Afterall she was also guilt-riddent for letting him go as an infant. She might have thought, she had no right to rob his happiness again.

But approaching decisive war changed everything. This time everyone knew would be a war with no return. She could wait no longer.

WHY DID SHE ASKED KARNA TO SPARE THE LIVES OF HIS FOUR SONS?

Kunti never asked this. It was Karna’s proactive offer, which of course Kunti couldn’t refuse. Why should she refuse such a grand offer. One of his son suggested that he would not kill his four brothers. That was a victory of her love. She gladly accepted it.

But if you ask me, it was cruel of Karna to say a mother that he would kill one of his son, knowing he was talking about his own younger brother.

Now there is an interesting twist here. Karna requested Krishna not to disclose this secret to Pandavas. Why? Because he knew Pandavas knowing his identity would sacrifice their right but won’t fight him. Unfortunately Karna couldn’t bring himself to have heart so great as to spare Arjuna’s life as well.

Here I answered why Kunti approached Karna based on the authentic sources of Mahabharata.
