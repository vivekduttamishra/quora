Ignoring the baseless and absurd claims made in this question, let me focus on the last part of the question.

Who is a just king?

I believe a just king is one who delivers the justice, without caring about caste, race or gender. He doesn't let useless egos cloud his judgement. Why should a criminal be let off, just because she happens to be a ‘she’? Where is the justice in it?

A just king should also ensure that punishment is proportionate and after you have tried to warn off the criminal. This is what Rama did.

Was Ravana a just king?

Forget just, Ravana wasn't a king. He was a coward thug, posing as the king. He was a robber and a molester with series of crimes tagged to his career.

Had he been a king or with slightest notion of justice, he wouldn't have kidnapped Sita. He would have punished Rama. But he dared not attack him directly. So like a lowly thug abducted her when Rama was away.

If you missed the facts, the only reason he didn't touch Sita was because the coward was afraid to touch her due to the curse of her other victim, Vedvati.

So, how do you compare a just king with no lust for power and who can sacrifice his family for the kingdom, with an evil man who let his entire clan perish just for his lust?
