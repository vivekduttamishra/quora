When Sardar Patel won the majority, then who made Nehru as PM?

📅 Jun 09, 2022 04:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I believe the questioner knows the answer or has the delusion that he knows the answer. So let us tackle the question from the proper perspective.

In India voting for the Prime Minister and President takes place using different processes. Generally a PM is selected from among the people who are directly voted by the people of India. Whereas voting for the president is done using a electoral college that consists of MLAs and MPs. This is the process of indirect votes and people of India don’t have a direct say in this election.

In traditional voting process, that happened for the first time in 1951, Nehru won with a convincing majority in votes casted be the people of India. He continued to get elected unopposed in the democratic process in 1956 and 1962.

But I believe the question doesn’t refer to the actual election of PM by the people of India. I believe the question seeks response about the election for the post of congress president in 1946, which in all probability was going to be the first care-taker Prime Minister of dominion of India (not the republic of India). This election can not and should not be equated to a direct election as it was more like a internal voting among the committee members who were never, in the first place, elected to elect the Prime Minister of India. It was choosing a party president. In this internal party election Sardar Patel won with a thumping majority. But then it wasn’t people’s mandate. Just the mandate of an internal committee.

And now coming to the answer —

Who made Nehru as PM?

The credit of this visionary step goes to Mahatma Gandhi. He chose Nehru over Patel, a selection that was not only endorsed by the people of India repeatedly but also by Sardar Patel when he said —

Since Gandhiji’s death we have realized that our leader’s judgment was correct’

—Sardar Patel, on Gandhiji’s decision about Nehru

What some may find interesting and objectionable is Gandhiji wasn’t even a member of congress at this point of time and had no official say in the process. But then an equal and undeniable fact is no one had more endorsement in India 1946 from the people than Gandhiji.

Another point that must be noted, is this decision was neither random nor spontaneous. Gandhiji had declared Nehru as his candidate for future Prime Minister several years ago and not without a reason. Nehru was fifteen years younger to then 72 years old Sardar Patel who was ailing from TB and struggling from a poor health which led to his demise in 1950 and wasn’t there when people voted their first Prime Minister.

But if people still doubt, had Patel been alive or had a true election happened earlier, who among Patel or Nehru had won the people mandate, would be clear from the answer of Sardar Patel, one he gave to American journalist Vincent Sheean, with reference to a huge crowd that had surrounded him and Nehru in Bombay (now Mumbai): ‘They come for Jawahar, Not for me’.

I would like to take this platform to condemn the contemporary and rotten mindset where people take pride in praising one leader at the cost of condemning another leader. These leader, Patel, Nehru, Bose and Gandhi gave their entire life for the nation and took decisions that they deemed fit in their wisdom. They often differed and disagreed, but never disrespected each other. After the election was over, Nehru and Patel shared great synergy working together as a team to build our nation. And only people carrying such kind of delusion based on social media propaganda today can try to pit them against each other with such stupid questions.
