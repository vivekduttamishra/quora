Is Mahabharata the first nuclear war in history?

📅 May 23, 2020 11:51 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This question exposes two interesting limitations of human reasoning —

  1. Inability to comprehend beyond modern scientific achievements.
  2. Not realizing analogy by definition is not exact.

Our epics refer to celestial weapons and not nuclear weapons and we must understand that the two are different. They both can wrec havoc — that's analogy. But that doesn't make them same.

Celestial weapons weren't nuclear weapons.

  1. Celestial weapons were divine. There isn't anything divine about Nuclear weapons.
  2. Celestial weapons required personal worth unlike nuclear weapon that requires just a remote. Arjuna God Pashupatastra from Shiva. No one could have used it other than him.
  3. Karna got the knowledge of Brahmastra from Parshuram, along with a curse. If it were a nuclear weapon, the cause won't matter — trigger would.
  4. Any one can possess a nuclear weapon. It's just formula and some blueprint. Divyastra wasn't something you create.
  5. Have you heard about nuclear radiation? Do you find any mention in the epics?

So, Neither Ramayana nor Mahabharata was a nuclear war.
