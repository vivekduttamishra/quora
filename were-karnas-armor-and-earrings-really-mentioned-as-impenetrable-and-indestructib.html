Were Karna’s armor and earrings really mentioned as impenetrable and indestructible in Bori Mahabharata?

📅 Dec 03, 2023 08:05 AM PST  |  🌐 English

The term impenetrable is a relative term and often used in an exaggerated way. Does it mean absolutely impenetrable? Or impenetrable compared ordinary armours? Did the armour covered head and foot also or just the torso? And if it was absolutely impenetrable, how did Karna cut it from his body to give to Indra?

There are a number of occasions where Karna’s body were penetrated like during Draupadi ‘s Swyamvara and ghosa battle. That proves, either it wasn't fully impenetrable or it didn't matter if he can be gravely injured despite wearing it.

But one thing is sure, it could protect atmost the bodily injuries and not the soul. It could protect the wearer from death, but not fear leading to defeat. Karna continued to be defeated, continued to be flee battles out of fear.
