Did Arjuna betray Draupadi by stopping bhim from helping her and himself remaining silent?

📅 Dec 30, 2020 07:51 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Arjuna and all Pandavas did betray Draupadi as they couldn’t prevent her molestation and Krishna a.k.a. Dharma had to intervene to protect her. Arjuna too was guilty of inaction by our contemporary standards.

We must first understand there is a difference between law and justice. Justice is Dharma, law or protocol is just a mechanism to uphold justice. Unfortunately, sometimes to uphold the law, you end up sacrificing justice. Take the example of Nirbhaya case. One of the most brutal of all the criminals went unpunished. Why? Because as per juvenile justice, he couldn’t be punished. Now the question is —

  • Was the law upheld? YES.
  • Was justice served? NO.

Didn’t our law and our constitution failed Nirbhaya?

All of us know the answer. Yes. We failed her. Our law failed to deliver the justice. We may have to change the law, to serve better justice. But till that day, unfortunately, we can’t punish that brutal underage evil. We would all remain guilty of not delivering the justice to Nirbhaya.

Arjuna was bound by the same law. Just like we saw, we felt the agony, but could do nothing for Nirbhaya, Arjuna remained moot, and in agony. Arjuna failed Draupadi the same way our law failed Nirbhaya.

Contemporary or Ancient, law or justice, everyone condemns molesters.

But let us not lose the main picture. Contemporary or ancient, law or justice, no one fails to condemn molesters. We failed Nirbhaya, but we didn’t brutalize her. That was done by those henious criminals who used the loop-holes of the law and deceit to subvert justice. And while Arjuna failed Draupadi as law failed Nirbhaya, Duryodhana, Karna and Dussassana like a gang of molestors actually moested Draupadi.

You can’t compare brutal animals like Duryodhana-Karna-Dussassana trio with helpless upholders of the law like Arjuna.

Did Arjuna stopped Bhima from helping Draupadi?

The current accusation which is specifically on Arjuna that he stopped Bhima from helping her is a distortion of the fact.

  • Bhima wasn’t trying to protect Draupadi, he was exploding on Yudhishthir for having committed the mistake.

I think you committed a most improper act in staking Droupadi. She did not deserve this. After obtaining the Pandavas, this maiden is suffering this despicable and cruel oppression from the Kouravas only because of your act. O king! It is because of her that my anger descends on you. I will burn your hands.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3343-3345).

  • Not a minor mistake, but a blunder, it was, but Bhima’s reaction wasn’t helping either Draupadi or Pandavas; It was just helping Kaurava’s. They reached closest to splitting Pandavas that they had been trying for a long time.
  • Arjuna helped Bhima see the err or his ways and foiled the final ploy of team Duryodhana that was to create rift in them.

‘Arjuna said, “O Bhimasena! Never before have you uttered words like these. The cruel enemies have destroyed your pride in dharma. You should not make the desires of the enemy come true. Observe the supreme dharma. According to dharma, one should never cross one’s elder brother. The king was challenged and he followed the dharma of the kshatriyas. He gambled because of the desires of the enemy. That is our great deed.”

‘Bhimasena replied, “O Dhananjaya! Had I not known that he has not done it for himself, I would forcibly have grasped his hands and burnt them in the blazing fire.”’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3346-3350).

So It is illogical to —

  1. Suggest that Arjuna stopped Bhima from helping her.
  2. Comapre the incation of Pandavas with the crimes of Kauravas.
