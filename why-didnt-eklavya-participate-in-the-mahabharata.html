Why didn't Eklavya participate in the Mahabharata?

📅 Aug 24, 2021 09:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Eklavya didn’t participate in Mahabharata war because he was dead long before the war happened. His met his maker at the hand of Shri Krishna and the same was confirmed by Shri Krishna on the fourteenth day of Mahabharata battle after the death of Ghatotkatch.

Eklavya, in my understanding is an overhyped character in modern era. The epic finds just three reference of him.

  1. At an early age he aspired to be an archer and approached Drona, who refused to accept him as his disciple. Ignoring his explicit denial, Eklavaya continued to learn archery considering Drona as his guru. Drona asked for his thumb.
  2. Eklavya went on to become King of his tribe and by all accounts was a close ally of Jarashandha, who was famed evil king of that era. We find his presence during Yudhishthira’s Rajsuy yagna where he was holding his shoes in the ceremony — a ceremonial acceptance of Yudhishthira’s emperorship.
  3. On day 14, after the death of Ghatotkatch, Krishna informs that Eklavaya continued to mount attack against him, most probably to avenge Jarasandha’s death and was finally killed by Krishna on his mission to eradicate all evils.

It may not be out of place to bust a common myth that Eklavya was reborn and Dhristhduymna which would be apparent if you checkout point 2 above — Both Dhristduymna and Eklavaya were present during Rajsuy yagna.
