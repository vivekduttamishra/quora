Hi, Thanks for the A2A.

But let us understand no one becomes Mahatma by a single incident. So Gandhiji cannot be defined by a single movement.

What made Gandhiji a Mahatma was his capacity to empathize with humanity, in fact even with animals with same zeal as to fight for the freedom. He didn't just participate for the political freedom, but worked for the humanity in general.

He travelled across the length and width of India to understand its diverse nature before taking the political baton. No wonder non-cooperation movement was the first nation wide movement. It didn’t just remained a political movement but united India with a common cause.

His movements be it boycotting the foreign goods or adopting Khadi were aimed at bridging the divide based on economy, region or religion. He knew a nation ridden with illiteracy and poverty can’t be transformed into a warring clan, but can be transformed into Satyagrahi — a force, no power of the world can crush.

His emphasis on cleanliness, and against untouchability and cottage industry were the blueprint of a truly free India — not just political that most leaders strived for but a India free from hunger and disease, an India that would be self reliant, where individuals would own responsibility and each Indian would transform into an enterprise.

What made him a leader was his emphasis on practice before your preach.

But what made him truly a Mahatma was his contribution in safe keeping the peace in Calcutta during Partition. If you go through historical comparison of two borders where partition happened — Punjab and Bengal, you would understand why history uses the term Miracle of Bengal. The role of Mahatma Gandhi in upholding peace in a time where an entire Army miserably failed is unrivalled in the world history and this single act alone is sufficient to confer the title of Mahatma on Gandhiji.

But again, Mahatma Gandhi didn’t become Mahatma because of miracle he performed in Bengal. He managed to perform that miracle because he was already a Mahatma.

As I said no single incident defines a Mahatma. It is some total of his achievements.
