Why did Pandavas not reveal themselves after the period of 13 years was over and spent another five months extra? Didn't they reckon the period properly or was Bhishma wrong?

📅 Nov 24, 2020 10:27 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Let me first clarify the last part of the question first.

Was Bhishma wrong?

Bhishma was the disciple of Vashishtha and Brihaspati and was like a subject-matter-expert in these issues. No one rejected his calculation. Not even Duryodhana, who would have tried every possibility to turn down Pandavas claim as generally shown on TVShows. So, Bhishma wasn’t wrong.

‘Bhishma said, “Parts of kala s, muhurta s, 128 days, fortnights, months, nakshatras, planets, seasons and years are joined together and the wheel of time revolves in accordance with these measurements of time. Because of an excess of time and deviations in the paths of bodies in the sky, there is an addition of two months in every cycle of five years.129 I think that calculated in this way, during the passage of thirteen years, there are five additional months and twelve nights.130 They have done everything that they had promised to do.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1738-1742).

To this, Duryodhana said —

‘Duryodhana replied, “O grandfather! I will not give the kingdom away to the Pandavas. Therefore, without any delay, let us make arrangements for the battle.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1754-1755). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Now if Duryodhana had a disagreement, he would have refuted Bhishma’s claim. There are other indicators as well that proves

Why Pandava’s tenure was positively over.

1. Bhishma gives a logical and irrefutable reason.

They do not wish to acquire the kingdom by any means possible. Else, the descendants of the Kuru lineage would have displayed their valour at that time. They are bound by the nooses of dharma and they have not deviated from the vow of kshatriyas. If there is a choice between being called untruthful and confronting defeat, the Parthas would choose death over being untruthful.—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1745-1748). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

2. It must be noted that there is only one lie mentioned in the account of Yudhishthir, proving Yudhisthir didn’t lie about his tenure to be over.

3. Dharmaraj himself granted boon to Yudhisthir that they would not be recognized while in Agyatvas —

‘Yudhishthira said, “The twelve years of dwelling in the forest have passed and the thirteenth has arrived. Wherever we may live, may people not be able to recognize us.”’

Vaishampayana said, ‘The illustrious one granted him that boon. He again comforted Kounteya, for whom truth was his valour. “O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Even if you travel the earth in your own forms, no one in the three worlds will be able to recognize you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8757-8760).

Now this proves, beyond doubt that the period was over before they could be recognized.

That brings to the first part of the question

Why didn’t Pandavas revealed themselves earlier?

There could be so many reasons. Since it is not clearly indicated we would list the most possible ones —

  1. In India there are different calendars and different ways to calculate. Pandavas may be waiting for all of them to conclusively indicates that the period was over.
  2. They may be preparing themselves before they would reveal their credentials. They may be waiting for some news from allies like Krishna and Panchal.

Whatever be their reasons, there are four prime indicators to proof that the period was conclusively over —

  1. Bhishma detailed explanation of why the period was over.
  2. Dharma’s boon to Yudhishthir
  3. Yudhishthir’s own character and adherence to dharma.
  4. Duryodhana’s acceptance to Bhishma’s calculation.
