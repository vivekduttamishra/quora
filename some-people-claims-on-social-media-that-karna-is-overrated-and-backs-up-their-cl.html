The Grand Epic Mahabharat

An incident that happened over five thousand years ago, passed onto us by Maharishi Vyas, entrusted to us a prized possession so we pass it on to our generation next. Why? Because the epic contains a lesson, it contains the DNA of the civilization of its time and the definition of good and evil.

I won’t answer this question for/against Karna/Arjuna, but I would stand here on behalf of the epic Mahabharat, which is constantly being distorted to an extent that a question like this should be answered.

And since I stand for the epic, I won’t ask you to go and read it, lest it may lie.

  • Let us now look at each major episode through three different lenses with single objective to answer this question alone.
  1. Gurudakshina Battle — Drona seeks imprisionment of Drupad from his disciples.
    1. BR Chopra — Team Duryodhana including Karna, looses the battle
    2. Star Plus / Karna doesn't take part in the war
    3. Suryaptura Karna — Karna was busy fighting Jarasandha far away

      Can we agree that later the stories are progressive attempts to distort epic?
  2. Lakshagrih — When Duryodhan conspired to burn Pandavas alive.
    1. BR Chopra/Star Plus Mahabharat — Karna disagrees with the plan but still goes with it.
    2. Suryaputra Karna — Sets Duryodhana’s palace at fire. Guess what no one knew this story ever.
      Can we again see the exaggeration in the tale with each passing day?
  3. Draupadi Swyamber — When Arjuna wins Draupadi Swaymber, Karna fights Arjuna along with other Kings thinking him Brahmin.
    1. BR Chopra — Arjuna breaks Karna’s bow. Karna retreats suggesting that only Indra, Drona and Arjuna could have done this.
    2. Star Plus Mahabharat — Karna refuses to fight Arjuna.
    3. Suryaputra Karna — He wasn’t really fighting, just trying to verify if he is Arjuna. Once he finds out he retreats.
  4. Draupaid Cheerharan
    1. BRChopra — Karna calls Draupadi Vaishya.
    2. Start Plus Mahabharat — Karna abuses Draupadi, and calls him Vasya, but only to remind their imaginary insult in Dyutsabha, where there were asked to surrender their weapon, An incident not only missing in original epics but also BRChopra Mahabharata.
    3. Suryaputra Karna — Actually warns Pandavas against the conspiracy by his friend. He cried and pleads Duryodhana to spare Draupadi.
  5. Gandharva Battle — Duryodhana and team are out to mock Pandavas in Vanvas, where Duryodhan gets captured by Gandharvas and Pandavas rescues him.
    1. BRChopra — Karna failed as he was drunk. but came back to save Duryodhana. This varies from Mahabharata where Karna looses, escapes and never returns.
    2. Star Plus Mahabharat/Surya Putra Karna — Skips the episode as if they never happened.
  6. Virata Battle — Arjuna routes entire Hastinapur army
    1. BRChopra — Arjuna defeats entire army including Bhisma, Karna, Drona.
    2. Star Plus Mahabharat — Battle between Karna and Arjuna ends in a stalement with a notional victory for Karna as he reveals Arjuna’s identity. Bhisma intervenes.
    3. Suryaputra Karna — Karna concedes defeat as he made some imaginary promise to Kunti
  7. Abhimanyu Vadh — All Kaurava generals including Drona, Karna, Duryodhna kills Abhimanyu
    1. BRChopra — as per the script. Abhimanyu fought valiantly
    2. Star Plus Mahabharat — Karna gives a mercy death to Abhimanyu against the crulty of Duryodhana
    3. Suryaputra Kara — Karna gives a merchy death to him.

As you can clearly see the exaggaration with each of these narration, which conflict with each other giving sufficient evidence to this question. So tell me what option you have other than going throught the original text.
