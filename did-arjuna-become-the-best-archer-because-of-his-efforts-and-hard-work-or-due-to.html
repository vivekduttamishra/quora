Did Arjuna become the best archer because of his efforts and hard work or due to the advantage that he’s the son of Indra?

📅 Mar 17, 2021 02:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Shouri Das, Thanks for the A2A.

To understand the answer to this question, let us ask ourselves few more questions —

  • There were sons of Dharma, Vayu and Surya. Why didn’t they become the best?
  • Do the son of a great cricketer become a great cricketer? Do the son of a scientist become a great scientist?
  • Have you heard the incident where Arjuna practiced even during the night? Was that hard-work or perk of being the son of Indra?
  • Shiva bestowed his grace on Arjuna. Was it because he was the son of Indra?
  • Did Indra helped Arjuna fight Matasya battle or Gandharva battle?

But yes, anyone can work hard and dispite of hardwork, there is no assurance that you would become the best. Arjuna had few more qualities that made him the best.

  1. Life-Long Learner — Arjuna, unlike most of the other warriors, never stopped learning. Dronacharya wasn’t his last guru. He learnt from Chitrasena, the gandharva and even from the God of the Gods, Mahadeva Shiva. Who could have competed with him?
  2. Perseverance — Arjuna had , and that is how he sustained himself against Mahadeva. Naturally you couldn’t have pleased shiva by running away like some other warriors of his time!
  3. Becoming a guru — Arjuna spread his knowledge. He became an guru. And it is said, the best way to learn is to teach. He was not just a worthy disciple, but also a great teacher.
  4. Adherence to Dharma — Believe it or not, this one quality of the Arjuna ensured that even destiny would cherish him being the best warrior. That's the reason Krishna proudly proclaimed him to be his inseparable existence.

And none of these qualities, were because he was the son of Indra. True Indra might have been proud of him, but Indra didn’t made his life easy. He had to prove his worth before getting any benefit of being his son —

  • Didn’t Indra fought tooth and nail against Arjuna during Khandava Dahan? Who won — the son of Indra or the disciple of Drona?
  • Indra didn’t gave him a straight passage to swarga. He was admitted to swarga only after Mahadev Shiva graced him, and that was no small feat.
