What made BORI consider Draupadi humiliation as an interpolation?

📅 Jul 27, 2020 07:35 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A Surya Selvaraj

The idea that Draupadi’s humilation is an interpolation, is actually an interpolation.

BORI doesn’t consider Draupadi’s humiliation as interpolation.

Draupadi’s humiliation is very much included in BORI depiction. It has tons of references throughout Mahabharata —

  • It has been mentioned by both Pandavas and Draupadi to Krishna in Udyoga Parva
  • Sanjay mentions it to Dhritrashtra.
  • Kunti laments it
  • Ghatotkatch talks about it.
  • Bhima refers to it
  • List is endless.

Probably, what few people would like to pass out as interpolation is the disrobing of Draupadi as it doesn’t have too many cross references. While at most cross reference, Draupadi being dragged to the courtroom by hair is mentioned, people don’t talk openly about her disrobing.

Now even without disrobing, dragging a menstruating woman, clad a single garment, by hair in public is no less humiliation.

But let us once again be clear —

DRAUPADI’S DISROBING INCIDENT IS CLEARLY INCLUDED IN BORI

Then Duhshasana forcibly tugged at Droupadi’s garments. In front of everyone in the sabha, he forcibly pulled. O lord of the earth! As Droupadi’s garment was being tugged away, another similar garment appeared every time.214 At this, a terrible uproar arose. All the assembled kings witnessed the most extraordinary sight in the worlds and approved.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2, Sabha Parva Chapter 27

This is from BORI Mahabharata.

WHAT ABOUT THE LACK OF REFERENCES?

While most of the references fell shy of explicitly mentioning disrobing, I can see simple reasons —

  1. Disrobing wasn’t really successful.
  2. It was too tormenting to be explictly mentioned.

However, that doesn’t mean this incident is absolutely void of cross references. Here is one —

CROSS REFERENCE FOR DRAUPADI’S DISROBING ATTEMPT

This cross reference happens immediately after Duryodhana’s death in Shalya Parva, Chapter 58, Shloka 10 —

‘Droupadi was in her season and they disrespected her. They deprived her of her garment there. Behold. Through Yajnaseni’s39 austerities, in the battle, the sons of Dhritarashtra have been slain by the Pandavas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7,
—BORI Shalya Parva, Chapter 58, Shloka 10

Apart from the cross reference, almost every ancient manuscript mentions Draupadi’s humilation and disrobing. I included references from BORI as the question sought BORI references only.

SUMMARY

  • BORI includes Draupadi’s humilation and disrobing.
  • BORI also includes cross reference of humilation at multiple places
  • BORI also includes cross reference to disrobing in Shalya Parva
  • Almost all ancient manuscripts include Draupadi’s humilation and attempt to disrobe.
  • There was no reason for BORI to consider this episode as interpolation and they didn’t
