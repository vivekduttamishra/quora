Is the story about Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose and Hitler true?

📅 May 15, 2020 11:06 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This story doesn’t appear to have any reference and as much as I have understood Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose including many of his own writing, it sounds illogical. He was far great a strategist, to be mocking someone, from whom he may be seeking alliance, and that too when sitting in a devil’s den. He was a grand strategist and not some arrogant fool. Such stories make mockery of a great leader like Netaji and does more harm than convey his message.

Warning — To every action, there is an unequal and disproportionately great reaction!!!

It has become an unfortunate trend in contemporary India that we try to deglorify one of our heritage or leader in order to glorify the other. Is it really required —

  • If I have to talk great about Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, Do I necessarily need to condemn or criticize Gandhiji or Nehru?
  • Do I need to condemn Bose to prove the superiority of Nehru?

And eye for an eye makes the world blind! — Gandhiji

This is exactly whats happening to our history and culture. In order to stand for one of our leader, we are condemening the other. And soon we will destroy our own legacy just like in the fight to two cats, monkey wins!

Why can’t we realize and appreciate all our great leaders — From Gandhiji to Subhas Chandra Bose, From Chandrashekhar Azad to Maharana Pratap, and from Bhagat Singh to Udham Singh? Won’t we all be reach by a much grander tale?

Few Facts!

  • It was Subhas Chandra Bose who first called Bapu — a name which caught the imagination of masses. It is this name that translated to the father of the nation.
  • Only two freedom fighter finds a place in the original book of Indian constitution in form of a painting. And one of those picture is this. I would recommend to recognize both the face and text in this picture —
  • Had Nehru really not respected Bose, would he have allowed this great honor to be bestowed on Bose?
  • Hitler was indeed a devil and not a friend one should seek. But Netaji wasn’t seeking his friendship, he was seeking cooperation is getting Indian prisioners released that would be part of Indian National Army. While it was a right strategy or wrong can be debated, his nationalist view point can’t be.
  • Same holds true for other great Indian leaders.

Let us not be duped by fake stories, conspiracies who are trying to disintegrate Indian and forcing us to strife among ourself and dismantle our legacy by our own hands.
