Why did Sarojini Naidu say that Mahatma Gandhi's poverty is very costly?

📅 Nov 23, 2020 09:53 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The argument put forward by Sarojini Naidu was on the lines — To ensure Gandhiji’s safety and wellbeing as he travelled in third-class railway compartment and mingled with commoners were a cost center. Someone needed to ensure that he is safe among his people.

This essentially doesn’t mean that I am endorsing her point of view. I am also not contradicting her statement. What she presented may be a fact, and yet, this statement comprehensively fails to appreciate that cost is a relative term. This statement also doesn’t do much needed cost-benefit-analysis.

Gandhiji’s mingling with the common people weren’t just some political stunts. Before joining active politics in India, it had travelled across India in those third-class compartment, getting closer to the people, their needs and problems. It made him connect with masses better than any other political leader of the time. No wonder, his movements were not only acknowledged by masses but also supported by them. It was these experiments that converted congress from the party of an elite to the party of the people. And that was something, no money could buy.

Second important point to note is, Gandhji never demanded such expenses should be made for him. There is no evidence to suggest he even knew about those expenditures which were, no doubt, done with good intentions. There is ample evidence that Gandhiji desisted such expenses and always confronted the need for his personal security. He never allowed stringent security for himself and that was one of the prime reason, he became such an easy target to a maniac criminal’s bullet.

Third important point, Gandhiji wasn’t just a political leader. Had it been so, his area of influence would have been limited to politics or at best India, and he won’t have become a global phenomenan, he is today. His life is a lesson and inspiration for citizens across the globe. He showed the model way people and its leader should connect. Thos who can implement a quarter of this would be win masses. And there can be no cost attributed to such a life lesson for either the people or the leader.

To summarize, there may have been a cost to keep Gandhji poor, but that made him and people connect better and no cost can be attributed to this achievement.
