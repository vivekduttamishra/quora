Short answer — Krishna did stop Drawpadi's Vastraharan.

The question may be seeking to know —

Why Krishna didn't prevent the situation from escalating or why he didn't punish the culprit before the committed the crime?

I believe this notion is western not sanatan, As per sanatan philosphy —

  • Krishna was no vigilante to punish someone from crimes they may commit tomorrow.
  • God’s is no assistant who runs after each person and prevent them from making minor mistakes and preventing a car crash. God is no assistant. People get this opinion by watching movies like OMG which asks such an absurd question — why a car with God’s idol meets accident.
  • God doesn’t keep correcting your mistakes.
  • There is no force is universe parallel to God. There is no Saitan or devil.

In Gita Krishna talks about Karmayog. He emphasizes on Karma and KarmaFala. While you can’t control the outcome of your action, nevertheless, actions shall have consequences and therefore, he concludes, act in my devotion — selfless duty.

  • Your actions are the outcome of your composite Guna — Sat, Raj, Tamas. Either you can control it or they can control you.
  • Make mistakes and pay for it. Don’t expect God to be your assistant.

Then why did Krishna intervene at all?

This too explained in Gita and perhaps the most famous of all Slokas —

yada yada hi dharmasya
glanir bhavati bharata
abhyutthanam adharmasya
tadatmanam srjamy aham

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

—Gita Chapter 4 - Sloka 7 & 8

Whenever there is a decline in righteous conducts, whenever there is a rise and spread of the evils, I would incarnate — to protect the good, to destroy the evil and to reestablish the righteous conduct — again and again.

Krishna had to do multiple jobs here —

  • Punish the culprits and expose the decline of Righteous conduct. Imagine a person considered as manifestation of Dharma, falls in the trap of vile habbit and baits his own kingdom and family! Had he prevented, would society understand the decline of rightous conduct?
  • But was only Yudhisthir the culprit? Was he the biggest one? What about the King and the council of ministers, who were moot spectators? Krishna gave all of them occassion to redeem themselves. Alas, they failed.
  • Krishna had to protect the innocent whom the society failed to redeem. But above all he had to prevent the decline of Dharma.

You must also realize Krishna didn’t intervene again, when Yudhisthir accepted and lost dice game a second time — why?

Pandavas too needed to suffer for their mistake. Isn’t it.

3. Why didn’t Krishna killed the 4 evils rather than protecting Draupadi. Afterall, they did commit crimes.

  • What’s the lesson in it? That you need not do anything. Just keep making mistake and God will fix them all?
  • The main philosophy here is God can be your guide, he won’t act on your behalf.
  • If human system terribly collapses, God will intervene.

4. How can God endorse such a brutal war?

  • Again God as a loving parent, is a biblical philosopy and not Sanatan.
  • In sanatan Dharama, there is no one parallel to God — Evils don’t guide your bad acts. You are responsibile.
  • At the end of the time, Devadidev Mahadev Shiva, inhaliates the entire universe so that it can reborn. So if a war or destruction is bad how is Shiva the God of Gods?
  • Would you not endorse India going for a war against hostile neighbours? Shouldn’t we have killed terrorists in Mumbai attack and elsewhere?
  • Would you allow a kid to ride a bycycle knowing well he or she may meet and accident and wound themselves? Or you would rather be a loving parent and never let them ride a bycycle? Or for that matter let them do nothing?
