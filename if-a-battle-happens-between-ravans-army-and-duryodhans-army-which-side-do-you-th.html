If a battle happens between Ravan's army and Duryodhan's army, which side do you think would win?

📅 May 03, 2020 10:39 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

A great question —

Whom would you like to win?

It's would be a tale of two abusers — Ravan and Duryodhan? Whom would I cheer for?

Why are they really fighting?

Ravan certainly won't fight for Drawpadi's honor, neither Duryodhan to free Sita. So why are they fighting?

  • Did Ravan abducted Duryodhana's wife?
  • Or Duryodhana tried to disrobe Mandodari?

The villain of that story shall be demolished. Every time. Without a blink!

Don't want a moral science lesson? Just the raw powers?

Why Ravan will win? (Popular View)

  • Ravan, Indrajeet and Kumbhkarna were almost invincible. On the top they had a army of might demon with special powers and thousands of years of war experience.
  • The demons of Lanka had defeated gods like Yama, navgrah etc. What chance Kaurava's force have against them?
  • Kauravas were just one man army — Bhishma who held his ground for 10 days. Sans Bhisma the force was routed in 7 days.
  • Kauravas army with Drona and Karna couldn't fight one Ghatotkatch an hybrid demon. What chance they would have against an army?
  • Would it be difficult for Indrajeet to sink someone's chariot in blood puddle and slay him?

Why would Ravan lose (critical view)

If and only if Kaurava sided with Dharma. Let us assume there are no Pandavas. Duryodhana is not insecured or jealous and fights against Ravan the demon. Kauravas can win —

  • Ravan had a might army — Fact. They vanquished even Gods — Fact. They were routed by two humans, commanding forces of vanars carrying primitive weapons.
  • Ravan was destined to die in hands of humans (or monkeys). And Kauravas would have been humans, if they followed Dharma.
  • Bhishma would have fought the war with full concentration and not under an oath not to kill anyone. With no soft corners for Pandavas, one who defeated Bhagwan Parshuram and who is guarded by Iksha Mrityu would be invincible. Trained by Vasishtha the Guru of great Ram, and Bhagwan Parshuram, and being human would have defeated Ravan and completed his destiny.
  • Drona, another disciple of Bhagwatam Parshuram would have played a Lakshman to Bhishma. With no Arjuna around he would be a great adversary.
  • In this parallel universe, with no Pandavas, even Karna would be a great warrior, and may not lose his Devine shield. Why would Indra weaken someone fighting on sides of Dharma?
  • Ashwathama would have fought with his true potential, marred being on Adharma.

In the end, it comes down to Dharma — Dharma would win the war, army strength won't. That's the divine lesson of our ancient epic.
