Should I firmly believe that Mahatma Gandhi was a good person despite knowing his secret roles in the division of India?

📅 May 24, 2022 01:20 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Honestly it doesn’t matter what you believe or how you want to treat Mahatma Gandhi. What you should actually concentrate on is ‘knowing’ rather than ‘believing that you know’.

Had you really known the history of India, you wouldn’t have talked about your secret knowledge of Gandhiji’s secret role in the division of India. For if something remained secret even from the contemporaries of his time, none of whom blamed it on him, it is not called secret but fabrication and prejudice. And when you mention such prejudice is public forum like this, you do not define Gandhiji, you define yourself. And once you have exposed so perfectly well, what you believe with your colored vision doesn’t matter for anyone except for you.

But for those, who really want to see the naked truth, it would go like this — Gandhiji was against India’s partition till the very end and he tried his best to prevent it. But he was not part of any political entity. He wasn’t an office bearer. He wasn’t the official signatory from either side. And his voice was ignored even by his disciples. And after direct action day of 1946, if he understood there is partition was inevitable and he stopped his attempt to prevent it. He NEVER advocated for it. There are moments when you need to sacrifice a limb to protect your healthy body. And if the history of the other part of divided land is any testimony you can easily corelate it with the analogy of infect limb that must be severed off to prevent poison infecting the entire body. Don’t you see a direct action day, everyday on that part of the land? Can you really compare either the growth of peace on the two sides of the border? Once Gandhiji realized that his dream of united free India is impossible, he tried to salvage the situation by keeping quite.

But if you really want to know his ‘secret’ role in division, it was the fact that he didn’t consider a divided India, as true independence. He wasn’t basking in the glory of Independence near Red Fort or Indian capital. He had made a promise to himself, while leaving Sabarmati Aasram on the starting of civil disobedience movement. And the promise was he won’t return back to the Aasram till India is free. He never went back. Because the freedom we achieved wasn’t the freedom of his dream. Any one with secret role, should be proudly celebrating his success. I don’t see Gandhiji doing it. Just wondering what may be that secret? Did he get a huge amount in some Swiss Bank Account? I don’t see even his next two generation enjoying that money!

If you still don’t understand Gandhiji’s secret trace his steps from Jan 1st, 1947 to Jan 30th, 1948. You would find him walking as one man army in remote village of Bihar, in the heart of Calcutta preventing it from burning down on the eve of India’s freedom and his determination to go to Pakistan to find solace for the Hindu’s that were strangled on the other side of the border. Even Jinnah had allowed his visit in the hope that he could perform some miracle. Had he succeeded in reaching Pakistan he would have performed the same miracle he performed in Calcutta between 15th August 1947 to 27th August 1947.

But Alas, he was shot dead by a heinous criminal, a mindless murderer, who didn’t just kill the Mahatma but along with him sealed the fate of millions of Hindus strangled on the other side of dividing line who slowly languished and perished.

If you want to find out the true culprit for the division of India, you should better find out who were the first people who proposed the two nation theory, who were the people who made the environment so toxic that division became eminent, who were those people who restrained themselves and their follower from proactive partition on India’s freedom movements like Quit India Movement and who were those conniving criminals who tried to repeatedly murder Mahatma Gandhi long before partition of India was even discussed.

And if you can’t dare to learn the true facts that are not really secret, you may choose to believe whatever you like. I can point to what visionaries of that era believed —

  • Subhash Chandra Bose called him ‘Father of the Nation’. And he was a visionary man.
  • Dr Rajendra Prasad equated him to Ganga. And he was the president of the constituent assembly of India before becoming the first president of the country.
  • Ho Chinh Minh father of the nation of Vietnam said that — “I and others may be revolutionaries, but we are disciples of Mahatma Gandhi, directly or indirectly, nothing more nothing less”
  • Lord Mount Batten equated him to Jesus Christ and Gautam Buddha. He didn’t equate even his own leaders with those people. In fact he was the overseer of India’s Independence and partition and in glorifying Gandhiji’s role he needed to explain his own inadequacy along with that of Nehru and Patel. Nevertheless He said his peace with grace.
  • Albert Einstein said — Generations to come will scarce believe that such a one as this ever in flesh and blood walked upon this earth.

And these are the people who shared the era with Gandhi, saw the struggle of the period and made views without the advantage of WhatsApp.

And Albert Einstein was right. Generations to come questions if he can believe Gandhiji was a good man. They don’t care what their own great leader like Subhash Chandra Bose or Dr. Rajendra Prasad said. They don’t care if they are isolated with their belief in a world that revers Gandhiji as a good man. All they care is what their WhatsApp university taught them and think that they have a secret knowledge burried in some tomb and whole world is ignorant about.

Please believe whatever you like, but then don’t trust everything you believe.

— Vivek Dutta Mishra
