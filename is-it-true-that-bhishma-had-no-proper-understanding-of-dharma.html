Is it true that Bhishma had no proper understanding of Dharma?

📅 Feb 15, 2021 08:35 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I can see where the question is coming from. Most modern depiction and narratives show Bhishma as a confused old man who got himself entangled into web of his own oath and saw the doom of Hastinapur haplessly. He supported the side of Adharma.

But then that is a leading narrative. The narrative conveniently fails to mention that Bhishma has always been referred as an authority of Dharma. He learnt Dharma from Vashishtha, Parashurama, Brihaspati to name a few.

You can’t understand any action by removing intention from it. Without the intention, action has no meaning. Bhishma’s actions too sometimes may appear confusing because its intent is not always mentioned alongside — can’t always be mentioned along side. Therefore there is an undeniable baseline mentioned — he was an authority of Dharma!

Keeping the baseline intact, you should revisit his actions to understand why Bhishma did, what he did and you can see the entire epic in a much better light.
