In the unlikely scenario of Krishna giving the first choice to Duryodhana, whom would he have chosen, a non-fighting Krishna or a fighting fit Narayana Sena? Why?

📅 Apr 16, 2021 03:02 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I have recently answered a variant of this question here —

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Had Duryodhana approached Krishna one day in advance to Arjuna and sought his help, would Krishna have offered him any options or point blank refused to join his side?

To give a short description, question like these assume that there were people or factor other than Krishna who were taking the decision here. But let me assure you Krishna wasn’t someone whom you can manipulate. Duryodhana got, what Krishna wanted to give him. No more and No less. The only way Duryodhana could have got Krishna was through a total surrender and devotion, something he was utterly incapable of.

Had Duryodhana the sense of Dharma, he wouldn’t need to chose either in the first place. And if given a choice an arrogant fool like Duryodhana would never chose Krishna. Interestingly on another occasion, Duryodhana had a chance to chose Krishna. This time, it wasn’t Duryodhana who was seeking Krishna, but Krishna who sought Duryodhana — Dev yaan Parva — when Krishna appeals to Duryodhana for a peaceful settlement. That fool tried to imprison Krishna. Do you think he who knew nothing than conspiracy would chose Krishna? He who lusted for power would reject such a magnificent army?

No. You can rest assure — Duryodhana and Krishna both were in agreement and things would have followed exactly the way it proceeded.
