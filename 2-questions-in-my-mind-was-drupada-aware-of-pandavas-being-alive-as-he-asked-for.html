2 questions in my mind-Was Drupada aware of Pandavas being alive? As he asked for daughter for Arjuna.If yes, What was the hurry for the Swayamvar, when he was aware that Yudhishthir is still unmarried and Arjun will respect his words by all means?

Thanks for the A2A.

First things first. Drupada wasn’t aware that Pandavas survived Lakshagriha conspiracy. He certainly wanted a daughter whom he could marry to Arjuna. But that was a thought that predated the Lakshagriha after which Pandavas vanished making world believe they died.

And that may be the reason for Drupada to announce the Swaymavara. Life must go on. And he needed to forge some political alliance in the process. This is very clear from the fact that —

  • Swyamvar was open for everyone disregarding the caste with a single quality — the person must be a great warrior. They firmly believed only a person of high birth and noble lineage could achieve this feat, although they didn’t debar anyone from the participation.
  • When Arjuna succeeded the swaymvar he was dressed as a Brahmin with non specific lineage. Drupada wasn’t even sure if he was actually a Brahmin or someone from lower strata. Yet, bound by the rules of swaymvar he couldn’t stop Arjuna from either participation or taking his daughter away.

Now I believe the basis of the second question is gone — the little problem of Yudhishthira being unmarried. Well this incident is made hyper glossy by later narrative and it loses the ancient narratives.

What is relevant here to note is, Yudhishthira being elder or unmarried was not relevant to the issue. Yudhishthira didn’t object Arjuna’s participation in the swyamvar and he was confident of Arjuna’s victory. But that didn’t matter a bit. There was no question of respecting Yudhishthira.

Second, Polygamy and polyandry were both known concepts although former was certainly more popular. Yudhishthira may have been a married man and yet he could have entered in this polygamous marriage. You may realize this if you recall that Bhima was already married at this point.

And the most important point that the question missed is the destiny that was pre-ordained by the blessing of Lord Shiva. Who could have thought to contradict his words?
