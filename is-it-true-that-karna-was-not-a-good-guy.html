Is it true that Karna was not a good guy?

📅 Mar 10, 2020 03:12 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Any guy is good or bad can’t really be an absolute answer. It is relative and on a wide spectrum rather than binary as this answer seeks. So I would answer this question on the broader aspect.

Karna was a good archer (not best) and had the ambition of being hailed as the greatest warrior. When he didn’t get the recognition, he felt he should get; he expressed his resentment against the society, jealousy against the person who in his opinion got better recognition and spent his entire life proving he was a better warrior.

But in his opinion to prove himself a better warrior he needed a war and always jumped at any chance he could get. Little did he realize that the key trait of a warrior is to follow the war ethics and that is to avoid the war itself. So focused was he on being proven a great warrior, his focus shifted and lost several wars in the due course. To make the matter worse, he made terrible choices in his life —

  1. He not only participated in Draupadi’s insult, he was the one who actively promoted it. While many can blame Draupadi for rejecting him in Swaymber must remember —
    1. The ancient dialects of Mahabharat differ on this point and is generally acceptable that such incident is a later addition and never actually happened.
    2. Even if Draupadi had rejected him (added only because of popular perception) it was still her Swaymber and her prerogative — while insulting Draupadi was not Karna’s prerogative — it was the result of a malice he carried against Draupadi for the felt wronged.
  2. He instigated Duryodhan to go and mock Pandavas in exile — another war and reputation lost.
  3. On his support Duryodhan rejected sage advice of his father, gurus, and Bhishma. He always actively pushed Duryodhan towards the war.
  4. He rejected peace offer by Krishna and Kunti because he wanted a war and kill Arjuna. Think Kunti never asked to spare the life of 4 sons. She wanted him back. He refused to return back (he may be justified). He spared 4 Pandavas ( was it Kunti’s wish or Duryodhans) and brutally announced to kill Arjuna ( for Kunti or Duryodhan or himself)
  5. He never stopped attacking Bhima on day 14 when his chariot and weapons lay broken and he had no defense. It was much worse than the day he died becuase only his chariot was immobilized and he still had his weapons.

So Karna, like everyone else was not born good or evil, but his lust for recognition, and his jealousy against Arjun brought worse out of him and that overshadows any good that was in him. He certainly could never become a warrior — just an archer or a war monger.
