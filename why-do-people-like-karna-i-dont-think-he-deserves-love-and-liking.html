Why do people like Karna? I don't think he deserves love and liking.

📅 Mar 13, 2020 11:05 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Since this question involve views rather than fact and I would dare to speculate here rather than sticking to usual references which mostly agrees to the last part of the question.

  1. Mahabharat as the incident occurred over 5000 years ago. However, the compilation as written work started less than 2000 years ago. Over time several smaller details were added.
  2. The compilation happened in parallel my multiple authors whose views were also clouded by contemporary issues rather apart from the original incidents. This resulted in several different variations of Sanskrit text and at a base estimate over 300 variations existed by 1400AD. We can broadly classify them can be broadly as Nothern and Southern.
  3. For most people interpreting sanskrit was difficult so we depened on translation done over period of time. Most important ones include the assmblying by Neelkanth Chaturdhara followed by english translation by Krishna Mohan Ganguli and much later in recent time a critical edition called Bori. This is apart from the Nothern compilation of Mahabharat and Southrn compilation of Mahabharat.
  4. Since most of these works are done after 1700 AD, a certain degree of biased related to caste system creeped in the narration which is quite different from the original retaling.
  5. This narration took Karna as an example for a great warrior denied his due because of his caste. Guru rejects him, Draupadi rejects him, He was cheated of his Kavach and Kundal, He was killed while he was busy pulling his wheel from the mud and so on. But do these narration in line with ancient epic?
  6. The problem is no one has time to read the largest epic spanning more than 1 lac verses. So we accepted the TV show versions and the comic version and build more creative stories around them.
  7. But then there were contradictions. How do we justify that in ancient Mahabharat verses and identified by BORI and present in southern versions Karna fails at Swyamber rather than rejected by Draupadi? How was he a great warrior then? So what do we do? We stick to the story that says Draupadi rejected him.
  8. How do we say that Karna was the person that commanded Dussasan to disrobe Draupadi? What kind of hero does that? So we attributed this misdeed to Duryodhan the known villian.
  9. How do we say Karna lost in Virata war and Gandharva war? So we put those stories under the blanket.

And this way stories after stories are narrated that gradually made Karna the grand hero he was not as per Vyas.

Since most of the people don’t read any text related to Mahabharat other than few novels and TV shows, they believe whatever is presented there and most of them glorified Karna.
