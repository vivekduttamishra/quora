Why did not Bhishma allow Karna to be a part of the Mahabharata war?

📅 Mar 13, 2020 05:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Contrary to the popular perception as propagated in favour of Karna, it was Karna who refused to fight under the command of Bhishma.

Before the battle while analysing the strengths of Kauravas army Bhisma belittled Karna by referring him adhirathi. He also called Karna selfish and a war monger and cautioned Duryodhan against Karna. Bhisma's view was seconded by Drona and Kripacharya.

Anguished and angered Karna confronted Bhishma with equally abusive language and suggested Duryodhan to remove Bhisma from the position of Commander in chief. He said an old man like him is worthless and his mind has become childish.

Finally Karna announced he won't fight under Bhisma's command but Surely vanquish Pandavas and their army once Bhisma is removed.

Karna also mentioned that the credit of a soldiers victory goes to general. He was worried that he won’t get the credit for his deeds and since he won’t get the credit he won’t fight. But once Bhishma is gone, he would certainly fight.

This is as per Vyas Mahabharat. Anything else is a stretch of imagination or attempt to glorify Karna.
