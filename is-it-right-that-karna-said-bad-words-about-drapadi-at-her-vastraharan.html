Is it right that Karna said bad words about Drapadi at her Vastraharan?

📅 Apr 17, 2021 05:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short answer: ‘Bad’ is a weak adjective. The correct choice should be ‘brutal’ or ‘beast like’

And the epic didn’t miss this and left no room for doubt. Here is direct references—

Radheya, who was almost senseless with anger, gripped his lustrous arms212 and uttered these words, “I have witnessed many distortions in Vikarna. Like fire destroys the block from which it has been kindled, his destruction will come from the fire he has created. Though urged by Krishna, those who are assembled here have not uttered a word. I consider that Drupada’s daughter has been won in accordance with dharma, and so do they. O son of Dhritarashtra!

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 3365-3368).

No Karna’s real abuse doesn’t start here. This paragraph is to show his temprament and posture as he stood up to censor Vikarna — senseless with anger and lustrous. He further says —

O bull among the Bharata lineage! Droupadi is included in all his possessions. When Krishna has been won in accordance with dharma, how can you think she has not been won?

He mentions Drauapadi as a mere possessions. Now some may say even Pandavas did that. But that would be flawed assertions. Yudhishtir was undoubtedly wrong to trust Hasitnapur court and bait his wife. But he didn’t consider her, as possession. He baited himself before Draupadi. Even in that manner he put her above himself. And as per Karna’s statement, it didn’t matter if Yudhisthir baited Draupadi or not, she was just a possession and was won regardless — she is included in all his possessions.

But we haven’t heard worst of his words —

It has been ordained by the gods that a woman should only have one husband. However, she submits to many and it is therefore certain that she is a courtesan. It is my view that there is nothing surprising in her being brought into the sabha in a single garment, or even if she is naked. In accordance with dharma, Soubala has won all the riches the Pandavas possessed, including her and themselves.

—BORI

Now few fools, may argue that Karna has made a statement in accordence with what is ordained by Gods. Don’t be fooled. This lusturous person was no vedic scholar. There is no vedic verse to be referred. God of Gods, Mahadeva Shiva himself ordained Draupadi to have five husbands. Ved Vyas seconded the idea. There were ancient references of polyandry available. And surely they all knew more of Vedas than someone senseless with anger, waving his lusterous hand.

So is that the end of his abuses. By a large margin no. He was sowing the seeds of Kurukshetra war —

Strip away the garments from the Pandavas and Droupadi.” O descendant of the Bharata lineage! On hearing these words, the Pandavas took off their upper garments and sat down in the sabha. O king! Then Duhshasana forcibly tugged at Droupadi’s garments. In front of everyone in the sabha, he forcibly pulled. O lord of the earth! As Droupadi’s garment was being tugged away, another similar garment appeared every time.214 At this, a terrible uproar arose. All the assembled kings witnessed the most extraordinary sight in the worlds and approved.

It wasn’t Shakuni. It was’n Duryodhana. It wasn’t even Dussasana who was guilty for the most henious attempt to molest and disrobe a woman in public. Even the evil Raavana dared not do it.

But an evil worse than Ravana continued…

‘Karna said, “There are three who can own no property—a slave, a student and a woman. O fortunate one! You are the wife of a slave and have nothing of your own. You have no lord and are like the property of slaves. Enter226 and serve us. That is the task for you in this household. O Princess! All the sons of Dhritarashtra are now your masters and not the sons of Pritha. O beautiful one! Choose another one for your husband, one who will not make you a slave through gambling. Remember the eternal rule among slaves. Sexual acts with one’s masters are never censured.

And make no mistake. Here Karna’s statement is not only against Draupadi but is misogynist. And once again don’t be fooled into thinking that his statements are based on some Vedic sutras, but on his own pervert thinking — sexual acts with own’s master are never censured, and Dhritrashtra’s sons are now your master!
