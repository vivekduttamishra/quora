Before I try to answer this question, I must say I agree with neither of the terminology — Mythology or Itihaas or History.

WHAT IS MYTHOLOGY?

Mythology is a word with it’s origin in Myth or fable. This is a crude attempt of western historians to write off our great ancient preserved wisdom as a worthless piece of literature. The alternative was to realize that what we preserved over tens of thousands of year is far superior both in terms of it’s value and rationality.

Another simple reason English language has no real or direct word to represent the idea of Shruti-Smriti and Purana.

WHY ARE OUR EPICS NOT ITIHAAS OR HISTORY?

It is a popular trap. Western scholars say, our epics are not history and we rush to prove it is history. But if you care to analyze the context, trying to label our epics as history or Itihaas is actually degrading their values. I won’t do that.

Let us understand history, by definition, is a documentation of the past. It is a past tense — description of an era gone by.

Iti-haas has the same meaning. Iti means something that has finished (past).

Now what may be the value of something that has finished or a matter of past?

If you look into our ancient wisdom from vedas, puranas and upnishads, you would realize that the concept of time line that demarcates past, present and future is a flawed and unscientific measurement. Time doesn’t move in a straight line. It is circular (Kaalchakra). And how do you find past or Iti in a circle?

That is the reason, epics don’t put a date to the events. They are not past. They are the law. Law proved by repetitions. Again and Again. That makes them relevant for what is popularly termed as future.

Let us not degrade our great epics by comparing to history or Itihaas and let fools believe they are mythology for they would never understand something as superior and history.
