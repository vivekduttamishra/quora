Has Arjuna ever defeated Karna in a fair fight?

📅 Nov 23, 2020 05:49 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Hi, Thanks for the A2A.

Define fair fight!

You want the two warriors to be of equal strength to have a fair fight? Then there never was a fair fight, because Arjuna and Karna were warriors with unequal calibre and skills.

Arjuna had earned his skills and strength through hard work, penance and constant practice. He never requested out of the way or use deceitful means or some exchange to earn the weapons. So you can’t call it a fair fight.

But let us check out

  1. Drauapadi Swyamvara battle — Arjuna was unprepared for a battle against the Kings. He wasn’t carrying his usual weapons. The two Pandava brothers were attacked by many kings together. Would you call it a fair fight? But, Arjuna and Bhima proved stronger than them. So after trying his hand against Arjuna, Karna retreats. Scared. And before he retreats he compares Arjuna with Rama, the personification of knowledge of weapons, Indra and Vishnu. Even Ved Vyas compared Arjuna with Vishnu as he hit the swyamvara target. Karna couldn’t even lift the bow. So if someone whose skills are compared with Rama and Vishunu, and he fights an ordinary warrior, even with ordinary weapon and scares him away, how can that be a fair fight?
  2. Virata Battle — Arjuna was alone. In disguise. Karna was tagged along with an army comparasing of warriors like Bhishma, Drona, Kripacharya, Ashwatthama and Duryodhana. Before the battle, Karna is sure to strike down the monkey flag and Arjuna. He had Vasvishakti given by Indra. There was a large army to add on to it. And in this situation, Karna and Arjuna fought. One Vs an army — can you call it a fair fight? Karna was defeated thrice — twice he retreated. Last time he wasn’t in a position to retreat. So that was another unfair battle where Arjuna won against Karna.
  3. Jaidradh Vadha — The day 14 was fought with a single target from Kaurava’s side. To protect Jaidradh. Again one vs an army. Was that a fair fight? But sure enough, Karna tried to protect Jaidradh from Arjuna and lost again.
  4. Day 16 — Day ended in a disaster for Kauravas. Dhritrashtra cried. Karna was like a crushed serpant. One warrior defeated Karna and the rest by his skills. Was that a fair fight?
  5. Karna’s final battle — Karna’s chariot wheel stuck on the ground. Very very unfair. But warriors don’t fight with chariot, they fight on chariot. Karna fought from ground. Same way, as Abhimanyu, Satyaki and Bhima fought from ground with any chariot. And do you know who was common opponent who kept fighting a Abhimanyu, Satyaki and Bhima while they were without a Chariot? Well the defintion of fairness keeps chaning.

So if we compare the mertis of the warriors, every Karna war against Arjuna was bound to be unfair because of his superletive advantage over Karna. But if you consider two warriors to be equal, Arjuna won every battle which was unfairly unbaised against him. He had a nack of winning unfair battles.
