Was Karna more powerful than Bhishma?

📅 Mar 26, 2020 09:01 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

I am not sure why this Karna Vs ??? question keeps returning to Quora. And many answers below have tried to compare each warrior to bring out their point. Unfortunately, that de-focuses the question. One answer in particular quotes several fragments from Mahabharat, however, the analysis presented are completely unrelated to the phrase and often starts or ends with “I don’t care…”. That makes reference irrelevant!

My answer would not just be for the question but also refuting as few of those given answers —

First let us attempt what does being powerful mean? Is it just on the basis weapons you possess? According to our Shastras, power is also a question of morality — Atma Shakti — and it is the most honored forms of power.

What are the traits of a powerful person?

  • They are not only physically but also mentally powerful. That's why it was expected to do tapas and sadhnas to earn divine blessings or celesial weapons.
  • You are so sure about yourself that you don’t need a constant proof.
  • Your real power comes from a strong character —
    • You don’t try to steal
    • You don’t try to mock and humiliate others, specially weak and hapless ones.
    • Only a weak will torment, mock or abuse a weak and hapless person because there he gets a satisfaction of his psudo powers.
    • You don’t rebuke others for your failures

Now Let us analyse

Bhisma

  • A dutiful son who sacrificed his kingdom and happiness for his father. (Earned him the name Bhishma). He never searched exception in his oath. That's Atma Shakti.
  • He defeated Bhagwan Parshuram. This can’t be evaluated with I don’t care attitude. Bhagwan Parshuram is the 6th incarnate of Vishnu and Kshatriya slayer for 21 times. A proof of his power was his famous curse with Karna’s fan lament and we will come back to it later!
  • Bhismha never forgot or diluted his promises with exceptions. He never worked with a half-heart no matter it takes him.
    • He started the war with clear disclosure that I will not kill any Pandavas? Note he made it clear before the start of war, and made it clear to both Duryodhana and the army.
    • He had made it clear that he would not fire at Shikandi and avoided him in the war. Again one answer below says I don’t care — If we don’t care the power of personal resolve (Atmashakti), how do we identify who was powerful?
  • Coming to physical war capability
    • Bhishma promised to kill 10 sahashra soldiers per day, and I saw someone talking about if Bhishma could have killed an elephant or not. Funny Isn’t it?
    • Bhishma’s attacks were so powerful in the war and he was so utterly in-vanquishable that Krishna himself had to stand, setting aside his vow, to kill Bhishma. In fact Bhishma shared the secret of his death!
    • Bhishma commanded the army for 10 days, always dominating the war. War ended with all their other commander in lesser time.
    • I will not detail here about the final war between Arjun and Bhishma and if it was justified or not (it will digress from the matter), it doesn’t change the fact that Bhishma himself divulged the secret of his death. He expected it.
    • Bhishma was defeated only once in his career — the Virat war. Otherwise he remained invincible.

Karna

  • Unlike Bhisma was ambitious — not for throne — but for recognition as the greatest warrior. This caused him to carry resentment for his mother for the life. This didn’t take him towards mental resolve but to the part of deceit and he cheated Bhagwan Parshuram. He got cursed by same Bhagwan Parshuram was defeated by Bhishma earlier.
  • Karna oaths are an interesting paradox and never without a condition —
    • I will give my Kavach and Kundal only if you give me a celestial weapon.
    • I will spare Pandavas with the exception to Arjuna? Had he ever conveyed his oath to Duryodhana or the Kaurava’s army? Several references and dialogs between Dhritarashtra and Sanjay proves otherwise.
    • I will enter the battles only after Bhishma is gone.
  • Now coming to Karna’s powers
    • No doubt Karna was an excellent archer, and an expert of weapons and war technique. He was disciple of Bhagwan Parshuram, Dronacharya and Kripacharya.
      • He had several victories to his credit.
      • He on occasions defeated Drupad, Bheem, and Satyaki.
      • Did Digvijay yatra
    • But Karna had been defeated on a number of occasions by these people before the start of the battle
      • Drupad — in gurudakshina war, along with other Kauravas
      • Gandharva — in vanparva when the set out to mocks Pandavas
      • Arjuna — In swyamber Parva and Virata Parava
    • It is a myth that Karna couldn’t be defeated with his Kavach and Kundal on. I say this is a myth because all the battles mentioned above, except Virat, happened long before Indra took away his Kavach and Kundal and Karna lost each of them with his Kavach and Kundala on.
    • In the battle Karna enters the battle on 11th day and dies on 17th day (6 days). During this period, He was defeated by —
      • Abhimanyu — Day 13 — Karna Had to kill him with a pack of other hungry hounds because he defeated each of them.
      • Bhima — Day 14 — Defeated Karna several times, each time Karna fleding the scene. Karna however won the final time and mocked Bhima. Bhima reminded him of Keechaks death and Karna, fled one more time.
      • Ghatotkatch — Day 14. Well Ghatotkatch dies at the end. But the question is why Karna needed to use the celestial weapon? Because normal means couldn’t contain him.
      • Some use Ghatotkatch to show Karna a victim. They say Ghatotkatch stripped Karna from his celestial weapon. True. He did, but it is also important to note that Ghatotkatch was not a surprise entry on day 14, but had fought in the war for a time longer than Karna. But that brings previous question back — he didn’t beg Karna to kill him using a celestial weapon. What stopped Karna from killing him using his other weapons? It’s a classical case of You want to eat your cake and save it too. That doesn’t happen.
      • Satyaki defeated Karna.

I believe being a Victor and warrior are two different things — we award Paramveer Chakra to a soldier not to a terrorist, no matter how many people they may kill.

So who was more powerful? You can judge…
