Thanks for the A2A.

First let us clarify the question's premise. I believe here Hero is used for opposite of evil and follower of Dharma.

I wouid advise you to refrain from searching a single shloka rather look at the message from every single epic and shastras. But if you insist on some direct shloka here are some —

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

  • Bhagwat Gita

To protect the good (heros) and destroy the evil, to reestablish righteous conduct, I shall manifest myself again and again.

You would see a direct reference here, which is also summarised in one of my favorite shloka

Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitaah

And again in

Yatho Dharma Tatho Jayah

Where there is Dharma there is victory.

Beyond the shloka we should consider the acts — Why was Shri Ram biased against Bali and towards Sugriv? What made Krishna pronounce Arjuna as his inseparable existence?

I guess you have your answer.
