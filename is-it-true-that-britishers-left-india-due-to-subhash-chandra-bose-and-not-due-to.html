Is it true that Britishers left India due to Subhash Chandra Bose and not due to Mahatma Gandhi?

📅 Jan 24, 2021 10:20 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Shubhash Chandra Bose was a great leader and has immeasurable contribution to India's freedom movement. Imagine, comanndering an Independent Army!

But unfortunately, he was gone from the scene by year 1944. So unless there was going to be a ghost story, it is just a heresay. Someone said Atlee said so. There is no official record. And in any case may have been one man's view — the same man who was PM when Britishers had to give up India. And he wanted to deman India's freedom movement. Because accepting Mahatma Gandhi's contribution would be accepting Indians contribution to their own freedom — because Mahatma's movement was not his alone. It was people's movement. Our national movement. Unfortunately not only Britishers a section of contemporary Indians today, too want to demean this movement, because in their skewed thinking by condemning Mahatma Gandhi, they are condemning Gandhi family of this era — let me assure you, they are as far different as two people can be and it is people like Narendra Modi who are carrying the legacy of Mahatma Gandhi.

WHY DID BRITISH LEAVE INDIA?

To understand this, first let us understand how they ruled India in the first place? The famous slogan — Divide and Rule? But what does that really mean? That a divided section would actually co-operate with them.

Why did people cooperate with Britishers? Some were greedy for money and power. Others were poor, and for many it didn’t matter they were foreigner or native, they were the bread provider and oppressor. Many even turned loyal to them as there was no all India feeling. And the climax of this story reached with Jalianwala Bagh, when Indian’s opened fire against Indians.

Then came The non-cooperation movement. It changed the game for Brithishers forever. And mantra was simple. Attack the core strategy of Brithishers. On one hand, pull out the support on which the empire stands and on other hand unite all Indians together.

Some may ask, why then India didn’t get its Independence in 1920’s. Because no tree bears the fruit on day zero. Or even day one.

Let us fast forward to 1944–46. Britishers suffered heavy losses in the war, although they were victorious. On the Christmas Eve of 1946, many Britishers couldn’t afford a turkey. How were they supposed to administer a colony who was not ready to cooperate with them anymore?

Then they feared even Army may revolt, although it never did revolt even post Independence. Would you call it Subhash Chandra Bose factor? Would you call Gandhi factor?

Must I give credit to one of these great people by snatching it from the other? I don’t think so. Neither did Subhash Chandra Bose thought when he called Gandhiji as the father of the nation in his radio broadcast 1944. And Gandhiji called him Netaji. These two prominent leaders had a multitude of followers and all of them had something in common — They were Indian, fighting for India’s independence.

India got Independence because a weakened British Empire could no more affort to control an awakened nation — awakened to her Indentity. A great nation was taking shape and no empire had the power to control it. We won that day.

Unfortunately today people are using same divide and rule to break our national pride. Be aware! Be proud. Jai Hind.
