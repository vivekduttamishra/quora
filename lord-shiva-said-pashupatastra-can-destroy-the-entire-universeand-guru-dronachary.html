Lord Shiva said "pashupatastra can destroy the entire universe"And Guru Dronacharya said the same thing for brahamashira weapon. Is That means bhramshira=pashupatastra? If no then why?

📅 Sep 29, 2020 06:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

A gun can kill a person, and a poison can kill a person. Does that make them equal?

People can reach from Bangalore to Delhi on a car and a flight. Does that make them equal?

It’s not arithmetic, so the equality doesn’t apply.

Having set the premise, let see what Dronacharya and Mahadeva said —

Droanacharya said —

  • Drona’s warning to Arjuna while handing him Brahmashira.

Bharadvaja’s son told the great-souled maharatha, “O mighty-armed one! Receive this invincible and supreme weapon, named brahmashira, with the knowledge of releasing it and withdrawing it. You must never use it against human beings. If it is used against an enemy whose energy is inferior, it will burn up the entire universe.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 5106-5108). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • This is what Mahadeva said about Pashupatastra

Partha! But it must not be suddenly released at any man. If it is released at someone who lacks in energy, it will destroy the entire universe. There is no one in the three worlds, mobile or immobile, who cannot be killed by it.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 5484-5486). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

Forget equality or inequality for a moment and think — What is the point of having a weapon that destroys the entire world? If everyone dies then no one wins. And who would ever use that weapon?

Here the defining part of these statement is ‘If’. It was easier to destroy the entire universe than to initiate it against a specific desired target. And remember these weapons weren’t like firing a bullet from a gun — once fired can’t be recalled. Such was their precession.

Both Shiva and Drona are have issued warning against misusing this weapon against a weaker enemy. Why? So that you are not tempted to use it against every one on every trivial issue. The weapon must be used with extreme caution and only when absolutely necessary and not in a haste. A similar warning was issued by Indra while bestowing Vashvi Shakti on Karna. However, he worded it differently as he was talking to Karna and not Arjuna —

But if you unleash this invincible weapon in a fit of fury, when you possess other weapons, there is no doubt that it will descend on you.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8561-8562).

People compare the powers of these weapons because they think of them as a material weapon like modern days nuclear weapon. They were not. Their powers also depended on the worthiness of the wearer and that is why not everyone was allowed to wield them.

Interstingly what is commonly referred as Parshuram’s curse, is also a very similar statement proving my point —

However, he smiled and said, ‘You acted in this false way because of your greed for weapons. O stupid one! In a different place, when the time for your death has come, you will be engaged in a fight with someone who is your equal and the brahmastra will not manifest itself before you. The qualities of a brahmana will never remain with someone who is not a brahmana. Leave this place, since this is not meant for an untruthful one like you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2403-2406). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

If you see it is a statement and not a curse. Someone greedy for weapons lacks Brahmna quality. Such untruthful person can’t invoke a Brahmastra when in distress at the time of his death.

So its about the worthiness of the warrior and not the weapon and it was not about making it more lethal but less lethal.

What was more powerful — Brahmasira or Pashupatastra

It is without any doubt — Pashupatastra.

  1. Mahadeva himself declared — There is no one in the three worlds, mobile or immobile, who cannot be killed by it.
  2. Mahadeva also declared that no one knows about this weapon — not even gods. So it is imparative that even Dronacharya had no true idea of Pashupatastra. He made a statment only about Brahmsira.
  3. Shiva obviously knew Brahmsira and yet he made a statement that there is nothing that can’t be killed (or destroyed) by Pashupatastra.
  4. Pashupatastra destroys the world at the end of every kalpa — This is a known fact. We don’t have a similar fact to back the claim regarding Brahmsira.

But as I said, the idea is not to test the potency of the weapon in terms of which is more lethal, but that it should be use with caution and not in a haste. Don’t try to kill a mosquito with a nuke is the underlying message.
