What is a critical comment on Karna's allegiance to Duryodhana?

📅 Feb 20, 2022 08:40 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Karna-Duryodhana friendship is an interesting paradox, based on false premises, whose popular narratives are governed based more on words than on deeds. And the greatest proof of this fact is recent attempt to white wash Karna’s character way beyond it is depicted in original epic. Glorifications have been added, inconvenient facts heavily censured and a web of deceit makes it tangled at best.

1. KARNA BECOMES ANGARAJ

Popular Narrative

The poor down trodden Karna who was denied education by Drona challenges Arjuna in an open competition. Fearing Karna may outshine Arjuna Kripacharya forbids his participation in the name of caste prejudice. Duryodhna, appalled by injustice of the society crowns him as the King of Anga starting an eternal friendship between Duryodhana and unknown warrior.

FACT

Karna was trained by Drona and Kripacharya before he moved on to Parshurama. During this period Karna and Duryodhana were friends — thick as thieves or shall I say think as criminals? Karna participated in several criminal conspiracies against Pandavas!

The rangabhoomi wasn’t an open-to-all ceremony. It was to show case the skills of Kuru Princess. Karna gate crashed the ceremony and wanted an uncalled for duel. And even before he challenged the duel, Duryodhana had already welcomed Karna to his camp. Only reason that can be attributed to this incident is his earlier close proximity with Karna and his realization that he was the only available choice for someone who can remotely compete with Arjuna.

So yes, there existed an allegiance between Duryodhana and Karna — it appeared as a mutually beneficial agreement.

It may also be considered as one of the earliest cases of gambling on a winning horse.

Nevertheless, Kripacharya tried to defuse the situation that could have resulted in a scandal of a royal prince fighting a commoner for no justifiable reason. Here we must note

  1. Kripacharya is explicitly mentioned as expert of Dharma and war ethics.
  2. Kripacharya never asked for Karna’s caste. He asked for family lineage and royalty. This was in line with protocol of duel and not with exploitation in the name of caste.

CAN WE CALL KARNA-DURYODHANA ALLIANCE AS FRIENDSHIP?

In a formal way of speaking they were friends. At least Duryodhana always treated and trusted Karna as a friend. But we can’t say the same thing other way round.

A true friend generally seeks the welfare of his friend. A true friend would have tried to protect his friend from going astray and certainly do everything in his power to protect him against certain destruction. But Karna’s every single action goes against the very definition of friendship —

  • Not only Karna was a partner-in-crime in all of Karna’s conspiracy, we see many a conspiracies were actually his brain child — Ghosha yatra, Matasya battle.
  • Karna was one person who never discouraged Duryodhana from waging a war despite of knowing full well that it was result in the destruction of Kauravas and Duryodhana.

WAS KARNA LOYAL TO HIS ALLIGIANCE TOWARDS DURYODHANA?

Popular Narrative

Karna was so loyal to Duryodhana that he outright refused the Kingship offered to him by Lord Krishna. He also declined his mother’s request to join the family camp, although he generously vowed to spare the life of his four brothers. This shows both the true friendship and generosity of Karna who was full of compassion.

FACT

  1. Karna deserted and fled during Gandharva war leaving Duryodhana and his family to perish. Had Pandavas not intervened that would have been the end of Kauravas. It certainly shows the contrast between words and deeds. But if that were an isolated incident it could simply be attributed to his cowardly nature and not disloyalty.
  2. Karna rejected Krishna’s proposal of Kingship. But was this act of his in favour of Duryodhana? He himself said at this point that he knows it would lead to destruction of Kuru lineage. Then how was this rejection, a proof of his loyalty to Duryodhana? Consider the alternative. Karna had no ambition of being the King. And preventing the war was against his real ambition. What was that ambition, I would leave you to guess.
  3. Karna refuses to fight in the war for first ten days because of his hurt ego. How does his action prove his loyalty to Duryodhana?
  4. Karna spares the lives of four Pandava princes. One of them was the direct contender to the throne and other the sworn nemesis of Duryodhana, oath bound to Kill Duryodhana. How sparing their lives loyalty to Duryodhana? If you are still struggling to guess the reason, think why he made an exception for Arjuna and not Bhima?
  5. Worst, Karna never confides with Duryodhana about his secret pact with the enemy to spare four prominent enemies. He let them believe that he would kill them. This is is not just disloyalty but outright treason.

As I see Karna’s allegiance and loyalty toward Duryodhana was subject to conditions written in fine print. He was loyal as long as this loyalty doesn’t interfere with his selfish interest and it was a comfortable excuses, again when it suits his own interest. When his personal interest, ambition and self preservation came in focus, they always became a priority. Without exception.

BUT DIDN’T KARNA DIE FOR DURYODHANA?

Karna died in war. That is what we call as occupational hazard. Even machinery soldiers die in war. Bhurisrava died. Samshaptaka died. Shalya died. Thousands of soldiers died. Many died without deserting Duryodhana. None of them had were guilty of treason.

And remember Karna always pushed towards the war. He alone had a unique ability to prevent the war. He didn’t. So question is —

Did Karna die for Duryodhana or die in a war he himself desparately wanted?

— Vivek Dutta Mishra


HOW WAS DURYODHANA’S ALLIGIANCE TOWARDS KARNA?

While Duryodhana’s act of making Karna was by no means a selfless or spontaneous act born out of compassion nevertheless his loyalty or friendship to Karna was undoubtedly closer to the notion of friendship than vice-a-versa.

Duryodhana never deserted Karna. He never condemned or blamed him for his consistent failure to yield favorable results. He lost 31 brothers on day 1 just to protect Karna death at the hand of Bhimsena’s.

Once he took him a friend, he always respected him and honored his judgements which more often than not resulted in total devastation for Kurus.
