In Mahabharata, Lord Krishna gave lessons of Bhagavad Gita to Arjun and others. Can you say that he was instigating Arjuna for war or he was doing his normal duty?

📅 Feb 06, 2021 10:43 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Instigating is a big word specifically used in negative terms — incite someone to do something, especially something bad.

And it clearly contrasts with Krishna’s peace attempt.

This question makes too many suppositions and the most important is —

Are wars bad?

Well, we can safely say war is a necessary evil. We all wish a world without conflict and war. But then we all know that is a wishful thinking. And when war becomes necessary, running away from them would be distratous to the cause. Why else every nation has an Army? Because we need to indulge in war to preserve the values even if we despise the war. That is why even the greatest proponent of non-violence in last thousand years — Mahatma Gandhi — didn’t object Indian’s participating in world war. We had to preserve our values.

Krishna did the same thing over five thousand years ago. He first tried all peaceful means of perssuation to prevent the distraous war. But once in the battlefield, there was no turning back. To fight and protect your values was the only duty.

Summary

Krishna didn’t instigated Arjuna; He motivated him.
