Can you rank Bhisma, Karna, Arjun, and Dronacharya on the basis of only pure fighting skills (not based on boons and other special advantages)?

📅 Mar 08, 2021 09:54 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Here is the ranking —

  1. Arjuna (without doubt) — Arjuna defeated all the other three warriors on almost all the occasion they fought. All the other three warriors admitted to those defeats and conceded that there is no warrior better than Arjuna. Apart from them, Parshurama, Krishna and above all Mahadeva himself declared Arjuna the greatest of all those warriors. Another thing to Arjuna’s credit is he never used the highest weapons available to his disposal and few others could resist it.
  2. Bhishma — The legendry warrior who not only vanquished multiple rulers of his time, but also Parshurama himself. In the greatest war of the history, he held the forte for Kauravas for the longest period of time. Ten days. That was more than all others managed together. So strong was his presence that it forced Krishna to take up the arms against his previous resolve. That speaks a volume about his worth.

Now the remaining two warriors can’t be put the same comparision plane as the above two, so I drew a partition line.

3. Karna — Was a warriors, often referred as technically as skillful as Arjuna if not more. He won many battles of his time. To his credit is killing of Ghatotkatch, however since it was on the basis of one of the greatest weapon of the time — vasvi shakti — it can’t be included as per the constraint of this question. Karna also had clear lack of resolve that resulted in his fleeing the battle for the highest number of time. But his cowardly and ruthless nature as shown against Abhimanyu and Bhima is sufficient to make him a dangerous, though unethical, adversary.

4. Drona — Dronacharya was one of the greatest acharya of the science of weapon. Yet he was not a warrior, but more of an academician. He never fought a real war before Matasya, where he shared his defeat with Karna and others. He became the second commander of Kauravas army, also the second longest serving commander. However, unlike Bhishma, he was aided by Karna. He was always reluctant to face Arjuna, and avoided forming chakravyuh in his presence. Later he found it difficult to face even a lone warrior Abhimanyu and persuaded Karna to break his bow from behind. His lack of skills as warrior got him killed, when he started lamenting his son’s death in the mid of a battlefield.
