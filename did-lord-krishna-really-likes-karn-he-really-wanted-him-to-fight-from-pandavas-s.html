Did lord Krishna really like's karn. He really wanted him to fight from Pandavas side or in order to save Pandavas in the war from him. When babruvahan killed Arjun Krishna restored him same for parikshit also. Why can't Krishna restored karn?

📅 Sep 28, 2020 08:06 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Sometimes in question lies the answer. So ask these questions —

  • Was there ever a any friendly relation or interaction between Krishna and Karna mentioned in entire epic other than on that occasion when Krishna asked Karna to switch sides?
  • When Krishna approached Karna, wasn’t that his last attempt to prevent the great war?
  • Whom did Krishna approached first to prevent the war — Duryodhana or Karna? Does that mean he liked Duryodhana more than Karna?
  • Didn’t Krishna already announced in vana parva and again in Udyoga Parva that all the evils will be slain. Did he made any exception for Karna there?
  • Didn’t Krishna concluded his discussion with Karna with the dire projection of total annihilation of Kaurava army?

Answer these questions and you will find the answer to first part — Did Krishna really liked Karna?

It must be pointed that Krishna indeed praised Karna a few times. But there is a mark difference in appreciation and liking — you may like certain traits of even the worst people alive. But that doesn’t mean you would necessarily like the person. Some one may be intelligent but arrogant and wicked. So I praised his intelligence. Does that mean I like him as a person? Well no. Ravana and Meghnad were great warriors. Do I like them? No. Akbar won the war and Maharana Pratap struggled for his entire life. Can you guess whom do I really like? Great Maharana of course. Bhagat Singh failed to see India independent? Does that make him any less hero? So what does Krishna say —

  • Karna was virtuous and at the same time insolent.
  • He was a great warrior with Kavacha, but can be considered slain without his Kavacha or Vasvi Shakti.

Krishna appreciated Karna. Does that mean he liked him?

  • After Ghatotkatch Vadh, Krishna was happy — with Vashvi Shakti gone, consider Karna already slain —

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6414-6415).

  • Would you be happy the prospect of a dear one getting killed? And see how beautifully he nullifies all the great things of valour he said about Karna —

O tiger among men! However, though that spear has now gone, I swear to you truthfully that he is incapable of being killed by anyone other than you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6425-6427).

  • And check out Krishna dialogs to Karna to find the final proof of how much Krishna liked Karna. —

“Vasudeva was stationed on his chariot. He said, ‘O Radheya! It is fortunate that you remember dharma. Quite often, when they are immersed in hardships, inferior ones censure destiny, but not their evil deeds. O Karna! When you, Suyodhana, Duhshasana and Shakuni Soubala brought Droupadi to the assembly hall in a single garment, did dharma not show itself to you? When, in the assembly hall, Yudhishthira, who was not skilled at dice, was defeated by Shakuni, who was skilled at dice, where did dharma go then? O Karna! During her season, Krishna798 was under Duhshasana’s subjugation in the assembly hall and you laughed at her. Where did dharma go then? O Karna! Resorting to the king of Gandhara and coveting the kingdom, you challenged the Pandavas.799 Where did dharma go then?’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4952-4958).

Why can’t Krishna restore Karna?

Wrong Question. It’s not why ‘can’t’, it’s why he didn’t.

And to be honest, it has nothing to do with if he liked or disliked Karna. Many a warrior favorite to Krishna perished in the war — Abhimanyu, Uppandavas. He never ressurcted any of them. There need to be a more than prominent reason to make exceptions. Arjuna was Krishna’s soulmate. He restored him. Parikshita was important for the future of Aryavart, so he get got to live. But these were exception.

Karna was not ressurcted because he was that would have been a contradiction. Krishna wanted him dead. But this no way reflects his liking or disliking. Anyway, important is it was Karna who rejected to be on Gods side. He had it coming.
