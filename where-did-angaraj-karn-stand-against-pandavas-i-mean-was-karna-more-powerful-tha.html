Where did Angaraj Karn stand against Pandavas? I mean, was Karna more powerful than Pandava?

📅 Mar 17, 2021 11:32 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I wasn’t really going to answer this question but for a few extremely distorted narratives mentioned in other answers.

Before I try to answer the question, I would like to understand. What does powerful really mean? Is it brute strength, like a lion who is no doubt powerful, but isn’t a warrior. There were many powerful evils in the past and future. Are we to consider the brute strength alone and not bravery?

Again, what power are we talking about? Just the physical strength to wield the weapon or spiritual strength also? Are we just asking powerful or brave also, because the two words are not synonymous — physical strength makes you physically powerful, spiritual strength makes you brave.

Having set the context, let us analyse power and bravery separately.

Karna vs Pandva (Raw Power and skills, minus Spirtual Strength)

Karna, in terms of raw strength and skills, was apparently greater than Yudhishthir, and the twins. He could have defeated them easily. To add to it, Yudhishthir was always terrified of Karna for some unknown and unsubstantiated reason, I would attribute it to psychology. That makes him weaker than Karna as a fighter. This is also clear from the fact that Karna spared the life of these three Pandavas.

When it comes to Karna vs Bhima, I would say the result would be indecisive. They both defeated each other on a number of occasions. They both spared each other on number of occasions. Bhima had several opportunities to kill Karna, but he didn’t. On several of these fights other warriors rescued Karna from Bhima. Many Kaurava brothers lost their life saving Karna and all Karna could do was to cry for them. Some may say Karna was oath bound not to kill Bhima. Then Karna sparing Bhima can’t be considered his generosity but duty. However, Bhima wasn’t oath bound to spare Karna and yet he did that. That can only be attributed to a warrior’s ethics. But it can’t be concluded without doubt who would be considered more powerful.

Arjuna vs Karna

Without a doubt, Arjuna was way more powerful than Karna. In every direct and indirect encounter between Arjuna and Karna, Karna was defeated without any exception.

Arjuna thrashed Karna after Draupadi Swyamvara where a scared Karna retreated. These are the exact words mentioned in BORI CE.

In Matasya battle Arjuna again thrashed Karna who fled several times before he became unconscious and Arjuna spared his life again along with others.

In battle against Gandharavas, Karan fled for his life. Same Gandharvas were beatend and defeated by Arjuna.

On day 14, Arjuna again defeated Karna on his way to slay Jaidradh. Karna could do nothing to protect Jaidradh. On this day Arjuna challenged Karna that he would kill his son in front of his eyes and he could do nothing to prevent that.

And behold, Arjuna later fulfilled his promise in the exact way, in front of Karna and Karna couldn’t protect his son.

On day 16, Arjuna again defeated Karna so badly that everyone lost any remaining hope of victory. The defeat was acknowledged by Karna.

On day 17, Karna himself acknowledged no one could defeat Arjuna, and a doubt crept into his mind and he kept repeating that either he would kill or Arjuna or die on his hand. Death was already on Karna’s mind.

Arjuna spared Karna’s life on more occasion than he spared the lives of all other Pandavas taken together. I guess, Karna was still in Arjuna’s debt when he met his maker.

KARNA VS PANDAVAS IN SPIRITUAL STRENGTH (HUMANITY AND WARRIOR ETHICS)

If we consider the warrior ethics and humanity Karna didn’t come closer to any Pandavas who were good humans established in Dharma. It is confirmed by Shiva, Krishna, and Ved Vyas.

  • Karna was an evil person who conspired with Duryodhana to Kill Bhima and other Pandavas on multiple occasion. Pandavas never conspired against them.
  • Karna challenged Arjuna for a duel after Draupadi’s swyamvara. He had invisible divine armour. Arjuna had no special divine weapon. He was using a newly acquired bow. Yet Karna retreated scared. Why? because invincible kavach could protect you from physical damage but can’t make you brave and fearless. This is one proof of spiritual weakness.
  • Bhima defeated Karna in Rajsuy yagna (BORI CE) and spared his life.
  • Karna insulted their wife. Karna devised an evil plan to mock Pandavas who were suffering in exile. The plan backfired. Gandharvas attacked them. Once again Karna had his invincible Kavacha. And once again he fled away. Kavacha could have protected him, but couldn’t help him overcome his fear.
  • I won’t talk about mighty Arjuna’s feat against Gandharvas. I will talk about Nakul and Sahadeva. They weren’t the renowned warriors. They had no divine weapon in their possession. They had no divine Kavach like Karna. Yet they went on to fight Gandharvas to rescue women of the royal family. They didn’t flee, fearing Gandharvas. In absence of divine weapon or protection they were more likely to be killed in battle than Karna and yet they fought fearlessly and Karna fled — Karna may have been physical stronger, twins were braver than Karna. They never fled any battle.
  • Karna happily traded his kavach for Vasvi Shakti. (Happily is mentioned in BORI CE). He openly admitted that he is scared of Arjuna. He hoped that he would kill Arjuna using Vasvishakti. Why did he need Vasvi Shakti? Because he was scared and knew he couldn’t win Arjuna otherwise. When Vasvishakti was expended on Ghatotkatch, he sought the help of a serpant to kill Arjuna! What about his own powers? Well, Karna never felt confident that he could kill Arjuna with his own strength, therefore Krishna was delighted when Vasvi Shakti was gone. He said consider Karna already slain. It wasn’t Karna he was worried about.
  • Another interesting point to understand is how Karna lost Vasvi Shakti — the most potent weapon in his possession? He used it on Ghatotkatch. Why did he do that? Why couldn’t he defeat Ghatotkatch using other means? Was Ghatotkatch the strongest warrior of Mahabharata? Arjuna never using his most potent weapons — not even against Bhishma and Drona. The reason is simple — Karna lacked perseverance to fight. He lacked spiritual strength. He would get frightened easily and out of this fear he used the weapon on Ghatotkatch. Had he been braver, he could have devised another solution. If we say there wasn’t another solution, the question is what was the raw power of Karna without those divine artifacts?
  • Yudhishthir was ready to give up his claim and vengeance to avoid a devastating war. Was Karna brave enough to leave his ambition aside and do something for humanity? How was Karna more powerful than Arjuna?
  • Bhima volunteered to fight Banasura to save an entire village from a cannibal. He risked his life to protect others. Karna fled Gandharvas, leaving his best friend and family behind. Guess who was more powerful? More important, whose power was worth more?

Who do you think was more powerful? Someone who won a few battles and fled other battles to side with selfish evil purposes or someone who dared to fight for others when they weren’t supposed to be?

Who would be you hero — A mighty powerful, invincible cruel evil who conspires to burn an elderly woman his son alive and flee his own friends or perhaps a less powerful warrior who is ready to fight for people in distress, risking their own life?
