Who wrote bhagvad geeta when krishna narrated it to arjuna?

📅 Mar 28, 2020 01:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I have recently answered a very very similar question hence I will not repeat myself here completely. I would recommend looking at my other answer here → Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Where is the actual original written Shrimad Bhagwad Geeta kept?

But a short answer → Gita was a live commentary (something like realtime streaming) and conversation between Krishna and Arjuna and also heard by Sanjay and Vyasji. It was during the great epic war and no one was taking dictation at the time Krishna narrated the Gita.

Another important point is no written record (could) survive 5000 years, and was not the philosophy of our ancient teachers. The information was passed on from one generation to another verbally because it was considered (and should still be considered) a great means of preserving the knowledge. Thats where our most ancients wisdoms are referred as Shruti — what is heard.
