In Mahabharata, was Karn the biggest warrior of all Kauravas and Pandavas?

📅 Apr 27, 2021 10:49 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Oh! yes. It would not be an exaggeration to say that Karna was the biggest warrior not only in Mahabharata but the entire Santana epic with such great achievements which no one else can claim — neither gods nor asura.

Do you know anyone from the entire epic who had the dare to cheat his own guru? Do you know even a demon who had done that? But Karna did it without a blink. Who can be bigger?

Have you heard about a foolish warrior named Lakshamana (from Ramayana)? He wasted his whole life running behind his brother. But here we had a great warrior who didn’t even blink as he announced to his own mother that he would kill one his own brothers. How many warriors existed ever who could have dared to say on the face of his mother that I know my brother is on side of Dharma but if I get a chance I would kill him! Even that weak Arjuna hesitated at the thought of killing his kin. But not the big warrior Karna.

Didn’t you see how valiantly Karna fought against all those lowly kings, who were trying to throw Draupadi back to fire because they failed to win the Swaymavara? Imagine had Karna not been there, Draupadi would have died that very day. But he forced everyone to blackout and protected Draupadi. Had Karna been weaker, he would have retreated scared from the battle considering the opponent to be invincible.

And again didn’t you see, how valiantly Karna faced all those Gandharvas who had attacked his friends and families which included all the royal women? Imagine, had Karna not defeated Chitrasena, Duryodhana and royal woman of Hastinapur would have been captured. But the great warrior Karna single handedly defeated all those Gandharvas and freed not only Kauravas but also Pandavas who too were captured by Gandharvas! If Karna were a weaker warrior he would have jumped on Vikarna’s chariot and fled leaving everyone behind.

And then don’t forget the Matasya battle. Remember Pandavas were on their expedition to loot Matasya Kingdom’s wealth after Keechak’s death. But valiant Karna single handedly defeated the robbers and stripped them of their cloths. Had Karna been weaker, he would have fled at least thrice in the battle.

Remember the time when Karna went to ask Shiva for his Pashupata and Shiva came to fight him in the disguise of a Kirata and how Karna vanquished him in a single blow! Had Arjuna gone to fight Kirata he would have surely been vanquished by Kirata. But then Karna was the biggest warrior.

And remember how valiantly Karna fought alone in Drona’s chakravyuh! Remember how Arjuna fled after seeing his brother killed, and how Abhimanyu cut down his bow from behind. But despite of being weaponless Karna defeated everyone. When he lost his bows he took up a chariot wheel to protect himself. And no one could kill him! In fact both Kauravas and Pandavas tried to kill him together but they all failed to kill him.

Remember how bravely Karna managed to protect Jaidradh from a sure death at the hands of Arjuna. If Jaidradh managed to live beyond fourteenth day it was all because Karna didn’t let Arjuna reach Pandavas.

Karna never cared about any weapon. He simply didn’t care, and that why he used Vasvi Shakti on Ghatotkatch whom he could have killed with his bare hands.

And don’t forget how that great warrior was killed! When he was without his chariot! Have you known any warrior who fought without a chariot? Now I know Arjuna fan’s will tell a fake story that Arjuna fought without chariot on fourteenth day when Krishna tended to his horses. But we all know it is a fake thing which some scholar must have declared and interpolation. Please… that must be an interpolation…

And then remember the authentic stories where —

  • Karna donated his tooth before his death
  • Karna pushed Krishna’s chariot few steps backs

And forget weak warriors like Rama, Krishna and Shiva, Karna had the capacity to even defeat Rajnikanth!

On a serious note —

There had been power warriors in the past like Raavan, Tarkasura and Hiranyakashayap. There existed powerful people later like Hitler. But the question is what is the worth of such a power that caused more havoc than good for the society? Shouldn’t we asking —

  • What good Karna did for the society?
  • Did Karna protect a woman in distress?
  • Did he try to protect a kid from getting poisoned?
  • Did he try to protect a family from getting burnt?
  • Did he try to protect a woman from being thrown in fire?
  • Did he try to save the modesty of a women?
  • Did he try to protect his own family, friend, brothers, son, woman from adversary?
  • Did he do anything in his life that would make his life remarkable or valuable for humanity?

Sadly all these question draws a blank
