Will Arjuna ever be as popular as Karna?

📅 Aug 26, 2021 09:13 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

If Arjuna ever becomes as popular as Karna, that would be a decline in his popularity. For if you ignore the recent surge or hype, Arjuna had always been the way more powerful character of Mahabharata.

Krishna described him as his inseparable existence, Shiva graced him with his most potent weapons, Parshurama declared him invanquishable and Karna called him an incomparable warrior. But all these point simply go on to prove true greatness and not glimic or popularity.

So who has been more popular? Guess there is an award in one person’s name and not in another person’s names. There are many other milestones that are mentioned in his name. We have lessons learnt from the childhood where one person could see only the bird’s eye while other invested all his life competing the bow who could see only the bird’s eye.

But question may arise — Why is there a surge of popularity in Karna’s favor? Why there are TVShows after Karna and not Arjuna? To understand this, I would encourage you to go and watch the TVShows based on Karna’s life and you would find the full of fabrication —

  • Imagine Krishna being the charioteer of Karna! Not only Karna but the modern fans of Karna too wanted Karna to be like Arjuna. So guess who is more popular?
  • Imagine Karna imploring Duryodhana to stop humiliating Draupadi instead of encouraging Duhashasha to strip her! If you look you would realize that even those Karna fan’s are horrified by his act and trying to create an alternative narrative to make a hero, something he wasn’t.

It requires creative skill to make portray a negative character as hero. It is a challenging task and ensures viewer have something new to watch even if majority of it garbage or nonsense. This may make Karna popular, but can we still call him Karna? Looks like people are fascinated by the name of Karna and have crafted fictional image of what he should have been rather than what he really was.

Now you don’t need to have creative skill to portray Arjuna. Do you know why? Not because he is a good guy. But because he has already been portrayed brilliantly in one of the greatest epic ever written — the one which is feeding the imagination of all modern narratives. And in that narrative Arjuna is the undisputed hero. Do we need more proof of popularity?
