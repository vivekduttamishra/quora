Did Arjuna abduct Subhadra without her consent?

📅 Jun 16, 2020 02:05 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

I would say Arjuna abducted Shubhadra without her explicit consent, but original and undisputed narratives leaves no doubt about the implicit consent, apart from explicit consent of Krishna —

The son of Kunti, afflicted with the shafts of the god of desire, suddenly rushed towards that Yadava girl of faultless features and forcibly took her into his car. Having seized that girl of sweet smiles, that tiger among men proceeded in his car of gold towards his own city (Indraprastha).

The mention of the sweet smile doesn't leave any doubt about her desire. But let us seek some more convincing and circumstantial evidence —

  1. How likely it is that Krishna the protector of Draupadi would let someone brutalize his own sister? True he wasn't sure of her exact feeling, was it unlikely that he had an incling?
  2. They return back to Dwarka where they are wedded and lived for a long period? Did she resent Arjuna's action at any point?
  3. There is no mention of Shunhasra's torment or stigma of abduction anywhere in Mahabharat. Why?
  4. Bhagwat Puran clarifies Shubhadra's desires more clearly.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
