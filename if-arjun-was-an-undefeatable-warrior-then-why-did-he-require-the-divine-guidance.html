If Arjun was an undefeatable warrior then why did he require the divine guidance and protection of Bhagwan Srikrishna during all 18 days of the Mahabharat War?

📅 Oct 30, 2021 01:26 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I dare say, the premise of the question is flawed and seeks to negate one great quality with another assuming them to be mutually exclusive, when they are not. Let us see.

Being undefeatable warrior isn’t greater than being a better human being. Arjuna could have vanquished his enemy by misusing innumerable divine weapons bestowed upon him. But for him being a human was more important for him when he decides —

But I speak the truth when I say that you should not be tortured in your mind. When I have Vasudeva as an aide, on a single chariot, I can slay the three worlds in an instant, with the immortals, with their mobile and immobile objects, and with everything that is the past, the present and the future. That is my view. I possess the terrible and great weapon that Pashupati gave me when there was a duel with the hunter. 114 At the end of a yuga, that is employed by Pashupati to destroy all the beings. O tiger among men! I know how to use that. Gangeya does not know this, nor do Drona and Goutama. O king! Neither do Drona’s son and the son of the suta. One should not use such divine weapons to kill ordinary people in battle. Without resorting to deceit, we will vanquish our enemies in battle. O king! These tigers among men are your aides. All of them are skilled in the use of divine weapons.

— Udyoga Parva

See, his politeness and lack of ego. He considers other warriors as great. He gives credit of his capabilities to Krishna and Shiva. He puts humanity before his ego and victory. And he doesn’t use any higher weapon during the battle. That makes him greater than just undefeatable.

Even Gods need their champion on earth. It was because of these human qualities Krishna pronounced Arjuna to be his inseparable existence. Aren’t we getting swayed by our prejudice while trying to separate those two inseparable existence?

Now having set the right pretext, let us attack the question itself —


Why did Arjuna require the divine guidance of Bhagwan Srikrishna?

Only an absolute fool would consider that he doesn’t need a divine guidance and only a complete devil would reject it if opportunity presents itself. Arjuna was neither. But we had example of both (fools and devil) in the epic, if you look carefully.

Even an undefeatable warrior needs guidance. This was specially true when he values humanity and compassion over victory and ego. Same thing happened with Arjuna. He couldn’t muster sufficient ruthlessness to pronounce death for his dear ones, knowing victory means killing them. He gave up his arms. And there comes the divine guidance that is eternal. Now think, had Arjuna been just an ordinary fighter, he didn’t needed the guidance. And then such person would not have been Krishna’s inseparable existence. Do you know any other person whom Krishna called with this name?

God bestows their guidance on the deserving. Fools may consider it Arjuna’s weakness, instead of the greatest proof of his worthiness, something it really was.

But Krishna’s guidance couldn’t eradicate his human values and sentiments completely. He needed to keep him guiding as he keep on fighting mildly against his grandfather Bhishma, his guru Drona and Kripacharya. Without his guidance, Arjuna was lost between being a warrior and a kind souled human.


Why did Arjuna require the divine protection of Bhagwan Srikrishna during all 18 days of the Mahabharat War?

Let me first clarify Krishna protected Arjuna only thrice in the entire eighteen days —

  1. Against Vaishnavastra of Bhagdatta
  2. Against Naryanastra of Ashwatthama
  3. Against Naga Ashwasena fired by Karna

All these weapons were fired with irresponsibly without a dire reason. Ashwatthama initiated Narayanastra out of anger against weaker opponents, Bhagdatta fired it when Arjuna was fighting him without using such a weapon. But Karna’s initiation was most treacherous. It wasn’t a divine weapon but an evil demonic one. It was a conspiracy between an evil human and a devil snake. Naturally God intervened in those precious moments, lest evil might have won.

Some may want to include Krishna trying to avoid Karna because of Vasvi Shakti as his protection. But there are two points to be noted —

  • It was a human strategy and not a divine strategy something any friend would try for his other friend.
  • Karna and Arjuna fought on day 14 and before that on day 12. Karna had Vasvi Shakti on both occasion and yet he was bitten and forced to retreat. So the idea that Arjuna didn’t confront in this period doesn’t hold water.

WHEN KRISHNA DIDN’T PROTECT ARJUNA?

  • Krishna didn’t protect Arjuna on first ten days. He rather motivated Arjuna to kill Bhishma.
  • He doesn’t protect Arjuna on any occasion other than the three above mentioned scenario
  • He doesn’t guide or protect Arjuna during Gurudakshina parva where he defeats Drupad.
  • He doesn’t guide or protect Arjuna during Swyamvar battle where he forces scared Karna to retreat.
  • He doesn’t guide or protect Arjuna during his battle against Nivatakavacha.
  • He doesn’t protect him during Gandharva battle where Arjuna defeated large Ganhdarva army to protect evil Kurus.
  • He doesn’t protect him during Matasya battle where he defeated entire Kuru army.

S U M M A R Y

Arjuna was undefeatable warrior on his own accord with a proven track record. But that was just one aspect of Arjuna. He being a good and compassionate human was another and greater trait of his life won him the grace of Gods who helped him in his endeavor and protected him.
