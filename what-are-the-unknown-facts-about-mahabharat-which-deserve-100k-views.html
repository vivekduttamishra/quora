What are the unknown facts about Mahabharat which deserve 100K views?

📅 Apr 30, 2020 01:17 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I believe, by unknown the fact means —

  1. Popularly unknown by those who didn't get chance to read the authentic version.
  2. Misguided by popular TV shows and fiction that reimagined the story.

I am not including those stories which are added by later folklores and fiction and promoted as unknown story of Mahabharat such as —

  1. Story of Barbarik
  2. Story of 5 Golden arrows of Bhisma

Here are some almost unknown facts of Mahabharat —

  1. Ganga had first approached Shantanu's father for marriage and he had pledged his son to him, long before latter's birth.
  2. Dronacharya neither taught exclusively to Kuru princes nor ever rejected Karna from his gurukul. Karna studied under three guru's Kripacharya, Dronacharya and Parshuram. Drona only refused the knowledge of Brahmastra. This is mentioned in three different Parvas of Mahabharat.
  3. Karna had deliberately introduced himself as a Brahmin.
  4. Karna did participate in the Gurudakshina war alongside Duryodhana. Later Arjuna won the war.
  5. Eklavya was slain by Lord Krishna.
  6. Shakuni was not the mastermind of most conspiracies attributes to him. He has negligible role other than the dice game. He was almost insignificant in Poisoning Bheem, varnavrat or Gandharva episode or Virat. In fact tried to persuade Duryodhan to accept Krishna's peace deal.
  7. Shakuni's dice is not made of his father's bones.
  8. Karna failed at Draupadi Swaymber unlike popular perception that he was declined by Draupadi. This is mentioned by Neelkantha edition, BORI, Dakshinayat, Rajgopalachari and almost all authentic reference. However Draupadi rejection is also mentioned by Neelkanth (along side his failure)
  9. Draupadi never laughed at Duryodhana at Indraprastha.
  10. Ghatotkatch and Abhimanyu were not surprise entry to Kurukshetra war on day 14 and 13 respectively. Abhimanyu fought from day 1 and Ghatitkatch is found fighting as early as day 4.
  11. Indra didn't obtain Kavach and Kundal by deceit, but as a barter for Indrastra. True Indra came in disguise and demanded Kavach Kundal, but Karna clearly said he won't give the Kavach and Kundal unless Indra gives him Indrastra.
  12. Arjun killed Karna in a fair fight. Karna's chariot was stuck up but he wasn't unarmed. He fought for some time, countered Arjuna's attack before dying.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
  • Mahabharat by Rajgopalachari based on Dakshinayat
