Why could only a few like Arjuna, Karna, Sita, Rama, and Parshuram lift Lord Shiva’s bow/dhanush?

📅 Jan 06, 2022 05:51 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Ask yourself, if a bow is too heavy to lift what would be it’s usefulness?

Now if you mediate on this question you would realize lifting a divine bow has nothing to with physical power, it is a testimony to your character strength. And it is absolute opposite of arrogance of physical strength. Strength of character makes you humble. It makes you a devotee. It makes you surrender to divinity.

Only those were capable of lifting the bow who were devotees and became a channel to deities strength. Those who channeled their own ego failed to lift the bow.

Shri Rama, Devi Sita and Bhagwan Parshurama all qualified for this trait of character and hence could lift the bow. Mind it they acted as conductor for Shiva’s strength. Because they all were Shiva devotee and Shiva had blessed them.

Arjuna never had a chance to life Shiva’s bow. But seeing how Shiva graced him with his presence and Pashupata it would not be a stretch to realize that Arjuna might have lifted the bow, although there is no proof that he ever did. Shiva himself proclaimed him the greatest warrior of the era and one who is capable of unleashing even Pashupata.

Coming to the last contender, Karna, I see no possibility that he could have lifted the bow of Shiva. He was full or ego and arrogance and had the audacity of rejecting Lord Krishna himself. He was deceitful and lacked strength of character. Such cursed people could never channel divine energy and that is what Parshurama indicated that you would fail to initiate divyastra —

He (Parshurama) smiled and said, ‘You acted in this false way because of your greed for weapons. O stupid one! In a different place, when the time for your death has come, you will be engaged in a fight with someone who is your equal and the brahmastra will not manifest itself before you. The qualities of a brahmana will never remain with someone who is not a brahmana. Leave this place, since this is not meant for an untruthful one like you.

Now note, here Brahmana refers to quality and not some caste. As a proof Brahmastra was used by Shri Rama although he wasn’t a Brahmana but a Kshatriaya. He possessed the Brahamana quality of devotion and truth.

There is no reference that Karna ever did lift one of Shiva’s bow. Some people may be trying to refer to “Vijaya Bow”. It must be noted that —

  • Vijaya bow was Indra’s bow
  • It belonged to Rukmi as mentioned by Vaisampanya and not Karna

Those who reside in heaven possess three divine bows—Varuna’s Gandiva, the great Indra’s bow Vijaya and Vishnu’s Sharnga, which is said to be a celestial bow that is full of energy. Krishna wields it and strikes terror in the soldiers of enemies. The son of the chastiser of Paka19 obtained Gandiva from the fire god in Khandava. The immensely energetic Rukmi obtained Vijaya from Druma.
