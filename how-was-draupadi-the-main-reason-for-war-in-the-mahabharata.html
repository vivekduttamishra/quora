How was Draupadi the main reason for war in the Mahabharata?

📅 Nov 29, 2021 06:42 AM PST  |  🌐 English

BEYOND DOUBT, DRAUPADI WAS ONE OF THE MAJOR REASON FOR THE MAHABHARATA WAR.

I understand, based on the other answers to this question, many don’t agree to this suggestion. And they are not wrong from the point of view they see it.

Druapadi was no evil. She never did anything wrong. The evil tree had spread it’s poisonous fruit predating the birth of Yagyaseni. They had poisoned Bhima, tried to burn down Pandavas long before Draupadi’s arrival at the scene. How can she be held responsible for a war that was a direct consequence of the lust, greed, ambition and devilish mindset of evils like Duryodhana, Karna, Shakuni and Dhritrashtra. Didn’t even Karna admitted this basic fact —

Karna honoured Krishna Madhusudana31 and spoke these words. ‘O mighty-armed one! Knowing everything, why did you wish to confuse me? The destruction of the entire earth is imminent. Shakuni, Duhshasana and Dhritarashtra’s son, King Duryodhana, and I are responsible.

—Karna Upanivada Parva

SO WHY DID I SAY, DRAUPADI WAS A MAJOR REASON FOR MAHABHARATA WAR?

Because there is a difference between “reason” and “responsible”. And also because, in a Dharma Yudha there are two parties involved — Dharma and Adharma. Take out one of them and there is no war. Unless someone rises against adharma, there won’t be any war. Remember Krishna incarnated to reestablish Dharma by —

  1. protecting the good
  2. destroying the evil.

So can we say only one of these factors was the reason for Krishna’s birth?

One of the answers to this question, took a contemporary example that is closer to my heart and I have often used it in a similar context in my answers — Nirbhaya.

It would not only be absurd but also brutal and inhumane to think, Nirbhaya was responsible for heinous acts of those brutal beasts. They got what they deserved.

But it would be a capital mistake to think, that the story of Nirbhaya starts on the sorry note of her merciless torment and concluded with hanging of those beasts. Those beasts certainly ended their vile and worthless existence on the gallops, but Nirbhaya lived on. She lives on to point out the flaws in our law. She lives on to haunt our civil society at the mockery of justice as the juvenile criminal walked on technicality and others got lease on their lives for many more years, when they should have met their maker lot sooner.

Is not Nirbhaya the reason, that our legislators had too re-look into our criminal laws? Can someone steal the credit from her for being the reason that society would be a bit more safer for future Nirbhayas? Won’t she be the reason if we can have more stringent laws to keep these beastly creatures at bay?

WHY WAS DRAUAPADI, A MAJOR REASON FOR THE WAR OF MAHABHARATA?

Draupadi snatched away the facade of justice and righteousness from then contemporary society. A society that debated on technicality of gambling and if Draupadi was won rightfully or not rather than the morality of gambling.

To understand the significance of Draupadi in the Mahabharata war, I would urge you to remove her from the equation for a moment and then ask yourself what was the war really about? A piece of land? Fight between the cousins? Greed and ambition? Where is the Dharma in it?

Now introduce her back to the equation. What do you see now? A revenge story? Look deeper. Do you see the misinterpretation of justice? Do you see the denial of it? Now do you see, why Draupadi was one of the major reasons for the war?

SUMMARY

A Dharma Yudha has two sides — Dharma and Adharma. Adharma is the reason war starts, and it ends in establishment of Dharma. Adharma is responsible for it’s starts, Dharma the reason and ultimate goal of this war.

— Vivek Dutta Mishra
