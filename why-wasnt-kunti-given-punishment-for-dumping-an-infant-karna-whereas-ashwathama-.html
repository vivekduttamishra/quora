Why wasn't Kunti given punishment for dumping an infant (Karna) whereas Ashwathama was given punishment to suffer till the end of time when he showed no mercy on an unborn child (Parikshit)?

📅 Oct 07, 2022 01:22 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question shows an absolute lack of understanding of the situation here and such degree of ignorance certainly leads to confusions like these.

First let us see Ashwatthama. He was a killer. He used divine weapon in his blind raze to kill an innocent unborn. For his most henious deed and worse of intention he was given an unprecedented punishment. Period.

Kunti, on the other hand was a saviour. She surrendered herself to Surya to protect his family and Kingdom from the ire of Surya. Protector!

She knew she can't keep her son with him without doing irrepareable damage to the reputation of his family, herself and even Karna? Can you quote one instance from Mahabharata where an unwed mother held on to her son? She separated Karna from herself. But that can't be called dumping.

Even when lettings Karna go, she was protecting him. She extracted a divine armour to protect his life, unlike Ashwatthama who used divine weapon to kill. She sought blessing for Karna from Surya. She loaded Karna's cart with so much wealth that he was known as Vasusena.

Now if you need to dump someone, you just need to follow Devi Ganga’s lead. You don't need to procure divine armour, stuff wealth and ensure he is safe by assigning watch.

By the way by that parameter even Devki dumped Krishna. If you don't understand intention, it's a waste.
