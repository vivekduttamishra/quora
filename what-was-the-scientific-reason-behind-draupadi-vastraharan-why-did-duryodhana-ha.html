What was the scientific reason behind Draupadi Vastraharan? Why did Duryodhana hate Draupadi?

📅 Feb 23, 2022 12:48 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Scientific Reason! Really?

Well let me guess. You may call it a neurological imbalance, or a genetic disease generally found in underdeveloped humans, who retain their beastly quality and can’t be considered as fully formed human. They lie somewhere between inhuman and beast. This disease has a common name — pervert.

If, however, you sought a political reason instead of scientific, I would call it a political blunder. Kauravas lost everything that they had won in one clean sweep. They had a short term goal — humiliate Pandavas and didn't’ realize what happens when you try to humiliate people more powerful than you. They may annihilate you. That’s exactly what happened.

Now coming to the next question —

Why Duryodhana hated Draupadi?

Duryodhana hated Pandavas and Draupadi was now part of Pandavas. The hate was naturally redirected towards her from a ruthless, spineless, evil who didn’t hesitate to attempt to burn them down along with their mother. I don’t see a reason why he would have behaved any differently with Draupadi.

That said, Duryodhana wasn’t the one who commanded Draupadi’s vastraharan. That doesn’t mean he is not to be blamed. He commanded dragging of Draupadi to the court room. His goal was public humiliation of Pandavas and Draupadi was a mere tool to do so. His worst act would have been calling her names.

But if you are searching for the biggest pervert of the era, that award will undoubtedly go to his great friend Angaraj Karna. It was his generosity and valor to do something, even Ravana didn’t dare in his time — make a woman public in naked. It was he who commanded Duhshashana to strip Draupadi, because he felt it is ok for the slave to be naked and wife to be mere property of her husbands.

And if you want to dismiss his great deed as one off act in a moment of excitement, you would be wrong. It was who he was. In his own words — all the prosperity or even obtaining a son is pale in comparison to see your enemy tormented, specifically the joy of seeing Draupadi and Arjuna in tree barks. Then not to forget, his choicest selection of words to describe Madra women or even the great generous attempt to offer his own wives to the foot soldier who may reveal the location of Arjuna. Why should then, it be a surprise that such a pervert would hate Draupadi? Or Pandavas?

In summary, the reason why Kaurava camp hated Draupadi was, she was a constant reminder of Pandava’s prosperity and their own failures. For Duryodhana she was just a means to humiliate Pandavas. For Karna, a lustrous misogynist, she was constant reminder of her humiliating failures during Druapadi's swaymvar, be it failure to lift the bow or scared retreat after his foolhardy of challenging Arjuna in the battlefield. He knew, he couldn’t win against Arjuna in the battlefield. In this humiliation he may have found a tiny bit of victory.
