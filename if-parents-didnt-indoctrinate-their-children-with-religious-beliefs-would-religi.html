If parents didn't indoctrinate their children with religious beliefs, would religion vanish?

📅 Feb 18, 2022 09:56 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Ah! This wishful thinking has existed for ages. Can’t say many thousands of years, because no other existing religion are older than couple of thousand years that their follower would try to eradicate it.

The main reason every such attempt has failed is your failure to realize — you can’t eradicate what doesn’t exist. Neither can you eradicate what is omnipresent.


‘HINDUISM’ DOESN’T EXIST.

It NEVER really existed. It is not a religion. It is the product of narrow and ignorant mindset who needed to coin a word for something omnipresent down to their level of limited understanding. They tried to mock it, ignore it, destroy it. But as I said, you can’t destroy what doesn’t exist.

Hinduism isn’t a religion like Christianity or Islam. It is not propagated by some one prophet or follows some one book that you may seek to destroy. We don’t have a holy book so ‘holy’ that talking against it can make us frantic or fanatic enough that we create mayhem or kill scientists to suppress the truth.

We don’t need to widen the the number of followers like a cult by hook or crook, bribe or fear. Because we are not a religion or a cult.

How would you destroy or eradicate something that doesn’t really exist?

I will give you a mantra and that would surely destroy ‘Hinduism’. Come out of the narrow and ignorant notion that there is a religion like ‘Hinduism’. Delete the word from dictionary and you would find Hinduism is eradicated. And that would be the greatest favour you would do to us. And also to yourself.

Once you remove this word from your mind you would see another aspect —


THE SANATANTA HINDU IS OMNIPRESENT AND OMNIPOTENT

What is Hindu?

You would realize what you call ‘Hindu’ is the Sanatana civilization — the very definition of humanity. A civilization that emphasized on no single guru, no one propagator, no one religious book, no one deity. We are not bound as a cult or even as religion. We are bound by one common belief —

ॐ सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः ।
सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु मा कश्चिद्दुःखभाग्भवेत् ।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥

See the higher goal — May all be happy. May all be free of disease. May all see what is auspicious and may no one suffer. Let there be peace everywhere.

And when we recite this mantra, in all we include even you, you who cherish a dream of eradicating Hindu. That is the all inclusiveness. That is a prayer for humanity.

And as long as there would be people following any religion or cult but genuinely follow the welfare of humanity, they would be Hindu.

Now tell me how do you eradicate Hindu? And more important, what would be left once you eradicate Hindu?

Hindu and Secularism?

When government around the world are trying to bring in harmony among followers of different religions by coining the terms like secularism, what few realize that they are just trying be Hindu.

What they are trying an failing miserably is to realize a simple truth —

एकम सत्यम विप्रा बहुधा वदन्ति

Truth, and by extension, One God, is followed in different names.

This is one of the discriminating factor between religion and the essence of humanity (Hindu). Only something of the higher order than a religion can dare to announce that same god or truth is perceived differently by different people. A religion, that by it’s egoistic definition can never reach this truth and as such can never wish well for others. When they do, they become Hindu.

Thirty Three Crore Deities

We proudly say we have 33 crore deities. I would say it is an old census today we may have multi-times more. Does that mean every Hindu worships millions of deities?

NO. All it means is —

  1. Everyone is free to chose whom you want to worship. You would still be Hindu. You may worship Jesus or Buddha or Gurunanak or trees, rivers, stones or even nothing, and you would still be a Hindu if you follow the civil codes of humanity and want to be called a human or Hindu and abide by the second rule.
  2. We respect your choice of deity. I may not personally worship that deity but I have no qualms bowing before him/her. Your deity is mine as well. And that is how we have crores of deities. And that is why, a Buddhist or a Sikh is still a Hindu. I have no problem is bowing before Jesus. For me even Jesus is one among the crores of deity. A Hindu can’t harbor such hate.

And it is a part of our core belief system. Krishna himself said in Gita — Anyone who worships any deity, I ensure he concentrates on the same deity and would still reach his goal.


Can world afford to eradicate Hindu?

That is the right question one should be asking. Hindu is synonym to humanity. Hindu is synonym to oldest existing civilization. Hindu is part of your genes. Everyone who cherishes world peace his a Hindu. Everyone who seeks welfare of others is too Hindu. Everyone who strives to be secular is Hindu. Everyone who respects others despite of their religion or belief is a Hindu. Everyone can hope to co-exist is a Hindu. You must have a part of yourself that can be called Hindu. Now tell me, how do you eradicate that Hindu from within you? Tell me what would be left if you do succeed in eradicating the very thought that is definition of Hindu?

Be careful what you wish for…
