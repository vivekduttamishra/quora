Can we say it was Bhima's own cruel and bad Karma which led him to being poisoned by Duryodhana because Bhima himself used to tortured and harrased (both physically and mentally) innocent Kaurava brothers in childhood and almost used to murder them?

📅 Apr 14, 2021 07:48 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This kind of leading questions has just one goal — malign and distort the great epic.

The fact that question refers to a section of Adi Parva, indicates questioner knows the question is in fact incorrect. Here are two important reference both from Adi Parva —

When they played childish games in their father’s house with Dhritarashtra’s sons, they excelled over them—in running fast, in hitting targets, in eating and in raising dust. Bhimasena defeated all of Dhritarashtra’s sons. Playfully, he grabbed them by the hair above their ears. He caught them by the heads when they fought with the Pandavas. Alone and with the least effort, Vrikodara oppressed those 101 sons, who possessed great energy. The strongest of the strong would kick them with his feet. He would throw them down on the ground and break their thighs, heads and shoulders. When playing in the water, he would sometimes catch ten of them in his arms and hold them down in the water, letting them go only when they were about to drown. When they climbed a tree to gather fruit, Bhima would kick the tree with his feet and make it shake. Shaken by the force, the tree would whirl around and swiftly fall down on the ground with its fruit, frightening the princes. In fights, speed and martial exercises, the princes were never able to get the better of Vrikodara in any competition.

Thus, in any competition, Dhritarashtra’s sons began to hate Vrikodara, even though he bore no ill will towards them, since he was only a child. ‘Bhimasena’s great strength became well known. On learning this, Dhritarashtra’s powerful son320 revealed the evil side of his nature. He was wicked and inclined towards evil. Out of delusion and greed for riches, an evil thought occurred to him. “Kunti’s son Vrikodara, the second of the Pandavas, is the best in strength. I must find some trick so as to kill him. Then I will overpower his younger brother and his elder brother Yudhishthira. I will tie them up and reign as the sovereign of the earth.”

Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 4922-4923). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

—BORI, Adi Parva

So what makes Vrikodhara tormenting Kauravas a playful act and Duryodhana’s action as evil? Some may say that the narrator was a biased person. But must I remind, he is the same narrator who narrated how Pandavas baited their own wife or how Arjuna failed to protect every last Dwarka women from ordinary robbers? Ved Vyas was a common ancestor to both Kauravas and Pandavas and had no reason to favour one over the other.

So what is the reaons of calling one as playful and other as evil? The point is actions don’t count; intentions do. Tell me—

  • What is the difference between a serial killer and a valiant army officer? Both kill.
  • What is the difference between a supari killer and court judge? both pronunce death sentence for others.
  • What is the difference between a robber and a tax collecting government? Both deprive you of your hard earned money.

If you think the demarcating idea is intention.

Bhima never really intended to kill Kauravas and that is important. He played, he was strong, and he overpowered them. Few bones were broken. But breaking of bones while playing was a common occurrence not only in Mahabharata era but even among people who were growing up in 1980’s and 1990’s. I have few of my broken bones as a testimony to it. When people play outside, when they try to climb a tree or fly kite from first floor, all sorts of accidents occur. But kids of current era who don’t venture for outside sports may not understand this part properly.

On the same line what made Duryodhana an evil, was his intention. He intended to kill Bheema. And his intention wasn’t limited to Bheema. Bheema was the stepping stone for his bigger mission — Then I will overpower his younger brother and his elder brother Yudhishthira. I will tie them up and reign as the sovereign of the earth.

Now was Bhima’s action responsible for the fued. Definitely yes. Was it his cruelty? No. It was his capablity. He excelled over everyone else. Dhritrashtra’s son felt threatend of competition.

And if it was Bhima’s Karma that led to his poisoning, then it was same Karma that got Bhima’s strength and his ultimate victory. Duryodhana, too, paid for his karma. So even the Karma theory vindicates Bhima and pronounced Duryodhana as evil.
