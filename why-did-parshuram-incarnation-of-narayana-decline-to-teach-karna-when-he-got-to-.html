Why did Parshuram (incarnation of Narayana) decline to teach Karna when he got to know that he was a soot putra?

📅 May 27, 2021 12:06 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question is based on incorrect assumption.

Parashurama taught Karna everything he could. Now the question is why did he expell him?

WHY PARSHURAMA EXPELLED AND CENSURED KARNA?

He expelled him when he got to know the truth. He didn’t expel him because he was a Suta, but because he was a liar. This is explicitly mentioned by Parshurama —

However, he25 smiled and said, ‘You acted in this false way because of your greed for weapons. O stupid one! In a different place, when the time for your death has come, you will be engaged in a fight with someone who is your equal and the brahmastra will not manifest itself before you. The qualities of a brahmana will never remain with someone who is not a brahmana. Leave this place, since this is not meant for an untruthful one like you.

— Shanti Parva, BORI

Karna paid for his deceit and not for his lineage. And Karna too knew the reason why Parashurama censored him —

“In earlier times, when Rama found that I had obtained the brahmastra weapon from him under false pretences, he told me, ‘When your end has come, it will not appear before you. ’143

— Yaan Sandhi Parva, BORI

Same was emphasized by Bhishma later —

When he obtained weapons from the unblemished and illustrious Rama through deceit, proclaiming himself to be a brahmana, Vaikartana, the worst of men, destroyed his dharma and his austerities.”

— Yaan Sandhi Parva, BORI


DID PARSHURAMA EXPEL KARNA BECAUSE HE WAS A SUTA

That is one of the common misconception with a flawed assumption that Suta was a lower caste. Sanatana civilization doesn’t have the notion of caste, but Varna. It allowed son of a fisherwoman be recognized as the greatest scholar of the time (Ved Vyas), son of a maidservant as the Prime Minister (Vidura) and a Suta as King (Karna) or army general (Keechak).

Let us first understand who were Suta in the words of Karna himself —

Karna was frightened of being cursed. He sought his favours and said, ‘O Bhargava! Know me to be between a brahmana and a kshatriya, born as a suta.23 People on earth speak of me as Radheya

— Shanti Parva BORI

footnote 23: A suta was born from a kshatriya father and a brahmana mother.

Also note, Drona taught Karna knowning he was a Suta. Parshurama was himself a Brahmana who had acted as Kshatriya warrior all his life and had taught Kshatriya prince like Bhishma. So there was no reason to reject Karna for being Suta.


SUMMARY

  • Parshurama rejected Karna, because of his deceit and not because he was a Suta. This is explicitly mentioned by Parshurama, Karna and Bhishma.
  • Parshurama taught Kshatriya, Drona taguth Suta.
  • People born in so called low class families too had chance to reach hight and honorable positions and there are ample of evidence present in the epic.
