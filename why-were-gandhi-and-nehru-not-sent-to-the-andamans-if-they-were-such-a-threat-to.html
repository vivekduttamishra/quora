Why were Gandhi and Nehru not sent to the Andamans if they were such a threat to British rule?

📅 Mar 05, 2020 10:43 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Not everyone who was a threat to British were jailed in Andaman and if that would be the criteria for being nationalist, patriot or effective let's see a small list of people who won't make it to the list —

  1. Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Lala Lajpat Rai, Bipin Chand Pal, Gopal Krishna Gokhale
  2. Shubhas Chandra Bose
  3. Bhagat Singh and other HSRA members.
  4. And almost all of the national leaders of the time.

To sent Gandhiji to the Andaman was doubly difficult. Gandhiji's movements were non violent and not only had endorsement of Indian people (yes people those days followed Gandhi as there was no whatsapp or Facebook) or international political circle but even among British society — where kids were writing essay on him.

  1. By sending a non violent Mahatma to Andaman, British government would have made a mistake similar to Jalian Wala Bagh — attacking nonviolent movement with brute force — they continue to apologize after a century. More over international and Internal pressures were no less deterrent.
  2. Mahatma Gandhi was also a voice of conscience — his presence would ensure peaceful protest and a lot less bloodshed — which suited both Indians and British.

And incase you are wondering how effective Gandhi was (despite not going to Andaman) — The greatest genius of Gandhi's plan was while even peaceful protests were making its impact internationally, it was disarming British government from extreme actions.
