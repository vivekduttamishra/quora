If Kunti had revealed to Karna that he is her first child in the beginning, how would it have ended Mahabharat?

📅 Mar 24, 2020 02:52 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi,

This is one of the many what if questions that is flooding Quora and they must be appreciated as these questions give us, the storytellers and authors food for thought and a new tale can get spawned —

However, I believe little, if anything, would have changed in the entire process —

  • Karna's impact on the battle of Mahabharat was negligible :
    • He remained out of war for first 10 days
    • He managed to kill Abhimanyu and Ghatotkatch only.
    • He failed to save any Kauravas or Jaidradh
    • With or without him if war happened Pandavas would have won!
  • If Kunti had revealed Karna's identity would there have been no war?
    • Would Drupad Drona tussle averted? Would Drona have forgotten his humiliation or Drupad had forgotten is defeat?
    • Would about Shikandi and Bhisma?
    • What about Satyaki and Bhurisrawa?
    • What about sons of Jarasandha?

It would be a capital mistake to think that Mahabharat was just a battle between two brothers fighting for the Hastinapur's throne, a small deviation to the story could have changed the outcome greatly.

Let's analyse Karna further —

  • His greatest ambition was to be considered the greatest archer.
    • Would that change with Kunti's revelation? It didn't change on the revelation in the current time line.
    • How would he prove himself the greatest warrior without fighting?

Let us now see alternative time lines to understand the changes in Mahabharat story depending on when Kunti would have revealed Karna's identity.

  1. When Karna confronts Arjun in the passing out ceremony and that was the first time, she recognized his son. She could have revealed her identity.
    1. Parshuram had already cursed Karna by now.
    2. He already had a competition and jealousy against Arjuna from the Drona gurukul days.
    3. Karna was already in the camp of Duryodhana from the Guru Drona days. He was part of Bhima poisoning.
    4. So how do you think Karna had reacted to the news?
      1. Would he have not blamed his mother for not revealing her identity earlier?
      2. Would he have forgotten his urge proving himself best?
      3. Would Bhim had forgotten Karna being part of the conspiracy?
    5. Now continue to case 1+2 aftermath below.
  2. Kunti may have revealed her identity to Pandu (before the Birth of Pandavas)
    1. Pandu may have recognized Karna as his son.
    2. But Pandu may still have wanted more son’s. Remember he didn’t stop with Yudhishthir.
      1. So lets assume by the end of the story, Pandus would consist of six brothers (Karna+5).
    3. Karna would be the eldest Kuru and natural successor to the throne.
    4. He may not have the urge to prove himself the best warrior.
    5. He won’t be given the knowledge of Brabhmastra by Parshuram who wont teach a Kshatriya (with exception to Bhishma)
    6. Now continue to case 1+2 aftermath below.

Scenario 1+2 Continued (Kunti has revealed Karna who has been recognized eldest Pandava and heir to throne)

  1. Duryodhana still needed the throne. He would still be plotting against Pandavas.
  2. Now Six Pandava and Kunti need to escape the Lakshagrih.
  3. Karna may still have failed at Draupadi Swyamber (as mentioned by Neelakantha, Bori, Gita Press Footnotes and so on)
  4. Arjun may still have won the Swyamber.
  5. Now Karna may have ruled Indraprastha.
  6. Karna may never have faced the stigma of defeat from Arjun or blemish of killing Abhimanyu.
  7. Indra may never have demanded Karna’s Kavach Kundal — The word Danveer may never have been prefixed to Karna.
  8. Indra may never have given Shakti to Karna.
  9. Mahabharat war would still have happened.
  10. Now Ashwatthama may have replaced Karna in Duryodhana’s camp and may have become the commander-in-chief on the day 16 after the death of his father Drona.
  11. After the battle instead of Yudhisthira, Karna may have been the king. But the war would still have happened.
