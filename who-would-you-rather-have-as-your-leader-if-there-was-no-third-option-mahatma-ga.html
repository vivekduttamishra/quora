Who would you rather have as your leader if there was no third option, Mahatma Gandhi or B. R. Ambedkar?

📅 May 01, 2021 07:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Make no mistake — It’s Ambedkar and not Gandhi who leads contemporary politics in India. And I am saying this not because Ambedkar was the chairman of committee that drafted the constitution after the articles were passed by the constituent assembly of India that created our constitution. I say this, because our politics, today, revolves around that one provision of Indian constitution for which Ambedkar had become part of the constituent assembly.

There can be two kind of leaderships — All inclusive and Divisive. Gandhiji followed the former type and firmly believed — A system should take care of the welfare of every one and not just few. Here are few snippets of his belief system which was stashed away under carpet —

True democracy cannot be worked by twenty men sitting at the centre. It has to be worked from below by the people of every village.

"I understand democracy as something that gives the weak the same chance as the strong." - M K Gandhi

But India adopted neither. We followed a different divisive agenda, where few people would have more privilege than others. And todays politics revolves around who are going to be those privilege ones. Any leader who would dare to oppose risks losing their vote banks.

So make no mistake, Ambedkar led Indian politicians all these years and with each passing year his dominance has only increased.

But is current system sustainable?

I believe we have tried Ambedkar’s leadership for last half century and more and have failed. Miserably. It has not achieved the desired objective of its founding father — There is still a rich and poor divide, something that exists in all parts of human existence and not only in India.

The mechanism may have succeeded in denying the opportunity to those ‘general’ segment it meant to de-bar privilege's, it certainly hasn’t uplifted those whom it sought to favor. Instead it created a divide even there. Still few people get the benefit of those privilege called reservations and others keep languishing as before.

The only ones it has truly benefitted is the politicians who used it as a tool of mass illusion to ensure their vote banks.

So, the question whom would I have as my leader it would be without a blink —

MAHATMA GANDHI

Mahatma Gandhi followed a truly all inclusive politics — Welfare of all, Dignity for all.

And unlike others neither he had a vendetta politics nor unproven agenda. When he talked about the welfare of all, he had a blue print. He realized that every human can be empowered. He advocated empowering villages and individuals.

He advocated cottage industry. Many people laughed and considered him anti-industrialists. While people laughed in India here are a few example of cottage industry —

  • Facebook
  • WhatsApp

No wonder, Steve Jobs considered him his mascot for ‘think different’ campaign and the leader of largest capitalist country of the world considered him his ideal.

As I see today a Zomato delivery guy or an Uber driver who claim to earn a respectable earning of around 50–70K a month. And I see them proud of their achievements. They no more beg. They don’t depend on the mercy of some land lord. They don’t require much of a capital to start working. And, I see a Gandhian cottage industry in them.

I firmly believe reservations did no good to anyone, not even those, whom it claimed to have helped, it helped only a fraction, creating more poor in the process. I never resented it for personal reason despite knowing I would be excluded from many opportunity.

For me, there existed another world. A world untouched by those reservation and where the currency is your merit and hard-work. Yes, I am successful even after those reservation denied me the opportunity.

And the only way move forward, I see is Gandhian world — empowerment not reservation. Creating opportunity and remind people that each one of them have the capability to be an industry in themselves and distribution of power rather than it’s centralization.

Oh! Yes! I yarn for Gandhian leadership and his vision any day.

And let me tell you this — One day we all will seek Gandhian leadership — may not be today, may not be tomorrow. But one day.

That day people would find there is a world where everyone can live with respect and prosperity without depending on aids, when self respect would evolve in mutual respects and the spiteful government led agenda would collapse and country would leave the crutches that is stopping it from running — that day we would have Gandhi leading the nation.

And I am as sure of the fact that dawn of Gandhi’s leadership is drawing near as the fact that we have our politics led by Ambedkar.
