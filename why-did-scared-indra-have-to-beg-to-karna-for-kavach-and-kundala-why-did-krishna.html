Why did scared Indra have to beg to Karna for Kavach and Kundala? Why did Krishna have to save Arjun by sacrificing Ghatotkach? Was Krishna too scared for Arjun?

📅 Sep 23, 2020 09:22 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi Mayanaka, thanks for the A2A.

You have two questions. Let us take them one by one.

Why Indra took Kavacha from Karna?

Indra indeed, approached Karna to ask his Kavacha. And as per Surya —

O Karna! To ensure the welfare of the Pandavas, Shakra will approach you in the form of a brahmana, so that he can take away your earrings.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8277-8278).

It may be true, although Indra never said, why he asked for Kavacha? But let us go back to the original question. Can we call it beg or rob?

  1. Surya had already informed Karna about Indra’s arrival. Karna knew he was coming.
  2. Karna and Surya had formed a plan. And Karna explicitly asked for an exchange and refused to donate. Checkout the highlighted text.

O god! If I give you my earrings and armour, I will be liable to be killed. O Shakra! You will become an object of ridicule. O Shakra! Therefore, take my earrings and supreme armour, if you so wish. But take them in exchange. Otherwise, I will not give them.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8545-8546).

But the question is —

Why would Karna exchange his invincible Kavacha for a Indra’s weapon, when he had other weapons?

Well there are two parts of this answer —

  1. Karna’s Kavacha was not as invicincible as you are made to believe.
    Before Karna parted ways with his Kavacha, he had already fought and lost several battles. The important ones are —
    1. Draupadi’s swayamvara battle. Checkout my detailed post on this battle, where Karna scared away by comparing Arjuna to Rama, the personification of knowledge of weapons, Indra and Vishnu. He had his Kavach with him.
    2. Rajsuy Battle against Bhima. During Yudhishtir’s Rajsuy yagna, Bhima defeated Karna. He had is kavach with him.
    3. Gandharva Battle Karna fights Gandharva and when he could fight no more, fleds the scene leaving Duryodhana and other members of the group that included many royal women. He still had his Kavach with him.
  2. Karna, too, badly needed this astra and was pleased with the exchange. Let me share two references so that you understand the true nature of Indra-Vasavi Shakti exchange —
  • Karna was pleased with the exchange. This is the Karna’s words. Notice the hightlights

O Karna! Therefore, ask for what you want. With the exception of my vajra, you can ask for whatever else you wish.” At that, Karna happily approached Vasava. ‘Content in his mind, he asked for the invincible shakti. Karna said, “O Vasava! In exchange for my armour and earrings, give me the invincible shakti that kills large numbers of enemies on the field of battle.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8548-8550).

  • Why is Karna content and pleased here? Is he happy because he gets to donate and make Indra happy? or Is it because he is getting a wonderful weapon?
  • Why Karna needed this exchange? This, too, is answered by Karna. Check this out —

Karna replied, “I wish to kill only one enemy37 in a great battle. He roars and blazes and is the source of fear for me.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8554-8555).

Summary of Indra-Karna exchange

Here we can clearly see, while no doubt, Indra approached Karna for his Kavacha, rather than begging, it was an exchange — demaned by Karna. And Karna had a reason to be pleased with the exchange.

WAS KRISHNA SCARED FOR ARJUNA?

Yes. Krishna was scared for Arjuna. But wait before you jump to erroneous conclusion. Krishna wasn’t scared because of Karna. Krishna was scared.

  • Why was Krishna scared — Krishna was scared of weapons and shields that Karna had possessed and not by the skill or valour of Karna. He knew the potential of Vashvi Shakti. This is what Krishna himself said in this regard.

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain. Had Karna possessed the spear in his hand, no man in the world would have been able to stand before him.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6414-6416).

  • Check out whom is he praising. He wasn’t scared of Karna but the spear. He agains and again emphasizes the fact.

It is through good fortune that the invincible spear has been used up on Ghatotkacha.
…
Since the time he obtained it, Vrisha has always thought that you have been killed in the battle. O unblemished one! O tiger among men! However, though that spear has now gone, I swear to you truthfully that he is incapable of being killed by anyone other than you.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6425-6427).

  • watch out — once the Indra’s weapon is gone, Krishna has no doubt that Arjuna can kill Karna.
  • We must also note, a degree of exaggaration in Krishna’s statement here. A great teacher, that Krishna was, he was trying to motivate Arjuna to out perform himself. In that sequence he praises Karna’s Kavacha as well here —

Had he possessed the armour and had he possessed the earrings, the powerful Karna would have been able to defeat everyone in the three worlds, even the immortals. Vasava, Kubera, Varuna, lord of the waters, and Yama would not have ventured against Karna in an encounter. Had that bull among men possessed those, you with your Gandiva and I with my sudarshana chakra would not have had the capacity to defeat him in a battle.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6418-6421).

  • We must note that this statement is just an encouragement to Arjuna and not a factual as —
    • The Armour and Indra’s weapon were exchange. Karna couldn’t have possessed them together.
    • Karna had already been defeated on mulitple occassions by Arjuna, Bhima and Gandharvas.
    • He himself admitted that he is scared of Arjuna (reference is given above) even before he had given his kavacha.
  • In any case the glory here lies with the armor and spear, not with Karna.

DID KRISHNA GOT GHATOTKATCH KILLED TO PROTECT ARJUNA

This is not true. Here is what Krishna says in this regard —

And I thought of means to get Hidimba’s son killed by Karna’s spear. Had he not been killed by Karna’s spear in the great battle, I would have had to kill Ghatotkacha, Bhimasena’s son, in the future. I did not kill him earlier, because I wanted to ensure your pleasure. This rakshasa hated brahmanas and hated sacrifices. Because he caused the destruction of dharma and because he was evil in his soul, he would have had to be brought down. O unblemished one! I thought of a means through what had been given by Shakra.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6464-6468).

  • Note: Ghatotkatch was destined to die, with or without Indra’s Shakti, and irrespective of Karna-Arjuna war.

Another element very commonly believed is —

Indra’s spear wasn’t a great exchange for Karna’s Kavach as Karna had other weapons.

This is not true. And I will show you two references from BORI to prove that Indra’s spear was actually a boon to Karna. First, while we all talk about the condition that spear can be used only once, there was another pre-condition for using the spear. Here is what Indra said while giving Karna the spear —

  • The weapon can be used only when Karna had no other weapon to use

But if you unleash this invincible weapon in a fit of fury, when you possess other weapons, there is no doubt that it will descend on you.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 8561-8562).

  • And according to the pre-condition of the weapons use, Karna fired it on Ghatotkatch

His divine weapon had been destroyed by the maya. He began to think about what should be the appropriate course of action.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6370-6371).

‘“Karna made up his mind to use the spear. Unable to bear the assaults made in the battle, he was like an angry and intolerant lion. That supreme spear was always victorious and he decided to use it to kill him.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 6376-6377).

  • As it is clear, that Karna used the Indra’s weapon, when he had no option let.
  • Now imagine, what would have happened to Kaurava’s army without Indra’s weapon? There won’t be another day to left fighting.
