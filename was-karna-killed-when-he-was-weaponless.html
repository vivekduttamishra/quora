Was Karna killed when he was weaponless?

📅 Aug 04, 2022 01:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

No. Karna died fighting. I wonder why some so called fans of Karna want to snatch this glory of Karna. Rarely did he do something honorable and dying was certainly one of them.

Now let us break the incidents in several smaller facts so that we can prove the point beyond reasonable doubt.

1.Karna's chariot stucks in mud.

Call it destiny, call it justice, call it curse but Karna's chariot wheel got stuck making him immobile. And while it was a big tactical disadvantage, three things are undisputed —

  1. Warriors have fought valiantly even without chariot. While Sri Rama and Arjuna won from Ground, other warriors Abhimanyu, and Bhima fought with honour without begging. Agreed Karna can't be compared with those great warriors in terms of courage or valour.
  2. There was provisions for getting replacement chariot. Drona changed several on Day 14 when Bhinasena wrecked havoc on him. Now why didn't Karna apply the common sense beats me. But whatever the reason can't be an excuse.
  3. Most importantly, and important to this answer, his weapons were intact. They were not stuck in mud. So even if Karna choses to throw them away, we can’t call him weaponless. We can call it stupidity but not being weaponless.

2.Karna pleads for recesses which was declined by Krishna

Again the next two points I am mentioning is also undisputed. No controversy there.

  1. Karna reminds Arjuna that he is brave and virtuous and as such he should take a pause till Karna pulls out his chariot. Krishna declines his request reminding him of all his misdeeds including Abhimanyu’s death when he too was without a chariot.
  2. Karna feels ashamed at his past sins and returns back to battle fields. He fires Brahmastra and Varunastra.

3. Controversey: Was Karna weaponless at the exact moment of his death.

The controversy is raised based on a slightly elongated narrative in Neelkantha commentary (English translation by KMG). Let us first understand what gives rise to this controversey.

Neelkantha (KMG) narrates that after Krishna declined to pause the battle, Karna started fighting again and in one of his attack, Arjuna lost his control and his bow fell down. Karna took it as an opportunity to pull out his chariot wheel. Arjuna regained his composure and attacked Karna.

BORI CE doesn’t include this additional narrative. BORI doesn’t include those narratives that are present on one fewer scripts and doesn’t have cross reference in majority of the script. So deriving a conclusion based on a reference that doesn’t exists in BORI can never be conclusive.

But for the continuation of the debate, let us move along with that Neelkantha commentary that Karna’s fans want us to believe that he must have been weaponless as he was trying to pull out his chariot. Now here are the key element that we must understand.

  1. Keeping aside your weapon in amidst of battle is NOT same as being weaponless. You chose to leave your weapons aside in the battle field can be considered stupidity not haplessness.
  2. Arjuna lost his control momentarily. He didn’t plead Karna to stop. He didnt’ run away from the battle. His charioteer didnt’ drive him away. What made Karna decide that now he doesn’t need weapon can’t be explained. But as I said it falls in the category of a choised, even if stupid, decision to keep your weapon aside and not being weaponless. If you chose not to use weapons in battle field, you can’t blame others. Arjuna hadn’t paused the battle. If this is allowed Karna would never have taken a weapon in the hand. Everytime he feared his death, which is most of the time, he would simply have dropped his weapon.
  3. Divastras don’t need you to explicitly use your hands. They can be controlled by mantra, eys and thoughts. Maybe Karna didn’t learn it well. But he could have countered Arjuna even without using his hand. But then he couldn’t have countered it anyway.
