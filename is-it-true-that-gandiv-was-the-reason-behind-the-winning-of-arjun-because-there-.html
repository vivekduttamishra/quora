Is it true that Gandiv was the reason behind the winning of Arjun, because there is no war or fight he has fought without Gandiv?

📅 Aug 08, 2021 12:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question is a classic example of ignorance and prejudice, when when has read nothing and has a prejudice against someone — you invent question based on your false assumptions.

The questions basic premise is wrong. No warrior becomes the greatest warrior because of a bow or a shield. You don’t fool Mahadeva into believing that some one is the greatest warrior. Interestingly no one in the entire epic mentioned even once that Arjuna is a great warrior because of Gandiva. Not once. It is a great contrast to the statement of Surya, Krishna and Bhishma who made it clear that Karna is vulnerable without his divine ear-ring, shield and Vasvishakti.

Now let us see some of the victories of Arjuna without Gandiva.

1. Gurudakshina Battle

Gita Press, KMG and southern recitations gives vivid details of how Arjuna won the Gurudakshina battle and imprisoned details while Kauravas were routed. While BORI has the details stripped out it doesn’t fail to mention Arjuna won the battle for them. During this period, Arjuna had no Gandiva.

2. Swyamvara Battle

When Arjuna won the swyamver and other warriors failed, they decided to destroy Drupad and throw Draupadi back to fire. Arjuna came forward to protect the distressed family from all those wicked kings. There Arjuna faced a famed warrior. He had no Gandiva. Yet he uprooted the opponent so badly that he retreated scared comparing Arjuna with Parshurama, Vishnu, Indra and personification of art of war!

3. Khandava Robbers

Arjuna defeats Khandava robbers to protect Brahmins wealth with his ordinary weapons. He suffers an exile for the same.

4. Dwarka Robbers

Many years later when Krishna left for his divine abode Arjuna took the custody of distress women of Dwarka and started for Hastinapur. He was attacked by robbers. His Gandiva and other divine weapons failed him. But he continued to fight and pushed back the robbers who fled taking few women along with him. While some critics rejoice the incident as Arjuna’s failure, this arguably is one of the greatest feat of valor and contradiction to the question. A warrior isn’t brave because of the weapon but in spite of it.

A great warrior may even die in the line of his duty. We don’t call them failures. We cherish their bravery. A warrior isn’t one with strongest gun, but one who dares to fight when he is out of Ammunition.

Arjuna was every bit a warrior and a victor with and without Gandiva.
