How different would have been the result if Arjuna himself had went into the Chakravyuh instead of Abhimanyu?

📅 Sep 28, 2020 04:51 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

That’s an interesting question and searches for perceptions rather than proofs. But there isn’t much of guesswork here. Let us check out the chain of events —

  • Dronacharya, too, was worried about this question. That is why he waited for Arjuna to be safely out of range to form Chakravyuh. He knew Arjuna can destroy his Chakravyuh. So, I would go by Dronacharya’s assumption that the least that would have happened is the Chakravyuha would have been destroyed. There is no doubt about it. Drona had none.
  • With Arjuna in the front, Shiva’s boon to Jaidradh would have no effect, as Arjuna was an exclusion. So not only Arjuna, but other Pandavas too would have entered the Chakravyuha.
  • Abhimanyu was a strong warrior since the inception of the Kurukshetra so he would be one of the the warriors to enter Chakravyuha, not the lone warrior.
  • So now imagine what would have happened to that Chakravyuha that couldn’t contain one warrior. This time that one warrior would be there with the protection of his father. So the pack of hynas that who had managed to attack and kill Abhimanyu would have to answer Arjuna. Could they have done that? Let us check it out.
  • After Abhimanyu died, the very next day the entire pack stood to protected Jaidradh from the wrath of Arjuna. Did they succeed? Do you really think, those people would have managed Chakravyuha with Abhimanyu, Bhima and Arjuna? At least they never thought, they would.
  • After the death of Abhimanyu the joint forced couldn’t hold the front for five days, that is half the time, Bhishma had kept the war going.
