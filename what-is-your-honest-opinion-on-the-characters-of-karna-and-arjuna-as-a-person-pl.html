Unfortunately, no comment shall ever be perceived as honest when it comes to compare Arjuna and Karna. Even Krishna, Shiva, Bhishma and Drona have been labeled prejudice on this great platform called Quora. Yet, I would try to be fair in my assessment.

ARJUNA

Is protagonist of Mahabharata and has been stated as the trunk of a tree of righteousness. He is everyone's favorite — from Krishna to Shiva, Drona to Parshurama and Indra to Bhishma. And he must have done something wonderful to earn that place.

His great qualities —

  • His dedication and hard work. It was this quality that earned him his guru's grace in the first place. He was completely devoted to his guru. His hard work, and continuous practice made him the best archer of the time.
  • His eagerness to learn. This quality sets him miles ahead of everyone else. He never stopped learning. He learnt from not only Drona, but Chitrasen Gandharva and God of Gods Mahadeva. Not only did he learn, he also taught. And nothing makes you learn better than when you are teaching.
  • His focus and perseverance This is quintessential for a warrior. He would focus on the fight even if it is unwinnable. He knew fight with Shiva was unwinnable, yet he fought. It was his perseverance that made him victorious against large armies like Gandharvas.
  • His lack of personal ambitions Arjuna never asked for Brahmastra from Drona. He earned it. He earned Pashupatastra and could have used it for personal glory, but never used it. He never tried prove he was the best archer. He never desired a throne. He didn't ask for a war, not even Kurukshetra, and never fled one that came his way be it Draupadi’s Swayamvara, or Gandharva or Virata or at Indraloka. And none of them were to showcase his personal valour.
  • Empathy Most of his wars comes from empathy. He fought a war to protect Drupad after swyamvara when other kings tried to kill him. He had no reason to fight his friend and guru Chitrasen to protected those who always had ill-will for him and his family. Yet he fought to protect them and their women folks. Similarly he could have maintained his cover and not fight for Matasya. Yet he fought for them. And when it came to fight for his own interest, he asks Krishna to strive for peace. He decided to put aside his weapon.
  • A True Friend — Krishna-Arjuna friendship was eternal. Even dying Krishna knew Arjuna would come. And he did came. He tried to protect women folk of Dwarka — yadavas, Vrishnis — not just Krishna’s family but even of those who fought against him in Kurukshetra and aided Aswhatthama in cold blooded murder of his children. His celestial weapons failed him. His Gandiva failed him. But he continued to fight dwarka robbers and didn’t flee. Many women decided to walk away with the robbers (as per orginal scripts). And then tormented Arjuna continued to see through the rehabliation of those family. There are few greater example of what true friendship and empathy could look like. Giving away treasure is easy.

His Critics

  • He burnt down Khandava vana. This is a common critisizm by people who haven’t read the epic properly to realize that it was the will of Brahma and an act supported by Krishna. While BORI has a summary of the events, other texts like Neelkanta and KMG has the detailed which is not in contradiction to BORI and can’t be considered interpolation. Even BORI doesn’t condemn Arjuna’s act, rather glorifies it.
  • He remained mum during Draupadi’s molestation — I have my own views and wishful thinking here. Pandava’s should have itnervened and protected Draupadi. But I can leave with a hint, that no one has greater claim to resent than Draupadi. And Draupadi’s resentment was directed both against Pandavas and Kauravas but they were of different nature — complains and disappointement with Pandavas and hate and vengeance of Kaurvas. I won’t write a lot about it here, lest I spill the plot from my upcoming epic fiction — the sequel to The Accursed God.

KARNA

Karna has become one of the greatest hero of Mahabharata in our today’s era, with each story exaggarating his personna to such an extent that some call him — Mrityunjaya — A title given only to Shiva in our ancient text, or Daanveer, another title applied from modern times. There are now tales which can be truly called larger than life — one where Krishna becomes the carioteer of Karna.

I will limit my description of Karna from the outdated text, the one authored by Ved Vyasa.

Good

  • The eagerness to prove himself and seek the sky — Karna was a eager student. He got the opportunity to be trained under one of the greatest and best of the era — Dronacharya.But he wasn’t going to be satisfied by being just another student. He sought the position of being the best and as such always competed with the best student of the gurukul. He asked Dronacharya for Brahmastra, and once denied moved on to his next guru Parshurama. Thus he was blessed to be desciple of two of the greatest and best gurus of the time.
  • A Great Archer — Karna proved himself to be a great archer, in fact one of the best of the era. He earned a fame for himself and is often referred in the circle of great warriors at per with the likes of Shalya. So great was his valour that he became heros of several myths which hid his failures behind fables.
  • A Great donor — Karna has been referred as a great donor who often donated wealth to seeking Brahmin. It is said there is nothing that he won’t donate to a Brahmin during his sun worship. So renowned was he for his donation, that Indra himself decided to use this opportunity to deprive Karna of his Kavacha. Although what transpired between Karna and Indra doesn’t truly fall in the realm of donation.
  • Loyalty for Duryodhana — Karna in his own way, remained loyal to Duryodhana. They became friend in Drona’s gurukul days and remained so till death parted them. Duryodhana made him a king and he supported his benefector in all his endveour, most of them were evil conspiracies. Duryodhana trusted him so much that based on his calibre alone he decided to take on Pandava’s in the Kurukshetra battle and there are still many wishful thinking — what if Karna had abandoned Duryodhana. That was not to be. Karna had an opportunity to abandone Duryodhana and become the King of Aryavart, an opportunity provided by Krishna himself. But he refused.

Bad

  • Over Ambitious — This one trait clouds every other great traits of Karna. His ambition wasn’t for throne or prosperity, but recognition. He wanted to be deemed as the greatest archer. To be more specific, an archer greater than Arjuna. He lived and died for this one dream and none of his actions ever were completely unattached to this goal — weather it was to deceive Parshurama or an attempt to humilate them in dyutshabha or later in Vanaparva. He accepted throne from Duryodhana to fight Arjuna. He exchanged his Kavacha for a divine weapon to kill Arjuna, He made an exception to his promise for Kunti and rejected Krishna’s offer — all were the part of same one focus. A careful examination of Karna’s life would reveal that it actually orbitted around Ajruna making him the greatest fan of Arjuna.
  • Lack of courage and perseverance Probably the greatest weakness of Karna was lack of courage and perseverance. Karna was a great archer, but he wasn’t brave. He wasn’t able to hold himself in the battle agains adversary for a long enough time. And he fled several crucial battles. In fact he fled so many times that he had been mocked for this by other warriors on several occassion. He fled scared after fighting Arjuna soon after Draupadi’s swyamvara, he fled Gandharva battle, Virata battle, several times against Abhimanyu and the list goes on. In fact had he stayed put, he might have been able to repel Gandharvas. It was this lack of courage and perseverance that made him a mediocre warrior and a subject of redicule.
  • True Friendship — Again his fixition on his goal to be deemed as the greatest warrior made him defocus everything. Had he been able to let go that one ambition, he could have proved more valuable friend to Duryodhana. He could have stopped him from committing evil acts — thats what friends are supposed to do. He could have stopped the war. He could have handed the kindgom to Duryodhana.
  • Arrogant and Boastful — Again Vyas Bharat often refers Karna as arrogant and boastful. He has been referred by these names very often and his actions have proved it more clearly. It was this arrogance that made him remain absent from the first 10 days of Kurukshetra putting a question mark on his loyalty for Duryodhana.

Summary — Karna was born to be great but his arrogance, fixation of on single focused ambition and lack of courage and perseverance snatched the opportunity of achiving his true potential.
