Is it true that Karna's son Vrishasena beat Bhima and Nakula senseless in front of the eyes of Arjuna, who was just watching helplessly?

📅 Apr 04, 2021 11:06 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Vrishasena was a powerful warrior, but not powerful enough to take on Bhima. He indeed fought violently against Nakula and even jumped on Bhima and Arjuna was there and he looked on. But the rest of the question is a gross exaggeration. Here is a direct BORI reference —

He then struck him again between the breasts with sharp and yellow arrows and pierced him grievously. Madri’s son was tormented by Karna’s son. His horses were slain. He jumped onto Bhimasena’s chariot, like a lion leaping onto the summit of a mountain, while Dhananjaya looked on. ‘ “Nakula’s bow and arrows had been severed. He was without a chariot and afflicted by arrows. He had been mangled by the weapons of Karna’s son. On discerning this, the five foremost sons of Drupada, Shini’s descendant as the sixth, and the five sons of Drupada’s daughter, all destroyers of enemies, swiftly advanced against those on your side and devastated the elephants, chariots, men and horses.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4599-4604).

What we can clearly see

  • Nakula’s bow was severed and he was mangled by Vrishasena.
  • He jumped on Bhimasena’s chariot
  • Arjuna looked on.

What is not mentioned here is

  • Nakula was senseless
  • Vrishasena could win over Bhima
  • Arjuna looked helplessly.

Was Arjuna looking Helplessly?

Well you look at something as spectator and you look at it helplessly is two different things. All we see here is Arjuna doesn’t intervene. He doesn’t feel the need of intervention. As you can see Nakula is mangled but not senseless. Bhima was there and no one was in mortal danger. Arjuna didn’t interfere because he didn’t need to.

But could Arjuna have been helpless? Well, there is direct reference to to prove this here —

“Karna’s son pierced Shatanika with three swift arrows and Arjuna with three more. He pierced Bhima with three arrows, Nakula with seven and Janardana with twelve. On witnessing that superhuman deed, all the Kurus were delighted and applauded him. But they also knew about Dhananjaya’s valour and thought that he738 was like an oblation that had been poured into the fire.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4638-4640).

  • As Vrishasena turned towards Arjuna everyone knew what would be the outcome. They knew Vrishasena is about to turn into an oblation (religious offering) poured in fire.

What helpless looks like?

When Vrishasena was fighting his last battle, where was his father — the mighty Karna? Well, next reference from BORI clarifies that —

Kiriti, the slayer of enemy heroes, saw that the best of men739 was without his horses, which had been slain. In the battle, he attacked Vrishasena, who was stationed in front of the son of a suta. In that great battle, he descended, with thousands of arrows.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4641-4642). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

  • Oh! Karna was standing right behind his son as he fought his last battle and became an oblation in Arjuna’s fire.

Kiriti pierced him violently in the inner organs with ten arrows. With four razor-sharp arrows, he severed his bow, his arms and his head. He was struck by Partha’s arrows and fell down from the chariot onto the ground, deprived of his arms and head. He was like an extremely large and flowering shala tree, with a lot of leaves, which had been struck by a storm and brought down from the summit of a mountain. The son of a suta saw that his son had been struck by arrows and had fallen down from his chariot. He was tormented because his son had been slain. Powerfully and violently, he advanced on his chariot towards the ratha Kiriti.” ’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4647-4652).

  • As Karna stood right behind his son and saw him slain, he was tormented.

I wonder how helpless Karna must have been to have looked on andcouldn’t protect his son from the wrath of Arjuna, who had already promised Karna that he would kill his son in front of his own eyes. And Arjuna fulfilled his promise.

I see many answers where author claims that Arjuna’s challenge to Karna that he would kill his son has been removed from BORI. Apparently they haven’t read BORI properly to realize that it exists in to indirect verses. Arjuna had warned Karna of the outcome after Jaidradh’s death. Here is another BORI reference from Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva, where Arjuna said —

But you made Vrikodara hear manyharsh words. And when he was not in my sight, you killed Subhadra’s son. You will reap the fruits of those offences today. O evil-minded one! It was for your own destruction that you severed his21 bow then. O foolish one! You will be killed by me, with your servants, soldiers and mounts. Do everything that you wish to, because a great calamity confronts you. In your very sight, I will kill Vrishasena22 in this battle. If any of these other kings are confused in their intelligence and advance against me, I will kill all of them. I swear this truthfully, on my weapons. You are stupid and devoid of wisdom. But you pride yourself in this battle. On seeing you brought down, the wicked Duryodhana will lament grievously.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 4738-4743). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

BORI clearly states Arjuna’s oath on his weapons that he would kill the evil-minded Karna for Bhima’s insult and what he did to Abhimanyu. Karna had an advance notice of what Arjuna would do to his son and in front of his. Yet, Karna looked on and couldn’t protect his son. That is looking on helplessly.
