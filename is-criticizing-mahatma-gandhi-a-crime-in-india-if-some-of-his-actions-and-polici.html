Is criticizing Mahatma Gandhi a crime in India? If some of his actions and policies are criticized logically, then why do some members of intelligentsia take exception?

📅 Jan 28, 2021 10:49 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

NO. Criticizing anyone is not a crime, how much irrational it might be. People criticize even Rama and Krishna, and they too think they have a logic to do so. So what value does Gandhiji hold? Your criticism can’t destroy the legacy of Gandhiji worldwide, though it may hurt India’s image as ridiculing him who is universally regarded.

Then, there is a corollary to this question —

Is it a crime to take exceptions to Gandhji’s criticism, if someone feels he is unduly and illogically criticized based on fabricated or irrelevant data points?

Further, I must point out that this is a leading question. The seeker seems to believe that only they have a logical explanation to condemn Gandhiji and the so called members of intelligentsia are illogical to take exceptions! Now, How logical is this thinking itself?

The point about logic is, it is a double-edged sword, and often cherry pick the points to build up their cases, and what they certainly ignore is the —

  1. The time and context difference between 1914–1948 and 2000–2020. These are two different era. Their values are different, ecomonic situation is different, demography is different, economic situation is different. Does the logic incorporate all those differences?
  2. Doesn’t take into account that decisions taken then were based on the ground reality of that time and not current time.

Now coming to the so called logic often used to criticize Gandhi. Tell me how logical is this to say —

1.All the history books are biased in favour of Gandhiji?

Does this logic account for the fact, that —

  1. Indians of 1947 didn’t read those history books before following Gandhiji? Yet crores of Indians followed him. lacks of Indian homes mourned his death and performed his shradh as one of their own family member — a phenomenan without a parallel. Is it logical to assume they all were fools?
  2. World didn’t learn about Gandhiji via politically motivated books of history. It was an era of photograph, radio and videos. It was the era of connected world — connected enought to fight world war. The world was watching and making first hand impression, not reading book by Indian authors. How logical is it to ignore this entire context based on your own narrative?
  3. World leaders from all walks of life — from Albert Einstien to George Bernard Shaw, from Nelson Mandela to Dalali Lama and from Barak Obama to Steve Jobs — appreciates the greatest man of the century. How logical is it to consider all of them biased or ignorant fools?
  4. If all history book and narratives from India and global is biased, what is the data point anyone has to build their logic? Narratives over social media? How logical is it to condemnt all well recorded reference and value only social media narrative?

2.Gandhiji is overglorified for the political mileage of congress

  1. How logical is to imagine that Albert Einsitien and Dalai Lama would be motivated to work for political gains of congress which has already passed its expiry date!
  2. PM Nehru refused to give refuse to Dalali Lama. How logical is to imagine that Dalali Lama would work for their political gain?
  3. Nehru and Dr Rajendra Prasad didn’t always see each other eye to eye. How logical is to imagine that the first President of India was a biased man to have blindly followed and compared Gandhiji with Bhagirathi Ganga?
  4. How logical is to imagine Shubhash Chandra Bose to be a biased man to call Gandhiji as the Father of the Nation?
  5. Why would PM Modi be biased in favour of Gandhiji? Would it not be a political misadventure to glorify Gandhji, if glorifying Gandhji is a political miliage for Congress?

CONCLUSION

  1. Everyone is free to build their own perception. But it would be nicer if the rationale behind those perceptions and logic is authenticated information rather than fabricated/biased narratives propagating on social media.
  2. Not just you, everyone has the right to perception. So if you have right to criticize, others too have right to take exceptions.
  3. Are you sure you are limited to logical criticism and have not crossed the boundry — from criticism to hate and from logic to biased?
