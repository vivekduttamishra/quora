Did karna had any part in lakshagriha according to vyasa Mahabharatha?

📅 Feb 12, 2021 02:25 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Short Answer : YES. Karna was a part of the Varnavrat Conspiracy and it is clearly mentioned in BORI CE, apart from KMG, Neelkantha etc.

The explicit reference of Karna’s participation is mentioned in the Adi Parva —

When the Pandavas were not killed through all these means, open and hidden, since they were protected by fate and destiny, he16 consulted his advisers: Vrisha, 17 Duhshasana and the others. With Dhritarashtra’s consent, he had a house of lac built.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 2366-2368).

The same was again very explicitly confirmed by none other than Krishna himself on the 17th day of the war. Here is what Krishna said —

Karna is evil-minded and extremely injurious towards the Pandavas. O best of men! Kill him today with your sharp arrows. This is your supreme task and there is nothing wrong in it. We applaud this and there is no sin attached to it. O unblemished one! O Arjuna! Whether it was the attempt to burn down your mother with her sons in the night, 646 or whatever Suyodhana attempted towards you in the course of the gambling match, the evil-souled Karna was the root of all that. Suyodhana always thought that he would be saved by Karna and angrily tried to seize me too.647

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4025-4029).

There is not a single cross-reference, that suggests that Karna may not have been involved in the conspiracy.
