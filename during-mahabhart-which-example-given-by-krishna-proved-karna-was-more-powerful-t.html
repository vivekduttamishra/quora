During mahabhart which example given by Krishna proved karna was more powerful than Arjun?

📅 Mar 06, 2021 09:39 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Question like this has become redundant — leading question based on selective references to prove Karna was a powerful warrior. References are highly selective and often blown out of proportion.

I would put my answer in three parts —

  1. Starting with a question, to question the relevance of this question.
  2. A short summary of my views on Krishna’s views.
  3. A Detailed reference and cross reference from authentic version to prove my point.

Part 1 — IS IT REALLY IMPORTANT TO BE POWERFUL?

What would someone achieve by proving Karna was powerful?

Let us accept, he was powerful. Few want to belive, he was more powerful than Arjuna. Let me humor you for a while — let us say he was. So what?

Ravana was powerful. Hiranyakashyapa was powerful. Hitler was powerful. And yes, like them Karna was powerful.

But what did he do with the power bestowed on him?

  • Did he use his power to protect a woman’s dignity?
  • Did he rush towards enemies to stop them from captivating women of Hastinapur royal family?
  • Did he rush to protect a family being burnt alive?
  • Did he became a shield for a princess, whom Kings wanted to burn alive because, they had failed her swyamvara?
  • Did he killed some demon to save a village from losing their loved one
  • Did he used his power to prevent a devastating war?

No. He never used his power for the welfare of the society. He always abused the great gift bestowed on him.

A Lion is a powerful beast. But his power doesn’t make him a warrior, because he is just a beast. Power is not sufficient. How you use your power makes you great.


PART 2 — S U M M A R Y

Before I answer this question, let me re-emphasize what I have done so many times earlier — Krishna is no Yudhishthir. In his grand scheme of things, a trivial fact or useless truths have no place. His job was to be a great teacher, motivator, and a catalyst to establish Dharma. That is why you would find so many contradictions in what Krishna said.

  • Krishna on, occasion, has exaggerated Karna’s capacity, to motivate Arjuna to out perform himself. I am using the word exaggeration, because of the terms he used, that are known to be not true.
  • He said with Kavacha and Kundala, no one in three worlds would have defeated Karna. While he said so, he knew well —
    • Karna had fled scared from Arjuna after swyamvara battle.
    • He was badly routed by Gandharvas, where he fled, leaving his best friend behind.
    • On both these occasions Karna had his Kavacha with him.
  • He said that following Dharma; it was impossible to vanquish Bhishma, Drona etc. Now even Bhishma and Drona would blush at those comments as they vividly recalled so often that Arjuna alone routes entire Kaurava forces, including them in Matsya battle. Here he said this only to make Pandavas accept the war strategies that may appear out of war ethics.
  • Krishna himself presented a very contradictory picture on several occassions.
  • Even when he praised Karna, never once did he suggest a possiblility that Karna may vanquish Arjuna — not once.
  • He praised Arjuna’s valour over Karna more number of time with more certainty and without reservation.

So if we can’t take Krishna’s word for truth in case of Karna, should we accept them in favour of Arjuna? That would be a valid point. So here is what I propose.

Cross-references Krishna’s word against the words of others —

  • What Parshurama said?
  • What Dronacharaya and Ashwatthama said?
  • What Karna said?
  • What Mahadeva said?
  • What really happened. Every single time!

I am not asking you to distrust or discount Krishna’s words. But when you do a proper cross-reference you would understand, not only the words but its essence, not the truth or lack of it, but its wisdom.

  • Today, is another great emphasis. As if it is Arjuna’s will, do this job today because you can do it.

Notes on the selective reference

  • This praise isn’t of Karna but that of Vasvishakti and the divine Armour.
  • Question is what is Karna without those vasvi shakti and divine armour — like an angry snake, whoe energy has been sapped, or a fire whose flame has been pacified.
  • He is not the one whom no one can vanquish in three world.
  • I must remind you, even with kavach, Krishna’s praise of Karna was an exaggaration. Karna had already been vanquished on several occasion including that by Gandharvas when he had his armour and at Matasya while he had his vasvi shakti.

Now let us look at the bigger perspective Krishna talks about which is missing from this selective reference —

Because of Ghatotkacha, the spear has been used up. O Dhananjaya! Therefore, know that Karna has already been slain. Had Karna possessed the spear in his hand, no man in the world would have been able to stand before him.

Now time for cross reference that contradicts the core of this question. Krishna approaches Hastinapur court for his final attempt to avoid war. Here is what he said —

This entire army of kings assembled by you, with Bhishma, Drona, Karna, Kripa, Somadatta’s son Bhurishrava, Ashvatthama, Jayadratha—all of them are incapable of withstanding Dhananjaya. An angry Arjuna is incapable of being vanquished by the gods, the asuras, men and the gandharvas. Do not get fixated on battle. In this entire army of kings, is there a single man who can face Arjuna in the field of battle and return safely to his home? O bull among the Bharata lineage! What is the point of this destruction of men? Show me one man whose victory will amount to a victory for you. In Khandavaprastha, he defeated the gods, the gandharvas, the yakshas, the asuras and the serpents. Which man can fight with him? In the same way, a great wonder is heard about Virata’s city. A single one was enough to counter many. That was sufficient proof. Nevertheless, you wish to defeat in battle the invincible, unassailable and undecaying Jishnu, the brave Arjuna. With me as a second, who can challenge Partha when faced with him in battle? Not even Purandara himself! He can pluck out the earth with his arms. In his rage, he can burn up beings. He can topple the gods from heaven. Who will defeat Arjuna in battle? Look at your sons, your brothers, your kin and your relatives. O supreme among Bharatas! Let them not be destroyed because of what you do. Let the Kouravas survive.

~BORI CE UDYOG PARV CHAPTER 122
