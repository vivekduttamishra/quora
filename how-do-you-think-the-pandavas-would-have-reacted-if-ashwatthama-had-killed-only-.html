How do you think the Pandavas would have reacted if Ashwatthama had killed only Drishtadhyumna.Assuming the upapandavas are also saved, would the Pandavas be softer on him.?

📅 Apr 17, 2020 09:02 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question is based on supposition and most of the questions based on supposition can have many different answers. I believe this question has a definite answer.

The question expects certain degree of apathy from Pandavas when their son's weren't killed. However I think, there is a difference between apathy and softness. Softness implies compassion and conscience.

So here is my counter question —

Were Pandu not already soft beyond reasonable limit?

Consider this —

  • Did Pandavas really punished Ashwathama?
    • He was allowed to live.
    • Arjun had conscience to take back his Brahmastra.
    • It was Krishna who punished them.
  • Duryodhan poisoned Bhima. Did they punish him? No they continued to stay as family.
  • They were attempted to be killed in Lakshagrih. What did they do?
  • They all were humiliated in worst possible way, in the great gamble. For a much smaller humiliation Vibhishan had left Lanka. They were exiled. And yet they rescued Duryodhana from Gandharvas.
  • Even if all uppandavas were saved, Ashwathama was already party to killing Abhimanyu. But were they planning to retaliate?
  • I daresay, they would still be compassionate had a few Pandavas be among those slaughtered.

I often feel Pandava's sense of compassion often clouded their sense of justice.

Ashwathama was punished by Krishna, and not by Pandavas. And Krishna punished him not for who he killed, but how he killed. He was punished for his ghasty act!
