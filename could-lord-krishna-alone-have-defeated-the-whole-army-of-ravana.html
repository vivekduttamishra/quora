Could Lord Krishna alone have defeated the whole army of Ravana?

📅 Mar 22, 2020 09:26 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

In answer to a few similar questions, I have already mentioned that it would be a a capital mistake to differentiate Ram and Krishna as they are one.

Could Krishna alone have defeated Ravan and his whole army? Sure.

Would he have done it? I doubt it.

One of the foremost reasons of incarnations was to give us a lesson. Lesson for the ordinary people. What lesson would it be that I won because I am an incarnation?

Why Krishna wouldn't have defeated Ravan in a day?

  1. He could have done it in Kurukshetra but he chose to sit out. He wanted to show that those who don't take up arms are still important part of the war.
  2. He could have finished off Jarasandh in a day but instead he shifted capital to Dwarka.
  3. He wasn't trigger happy like someone with a nuclear power. He always used as much as needed and most important he knew the difference between what he can and what he should.
