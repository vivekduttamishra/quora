“No one is as generous as Karna.”Do you agree with the statement? Support your answer with reasons.

📅 Jun 06, 2021 10:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Let me share the story of a few people from our epics —

  1. Sage Dadhichi — He donated his bones so that it can be used for casting into Vajra for Indra. And in doing so surprisingly, he died. He didn’t ask anything in exchange for his generosity for the welfare of humanity. Surely he couldn’t be as generous as Karna. Right?
  2. King Sivi — He offered his own flesh to an eagle. In exchange,, he saved the life of a poor pigeon who had sought his refuge. Is it a similar exchange that Karna asked from Indra for his Kavacha? What do you think?
  3. Jatayu — Died tried to protect an unknown woman in distress. He knew he is fighting a powerful evil. He died but didn’t flee. Can we compare his generosity with Karna, who fled leaving behind his best friend and their family?
  4. King Bhagiratha — Did extreme penance to ensure Ganga manifests on Earth for the welfare of humanity. Surely that couldn’t be compared with the generosity of Karna, who refused Krishna’s plea to prevent a disastrous war.
  5. Lakshmana — He devoted his entire life to his brother, fighting his battles. He nearly died for his brother’s cause. Surely he can’t be compared to his Karna who fled while Arjuna and Abhimanyu slaughtered his two brothers in front of his eyes.

Karna was so generous that —

  • He couldn’t forgive his mother for abandoning him despite knowing the her reasons. Even if we consider Kunti wrong, does that make Karna generous? Is generosity a trade?
  • He couldn’t bring himself to forgo his feud with Arjuna, knowing that he is his younger brother. He was so generous that he couldn’t let go his ambition.
  • That he didn’t fight first ten days of war because someone hurt his ego. His ego was greater than his friendship and loyalty.

While it is understandable that few people may idolize someone. It still doesn’t give them the right to ridicule the entire epic culture trash the contributions of great souls who led down their life for a selfless reason for the welfare of the society.
