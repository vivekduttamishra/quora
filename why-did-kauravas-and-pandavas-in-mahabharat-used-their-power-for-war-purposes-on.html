Why did Kauravas and Pandavas in Mahabharat used their power for war purposes only and not for domestic purposes like traveling, changing the room temperature, manifest meals, etc.?

📅 Mar 05, 2021 08:49 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Wars are not evil. When greedy and arrogant peple go to war for their selfish design and to torment others, it becomes evil; When heros rise to crush the evils, it becomes yagna. Not crushing the evil is a dis-service to the society

—Vivek Dutta Mishra

It would be wrong to say Pandavas used their power only for the purpose of wars and not for domestic purpose. In fact, majority of their life they controlled their powers and constrained themselves to avoid the war — smaller battle, they were often the tools to bring order to chaos.

  • Pandavas could have confronted Kauravas after lakshagrih, but they didn’t.
  • They killed demons like Bakasura. That was no war. That was for the society — bring justice and protecting an entire village who was under constant torment.
  • Arjuna and Bhima subdued the reckless and arrogant kings after Draupadi swayamvara, who were hell-bent on a massacarre to cover their failure. They had resolved to burn Draupadi alive and kill Drupada and his sons. That was protecting society.
  • Pandavas established Indraprastha out of a inahbitable land — dawn of a civilization. What greater domestic purpose, one can expect? They at the behest of supreme creator Brahma destroyed Khandava — the land of the enemies of God.
  • Pandavas saved hapless women of Duryodhana’s camp from getting imprisioned by Gandharvas. And that was another idea of domestic purpose.

Coming to Kauravas, their character, their blind ambition and their evil acts are the evidence and testimony to the fact that the wars are not evil. Not crushing such evils are dis-service to the society.

Coming to the last part

WHY DIDN’T THEY USE THEIR POWER FOR TRAVELLING, CONTROLLING TEMPRETURE AND MANIFESTING FOOD.

Probably we expect them to deploy some si-fi elements. There are few points that we must consider here.

First, these belong to the domains of scientists and not warriors. They were warriors. Their job was to establish the rule of Dharma.

Second, any invention and innovation is driven by the needs of that period and not our period —

  • There was a lot more greenery and naturally temperature regulated climate during that period. Your houses were surrounded by trees, and gardens unlike the cement and concrete jungle we currently live in.
  • Foods were abundant in supply. Population was much lower. There isn’t a mention of a starving person. People were consuming nature manifested ready-to-eat-fast-foods like fruits and milks.
  • They had more climate friendly mode of transportation that didn’t emit carbon-mono oxide, keeping their temperatures even better controlled.

Hope that answers your question.
