Who is the real impotent villain in the game of dice? Duryodhan or Dharm

📅 Jun 13, 2021 11:42 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

An Interesting question after a long time.

My answer would be neither. Let us understand why.

Duryodhana

He was a crooked, greedy person who wanted to snatch away the prosperity of Pandavas by whatever means possible. He had earlier tried many conspiracies of murder, all of which had failed. By his standard, it was a minor offence.

Second, we must understand, Duryodhana wanted to usurp Pandavas’ prosperity, but it wasn’t his original plan. It was Shakuni’s plan. It was he who vanquished Pandavas by his deceit and their foolishness. So was it Shakuni? No, Shakuni played the game under set and accepted norms, even if through deceit. Had this incident ended with Pandavas losing their kingdom, wealth and honor, Shakuni would have been hailed a hero who won a bloodless battle. But story doesn’t end here…

Now, Duryodhana pressed his luck harder and decided to humiliate Pandavas further by dragging Draupadi to the courtroom. His mentions like Karna made the situation worse and pushed it to a path of no return. So the question is was it Duryodhana alone to be blamed?

Yudhishtira

But then there is another aspect to this question. Were Kauravas alone to be blamed? Weren’t Pandavas equal partner in crime? Didn’t Yudhishthira bait his brothers, himself, and finally Draupadi?

One of the important idea that we must understand is, action alone can’t be good or bad — intentions make them good or bad. Think this way, an army soldier and murderer, both do the same thing — kill people. Is killing good or bad? Intention is the debarking line.

Yudhishthira played with his hand because he thought he was bound by Dharma! He can't refuse. He played without deploying deceit. He didn’t device any conspiracy. He was trapped by what he thought was his Dharma!

Dharma

So, was Dharma the main villain as the question seeks to understand?

The fact that this question even came to mind suggests the swallow understanding of Dharma! And no wonder, such confusions are likely because of a flawed understanding of the word.

First we must understand, the word Dharma has not exactly equivalent in English language and the accepted term religion is not even a remote match to its true meaning. We can loosely equate Dharma to duty or justice. But with detailed analysis, it is a philosophy that can be better explained by following Sutra —

ॐ सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः
सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः।
सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु मा कश्चिद्दुःखभाग्भवेत।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः॥

That makes Dharma an abstract entity like justice. And you need some ground rules to implement them. They are like law, or protocol, or traditions. Unfortunately, over a long period these traditions impersonate Dharma and even experts fail to realize the dividing line. This is exactly what happened during the dice game.

Even the great teachers, scholarly experts who sat in the court-room that day failed to realize the difference of Dharma and tradition.

And I think, that was the greatest reason for the mishap that happened.
