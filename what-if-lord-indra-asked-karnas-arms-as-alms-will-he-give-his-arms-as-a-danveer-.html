What if Lord Indra asked Karna’s arms as alms? Will he give his arms? As a danveer he would right?

📅 Apr 01, 2022 10:34 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

SHORT ANSWER : NO

Karna had declined to give even his Kavacha and Kundala unless he got a favorable bargain. Danveers don’t seek exchange for their offerings. But Karna sought for exchange. He reserved the choice if he would give Kavacha or Kundala or not.

So could Karna have given his arms for a favorable exchange? Again the answer would be NO. If you check out the character of Karna you would notice to clear traits —

  1. Self Preservation: It always ranked highest for Karna. Nothing ever toppled his. Not the danger on the life of his friend, family, brothers. He always escaped leaving them to face the mortal danger. Duryodhana was captured and his two brothers were killed by Arjuna and Abhimanyu. But in all these cases Karna simply fled. Karna was the only warrior on Kurukshetra who begged for a recesses on a flimsy ground that his chariot has been immobilized despite the facts that he still had weapons to fight. How someone so attached to his life would have given his arms?
  2. Self Preservation: Indra sought his Kavacha. He gladly offers it in exchange for a weapon. But then He sought something else too. That his body shouldn’t look ugly and that Indra should bring it back to a perfect look. See was more worried about his look that losing the divine protection. Do you think such a person would have given his arms? Are we talking about Sivi and Dadhichi? NO. We are talking about Karna here.

But, most important. Indra would never have asked for his arms. They were never great or a matter of concern. They were connected to limb controlled by a fickle mind and a weak heart. He knew, his heart would give up, his mind would manifest fear and his legs would run for cover. What can a hand do in such a case?
