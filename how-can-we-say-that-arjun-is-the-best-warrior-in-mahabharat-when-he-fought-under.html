How can we say that Arjun is the best warrior in Mahabharat when he fought under the impregnable protection of Bhagwan Sri Krishna and Bhagwan Sri Hanuman while Karna was made defenseless by stripping off his Impregnable Kavach and Kundal?

📅 Mar 28, 2020 02:28 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

In question lies the answer! There are a couple of different ways to answer the question. Lets see

  1. Let’s rephrase the question and you will get the answer — Why Bhagwan Sri Krishna, Sri Hanuman and Mahadev Shiva extended their protection to Arjuna alone?
    Naturally, Adiguru Shiva, Gyan-gun-sagar Hanuman and Lord Krishna wont extend their protection to an unworthy person. They didn’t go to extend the protection to Shakuni or to Duryodhan (or to Karna). Shouldn’t that prove beyond any reasonable doubt that Arjuna was the best warrior?

    Now lets rephrase the secon part of the question — Why Indra stripped Karna from impregnable Kavach and Kundala?
    Was it just because he was Arjuna’s father (popular theory)? Are we not forgetting Indra being a Vedic God? Ok, lets extend this question further — Why no gods, including Surya (his father), extended any protection to Karna during the war?

    Is there a lesson here? Is there an answer to this question hidden here? What lesson would Surya have presented had he sided with an evil warrior? Remeber Surya is also a Vedic God.
  2. Was Arjuna always under protection of Krishna, Hanuman and Mahadev?
    1. Arjuna defeated Karna in Draupadi Swaymber. Karna had his Kavach and Kundal and no one was helping Arjuna. Yet Karna questions are you Great Vishnu or Ram to fight with such a power?
    2. Arjuna defeats all Gandharvas that had made Karna (still with his Kavach and Kundal) fled. Arjuna wasn’t under the protection of any God.
    3. Virata war. Arjuna was alone. No Krishna was around and he defeated Karna twice and he fled twice. I have seen few argue that Arjuna's flag had Hanuman on it. They quote it out of context as Hanuman was a symbol on the flag.All it mentions is the great monkey was making a high noise. While we should see it as a mere metaphor (likes things going south, doesn’t mean someone is going south.) for the flag was waving in such a way as if Hanuman himself was present. It doesn’t mention even a single occasion where Hanuman either fired a weapon or deflected one fired by Arjun.
    4. Similarly neither Hanuman nor Shiva anyway interfere in Mahabharat war other than endorsing the fact that Arjuna is siding with Dharma.

I hope this dispels the confusion about the best warrior.
