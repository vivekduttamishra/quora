Why did Bori remove the prayer of Draupadi to Krishna during Vastraharan?

📅 Nov 27, 2020 06:39 AM PST  |  🌐 English

You need to understand the process applied by BORI to bring out the critical edition. They used two prime techniques to decide the inclusions and exclusion —

1. Consistency in different variants.

BORI compared 1259 variants and checked if an incident in present in most of those scripts. Those that were present in larger number of scriptures were included, those appearing only in handful of manuscript were removed as interpolation.

Take an example, Draupadi stopped Karna from participating in swyamavara is present in only 5 out of 1259 available manuscript. That is less than 1% of manuscript. Hence BORI rejected it.

2. Cross-reference technique

An incident that is authentic is likely to be referred again at different point of time by virtue of recitation by different people or reminding such incidents in different context.

Example1. Draupadi’s molestation in the dyutbhawan is not only present in larger number of manuscripts, but also cross referred as dozen of places by different people including Krishna, Kunti, Pandavas, and Draupadi herself.

Example2. Gandharva battle, laksha grih, Virata battle have been referred at number of places with same narratives. This has been incarporated in BORI.

Example3. Dronacharya taught Karna has been referred in three different Parvas of Mahabharata and is present in all major manuscript.

Example4. Draupadi rejecting Karna not only is rejected because not just because it is present only in 5 of 1259 variation, it’s contradcition exists in not only other versions but even in some of those 5 versions. Most versions mentions that Karna failed at Draupadi Swyamvara. Now Neelkantha edition, which is one of those five manuscript that suggested Draupadi rejected Karna, also includes Karna failed at Draupadi’s swyamavara. Such inconsistencies are weeded out.

Now let us come to the main question —

Why did Bori remove the prayer of Draupadi to Krishna during Vastraharan?

This incident is present in almost every ancient scriptures and it doesn’t fail on point 1. Apparently Krishna intervening himself is not cross-referenced. Also they removed it mostly because of point 3. It sounded untrue. There is another reason why BORI may have removed it. When Draupadi confronts Krishna, why he didn’t save her, he didn’t say, I saved you. The fact that Krishna saved Drauapadi is present in most of the versions of Mahabharata, although removed by BORI, has been acknowledged in the footnotes —

The critical edition excises some shlokas that exist in regional versions and in popular renderings of the story. Droupadi prayed to Krishna and he ensured that Droupadi wasn’t stripped.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 7608-7609).

So, this is clear, BORI didn’t remove this incident for lack of available reference. Also note BORI has just removed Draupadi’s prayer and NOT the consequences.

BORI didn’t really remove the episode

There overwhelming evidence of Druapadi’s humilation and molestation in dyutshaba in the hands of Dussassana and Karna. There is a lot of cross-reference to prove Druapdi’s molestation attempt took place. And the disrobe attempt also has been included in BORI. It only doesn’t explicitly names Krishna —

O king! Then Duhshasana forcibly tugged at Droupadi’s garments. In front of everyone in the sabha, he forcibly pulled. O lord of the earth! As Droupadi’s garment was being tugged away, another similar garment appeared every time.214 At this, a terrible uproar arose. All the assembled kings witnessed the most extraordinary sight in the worlds and approved.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3380-3382).

Note:

  • At the behest of Karna, Dussassana didn’t try to strip Draupadi.
  • And people saw the most extraordinary sight — As Draupadi’s garment was tugged away, another appeared everytime.

So now the only question is who ensured, Draupadi isn’t disrobed? No human, present there did it. Right? You may name this force as God, Dharma, Divine intervention. And according to Vyas Mahabharata, Krishna was an incarnate to establish Dharma. Krishna manifests its Virata Swaroop twice dispelling any doubt that Krishna is the God Incarnate and Dharma. So it doesn’t matter if Krishna’s name is explicitly mentioned here or not. It doesn’t matter if you believe he was Krishna or someone else. It is sufficient to realize, evils were crushed by a divinity, and Krishna was the living divinity of the era.

BORI didn’t remove the disrobing episode

Many people try to mis-interpret BORI by suggesting that BORI removed the disrobing incidents altogether. The above references, clearly indicate that BORI has incorporated the entire episode include Dussassana’s attempt to disrobe Draupadi and Draupadi was protected by extending her garments. The only portion actually removed is the Prayer of Draupadi and explicit name of Krishna as savior.
