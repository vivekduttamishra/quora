Can anyone point out the full list of wrong things done by Yudhishthira?

📅 Jun 19, 2020 05:03 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

I must admit, this is a nice recent addition to Quora. A full list of Yudhishthir's wrong doing. Honestly few years back I could have counted a dozen, today I can list only two mistakes — not wrong doing.

When I set down to write my novel few years ago, I had deciced highlight Yudhisthir’s follies and rob him off the mask of Dharmatma. But that was, as I said, several years ago.

First, we must understand wrong doing is something which is acknowledged wrong and has a malice intention associated with it. Mistakes don’t have malice intentions associated with them.

Before going to talk about that one fault, let me explained what changed in the last few years that reduced dozens of wrongdoing to two mistakes (not wrong doing) —

  • I must say, I became a lot less foolish than I was years ago.
  • I realized, I can use contemporary yardstick to an event that dates back over 5000 years.
  • It is easy to analyse an event retrospectively when we know the outcome, rather than when we need to decide the future course of action.

Ok, having expressed the foundation of my belief, let us move to the list —

Full list of mistakes done by Yudhishthir.

  1. Yudhishthir failed to differentiate between Dharma and Parampara.
  2. His lack of trust in the capability of Arjuna, something later had proved on every single occasion.

We must understand that Dharma, while being a timeless moral code of duty and justice, is still an abstract concept and its actual interpretation requires a wisdom is beyond everyone's reach. That is why we boil it down to a list of do’s and donts — similar to law or protocol. Over a period of time these laws and protocols tend to impersonate justice or Dharma.

We realize our mistake only when some mishap of mighty proportion hit us on our face. Unfortunately, at that point it becomes too late to take immediate corrective measure. Take the example of Nirbhaya case. We realized juvenile law has become an obstruction to justice. But could we change and apply the law against the killers of Nirbhaya?

Law can’t be applied retrospectively; criticism can!

During Mahabharat period, there were no supreme court to act as arbitrator among Kings. To manage the convention, there were protocols, conventions which each King was supposed to accept and follow. Yudhishtir too followed the protocol by accepting the invitation for dice game. He must have realized the deceit from Shakuni, yet could do nothing. Because like most of us, he had accepted Parampara (Protocol) for Dharma. He shouldn’t have gambled, and surely not gambled his kingdom or family. None of us would. But then, as I said, I live in 2020, not in 5000BC, where the words of King was the law?

We must realize that no one could answer the question related to the morality of the dice game to our satisfaction — not even Vidur! So, yes, Yudhishthir made a mistake in dice game. He was confused between Dharma and Parampara — He should have abandoned the game with clear explanation.

Other wrongdoings of Yudhistir, where I exonerate him now.

  1. Yudhistir’s role in creating the rift between Kauravas and Pandavas as a kid — I have seen Yudhishthir being blamed for
    1. Not putting Bhim in the check as he bullied Kauravas and that may be the initial reason of conflict.
    2. Yudhistir didn’t let Bhim tell everyone that Duryodhana poisoned him.
      I believe these two accusations cancel each other out. I also believe you If there has to be some bully, it must be one with Dharma on his side. And we may blame Yudhishthir for being soft of Kaurava which made them even more daring. But then, when we see, it an attempt to mellow down the situation, I am not sure If I can blame Yudhistir. Why else, India’s reaction to sponsored terrorism and nuxal often appears soft?
  2. Yudhishthir allowed Draupadi’s marriage to 5 Husbands.
    1. This is the most clear exemplification of how we become a captive of Parampara. We never question to polygamy of Krishna, Arjuna, Pandu or King Dasharatha. We don’t call it wrong doing. In case of Polyandry we question morality! This is mental conditioning which doesn’t follow the rationale of justice or Dharma.
    2. Since polyandry is an English word, it is apparent that such a culture must have existed beyond Indian and ancient times as well. Just it's not as prevalent.
    3. Krishna and Ved Vyas endorsed Pandavas endorsed Draupadi’s marriage to five Pandavas and is described as pre-sanctioned by Lord Shiva. I would say blaming Yudhishthir for this would be way above his pay-grade.
  3. Yudhishthir didn’t punish the culprit who conducted the genocide by burning Khandava Vana.
    1. This is another proof of our contemporary thinking. Earth of 2020 is a victim of deforestation and massive abuse by echo system. A story of burning wildlife and an entire forest appears brutal.
    2. But is there a single shloka in Mahabharat that condemns this act of Arjuna? Why is this incident narrated as a glorification and not to condemn? Mahabharat mentions it is clearly that Lord Brahma willed it, Lord Agni requested it and Krishna, too endorsed it.
    3. I would again say, It would be above the pay grade of Yudhishthir to be blamed for decisions taken by Brahma, Agni and endorsed by Krishna.
    4. Take contemporary examples.
      1. We create medicines that kills colonies of bacteria and virus. Bacteria is a living organism. We don’t send a scientist to jail for killing them. — we hail them as great. Why no one fights for the rights of those poor bacteria?
      2. As soon as we get a hint of bird flu, thousands of birds are killed. Why no environmentalist condemns someone to jail?
  4. Yudhishthir abused Draupadi in the Virat court when she complains against Keechak.
    1. This may be considered as one of the greatest strategy of Yudhishthir and also his first undocumented lie.
    2. Yudhishthir knew King of Virat couldn’t punish Keechak. He also knew he would be punished.
    3. There was no point in divulging the secret in advance.
  5. Yudhishthir enumerates mistakes of his brother and Draupadi as they melt down on their way to Himalaya.
    1. What Yudhishthir said was his opinion. His opinions may be wrong. This doesn’t make him wrong. If we censor or judge Yudhishthir for voicing his opinion, what right do we have to judge him here?
    2. Yudhistir’s opinion may be his way to find a rationale for loosing his loved ones. He may have been trying to find a reason to console himself as to why he had to lose them.

This is not my attempt to show Yudhishthir guiltless, but an attempt to find the rationale of why our great epic called him Dharmatma despite of several questionable action. I can’t mistrust epic, so naturally there is an alternate explanation.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
