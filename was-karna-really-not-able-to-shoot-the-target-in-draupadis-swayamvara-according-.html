Was Karna really not able to shoot the target in Draupadi's swayamvara according to the authentic version?

The reason Karna couldn’t shoot the target was he couldn’t string the bow. This has been clearly narrated in most authentic and popular versions.

BORI References

यत्कर्णशल्यप्रमुखैः पार्थिवैर्लोकविश्रुतैः |
नानतं बलवद्भिर्हि धनुर्वेदपरायणैः ||४||

— shloka 4, chapter 179, Adi Parva

other, “O Brahmanas! If Kshatriyas like Karna and Shalya, who are famous in the world, have great strength and are well versed in Dhanur Veda,22 could not string the bow, how can this weakling Brahmana, with no knowledge of weapons, succeed?

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 6585-6587).

Unfortunately few people try to mis-interpret the above text stating that here they have mentioned Karna as Kshatriya, so it can’t be the same Karna. This however is a misinterpretation due to the translation and I have discussed it in detail in the above article with reference to statements of chief editor of BORI, Mr Vishnu Suthankar. But a short summary is —

  1. Bibek Deboray translated the word पार्थिवै as Kshatriya here, however the word has a different meaning aswell and that is Kings which is how he translated it in another shloka 34 of chapter 176. below

इदं धनुर्लक्ष्यमिमे च बाणाः; शृण्वन्तु मे पार्थिवाः सर्व एव |
यन्त्रच्छिद्रेणाभ्यतिक्रम्य लक्ष्यं; समर्पयध्वं खगमैर्दशार्धैः ||३४||
—Shloka 34 of chapter 176, Adi Parva (BORI)

O assembled kings! Hear that this is the bow, that is the target and these are the arrows. With these five arrows, shoot the target through the hole in the centre of the machine

2. Those who claim that the Karna mentioned in the above shloka is some other Karna fail to realize —

  • Dristdumna named several sons of Dhritrashtra and other invited guests from different Kingdom by their names, and No other Karna was invited.
  • There is no other Karna mentioned in entire Mahabaharata who is referred as a great warrior to be explicitly named along with Shalya and above Duryodhana and others.

BORI and Gita Press (Neelkantha Edition)

If you see the use of word पार्थिवै in the original shloka it is not done for Karna and Shalya but with reference to other kings. This is also as per the Gita Press version of Mahabharata

KMG Reference

And that bow which Rukma, Sunitha, Vakra, Radha's son, Duryodhana, Salya, and many other kings accomplished in the science and practice of arms, could not even with great exertion, string, Arjuna, the son of Indra, that foremost of all persons endued with energy and like unto the younger brother of Indra (Vishnu) in might, strung in the twinkling of an eye. And taking up the five arrows he shot the mark and caused it to fall down on the ground through the hole in the machine above which it had been placed.

—Adi Parva: Swayamvara Parva: Section CLXL

All these references from various source comprehensively explains why Karna failed to hit the target.
