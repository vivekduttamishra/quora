Is it true that Arjuna Nivatakavacas Fight is an interpolation? Because Arjuna who defeated by foot soldiers how could he defeat powerful Nivatakavacas?

📅 Jan 21, 2021 04:40 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Great attempt to throw some more dirt on our epic. And the trending word is interpolation. Do you understand the meaning of interpolation?

  • Who decides interpolation?
  • What is the basis of weeding out interpolation?
  • Can we consider an incident mentioned in BORI CE, as interpolation based on fake narratives and perceptions?

A.What is the basis of considering an incident authentic or interpolation?

There are fundamentally three techniques applied to identify if a particular incident is an interpolation or not and the techiques are complementary not mutually exclusive

  1. How many ancient scriptures mention this incident.

The idea is an interpolated incident will have fewer ancient references. Take and example of Draupadi Swyamvara and the narrative that

Case Study 1 — Druapadi denied Karna a chance to particpate

This incident is mentioned only in 5 our of 1259 referred manuscript, excluding the most ancient Nepali, entire Bengali edition, southern edition and sharda edition. This incident is, however, present in Neelkantha edition which is the basis of first English translation by KMG. We would discuss Neelkantha edition and KMG in our next section.

Case Study 2 — Arjuna defeated Nivatkavacha

This incident is present in almost all the editions of ancient Mahabharata and that is the one of the reasons, BORI CE included this incident.

2. Cross References

Most of the interpolation fails cross-reference text. The idea is if you insert and incident to the epic, it may not have any cross-reference. A cross-reference generally appears as a discussion about the incident by different people and different point in time. Cross-references prove that a particular incident is internsic part of the narrative. On the other hand if there a cross-reference contradicting the narrative, it proves that such fake narratives are inserted in the epic.

Case Study 1 — Druapadi denied Karna a chance to particpate in swayamvara

There is absolutely zero cross reference of this incident in the entire epic. No one ever talked about this incident anywhere. Neither Karna nor anyone else ever mentioned the injustice or insult Draupadi caused to him.

Neelkantha edition, which includes Drauapdi’s denial to Karna also includes a contradiciton of the incident by clearly stating that Karna failed to string the bow. Now if he failed, he couldn’t have been denied a participation. And the contraction proves the incident to be an interpolation.

Case Study 2 — Arjuna defeated Nivatkavacha

The story of the valour of Arjuna is recounted by multiple people in different parts of Mahabharata. Bhisma and Kripacharya recounts the incident during Matasya battle and Udyog Parva. There are several more references proving the validity of the incident.

3. Consistency of character

In a large epic like Mahabharata, characters are formed with specific traits. Now if a story doesn’t conform to that trait, it should be consider as an interpolation.

Case Study 1 — Draupadi denied Karna a chance to participate in swyamvara

If we look into the character of Drauapdi, she is docile and submissive. Her swyamvara was not her choice but his father’s descision. His brother announces to her that she has to marry whoever shots the target. She had no will to refuse anyone or select anyone. She doesn’t object to a poor, undistinguished brahmin from taking the shot or marrying him.

She doesn’t object even when Pandavas decided to have a polyandrous marriage. How could she have refused to Karna, who was an invited and explictly named guest? This is what prof. Vishnu Shuthankar, the editor-in-chief of BORI CE makes his case.

Case Study 2 — Arjuna defeated Nivatkavacha

What kind of character Arjuna was supposed to be to defeat Nivatkavacha?

  • Someone who defeated warrior like Karna repeatedly (although it won’t be considered a great feat)?
  • Someone who vanquished even Indra in Khandavadhana
  • Someone who routed an entire Gandharvas army, the same army which vanquished all Kaurvas and forced Karna to flee.
  • Someone who again vanquished all Kauravas, in Matasya war.
  • Someone who was deemed undefeatable by the likes Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama, Karna and Parshurama.
  • Someone who could face and impress even great Mahadeva.

Was Arjuna defeated by Ordinary foot soldiers?

There is no such incident mentioned in Mahabharata. And yet it is natural. What do you think happens when a pack of heynas attack a lion. They may overwhelm him for a moment. But that is not victory. Arjuna could have destroyed them with a single divyastra, and he didn’t — that is a victory. And victory, not just on the moral ground. Finally Arjuna did emerge victorious in the literal sense.

So, while you are free for your perception, you can’t call any and every incident as interpolation, simply because it doesn’t suite your narraitve.
