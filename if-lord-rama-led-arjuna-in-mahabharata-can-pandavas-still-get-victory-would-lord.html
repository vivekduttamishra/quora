If Lord Rama led Arjuna in Mahabharata, can Pandavas still get victory? Would Lord Ramas be able to handle people like Shakuni?

📅 Jun 08, 2020 10:30 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Great question, but something that comes from forgetting the fact that two are same — how they differed is the era they lived in. So it doesn't really matter what way you switch them they would ensure Dharma wins!

Ram in Mahabharat

I am assuming, just like Krishna, Ram, too, won't fight with weapons. He would be the guide.

  • Ram too would have approached Hastinapur, making every attempt at peace. Afterall didn't he do the same thing by sending Angad to Ravana's court?
  • Ram knew how to counter deceits of the likes of Duryodhana or Shakuni. Didn't he managed Bali and Meghnad and none of their boons saved them?
  • How would Kaurava win against Ram? He had such a galvanizing personality that he could win anyone — from Sabri to Bali.
  • He transformed an army of apes to a Victor, Pandava has a true army.
  • It was highly likely that Ram would have won Bhisma and Drona before the beginning of the war by his personal aura and with Bhisma gone Duryodhana had no one to fight.
  • Ram the fighter was great, but Ram the human was even great influencer. No deceit could harm his simplicity and aura.
