Which epic chronicles love among brothers more truly, Ramayana or Mahabharata?

📅 May 03, 2020 11:37 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

Both Ramayana and Mahabharat depict the genuine loves between brothers, both also include exceptions. If there are differences, it should also be attributed to the fact that belonged to a different era — Treta and Dwapar.

While a natural answer would be Ramayana — and the love of four princes of Ayodhya. Their affection and respect for each other were exemplary. A brother (Ram) is dethroned for the benefit of another brother (Bharat), and yet, he is happy for Bharat. Bharat refuses the kingship for Ram. Ram is exiled by the king's order, Lakshman takes a self-imposed exile for his brother’s love. such love would be unparalleled. But then it was Treta yug — an era where Dharma and duty presided over lust for the throne. And yet this yug and Ramayana also tells the tale enmity of Bali and Sugriv as well. Once again note, the reason for difference between Bali and Sugriv was not about some throne, but a sense of betrayal.

Mahabharat is a tale of centuries later of a different yuga — Dwapar. Lust for throne had increased. And in that era there existed five brothers, who stayed together through all happiness and sorrow. They faced trying times like few may have faced. They shared not just fortune and ill-fate, but also wife. Their elder brother was no Ram. He made mistakes, nay, blunder. And yet the brothers stood together. True there are occaisions where Bhim and Arjuna both revolted against Yudhisthir, but the remaining calmed them down and they remained together.

So who wins? Is there a problem if it is a stalemate? We certainly win if we learn a lesson here.
