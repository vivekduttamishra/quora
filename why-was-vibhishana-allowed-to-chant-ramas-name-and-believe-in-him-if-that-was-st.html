Why was Vibhishana allowed to chant Rama's name and believe in him if that was strictly disallowed in Lanka?

📅 Apr 25, 2020 04:55 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

I don’t remember a mention that Rama’s name was disallowed in Lanka. Ravan was no Hiranyakashyap that had proclaimed that he should be worshipped in place of God.

The Ravan brother’s had achieved their Shakti by worshipping Gods. So the question, that he Gods weren’t allowed doesn’t arise. Once again by the boon of Brahma he was unconquerable by even Vishnu — so why bother?

Vibhishan was ousted for his stand against Ravan in the Sitaharan incident because of which Lanka ultimately faced a vanquishing battle.

My answer is based on the story of Ramayan, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers Balmiki Ramayan and Ramcharit Manas apart from Ved Vyas retelling as Part of Mahabharat. I have studied the Sanskrit Hindi Version from Geeta Press
