Why did Krishna prefer Karna to Arjuna?

📅 May 24, 2020 04:51 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question should be broken up in there parts —

  1. What did Krishna preferred?
    • This is the most important question, and surprising answer is not Arjuna. Krishna favoured Dharma. He says so clearly in Gita.
    • He explains how to ensure you are on Dharma side — Niskama Karma and devotion.
    • Krishna always showed path to everyone, but to achieve him you need to approach him. You need to surrender your egos and prove your worth.
  1. Why did Krishna preferred Arjuna?
    • Krishna didn't support him and prevented his sufferings. He suffered humiliation, exile and slavery.
    • Krishna didn't help Arjuna during Gandharvas war, Virat war and so on…
    • K rishna didn't provide any divine weapon to Arjuna. He could have given his sudershan Chakra.
    • Arjuna proved his worth by not whining his bad luck. He not only pleased Krishna but also Shiva.
    • Armed with Pashupatastra, he never let lust of power, ego beat him.
    • He refused to fight, not out of fear, out of compassion for others.
  1. Why didn't Karna get lucky?
    • Krishna didn't abandoned Karna. He approached Karna and invited him to be with him. Karna refused Krishna.
    • During the battle Karna attacked Krishna on multiple occasions.
    • Moments before Karna's death, Krishna reminded him of his follies. Even though Karna acknowledged and regretted his mistake, he wasn't ready to surrender. Had he surrendered…

Now here is a question for you — is there any reason why Krishna would change his preference?
