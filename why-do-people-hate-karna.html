Why do people hate Karna?

📅 Mar 13, 2020 11:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Consider a young man who gets the rare opportunity to be trained by one of the greatest Acharya of the time (Drona). He finds another student who excels in everything. He requests his guru to teach him Brahmastra — why — so he can defeat the other student. What Guru does — he refused. So unfare of Guru not to bestow a jealous student with one of the most divine weapon. Bad bad Guru! He doesn’t deserve to be called Guru of Great Karna!

Then he goes to another guru fore Brahmastra. The next Guru cursed him. Why — He had cheated and lied. So what if he lied? Would Guru have given him the knowledge of Brahmastra if he had told the truth — he had a person grudge? And what about he let him have a peaceful sleep when insect was biting him. Another bad Guru! True why should you hate Karna?

He re-enters the scene again and gate crash a ceremony organized by his first guru, out of self boasting and jealousy with the original rival in school. He is made a king by his old friends from Gurukul — you can say impudent, but not evil. Accepted.

He conspires with his old found friends to burn his arch rival with 4 brothers and her mother alive — So what people die. An old lady and five brothers. Isn’t that what friends do? Shall we applaud for Karna who can kill any innocent to keep his friend happy!

He goes to take part in a swaymber. Now here is two different narration.

  1. Popular — Draupadi rejects him — the narration started half hearted with Neelkanth edition that influenced KMG translation.
  2. Critically acknowledged — He fails to string the swaymber. This is popular with several nothern edition, southern edition, Bori and endorsed by C. Rajagopalachari.

Now take whichever version suits you, the conclusion remains — Arjun won Draupadi’s swaymber. So what would you do if you were Karna? Ofcourse you would attempt to kill the impudent Arjuna and snatch and tear apart Draupadi. Well Karna did the same. I wonder why people don’t endorse his actions!

Next comes the darkest part of the story — The Great Gamble — where Karna sat knowing the wicked trickery that Shakuni was using — that is what friends do. Isn’t that?

Just hang on because he was a true friend he went beyond. He rebuked foolish Vikarna for speaking for a hapless Draupadi. So Karna takes it as his cause to call her an unchaste woman and even a property of Yudhisthir. And story doesn’t end here (as in TV Shows). He is the brave one commands Dussasan to disrobe her. ← read this line 3 times.

Now I am wondering how can someone hate Karna for such a courageous attempt! Who could have imagined disrobing a woman in public at that time? Don’t we have advocates trying their best to save Nirbhaya’s rapist!

Pandavas are exiled. He comes with a new plan — lets go an mock already suffering Pandavas — What would be a better fun! And when Gandharvas attack the greatest friend ever fled the scene leaving his friend at the mercy of same Pandavas — afterall was it not Pandavas duty to protect there King. Please go an applaud Karna for giving Pandava a chance to glory.

His mother comes calling him comeback home. The same mother than abandoned him — how dare she. Shouldn’t she be taught a lesson? The Danveer Karna offers a gift to his mother — he announces not only he won't return to her, if possible he would kill Arjuna. Do you want to applaud he spared the rest. Don’t mind if I can’t applaud this cruel gesture!

The brave hero mocks the Grand Bhisma and refused to enter the war (even for his best friend) under Bhisma’s command. Would this be in the interest of Duryodhan? Nah! So what! that fool had it coming when he chose Bhisma as commander-in-chief. How dare that old man insult Karna by calling an Adhirathi and selfish?

Once he enters the war he along with another pack of hungry hound kill a lone warrior — Please don’t hate him! He was just one of those hounds. And that son of Arjun had it coming. He defeated him in the war. How dare He?

There are many more stories of great Karna that I can go on but I think these few incidents should help you wonder more properly why some people hate Karna!
