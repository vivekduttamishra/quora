What would have happened if Acharya Dron would not have ignored Karna's capabilities and would have given equal attention towards his education likewise Arjun? Would Karna & Arjun have become friends then? How would Mahabharat tell it's tale?

📅 Apr 01, 2022 10:18 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The question’s basic premises is wrong. Each part of it. Let us discuss one by one.

1. What if Drona would not have ignored Karna’s capabilities and would have given equal attention towards his education.

Drona didn’t ignore Karna’s capabilities. In fact, he recognized his capability of infinite deception and wickedness and therefore didn’t give him the ultimate weapon. It is well documented in Mahabharata.

A proof of this is the fact that despite Karna eventually receiving education from Parshurama, couldn’t become a better person. He dedicated his life to lust, loot, conspiracy and heinous crimes like trying to molest a women in public. Imagine, how well guru had predicted his capabilities.

No let us compare their passion for learning. Arjuna completed his education from Drona and Karna from Parshurama. What happened next? Did Karna continue to strive for being better warrior? No. There is no evidence. In his ego he was already best. On the other hand Arjuna never stopped learning. He expanded his capability by learning from Mahadeva, Chitrasena and Indra himself. Not only did he learn, he also gave back his education to warriors like Satyaki. Do we have any such reference for Karna?

Now tell me, how would Karna had competed Arjuna even if Drona would have given him destructive weapons ignoring his wicked nature? Do you think Shiva would have been equally blind and handed him knowledge simply because he would have walked and asked for it?

2. Would Karna & Arjun have become friends then?

NO chance. Karna would have remained bitter enemy of Arjuna. Remember, Karna was Duryodhana’s friend even before Drona refused to give him Brahmastra. He was already involved in numerous conspiracy against Pandavas and more important, he had eventually received the weapons from Guru Parshurma.

The problem was Karna had no morale compass directed towards Dharma. It was directed towards his personal ego. He wanted to be recognized as better than Arjuna, something he wasn’t. And every direct or indirect conflict between them is a testimony to this fact.

Now how can a follower of Dharma be friends with an evil man?

3. How would Mahabharat tell it's tale?

Exactly how it proceeded. Knowledge of weapons would not have turned an evil man into a good one. Karna would have become even more arrogant and worse. And weapons just make you powerful and strong, they don’t make you brave or courageous. Karna still would have fled from his battles and he would have still died while trying hard to plead Arjuna — Please don’t kill me because my chariot is stuck! I never cared about Abhimanyu, Bhimasena or anyone fighting without chariot. I continued to strike them. But please don’t kill me. It doesn’t matter that warrior like Bhimasena, Abhimanyu, Drona and Arjuna all fought on occasions without chariot, sometimes even without weapons. It is a different matter that they didn’t plead. Braves never do. But then we are still talking about Karna. Aren’t we? So yes, Karna would have been dead in the same way. Without a chariot to run away. Fighting.

Evils always die in the end. Defeated. There is no glory for them.
