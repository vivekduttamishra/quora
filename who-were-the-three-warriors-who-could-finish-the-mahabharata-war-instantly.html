Who were the three warriors who could finish the Mahabharata war instantly?

📅 Jul 01, 2020 10:04 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Before I write down the answer to this supposition, let me point out —

Mahabharat battle wasn’t supposed to finish instantly. There was only two ways the war could have ended instantly —

  1. One side surrenders even before the battle starts.
  2. Total annihilation of the other side.

Now coming to the question. There are only two people who truly could finish the war instantly, and when I tell this, I do not base it on some biased evidence or by including multiple ifs, buts and exceptions or constraints of changing the events as they progressed — if only Krishna wasn’t there or if only some weapons had worked. So lets get started.

Lord Krishna

I don’t think anyone can refute this. If only he wanted to finish the war. Krishna had the capacity to deploy both of the above options in the war. He could have allowed Arjuna to back off and the war would have ended instantly or he could have destroyed the entire Karuava’s army even without a blink.

Since Krishna chose not to end the war instantly, there was no way it could have happened.

Arjuna

Yes, I know many critics will jump to tear apart my question or brandish me as Arjuna fan. But interestingly even Arjuna could have ended the war by deploying either of the two options I mentioned above.

  1. Arjuna had almost ended the war instantly by deciding not to fight it. It requires a great courage and compassion to give up a fight to save those who never reciprocated in the kind. Krishna had to apply all his persuasive skills to dissuade him from this step.
  2. Arjuna’s statement in Shanti Parva —

I tell thee truly that with Vasudeva as my ally, I can, on a single car, exterminate the three worlds with even the immortals, indeed, all mobile creatures that were, are, will be, in the twinkling of the eye. This is what I think. That terrible and mighty weapon which the Lord of all creatures (Mahadeva) gave me on the occasion of my hand-to-hand encounter with him (in the guise of) a hunter, still existeth with me. Indeed, O tiger among men, that weapon which the Lord of all creatures useth at the end of Yuga for destroying created things, existeth with me. Ganga's son knoweth not that weapon; nor Drona nor Gautama (Kripa); nor Drona's son, O king! How, therefore, can the Suta's son know it. It is not, however, proper to slay ordinary men in battle by means of celestial weapons. We shall (on the other band) vanquish our foes in a fair fight.

—Udyoga Parva: Uluka Dutagamana Parva: Section CXCVII

And before you discard this statment as babbling of some fool, please read the highlighted sections —

  • Arjuna didn’t give the credit of this claim to himself, but to Krishna and Pashupatastra. This section also defines the potential of Pashupatastra.
  • Arjuna himself decided against this action and time frame and that showed his moral character. He never used Pashupatastra in the entire ware because he believed and did vanquish foes in a fair fight.
  • He didn’t use Pashupatastra against any weapon — be it Narayanastra, Vaishnavastra or Brahmastra.

Neither Krishna, nor Arjuna had any plan to finish the war in a single day.

Why none of the Kauravas could have finished the war in instantly.

  1. No Kaurava even dared to claim that they could finish it in one day. Only their fans can have this wishful thinking.

    Duryodhana. asked Ganga's son of great vows, saying, 'O lord, in what time cant thou annihilate the troops of Pandu's sons?' Indeed, the wicked Duryodhana was answered by him in these words, viz.,--In a month! Drona also declared that he could do the same feat in about the same time. Gautama (Kripa) indicated twice that period, as hath been heard by us. Drona's son acquainted with weapons of high efficacy declared the period (in his case) to be ten nights. Karna also, acquainted with weapons of high efficacy, asked in the midst of the Kurus, declared that the could complete the slaughter in five days.
    —Udyoga Parva: Uluka Dutagamana Parva: Section CXCVII
  2. Kaurava’s head (not senapati) Duryodhana was too arrogant to surrender and opt for peace. So option 1 was not a possibility.
  3. Option 2 — annihilation Pandavas would mean annihilating Krishna along with it. And as we all know that was impossible.
  4. All Kaurava’s did manage to finish the war in 18 days, same period as Pandavas. So they should get equal credit. This war couldn’t have ended with their victory even if they fought till the end of the days.

Why didn’t Krishna wanted to end the war instantly

  • It was a Dharmayudha not a slaughterhouse.
  • He needed to establish Dharma — establish the teaching of do’s and don’ts
  • He needed to show the worst and best among people
  • He needed the us to see why it was a Dharmayudh
  • He needed to establish that humans should fight their won war.
  • He needed to remind even without fighting the war, God sides with Dharma
  • And even against a much greater adversary Dharma wins.
  • A true hero doesn’t choose powerful army, he chooses the right guide who can take him to the path of Dharma.

A battle that would finish by brute force wouldn’t have these traits.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
