Social Changes Over Past Few Centurie

In last few centuries, Indian society underwent a great transformation. Part of the reason was constant attack on Indian culture by foreign invaders, first Mugals and then Britishers. With advent of Mugals, Aryan culture became more cautious and rigid. It was a natural defensive reflex. To preserve your family tradition, an isolation mechanism was created, and caste system became more rigid. Britishers exploited these compartmentalizations to apply their divide and rule. They succeeded.

The defensive mechanism over time became offensive against a section of your own people. One caste oppressed the other, and they did all this in the name of ancient sacred texts. Many naturally turned against those text.

People wanted to condemn the current caste system which they believed existed from ancient times. Let me be clear here — Caste system existed from ancient times, the oppressive nature was a more recent transformation.

But people wanted to blame the caste system itself. To unroot the system they wanted to link it to our epic. And how would you link it — Find someone who was oppressed due to his caste.

Rise of Karna

  • Karna originally seemed to be a role model for someone oppressed because of his caste. The other characters picked from the epic are Eklavya (from Mahabharat) and Shambuk (from Ramayana). These two however are minor character and not much narrative could be presented on them.
  • Karna appeared an ideal candidate for so many reasons as per original epic —
  1. A divine lineage — Suryaputra.
  2. His mother abandons him.
  3. He wasn’t allowed to compete with Arjuna because he was not royal blood.
  4. He was a Šuta was known later.
  5. A skilled archer acclaimed at various places in Mahabharat. He is the principal opponent for Arjuna, one of the most important hero of the era.
  6. Indra took away his Kavach and Kundal.
  7. He promised he would spare his 4 brothers in the war.
  8. He refused to switch sides on Krishna’s advice.

These narratives superficially tried proved that Karna despite of divine lineage and noble qualities suffered because of the caste system. Although only point 3 remotely favoured their narrative.

But how could you prove something that doesn’t exist? As they moved forward with Karna as their mascot against the caste system, they realized their ins’t a lot of material to go on. In fact there are more contradictions than prove —

  1. Vidur, a maids son was the Prime Minister of Hastinapur
  2. Karna wa accepted as the King of Anga
  3. Krishna belonging to caste of cowherds is acclaimed an incarnation.
  4. Eklavya was an honored guest in Yudhisthir’s Rajsuy yagna.

Since few could read an epic spanning in over 9000 pages (Gita Press), the best bait was to create narratives centric to Karna. The most notable work in this line is Rashmirathi by Ramdhari Singh Dinkar (1952). These narrative added following ideas —

In contradicting to the original epic —

  1. Dronacharya refused to teach Karna. Original epic mentions Drona teaching Karna explicitly
  2. Karna was denied a participation in Draupadi’s swaymber because of his caste. Note this is not a modern narrative but existed in 5 out of over 1259 manuscript dating before 1800. That is merely 0.1%. But that suggest that such narrative is not new but existed since British era proving my above point. Further this incident is not cross-referenced anywhere even in those 4 manuscript anywhere else. To add to it there are other incidents which contradicts this story. This story without a doubt is fake.
  3. Karna was killed when he was trying to pull out his chariot’s wheel, unarmed in unjustifiable manner. Karna, while was in distress and his wheel was stuck up, was not unarmed. He died fighting, and not while attempting to pull out his wheel. He certainly requested Arjuna to wait till he pulls out his chariot. Once Arjuna refused, Karna took to fight and continued to fight till the end. This paints him as a good warrior rather than unfortunate warrior that garners more sympathy.
