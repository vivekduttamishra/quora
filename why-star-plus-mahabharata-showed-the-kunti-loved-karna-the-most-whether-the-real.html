But it is important to highlight Kunti’s relationship to Karna.


WHY DID KUNTI GAVE BIRTH TO KARNA?

Karna birth was the result of Kunti’s curiosity and Surya’s pressurization on her. She made an insolent mistake when she tested Durvasa’s prayer that brought Surya to him. On realizing her mistake she pleads Surya to leave and not impregnate her. But Surya doesn’t buzz and warns her of dire consequences should she refuse to submit herself —

Kunti replied, “O illustrious one! Return to the place you arrived from. O illustrious one! I summoned you out of curiosity. Please be gracious.” ‘The sun said, “O slender-waisted one! I will go as you have asked me to. But having invoked a god, it is not proper to send him away in vain. O extremely fortunate one! Your desire is to have a son through Surya. He will be unrivalled in this world in valour and he will wear armour and earrings. O one whose gait is like that of an elephant! Give yourself to me. O beautiful one! I will beget a son, like the one that you desire. O fortunate one! O sweet-smiling one! I will depart after having united with you. Else, I will leave in anger and will curse that brahmana22 and your father. There is no doubt that I will consume them because of what you have done. Though he does not know about this offence, your father is stupid. That brahmana gave you this mantra without knowing about your character and conduct and I will impose extreme humility on him.

—Kundala harana Parva

  • Kunti pleads to Surya to be gracious and leave her
  • Surya threatens to curse her that would destroy them (consume them).

Kunti submits for the sake of her families safety and dignity. I don’t see, she had any other option but to give birth to Karna.


DID KUNTI LOVE KARNA?

There are sufficient evidence to prove that Kunti really loved Karna. Let us go through them one by one.

1.Karna’s famous Kavacha and Kundala

Karna’s famous Kavacha and Kundala was actually Kunti’s bargain with Surya in exchange of her submission to him —

‘Kunti said, “O dispeller of everything that is dark! If I obtain a son through you, may he have earrings and armour. May he be mighty-armed, brave and immensely strong.” ‘Surya replied, “He will be mighty-armed and possess earrings. O fortunate one! He will be clad in divine armour and both25 of these will originate in amrita.” ‘Kunti said, “If my son’s earrings and the excellent armour are created out of amrita, as you have said, you may beget him on me. O god! O illustrious one! Then I will unite with you.

—Kunda Harana Parava

Now we must realize Kunti never asked any other Gods to grant any protection for her other sons. Does this not show her love?

2. Kunti pain at abandoning her son

She cast away the basket in the waters of the river Ashva. ‘Having done this, Kunti wept and spoke these words. Listen to them. “O son! May you be safe from all the beings in the sky, the earth and heaven and from those that roam the water. May your paths be auspicious and may you be free from obstructions. O son! May those who meet you have no enmity in their minds. May King Varuna, lord of the waters, protect you in the water. May the wind-god, who travels everywhere, protect you in the sky. May your father, foremost among those who provide heat, protect you everywhere. O son! Driven by destiny, he is the one who has given you to me. The Adityas, Vasus, Rudras, Sadhyas, Vishvadevas, the Maruts, together with Indra, the directions and the lords of the directions and all the gods must protect you, in terrain that is smooth and rough. Even if you are in a different region, I will get to know you through your armour. O son! Your father, the god Bhanu Vibhavasu is fortunate. Fortunate are those who will see you become a youth, like a lion with a mane, in the forests of the Himalayas.”

— Kundala Harana Parva

Kunti envies the luck of those who would take care of his son and see him grow. Now the question is —

  • How was Kunti so sure that someone would adopt her and he would grow up?
  • Wasn’t she trying to get rid of him by throwing in river?

3. Didn’t Kunti simply threw Karna in river?

Well do we know the story of Ganga and how she liberated seven Vasu’s? By drowning them in water. A quick and easy solution. Right? So why didn’t Kunti simply got rid of Karna same way? Why did she demand such strong protection from Surya? How was she so sure that Karna would live and grow up?

4. Kunti faints on seeing Karna going to rival camp

When Kunti saw Karna during the talent showcase event, we she two distinct emotions and both speaks volumes about her love for her abandoned child

First she faints at seeing her son and not able to openly acknowledge her —

5. She reaches out to him before the final battle

Some may say she reached out to Karna for the protection of her other sons. But that is illogical. Everyone including Karna knew that Pandavas are powerful and well protected and he is unlikely to kill them. Here is what Kunti though as she approached Karna —

But there cannot be a victory in the destruction of relatives. The grandfather, Shantanu’s son, the preceptor who is foremost among warriors and Karna are with Dhritarashtra’s son and that increases my fears. The preceptor Drona will never willingly wish to fight with his disciples. Why should the grandfather not be kindly disposed towards the Pandavas? This leaves only one48 who unnecessarily follows the evil-minded son of Dhritarashtra. Because of his evil delusions, he has always hated the Pandavas. He is immensely strong in his resolution and he is also powerful. Karna has always been against the Pandavas and I am now tormented by that. Today, I hope to turn Karna’s mind towards the Pandavas. I will meet him, tell him the truth and seek to obtain his favours. Satisfied, the illustrious Durvasa granted me a boon. When I dwelt in the inner quarters of my father, King Kuntibhoja’s abode, I was honoured by the ability of summoning the gods. With a trembling heart, I thought about this in many ways, about the strength and weakness of the mantra and about the brahmana’s boon. Because of the nature of a woman, who also possessed childish sentiments, I thought about this repeatedly, though I was protected by a trusted nurse and surrounded by friends. How could I avoid taints and how could I preserve my father’s reputation? How could I do something good for myself, without committing a sin? Thinking about that brahmana and bowing down before him, I was overcome by curiosity and behaved like a child. Though I was still a maiden, I summoned the sun god to me. Though I was a virgin, I conceived him in my womb. He was protected like my son. Why should he not listen to my words, which will be beneficial for the cause of his brothers?”

—Karna Upnivada Parva

  • Kunti sees no victory in destruction of relatives
  • She knows Karna is delusional and evil because he doesn’t know the truth.
  • She hoped to win her by truth. She hoped he would realize she protected her.
  • She also hoped he wouldn’t try to hurt his brothers.

Here it doesn’t reflect her fear rather she was tormented by Karna’s evil gestures. There is a difference between fear and grief. This is what she requested Karna —

Arjuna. Let those who are evil bow down. Let Karna and Arjuna be like Rama53 and Janardana. If the two of you are united in your minds, what can you not achieve in this world?

But she was herself delusional. Karna was unmoved by her truth. Surya corroborated her story. Karna remained unmoved. Not only he refused to return, he also announced that while he would spare his four brothers, he would strive to kill Arjuna. Now some may say it was a great charity, I see it as the most cruel thing one can say to anyone’s mother — forget your own.

Kunti still doesn’t curse or reprimand Karna. Rather returns back.


ALLEGATIONS ON KUNTI

There are some common allegations on Kunti. Some I answered above and some should still be answered.

1. Kunti abandoled Karna by throwing in water.

This is partially true. Kunti had to abandon Karna for the reputation of her family. But she didn’t cast him in river to die —

  • She got divine protection for him.
  • She rowed her in a well protected luxurious box guarded by her confidants.

Now if she had to get rid of him there were easier ways.

2. Why did Kunti abandon Karna in the first place?

Kunti had no other choice. Not only keeping him would have ruined her and her family's reputation, it would have done Karna more harm than good. Imagine! Who would have married Kunti knowing she had a son? What was the chance of Karna having a honorable life with an unwed mother and unknown father? Did Satyavati ever told Shantanu about her Son Ved Vyas? Karna would have a more miserable life had Kunti not separated him from herself.

3. Why didn’t Kunti divulge the secret to Karna earlier?

There was no earlier time. It was already late when Kunti first met Karna after those many years. Her husband, who was the only person who could have legally adopted Karna was long dead. Karna was already a friend of Duryodhana for years and was part of several conspiracies against Pandavas. There was no easy option to divulge the details. Moreover, while her other son’s had a life full of struggle where their very survival was a question, Karna lived a life of comfort and luxary. Kunti couldn’t have seen any reason to approach Karna earlier.

4. Didn’t Kunti approach Karna for the protection of her other sons?

This is a common allegation, but nothing in the epic proves this. She was tormented by Karna’s evil delusions and she certainly wanted her son’s safe. Which mother wouldn’t. But she wanted to bring Karna to her camp. That would have ensured even his protection. She goes to Karna. Doesn’t ask her to spare her sons — It was Karna’s own idea. Interestingly, when Karna announces that he intends to kill Arjuna, she doesn’t even flinch. She doesn’t make any further appeal to ensure Arjuna’s safety. Now why would she not try that if she was afraid? The answer is simple — there was no past incident where Karna ever fought Arjuna on equal footing. Karna always fled scared. Kunti had no worry on Arjuna’s part. Even Karna said the same to Kunti and earlier to Krishna.
