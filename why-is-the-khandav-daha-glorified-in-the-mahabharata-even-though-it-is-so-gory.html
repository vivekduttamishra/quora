Why is the Khandav-Daha glorified in the Mahabharata, even though it is so gory?

📅 Apr 30, 2021 11:56 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I have answered this question more than a dozen times till now and I would ignore the leading nature of the question.

What people asking such questions do not understand is —

1. Every era has it’s own problem that defines morality — glory or gory!

A contemporary standard can’t be applied to a civilization five thousand years ago or to a civilization that may come five thousand years later. Who knows five thousand years later earth becomes so devoid of trees that even cutting a branch of tree be considered as a gory act?

Let me ask you this — Every now an then we get the news of lacks of birds being culled (killed) to prevent the spread of bird flu. Many of them aren’t even infected. Many carry eggs inside them and many are small chicklets. Why no one raises question on this gory act of our contemporary society? Why don’t we apply the same standard of culling to prevent covid19? Why killing birds and animals are not tried under section 302 — murder? And I am talking about contemporary society.

Take another issue. Do we recognize a doctor creating antibiotic a hero or villain? Do you think someone who consumes an antibiotic a patient or a selfish beast? Are you aware that an antibiotic kills millions of bacteria who are living beings?

So why do we call these things as a gory act?

2. Nature seeks balance.

Unlike contemporary world that has suffered human brutality against nature — deforestation and wreck in the ozone layer leading to global warming we are talking about an era that existed at the dawn of civilization. There were more forests and wild beasts than place for towns and cultivatable land. More predators who would pounce at human colonies. There were cannibals and poison’s serpents that were threat to the society just like modern day bacteria.

Nature seeks balance. Today killing a tiger is heinous. They are on verge of extinction and not a threats to human lives, rather we are a threat to them. But five thousand years ago…

Ashoka chose non-violence over his violent past and propagated peace. That was the need of that hour. But when we follow same ideal for over few centuries blindly, we lost our combative nature and became easy target for foreign invasion. Do you see the need of balance?

3.Time is cyclic (Kalpchakra) and not a straight timeline

You can’t see or understand Khandava dahana as an isolated incident to either call it gory or call it a glory. It is a dot in the cyclic timeline. It has happened in the past and it would happen in the future.

Do you remember Australian wildfire last year where millions of animals perished? Who was responsible for that massacre? Would you call nature? Would you call Agni Dev (Fire)? Who wanted to burn Khandava all those years ago? Whose order was Agnidev trying to execute — Brahma or destiny — the supreme creator of the universe.

Who mascaraed all the dinosaur? It happened long before Mahabharata era. Who do you blame? Glory or gory?

Neither. It was nature balancing itself.

Nature’s course correction will happen no matter how we term it. Nature doesn’t follow human’s lesson of morality. It follows just one formula — balance — and it knows how to get there.

—Vivek Dutta Mishra
