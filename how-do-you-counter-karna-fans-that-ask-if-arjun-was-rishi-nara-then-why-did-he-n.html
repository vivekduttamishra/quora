How do you counter Karna fans that ask if Arjun was Rishi Nara then why did he not reach heaven like Yudhishthira (alive)? Why did he fall? How can Rishi Nar be arrogant (as stated by Yudhisthira)?

📅 Aug 07, 2021 12:09 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Must be counter ever irrelevant queries and claims? Doesn’t Mahabharata, at multiple places, mention Arjuna as an incarnation of Nara? But question, indeed, poses two important questions —

How can a person who fell be an incarnation?

Parshurama had to behead his mother. Goddess Sati immolated herself. Devi Sita was abducted by force. Ganesha was beheaded. Great Rama couldn’t prevent Sita’s abduction and was forced to exile her later. Lord Shiva couldn’t prevent Sati’s molestation. Krishna was hunted down like a prey. Does that make them lesser? Or there is a greater lesson that the questioner missed?

Let us take more examples — Remember Captain Vikram Batra? He too fell. Remember the martyers of Pulwama? They all fell. Does that make them lesser brave? Lesser great?

God has a grand design. They often remind people that great people don’t fear falling down. They don’t get scared from performing their duty even if it means falling down.

How can Rishi Nar be arrogant (as stated by Yudhisthira)

Second part of the question answers the first. It was stated by Yudhishthira. It was his perception. Period. What was Shiva’s perception? Did he consider them arrogant or worthy? What was Lord Parshurama’s perception? What was Drona’s perception? What about Krishna, who called him his inseparable exitance? Who perception should I value more?

Does that mean I am calling Yudhishthira a lier? Actually know. If you say something, you truly believe in, you are speaking your truth even if it is not a correct fact. Yudhishthira thought Arjuna to be arrogant. That was his truth — not the fact, not the universal truth.
