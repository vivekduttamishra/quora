Is Accursed God accurately based on Mahabharata?

📅 May 03, 2021 11:07 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for asking.

The Accursed God is an epic fiction based on Mahabharata. By definition, a fiction shall have fictional elements and characters to elaborate the points. But please don’t consider this as a let down or que to move away. At least read this post to the end and I would tell you exactly how authentic you can expect The Accursed God to be.

But in short, I must say this book doesn’t betray the original spirit of the epic. It adds non-evasive elements to expand the epic and not to distort it with narratives it doesn’t intend to convey.

Adding narrative to epic saga has been an ancient epic tradition, almost imbibed to the core values of Shruti-Smriti tradition — adapt the epic saga to convey a subtle message.

Remember! Lakshman Rekha too is a fiction

But that doesn’t mean it is less valuable that the facts. Lakshman Rekha’s origin can be traced to the works of late 10th century works like Ranganathan Ramayana in Telgu and a few Bengali version. This insertion of this narrative coincides with the foreign invasion in India. Loot, rape and abduction were on rise and we needed a way to convey a subtle message to keep our family safe. And what could have been a better way to say — Even when Devi Sita ignored Lakshmana’s advice, she had to suffer. So we safe, we with guardians of the family, because you don’t know if an evil is lurking in the corner!

So what is the take away —

Fiction in itself is not bad, and may be immensely valuable — It’s intent makes it good or bad? Ask is this addition contributing to epic culture or trying to destroy it’s legacy!

Why did I write an fiction based on Mahabharata?

Honestly people get motivation from something they like. My motivation was something I disliked — Ajaya by Anand Neelkanthan and Jaya by Dev Dutta Patnayaka!

While these books are worst of their kind, they are not isolated incidents. There has been a host of fiction that have been written around the epic saga. While some are based on particular characters, others are the story with different perception.

The books I referred above are blatant attempts to distort the epic — glorify the villains! Take Ajaya, the core intent, in the word of the author, is to tale a saga of anti-hero. And when you try to carve hero's out of villain's, hero's would be condemned to take the role of villain's. While some may call it creativity, I see it brutalizing the epic — It doesn’t add value to the epic. It doesn’t glorify the epic. All it does is to mangle and degrade the epic.

And even as a fiction writer, I don’t see any creativity. It’s simply inversing the story. Make good one bad and bad ones good. Where is the creativity? It’s just a mirror image!

These fictions told the story of a abusive society — where there was an evil caste system that crushed peoples aspirations based on their birth. Where people were denied opportunities simply because they were born to lower caste. And people believed them!

They ignored son of a fisherwoman was the Emperor of Hastinapur and her other son the author of the saga! Son of a maid servant was the prime minister of Hastinpura and a cowherd was deemed as as Vishnu incarnate!

I wondered, are we condemned to see our epic brutalized? And answer was simple — if people can write fictions to brutalize them, why shouldn’t there be a narrative depicting it’s true glory?

Why do we nee need any fiction in the first place?

Two reasons.

First, not many has perseverance, capacity or knowledge to read the authentic version of the epic, which spans in tens of thousands of pages in Sanskrit. We may read a translation like that Bibek Debroy or Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) but can’t deny the inclusion of perceptions of the translator.

Second, it is difficult to say what is authentic. I consider BORI CE to be an authentic and honest attempt. But then critics point out, is not ancient. And when it comes to ancient, there are variation differing from each other — essentially having fictional contents. And the most ancient versions are written over last thousand years or so. Before that it was propagated by words of mouth.

Therefore people often chose an easy path — trust TVShows and fiction books trusting they must be honest to the original epic. Alas! more often than not they betray the core values of the epic.

If a major population would read only fictions to understand our epic, why not give them something that glorifies our Sanatan culture? But that is one part of the story, you should read this fiction to get another perspective on our great ancient epic.

Having set the context let me come to actual question —

How Accurate is The Accursed God

It has been my sincere effort not to betray the core spirit of the epic. Although there are several minor alternation to narrative, I can proudly say, there is no distortion to it.

There are places where epic story is not elaborate and leaves a lot of unanswered question. The Accursed God reads between those line, attempts to fill gaps and answer some important questions, without betraying the authentic narrative. Let us take a few examples —

  • Bhishma’s brother Chitrangada was killed after a long fight. But epic doesn’t go in details. What puzzled me, was — What was Bhishma doing while his brother was killed?
  • Most modern depiction of divyastra make them appear as a magical arrow with powers ranging from that of a missile to a nuclear weapon. But what was the true nature of Divyastra? I daresay, none of the fictional narrative bothered to research based on epic narratives. You can find the most detailed discussion expanding on the original reference of Shiva and Drona.
  • Was Bhishma a learned disciple of Brihaspati and Parshurama or a fool who got trapped in his oath and made mistakes leading to doom of Hastinapur. Or was he a genius whose each moves were planned to the finest precession.

Answering these questions, didn’t betray epic rather highlights it’s greatness — There must be a reason Bhishma is highly regarded in ancient epic — something that escaped our understanding. Right?

But most important aspect — Why was it called a Dharmayudha! Wasn’t it just another struggle for throne or vengeance?

My quest for all these questions convinced me, that in the long process, what we have a is The Lost Epic and more I tried to find the answer, more it led me to — The Accursed God!

The Accursed God isn’t a typical Kaurava-Panadava Saga!

It has a much broader and giant canvas, starting all the way when Shantanu was just the prince of Hastinapura! Here we try to explore what make the battle of Kurukshetra a Dharma Yudha.
