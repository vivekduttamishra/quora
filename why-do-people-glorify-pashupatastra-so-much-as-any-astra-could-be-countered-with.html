Why do people glorify Pashupatastra so much as any astra could be countered with Brahmasira astra (as said by Drona)?

📅 Sep 03, 2020 10:22 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Pashupatastra need no glorification — minor characters and minor weapons needs glorification. Pashupatastra is not the weapon of Arjuna so that his critics or rivals’s fan can condemn it. It is the weapons of Mahadeva — the means of ultimate pralaya and end of yuga. This is how Shiva himself explained Pashupatastra —

'O powerful one. I will give to thee that favourite weapon of mine called the Pasuputa. O son of Pandu, thou art capable of holding, hurling, and withdrawing it. Neither the chief himself of the gods, nor Yama, nor the king of the Yakshas, nor Varuna, nor Vayu, knoweth it. How could men know anything of it? But, O son of Pritha, this weapon should not be hurled without adequate cause; for if hurled at any foe of little might it may destroy the whole universe. In the three worlds with all their mobile and immobile creatures, there is none who is incapable of being slain by this weapon. And it may be hurled by the mind, by the eye, by words, and by the bow.'

— KMG Vana Parva

Shiva uses this weapon to inhaliate entire universe including any weapon or warrior contained in it. Which other weapon has that power? Which other weapon has been referred as such.

What about Drona’s statement?

Well we if we need to choose between Drona and Shiva, I don’t think it is a matter of choice. Anyway Drona could have explained or compared only those weapons that he knew about or could comprehend. Pashupatastra was a weapon not known to any humans. So it is clear Drona compared only the weapons known to him.
