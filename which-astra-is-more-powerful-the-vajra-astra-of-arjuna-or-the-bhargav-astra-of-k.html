Which astra is more powerful, the Vajra Astra of Arjuna or the Bhargav astra of Karna?

📅 May 09, 2022 10:51 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question emits from the lack of understanding about the working of divine weapons (Divyastra) and myths that are propagated by modern retellings and tv shows. I have written about them so many times on Quora and have a great deal of elaboration in my Amazon #1 best-seller The Accursed God .

First things first, the Divyastras were nothing like modern weapons or so called missiles. They weren’t fueled by some physical or chemical energy source. Consider a modern weapon, say a automatic aimed and guided missile. Whoever can set the target and fire, it will have the same devastating effect. It doesn’t care about who fired it or on whom it is fired. It has no brain of it’s own. Then go back to Naraynastra intiated by Ashwatthama after the death of Drona. It was an almighty astra, but did no damage to anyone. Reason? It detected no enemy combatant. And it detected it’s own misuse by the wielder. It refused to inflict the damage. Can you compare it with modern narratives?

Take another example. Consider the cricket bat of Sachin Tendulkar or Virat Kohli. Are they manufactured to perfection? Yes. Customized? Probably. Would I become a equally great batsman if use their bat? Not even remotely. Who would bat batter I with Sachin’s bat or Sachin with an ordinary bat? Well, I believe you know the answer. Ask again would I be able to hit the bull’s eye if I use the gun of an Olympic gold-medalist shooter? That gun must be so perfect. Right?

Dronacharya, Indra and above all Deva-di-dev Mahadeva, all pointed out that Divyastra’s power depends not only on the power of it’s wielder but also on the power-ratio of the wielder and the destined target.

So it would be stupid to ask who is powerful — Vajra or Bhargavasra.

Correct question who is more powerful — (Arjuna+Vajra) or (Karna + Bhargavastra). In fact even this wouldn’t be a perfect question because there is a third factor — the responsible use case.

So the new equation becomes

  • Responsible Arjuna + Vajra vs Irrisponsible Karna + Bharagavastra

And this is no brainer. Just remember what happened when they were initiated. What worked and what failed. Karna initiated Bhargavastra on a weaker opponent unprovoked. No wonder no warrior of repute was hurt. In case of Arjuna, he initiated on a hoard of hostile soldiers who attacked him unethically from all sides. And we all know what happened in that great war.
