What happened to Shakuni's dice after the Great War of Mahabharata?

📅 Mar 28, 2021 06:48 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Shakuni’s dice was lesser significant than Shakuni, who himself wasn’t as important as modern narratives want us to believe. The epic refers to him as a branch of the evil tree — not root and not the trunk. He was more like a minion to Duryodhana, like Sambha was to Gabbar Singh.

The only time he really played an important role was in a dice game, where he threw his skills, deceit and maya defeated Yudhishthir and won his entire kingdom, brothers and even his wife. And yet next time forced him to exile for thirteen years. While this alone is one of the most significant and effective contributions done by any other minions of Duryodhana, including Karna, that was the only occasion where Shakuni proved useful. For most of the time he had a side role.

In fact he hardly played any role in that humiliation of Pandavas that put them back into the path of revenge. In fact it would not be wrong to say his great masterstroke was rendered useless by rash and unthoughtful actions of Duryodhana, Dushasana and Karna.

The dice was never talked about later as it wasn’t some Divyastra. Just a humble dice in the hand of a wicket player. And in case you may be wondering, no, dice wasn’t made of the bones of Shakuni’s father who was healthy and always sided with Hastinapur.
