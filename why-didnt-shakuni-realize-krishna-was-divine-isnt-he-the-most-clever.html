Why didn't Shakuni realize Krishna was divine? Isn't he the most clever.

📅 Oct 26, 2020 11:09 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Its rare that in two part question both part is wrong. But I don't blame the questioner. Our modern narratives in form of TV shows and fictions influenced by them has transformed a mere minion to a super villain and a super villain into a super hero. In the very beginning of Adi Parva where Ved Vyas compared evil Duryodhana to a metaphorical tree of Passion, Shakuni is mentioned as a mere branch, not the root or the trunk.

Shakuni, in original epic has been mostly shown as a guy standing behind Duryodhana, something like Sambha to Gabbar Singh. And to consider Shakuni the evil genius is like saying Sambha was the boss because once Gabbar Singh asked his advise.

Yes, it was just one case where Shakuni took the command — the great gamble. He defeated Yudhisthir. Even this was his attempt to avoid a war which everyone knew in Kaurava's camp, not winnable. He had no role in Draupadi’s disrobing attempt.

Now coming to first part, Did Shakuni failed to recognize Krishna's divinity. No. He was one of those people who counseled Duryodha to agree to Krishna.
