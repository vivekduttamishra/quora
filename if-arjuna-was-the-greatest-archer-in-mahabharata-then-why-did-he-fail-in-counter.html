If Arjuna was the greatest archer in Mahabharata, then why did he fail in countering Narayanastra of Ashwatthamma and get defeated?

📅 Jun 25, 2020 09:30 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question stems from the center idea to discredit Arjuna as a great archer, and tries to trap him in a single thread spider web. Surely Arjuna knew sufficient archery break this thread.

But the same thread traps the question itself —

What is the quality of a great archer and what does countering Narayanastra or any astra really mean?

If there is no difference in courage and foolhardy, then Arjuna was not the greatest archer, perhaps you can call Ashwathama the greatest one who thought he alone knew Narayanastra.

Narayanastra, as per Ashwathama, would have devastated Pandavas. Did it? So who failed here? Arjuna who knew how to defuse it or Ashwathama who thought he would destroy Pandavas?
