Was it wise for Eklavya to give his thumb. imagine you are learning from internet as distance learning program like Udemy but when payment page comes It ask for your thumb or kidney something like that?

📅 May 01, 2020 09:24 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

The question uses wrong premise to understand our epic. The incident can not be compared to Udemy. Before we try to understand Eklavya or Drona or the quantum of punishment, it's important to set the context right —

  1. Udemy payment page comes before you attend the course, not after. So the question here is — If you are poor, does this grant you right to pirate and use illegal means to rob the author? Didn't author worked hard to create the course? Shouldn't piracy we punished? I am not talking about quantum of punishment here.
  2. The course you are attending is not about your survival, but ambition. You still chose to steal it. Is it not wrong?
  3. It wasn't really a udemy course, or some distance learning. It was an admission to a gurukul. Shall we move from Udemy to IIT or IIM? A student fails to secure admission. Can he gate crash or bug the center? Do you think he would go unpunished? Should he go unpunished?
  4. It wasn't a distance learning — can you become a doctor by taking a Udemy course? Should he be allowed to practice? Udemy or such course doesn't make you doctor. But you have seen from implanted spy camera that they need to cut open a human body, and you become expert in level one — cutting open. Do you also understand those intricacies that can be learnt only on ground zero?

Quantum of punishment

Now let's talk about the severity of punishment. Do you get same punishment for stealing a piece of bread and fodder scam involving crores of rupees? Should you get same punishment?

Now forget Udemy/IIT/IIM. What if it is a defence organization and what is being stolen is a military secret by someone already rejected by the organization? What do you think should now be done?
