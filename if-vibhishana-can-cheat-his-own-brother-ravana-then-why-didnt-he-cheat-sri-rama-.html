If Vibhishana can cheat his own brother (Ravana), then why didn't he cheat Sri Rama in Ramayana? Isn't that once a cheater, always a cheaters?

📅 Feb 11, 2021 07:17 AM PST  |  🌐 English

I read this question and then few answers. Sadly, while most of them are factually correct, they seem to miss the most vital points.

Before I put my answer, let me categories most other answers in two Types —

  1. Ravan was Adharmi, and he kicked Vibhishan — Vibhishan tried his best to convince him the err of his ways, and when he was kicked out, he approached Shri Rama.
  2. The popular story of Ravana’s death is incorrect and Vibhishana didn’t help Rama kill Ravana — The Amrita story is incorrect as per Valmiki Ramayana. In fact, Rama killed Ravana by hitting him in the heart rather than the naval.

So are these answers wrong? No. Both these answers are factually correct, just incomplete. Think —

  • Even if Vibhishana didn’t share the secret of Ravana’s death, does that negate the fact that he in fact went against his brother? Doesn’t that still make him Kuldrohi?
  • It may be Ravana who kicked Vibhishana out of Lanka, but does that mean Vibhishana should have joined hands with the enemy of the brother? Doesn’t that make him Kuldrohi?

WHAT IS MISSING?

I think, the fact that Vibhishan was brother to Ravana overshadows the greater issue — The Nation.

No one seems to be asking what was Vibhishan’s responsibility towards Lanka and Lankan People. It isn’t about just Ravana and his crimes; it was also about his foolhardy and the destruction of Lanka. And it is a common wisdom — A nation is always greater than an individual.

Ravana had been a pretty criminal, whose rap sheet had multiple unpardonable entries. What made him look greater than pretty was the power he possessed. Even behind those powers, he was still a low rate criminal whose crime included kidnapping, raping, ransacking etc.

A criminal that he was, his crimes were neither out of world nor were a threat to Lanka’s security. Vibhishana wasn’t in charge, had no pressing reason or power or courage to go against his brother. Does that make him righteous? Maybe not. But it was the order of the day.

But something changed this time. A large army had arrived at the gates of Lanka, commanded by an unvanquishable warrior with a known track record. One of his generals had ransacked entire Lanka, burnt it down to ashes. Time for retribution had arrived. Ravana was going to pay for his crimes — in full. There were two paths here onward —

  1. Ravana should do amends. Return the woman he abducted and save Lanka for a destruction. This would protect everyone his brother, his family and Lanka. Only if, Ravana had agreed!
  2. Having failed to convince Ravana and with it failed possibility to save everyone, Ravana tried to protect what he could have — Lanka. That is what Vibhishna did.

You may call Vibhishana a Kuldrohi, but remember his actions protected many more kulas (family) of Lanka. On the balancesheet, Vibhishana did a job worth applauding.
