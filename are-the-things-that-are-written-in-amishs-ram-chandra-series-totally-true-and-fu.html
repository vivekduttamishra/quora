Are the things that are written in Amish's 'Ram Chandra series' totally true and fully taken from the original Ramayan or are there additions by the author?

📅 Sep 08, 2021 09:23 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: NO

Ram Chandra Series is a work of fiction and conveyed by the author himself. Generous creative liberty has been taken in the series as there existed no burden to be true to authentic version. The only thing that can be said true is existence of main characters and probably the end result that is obviously yet to be written.

The story more revolves on the lines of fictional world Amish created in his first series — The Shiva Triology and carried it back in time more like a loose prequel.

As an author of an epic series myself, I don’t resent creation of fictions around the epic. Many such narratives have been added even in the past which are now considered part of the original epic. But as a fiction writer it is the responsibility of author to inform the world that they have written a fiction lest reader may get confused between authentic version and the fictional narrative and I must say, Amish has done this very well — He never claimed his work as a original or based on authentic narrative. He do, however, mentioned other variation of the epics such as Bengala, Jain and others, which vary greatly from the epic, to suggest that such works are acceptable. But even those works are not considered authentic work which till date his Valmiki Ramayana.


Some of the important purely fictional element that I can remember off my head is listed below. However, I would recommend you to stop reading here in case you haven’t finished all the published part of the book and intend to complete them. Also you should skip the next section if you want to maintain the integrity of the story and want to believe everything in their is true.

  • Manthara wasn’t a buisness women.
  • Her daughter’s rape is more inspired by Nirbhaya than authentic retelling.
  • Vishwamitra and Vashishtha certainly had a rivalry but were not peers or class mate. There didn’t represent the camps of Vishnu or the camps of Shiva. Nor Did Hanuman represented one.
  • Ravaan had raped Vedsvati and wasn’t her savior.
  • Vishwamitra is a revered Rishi and not a conspirator who would create a demon to get it killed. This is probably the worst extension of the narrative trying to distort the epic value, something not expected from an author like Amish.
