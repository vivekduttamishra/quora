Why is Karna's depiction in TV shows entirely different from that of BORI and other authentic versions?

📅 Mar 28, 2020 11:59 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Before we try to understand why Indian TV shows differ from not only BORI let us also understand that they differ from every authorative versions and ancient version of Mahabharat including Northern editions, Neelkantha commentary and Dakshinayat.

But TV Shows or contemporary literatures alone can't be blamed. There are older works from late 1900 (eg Rashmirathi by Dinkarji) that have glorified Karna. Even Neelkantha at a point showed some softness for Karna. To understand the reason we need to understand our society. By this time certain stiffness has crept in Indian caste system and situation has been made worst by British divide and rule. Liberals and socialist ideology were preaching against caste system rather than voicing against the few problems in them such as exploitation in the caste system It was a pretext to denounce ancient Indian culture. They needed to find an evidence that the caste system was a bad idea from inception and they used Karna as a case study.

The new narrative imagined Karna as a great warrior, who was abused by the society simply because he belonged to lower caste. This required creation of a symphetetic narrative — powerful but misfortunate and above all abused:

  • His mother abandoned him.
  • Guru rejected him because he belonged to lower caste.
  • Guru cursed him despite being dutiful.
  • Brahmin (upper caste) cursed him
  • Draupadi rejected him for being lowercaste.
  • Indra cheated him.
  • He was the greatest archer, friend and philanthropist.

Great narrative for garnering sympathy for a great man who was Condemned only because of his caste.

But there was a flaw in the selection —

  1. Would you really be symphetetic to a man who commanded a women to be stripped in public? So the narration was changed by TV Shows and the role of abuser attributed to Duryodhan and Shakuni. Karna was guilty of using bad words, that too were removed by newer shows. And now those who don't study Mahabharat are fooled into thinking that he was a champion of women right. An evil man becomes a champion of woman right!
  2. Would you really consider him a great friend if you knew he abandoned his friend in Gandharva battle? So TV shows created narrative which started with him being drunk to completely deleting the story.
  3. Would you consider him a worthy sidelined warrior if you knew he lost wars against Bhim, Abhimanyu, Satyaki and on multiple occasions from Arjun? This was most important as we had to show a worthy warrior was sidelined. What's so great if someone mediocre is sidelined? So TV shows continued to create narrative that he was equally powerful to Arjun and was executed defenseless.
  4. Would anyone consider him a great doner if they know he refused to give up his kavach kundal unless Indra offers him a celestial weapon? So in knew narrative he gives up his shield unconditionally and a guilty Indra offered him a celestial weapon to reduce his guilt.
  5. How will someone believe the story of exploitation of Karna, if Drona never rejected him? This would collapse their theory of an exploiting ancient society. So in modern narration Drona refuses to teach him something mentioned in 3 separate parvas of Mahabharat.
  6. What would be your opinion if you know Krishna referred him as trunk of the evil tree? So new narratives shows Krishna praising him.

All this was done to preserve the narrative against ancient Indian culture and society that drafted the caste system.

Karna actually got lucky as he was made official spokesperson against ancient Indian culture and now people who know nothing about Mahabharat think of Karna some kind of demigod. And so thoroughly they are brainwashed that they ignore every authentic references and are ready to condemn Krishna, Vyas and even the epic itself. For them only Karna matters and hell with the epic whose Karna is a small part.
