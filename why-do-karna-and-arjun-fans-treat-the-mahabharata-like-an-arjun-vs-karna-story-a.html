But unfortunately the modern narratives has reduced the story to single person saga — be it Karna, Draupadi, Arjuna or Duryodhana. And when you focus on an individual , naturally you relegate others out of focus, often applying unrealistic filters.

But the problem is when a question is raised and some present distorted fables are presented what are the choices a follower of epic has —

  1. Counter the facts and be branded as a XFan or YHater.
  2. Ignore

The second choice is easy and tempting — do nothing let people fight a silly debate. But then I realized this is state what Krishna explained in Gita Chapter 3, Shloka 22.

न मे पार्थास्ति कर्तव्यं त्रिषु लोकेषु किञ्चन |
नानवाप्तमवाप्तव्यं वर्त एव च कर्मणि || 22||

There is no duty for me to do in all the three worlds, O Parth, nor do I have anything to gain or attain. Yet, I am engaged in prescribed duties.

But then there is a problem of this inaction, that Krishna explains in the next two shlokas —

यदि ह्यहं न वर्तेयं जातु कर्मण्यतन्द्रित: |
मम वर्त्मानुवर्तन्ते मनुष्या: पार्थ सर्वश: || 23||
उत्सीदेयुरिमे लोका न कुर्यां कर्म चेदहम् |
सङ्करस्य च कर्ता स्यामुपहन्यामिमा: प्रजा: || 24||

For if I did not carefully perform the prescribed duties, O Parth, all men would follow my path in all respects.
If I ceased to perform prescribed actions, all these worlds would perish. I would be responsible for the pandemonium that would prevail, and would thereby destroy the peace of the human race.

The problem is, in ignoring just malicious attempt on destruction of our epic and its values, we would let those fables slowly replace the epic in minds of those who don't know the real epic and trut on social media and TV Shows to make their opinion. And we would be responsible for our inaction.

In this second choice, where you are quickly branded as XFan or YHater, but at least I would be true to the epic and follow Krishna.

And I don’t see there is much of a choice here.
