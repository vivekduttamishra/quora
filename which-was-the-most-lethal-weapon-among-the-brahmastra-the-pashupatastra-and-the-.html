Which was the most lethal weapon among the Brahmastra, the Pashupatastra, and the Narayanastra?

📅 Sep 24, 2021 11:58 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Before we embark on the journey to crown one of these weapon as the greatest and belittle others, we must understand that these divine weapons are very very different from modern perception of weapons, something our modern science not only hasn’t achieve to build but completely failed to even comprehend.

In my retelling of the epic through my best-seller book The Accursed God, I have explained in detail the true nature of Divyastra. While the said book is an epic fiction, the narratives of divyastras is based on study divine weapons discussed in Mahabharata, Ramayna and other epic narratives. And based on those research, these are the important aspect of a Divyastra —

  • A Divyastra wasn’t great because it could do a lot of damage, it was great because it can be used in more controlled manner that possible with weapon of modern warfare. Ashwatthama invoked divyastra to kill the unborn child in Uttara’s womb, without harming Uttara herself.
  • Those weapons had something similar to soul that made it Divya or divine. Why else would Vaishnavastra become garland to Krishna?
  • The weapon didn’t work on some fuel but on comparative strength of wielder and the opponent. This is clearly evident from the fact that Narayansastra did no damage to the opponents not willing to fight and because of the fact that Ashwatthama had misused the weapon.
  • Each of these weapons came with the prescribed condition of use. What would happen if prescribed condition is not met? It can back fire. It can prove more devastating. It can destroy the world. Again realize, the focus is in better controlling of the weapon and prevent it’s misuse.

That said, let us focus on the term “most lethal”.


WHICH IS THE MOST LETHAL OF ALL WEAPONS

That would be no doubt Pashupatastra. And this is not a mere assumption or some logical conclusion drawn on circumstantial evidence. It is the only weapon that is known to annihilate the entire universe. Again and Again. At the end of a Kalpa.

At the end of a yuga, that is employed by Pashupati to destroy all the beings.

—Udyoga Parva

‘“For the welfare of Pandu’s son, who was engaged in Saindhava’s death, the lotus-eyed and immensely energetic one spoke these words. ‘O Partha! There is a supreme and eternal weapon named pashupata.

—Jaidradh Vadha Parva

He faced the immensely generous Mahadeva himself in a battle and satisfied him.683 He thus obtained the extremely terrible pashupata, the great weapon that can destroy the three worlds.

—Karna Vaddha Parva

No other weapon has a history or track record even remotely close.
