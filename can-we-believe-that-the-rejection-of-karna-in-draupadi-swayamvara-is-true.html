Beyond your belief, there is sufficient evidence that Draupadi’s rejection is completely fake. You may read my detailed post on Had Draupadi Really Stopped Karna from participating in Swaymvar?

That said, here is a short summary and common refutation —

  • Those who criticize BORI don’t realize that BORI itself is based on ancient authentic scriptures available.
  • There are just five manuscripts out of a total of a1259 manuscript that BORI considered that include Draupadi’s rejection.
  • The most famous source of this rejection is Neelkantha edition, which is a reputable source. But same Neelkantha edition contradicts this story by clearly mentioning Radheya Karna’s failure.
  • The ancient Bengali recitations don’t include Karna’s rejection. Saying that the Bengali translation of Neelkhantha includes is like baiting on the highlight of a match after baiting on the match itself.
  • Few people falsely claim that rejection in included in ancient Jaimini recitation. It is a fake claim because Adi Parva of Jaimini recitation isn’t available anywhere. Only Ashwamegha Parva is available. Such blatant lies only go on to prove that those who use references like these to support Karna rejection theory are either ignorant or do it with malicious intent.
  • Apart from the fact that BORI CE, the rejection is explicitly present in Neelkantha, KMG, Sourthern recitation, Bengali Recitation, Sarda recitations and one of the most ancient script Nepali N3 editions. This is mentioned by researcher, scholar and chief editor of BORI here —

Unfortunately, this melo­dramatic interlude, to judge by the documentary evidence, appears to be the handiwork of a very late Vyāsaīd, as it is found only in K * Ñ2 Dn D2.4. c, that is, one manuscript of the K group, one Nepali manuscript, and three composite Devanāgarī manuscripts, besides the Nīlakaṇṭha version! All of these are late and inferior or conflated manuscripts.

It is missing, on the other hand, not only in theŚāradā version and the Southern recension (as in the case of many of the interpolations of the Vulgate ), but for once, also in the entire Bengali version.

— Page LXI, Prologomena of Vishnu S. Suthankar

  • We can see that the views of Prof. Suthankar is not based on his editorial work BORI CE, but on acient recitations.
  • It is also important to note that the rejection is logically ludicrous on following ground apart from known references —
    • How would a lady arrogant enough to reject a King, would silently marry an unmarked poor young man?
    • If you think she married him because he was a Brahmin, think again. Even Drupada wasn’t sure if the person was actually a Brahmin. He himself said he wasn’t aware if he was a Brahmin or a Sudra! And yet he couldn’t refuse him a chance. How could anyone have rejected Karna who was explicitly mentioned by Dhristhdumnya as a respected guest?
    • If you trust Neelkhantha, how can someone fail in a swyamvara if he is refused a participatation.
  • Some say Karna that failed swaymavara was some other Karna. That is other face saving excuse —
    • Neelkantha Mahabahrata clearly mentions ‘Radheya Karna’
    • Dhirtrashtra too, had a son named Karna. But neither was he present in the swyamvara nor considered as a prominent person to be explictly named. Dhristdumnya explicitly names all the sons of Dhritrashtra who participated in swymavara.
    • Dhristdyumna also mentions names of other guests extensively. But it doesn’t include other Kings.

S U M M A R Y

  • BORI excluded Karna’s rejection because —
    • Very few ancient references
    • Out of context story without rationale reasoning.
  • Ancient Neelkhantha that includes rejection story also includes Karna’s failure explicitly.
  • Other references often presented, such as Jaimini recitation, are fake references to bring in the confusion.
  • NO MATTER YOU TRUST BORI, NEELKANTHA OR YOUR COMMON SENSE, DRAUPADI DIDN’T REJECT KARNA.

If, even now, you want to believe Draupadi rejected Karna, you are free to believe. But then, as I said, don’t trust what you believe.
