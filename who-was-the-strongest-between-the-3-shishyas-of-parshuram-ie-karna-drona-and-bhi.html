Who was the strongest between the 3 shishyas of Parshuram ie Karna, Drona and Bhishma? Give reason with proof.

📅 Jan 17, 2021 12:24 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Bhishma without a doubt.

There is no denying that Bhishma was the strongest of the pupils of Parshurama. And the entire epic is a testimony to it.

Bhishma

Bhishma was the great old man of Aryavart, a man firm on his words and conviction, and followed his dharma without putting conditions in small print. Here are some of his greatest achievements, which are unparallel.

  1. He defeated great Parshurma — There is just one other person in the history of humanity who can say this — Great Shri Ramchandra. This alone is a sufficient proof of his being the strongest and worthy disciple of his guru.
  2. He was the longest service commander-in-chief of Kaurava’s army — Bhishma held the helm of Kaurva’s army for 10 days and made victory almost impossible for Pandavas. The other two contenders couldn’t hold the fort together for as many days. They were out in 5 and 2 days, respectively. And it wasn’t some weapon or armour of Bhishma that worried Pandavas. It was Bhishma himself.
  3. He made Krishna take up weapon — And this perhaps is the greatest achievement. He fought so vigorously made Pandavas so hopeless of the victory, that Krishna himself had to take up the bow much against his own resolve, and that is something no one else could achieve.

Dronachayra

Dronacharya was a great teacher and produces some of the finest warrior of the era and his disciples are the testimony of his calibre. While he was a great teacher, that doesn’t make him a great warrior by default.

We don’t see Drona in a battle mode anytime before Matasya (Virata) battle where Arjuna easily vanquished him along with other great warriors (Bhishma included).

Next we don’t see any extraordinary achievement from him even during Kurukshetra war. Once he assumed the role of the Kaurva’s commander, he refrained any direct confrontation from Arjuna and wasted first two valuable days waiting for the Arjuna to be away from the battle field. Third day he got Abhimanyu killed in a gruesome and cowardly way.

On day 14, not only he couldn’t protect Jaidradh from the wrath of Arjuna and his army suffered incomparable losses at the hand of Pandvanas and Ghatotkatch, but also made the demolished any hope of Kauravas victory. Both Duryodhana and Dhritrashtra lost any of their remaining hope. He died next day.

Karna

Karna can boast of greater achievement than Drona although he had the shortest tenure as the army chief among the three. He killed Ghatotkatch using Indra’s vasvi shakti and prevented a great devastation for already broken Kaurava ranks. He became the army chief after Kaurava’s had already lost any hope for victory and the sixteenth day of the battle further confirmed the same as Arjuna demolished the entire Kaurava rank reminding them, they had no reprive.

Apart from the two day stint as Army chief of Kaurava and participation in grusome killing of Abhimanyu, Karna lost several battles including those against Arjuna and Gandharvas. And this certainly doesn’t give much credit to him as a warrior or contender for the post of the strongest desciple of Bhagwan Parshuram.
