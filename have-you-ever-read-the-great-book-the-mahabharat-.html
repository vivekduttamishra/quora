Have you ever read the great book " The Mahabharat "?

📅 Apr 30, 2020 12:39 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Yes I have the fortune of reading the great book Mahabharat. In fact I have read several different editions —

  1. Neelkantha edition — This version along with other critical work is published in Sanskrit Hindi by Geeta Press in six volumes each having over 1500 pages.
  2. Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) — Is famous English translation, again mostly based on Neelkantha edition unlike first reference which also includes parts of Dakshinayat.
  3. BORI Edition — Often referred to as the critical edition of Mahabharat, is based on a research comprising almost all available works and consolidating differences. An English translation by Bibek Debroy is what I have read.

Apart from them, I have also read a short but to the point and a claimed work of Rajgopalachari, which is mostly based on Dakshinayat.

This study however leads to uncomfortable realization of interpolation and fictionalization done by most TV Shows and many modern fictions like Mrityunjay, works of Mr Anand Neelkanth and Dev Dutt Patnayak. In some extreme cases the very spirit of epic is Lost.

As a protest to this I have written my own epic fiction — The Accursed God which is retelling same epic. While, I too have included the element of fiction, I have ensured the true spirit of Mahabharat is not lost.
