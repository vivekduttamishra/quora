Why is Karna called Vrisha?

📅 Jun 06, 2021 11:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is an interesting question. Vrisha means, Dharma. And a follower of Dharma should be called Vrisha. But Vrisha isn’t an adjective here. It’s a noun. And when some one names his child, it reflects their aspirations and the child may or may not grow up to be worthy of his name — nevertheless the name sticks with him.

One of the rapist of Nirbhaya was ‘Ram Singh’. Now can we ask, why was he called Ram? His parent may have desired him to grow up like Ram. But Alas…

Some may quote Krishna’s narrative about his name from Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva —

O tiger among men! However, though that spear has now gone, I swear to you truthfully that he is incapable of being killed by anyone other than you. He is devoted to brahmanas. He is truthful. He has engaged in austerities. He is devoted to his vows. He is compassionate towards his enemies. It is for these reasons that Karna is known as Vrisha.2

—BORI, CE

Before we consider this statement of Krishna as the ultimate proof let us also remember that, Krishna also said — with Kavacha and Kundala no one in the three world could have defeated him, despite knowing that Karna was vanquished by Arjuna and Gandharvas even when he had is armour. If you see carefully here is the profound wisdom of Krishna — People may prove themselves as worthy of the greatness bestowed on them or they may fail to reach that greatness. While Kavacha and Kundala made him theoretically invincible, Karna actually lost battles after battle. Similarly, while his geneorsity could be considered as a justification for a name Vrisha, same Krishna also called him evil-souled that is exactly opposite of Vrisha.

It is interesting to note that all many things that Krishna said as the reason for his name Vrishna have no proof or cross references —

  • He is truthful — But the fact is he had been deceitful and conspirator and wasn’t truthful to even his best friend.
  • He has engaged in austerities — There is not a single reference of Karna performing any penance other than donation during the sun worship.
  • He is devoted to his vows — Often contradicted each other. His vow to Kunti was in contradiction to that given to Duryodhana. Despite of his vows he didn’t participate in Kurukshetra battle for first ten days.
  • He is compassionate towards his enemies — There is not a single reference where he has been compassionate towards the enemy.

What does all this mean — Krishna was hinting at a possibility.

Origin of the name Vrisha

Karna was called Vrisha from the begining. It is mentioned in his origin story in Kundala Harana Parva —

The boy was clad in expensive armour and wore golden earrings. On seeing this, the brahmanas gave him the name of Vasushena.32 Thus the infinitely valorous one came to be the son of a suta and was known by the name of Vasushena. The lord was also known as Vrisha.

— Kundala Harana Parva

Now let me give you another reference as to why Karna’s name Vrisha doesn’t mean much beyond being a name. It is from the Adi Parva.

‘“Thereupon, the evil-minded Duryodhana, with Karna and the son of Subala,13 tried to banish them and oppressed them in various ways. That evil son of Dhritarashtra gave poison to Bhima with his food, but the warrior Vrikodara14 digested it. One day, the evil one tied the sleeping Vrikodara on the banks of the Ganga and throwing Bhima into the water went away to the city. But when the son of Kunti woke up, he tore the ropes with which he was tied with his strong arms and Bhima’s pains disappeared. While he was asleep, he was bitten everywhere in his body by black snakes with virulent poison, but that destroyer of enemies did not die. However, in all this oppression, the great-souled Vidura was always on guard, to neutralize the evil plans and save them from oppression. As Shakra15 ensures happiness in heaven and the world of living beings, thus did Vidura always ensure happiness for the Pandavas. When the Pandavas were not killed through all these means, open and hidden, since they were protected by fate and destiny, he16 consulted his advisers: Vrisha, 17 Duhshasana and the others. With Dhritarashtra’s consent, he had a house of lac built.

—Adi Parva, BORI CE

Note, here we see a Vrisha, who is involved in conspiracy to burn down a family of five prince and their hapless mother. Could this be the reason, the person is called Vrisha? By the way do you know who is this Vrisha? Well you guessed it — He is the same person whom Krishna referred as Vrisha. I am wondering what great deeds did he perform to become Vrisha!
