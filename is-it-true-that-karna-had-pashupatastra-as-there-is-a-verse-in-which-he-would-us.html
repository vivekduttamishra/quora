Is it true that Karna had Pashupatastra as there is a verse in which he would use it against Yudhishthira? If yes, when did he get it?

📅 Mar 19, 2021 12:12 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: No.

The source is irrefutable — Mahadeva Shiva — the owner of this weapon.

‘The illustrious god said, “O Pandava! I will give you the great pashupata weapon, dear to me. You will be capable of bearing, releasing and withdrawing it. The great Indra, Yama, the king of the yakshas, 292 Varuna and Vayu do not know it. How can it be known to humans?

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 5482-5484).

I think this should settle the debate.


WHAT ABOUT THE REFERENCE THAT SAYS KARNA HAD PASHUPATASTRA?

But then there is an apparent contradiction, which makes people wonder. Here is one such contradiction, when Krishna says —

Pandaveya Yudhishthira will be killed. Karna will urge the immensely strong sons of Dhritarashtra towards Partha. He will shroud the king with sthunakarna, indrajala and pashupata and the maharathas will follow him.539 O descendant of the Bharata lineage! It is my view that the king must be in distress. At a time when speed is of the essence, the Panchalas and the Pandavas, supreme among all wielders of weapons, are following him.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 3435-3438).

But can we take the words of Krishna lightly? You need to understand, why Krishna says what he says. They are often motivating and call for action and may not always be factual. Why?

Let us re-read the statement. Krishna says Karna will kill Yudhishthir. Does that mean Karna was lying to Kunti and he would have killed him? Or Krishna was projecting a gloomier picture to motivate Arjuna into slaying Karna. Maybe the next reference will prove the true intetions of the Krishna. It is part of the same dialog of Krishna where he later says —

If you desire fame, the kingdom and supreme happiness, make efforts to kill him, with all the evil-souled ones who are with him. O bull among the Bharata lineage! You have controlled your soul. Glance towards your own self. Radheya is firm in his hatred for the great-souled Yudhishthira. Accomplish the end that Radheya has set for himself. O noble one! Set your mind on fighting and repel that leader of rathas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 3459-3462).

  • Karna hates Yudhisthir.
  • Put an end to him.

Krishna knows Arjuna would be motivated to kill Karna, if he sees mortal danger to Yudhishir. Therefore knowing that Karna had taken an oath not to kill Yudhishir Krishan still paints a gloomy picture and induces a sense of urgency to kill Karna.

Let me present you one more reference from the same dialog stretch as this one —

O Partha! Duryodhana is the king of all the worlds. He is armoured and is following the king with an array of chariots. With his brothers, the powerful one desires to kill that tiger among men.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 3417-3418).

  • Now was Duryodhana really the king of all the world? Forget all the world, was he the king of anywhere?

CAN WE BE SURE THAT KARNA DIDN’T HAVE PASHUPATASTRA

Yes. There are two important proofs. First is Karna’s own statement where he awed at the fact that Arjuna has Pashupata. He also clearly mentions that he doesn’t have a counter for them. Here is the reference —

He faced the immensely generous Mahadeva himself in a battle and satisfied him.683 He thus obtained the extremely terrible pashupata, the great weapon that can destroy the three worlds. The various guardians of the world assembled and gave him weapons that have no measure. The lion among men swiftly slew in battle the assembled kalakhanja asuras. In Virata’s city, alone on a chariot, he defeated all of us who were assembled there.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4372-4375).

The various guardians of the world gave him weapons that have no measure. Do you need a more clearer proof?

You must also take a note of another point. Karna used all the weapons at his disposal at some point of time. During the battle he used Brahmastra, Bhargavastra and Vasvi Shakti. The question is why didn’t he ever used Pashupata? You must also realize that the pre-condition for firing Vasvi Shakti was that you have exhausted all other possibilities. Does that mean he tried Pashupata before trying Vasvi Shakti? And the weapon that could destroy the entire universe, failed to kill Ghatotkatch!


A Short Note on Pashupatastra

The following section would prove beyond reasonable doubt that only Arjuna had Pashupat weapon. Let me remind you the sequence of events that led to acquiring this magnificant weapon —

  1. Arjuna performs a strict penance for years to please Mahadeva. Did anyone else did the same?
  2. Shiva tested Arjuna for the eligibility and granted him Pashupat only when he was satisfied of his ability? Do you see any parallel?
  3. Shiva himself trains Arjuna in the usage of Pashupat weapon. Again is there any parallel?
  4. This entire incident took place in Vana parva, when Pandavas were in exile.

But the story doesn’t end here…

When Arjuna swore a terrible oath to kill Jaidradh, Krishna advises him to seek Pashupata from Mahadeva. Again.

‘“For the welfare of Pandu’s son, who was engaged in Saindhava’s death, the lotus-eyed and immensely energetic one spoke these words. ‘O Partha! There is a supreme and eternal weapon named pashupata. With this, the great god Maheshvara killed all the daityas in battle. Had you known about it today, you would have been able to kill Jayadratha tomorrow. So that you may obtain the knowledge, think about the god with the bull on his banner in your mind.45 As his devotee and with his favours, you will obtain that great object.’ On hearing Krishna’s words, Dhananjaya touched water. He seated himself on the ground and single-mindedly fixed his mind on Bhava.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 1343-1348).

Just having this great weapon is not enough. You need to meditate again and seek Shiva’s grace. Is there any reference of Karna or Drona performing similar act? Did either of them followed Shiva’s advice related to initiation of the weapon?


SUMMARY

  • Shiva clear announced no one possesses his terrible weapon.
  • No one passed eligibility test laid down by Shiva.
  • Krishna’s statements were to provoke Arjuna to kill Karna. He said several things that he didnt’ mean for fact.
  • Karna never used Pashupata and himself admitted that he doesn’t have any weapon parallel to Arjuna’s Pashupat.
  • Even after having Pashupat, you still needed to medidate and take Shiva’s blessings before using them.
  • All these things point out to one single peace of truth — no one else had Pashupat.
