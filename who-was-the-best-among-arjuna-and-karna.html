Who was the best among Arjuna and Karna?

📅 Oct 24, 2021 08:53 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Before we can answer this question that has been asked and answered a hundred times, we must attempt another very important question —

WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN A WARRIOR AND A FIGHTER?

Krishna says in Gita — Among the wielders of weapon, I am Rama!

Why Rama? Why not Ravana or Meghnaada, or Hiranyakashayapa, or Tarkasura or even Lord Kartikeya or Devi Kaali? Because Raama alone knew when not to arm his weapons. He knew more than anyone else the value of restraints. And that makes him a true warrior.

Let us take another example — Do we ever ask if a Lion is a great warrior or not? We may say he a great fighter, but never call him a warrior? So what is the difference between a warrior and a mere fighter?

Take more examples — do you respect those terrorists the way you admire a brave solider? What is the difference? They both learn to use the arm at almost the same age, and a terrorist may have a greater experience, may have even more lethal aim. But does that make them a warrior?

The difference is — a fighter lives for fighting. where as a warrior avoids a war if he can. A fighter fights because he seeks some kind of wealth, power, glory and the very idea of fight thrills them. On the other hand, a warrior fights for a cause, he fights with ethics, and for a goal greater than personal glory. He doesn’t fight for the sake of fighting or glory.


Ok, I am still not ready to answer the question. Just for one more moment—

WHY BEING A WARRIOR MATTERS?

How does a warrior who lived and died over five thousand years matter today? Why should we care, who was more complete warrior? Do we What is the takeaway for us? Power? Profit? No. Just a lesson for humanity.

Now what is the value of a warrior, if he never stopped to ask what is he fighting for? How would it matter if you never strived for a goal greater than some lust for personal recognition. If you never cared for anything other than you and your inner circle, why would anyone, but a complete fool care for your and your glory?

Closed your eyes and think who are those people who left their permanent foot mark on history in a good way? Chankaya, Chandragupta, Buddha, Ashoka, Vikramaditya, Maharana Pratap, Prithviraj Chauhan, Chandrashekhar Azad, Mahatma Gandhi. What is common in all of them? None of them lived for a personal glory. They sought higher goals.

—

Ok, let us finally move to the question —

WHO WAS A MORE COMPLETE WARRIOR — ARJUNA OR KARNA?

The answer is easy to find in a few simple questions —

  • One of them lied to his guru for his person ambition of amassing weapon and recognition as the world’s greatest archer, other saves his guru’s life. Who was a greater warrior?
  • One of them was part of a crowd that tried to burn down a new bride. The other moves forward to protect her from a hoard of killers. Who is a more complete warrior?
  • One of them flees leaving his best friend field for himself and his family against a powerful foe, the other rushes to protect his worst enemy. Are both of them warriors?
  • One of them schemes to plunder a kingdom is distress, the other rushes to protect the kingdom against the looter. Single handed. Can you compare the two?
  • One of them refused to fight on behalf his best friend because his ego was hurt, and he thought he won’t get personal glory. The other one too refused to fight even for justice and personal glory to avoid bloodshed and protect humanity.
  • One couldn’t muster sufficient ruthlessness to kill his relatives and Krishna had to pursue him to realize his duty, other rejected Krishna’s offer with an open proclamation that he won’t hesitate to kill one of his own brother, while generously sparing the other four. Who was a great warrior.

I won’t waste my time comparing the relative power of arsenals. Arsenals don’t make warrior, the strength of character does.
