Without divine weapons, who will win between Arjun and Karn?

📅 Dec 20, 2021 12:20 AM PST  |  🌐 English

This question takes the form of supposition, as if we need to reach upon a conclusion based on circumstantial evidence, as if there is no proof that exists.

The reality is there is nothing to suppose here. Arjuna won against Karna, both directly and indirectly, without any divine weapon.

A. Gurudakshina Battle

During Gurudakshina Parva It was Arjuna who won the swyamvar. It is clearly mentioned in BORI. KMG mentions Karna’s failure explicitly. But even if you ignore KMG, the question is if Arjuna won the battle, what was Karna’s role? There can be three scenarios —

  1. Trust KMG that doesn’t contradict BORI. Karna fought and lost.
  2. Karna fought the battle as a subordinate to Arjuna and didn’t perform great enough to be remembered for that battle.
  3. Karna didn’t fight the battle and like a coward, selfish person ditched him right after he made him the King.

So essentially we need to chose if Karna fought and underperformed or didn’t fight and showed his selfish coward side at the beginning.

B. Draupadi’s swaymver battle

This is a battle where Arjuna and Karna had their first face-off. Forget divine weapon, Arjuna didn’t have his regular weapon. He had the swaymvar bow that was too heavy to even lift. Yet he fought so vigorously that Karna not only retreated scared but also declared Arjuna to be equivalent to personification of art of war, Vishu, Indra and his own guru Rama.

Now few blind fans do want to prove that Karna was winning as other losers cheered for him, forgetting what that the great feat they cheered about was Karna repulsed Arjuna’s arrow. He didn’t defeat him. He didn’t even pierce him or made him circumvent.

And apart from an explicit mention that Karna retreated ‘scared’, don’t forget he compared Arjuna to his own guru. Now do you want to say Karna was such a lowly beast that he would compare a losing opponent to his guru? Was that the limit of his respect for the guru. Nah! Even I don’t think evil Karna was low.

But the fact that one of them left scared and the other succeeded in protecting his side from brutal and agonizing death leaves no room for doubt except in minds filled with wishful thinking.

Now coming to the supposition —

WHY KARNA COULD NEVER HAVE DEFEATED ARJUNA WITHOUT DIVINE WEAPON?

Let us understand, weapons don’t make a true warrior — the courage and purpose does. One with impure objective won’t win. And if they do, it won’t matter.

Draupadi’s Swaymvar Battle

During Draupadi’s swaymvar, Arjuna knew he was fighting Karna. He knew he wasn’t carrying his weapons. And yet he encountered Karna. He wasn’t afraid because he had no special weapon or from the fact he was fighting Karna. He had a noble objective — protect the life of those who were unreasonable condemned for a brutal death.

Now think about Karna. He entered the battle to inflict brutal death to Drupad and Draupadi? Was his mission pure? Reasonable? He fought thinking Arjuna to be a normal person. And yet he fought to vanquish him with all his might. And once he realized he couldn’t defeat him retreated ‘scared’. Now ask, why Karna retreated despite having invincible armor and a well-known fact that he couldn’t die as long he had it on him? A little reflection, and you would realize — weapon or divine artifacts may make a person strong, but not brave. And cowards don’t win.

Kairata Parva and Ghosayatra Parva

In Kairata Parva, Arjuna faces great Mahadeva. Victory was out of question. No one faces Mahadeva and lives to tell the tale. But still Mahadeva not only proclaims him the greatest warrior of the era, and entrusts him with his most potent weapon Pashupata, he also opens the door of heaven for Arjuna. So, why did great Mahadeva bestow such an honor to a loser?

Because winning is not everything. How you face a certain defeat determines your courage and true character. Arjuna was thrashed badly and had no hope of winning the war. And yet, he stayed put and didn’t run away. Had he run away…

Now contrast with Ghosha yatra. Karna couldn’t face Gandharvas. Now all Gandharvas taken together and multiplied with infinity couldn’t be equated to Mahadeva. Can they be? Karna still had his divine protection on. And yet he fled. Could Gandharvas had killed him? NO. So why did he ran away? He himself said he got afraid and ran away to save himself. He deserted even his best friend and their family to die.

And that was not the first time. Nor was the last time. Once again, he let his own brother die in Matasya battle as he ran to save his life. He had all his divine weapon with him. On thirteenth day he again ran away from Abhimanyu and letting him kill another of his brother. So why did he let his brothers die? Once again it is the lack of the same courage which sustained Arjuna.

WHY WANTED DIVINE WEAPONS

Arjuna never cared or preserved any specific divine weapon to fight Karna. He fought him battles without caring about divine weapons. In fact he killed Karna using an ordinary Anjalika arrow which wasn’t any special divine weapon. I would like this idea to sink in. Arjuna not only defeated but killed Karna without using divine weapon.

On the other hand, though out his life, Karna tried to secure divine weapons to fight Arjuna as if he was sure he couldn’t win Arjuna without having them. He wanted Brahmastra from Drona because he wanted to defeat Arjuna. He cheated Parshurama, because of the same reason. Later he exchanged his divine armour for vasvi shakti hoping to use it on Arjuna some day. And as a fail safe measure he worshipped a snake called Ashwasena all his life so that he may use him some day to kill Arjuna. Now why would he preserve a divine weapon unless he was sure that his own might won’t be sufficient?

Finally before his death, Karna admitted what he always knew — Arjuna was the greatest warrior of the era.
