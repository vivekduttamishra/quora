If Arjuna was such a kind-hearted man, then why did he forcefully abduct Shubadra? Is there more to the story?

📅 Jun 20, 2020 11:34 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Some recent blog post on the print, promoting a fiction, has given rise to this fresh attack on the epic Mahabharat — Arjuna abducted Subhadra. The parent article compared Subhadra’s abduction with that of Sita’s abduction in Ramayana.

This question arises simply because of the word abduction without understanding the context. So let us first check the exact extract where

Arjuna abducts Subhadra —

Kunti’s son rushed at her and forced her onto his chariot. Abducting the sweet-smiling one, that tiger among men set out for his own city on his chariot,

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata(Kindle Locations 15143-15144).

Wow, what a smiling reaction on the forceful abduction! This is quite parallel to Rukmini’s abduction and not like Sita’s.

Now let us consider what transpired

Before Abduction

Before Abduction Krishna and Arjuna discuss the marriage proposal. Here is Two suggestions of Krishna

Statement#1 — Krishna-Aruja’s Dialog

If your mind is set on her, I can speak to my father myself.” Arjuna replied, “She is Vasudeva’s daughter and Vasudeva’s sister. She is beautiful. How can I not be captivated? If this daughter of the Vrishni lineage, your sister, becomes my wife, I must certainly have performed only good deeds.

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata (Kindle Locations 15125-15128).

Statement#2 — Krishna-Aruja’s Dialog

O Partha! But that is uncertain if one doesn’t know the inclination. Those who are learned in the ways of dharma say that for kshatriyas, who are warriors, abduction for marriage is permissible. O Arjuna! Therefore, abduct my beautiful sister. Who knows what she might do in a svayamvara?”

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata (Version 2) (Kindle Locations 15129-15131).

Here we must note the most important things —

We are talking incidents dating back to over 5000 years. In that era —

  • Abduction for marriage is permissible.
  • The above statement is apparent from —
    • Rukmini’s abduction — by her consent
    • Kashi Sisters’ abduction — without their consent
  • Marriage was a way to forge alliances. Krishna was worried about what happens if she marries someone like
    • Duryodhana (A very common theory found in Bhagwat Puran)
    • Shishupal
  • In such cases, Krishna may not be able to vanquish them and esablish Dharma (Although I believe no relation could have obstructed Krishna’s path to establish Dharma)

Who can contemplate the giving away of a daughter, as if she were an animal? Which man on earth would like to sell his daughter? I think Kunti’s son saw these blemishes in the other methods.

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata (Kindle Locations 15181-15183).

  • The prime question for Krishna was — Let her sister marry her choice that may be an Adharmi or marry her to the best among lot, Arjuna, without knowing her choice. What would you choose for your sister?

Wasn’t Abduction brutal for Subhadra, in person?

Let us see what happened after the abduction —

‘Restrained by them, Arjuna returned and married Subhadra. Kunti’s son lived there for a year and the lord spent the last part of his period 3 in Pushkara.

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata (Kindle Locations 15191-15192).

Does this sound like a tormented Subhadra?

They later return to Indraprastha.

Bhadra quickly went and paid homage to Droupadi, saying, “I am your maid.” Then Krishna arose and embraced Madhava’s* sister and lovingly said, “Let your husband not have a rival.” With a happy heart, Bhadra replied, “May it be that way.” O Janamejaya! The maharatha Pandavas were then happy in their hearts. Kunti was also extremely delighted.

Veda Vysa. The Mahabharata (Kindle Locations 15200-15204).

So can we see Shubhadra’s mind here? Can we see if Arjuna was kind hearted or not?

More References to Abduction Marriage

  • Was Gandhari’s marriage by abduction?
    • Strictly speaking, NO.
    • Was it stricktly in favour of Gandhari?
    • Which marriage you think was better — Shubhadra’s or Gandhari?
  • How is swyamber better?
    • Were Draupadi or Sita had any choice or freewill in their Swaymber?
    • Were these Swaymbers better than Subhadra’s or Rukmini’s marriage?

Contemporary Context

In India, even today, we have the concept of arranged marriage, where the parent choses whom to marry their daughter. How would that differ from an abduction?

Common cord between — Subhadra, Sita, Draupadi and Arranged Marriage

  • Parent/Guardian had best interest of the girl in the mind.
  • They tried to marry their daughter/ward to the best candidate — Lord Rama, Arjuna etc.
  • They favored daughters’ well-being over their wishes.

Was Arjuna kind hearted?

  • He politely asked Krishna for the hand of Shubhadra
  • He seeks permission from Dharmraj Yudhishthir
  • He politely returns to Dwarka for marriage
  • Pandava’s give due respect to Subhadra once she returns to Hastinapur
  • Her grandson finally rules Hastinapur.

Based on a comment that asks — It was Shubhadra's choice. Why Krishna didn't consult her?

Here are a few points —

  • Though Mahabharata doesn't elaborate, Bhagwat Puran clearly indicates Shubhadra's inclination for Arjuna. Mahabharata doesn't contradict it.
  • We have no evidence if Krishna consulted Shubhadra or not, but Shubhadra reaction is clearly favours Shubhadra consent. She nevers resents her abduction.

But I agree, it was Shubhadra's decision to make. Now the question is when Shubhadra never complained and even his brother has no say in it, what right any author has to portray her as something she was not?
