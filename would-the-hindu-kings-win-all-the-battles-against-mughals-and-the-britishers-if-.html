Would the Hindu kings win all the battles against Mughals and the Britishers if they followed the Chakravyuh strategy as in the Mahabharata?

📅 Mar 14, 2020 10:16 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

I won't be so certain.

One sure reason of failure would be not to evolve over ages.

  1. Chakravyuh that worked for a battle on a plane may not work on other terrains.
  2. Mughals and Britishers never fought with honor — initially both were more looters and finally established their empire for loot.
  3. Chakravyuh is a great lesson where Drona had to deploy all his greatest generals — its a lesson in United army — something the Maharana of Mewar or Prithviraj never had.
  4. Indian rulers didn't lose because of their war formation but because of the traitors like Mir Zafar, Jaichand, raja Man Singh of Jaipur and Shindhias. No army could have won Chittorgarh without a traitor, no Gauri without Jaichand and no Britisher without the like of Shindhias.
