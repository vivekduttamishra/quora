How close was India to attaining "Swaraj", when Gandhiji called off the non-cooperation movement due to the incident at Chauri Chaura?

📅 Dec 02, 2020 07:06 PM PST  |  🌐 English

India would have been quite farther from swaraj had the movement not been called off after chauri chaura.

You need to understand the context and significance of non-cooperation movement —

  • It was the first nation wide movement that had truly transcended boundaries of region, language and culture. The entire nation was responding to a call and making Britishers nervous. It had already suceeded in its primary mission to bring people together.
  • The movement was non-violent at its core. This was extremely important not just as an ideology but also as a strategy. It had made British tactics of violence ineffective. Any violent action from their side had global repercussions. The world was watching.
  • It started as a protest against a senseless violence of Jalian Wala bag, and incident which makes British apologistic even after a hundred years. Had the movement continued after another such mindless violence, we would have not only lost our moral right to talk from moral high ground, but also have given Britishers the opening they were looking forward, and crush the movement violently. They were better prepared to crush violent rebellion than a non-violent one. Jalianwala massacre may have been equated and cancelled against chauri chaura.
  • In calling off the movement Gandhiji showed a moral high ground and daring, British government failed to for their heinous act.
  • The movement was called off at our terms. While movemet was called off, it kept the hope for future alive — civil disobedience movement, Quit India movement. Had it been crushed a demoralized nation won't have rebelled for another half a century. Remember what happened after 1857.
  • As I said earlier, the movement was a success in uniting and energyzing the masses. Even young boys of about 12-14 years became part of national movement, and young Chandrashekhar, beaten by hunters for participating in the movement went on to redeem his position in history as the legendary Chandrashekhar Azad, the commander in chief of the HSRA. He chose a different path isn't important in the current context, what is important is the impact of the movement in masses.
  • Had it not been called off…
