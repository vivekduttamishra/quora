How would Mahabharata have gone if Karna had killed Arjuna instead of stopping his arrow because of the impending sunset?

📅 May 26, 2021 03:21 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer —

‘The sun may fall down from its place. The earth may shatter into many fragments. The fire may become cold. But Karna will not be able to kill Dhananjaya.

While the above words are not mine, I implicitly trust the speaker and that answers the question.

I can say with conviction, the Mahabharata still won’t have gone in that fictional direction where this question is headed. Let us divide the answer in two parts —


Did Karna stopped his arrow because of impending sunset?

Fact — Karna didn’t stop his arrow because of impending sunset. In fact, Arjuna badly overran the entire Kaurava army at the end of the 16th day and they fled because of the sunset. This is how the actual battle happened —

Arjuna himself countered Karna’s weapons with his own weapons. He enveloped the directions, the sky and the earth with his showers of arrows. Those arrows descended like clubs and bludgeons. Some were like shataghnis and others were as fierce as the vajra. The soldiers, the foot soldiers, horses, rathas and elephants, were destroyed. They closed their eyes, uttered woes of lamentation and fled distractedly in different directions. In that battle, horses, men and elephants were destroyed. The soldiers were killed by the arrows and terrified, fled. ‘ “Thus did they engage in battle, desiring victory. The sun approached Mount Asta.278 O great king! In particular, there was darkness and dust. We could not see anything, favourable or unfavourable. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! The great archers were frightened of fighting in the night. With all their horses, they therefore withdrew.279 O king! When the Kouravas departed at the end of the day, the Parthas were delighted in their minds at having obtained victory and also left for their own camps.

—Karna Vadha Parva, Chapter 1171, BORI

And this is some of the reactions from Kaurava’s camp —

‘Dhritarashtra said, “Arjuna killed all of us easily, as he wished. I don’t think Yama would have escaped, had he attacked him in a battle.

Karna angrily spoke to them, sighing like a snake. He rubbed one hand against another hand and glanced towards your son.286 ‘Arjuna is always firm, skilled and persevering. He understands and when the time is right, Adhokshaja287 instructs him. We were deceived by him today, because of that sudden shower of weapons. O lord of the earth! But tomorrow, I will destroy all his intentions.’288

And we must remember, when Kauravas continued to fight after sunset on day 14, Karna had no qualms against that.


What if as per this baseless narrative, ative Karna had shot the arrow?

So what? Was this the first time Karna was about to shot an arrow on Arjuna? He had fired arrow before. And I daresay, that arrow would have met the same fate as other arrows of Karna. Arjuna always won. Every single time.

But if you want to insist that arrow would have done something which real Karna couldn’t not achive even once in his life, then Krishna would have intercepted it just like he intercepted Vaishnavastra or the way he protected Parikshit from Brahmastra..

What is important to understand is such trivial fictions can’t derail the core epic values. Krishna incarnated to establish Dharma! And he wanted Karna be killed.

And the biggest point in favour of Arjuna was the fact — Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah!

The fabrications are wishful thinking of admires of Karna who are trapped in their own perception and desperately trying to find some theory of alternative universe where Karna might have won.

Interestingly, Arjuna asked a similar question to Krishna on the seventeenth day and here is what Krishna said —

Krishna laughed and spoke these supreme words to Partha. ‘The sun may fall down from its place. The earth may shatter into many fragments. The fire may become cold. But Karna will not be able to kill Dhananjaya.

He further adds —

However, if this does happen, the world will be destroyed. Using my arms in the battle, I will kill Karna and Shalya.’

Hope that answers the question.
