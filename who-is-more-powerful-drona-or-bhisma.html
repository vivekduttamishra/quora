Who is more powerful - Drona or Bhisma?

📅 May 08, 2021 05:43 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Short Answer: Bhishma

Bhishma was a renowned warrior. He had defeated many kings during his time. And of course probably the greatest feat was defeating Lord Parshurama himself, who is acknowledged as one of the greatest warriors of the era. And no one can claim this credit other than great Shri Rama himself. In case of Shri Rama, however, there was no real battle.

Coming to the Kurukshetra war, Bhishma was the unanimous first choice for commanding the Kuru army and he was crowned as the commander despite of objection from Karna, who abstained from the battle as long as Bhishma was at the helm.

During the Kurukshetra battle, Bhishma fought mildly as Pandavas were his own and he never wanted to hurt them fatally something he made clear even before the battle. Despite of this self imposed restriction, he held the forte of Kuru army for ten days, that is more than every other Kuru general put together.

As long as he controlled the reign of Kaurava’s army, victory for Pandavas was infeasible and Krishna, breaking his own vow, stood up with arms against Bhishma. That is another achievement to his grand carrier.


Drona was, no doubt a one of the greatest gurus of the era and has the honor of training some of the greatest and most powerful warriors of the period. He also had the opportunity to learn from Parshurama, the art of weapons that Parshurama had donated to him.

That said, Drona was not a warrior per-se. He was a great expert of weapon who was drawn into battle.

We don’t see him fight anytime before Matasya battle, where he shared defeat with rest of the Kuru warriors by his own disciple.

We don’t see many great achievements from Drona during Bhishma’s command during the first ten days of the battle.

Drona was apparently not the first choice for commanding the army after Bhsihma was sidelined. He got the post mostly because of his seniority. During the first two days of his command, Kaurava’s didn’t achieve anything significant as Drona was reluctant to make any decisive war formation in Arjuna’s presence. He knew Arjuna would thwart all his plans.

On the thirteenth day of the battle (third day of Drona’s command), they succeeded in taking Arjuna away from the main battle and formed Chakavyuh. But his best led plan went into drain as Abhimanyu single handedly held the ground. So frustrated was Drona and so called warriors at his command that they killed Abhimanyu by disarming him from behind, destroying his weapons and armors and killed by attacking him in pack — one of the the most cowardly acts!

On the fourteenth day of the battle, they failed to control the wrath of Arjuna who fulfilled his vows by slaying Jaidradh. It wasn’t just the death of Jaidradh, but the death any hope for Kaurava’s victory.

So frustrated was the Kuru commander after being reprimand from Duryodhana that he continued the battle even during the night — and suffered the most dire consequence.

It was the bloodiest three days of the battle. Pandavas lost Abhimanyu, Ghatotkatch, Drupada and King Virata. Kuru lost any hope of victory!

Drona was killed by Dhirstduymna to fulfil his life’s mission and avenge his father’s death. Drona was tricked in believing his son was dead and as he sat in the midst of the battle ground, grieving for his son. Dhristduynma attacked and beheaded him. The act of Dhristduymna wasn’t worth praising and Arjuna tried his best to stop him. But…

Drona’s death in a way was a poetic justice. He deserved the death he gave to Abhimanyu. And it was stupid to grieve in the midst of battle field surrounded by enemies.
