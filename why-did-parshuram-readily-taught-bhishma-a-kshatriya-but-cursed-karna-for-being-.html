Why did Parshuram readily taught Bhishma a kshatriya, but cursed Karna for being a Kshatriya even though he did not know he was one?

📅 May 23, 2020 01:46 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

In question lies the answer. Why Parshuram, the 6th Vishnu, who taught Kshatriya Bhishma would curse Karna for not being a Brahmin?

Why indeed?

Parshuram didn't curse Karna for his caste, but for his deceit. Karna introduced himself as a Brahmin of Bhargava gotra. Knowledge taken by the means of deceit is as good as stealing. Why shouldn't such act be punished?

Another question — Why did Karna really lie to Parshuram?

Because he didn't trust his guru that he would teach him? Why this lack of trust? Parshuram was guru of Bhisma so only Brahmin theory is ruled out. Drona taught Karna so caste biased too is out of context.

Karna probably lied because he didn't wanted to be denied Brahmastra for same reason as his previous guru denied — his quest for personal glory and vengeance.

But mistrusting you guru is insulting him. And then I wonder what alternative Parshuram had?
