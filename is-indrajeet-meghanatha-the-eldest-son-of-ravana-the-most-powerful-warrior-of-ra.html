Is Indrajeet (Meghanatha), the eldest son of Ravana, the most powerful warrior of Ramayana?

📅 Apr 25, 2020 06:22 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Indrajeet was indeed a great fighter who even bested both Rama and Ravan. But the story of warriors goes beyond just being a winning fighter. A warrior has war ethics and for a warrior, war is the last resort. Anyone can possess a divine weapons or art of illusion. What you do with power bestowed on you defines your character, which is not easy to obtain. Let us consider other claimaint for being the greatest warrior, and then we can conclude who was the most powerful warrior of all —

Hanuman

Hanuman is invincible. He is blessed by Lord Brahma that he wont be bested by any weapon. Yes Meghnath imprisons him with Brahmastra. Was Brahma’s boon, a myth? No it was Hanuman’s humility that he allowed Meghnath to subdue him. Powers and boons often make ordinary people arrogant but it made Hanuman more humble. Does that say something about the warrior he was?

But who was more powerful in a raw battle. Hanuman sets entire Lanka a fire to show his strength. What could Meghnath and his power do to stop Hanuman?

How many warriors used their strength to save life rather than take life. Hanuman did.

Vibhishan

Had no divine weapon. He couldn’t compare the strength of Meghnath or even a lesser warrior. But think, to fight a ruthless enemy is lot easier than fight your own family. On a similar occassion, centuries later, Arjuna would put his bows aside. And Vibhishan stood against his brother to save his country. How many people were powerful enough to stood against the combined might of Ravan and Meghnath?

Lakshman

Leaving aside the comfort of his royal palace when he wasn’t supposed to is going beyond the call of duty. He fought and finally Vanquished Meghnath. You may say he fought him while he was worshippin. But can the warrior who used illusion all hi life really complain?

So who is the best warrior of the time? Well who am I to answer this question when Krishna chose his answer for the greatest warrior of all the time when he said —

Among the warriors (wielders of weapon), I am Ram!

Lord Rama

The greatest and humblest of all the warriors had the greatest qualities — patience and never weavering trust on Dharma

  • He prefers weaker Sugriv over stronger Bali — strategically wrong descision but a brave one that only a powerful warrior can dare.
  • Had the capacity to dry up entire ocean, yet he chose to find alternative ways.
  • Crossed the ocean, reached the land of Lanka and yet gave a final chance of Lanka by sendig Angad as a final attempt to avoid the war.

My answer is based on the story of Ramayan, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers Balmiki Ramayan and Ramcharit Manas apart from Ved Vyas retelling as Part of Mahabharat. I have studied the Sanskrit Hindi Version from Geeta Press The story also refers to Bhagwat Gita the secred book of Hindus
