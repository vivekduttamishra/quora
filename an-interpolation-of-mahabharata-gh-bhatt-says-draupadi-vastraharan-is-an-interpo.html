An interpolation of Mahabharata g.h Bhatt says Draupadi Vastraharan is an interpolation. Is this version authentic?

📅 Aug 09, 2020 01:21 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Consider following points —

  1. Draupadi Vastraharan is present in all ancient manuscripts without any exception.
  2. BORI has included it in the main text after over 40 years of research.
  3. Vishnu Sukthankar doesn't mention it as an interpolation.
  4. Its present in KMG, Gita Press and Southern versions.
  5. The incident has a cross reference in Shalya Parva after Duryodhan's death.
  6. Madras Oriental Research is not BORI.
  7. The only illogic to say it may be an interpolation is the fact, that most of the of the cross reference talks about Drawpadi's dragging but not explicit Vastraharan. This is illogic because —
    1. Vastraharan didn't really succeeded, so no point talking.
    2. There is a a cross reference.
    3. It is cross referenced across manuscripts.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —

  • English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG),
  • Bibek Debroy translation of Bori critical edition and
  • Neelkanth edition Sanskrit Hindi version of Gita Press.
