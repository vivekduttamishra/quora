How is it that Karna being the son of Lord Surya Dev, who is the foremost amongst the Gods & worshipped as Suryanarayna, & Karna was also the disciple of Lord Parashurama, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, still ended up hating Arjuna & Lord Krishna?

Thanks for the A2A.

This is an interesting question and goes on to prove that birth and lineage doesn’t necessarily define your character — A man is known by the company he keeps.

Karna was the son of Surya. He was born with unique Kavach and Kundala. He had potential to be great. But then he falls in bad company. Duryodhana, an ambitious young prince, with an evil mind and hate against his cousins. Karna befriends this prince and there started a shared hatred — foe of my friend is my foe. Karna supports Duryodhana in multiple conspiracies to torment and kill Pandavas.

On personal front, Karna found himself good in archery. But guru considered Arjuna better. There germinates a personal rivalry which shaped the entire personality of Karna. He wanted to be better than Arjuna. Now Karna hatred for Pandavas or friendship for Kauravas were over-shadowed by his personal ambition of besting Arjuna.

But Arjuna continues to outshine. He saves his guru from the jaws of a crocodile. His dedication and hard-work earned him Brahmashira from his guru. That invoked a surge of jealousy in Karna. He approaches guru Drona demanding the celestial weapon so that he can defeat Arjuna. Drona sees the evil design and refuses to grant his wish.

So what does Karna do? He seeks someone who can give him what he wants. He approaches Parshurama and deceives him into believing that he is a Bhargava Brahmin (same gotra as Parshurma). Parshurama suspends him on learning his desire and deceit.

Here comes a big change in Karna. He is no more just supporting Kauravas against Pandavas — It was his personal battle now. He was rejected by two gurus because of his own deceit. But for him, there was one place to vent it to — Arjuna. And before long Karna was the trunk of that evil tree called Duryodhana — he was their support system. In fact he was the prime motivator behind Draupadi Cheerharana, Ghosa Parava where they were captured by Gandharavas and Matasya battle.

Karna was born with divine Kavach and Kundal. He was immortal with them. Yet, he lost battles after battle. He fled. Again and Again despite the fact that he could have fought without worrying about death. Why? because he didn’t had moral character to stay put.

The entire life of Karna goes on to prove — bad company and personal blind ambition can ruin your potential to such an extent that it doesn’t matter how virtuious your lineage, who your guru is or what divine virtues you are bestowed with. At the end of the day your own character alone matters. period.
