Why do people assume that Abhimanyu had a celestial bow and was wearing impenetrable armour during 13th Day of the war, while it is not mentioned that he was using a celestial bow and was pierced several times?

📅 Sep 04, 2021 08:46 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: People assume things because they suite them.

And such assumptions or arguments don’t require much rationale or reasoning. They see things that don’t exist, ignore that exists. And the argument goes on.

Let us tackle this question is two parts —

1.Did Abhimanyu possess divine or celestial armor or weapon?

Short Answer: NO. At least it is not explicitly mentioned. Here is the closest mention, one whose half reading let’s people make tall claims —

The preceptor smiled and spoke gently to Karna. ‘His armour is impenetrable. He is young, swift and valiant. His father has been instructed by me on the art of donning armour. It is certain that this destroyer of enemy cities knows everything about that. 113 But with well-aimed arrows, you are capable of slicing down his bow, his bowstring, the reins of his horses and the two charioteers who guard his flanks.114 O great archer! O Radheya! If you are capable, do this. Make him retreat and strike him subsequently.

— Drona Parva, BORI

Ha! Look. Drona himself said, his armour is impenetrable. What more proof do we need?

First things first, Drona called in impenetrable not celestial or divine. So can non-celestial things be impenetrable? Drona himself answered this in the very next sentence that people conveniently ignore — There are techniques by which you can wear impenetrable armour, something which Drona taught to Arjuna who may have passed the knowledge to Abhimanyu.

There is another thing you must understand about impenetrable — it doesn’t just define the quality of thing but also the force that is trying to penetrate it. A bulletproof vest is impenetrable by a normal gun shot, but not against a AK47 rifle or a rocket launcher. Am I making assumption here? Let us take few more examples of what are referred as impenetrable in the same Drona Parva where Drona makes this statement —

On the instruction of his father’s eldest brother, Abhimanyu penetrated the impenetrable chakra vyuha in many different ways.

—Drona Parva, BORI

  • Here we have a very interesting paradox — Abhimanyu penetrated the impenetrable chakravyuha. What does this really mean? Something that was thought impenetrable can still be penetrated. Isn’t it?
  • Was the Chakravyuha celestial because it is mentioned impenetrable?

Let us take more references —

Severed arms were seen in that battle. They held weapons and were clad in finger guards. They held swords and were adorned with armlets. They were decorated in golden ornaments. They held bows and arrows. There were bodies and heads with earrings and garlands. There were thousands of these on the ground. It was impenetrable because of the decorations, seats, long poles, shattered axles and yokes, fragmented wheels and many chariots.

— Drona Parva, BORI

  • we see more decorations that were impenetrable and yet were destroyed lying on the ground.
  • Do we need more proof of what impenetrable means?

2. Why do people make such assumptions?

It is not difficult to see why, once you realize who these people are. Let me give you a hint. They don’t really have something against Abhimanyu other than the fact that he too bested their favorite icon. And they can’t tolerate the fact that a person without any divine artifact managed to thwart their icon who fled while seeing his own brother killed. To reconcile with the fact they keep offering excuses sometimes in favor of their icon and at other times against Abhimanyu and yet other times on other factor that could have made it possible.

But when it comes to the grand epic Mahabahrata, it is mentioned quite ambiguously — Abhimanyu thrashed all the so called warriors of Kauravas who couldn’t find the courage to fight him singly and as such surrounded him as a pack of hungry hyenas and killed him.

IMPORTANT CONCLUSION

What people flaunting some ludicrous arguments don’t realize is the fact that Abhimanyu, in his death, won against both their icons and them.

On one hand Abhimanyu robbed the facade and any misconstrued notion that Kaurva side warriors were carrying about themselves that they were honorable warrior — they were neither. They were beasts who were hunted down one by one.

And second, Abhimanyu proved our modern thinkers wrong. He died even if he had so called impenetrable armor. That raises two questions that I would leave you to ponder about —

  • How impenetrable was that impenetrable armor if the wearer could still sustain mortal injury? Does an impenetrable armor really have a significant value? Some may say that Abhimanyu died with a hit on his head. But the epic clearly states he was already badly injured.
  • Realizing that Abimanyu had a impenetrable armour and he died despite of it, how valueable was the impenetrable armor of Karna? Did Karna reach the same conclusion when he traded his armor for a weapon that may prove infinitely more valueable?
