Why does the "Raghupati Raghav Raja Ram Patit Paavan Sita Ram" song have Allah mentioned in it?

📅 Dec 12, 2020 04:00 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Let us first clear some confusion that exists here and elsewhere related to this question. Contrary to popular belief that Mahatma Gandhi never altered the original lyrics of this famous Ramadhuna — Ishwar Allah Tero naam has been part of the original lyric.

To the best of my knowledge and research, this bhajan doesn’t have its root in any ancient scriptures. Rather, it’s first known reference is present in the book — My Experiments by truth, by Mahatma Gandhi. It is a popular belief that Vishnu Digambar Paluskar composed the music for this song hoping it would reach the masses. And it reached the masses who liked the song.

Now coming to the question —

Why it contains the word Allah?

Mahatma Gandhi was an adherent Hindu. And the most important trait of a believer of Sanatana Dharma is to accept that God is all prevailing, excluding none from his folds — not even those who don’t believe in you. And probably the greatest example of this doctrine is Buddha. Buddha has been one of the greatest critic of sanatan Hindu culture. And how did Sanatan Dharma responded? By proclaiming that Buddha was the ninth incarnation of Vishnu. And why not? After all didn’t Shri Krishna himself proclaimed that he would manifest in every era for the welfare of good and for the elimination of evil?

This Bhajan is one of the greatest proclaimation and dare to indicate the all encompassing existing of the ultimate divinity — Shri Rama. Rama, the master of the Raghu dynasty, who is the support for all fallen ones, is the one who is referred by everyone as Ishwar or Allah. I doubt someone can dare to lay down the fact today and survive to tell the tale.

It matched with Mahatma Gandhi’s doctrine where Ishwar, Allah or God were actually interchangable terms. And this is no differerent from vedic doctrine of — ekam sat vipra bahudha vadanti.

But wait, is it not an alternation done by Mahatma Gandhi to some ancient script whose original lyrics is different?

Politics. Sadly this disease is killing many great legacy of Indian history and culture. This beautiful Bahajan dedicated to Great Lord Rama appears to be next in line. The alternative variation that you see on the social media these days, is actually an adaptation from the original dhun popularized by Mahatma Gandhi. And, while I don’t find any problem in adaptation, what really is bothersome is you condemn the original bhajan which is nothing but a pure praise of Shri Rama in the name of malicious politics.

I also don’t understand the reason for condemning the lyrics of the original dhun? Do we really want to say that Rama is not supreme? Is he merely a hindu God? Merely the slayer of an corrupt evil power who lived several thousand years ago? And then left the earth? Why should he be worshipped, unless Rama is all prevailing, that his message lives on, that his teaching have the power to guide the world for all the times to come? Are we not talking about that Shri Rama who took even Vibhishan, the brother of his archa-rival to his fold? Are we not talking about that Shri Rama who personna was such that even the enemy would be forced to bow before him? Is he not the one who is God of all? Or is he a like the president of a country or sect who has no juridication on a foreign land.

At least I would like to share Gandhiji’s belief of all prevailing Rama.
