Is Ekalavya the most unsung hero of Mahabharat?

📅 Mar 07, 2021 09:16 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Eklavya is neither unsung nor a hero of Mahabharata, and most certainly, the word ‘most’ can’t be applied to him from any perspective.

He was just a minor episode in an extremely large epic. An incident of the gurukul, where guru prohibited an authorized use of his name or knowledge by an unknown youth. It was a reminder that art of weapon is an art of destruction and can’t be entrusted to anyone lightly. He had refused higher knowledge of weapons to one of his other students as well. And Drona was proved right in both the occasions.

Eklavaya was later seen in Yudhishir’s Rajsuy yagna where he stood, prosperous (not unfortunate) and under Indraprastha domination.

And finally we heard Krishna had to kill him on his path to establish Dharma!

The story ends. There is no heroic mentioned other than the way, without any hesitation he chopped of his thumb at Drona’s behest. His dedication is certainly worth applauding.

What would have happened if Drona had not demanded his thumb may be a matter of speculation — he may have turned better or worse? Since we don’t know for certainty, we must accept Drona’s wisdom, which didn’t fail in case of both Arjuna and Karna.

Either way, Karna was neither a hero nor unsung — rather he is over-glorified over ages considering his almost non-existent role and impact on the epic.
