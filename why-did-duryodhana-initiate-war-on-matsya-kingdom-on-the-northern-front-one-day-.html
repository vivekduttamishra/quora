Why did Duryodhana initiate war on Matsya kingdom on the northern front one day after Susarma's attack on southern front? What did he really want to achieve?

📅 Mar 16, 2021 05:41 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I think that was a master strategy of Kauravas with old theory — monkey gets all the share, when the two cat fights.

First Trigrath, attacked Matasya. That was an expected attack seeing the long running hostility between Trigarth and Matasya and several failed attempts on Trigarth’s part in the past years. Kauravas must have expected with a rationale that Matasya would deploy their entire forces against Trigarth, which they did.

One day of battle would weaken the two armies. And then Kauravas would lauch a surprise attack, when most of the Matasya Army would be expended in the Trigarth battle. They would have a busy win, all expenses paid by Trigarth who were more like a canon fodder.

What they had not calculated in — Arjuna factor — one man capable of destroying their well led plan. And why would they bother about someone whom they thought already destroyed?

But the interesting question is —

Why did Susharma of Trigarth accepted Kaurava’s war plan?

Based on the authentic texts of Mahabharata and reading between the line (which this answer seeks), few points are more than clear —

  1. Susharma or Trigarth may not be an overly powerful kingdom, if they were defeated again and again by the presence of a single person — Keechak.
  2. Trigarth army faced several defeats. That doesn’t say great things about their valour or calibre.
  3. It was Susharma who approached Kauravas for help and not otherwise. Naturally they had to play with Kaurava’s strategy, if they sought their help.
  4. The most important thing to understand is, Duryodhana knew how to use apparently stupid Susharma as a canon fodder to advance his own interest. He did the same thing at Kurukshetra battle on day 13 and day 17, where he asked Susharma to march his troop against Arjuna to keep him occupied. Weather this strategy worked for Duryodhana or not may be a matter of another debate, but it certainly resulted in annihilation of entire Trigartha at the hands of Arjuna.
