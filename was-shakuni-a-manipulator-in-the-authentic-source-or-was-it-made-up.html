Was Shakuni a manipulator in the authentic source, or was it made up?

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer: Shakuni’s character is over-glorified in modern narratives.

Shakuni was a smaller minion in the big scheme of things, which has been over-glorified and justified by modern narratives.

Shakuni has been referred as the branch of evil tree Duryodhana, whose root it Dhritarashtra and trunk is Karna — decidedly Ved Vyas didn’t attribute as much significance to him as modern narratives does. That doesn’t mean he was not evil. He was part of every evil conspiracy of Duryodhana, but most the time as a minor stooge, rather than as a mastermind.

His only prominent contribution is the dicegame where he wont the entire empire by his deceit and maya for Duryodhana. But that’s that. He didn’t play any active role in the humilation of Draupadi or other episodes that led to the downfall of Kauravas.

There is modern popular narrative where Shakuni wanted to avenge against her sister’s marriage to a blind prince or possible the murder of his entire family. But that is not true. Shakuni never carried any grivance against Duryodhana or anyone else. Rather he always made best effort in their benefit. In fact he too tried to perssuade Duryodhana to take the peace deal offered by Krishna. Unlike some of his other friends, he actually looked after the welfare of Duryodhana.

In summary, you can compare Shakuni to Sambha of Gabbar Singh, not to Chanakya as he is commonly potraid. An evil man not an evil master-mind.
