Did Karna participate in the planning of Lakshagraha according to authentic sources?

📅 Oct 03, 2020 09:18 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short answer YEA.

Let us take two of the leading reference in this regard.

KMG

Vaisampayana said, 'Then the son of Suvala (Sakuni), king Duryodhana, Duhsasana and Kama, in consultation with one another, formed an evil conspiracy. With the sanction of Dhritarashtra, the king of the Kurus, they resolved to burn to death Kunti and her (five) sons.

—Adi Parva: Jatugriha Parva: Section CXLIII

Many suggest that BORI doesn’t include Karna in the Lakshagrih conspiray. But this is not true. Here is the direct reference

BORI

When the Pandavas were not killed through all these means, open and hidden, since they were protected by fate and destiny, he16 consulted his advisers: Vrisha, 17 Duhshasana and the others. With Dhritarashtra’s consent, he had a house of lac built.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 2366-2368).

And before someone says he may have been a different person by the name of Vrisha, may be some son of Dhritrashtra, here footnote 17 clearly mentions Vrisha refers to Karna. Also note they had consulted to built hous of lac. Now lac is not a common material for building houses and has inflamable property. So don’t say Karna didn’t know the purpose of lac and thought it might be a cost cutting measure.
