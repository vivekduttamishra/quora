Should Subhash Chandra Bose’s photo be on the currency instead of Gandhi?

📅 Mar 24, 2021 08:34 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The question has a certain malice that I always object to — A divisive politics to malign someone to increase the stature of someone else.

The question should have been — Should Subhash Chandra Bose's Photo should also be on the currency. And I assure you we would get a lot more support, than you would when you use the word instead of Gandhi? You would be surprised to know that many in this country respect both these great leaders.

Let us imagine a hypothetical scenario. Imagine if both Gandhi and Bose were alive. Would Gandhiji who didn't run for a political position would care if you put your photo on the currency or through it out? Would Subhash Chandra Bose, who called Gandhiji as the father of the nation, would be willing to replace his Bapu on the currency? No. This malice exists only among the followers.

Why should currency contain an image of either? Why not Ram or Krishna? The idea is currency isn't a show piece, or a hall of fame. It is a national icon that plays in international market. It needs to be recognized internationally. As it so happens, Gandhiji is the greatest international icon, without doubt, even if this statement might upset those who condemn the greatest man of the century.

Shubhash Chandra Bose has a proud place in Indian history and his photo is present in the original book of Indian constitution.

Let us not divide our great heritage with our narrow petty thoughts.
