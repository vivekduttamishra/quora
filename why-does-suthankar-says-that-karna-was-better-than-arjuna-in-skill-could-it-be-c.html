Why does suthankar says that Karna was better than Arjuna in skill? Could it be considered legit claim?

📅 Mar 07, 2021 01:39 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer — You can’t consider something as a legit claim, if it was never made. Prof Suthankar never said Karna was better than Arjuna. Here is what he said —

PROFESSOR VISHNU SUTHANKAR’S EDITORIAL NOTE

If you check out his statement, here is what he says —

  1. In technical terms, Karna, at best was equal to Arjuna’s equal. If not his superior — is a phrase that indicates that even equality is a stretch.
  2. The comparison was limited to technical skills only. He didn’t compete Arjuna’s spiritual strength, which was evident throughout the epic.
  3. Karna was struck down as he stood against the cause of justice and righteousness.

And I believe this, after cross-referencing to epic, should be considered a legit claim.

But we must also understand the difference between —

TECHNICAL SKILLS AND SPIRITUAL STRENGTH

Consider an olympic gold-medalist shooter. He has excellent skill, perhaps greater than many Army Captains. But does that make him a worthy warrior? Does his shooting skill even qualify him to be a warrior? Why or why not?

There is a huge difference between an Olympic (or any sports) shooter and a warrior — The condition and reason for which the shoot.

A sportsman shoots under ideal condition, with his ears closed for outside noise and extreme concentration, and he shoots for glory and trophy. He is in no mortal danger. A warrior on the other hand fights under extreme pressure — to kill or to be killed. He fights for a greater cause than the glory and it is said — a true soldier never leaves a friend behind. He doesn’t need to be the best shooter; He need to be a better man — brave enough to put his cause before his self.

Now, do you remember the occasion where Karna rose to fame and glory? Yes, the only occasion where he showed his skills equal to that of Arjuna? It was a play arena. Karna had an excellent sporting skill, at par with the champion sportsman Arjuna.

But then the game was over. Karna got a lucrative post just like our sport people get. Sachin Tendulkar is an honorary Colonel and Dhone a Lt. Colonel. But we do not expect them to fight an actual war. They are sportsman not warriors.

But Karna had to face the battle —

SWYAMVARA BATTLE

Karna joins the team who were blood thirsty and determined to throw Draupadi back to fire and massacre the entire Panchala royal family. Do you think, that would give someone spiritual strength?

Karna fights Arjuna. He is great in skills. He starts strong, almost at par with Arjuna. But Arjuna hits him and he approaches him with great caution and reluctance. It was not a sporting arena. Soon, his courage fades. Whom he initially thought a hapless Brahman, now he sees as almost like Indra, Vishnua and Rama. He gets scared and retreats.

Now Karna had invinicble Kavacha and Kundala and great archery skills. He was technically un-killable. But those artifects are no replacement of raw courage and they don’t dispell you fear.

Gandharva Battle

Gandharavas attack Kauravas. They had an entire army. Once again Karna starts strong and kills many of them. He still had his invincible kavach and kundala. He could have continued to fight. Yet he chooses to flee.

Duryodhana and Dussaasna and other Kauravas were no match to Karna’s archery or techniques. They didn’t had invincible armour. Yet they stayed put. Duryodhana didn’t desert his army — he may be less powerful but lot more brave. He can be considered as a warrior.

Pandavas face the same adversary. I will not talk about Arjuna. Let us talk about Nakul and Sahdeva. They are not greatest of Archers, neither do they possess celestial weapons nor great armour. Yet the face the same adversary.

We can’t be sure, that Nakul or Sahdeva wouldn’t have died in that battle. Niether can be we sure that Karna wouldn’t have won the battle, if he stayed put. The battle may be winnable for Karna’s skills, but the lack of courage (spiritual strength), a lack of commitment for his cause, made him flee.

MATASYA BATTLE

That was a opportunity like none other. He would have been supported by Dronacharya, Kripacharya, Ashwatthama and Bhishma. Arjuna was alone. Kripacharya recommends to fight as a team.

But Karna, Once again, he boasts of having power to bring Arjuna along with his ape banner down, all by himself.

And what happened? Karna retreated thrice before he could retreat no more. Had he stayed put with other warriors of his formation and played in team, perhaps history would have been different.

A warrior needs to be way more than technical expert. He needs to know when to put down his egos.

KURUKSHETRA BATTLE

Karna continued to show case his lack of resolve. He resolves to help Duryodhana in the battle. So strong was his initial resolve that he rejected the sane request of Kunti and Krishna. He wouldn’t leave Duryodhana for anything in the world — not for kingdom, not even for his own family, not for dharma and not for preventing a great terrifying battle.

But then, he remained absent from the battle. For first TEN DAYS. Why? For Duryodhana? For Dharma? For Family? For Justice?

NO. Because his ego was hurt. He had been called Ardharathi. And he felt all his successes would be credited to the commander of the Army Bhishma — He wasn’t fighting for a cause. He was fighting for his egos, fame and credits.

Do you know any other warrior in the history, who fought or not fought for their ego, and ego alone?


SUMMARY

  • Karna, taught by Dronacharya, Kripacharya and Parshurama certainly had great technical skills, maybe at par with Arjuna.
  • But he was no warrior. He was great in showcasing his skills, fighting for trophies, but not real battles where his own life is at stake.
  • Suthankar notes that he was brought down in favour of dharma and justice.
