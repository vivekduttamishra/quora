Mahabharata (Hindu epic): Inspite of Bheeshma being a kshatriya, Parashuram taught him martial arts. Why?

📅 Mar 24, 2021 02:56 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

And as goes the popular saying — that is a million dollar question.

Why did Parshuram teach Bheeshma? Because Bheeshma was a worthy disciple. And unlike popular contemporary narratives, Parshuram never said he would teach only Brahmins.

In fact Parshuram also offered Shri Rama, another Kshatriya the great Vaishnava bow.

So, what is the background of this question?

It is trying to seek the reason Karna had to cheat Parshuram? It is to reprimand Bhagwan Parashurama. Why he denied Karna an education, when he gave a similar opportunity to Bhishma? We are trying to find some fault in Parashurama to vindicate the actions of Karna.

The truth is Karna cheated his guru. He not only announced himself as a Brahmana but a Bhargava, same as Parshurama. He had no reason to believe that Parshurama won’t teach a non-brahmana. There was evidence contrary to it and yet he lied.

This is an incident with no equivalent anywhere in the entire epic. Not even demons ever cheated their guru, the way Karna did.

I am surprised that people would condemn a Vishnu incarnate, and a revered guru to justify the deceitful act of an evil person who wanted divyastra, only for his selfish ego and revenge and would stoop to any depth to possess one.


Was Drona responsible for Karna’s deceit?

This is a new angle I found in one answer to this question. The answers refer to this dialog of Dronacharya, when he declined the divine knowledge to Karna —

He saw that Dhananjaya was superior to everyone in learning about dhanurveda. Karna secretly went to Drona and spoke these words. ‘I wish to know about brahmastra and the secrets of releasing and withdrawing it. It is my view that I should become Arjuna’s equal in battle. It is certain that the affection you bear towards your disciples is equal to what you bear towards your son. Because of your favours, make me accomplished and skilled in the use of weapons.’ Drona was partial towards Phalguna. He also knew about Karna’s wickedness. Having been thus addressed by Karna, he replied, ‘The brahmastra can only be known by a brahmana who is observant of the vows, or by a kshatriya who has performed austerities, and by no one else.’ Having been thus addressed by the best of the Angirasa lineage, he honoured him and took his leave.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 8 (Kindle Locations 2349-2354).

The answerer of the question now tries to shift blame on Drona. According to him Drona shouldn’t have said Brahmastra was for Brahmana or Kshatriya. It was because of this mis-information, Karna hid his true identity.

Let us re-look at the whole episode —

  1. Karna knew Arjuna was his superior.
  2. He wanted a fight with Arjuna and they were still in gurukul.
  3. Drona was partial towards his best disciple who had saved his life.
  4. He knew Karna was wicked.
  5. Most Important: He didn’t just mention Brahmana or Kshatriya. He described their qualities. Brahamana who is observant of his vows and kshatriya who has performed austerities.
  6. These people also miss the fact that Brahmana and Kshatriya was a varna and Drona was talking about the qualities that makes the varna.
  7. Guess they also missed that deceit wasn’t a quality of either of the two varna.
  8. Drona had rightly denied Karna the knowledge knowing is wicked and deceitful nature.

Karna even insulted Drona, by seeking that knowledge from Parshurama which was explicitly forbidden to him by his guru. This way he holds distinction of having cheated two gurus, where no one did to even one guru. This way Karna stands out only one in our epic culture.
