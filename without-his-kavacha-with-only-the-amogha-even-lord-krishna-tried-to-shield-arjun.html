Without his kavacha, with only the Amogha, even Lord Krishna tried to shield Arjuna from Karna. And undoubtedly the toughest fight was given to Arjuna by Karna on the last 16th and 17th days. Why couldn't Karna make good use of his Kavacha earlier?

Thanks for the A2A.

First things first, the question needs a correction — Even with his Kavacha, Karna was badly routed not only by Arjuna but also by Bhima, then why would Arjuna need a shield?

Second correction — Arjuna didn’t get a tough fight from Karna ever. Karna fled Arjuna, rescued by Ashwatthama on day 14, he was badly routed by Arjuna on day 16, and Arjuna killed Karna’s son in front of his eyes. How tough fight did Karna gave him on day 17 is evident from the statement of Bhimasena and Krishna is when the wonder, why is Arjuna not fighting with his best effort? It is evident from the fact that even when Karna invoked Bhargavastra, Krishna ignores it and asks Arjuna to convince Yudhishthira that you will Karna today. Do you see this as a tough fight? Krishna himself proclaimed on day 17 that Karna can’t defeat Arjuna, no matter what.

Now let us take two important questions raised here —

1.Did Lord Krishna tried to shield Arjuna from Karna?

Simple answer NO. Krishna never shield Arjuna from Karna. He did try to avoid Indra’s Vasvi Shakti. As I see, he wanted to avoid a direct confrontation of two divine weapons and may honor Indra’s Vasvi Shakti, which even Indra knew can not harm Krishna and Arjuna. He said that explicitly.

The only other time Krishna saved Arjuna was not by shielding him but avoiding the trajectory of Karna’s treachery. Yes, Karna had engineered a plan that was as evil as Karna himself. He had thrown a snake known as Ashwasena at Arjuna. It was not a weapon. It was not Karna’s power. It was not a divine power. It was two evils colluding with each other. And Krishna foiled it. Plain and simple.

2.Why couldn't Karna make good use of his Kavacha earlier?

I, at the risk of repeating myself a thousandth time, would like to remind you, a Kavacha is a protective gear. It can protect you from mortal injuries. It can’t win the war for you. It can make you strong but not brave. It can’t instill courage to a weak heart. Karna always found his legs more valuable protective gear than Kavacha. For Kavacha to be effective a warrior need to stay put in the battle and fight and still getting risk of injury. But his leg supported him well. He kept fleeing battle after battle and ensuring for himself and more surer safety than what Kavacha was offering.
