Do you find any similarities in the views of Karna fans and Arjuna fans on Quora? If so, where do they agree?

📅 Mar 26, 2020 06:20 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I must start by saying I am not a Arjun fan (although many of my answer may make some in doubting this statement). But let me tell you long back I used to like Karna. Rashmirathi is my one of the all time favorite books and I too once believed BR Chopra to have an authentic Mahabharat. (Although it is close to original epic, with fewer flaws)

I am passionate about Indian Epic, the rationality of it and the message it carries.

And anyone who would be actually reading the epic from whatever can be considered authentic will find Arjuna likeable as with his shortcomings and weaknesses, he was a likeable character supported by Krishna and Shiva, liked by Bhishma and Parshuram.

Similarly any one reading those ancient epics will realize Karna was an evil character — And here lies the main problem.

Most TV shows and modern adaptation portray Karna between good and great. His follies are attributed to a series of misfortunes and Injustice.

Unfortunately it is easier to watch and get influenced than to read and get influenced. Many trust that these modern TV Shows have done their research. Sadly they simply hide behind a disclaimer that absolves them from prosecution and allows them to misinterpreted and distort. Poor viewers forget the disclaimers and form a strong view in favour of Karna. And to make Karna a hero, ironically, they are ready, not just to condemn Arjun but also Krishna, Ved Vyas, and the epic itself. That's the paradox!

Now coming to the question of similarities —

  • Neither group is willing to listen, they have something to say. Epic fan's obviously won't learn from a baseless TV show and Karna fan's would not agree they are baseless.
  • Both defend their Forte! Epic fan will quote reference, Karna fan, well their references (TV Shows folklore and their arguments)

Interestingly after 5000 year, Karna is still fighting Arjuna (via fan club) —

  1. Just like 5000 years ago, Karna is still playing the victim card.
  2. Just like ancient times, Karna is ready to steal glory (Parshram's tale), this time posing victim and not Brahmin.
  3. Just following Karna's technique projections are used —
    1. Karna was great but…
    2. Karna could have won if…
  4. Arjuna’s story is also on similar lines. Those who learn the hard way like Arjun. He pleased Mahadev by his hard effort not tried to steal. Those supporting him have the hard work of quoting the script. Those supporting Karna doesn't (can't) refer a text — remember he is a victim and a hero!

Finally I belive Arjun is again winning because I know anyone who tries to read ancient references would ditch the evil.
