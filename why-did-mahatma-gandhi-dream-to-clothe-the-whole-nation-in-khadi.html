Why did Mahatma Gandhi dream to clothe the whole nation in Khadi?

📅 Oct 26, 2020 05:24 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

An interesting question. But, we must understand in Mahatma Gandhi’s elaborate dream, Khadi was but just a symbol — not an end but just a means.

He didn’t want people just to wear Khadi, but also spun it. Khadi was symbolism for self reliance and cottage industry. Khadi was a pronouncement of individual empowerment. Khadi was a message that self-reliant individual can be industry in themselves. Khadi was a symbol of dignity and self respect. It was a reminder that no one is big or small just because of his clothes.

Those who would toss aside such a great idea as idle dream should look at the birth of phenomenon called Facebook and Whats app. They are essentially what Khadi represented — individual struggle to create something greater, something valueable. You don’t need huge capital or industry to back your dreams. And become so big that giants come calling you.

That is what Gandhiji wanted for every Indian — clothe in Khadi, not just your body but your sout — and walk on the path of true independence.
