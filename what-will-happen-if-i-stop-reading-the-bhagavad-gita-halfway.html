What will happen if I stop reading the Bhagavad Gita halfway?

📅 Sep 21, 2020 07:16 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Hi Shree Shisogem Thanks for asking me to answer this wonderful question. What if we stop reading Bhagwat Gita Halfway? Well, this is a relevant question that has vexed many. Its answer, too, had been given by many prominent scholars over centuries. I am way below their stature for my voice even to register, forget, count.

So let me quote you the greatest authority on this subject ever — Shri Krishna. Read verse 40 of chapter 2 —

नेहाभिक्रमनाशोऽस्ति प्रत्यवायो न विद्यते |
स्वल्पमप्यस्य धर्मस्य त्रायते महतो भयात् || 40||

— Gita Chapter 2

Working in this state of consciousness, there is no loss or adverse result, and even a little effort saves one from great danger.

This shloka applies to our answer also. Even the little that we learn from Gita can only help us and there is no loss or adverse result.

I would also like to add, that if you read Gita only once, it is as good as reading half and understanding a quarter. It not unless we read it again and again we can make complete sense of the idea. For a casual or first read, the idea may sound impractical and yet when we understand it again and again we realize the relevance of it all.

But as Krishna said, working on the right path even with little effort has no loss or adverse result and can save from great danger.

Hope that helps.

May Krishna be with you!
