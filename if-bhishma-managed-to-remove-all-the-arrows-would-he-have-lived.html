If Bhishma managed to remove all the arrows, would he have lived?

📅 Aug 24, 2021 10:35 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Bhishma didn’t die because of those arrows. A single arrow killed Karna. A single blow of sword killed warriors like Drona and Bhurisrava. But Bhishma, he lived for another fifty four days before embracing his death. He could have lived for another fifty four years if he wanted.

So why did Bhishma die? Because he wanted to. He chose how he wanted to go out and when he wanted to go out. He chose when to leave the war and when to leave the world. He could have survived the arrow. He could have even continued to fight. But he knew it was time to retire.

Death of Bhishma was suppose to be a turning point in the war of Kurukshetra — A turning point required to establish that rule of righteousness for which Krishna had incarnated. Bhishma, The Accursed God, waited for the right time to aid Krishna in his mission and left when his defeat proved more beneficial than his victory. Before he left, he ensured that his life’s lesson is not lost.
