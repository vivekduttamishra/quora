Was Arjuna really a Great as said by one Arjuna fan?

Thanks for the A2A.

Edit 1

Question has been modified after my answer. The original question was — Was Arjuna really Runanjay as claimed by a Karna fan


I believe Karna’s fan are right on this. Arjuna was Runanjaya in various different context.

Jaya (जय)

Let us first focus on Jaya — Victory. Incidentally, this was the original name of the grand epic we are discussing. Now there are few variations of this word —

  1. Ajaya (अजय) — One who is invincible.
  2. Vijaya(विजय) — One who wins over the other.

रणंजय

Let us now focus on the word Run. The Hindi meaning of this word रण is war. That makes Runanjay — one who is victorious in wars. And there is no two doubts Arjuna was the war champion. Just as a reminder of his few wars —

  • Gurudakshina war — While all the princes went to the war, every war needs a leader to anchor it’s victory. Who was that warrior? While KMG is more explicit here, Let us seek the more obscure but explicit answer from BORI —

O king! Drona lived in Ahichhatra, which had towns and a countryside, and was won in battle by Partha and handed over to him.’

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Kindle Locations 5275-5276).

  • Swyamvara and Swyamvara battle — Arjuna won not only that swyamvara where other warriors failed, but won the battle that followed where his opponent got scared and ran away.
  • Rajsuy Yagna — Arjuna fought and vanquished rulers like Bhagdatta making Rajsuy Yagna a success.
  • Nivatkavacha — Arjuna defeated Nivatkavcha on the instruction of Indra.
  • Gandharva war — Once again Arjuna vanquished Gandharva to protect those henious people who did nothing but to conspire against him and and his family. He protected women folk of those people who humilated his wife. And he won that war, where one of the so called great archer ran away leaving his friend behind.
  • Matashya war — Arjuna once again defeated almost all the greatest warriors of the time, and made them flee (run away).
  • Mahabharata war — Of course he won against great warrior Bhishma, killed Bhagdatta and slayed several minor warriors, who couldn’t fight even his son and killed him as a pack of hungry Hyenas.

But perhaps this question doesn’t use the word Runanjaya (रणंजय) but an illogical comibination of enlish word (Run) with hindi word Jaya. Even this illogical combination fits for Arjuna

Runanjaya — One who was victorious of over running (fleeing) enemy

Now in this context Arjuna was Runnanjaya on two different accounts.

  1. Runanjaya — One who concurred his fear and doesn’t run away. Now in a war, everyone human would feel fatigued and tired at somepoint or other. Arjuna being a human was no exception. But he has lofty ideals and doesn’t see a way to run away from the battle —
    1. The brave one is like Yama and has performed a terrible deed. He is repeatedly casting extremely terrible glances towards me. I do not see any means of running away from Karna in this battle.
    2. On several occassions, Arjuna fought even without a chariot but unlike other famous warrior of the time never made loss of his cariot as an excuse for not fighting.
  2. Runanjaya — One who concurred Runaway (fleeing) enemy. There is one warrior in Mahabharata who has been referred on multiple occassion fleeing (running away) from the battle. So many times did he flee from his battle ground that warriors like Kripacharya and Drona often rebuked him for this act of his — be it Swyamvara or Gandharva war, Virata war or against Abhimanyu or Bheema. And Arjuna defeated that running (fleeing) warrior. That certainly makes him Runanjaya (one who won (jaya) against running away warrior)

What you expected a different meaning? If you expected Runnanjay to mean one who ran away, the word Jaya won’t come there, and if that were the case that Ved Vyas has clearly named one warrior as expert of fleeing with no one to compete him.
