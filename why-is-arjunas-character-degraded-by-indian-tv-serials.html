Why is Arjuna’s character degraded by Indian TV serials?

📅 Feb 10, 2021 03:44 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Degrading Arjuna is a vital element for contemporary modern narrative. Arjuna is a disease to their thought process. It destroys their belief system — one in which the ancient India was a conniving, brutalizing, inhuman system, rotten at its core.

They have chosen a few characters as a symbol of ancient India’s oppressive and rotten culture. A character who was a great warrior, famed for his charity and archery, Despite of born with divine armour and divine qualities, he was humiliated and rejected while Arjuna, despite of being inferior, was honoured. The humiliation and honour were based on caste and not merit.

But then, Arjuna is the biggest villain against this narrative. Though born to a king, he spent the majority of his life is exile. He doesn’t become the greatest archer just because he was born to a royal lineage, but because he constantly practiced and learnt. He worked so hard that even Lord Shiva was impressed, Lord Parshuram sung his glory and Krishna declared Arjuna as an inseparable existence of himself.

But the greatest crime of Arjuna was not just the greatest archer of his time, but defeated their selected hero, Karna, in every war they fought, most of them resulting in their hero running for his life. Now, if Arjuna was so great that their hero could never even compete him, what would happen to their narrative that suggests that the greatest archer of the time was made to lose just because of his caste.

Thus, TV shows decided to degrade Arjuna, suppress his valour and not let him interfere with their own imagined narratives.

But Arjuna is not the only character who was degraded to keep their own belief system intact. Here are other villians of their narrative —

  1. Dronacharya — He refused to teach Karna because of his caste. As per original epic, Dronacharya indeed taught Karna. In his gurukul Karna and Duryodhana became friend. He was jealous of Arjuna and couldn’t compete him and demanded Brahmasira weapon which Drona refused. It was then he went on to cheat Parshurama.
  2. Draupadi — She too refused him a participation in her swyamvara because of his caste. Although these is not 100% TV Shows fault as this skewd narrative existed in few ancient scripts (5 out of 1259), it was weeded out by BORI CE after over 40 years of research. But TV Shows not only decided to carry on with fake narrative rather than daring to show but also refrained from showing Karna’s failure in swyamvara also mentioned in same ancient script that depicts Draupadi’s refusal.
  3. Bhishma — Bhishma refused Karna to participate in Mahabharata war under his command and Karna had to wait for 10 days before he could be part of the war. How would they have shown that it was Karna who refused to participate in war under Bhishma’s command fearing that he wont get the credit of his feats.

ARJUNA’S ACHIEVEMENTS CENSORED

  1. Arjuna Karna war after swyamvara — After failing at Swyamvara, kings and princes decides to kill Drupad and burn Draupadi alive. Karna and Shalya attacks Arjuna and Bhima. After a prolonged battle Karna got scared and retreated. But in TV Shows either Karna refuses to fight or retreats out of his free will.
  2. Gandharva War — When Karna decides to mock Pandavas suffering in exile, they camp near Pandavas. Indra sends Gandharavas to punish and captivate Kauravas. A fierce battle breaks out. Kaurava army is routed and Karna flees the scene. Arjuna saves Kauravas and royal women by vanquishing Gandharvas. Now TV Shows generally don’t dare to show Arjuna’s feet which hands down confirms his valour against not only Karna but also Gandharvas. BRC Mahabharata, while showing Arjuna’s powers, tried to show to Gandharvas as small tribe and gave Karna an excuse of being Drunk!
  3. Virata war — Virata battle was one of the greatest testimony of Arjuna’s valor where he single-handedly defeated warriors like Bhishma, Drona, Ashwatthama, Duryodhana, Kripacharya and Karna. Karna fled multiple times before Arjuna made everyone unconscious. Naturally this feat of Arjuna doesn’t go well with modern narrative. How would they say a superior warrior didn’t get his due, when he was vanquished even when part of an army?
  4. Mahabharata War — On every occasion, Arjuna defeats the Kaurava warrior. Terrified of him, Drona doesn’t form chakravyuh in his presence. Once they manage to trap and kill his valiant son Abhimanyu, Arjuna wrecks havoc on Kauravas Army next day, drainng any hope of victory from everyone of them. But then, again, how do they show the greatness of their warrior. So they prefer the narrative that Arjuna could win only because Krishna faked a sunset. While BORI eliminates the sunset story, even in narratives that say Krishna causes a sunset, show a great battle after the veil of sunset was lifted.
  5. Mahabharata Day 16 — BRC Mahabahrata invented a story that Karna was overpowering Arjuna on day 16 and Arjuna was rescued by luck as sun was setting. This was just to glorify Karna one more time. In original epic even Karna admitted how Arjuna routed them and how he would win the battle the following day.

Many characters are degraded, others are uplifted with a simple goal — degrade great santana culture of India and made them beleive that it wasn’t worth being proud of. Be warned. Be aware. Be Proud.

Remember — Dharmo Rakshati Rakshitah…
