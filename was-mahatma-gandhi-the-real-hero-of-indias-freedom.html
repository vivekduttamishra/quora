Was Mahatma Gandhi the real hero of India's freedom?

📅 Jun 12, 2020 12:16 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

There is absolutely no doubt that Mahatma Gandhi is a real hero of India’s freedom. It doesn’t mean that India’s freedom-movement was a solo hero saga. Crores of Indians lived and died, dreamt and suffered, struggled and perished to see India free. Many names are household names — Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, Chandrashekhar Azad, Bhagat Singh, Ram Prasad Bismil. There are millions of other unnamed, unsung heros who died so we can be independent.

That said, if we seek that one person, who energized the masses like no other, who united the nation better than anyone, whom countrymen followed with absolute trust, and who remained a guiding spirit in life and his death — He is Mahatma Gandhi. It is not surprising that great leaders like Subhash Chandra Bose kept him in high regards. Revolutionary like Chandrashekhar Azad, too, sought his endorsement.

And yet calling Mahatma Gandhi just as the hero of India’s freedom would be a gross understatement. Gandhiji fought against untouchability, in favour of self-reliance, advocated cottage industries, emphasized cleanliness and became the icon of non-violence, world-wide. It wasn’t without a reason he made his mark on great minds like Albert Einstein to Steve Jobs, from Dalai Lama to Barack Obama and from Nelson Mandela to Narendra Modi.

Gandhiji is not just a front runner for freedom, but a social reformer, an innovator and an entire school of thought.
