Did Karna attack the unarmed Abhimanyu?

📅 Mar 07, 2021 10:56 PM PST  |  🌐 English

NO. Karna couldn’t have attacked an unarmed Abhimanyu.

Abhimanyu was the warrior of the highest order. Some may cut off his bow from the behind in a cowardly way, because fighting from the front weakens their heart, others may destroy his armors and chariot because they feel even Gods can’t defeat him in a fair fight. But no one can stop him from turning a chariot wheel into a weapon. How would you make such a great warrior weaponless, who fights with the shield of Dharma?

Abhimanyu thrashed each of those elite warriors more than once, forcing them to a swift retreat. They saw their brothers, their sons killed right in front of their eyes, and all they could do was to see them killed and retreat. Great Drona thought, even Gods can’t defeat him. Karna heart was weakened. So the two distinguished men decided if only they can cut his bow — of course from behind, too scared to take him head-on.

Karna cut down his bow from behind. So when he attacked, technically Abhimanyu wasn’t weaponless. They thought without bow, armour and chariot Abhimanyu would be an easy kill.

They may have thought Abhimanyu would beg them to stop fighting till he gets a new chariot, some of them might have done that had they been in Abhimanyu’s place. The pack of hungary hyneas thought, now Abhimanyu would be an easy kill. But…

Abhimanyu switched on to swords, still fighting, not begging to stop, not preaching them about Dharma, he knew they had none.

Then those Valiants snatched even the sword from Abhimanyu and if they had their way, Abhimanyu would have been weaponless at this point. But again, he was Abhimanyu. He took on a chariot wheel and continued to fight.

The question is how do you make him weaponless, who can use almost any artifect into a weapon? How do you vanquish him if you are terrified enough to approach him alone.

One of the answers to this questions says, Kaurava warriors didn’t attack him fataly. Perhaps Ved Vyas would like to disagree —

Those six great car warriors ,with great speed ,when speed was so necessary, ruthlessly covered that careless youth ,fighting singlehanded with them, with showers of arrows.

  • They continued to hunt in pack, from the safety of their chariot and using arrows, for who would dare to engage him a hand to hand combat.

But today we have group of people, who are blind to Abhimanyu’s valor, more blind than even Dhritrashtra. They see Abhimanyu was killed fairly by Dussasana’s son!. Here is what they quote —

The ratha who was Duhshasana’s son, the extender of the deeds of the Kuru lineage, arose first and struck Subhadra’s son on the head with the club, as he was about to get up. Because of exhaustion and because of the great force of the club, Subhadra’s son, the destroyer of enemy heroes, lost his senses and fell down on the ground, unconscious. ‘“O king! Thus, in that battle, a single one was slain by many. He had agitated all the soldiers, like an elephant among lotuses. The brave one was as resplendent as a wild elephant killed by hunters. The brave one fell down and was surrounded by all those on your side.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 6 (Kindle Locations 888-893).

  • The are quick to point out the the first line of their highly selective narrative. Watch out, Dussasasna’s son killed him in a fair battle.
  • They obviously turn a blind eye to everything that happened before or after this point.
  • In their defense, they were neither present their nor unbiased enought to believe Sanjaya.

But I tend to agree with them. We can’t say Karna or Drona killed Abhimanyu. They were to scared or coward to face him, how would they kill him? What really killed Abhimanyu was fatigue and exhaution (as per the original text), and when you are exhausted even an insignificant warrior like son of Dussassana can kill someone, only if he dares to fight. Looks like there was no other warrior that day on the battle field to do the honour.

I agree — Abhimanyu was not without a weapon and certainly never without his warrior code. He didn’t beg for a recess, pleaded other to wait and not kill him, didn’t try to flee.

So, yes Karna didn’t attack an unarmed Abhimanyu. Now you can post his shiny poster and show him bright as sun. Maybe that brightness protect that dark hollow inside Karna which Krishna refers to here —

O friend! O lord! I tell you truthfully that my limbs burn at the thought that even then, the evil-souled Karna attacked him. In that battle, he was unable to remain in front of Abhimanyu. He was mangled by the arrows of Subhadra’s son. He was unconscious and blood flowed from his body. He blazed and sighed in rage. However, afflicted by the arrows, he retreated. He retreated and lost all enterprise. He gave up all hope of remaining alive. He was supremely unconscious in that battle and exhausted because of the blows. Hearing the appropriate, but cruel, words of Drona in the battle then, Karna severed his bow. When he was devoid of weapons in that battle, five maharathas, skilled in deceit, killed him with their showers of arrows.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7 (Kindle Locations 4039-4044).

  • Krishna’s limb burnt at the thought.
  • He sees the decit, cruely and cowardise of those warriors.

But yes, I agree Karna didn’t attack an unarmed Abhimanyu. To attack him, you one needed the valour of a warrior.

Yes, go post a shiny bright poster of Karna, for he didn’t attack an unarmed Abhimanyu.
