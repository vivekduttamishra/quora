Was it fair for Krishna to fake the sunset using his divine powers to save overconfident Arjuna when the war was actually being fought with human prowess? Was it sensible for Arjuna to make such a vow in the first place?

📅 Jan 16, 2021 07:28 AM PST  |  🌐 English

I would like to answer this question in four parts it actually consists of —

1.Was the war actually being fought with just human powers?

If you realize this basic assumption that the question makes is factually incorrect as the war of Mahabharata wasn’t fought just with human powers. There were Divyastras at play. Bhishma had a iksha mrityu shield. Karna was honing a Vasvishakti from Indra, Ashwatthama had Narayanastra and Brahmastra, Bhagdatta had Vaishnavastra and Drona had multiple Divyastras. Of course, Arjuna too had a multitude of Divyastras many of which he never planned to use.

We must also go back to the main reason, why Arjuna took the oath to kill Jaidradh? It was because of him, no one could reach to aid Abhimanyu and he was killed by a pack of coward hynas. But how did Jaidradh managed to hold off all the Pandava Maharatha alone? I guess, you have the answer — Lord Shiva’s boon — And that boon wasn’t a human power to being with.

So it would be wrong to assume Krishna was the only one to have used divine powers.

2.Was it fair for Krishna to fake sunset using his divine powers?

If we go by BORI CE, Krishna never faked sunset and all the cross references suggest the same thing. Kauravas again and again remembered how Arjuna single handedly vanquished all the Kaurava warriors. Some important cross-references include —

  • In Adi Parva, Dhritrashtra explains how he lost hope of success when Jaidradha was killed and Drona, Kritavarma, Kripa, Karna, the son of Drona, and Salya were vanquished.
  • In Shalya Parva, Kripachayra remebers how Arjuna vanquished everyone as he slaid Jaidradha.
  • In Drona Parva, Ashwatthama mocks Karna on the same issue as they all failed against Arjuna to protect Jaidradh.
  • Again in Drona Parva, Dronacharya raises the issue of how Arjuna vanquished them all and a similar conversastion takes place between Duryodhana and Karna.

All these references are present even in KMG edition which includes the sunset story, and none of them blames Krishna for using any special power. For a detailed reference on these points I would recommend you to answer by Arnab Ghosh on to this question.

3. But if Krishna actually caused the sunset?

While BORI CE and all cross-references in other editions as well, completely contradict the story of sun set, it has its own mystic appeal. So let us for a moment consider if Krishna actually caused sunset, should it be considered fair?

In a war, where your opponent has taken an oath to kill you and you instead of accepting the challenge as a warrior, hide behind the ranks, is that fair? When you don’t stand up for a fair fight, do you really accept Krishna to do nothing and let Arjuna die? Do you think you would do that to your friend? And remeber, Jaidradh, too used divine power to stop aid reaching to brave Abhimanyu. So how Krishna’s act alone became divine and unfair?

Krishna used sunset (if we go by this story) only to rat out Jaidradh and not to kill him. Here is what happened when Jaidradh came out according to KMG version —

Once that coward was ratted out in open, Arjuna resumes the fight giving entire Kauravas army a fair chance to protect Jaidradh. But in Arjuna they saw Rudra — A warrior that had not existed in past, nor would be in future. And all the warriors who thought their only hope of victory was Arjuna’s sucide, now fled for their life.

And now the Most Important part —

WAS IT SENSIBLE TO FOR ARJUNA TO MAKE SUCH A VOW IN THE FIRST PLACE?

I see, many consider the action of Arjuna as a rash descision out of seer anger. However, I find this act to be completely sensible and justified. Here is why and how.

Let us understand, fourteenth day of Kurukshetra war was without any doubt, the turning point of the battle and by the time the day ended both Duryodhana and Dhritrashtra had realized that all hopes for winning the war was comprehensively lost and now one could save them. This wasn’t the case even a day before where they celeberated their cowardly victory over lone warrior Abhimanyu.

Despite of all Gita Sermon, we find Arjuna reluctant to fight first against Bhishma and then against Drona. But Abhimanyu’s death changed it. It was time for Arjuna to change is stance. When Arjuna too his oath, it wasn’t insensible — it was an oath well within Arjuna’s powress. You see, on 14th day no warrior really thought they could kill him in a fair or even unfair battle. Their only hope of victory was if Arjuna failed to reach Jaidradh, he would be dead. By the time battle ended, Kaurava’s army was not only physically vanquished they were also morally routed.

It would not be wrong to say, Pandava won the war on 14th day itself, rest was just a clean up drive.

After having a reluctant fight for thirteen days, Arjuna had finally woke up to the cause and he needed to remind it to the opponents. I feel, had Jaidradh fought like a true warrior against Arjuna and died, it wouldn’t have damaged Kauravas cause as much as the devastation caused through out the day.

As I see, Arjuna oath was a proclaimation of the victory and he knew, no one could really stand his wrath as explained by Sanjaya — Indeed, the like of what that illustrious warrior then did had never been nor will ever be. Like Rudra himself slaughtering creatures
