Was Devi Draupadi disrobed or not as a particular set of people are calling it interpolation with proof?

📅 Dec 03, 2020 11:33 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Short Answer — Draupadi wasn’t disrobed, but the attempt was certainly made and failed.

Those who are trying to label the entire incident as interpolation don’t have any proof, just have skewed perceptions and wild conjunctures to disprove the authentic text. Not only the incident is very clearly available in BORI, KMG, Neelkanth, Dakshinayat and Geeta Press version, there are enough cross-reference to further validate the authenticity of the incident.

Direct Reference two the Incident

(Karna said)

However, she submits to many and it is therefore certain that she is a courtesan. It is my view that there is nothing surprising in her being brought into the sabha in a single garment, or even if she is naked. In accordance with dharma, Soubala has won all the riches the Pandavas possessed, including her and themselves. O Duhshasana! This Vikarna is only a child, though he speaks words of wisdom. Strip away the garments from the Pandavas and Droupadi.”

O descendant of the Bharata lineage! On hearing these words, the Pandavas took off their upper garments and sat down in the sabha. O king! Then Duhshasana forcibly tugged at Droupadi’s garments. In front of everyone in the sabha, he forcibly pulled. O lord of the earth! As Droupadi’s garment was being tugged away, another similar garment appeared every time.214

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 2 (Kindle Locations 3376-3382).

Same is present in KMG in a greater detail.

(Karna Said)

Therefore, certain it is that she is an unchaste woman. To bring her, therefore, into this assembly attired though she be in one piece of cloth--even to uncover her is not at all an act that may cause surprise. Whatever wealth the Pandavas had--she herself and these Pandavas themselves,--have all been justly won by the son of Suvala. O Dussasana, this Vikarna speaking words of (apparent) wisdom is but a boy. Take off the robes of the Pandavas as also the attire of Draupadi. Hearing these words the Pandavas, O Bharata, took of their upper garments and throwing them down sat in that assembly. Then Dussasana, O king, forcibly seizing Draupadi's attire before the eyes of all, began to drag it off her person."

Vaisampayana continued,--"When the attire of Draupadi was being thus dragged, the thought of Hari, (And she herself cried aloud, saying), 'O Govinda, O thou who dwellest in Dwaraka, O Krishna, O thou who art fond of cow-herdesses (of Vrindavana). O Kesava, seest thou not that the Kauravas are humiliating me. O Lord, O husband of Lakshmi, O Lord of Vraja (Vrindavana), O destroyer of all afflictions, O Janarddana, rescue me who am sinking in the Kaurava Ocean. O Krishna, O Krishna, O thou great yogin, thou soul of the universe, Thou creator of all things, O Govinda, save me who am distressed,--who am losing my senses in the midst of the Kurus.' Thus did that afflicted lady resplendent still in her beauty, O king covering her face cried aloud, thinking of Krishna, of Hari, of the lord of the three worlds. Hearing the words of Draupadi, Krishna was deeply moved. And leaving his seat, the benevolent one from compassion, arrived there on foot. And while Yajnaseni was crying aloud to Krishna, also called Vishnu and Hari and Nara for protection, the illustrious Dharma, remaining unseen, covered her with excellent clothes of many hues. And, O monarch as the attire of Draupadi was being dragged, after one was taken off, another of the same kind, appeared covering her.

—Sabha Parva: Sisupala-badha Parva: Section LXVII

You should see, the only thing BORI has removed is the direct involvement of Krishna in saving Draupadi. But both version agree on the failed attempt to disrobe Draupadi.

So why do people call it an interpolation?

As, I mentioned earlier, it is based on skewed perception and illogic. Few scholars like to point out, that there are not many cross-reference to the fact that Draupadi was disrobed. In many later conversastions example, Draupadi-Krishna, Pandava-Krishna, Kunti, while they recall how Draupadi was dragged to court room, they didn’t explicitly mention disrobing. They want to suggest while Dussassana, indeed dragged her, they didn’t try to disrobe her as there is no cross reference.

Going just by the counter logic, they naturally didn’t mention disrobing, as it never succeeded and attempt to disrobe was part of that dragging. But if I stop right here by forwarding my logic, some may call it my perception. So let us focus on some of the cross-references that exists in Vyasa Bharata, which clearly points to the truth.

Cross References to Draupadi’s molestation

1.Bhima after slaying Duryodhana

‘Droupadi was in her season and they disrespected her. They deprived her of her garment there. Behold. Through Yajnaseni’s39 austerities, in the battle, the sons of Dhritarashtra have been slain by the Pandavas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 7,
—BORI Shalya Parva, Chapter 58, Shloka 10

2. Ashwatthama to Karna During Goharana Parva

O performer of evil deeds! She was dragged into the assembly hall in a single garment, when she was in season. In search of gain, you have severed the great root of a sandalwood tree. 109 O brave one! You made them perform tasks and what did Vidura have to say then? 110 We have seen that men exhibit conciliation, to the best of their ability. So do other beings, even insects and ants. The Pandavas are incapable of pardoning Droupadi’s molestation.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata: Volume 4 (Kindle Locations 1700-1704).

Now there are few very important observation. What task did Karna made Duryodhana perform? Duryodhana had given the order to Dussassana to drag Draupadi on his own and Karna wasn’t involved then. Karna’s involvement is as mentioned in above reference where he asks Dussassana to strip Draupadi. Also note a clear reference to Draupadi’s molestation in both the cross-references.

Why are some people hell bent on proving cheerharana to be an interpolation?

Go through the above references and you would see, Karna played a very important role in Druapdi’s molestation. He was the prime instigator which is referred not only in the dyutparva by both BORI and KMG it has also been cross-referenced by Ashwatthama. Now few people, out of their affection for Karna, and unable to find justifiable rationale to his extrme brute behavior feel, that if they could prove the incident as interpolation, it would exonarate their hero from being the performer of evil deeds.

But there exists no theories by any scholar that proves Karna didn’t provoke Dussassana into his attempt to moest Draupadi.

They have just perception. No proof.
