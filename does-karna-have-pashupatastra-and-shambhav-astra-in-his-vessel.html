Does Karna have Pashupatastra and Shambhav Astra in his vessel?

📅 Sep 24, 2021 10:48 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

SHORT ANSWER: NO

Karna didn't have Pashupata

Neither Karna, nor any other human of Gods possessed Pashupata during Mahabharata era. Not even Parshurama. This was confirmed by none other than it’s absolute owner — The God of Gods, Mahadeva Shiva —

‘The illustrious god said, “O Pandava! I will give you the great pashupata weapon, dear to me. You will be capable of bearing, releasing and withdrawing it. The great Indra, Yama, the king of the yakshas, 292 Varuna and Vayu do not know it. How can it be known to humans?

—Kairata Parva, BORI

This rules out Karna or anyone else having Pashupata. Arjuna also confirms this idea very clearly in Udyoga Parva as he informs to Yudhishthira —

I possess the terrible and great weapon that Pashupati gave me when there was a duel with the hunter. 114 At the end of a yuga, that is employed by Pashupati to destroy all the beings. O tiger among men! I know how to use that. Gangeya does not know this, nor do Drona and Goutama. O king! Neither do Drona’s son and the son of the suta.

— Udyoga Parva

But if some may doubt Arjuna’s statement, they can confirm it with none other than Karna himself —

He faced the immensely generous Mahadeva himself in a battle and satisfied him.683 He thus obtained the extremely terrible pashupata, the great weapon that can destroy the three worlds. The various guardians of the world assembled and gave him weapons that have no measure.

—Karna Vadha Parva, BORI

Look at the awe with which Karna spoke about that Pashupata that is capable of destroying the three worlds and confirms that he got weapons that have no measures. No parallel.

There is a fourth and indirect proof. Karna kills Ghatotkatch with Vasvi Shakti, the spear of Indra. But there was a pre-condition to use this weapon —

But if you unleash this invincible weapon in a fit of fury, when you possess other weapons, there is no doubt that it will descend on you.”

—Kundala Hara Parva

Now since Karna could successfully arm this weapon, proves that Karna had exhausted all other options. Now is it logical that Pashupata failed against Ghatotkatch?


On what basis someone may claim Karna had Pashupata?

There is one statement of Krishna, which makes few claim that Karna had Pashupata. Let us first see the statement in detail —

He is established in the strength of the brahman, but he does not possess a great deal of strength of the kshatriya variety. O Partha! I do not think that Yudhishthira, the great king, will remain alive, even though Bhimasena is with him and is roaring intolerantly, like a lion. O scorcher of enemies! The sons of Dhritarashtra are roaring repeatedly. Desiring victory in the battle, they are blowing on their giant conch shells. O bull among the Bharata lineage! Pandaveya Yudhishthira will be killed. Karna will urge the immensely strong sons of Dhritarashtra towards Partha. He will shroud the king with sthunakarna, indrajala and pashupata and the maharathas will follow him.539 O descendant of the Bharata lineage! It is my view that the king must be in distress. At a time when speed is of the essence, the Panchalas and the

— Karna Vadha Parva

Look! Krishna himself said he has Pashupata. But then, look at what else did Krishna say — Karna will kill Yudhishthira! If we simply trust a Krishna’s words without trusting it’s deep meaning, that would mean Karna had lied to Kunti and he was indeed going to kill Yudhishthira. Right? So why, did Krishna said so? It was just to emphasize to Arjuna that speed is of essence and Karna should be killed without delay.


Can we mistrust Krishna statement?

NEVER. But we must understand that Krishna is not Yudhishthira to blindly consider useless facts as truth. He is Ranchod! One of the greatest motivator and one who was born to establish the righteousness. In his grant mission, his talks were more motivational even if it carried a mix of fables.

Let me explain you with evidence. But first, I would like you to go back to previous reference and focus on his view about Yudhishthira — He is established in the strength of the brahman, but he does not possess a great deal of strength of the kshatriya variety

But then, Krishna contradicts this statement as he talks to Yudhishthira in Shalya Vadha Parva. First he says Shalya is more powerful that Karna, Bhishma and Drona —

Vasudeva told the king, ‘O descendant of the Bharata lineage! I know everything about Artayani.48 He is brave and immensely energetic. In particular, he is great-souled. He is accomplished and colourful in fighting. He also possesses dexterity. In an encounter, he is like Bhishma, Drona and Karna. The king of Madra may even be superior to them. That is my view.

—Shalya Vadha Parva

Should we simply agree? Or try to see his reason, which won’t be difficult if you follow his next statement —

He is like a tiger in his bravery. Barring you, there is no other man in all of heaven or earth. O descendant of the Kuru lineage! In a battle, there is no one else who can kill the angry king of Madra.

— Shalya Vadha Parava

Here Yudhshira, who earlier didn’t posses a Kshatriya strength all of a sudden becomes the most powerful man on heaven and earth — only one who can Kill Shalya. And Yudhishthira did kill Shalya.

The fact is neither Shalya nor Yudhshithira was as powerful as Krishna claimed here. So did Krishna lie? No. He simiply motivated Yudhishthira to do what must be done. He made Yudhishthira believe that it is his duty. That was a truth for Yudhishthira.


SUMMARY

  • Shiva announces no human or God possess Pashupata
  • Arjuna also confirms this statement to Yudhishthira
  • Karna informs Shalya that Arjuna’s weapon have no parrallel
  • Krishna tells Arjuna that Karna would Kill Yudhishthira with Pashupata. It wasn’t meant to be a fact but a motivation to Arjuna to quickly finish Karna.
