Why was Karn great among all in the Mahabharata?

📅 Dec 10, 2021 09:42 AM PST  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Karna was great among all the evils people of Mahabharata. He was so great that Krishna himself called him trunk of the evil tree Duryodhana.

His greatness lied in the fact that from an early age he supported Duryodhana in killing and tormenting Pandavas. Now for Duryodhana killing Pandava were a means to achieve some goal. But Karna had no such goal. No malice from Pandavas. Yet he never hesitated to be part of Duryodhana’s evil design. Never flinched. Yes, indeed he was great.

In the gurukul he approached his guru to ask for Brahmastra. What was the reason? He wanted to fight a fellow student using divine weapon? Now what had his adversary done to him? Nothing. He was just superior to him in his skills. Such greatness from such a young age.

When the guru refused to give him Brahmastra, he left him and reached another guru. He used deceit and presented himself as a Bhargava to gain knowledge from Bhargava Parshurma. There is not a single incident in our entire epic where even a demon was deceitful to his guru. Yes, Karna was greater than all of them.

He gate crashed a private ceremony to prove his caliber. The guru allowed him. But was he content. No he wanted a duel. Reason? Well to prove his worth, which he failed to prove all his life.

He stood along with the Kings to burn down a bride because they failed to win her swyamver. There were many and we can’t prove Karna was greatest there.

But Karna stood out few years later when he ordered to strip a woman in public! It was unheard of. Even Ravana didn’t do it. Yes, Karna was that great. No other great men dared do it.

And story didn’t end here. The great Karna suggested his less great friend to go and torment a family the so deceitfully exiled.

And Karna was fully aware of his greatness and how precious his own life was. He was determined to preserve it at all cost. So every time he felt his own life is at risk, he would escape without bothering about the life of lesser significant people such as his friend or even his brother. He would leave them to die or being captivated. Such was his great persona. Even Meghanad and Kumbhakarna never cared for their life more.

The fact is Karna had been referred evil, wicked, deceitful uncountable times and almost everyone called him by that name and not without a reason. Questions like these tend to distort our great ancient epic.
