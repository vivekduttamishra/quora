How powerful was Dronacharya? Was he capable enough to destroy the whole Pandava army within a day?

📅 Aug 29, 2021 01:07 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I would start by answering the second question first. And the short answer is: NEVER

And it is not my personal opinion. Even Dronacharya’s word and action concur.

Here is what Dronacharya himself said when Duryodhana asked the exactly same question —

Duryodhana asked the son of the river, the one who is great in his vows. ‘O lord! How long will you take to kill the soldiers of the Pandus?’ He told the evil-minded son of Dhritarashtra that he would take a month. Drona promised the same period of time. Goutama113 said double the time.

— Udyoga Parva, Chapter 195, BORI CE

The ware was over in 18 days. In that context, one month was like infinity tending to what I said earlier — NEVER.

Dronacharya desisted from forming chakravyuha on Arjuna’s presence. He would rather have Arjuna far far away for his plan to work. Do you think he hoped for a victory with Arjuna around?

Now, I know, few one jump at pointing the Krishna factor. First, we can’t negate the fact that Adharhma won’t have triumphed over Dharma, no matter what. And that too concludes back to — NEVER.

But if some of us are too eager to rule out Krishna factor, let me quote two incidents which would conclusively prove the point —

  1. Matasya Battle — Krishna wasn’t around. Dronacharya was supported by Bhishma, Karna, Ashwatthama, Kripacharya, Duryodhana and a large army. Could he win against a lone warrior? Could the army win? And do you really hope Dronacharya to destroy whole Pandava in a single day, when the whole army couldn’t destroy a single Pandava?
  2. Day 13 — Here story turns more conclusive. Not only Krishna, but even Arjuna is removed from the equation. And Dronacharya had a full day. Could he and his entire army destroy Pandavas in that single day? No the entire army was routed by a single Pandava son! Do you think, Dronacharya would have destroyed everyone in a single day?

That brings me to the first part of the question —

How powerful was Dronacharya?

Dronacharya was one of the greatest teacher, motivator with immense knowledge of warfare and weapons including celestial weapons like Brahmashira and Narayanastra. The proof of his capability is reflected by his great disciples including but not limited to Arjuna.

That said, a great teacher need not be a great warrior, in the same way as an Olympic gold-medalist shooter may not prove to be a great soldier.

Ask yourself what wars did Dronacharya fought in his entire life? He didn’t partcipate in the Gurudaskshina battle. There is no mention of he having ever fought a battle before Matasya battle. And here he was routed with everyone else. Understandable, as there is a difference between theory and practical, teaching and fighting.

Second was the epic war of Kurukshetra. Here he hardly showed his powers before day 15, which happened to be his last day in the war and the world. It may not be out of place to mention he wasn’t first choice of Duryodhana to succeed Bhishma. He got the position because of his seniority.
