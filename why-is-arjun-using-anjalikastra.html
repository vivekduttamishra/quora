Why is Arjun using Anjalikastra?

📅 Oct 20, 2021 11:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

A very interesting question indeed. The question assumes Anjalika was some kind of special or divine weapon that Arjuna used against Karna. IT WAS NOT.

During Mahabharata period, it was a common practice to name your favorite artifacts, such as bows, arrows and conch. However this is not the case with Anjalika.

Anjalika was the name of a common arrow with razor sharp crescent shaped head against common triangular shaped arrows. Then there were other arrows like vatsadanta that literally meant ‘of the shape of a bulls tooth’. A common analogy from today would be 9MM pistol.

It wasn’t a special or divine weapon and was used by several warriors during Mahabharata period including —

  • Bhishma
  • Abhimanyu
  • Ghatotkatch
  • Karna ← Read again!
  • Arjuna

There are several references to it’s use during the Kurukshetra battle. Simply speaking Anjalika stood for an arrow with crescent head. This would be more clear if you read the extract where Arjuna uses Anjalika —

‘ “To ensure Karna’s death, Pandava took out an anjalika arrow. It was like the great Indra’s vajra, or like a rod that was made out of fire. It blazed in its rays, like the one with one thousand rays.800 It was capable of penetrating the inner organs and smearing itself with blood and flesh. It was extremely expensive and was like the fire and the sun.

— Karna Vadha Parva

Notice the article ‘an’. It doesn’t say he took out ‘the’ Anjalika arrow. It was just a broad crescent arrow that was capable of penetrating inner organs. And if you take a note of it’s shape it was perhaps a great choice for severing the head in comparison to a pointed arrow.

Now some mistake it as some divine weapon, some call it Indra’s weapon. Nothing of the sort is mentioned in the epic. Some may get confused as it is compared with Indra’s vajra. What you may not realize is it is just another analogy as it was going to do as much damage. In Mahabharata many analogy has been used in superlative terms.

SUMMARY

  • Anjalika was a family of arrow with characteristic razor sharp crescent shaped head.
  • It wasn’t a divine weapons.
  • It was used by several warriors in Kurukshetra including Bhishma, Abhimanyu, Ghatotkatch and Karna.
  • Arjuna probably used it with the design of severing Karna’s head as it’s broader sharp surface was best suited to do the job.
