Did Arjuna not have many advantages over Karna? Why do people claim that he was better when they never fought on the same level?

📅 Oct 10, 2020 12:37 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Yes. I agree. Arjuna had many advantages over Karna and is pointless to compare the two. But the biggest advantage was Arjuna was a much better human being —

Let us count his advantages —

  • Born to a king in exile, lost his father early and grew in a family of hostile jealous relatives who tried to kill his family. He didn't count these trivial incidents unfortunate or setback and because of his good nature became favorite of Bhishma.
  • His dedication to work and guru made him favorite and earned highest Brahmastra after he saved his life. He never cheated his guru. Indeed he was a better man.
  • His foes tried to burn them alive. But his family didn't strike back. They continued to fight for people welfare and kill demon like Bakasur. Destiny rewarded them a powerful ally Panchal.
  • His compassion for a poor Brahman’s cow earned him anotger exile. Punishment it was, but not a curse. This expedition earned him a chance to get closer to Krishna. You see destiny rewards good people and curse the bad one.
  • His undying faith in God and Krishna earned him the famed Gandiva.
  • He was exiled, but rather than crying on his bad luck, used the time to his advantage and learned more skills making him a better warrior. He learnt from Chitrasen Gandharva, earned weapons from Indra.
  • His dedication and hard work earned him the grace of God of Gods — Mahadeva. What other advantage can equate a praise from Shiva? Indeed he was blessed. But why?
  • His wife was molested. The suffered another exile. 13 years. And yet he ran on first distress call of same molesters and their women, and fought his friend and Guru Chitrasen. Yes he was a good human. And that is no small advantage. Won't such an act make Mahadeva proud.
  • He saved Matsya from an extremely powerful foe — Hastinapur. But he didn't expect any reward in exchange. No kingship as a gift. Just a simple friendship.
  • He who chose Krishna over an army certainly had an advantage over anyone who fought against Krishna.
  • Despite of all hardships he alone had the courage to question the need of war.

Arjuna had blessings from Krishna, the mighty Hanuman and above all, Shiva. But this was not his advantage, this was the proof of his real advantage — A reward for being a good person. It wasn't something you could beg, borrow or steal. It wasn't something that can be compared.

I agree the two warrior in question weren't on the same level.
