Thanks for the A2A.

Sometimes the best answer to a question is another question. And the question here is —

Why should Brahmdev or Agnidev approached Karna for his help? They had not grown up watching modern narratives glorifying Karna. They knew, Karna for who he really was.

Just because he was involved in burning the Lakshagrih at Varnavart or attempt to burn a bride doesn't qualify him as a right candidate for such a mission as to weedout the enemies of the God.

And then, there are two questions –

Would Karna have accepted the mission?

Is there any incident or reference that suggests that Karna would have accepted the mission? Is there any precedence of him taking any cause that didn't involve his selfish interest? Did he protect a bride from a mob willing to burn her down? Or would he have taken up arms to protect cows of a Brahman risking an exile for himself or protect your own worse enemy or protect a distressed kingdom from a looter? The simple answer is NO. Karna NEVER did anything that didn't involve his selfish interest or that of his friends.

And this is not just a hypothesis. Krishna did reach out to him seeking his help in protecting humanity and preventing the war? Did he help him?

Was Karna capable of carrying out the mission?

As we know, Khandava had powerful protectors. Very powerful. Was Karna capable of fighting them? Is there a reference? Do you remember Gandharva battle? Or Matasya? Or against Abhimanyu? As Kripacharya pointed during Matasya what battle did Karna fight all his life to be considered worthy for competing Arjuna? And as Drona pointed he saw him mostly running away battles after battle. Do you think, Brahma or Agni were unaware of Karna's lack of perseverance?

And finally, I don't think they watched TV show Suryaputra Karna and had no reason to believe Krishna would come to assist Karna. I believe, you too should stop believing Surya Putra Karna.
