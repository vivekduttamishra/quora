How do you see Mahatma Gandhi's assassination by Nathuram Godse, a boon or a bane to India? Why?

📅 May 08, 2020 10:17 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Question — Is Kasab’s killing spree in Mumbai a boon or a bane for India?

Answer this and you have your answer. Godse was no different from Kasab and his action no less heinous than that of 26/11. Both killed for their whims, or you can say both were stooge on a larger canvas.

Mr Godse killed Gandhiji in cold blood. While Gandhiji had decades of contribution in Indian politics and was the source of inspiration and motivation internationally with his admirer including the likes of Albert Einstein, Martin Luther King Jr and so on, Mr Godse has no known contribution in either India’s freedom struggle or more Hindu cause he claims he killed Gandhi for.

Mr Godse’s or his supporters claim that he killed Gandhiji because of India’s partition or Gandhiji’s insistence to give Pakistan a certain sum of money is also completely baseless as he had also made a failed attempt of his life as early as 1944, when there was no partition, no Pakistan and no question of giving any sum to Pakistan. If Godse truly wanted to avenge the partition, he would have diverted his energies to Mr Jinnah, Mountbatten or Patel or Nehru. Gandhiji wasn’t even a signatory on the act of India’s independence or Partition!

Any civil society where people start endorsing killing and vigilante form of justice would soon slip into a state of complete chaos, and history is a testimony to this incident. When one killing is justified, other killing, too, automatically get justified.

Aftermath of Gandhi’s death

  • With Gandhiji gone the magic was gone which was internationally reported in the way he single-handedly ensured peace in Calcutta (now Kolkata) during initial days of riot following India’s independence.
  • Gandhiji’s plan to go to Pakistan died with him. He might have be successful in persuading peace in Pakistan the way he did in India. He may have succeeded in bring many Hindu’s back to India for which he had hinted to the government of India when he said that government should ensure saftey and settlement of Hindu’s and Shikhs should they return back to India. This was also recently debated by Amit Shah of CAA issue.
  • Even after so many years, India and Pakistan are still fighting bitter battles proving Gandhiji’s death wasn’t going to change anything.
  • The world lost the crusader of peace in the hand of a fanatic.
  • Many more died in a senseless violence that followed as a reaction go Gandhiji’s killing. Can you blame Gandhiji for it? It would surprise you. Many blame him!

This is what you expect from a senseless killing — people exemplify him as a Hindu terrorist, although he did nothing for Hindu or India. Ironically Godse did greatest dis-service to that ancient Santan Dharma, he claimed, he was fighting for.

No one gained anything, the loss is immeasurable!
