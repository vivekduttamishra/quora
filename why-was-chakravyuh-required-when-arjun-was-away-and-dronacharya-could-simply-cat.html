Why was Chakravyuh required when Arjun was away and Dronacharya could simply catch Yudhishthir?

📅 May 09, 2020 02:58 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

The point was Simply wasn’t simple enough.

While with Arjuna and Krishna around, it was impossible for Kaurava’s army to capture Yudhisthir, it wasn’t easy for them to do even with Arjuna out of the scene and it is proved at so many places —

  1. Day 13 proved that even with Arjuna out and Chakravyuh formed, Kaurava’s failed to capture Yudhisthir. And their plan was foiled by a single warrior. What do you think Drona would have done with other Pandavas in the war?
  2. Day 14, neither Drona couldn save Jaidradh from the wrath of Arjun or himself from Bheema. Bheema destroyed his chariot multiple times to ensure Arjuna completes his oath. What do you think he wouldn’t have done to prevent the capture of Yudhisthir?
  3. Day 14, Drona and his entire army couldn’t win Ghatotkatch and he could only be slain by a divine weapon. How would Drona have simply gone past him to capture Yudhisthir?

The idea of chakravyuh was to capture each of these warrior at different places in the formation, divide them so that while they struggle with the formation they would be unable to reach in time to protect Yudhisthir. Had their been a simpler way out, Drona wouldn’t have waited for 3 days for Arjuna to be out. And he must have tried similar thing in next two days.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic.
