S U M M A R Y

  • 1254 out 1259 available manuscript including oldest Nepali, entire southern edition, entire Bengala edition, sharda edition doesn’t include this narrative. They, however, mention Karna failed.
  • The most popular edition that included Draupadi’s denied Karna’s participation is Neelkantha Mahabharata which was the basis of first ever english translation (KMG). Even this version includes Karna’s failure to lift the string the bow during swaymavara.
  • The Editor in Chief of BORI in his editorial preface indicated several reasons why this incident couldn’t be true. He also mentions that Draupadi couldn’t have known the lineage of Karna to reject his specially since he was an invited guest (no matter what quora conspiracy theory suggests)

But since, many people continue to trust Draupadi rejected Karna, let me explore this line in a greater detail in this answer —

DID DRAUPADI REJECT KARNA FOR ARJUNA?

  1. Had she really loved Arjuna, she wouldn’t have allowed anyone else to participate either. There were so many warriors like Karna who were considered great warriors and were expected to win it. Arjuna was deemed dead and someone was expected to win.
  2. Had she really loved Arjuna, she wouldn’t have happily married an unknown Brahmin who strung the bow.
  3. Had she loved Arjuna she would have resented her marriage to five Pandavas.

WAS DRAUPADI RIGHT IN REJECTING KARNA?

  1. It was Draupadi’s swaymavara and she had the right to reject anyone she didn’t like to marry.
  2. If Draupadi knew Karna would hit the target, she must have been gravely mistaken as even those edition that mention Draupadi’s rejection do mention Karna’s failure to string the bow.
  3. Now she must have thanked her stars for rejecting Karna who soon after her rejection (going by the rejection theory) tried to burn her alive and later promoted Dussassana to disrobe her. This incident has remarkable and irrie similarity with modern day acid throwing lovers who torment the women for rejecting them. Draupadi, in restrosepection must have felt lucky to have rejected him.
  4. I daresay, real Draupadi may have actually repented for not rejecting Karna during Swyamvara.
  5. Draupadi must have been proud of her decision on knowing how Karna fled Gandharvas leaving the women of the family at the mercy of enemies. If only she had not rejected him and married, she would be one of the captors of Gandharvas.
  6. Draupadi was proud to marry the chapmpion warrior of the time who defeated Karna on multiple occaision. She was right in rejecting someone who anyway couldn’t have protected her.
