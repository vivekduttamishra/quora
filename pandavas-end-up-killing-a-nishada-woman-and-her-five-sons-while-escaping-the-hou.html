No. Pandavas didn't kill the Nishada woman here five sons. It is very clearly explained in Mahabharata without leaving any ambiguity —

THE ORIGINAL PLAN

Yudhishthira, the virtuous son of Kunti, addressing Bhima and Arjuna and the twins (Nakula and Sahadeva) said, I think the time is come for our escape. Setting fire to the arsenal and burning Purochana to death and letting his body lie here, let us, six persons, fly hence unobserved by all!'

Do we see any mention of Nishad woman or any body double here? No, only Purochan was supposed to die so that they leave unobserved.

Edit#1

Based on a comment below, I think I should also mention the equivalent shloka from BORI which adds a few phrase extra —

Let us escape, unobserved by anyone, after setting fire to the armoury, burning Purochana to death and leaving six bodies here.”

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 1 (Penguin Translated Texts) (Kindle Locations 5430-5431). Penguin Books Ltd. Kindle Edition.

People conclude murder from the last part — leaving six bodies here. However, it talks about bodies (that may be dead) and not about killing explicitly. Whereas burning Purochan to death is explictly mentioned, it is not mentioned for the rest. But keeping our suspicion intact, let us look at further shlokas to ascertain, if it was a murder.

— end of edit#1 —

In fact at this stage, Nishad family hasn't even entered the scene.

HOW NISHAD FAMILY ENTERS THE SCENE?

"Vaisampayana continued, 'Then on the occasion of an almsgiving, O king, Kunti fed on a certain night a large number of Brahmanas. There came also a number of ladies who while eating and drinking, enjoyed there as they pleased, and with Kunti's leave returned to their respective homes. Desirous of obtaining food, there came, as though impelled by fate, to that feast, in course of her wanderings, a Nishada woman, the mother of five children, accompanied by all her sons. O king, she, and her children, intoxicated with the wine they drank, became incapable. Deprived of consciousness and more dead than alive, she with all her sons lay down in that mansion to sleep.

Note important points :

  • All guests were supposed to return to their respective homes?
  • Who intoxicated the Nishada family? Pandavas? No.
  • Does it appear a plan that when you invite someone with a plan that they would return, you expect a family to get intoxicated and lie unconscious, so that you can burn them? Can anyone see, any action from Pandava to intoxicate them?
  • A conspiracy of murder is not carried out impelled by fate (or Driven by Destiny as suggested by Bibek Debroi). You must actively plan for a murder.

THE EXECUTION OF THE PLAN

Bhima then set fire to the house just where Purochana was sleeping. Then the son of Pandu set fire to the door of that house of lac. Then he set fire to the mansion in several parts all around. Then when the sons of Pandu were satisfied that the house had caught fire in several parts those chastisers of foes with their mother, entered the subterranean passage without losing any time.

  • Bhima sets fire where Purochana was sleeping. It's clearly mentioned. This is as per the plan.
  • He sets fire at several places, but not all. No one else is mentioned. Why didn't they mention Nishada family? Because they were supposed to return, and not be their.
  • What is the last thing they ensured? That house has caught fire at several spots. Again no mention of ensuring Nishada family is dead. Shouldn't they verify, if it's crucial to the plan?

Nishada families end was tragic and it indeed bought precious time to Pandavas else Duryodhana may have hounded them or Bhishma may have intensified search for them. But that's destiny and aftermath. No where in Mahabharata it is referred as conspiracy or murder. There is no cross reference.

OFF TOPIC

Few answer try to use Khandava Dahan episode as a proof of Pandavas brutal nature and to prove them murderers. you need to understand who ordained it?

The illustrious Creator of all things smilingly replied unto him, saying, 'O exalted one, thou hast eaten, for twelve years, a continuous stream of sacrificial butter poured into thy mouth! It is for this that illness hath seized thee. But, O Agni, grieve not for it. Thou shalt soon regain thy own nature. I shall dispel this surfeit of thine and the time for it is even come. The dreadful forest Khandava, that abode of the enemies of the gods, which thou hadst of old once consumed to ashes at the request of the gods, hath now become the home of numerous creatures. When thou will have eaten the fat of those creatures, thou shalt regain thy own nature. Proceed thither in haste to consume that forest with its living population.

—Adi Parva: Khandava-daha Parva: Section CCXXV

This was a command issued with Brahma, the lord or all to Agni, consume the forest with its living population. However, Agni failed. Then Brahma again instructed —

Then Havyavahana (Agni) in anger and disappointment, with his ailment uncured, went back to the Grandsire. And he represented unto Brahman all that had happened: The illustrious deity, reflecting for a moment, said unto him, 'O sinless one. I see a way by which thou mayest consume the forest of Khandava today in the very sight of Indra. Those old deities, Nara and Narayana, have become incarnate in the world of men to accomplish the business of the celestials. They are called on earth Arjuna and Vasudeva. They are even now staying in the forest of Khandava. Solicit them for aiding thee in consuming that forest. Thou shalt then consume the forest even if it be protected by the celestials. They will certainly prevent the population of Khandava from escaping, and thwart Indra also (in aiding any one in the escape). I have no doubt of this!' ,

—Adi Parva: Khandava-daha Parva: Section CCXXVI

As you can see, it was Brahma’s command that Arjuna should prevent the population of Khandava’s from escaping.

You must understand that if an action is sanction by a supreme commander, a soldier must obey. Even Krishna says in Gita, if you act in my obidience then you shall not be responsible for your act.

But how can we justify burning down an entire forest and living stock? We can't by today's parameters, but can't apply same logic 5000 years ago. Remember today we applaud for doctors who killed colonies of bacteria (living beings), we don't call them killers.

But can God ask to kill lives? Yes. They do? By the way what happened to diansaurs? Arjuna didn't kill them? Brahma surely ordained their death.
