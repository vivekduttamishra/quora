Why didn't Yudhishtir stop Ekalavya from chopping off his thumb and request Drona not to allow such a barbaric act because it is adharma to ask Gurudakshina from a boy he has never taught. Did his dharm go on a holiday that day?

📅 Jun 07, 2020 01:19 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question is so long, with too many parts and a greater number of supposition that it becomes difficult to decide where to begin answering. So let's start questioning —

  • Why and how could Yudhisthir have stopped either Drona or Eklavya? What was his locus stand?
  • Because it was adharma to ask… Let's stop right there. Who defined Dharma and Adharma here? Vyas Mahabharat does not deliberate this point. What about Eklavya stealing the knowledge?
  • Would it have been Dharma of Yudhisthir to either provoke Eklavya to defy the guru or tell guru he is Adharmi, because some quora question will pronounce his act as Adharma 5000 years later? Are we talking of college union leader insulting the teacher?
  • When Eklavya accepted him guru, what is the ground of the question that Drona never taught him? There is a indirect learning, motivation and inspiration. And Eklavya did learn from him.

And finally no, Dharma didn't go on a holiday — justice reigns with firm hand as it did on that day. By the way it may interest you to know Eklavya wasn't a lower caste and caste biase didn't apply.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat an Indian epic and refers to authentic versions including —
