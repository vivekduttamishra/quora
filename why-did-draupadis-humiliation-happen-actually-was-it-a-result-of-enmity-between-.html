Why did Draupadi's humiliation happen actually? Was it a result of enmity between kuru and panchala or enmity between Bhima and Duryodhana or enmity between Arjuna and Karna?

📅 Apr 30, 2021 12:34 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

That is an interesting question!

Why did Draupadi’s humiliation happen?

We can look at the episode from so many different angles and all parts of the question may play a small but crucial part to it.

It was combined outcome of Duryodhana’s and Karna’s foolhardy.

The goal of duytshabha wasn’t to humiliate Draupadi. It was to usurp the prosperity that Pandavas had earned and Duryodhana knew well he couldn’t match Pandavas. And that goal had been fulfilled after Yudhisthira lost everyone including himself. Perhaps it was time for Duryodhana to be graceful in his victory. After all he had vanquished his enemies forever, even if by deceit. Yudhisthira wasn’t coming after him. Nor would have any other Pandavas.

But then victory and stupidity have a deadly combo. Duryodhana blinded by his passion decided to take the game a little further. Humiliate Draupadi as a slave. Vikarna tried to stop, and had wisdom prevailed then, Duryodhana would still have come out with minor scratches only.

Here enters Karna. Not only he rebukes Vikarna, and abuses Draupadi and Pandavas he pushed Duryodhana’s luck toward certain doom — he asked Duhsasana to disrobe Draupadi in open court. His action didn’t just humiliate Draupadi but sealed the fate of Duryodhana for ever.

Was there other reason for humiliation?

It may even be a grand plan for Duryodhana to morally break Pandava’s with expectation of one of the two outcomes —

  1. Break them morally such that they never dare to rise out of their shame.
  2. Break them apart by creating a rift between them. This plan, if it was one, had almost succeeded with Bheema rising against Yudhishthir. But wisdom prevailed.

WHY DID THE HUMILATION BACKFIRE?

I think that is a more important question. Because Duryodhana failed to realize the true strengths of Pandavas —

  1. Bheema, the voice of Dharama — Bheema rose as a voice of Dharma even against the brother he revered. He didn’t do it for personal gain but because that was the right thing to do, just at the wrong time.
  2. Arjuna, the voice of reasoning — Arjuna realized Bheema’s rift is exactly what Kaurava’s needed and it wasn’t helping Draupadi. He warned Bheema just in time.

Both were right. Both were in sync with each other. Arjuna stops Bheema from going against Yudhishthira, but doesn’t stop Bheema later from taking terrible oath against Kaurava’s. He even complies to Bheema’s oath that Arjuna would slay Karna.

And Above all

Draupadi, the voice of consciousness — She played the smartest role in the whole episode. She was most balanced in the face of calamity. She doesn’t bother to respond to astray like Karna or even Duryodhana. She directly confronts the prominent whose-who of the court. She never leaves are husbands side and only challenges the technicality of the gambling in a bid to make it void. And she ultimately succeeds.

Duryodhana and Karna overplayed their hand which let to their ultimate failure!

IS THERE ANY OTHER ASPECT THAT MAKES THIS HUMILIATION SIGNIFICANT

Actually yes. It shows a decline in social structure and blurring of gap between social norms and Dharma. Yudhishthira gambled because he misunderstood a mere protocol as Dharma! He though a statement of fact as ultimate truth! And people played on his ego. The humiliation was the result of that perceived notion which forced the entire court in an eerie silence with only few voices of reasoning. And when system decays to such a level a drastic uprooting is needed. Draupadi’s humiliation was the symptom that pointed to a much larger disease.


Edit #1 5th September 2021

An interesting question in the comment section prompted me to add this section to my answer —

WHAT IF DURYODHANA AND KARNA HAD SUCCEEDED IN ACTUALLY DISROBING DRAUPADI?

I must first clarify, disrobing isn’t just a physical abuse or just the process of making some one naked. it is in equal nay in greater measure a psychological and mental process, as it is a social stigma.

From the point of view of Karna and Duryodhana the disrobing had happened. They made their intentions clear. They lost all their masks of righteousness or honor, and they paid the price of their crime in exact measure as they would have, had they actually succeeded. They proved the kind of beast they were and justified them being hunted down then, and being haunted by humanity ever since.

Even from the perspective of Druapadi's and her husbands’, she had suffered the humiliation for the lifetime for no fault of hers. She became a public exhibit. What more agony she might have faced?

Whom Krishna or Dharma, if you think there exists a difference, protected that day was not Draupadi but the entire society from getting totally naked. He extended a little lifeline to society to protect itself from the retribution that was to come. Alas! that proved insufficient. That day an irrevocable destiny of Aryavart was written and all that was left was mere detailing of when and how?
