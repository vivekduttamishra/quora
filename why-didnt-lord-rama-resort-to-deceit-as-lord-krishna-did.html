Why didn't Lord Rama resort to deceit as Lord Krishna did?

📅 Apr 18, 2020 03:42 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A.

Lord Krishna never resort to deceit. Deceit is fundamentally a negative word and is employed by weak against the strong and is used for selfish reasons. None of these is apply to Krishna, who was almighty without selfish reasons.

What he did is strictly Krishna Leela — the way a teacher gives a tricky puzzle to the students for their betterment. He applied strategy. He never punished innocent or tricked because he couldn't win otherwise —

  • He became Ranchod. Was it difficult for him to beat his opponent? No he had to Tech the world —
    • Might can be defeated by wisdom.
  • He could have vanquished Jarasandh, yet relocated to Dwarka, he defined peace more virtuous than egoistic conflict.
  • He showed great strategy can win greatest adversary.

Lord Ram did the same. People who would like to call Ram deceitful will find or invent their own argument — Bali wadh, shambuk vadh, and so on…

But such arguments would be baseless and original proponent of such idea are people out to mock Hindu civilization and propogant often include innocent people without their own study.

On another note Ram's journey was apparently more straightforward because of the Era (treta) he lived in. People had a different and higher code of honor. Even team Ravan had more honorable people.

Krishna on other hand lived with crooked like Shakuni and Jarasandh. He essentially needed a different strategy and message for the world.
