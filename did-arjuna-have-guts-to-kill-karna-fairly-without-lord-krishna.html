Did Arjuna have guts to kill Karna fairly without Lord Krishna?

📅 Sep 28, 2021 06:27 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

I think the answer is NO.

I know, Arjuna won the Gurudakshina battle where others failed. I also know Krishna didn’t support him.

It is a different story that Arjuna badly defeated Karna after Draupadi’s swyamver where Karna retreated scared comparing Arjuan to Indra, Parshurama and Vishnu. I know he won Draupadi’s swyamver where Karna failed. It is also a different matter that once again Krishna doesn’t help Arjuna and Arjuna had no special weapon.

Later Arjuna defeated those Gandharvas with their chief that had forced Karna to flee deserting his best friend at the mercy of their captors.

But if people thought that was the lowest Karna could stoop, they had not idea of how coward he was. He fled Matasya battle thrice and let his brother be killed. The death of his brother didn’t evoke any emotion for revenge. Rather it made him retreat. Once again Krishna was absent.

So why did I say NO?

Because the question ask “fairly”. And there was no way Arjuna could ever had defeated Karna fairly.

Don’t get me wrong. For a fair battle two warriors must be of equal level and there was no comparison between the two warriors in question.

Can you compare a sincere star student with another who devoted his time in helping his friend conspire to kill their relatives?

You can’t compare a warrior who would rush to save his guru from a sure death to a person who would deceit his own guru for weapons.

How would you compare a person who tries to burn down a bride with one who tries to protect her?

How would you compare a warrior who started on a single chariot to protect his benefector kingdom against an army of looter with one who considered his life more valuable than his friend and family?

How will you compare a warrior who faced Mahadeva himself to another who fled from Abhimany seeing his own brother slain?

There was no comparison, no equality between the two warriors and no matter how Arjuna had killed Karna it would have been an unfair death — a powerful warrior killing a coward!

Arjuna didn’t had the guts to kill Karna fairly, as being fair means stooping down to Karna’s level and Arjuna was utterly incapable of doing so.
