During Ramayan and Mahabharata age, Sarathi (Chariot driver) of Kings was an important post. Why they were not given due respect? Even the Great warrior Karna was always humiliated by the kshatriyas by calling him 'sootputra' (son of sarathi).

This question contradicts itself —

  • If Sarathi was an important post, how calling someone by that name became a humiliation?
  • You call a colonel a colonel, does that insult him? Not unless he thinks he is over deserving.
  • Krishna never felt humiliated and became Sarathi. Sanjay never did. Why should it be humiliation?
  • Mahabharat refers several people as Suryaputra and as a method of salutation and not disrespectful.

Some even feel humiliated when called Indian. It is when you are ashamed of who you are, you try to hide your identity and feel ashamed. It's not society but your fault.
