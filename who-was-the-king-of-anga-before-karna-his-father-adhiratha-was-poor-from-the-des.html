Thanks for the A2A.

The question has two parts —

1.Who was the king of Anga before Karna

Anga was kingdom under the control of Hastinapur. Such states may not have a independent King. And while not explicitly mentioned it was the most likely scenario —

  • Duryodhana made Karna the King of Anga and there was no objection from anyone. This could be possible only if Anga had no active ruler, else someone must have pointed it out.
  • Duryodhana’s proclamation also confirms, beyond doubt, the fact that it was a territory under Hastinapur.

2. Adhiratha was poor…

Let me stop your right there. Adhiratha wasn’t poor by any stretch of imagination. He was not just any charioteer, but the charioteer and friend of Dhritrashtra. Don’t think that because a flight piolet is a mere driver he earns same as a taxi driver.

Adhiratha by all indication was a quite well to do person. Let me share you two direct references —

  • One of the names of Karna is Vasusena and it is because of the riches that was found on his person (courtesy to his mother) when Adhiratha found him floating in the river. That wealth naturally increased the wealth of Adhiratha.
  • When Karna kills the Brahimin’s cow, he tries to offer his all possible bribes and wealth to escape the curse. Now as a student there wasn’t like to be much source of income for Karna other than his father’s wealth.

But that said, not every rich person gets to become King. You need to win the kingdom by power or by flattering the powerful.
