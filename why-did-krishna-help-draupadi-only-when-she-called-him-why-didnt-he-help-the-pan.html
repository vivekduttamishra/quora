I have answered this question so many times, and the question keeps popping, with slight different phrasing. One of the answer is this —

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Why did Krishna let the Draupadi vastaharan sequence happen when he knew what happened everywhere?

In my understanding, the important question is —

Why should Krishna really help Draupadi or anyone? Why didn’t help his brothers from dying and infant’s death? Why He didn’t help himself and killed Kansa instead of Putna?

I believe this is as dangerous an understanding as the story of Robin Hood. They try to suggest you don’t have to do anything — someone will come and do your work, it may be some Robin Hood or some Krishna.

Krishna could have supported have beheaded or Kauravas as this question seeks —

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Why didn't Lord Krishna send his Discus to sever the heads of Duryodhana, Dushshasana, Karna & Shakuni-During the Dice game in the Hastinapura Royal assembly instead of protecting Draupadi? Lord Krishna could have easily averted the Mahabharata War?

But these thoughts are inspired by a more recent western philosophy. In Hindu Philosophy, God isn’t a spoon feeder. They don’t interfere into your day-to-day life. They make universal law, like the law of gravity. You jump and gravity will pull you. You walk and gravity will support you. This is referred as Gunas — sato, rajo and tamas.

So why did Krishna interfere?

This, too, is imbibed in Hindu philosophy. God intervenes when human justice completely fails. This is what Krishna said in Gita —

yada yada hi dharmasya
glanir bhavati bharata
abhyutthanam adharmasya
tadatmanam srjamy aham

paritranaya sadhunam
vinasaya ca duskrtam
dharma-samsthapanarthaya
sambhavami yuge yuge

Gita Chapter 4 - Sloka 7,8

And this is very clear throughout Indian Epic. Ram didn’t Kill Ravan on the day#1, neither did Krishna Killed. They incarnated and grew up like mortals and struggled to win against the evil. Why?

Because, God imparts us a lesson via our grand epic —

  • We are responsible for our actions? Good and Bad both karma has its consequences.
  • Don’t expect God to be your faithful assistant and serve you every time you sneeze.
  • But Rest assured whenever human justice system fails, God will intervene.
  • The compassinate Lord gives everyone chance to mend his ways — even the devils. Why else did he come himself to Hastinapur to persuade them for a peace deal? Why did Lord Shiva, spoke through, Lord Hanuman and tried to impart wisdom to arrogant Ravan?

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkant Edition of Mahabharat (Geet Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
