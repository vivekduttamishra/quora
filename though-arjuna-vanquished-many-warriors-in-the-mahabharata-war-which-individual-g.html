Though Arjuna vanquished many warriors in the Mahabharata war, which individual gave him the toughest battle? Can you offer your opinion on five such warriors and rank them?

📅 Oct 23, 2021 07:32 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is an interesting question and it would be herculean task to find five warriors let alone rank them. I could think of just two.

1. Shiva — The Lord of Lords

There was no question of any tough fight here. No one wins against Shiva. Period. Arjuna was destined to lose the battle. Shiva thrashed Arjuna in the most hopeless way. But it would be wrong to say Arjuna lost the battle. He refused to give up. And he won Shiva’s heart. Non many could have done that. Most may have fled or fled.

2. Arjuna

There exist only one other warrior who gave him the toughest battle and forced him to surrender — Arjuna himself. He alone made the battle tough not only for himself but even for Krishna.

Arjuna gave up his weapons saying he won’t fight his family and relatives, prominently his grandfather, and his guru. He saw his family humiliated. They all suffered conspiracy, death threat, exile and infamy. He had a lifetime to prepare for the inevitable.

The same Arjuna who would run to fight and vanquish Gandharvas to save his evil relatives, the same Arjuna who would demolish demons like Nivatakavacha to help even Gods, and the same Arjuna who had the capability to defeat all Kauravas single handedly to protect their benefactor Matasya simply put off his arms!

What changed? Perhaps Arjuna thought the Kurukshetra war has a shade of selfish interest, or perhaps he knew there is no turning back. Unlike Matasya, battle he couldn’t just disarm his guru and his grandfather. It was a battle, a fight to the end. And Arjuna the caring grandson, Arjuna the dutiful disciple of his proud guru stood against Arjuna the invanquishable warrior much in the same way as Bhishma faced Shikandi. He couldn’t give his best.

Krishna had to break his oath and take up arms against Bhishma because Arjuna won’t go fatally offensive against his Pitamaha. That too after he gave him a detailed therapy known as Gita. What could have been a tougher battle?

Next came Drona, his guru. First Guru. Drona couldn’t perform any miracle against Arjuna in the first twelve day of the battle. Then he devised a conniving plan to win the battle by keeping Arjuna far and out. Drona failed and Abhimanyu died.

Next day saw the wrath of Arjuna and Kaurava understood in no uncertain term that they are doomed. The entire army put together couldn’t stop Arjuna from fulfilling his oath and killing Jaidradha. They couldn’t do it together. They couldn’t defeat Arjuna even one he fought from ground to give rest to his horses! So desperate did it make Kauravas that they extended the battle well into the night.

And even on those moments when Kauravas trembled against Arjuna, he avoided Drona instead of fighting. He couldn’t bring himself to ask Drona, why did he let his son killed in so unjust and brutal way? He was concerned if Kripacharya was hurt badly. And he rushed to protect Drona from approaching death at the hand of Dhristduymna. I would say that was a tough battle to fight.

Apart from these two (Shiva and Arjuna) no one came close to giving a tough fight to Arjuna. He won his battles in a convincing and comfortable way.
