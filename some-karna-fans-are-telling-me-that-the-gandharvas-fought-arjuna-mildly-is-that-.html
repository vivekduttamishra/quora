Some Karna fans are telling me that the Gandharvas fought Arjuna mildly. Is that true?

📅 Aug 01, 2020 11:32 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Originally, I had decided not to answer this question, but for one of the answers which claimed that Vyas said so. So I am pulled back in this question. Before we proceed let us understand the two important word that we would use in this answer —

Mild (As per Question) —

  • gentle in nature or behavior
  • moderate in action or effect
  • not being or involving what is extreme

—Marriam Websters on Mild

Feeble — The other word we shall talk about

  • lacking in strength or endurance
  • markedly lacking in strength
  • indicating weakness
  • INADEQUATE, INFERIOR

— Marriam Websters on Feeble

A LITTLE BACKGROUND

  • Arjuna in Swargarohan Parva visits Swarga and learns several war technqiue after which Indra declares —

Harivahana (Indra) touched my head with his hands and spoke these words. ‘Today, the masses of gods are incapable of vanquishing you in battle, not to speak of humans of the world who have not perfected their souls. You are immeasurable and unassailable. You are also incomparable in battle.’ Then, with his body hair standing up, the god again said, ‘O brave one! There will be no one who will be your match in fighting with weapons.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 3744-3747)

  • Arjuna and Chitrasen, the king of Gandharvas were actually friends. But this friendship wasn’t between Gandharvas army and Arjuna.
  • Karna propses to Duryodhana, that —

O tiger among kings! The birth of a son or the obtaining of a kingdom does not bring as much bliss as seeing one’s enemies in misery. What hathppiness will there not be, on being successful oneself, and on seeing Dhananjaya attired in bark and skins? Let your wives, attired in excellent garments, see the miserable Krishna, attired in bark and skins, and thereby increase her grief.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6283-6286).

  • Seeing this devil plan, Indra sends Gandharvas to teach Kauravas and Karna a lesson and imprison them. Here is what Chitrasent later said —

“O Dhananjaya! The great-souled god resides here. He knows the motives of the evil-souled Duryodhana and the evil Karna. Knowing that you were residing in the forest and suffering, though you did not deserve it, they came here to see you and the famous Droupadi. On learning their intentions, the lord of the gods told me, ‘Go and bind Duryodhana and his advisers. Bring them here. Protect Dhananjaya and his brothers in battle. The Pandava is your beloved friend and your student.’ On the instructions of the king of the gods, I swiftly came here. The evil one has been tied up and I will go to the abode of the gods.”

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6493-6498).

BATTLE BETWEEN KAURAVAS AND GANDHARVAS

  • Snippet #1

In many thousands, the gandharvas shattered the chariot. Holding a sword and a shield in his hand, the son of the suta jumped down from the chariot. He leapt onto Vikarna’s chariot and whipped the horses, so that he might escape.’

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6397-6398).

  • Snippet #2

‘O great king! When maharatha Karna was routed by the gandharvas, in the sight of the sons of Dhritarashtra, the entire army fled. O great king! On seeing all the sons of Dhritarashtra flee, Duryodhana refused to run away.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6399-6400)

  • Snippet #3

With Chitrasena, others rushed at Vivimshati and still others at Vindu and Anuvindu and all the wives of the kings. The soldiers of Dhritrarashtra’s sons were routed by the gandharvas.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6405-6407)

YUDHISTHIR ASKS PANDAVAS TO FREE KAURAVAS

This is one of the less quoted text that one must read —

The evil-minded one (Gandharvas) knows that we have been residing here for some time. But he has ignored us and done something that is unpleasant for us. Through Duryodhana’s forcible capture by the gandharva and through a stranger’s oppression of the women, our lineage has been sullied. O tigers among men! For the sake of those who have sought refuge with us and to save the lineage, arise and get ready, without any delay.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6424-6427).

Now comes the spirit of this question —

WHERE GREAT KARNA WAS DEFEATED, HOW CAN ARJUNA WIN UNLESS SOMEONE WAS FIGHTING MILDLY AGAINST HIM?

Let us look at the clue left by Ved Vyas —

  1. Yudhisthir’s instruction about how the war is to be fought —

Try all the different means of conciliation to free Suyodhana. If the king of the gandharvas does not return him through conciliation, free Suyodhana through mild use of your valour. O Bhima! But if the Kouravas are not released through a mild battle, use every means possible to crush those who are contrary. They must be freed.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6435-6438).

Here we see the first use of the word mild in the context and incidentally used by Yudhishthir, not by Gandharvas

2. When Pandavas enter the battle.

  • Gandharva’s action —

All the gandharvas were flush at the prospect of victory. On seeing the four brave Pandavas advance into battle on their chariots and on seeing them as resplendent as the guardians of the worlds, the residents of Gandhamadana53 established themselves in battle formations.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6447-6450).

  • Arjuna’s and Gandharvas in action —

Remembering the words of the king who was Dharma’s son, the first battle that was engaged was a mild one. But the soldiers of the king of the gandharvas were deluded in intelligence. They were incapable of realizing that something that was mild might benefit them.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6450-6452).

Here we see the second use of mild. Who was mild? He, who remembered the words of his king. Who was the king? Dharma’s son. Who was Dharma’s son? Well you know it. If you still can’t figure out the riddle, see the sentence that follows But —

  • Gandharvas were deluded.
  • Didn’t realize that the mild fight was in their benefit.
  • Only first battle was mild, that too from Pandavas side.

3. The Gandharvas refused Pandavas word of wisdom —

the gandharvas laughed. They replied to Partha in these words. “O son!54 There is only one person on earth whose instructions we obey. Under his rule, we roam around, without any anxiety. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! We do only what he commands us to do. Other than that lord of the gods, there is no one else who can command us.”

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6455-6457).

They made it clear, they are not going to give up without the use of force.

3. And battle became terrible and extraordinary — No more mild.

  • Exhibit #1

Partha Savyasachi Dhananjaya released sharp arrows, which travelled through the sky, at those roamers of the sky. The gandharvas were insolent of their strength and released a shower of arrows at the Pandavas, and the Pandavas did the same to those residents of heaven. O descendant of the Bharata lineage! Thus began a terrible battle between the swift gandharvas and the immensely swift Pandavas.’

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6459-6462).

Here we must note —

    • The Gandharvas were insolent of their strength.
    • The terrible battle was from both sides. Not one-sided.
  • Exhibit #2

There were four brave Pandavas and thousands of gandharvas. O king! They attacked each other in that battle and it was extraordinary. Just as the gandharvas had shattered the chariots of Karna and the sons of Dhritarashtra into a hundred pieces, they attempted to do it again.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6464-6466).

Again note a few important point other than the battle was extraoridnary (not mild). They applied the same war technique they had applied against Karna who was routed. So war was as mild or as extraordinary as the one against Karna.

  • Exhibit#3

When the gandharvas were thus killed by the great-souled ones with celestial weapons, they took to the sky and took the sons of Dhritarashtra with them. But Kunti’s son, Dhananjaya, saw them arise. He surrounded them on all sides with a great net of arrows. Having been tied down by that net of arrows, they were like birds in a cage. They angrily hurled showers of maces, spears and swords at Arjuna.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6471-6473).

Again Note:

    • Gandharvas tried to flee, taking prisoners along with them.
    • Their attempt was foiled by Arjuna
    • The angrily attacked again — not mildly.

Arjuna again counter attacks. Many Gandharvas were killed, others tried to escape but failed. And then to save them from Arjuna, Chitrasent enters the battle

  • Exhibit#4

On seeing the gandharvas terrified by Kunti’s intelligent son, Chitrasena grasped a mace and rushed towards Savyasachi.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6480-6481).

Notes:

    • Gandharvas were terrified after an extraordinary terrible battle and when their angry efforts proved futile.
    • Chitrasent rushed in the battle, not mildly but with a show of strength.

SO FAR IT IS CLEAR THAT, IT WAS ONLY PANDAVAS WHO STARTED WITH MILD ATTACK. THE GANDHARVAS ARMY WERE NEVER MILD, THEY ATTACKED WITH ANGER IN A TERRIBLE AND EXTRAORDINARY BATTLE JUST THE WAY THEY ATTACKED KARNA AND KAURAVAS. IN THIS BATTLE NEITHER COULD THEY WIN NOR FLEE.

So let us know come to what many claim was a friendly duel between Arjuna and Chitrasen (who was in a way, also a guru to Arjuna)

CHITRASEN AND ARJUNA BATTLE

  • Chitrasen enters the battle with a show of strength — not mild and not friendly

Chitrasena grasped a mace and rushed towards Savyasachi. In that battle, he held the iron mace in his hand. But with his arrows, Partha sliced the mace into seven pieces.

— Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6481-6482).

  • Arjuna countered his weapon and the battle continued —

he made himself invisible and began to fight with Pandava. Established in the sky, he repulsed those celestial weapons. The powerful king of the gandharvas hid himself through his powers of maya. Arjuna saw that he was invisible and was striking. He therefore attacked him with divine weapons that could travel in the sky, invoked with mantras. Arjuna was angered. Dhananjaya used the weapon known as shabdabheda and restrained his disappearance into many different forms.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3(Kindle Locations 6483-6487).

  • Notes:
    • Vyas Mahabharat doesn’t mention anything about a mild battle
    • Chitrasen used his maya to make himself invisible to attack (or so he thought)
    • After being invisible, he continued to strike at Arjuna. A similar technqiue was employeed by Meghnad against Ram Lakshman in Ramayana.
    • But Arjuna forced him to reveal himself.

WHEN DID THE BATTLE END?

Here is the conclusion of the battle, with Arjuna winning —

Pierced by the weapons of the great-souled Arjuna, the gandharva revealed himself to his beloved friend. 59 On seeing his friend Chitrasena, fighting feebly in battle, 60 the bull among the Pandavas withdrew the weapon he had released.

—Debroy, Bibek. Mahabharata Vol. 3 (Kindle Locations 6487-6489).

IMPORTANT NOTES:

  • Chitrasen was fighting feebly — indicating weakness — not mildly as people want to portray.
  • The chapter uses both the word mildly and feebly clearly marking the difference.
  • When Chitrasen was pierced by arrows, his invisiblity restricted and he was fightly feebly, Arjuna withdrew his weapon — This friendship benefitted Chitrasen not Arjuna, who had already faced an angry gandharvas in a terrible and extraordinary battle.

SUMMARY

  • The only people in the entire Gandharavas-Kauravas-Pandavas battle to start a mild battle were Pandavas.
  • Gandharvas, who knew no command other than Indras attacked Pandavs without reservation. The phrases used to describe this battle is —
    • Terrible
    • Extraoridnary
    • Angrily
  • While Chitrasen was a friend to Arjuna, Gandharvas army were not and they employeed same technique to fight four pandavas that had routed Karna and the entire army of Kauravas.
  • Chitrasen deployed maya war — he became invisible and continued to attack.
  • Arjuna attacked and shattered his attack and then he revealed himself as a friend.
  • Chitrasen didn’t fight mildly — he fought feebly.
