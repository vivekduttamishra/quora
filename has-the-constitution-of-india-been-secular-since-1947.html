Has the Constitution of India been secular since 1947?

📅 Feb 25, 2020 11:33 AM PST  |  🌐 English

The constitution of India reflected the true nature and defining traits of new India.

The preamble included sovereign democratic Republic.

  • Sovereign as India came out of colony rule.
  • Democratic Republic as India had not been since Emperor Bharat's time.

Preamble didn't state the sun shall rise in east as it was a universal fact and not a new trait.

Similarly the creators of constitution decided against including secular word (it was debated and rejected) as India has been secular (a core hindu philosophy) from the beginning of the time. The fundamental right and other sections of the constitution reflected inherent belief of Indian society.

Constitution by provisions was secular since beginning, however, preamble didn't include the word secular by design

The word was however added in 42 second amendment act in a undemocratic way with an obvious political agenda and should be perceived as a distortion to original ideas.

Today world in general finds Indian understanding of secularism as flawed.
