Bows don’t win battles, warrior do. Bows don’t fire rapidly, warriors do. A strong bow can allow warrior to fire as rapidly as possible and as far as possible without breaking down. Period. But if the warrior is busy running away from the battle, bows can do nothing for the warrior for they don't fire themselves. Whose bat is better, mine or Virat Kohli’s? Doesn’t matter. Virat would win every time, no matter which bat he uses and which one I do.

So it boils down to who was a better warrior. And the answer is simple enough. Who won every single time they fought against each other? You may say Vijay bow was better than Gandiva if you like, but you have to agree that Arjuna with or without Gandiva was always better than Karna with whatever he had.

Arjuna fights with the bow he obtained during Swyamvara and he didn’t possess Gandiva till then. Yet he defeated and forced a scared Karna to retreat. Karna had his armor and bow with him. Did it win the battle for him? Did Karna or his bow or his armor helped him win against Gandharvas? Could his divine weapons or army or bow stop him from fleeing Matasya battle abandoning his brother to die at the hand of Arjuna? Could his bow help him against Abhimanyu or Bhimasena? No bows don’t win battles, warrior do.
