Was it fair on the part of Dronacharya to call Arjuna as his favourite when a guru is supposed to treat all his students equally and have no favourites?

📅 Oct 20, 2021 12:03 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

I would like to challenge the very assertion of this question.

Why should a guru treat all his students equally and should have no favorite?

  • Should guru treat an obedient studious student and a cheat equal? Then where is the award for merit in it? Where is the justice?
  • Should a guru give equal marks to all students irrespective of their effort, sincerity and work? How will then, one really value education and hard work?
  • Did God created everyone equal? Did God create our five fingers equal? Did God gave equal powers to a rabbit and a tiger?
  • If guru doesn’t declare a favorite, how will others know what it takes to be a favorite?

Apart from the supposition here let us undertand a few more points —

  • Drona had almost no reason to chose Arjuna as his favorite. He wasn’t the son of Dhritrashtra the then (caretaker) King and power center in Hastinapur.
  • He wasn’t the eldest of Pandavas and had no chance of becoming the King.

His natural choice would have been eldest Pandava or eldest Kaurava. So how did Arjuna become his favorite?

  • Arjuna was the only person who promised to pay his gurudakshina on the day one, before the start of learning and without knowing what it would be. That showed utmost dedication.
  • He surpassed others in his dedication and hardward and proved to be the best student. Such people are often guru’s favorite.
  • While others looked on, it was Arjuna who saved Drona’s life from the alligator. Tell me who wouldn’t love such a student?
