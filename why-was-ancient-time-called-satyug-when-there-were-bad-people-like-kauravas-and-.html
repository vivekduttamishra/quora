Why was ancient time called "Satyug" when there were bad people like Kauravas and Ravana who did bad things?

📅 Aug 24, 2021 10:46 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

Let me start with a correction. Raavan lived in Treta Yuga and Kaurava’s lived in Dwapara Yuga. Satyuga predates all these Yuga and people mentioned in this question. So the exact question may be deemed irrelevant.

However, if we remove the specific names like Raavana and Kauravas, the question has a foundation — there were evil people even in Satayuga, so why is it called Satayuga?

Well I can give you two different explanations.

First in every context and situation, we have exceptions. Exceptions don’t contradict the majority, they give relevance to it. How would you describe truth if lies don’t exist? What matters is the majority mindset of the people.

During Satayuga people were seekers of truth. They may still carry egos, but that were about their truth.

Consider Daksha — He was a Vishnu devotee. And his devotion made him a Shiva hater. Even his foolishness was born out of his devotion.

Now consider Raavana. He fought a person from far away land. He was a devil person, but not against his own people. His brother betrayed him. Not for personal glory. For justice. For Dharma. But still he is condemned for betraying his faimily.

Now let us look and Kauravas. There was no consideration of Dharma. No consideration of humanity. Just personal selfish interest — and they were ready to kill his own brothers! While Vibhishana betrayed his brother for the welfare of his people, Karna betrayed the welfare of people for his own selfish goals.

As you can see, with each yuga the morality degraded. But that doesn’t mean everyone of that era would exhibit the same good or ugly character.
