Aren't Pandavas more wrong than Kauravas in Draupadi’s disrobe scene? They shouldn't have put Dra upadi on bet.

📅 Apr 27, 2020 09:27 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Pandava's were wrong to put, not only Draupadi, but also their kingdom at bait. But more than Kaurava's? Absolutely No. To understand this, let us analyse their actions.

First let us not forget we are talking about an incident which is over 5000 years old and social structure, beliefs and custom of the era was lot different from what we have now —

  • Gambling is illegal in many parts of the world and legal at other places.
  • Polygamy is illegal today, but norms that day. Many religion still endorse it.
  • Caste system wasn't exploiting then as it is today.

So let us understand the actions of Kaurava's and Pandava's

Pandavas

  • Tradition of the time demanded them to participate in such a game when invited. The tradition also allowed them to bait property and humans.
  • Their fault lies in not going against the tradition and protocol and misinterpreting them as Dharma.
  • But to be fair to them —
    • Yudhisthir didn't bait Draupadi till he had lost himself.
    • Pandavas never knew they had no winning chance as Duryodhan was using deceit to win. Had they known, they may have acted differently.
    • No one had expected such a treatment to a woman, a family member in a infamily court.
    • She was lost implicitly along with his husband's as Karna argued with Vikarna. Probably, Yudhisthir saw, her as last chance to win back everything.
    • Yudhisthir was wrong and fool, but not evil.
  • Pandavas repented and were subjected to cosmic justice —
    • Yudhisthir baited his kingdom and suffered an exile of 12 years.
    • Yudhisthir baited his brothers and wife to slavery, they suffered as slaves for a whole year.
    • Rest of Pandavas followed his brother's action in silence, now they suffered with him.
  • But why did Draupadi suffered?
    • Because as family our destiny is linked by each other's fate and action. This further points she was already lost even before the bait.
  • Pabdavas’ accepted their mistake with grace and suffered for them for 13 long years.

Kauravas

  • They invited them to play with the intention of jealousy and deceit. Their intention was cruel. Pandavas had no idea.
  • They used deceitful means to win.
  • So they won Draupadi. Even if Yudhisthir had not baited her, they would have claimed that she was lost along with Yudhisthir. It isn't a supposition. This claim was made. That makes what Yudhisthir did irrelevant.
  • Was Winning a slave same as stripping her naked? How many such examples exist in Mahabharat or entire epic? How many slaves existed in Hastinapur that walked naked?
  • Draupadi served the queen of Virata. Was she stripped there in public? One man tried to force himself on her and was punished by Bhim. Was Bhim punished by King Virat?
  • Draupadi may have become slave, but was she no more a woman and importantly a family member?
  • When did Kaurava's repented for their rash action?
    • After shabha? When they forced Pandavas to play again?
    • After their humiliating defeat against Gandharvas?
    • After their another humiliating defeat in Virat war?
  • They sent Ulluk, the son of Shakuni as their emisory. It happened after Krishna's last failed attempt to peaceful resolution. He reminded Pandavas of all past incident, provoking and urging them to fight.

No, Kauravas and Pandavas can't be compared.
