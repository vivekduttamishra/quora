What kind of unbelievably impossible sort of hurdles were arranged to counter Arjuna, that even surprised Arjun being most skilled and greatest warrior in Mahabharata and found it so difficult to surpass, even with Lord Krishna Himself by his side?

📅 Aug 29, 2021 02:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

There was only one greatest hurdle placed before Arjuna, and it was neither planned or surprising. But that was the only hurdle — being pitted against his near and dear ones like Bhishma, Dronacharya and Kripachrya!

Arjuna always knew they would be standing in front of him. Arjuna had faced them earlier also. He had rendered them immobilized and spared their lives. But that was a different time! Now he knew the greatest hurdle is winning the war while they are alive.

Against this hurdle, nothing could make Arjuna ready — Not Krishna, not Gita and not even the gruesome killing of his son, Abhimanyu.

On the first day of the battle, Arjuna gives up his arms. For him, dying or begging is better than killing these loved ones. It takes so great effort from Krishna to convince him that an epic was created — Gita! But the effect? Arjuna was still not willing to kill his beloved Grandfather. And the battle dragged for nine days making it virtually unwinnable for Pandavas. Once again Krishna had to break is vow and rush towards Bhishma to motivate Arjuna overcome this hurdle. Here again he was only partially successful. Arjuna agreed to disarm Bhishma, not kill him.

But the story doesn’t end here. The hurdle continued. Even with Bhishma out of way, Arjuna had still another mountain to climb — his beloved teacher Drona. He didn’t gave his best for the first two days. Third day into the battle under Drona’s command, Abhimanyu was brutally and cowardly hacked to death. Drona was most directly responsible. The fourteenth day of the war can rightly be called the — wrath of Arjuna. Arjuna decimated Kuru army leaving no doubt in anyone’s mind who is winning the war. But Arjuna avoids a direct confrontation with Drona despite his son’s death. Some may consider it as a proof of Drona’s power, but that would be a mistake considering how miserably Drona failed in his responsibility of saving Jaidradha and how he avoided earlier confrontation with him and not to mention how he was ousted during Matasya battle.

But is a clear proof of Arjuna struggling with the hurdle was his desperate attempt to stop Dhristdyumna from killing his guru, the same guru who mercilessly killed his son.

Finally Arjuna never directed his anger towards his third guru Kripa who was an accomplice in the gruesome night massacre of his family.

Beyond this, there weren’t hurdles that could have surprised or dared to bother Arjuna.
