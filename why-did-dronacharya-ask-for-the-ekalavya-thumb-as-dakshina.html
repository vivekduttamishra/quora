Why did Dronacharya ask for the Ekalavya thumb as Dakshina?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 04:27 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A.

I won’t answer this question directly as it may include spoilers for my epic fiction based on Mahabharat — The Accursed God.

But I can point you in the right direction. There are two way to tackle any question .

Popular Approach — Drona was wrong, jealous and cruel. He wanted to destroy Eklavya so that no one could compete Arjuna. This is a popular tale and interpretation.

Alternate Approach — I would like take you on a different track. Consider following points —

  1. Drona didn’t really wanted Arjuna to be the best. He wanted Ashwatthama to be the best. But Arjuna’s devotion and hardwork won his heart. Why was it not a possibility that he would have extended the same courtsey to Elkavya?
  2. Drona taught all sorts of people (irrespective of what BRChopra suggest). His desciple included Karna. So if he could teach Karna a suta, why would he refuse Eklavya?
  3. Drona was a revered Brahmin, living on alms without personal ambtion. Do you think it is possible that his action was more a reflection on Who Eklavya was rather than who he was?
  4. Karna was cursed by Parshuram; Eklavya by Drona. Is there a co-relation?
  5. What happened to Eklavya after this episode?

If you follow the clue, I think you would understand the true reason.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the great Indian Epic. I have taken reference from the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG), Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkantha Mahabharat (Geeta Press)
