Irrespective of Bori's or Kisari 's Mahabharata, honestly asking for Mahabharata why was Barbariek presently known as KahatuJi Shyam Baba given with three arrows only by Yagya's Devi?

📅 Aug 22, 2022 11:19 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for Asking me the question.

Since you want to keep the BORI or Neel kantha or any original reference out of context, let us talk a bit about our great Shruti-Smriti tradition that is the backbone of the technique that not only preserved our ancient cultural heritage but also provides a decent error checks.

Let us realize that the epic authored by Ved Vyas was first narrated nearly fifty years or more after the end of Kurukshetra by Vaisampanya and everyone who heart (Shruti) remembered it (Smriti) and passed on to the next generation. Now assume that five people heard and each repeated to five people who in turn repeated to five more. At this stage we have 125 people who thing they have the exact narrative that Ved Vyas authored. Now imagine that two people who heard it from Vaisampanya added a story of their own, but different from each other. Say Mr A added Barbarik, and Mr B added Draupadi rejected Karna. Now every downline of Mr A thinks Ved Vyas talked about Barbarik and every downline of Mr B thinks Ved Vyas said Draupadi rejected Karna. They earnestly believed it till one day all the 125 people gathered in one room and realized that out of 125 people, 100 never heard about Barbarik. Would it be difficult to find out that the narrative was a later on addition because it is known to a very small sub group of people? I hope that makes sense. Right?

Now let us say, one of the first downline of Mr A, Mr X was so inspired by Barbarik that he dedicated a temple for Barbarik imagining that Mr A was right. Now every one who is downline of Mr X worships Barbarik and Khatu Shyamji. Do you think, they would be ok to smash their belief because other 100 don’t want to believe? I dare say, they won’t let their belief go even if Mr A admits to adding this story out of his imagination. That is how many temples are build.

Let me give you some example. There are two temples in India who contest as one of the 12 Jyotirlinga — Vaidyanath temple, Deogarh, Jharkhand and Baijnath temple, Parli, Maharashtra. Now centuries ago, Shankaracharya clarified that Vaidyanathan jyotirlinga is in north-east part of India where Shiva and Shakti are together, and that points to Deogarh. But does that ever stopped anyone from claiming Parali to be a Jyotirlinga? No.

Another example would be the common attribution to Ravana for creating Tandava strottam. Now no valid epic portraits him in such a great light. Does that stop people from worshipping him? And yet another would be the famous Lakshamna Rekha. Can you really make people believe that it is not mentioned in the original epic but a later addon? But doesn’t not believing change the fact. NO.

There are more such legendry temples dedicated to many such legends which originally grew to boost the credential of the temple or the deity.
