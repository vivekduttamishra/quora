Before the imminent Kurukshetra war, Shri Krishna asked Arjuna to do tapascharya and acquire Divyastras. Why didn't any Kauravas who actually wanted the war, not do so? Why didn't anyone advise them to acquire Divyastras?

📅 Jul 01, 2022 08:45 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This is an interesting question that carries a lesson on human psychology.

Yudhishthira asks Arjuna to acquire weapons as he feels opponents have all the weapons worth knowing. He may have been right based on his understanding, which wasn’t immense in the matter of war fares. This is apparent from his conduct and insecurity through out the epic. A person who is truthful, doesn’t mean he always speaks facts or is always correct. A truthful person is one who is true (and honest) to his believe to the best of his knowledge. But what if something is incomprehensible to the person or unknown to the person? Can we say person is untruthful even if he is not factful? So let us leave Yudhishira with his views and focus on this question.

Why Kauravas didn’t acquire any Divyastra?

We can’t just put all Kauravas together. Bhishma and Drona had acquired enough Divyastra in their time through their dedication and hardwork. But that was till their limit.

The learning is not an easy thing. At least not for deceitful people. They prefer deceit and shortcut rather than following Dharma, or committing to hard work or dedication. It is easier to belief that they are well equipped rather than walk the mile. Karna wanted a shortcut when he approached Drona to get weapon he hadn’t worked for. Drona rejected him. They he cheated Parshurama to acquire them. It didn’t work for him. And he finally earned a divyastra by exchanging his armors. That appeared easy and only way.

Duryodhana on the other hand was wise. He knew he can buy people holding Diyvastra rather than having one for himself. He hired people for the job. period.

Why couldn’t they acquire Divyastra like Arjuna did?

They couldn’t because they couldn’t have. Acquiring Divyastra wasn’t a kids play or a devils playground. You needed to work hard. Perform tapas for years, passed a grilling test of Mahadeva. And then discipline himself enought to earn Mahadeva’s trust that he wont misuse it.

But who had the time for such a fool’s errand? Kauravas were happy in their life of lust and luxury. Do you think, such deceitful hearts had moral fiber to please Mahadeva? Had they strived hard, they would have been cleansed of their devilish greed and they won’t have needed those weapons.
