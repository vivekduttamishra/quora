Why was Abhimanyu so powerful? Many people claim that he was only a one-day warrior because he had boon on the 13th day of Mahabharat. Is it true?

Thanks for the A2A.

Why was Abhimanyu so powerful?

Just closed your eyes and try to visualize — A young man, full of energy. Trained by best of bests — Krishna and Arjuna. Now open your eyes you would understand why he was so powerful.

Was Abhimanyu just a one day warrior?

  1. During Krishna-Karna conversation following Bhagwatyaan Parva, Karna himself endorses the prophesy of Kaurava’s doom at the hands of Pandavas. There he explicitly mentions Abhimanyu as a extremely powerful warrior. Now that was long before day 13.
  2. Before the start of the battle (first chapter of Gita), Duryodhana explicitly named Abhimanyu as a warrior of repute while describing the comparative strength of the two forces.
  3. Abhimanyu had been a powerful force in the battle through out the 13 days and not just the thirteenth day.

What was special on the 13th day?

Have you heard about the battle of Longewala where just 120 Indian soldiers foiled the best led plan of Pakistan involving 2000–3000 soldiers and 30–40 tanks?

Have you heard about the PVC awardee Grenadier Yogendra Singh Yadav, who climbed 60 feet after being hit by three bullets and neutralized three bunkers on the Tiger hill during Kargil war?

And these two are not isolated cases. Do you know what is common in all these cases? They were battle for survival.

When you are left with no option you are ready for the ultimate sacrifice, you enemy would be left with no option to but to back off. They could kill you but not bow you down. So they would cow down. When you are not afraid of death, what can your enemy do?

On the other hand think of enemy lines. They had no character strength. They were not fighting for rights but to usurp them. They were too afraid to fight the direct battle against Arjuna. They device a plan hoping to capture Yudhishthira while Ajruna was away. They thought it would be easy.

But their plan back fired. They got Abhimanyu breaking into their ranks mounting an unexpected attack. Now their war formation had not accounted for this unexpected turn of the event. And before they could realize Abhimanyu had created havoc killing Duryodhana’s son, Shalya’s son, Karna’s brother, and scores of other warriors sending a pang of fear and frustration in the ranks forcing Karna and Duhshashana into hurried retreat. Drona had played his hand and failed. They fear and disarray made Abhimanyu even more powerful.

So one hand a renowned capable warrior was fighting a battle of survival, unafraid of dying. On the other hand the enemies were hurdled together with little room for maneuver into an unexpected circumstances. Abhimanyu was brighter than anyone.

But that doesn’t make him a one day warrior.
