Why are Lord Krishna and Bhishma Pitamah said to be the main people responsible for the Mahabharata War?

📅 Mar 05, 2021 11:44 PM PST  |  🌐 English

People are entitled for their opinion. And they need a scapegoat to put their blames on to. Bhishma is an easy scapegoat. He had the chance to rule, but he refused to be the king. While giving up the kingship, he holds on to his pledge to defend his nation from outsiders. People often claim his actions and short-sightedness and foolish. Why did he support Duryodhana? But did he really support Duryodhana? That maybe a question for some other debate.

To imagine that Bhishma could have comprehended what someone in third generation from him would do is as ridiculous as to assume that he should have prioritized the need of some distant future over obvious present. And on such skewed reasoning he is more responsible than blind and ambitious Dhritarashtra and his evil scions.

Bhisma should be held responsible for Mahabharata had he —

  • Tried to poison Bhima as an infant, starting a strife in the family.
  • Tried to burn down Pandavas in Lakshagrih.
  • Tried to win Indraprastha by deceitful game of dice.
  • Refused Krishna’s last attempt at peace.

But he didn’t do any of them. He wasn’t part of those conspiracy. He wasn’t blood thirsty or deceitful. So how was he responsible.

WHY IS BHISHMA HELD RESPONSIBLE?

The idea is simple. If we vindicate Bhishma, even partially, we would need to look forward towards another alternatives, the most obvious ones, nay, the most obvious five. Someone Vyas and Krishna mentioned as four parts of evil tree Duryodhana — Karna, Dussassana, Shakuni and Dhritrashtra.

But we have a sizeable faction of society which has turned to idolizing anti-heros like Hitler, Joker (of batman fame), Ravana and Karna. For them, these villains are heros. Their attempt progresses in familiar four stages of disease —

Stage 1 - Identify few good qualities they may have and make posters with great fonts.

Stage 2 - Potray them as victims of some abuse and downplay and offer weak defense to their misconducts.

Stage 3 - Try to project their action aggressively as justifiable actions.

Stage 4 - Try to delete all their mis-conducts as interpolation.

Interestingly, by the time they reach the 4th stage of disease, they realize that they too hate their idols and their actions. Why else would they actually try to deny their actions? But now they have invested themselves so long, that they fail to concede and hold on to their beliefs.

Bhishma is a support system for those people who are in one of the four stages of anti-hero worshipping disease for Karna or Duryodhana — Let us blame Bhishma!

And there is another ideology that is hell is distorting the message from the epic. In the epic Krishna, Arjuna, Bhishma, Drona are good and respected people. So they would attack them to attack the epic.

Finally, there is hardly any contemporary narrative that presents Bhishma’s perspective to the epic. He becomes sidelined and in absence of good defense, a deemed culprit.

KRISHNA

Krishna is a different ball game altogether. He is almighty. True, he could have stopped the war and therefore it is expected, he should have. The question is, just because you can, should you? And, what was the cost of averting that war? Let Duryodhana continue his evil rule? If war it is to avoided at all cost, then no country would ever have army and only hinious villains would rule.

Krishna wasn’t responsible for causing the war, he was responsible for not stopping it in favour of evils. He was responsible for victory of righteousness. And for those who can’t understand the difference, he was responsible for the war.
