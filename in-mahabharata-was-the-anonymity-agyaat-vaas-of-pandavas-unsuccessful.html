In Mahabharata, was the anonymity (agyaat vaas) of Pandavas unsuccessful?

📅 Apr 22, 2020 06:00 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for A2A

Pandavas Agyaat vaas was successful, and it was confirmed by Bhisma as also mentioned by Lakshmi Telidevara while answering this question. So I will not repeat the same answer. It is a different story that Duryodhana didn’t agree, and that would be natural. So how do we say for sure who was right?

Well, precisely for this issue we must consider a more holistic analysis of their character and conduct throughout the life, before and after.

  • If one thing is more than certain about the story, it is the character of Yudhishthir. Personally I feel he was confused between the essence of dharma and prevelent social customs, and at times he was honest to the extent of being stupid. But one thing was undeniable. He was not a liar. Duryodhana — Well do I need to spell out his character?
  • Arjuna was blessed by Shiva, Bhima was in good grace of Hanuman, Krishna bestowed his blessings on Pandavas. If he wanted, he could have violated the terms of exile long back and attacked Hastinapur. But he still subjected himself and his family to humiliations of slavery in Agyatvaas.
  • He found it difficult to to lie even during Drona’s death. And that's the only time he is recorded to lie. Naturally, his opinion about Agyaat vaas is not labeled as a lie.
  • Bhisma, Drona and everyone who eventually fought for Kauravas, too agreed that Pandavas completed the term successfully
  • If someone continues to be adment on an irrational debate and call an issue debate controversial, there is nothing to say.

My answer is based on the story of Mahabharat, the Great Indian Epic. My answer refers to the English translation by Kisari Mohan Ganguli (KMG) and Bibek Debroy translation of BORI edition and Neelkanth Edition of Mahabharat (Geet Press) Sanskrit-Hindi.
