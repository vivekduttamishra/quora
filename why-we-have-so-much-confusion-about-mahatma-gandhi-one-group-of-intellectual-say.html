An interesting question. But the answer is easy to find. Just ask one simple question — Who are the people who admire Gandhiji and who are the people who condemn him?

Let us begin with the first list —

WHO ARE THE PEOPLE WHO ADMIRE MAHATMA GANDHI?

  1. Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose called him “The Father of the Nation”. He named and INA brigade after him. This also shatters the claim of other group who want to depict the rift with Gandhiji and Bose.
  2. Dr Rajendra Prasad, the first President of India called Gandhiji as personification of Ganga. He, despite being in congress was a rival of Nehru and has never been given a due by current Congress. This can prove him a credible character for current politics. He was a close associated of Gandhiji.
  3. Gurudev Rabindra Nath Thakur revered Mahatma Gandhi and celebrated his birthday every year in Shantiniketan.
  4. Lord Mount Batten, the last viceroy of Britishraj regarded him at par with Jesus Christ and Gautam Buddha. For him his voice was next only to God. He had no reason to put Gandhiji as such high regard.
  5. George Bernard Shaw compared Gandhiji with Himalayas
  6. Ho Chi Minh, father of Vietnam, and a revolutionary called himself disciple of Mahatma Gandhi.
  7. Albert Einstein wondered if generations to come shall scarcely belief that a man of flesh and blood such as Gandhi ever walked on this Earth?
  8. Steve Jobs used him as mascot for his think different campaign.
  9. Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi, follows ideals of Gandhiji.
  10. Dalali Lama thinks world is in need of Mahatma Gandhi’s teachings.

And this is just a short list of some of the world’s greatest minds from all walks of human existences. Most of the names mentioned here have nothing to gain by praising Mahatma Gandhi. There are not flatters, not stupid. Their credentials are beyond doubt.

And not just humans but nations. More than 150 countries issued stamps on his names. Many country has his statue honoring him. You have have seen the large face of Mahatma of Bhurj Khalifa on October 2nd.

And that still doesn’t give the complete picture to someone who thinks Gandhiji was just a propaganda of Indian congress. They miss it by miles.
