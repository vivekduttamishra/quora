Why was Krishna silent in Karna's humiliation by Draupadi at her Swayamvara? If that was dharma, then why was Karna's silence in Draupadi Vastraharan considered adharma by Lord Krishna? How is this justified?

📅 Jun 10, 2020 11:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question has two sub-parts interestingly both are factually incorrect.

Why was Krishna silent on Karna's humiliation by Draupadi at her Swaymber?

Why indeed? Simple because Krishna had no idea that Draupadi humiliated Karna.

  • This tale has been conclusively missing not only from BORI and Dakshinayat but also contradicted by Neelkantha commentary and Gita Press. Further slokas too contradicts this story. For details on this please check out this post — https://www.quora.com/q/dqqdyatpbbobowgd/Had-Draupadi-really-stopped-Karna-from-Participating-in-her-Swyamber-Source-Reference-The-Mahabharata-Project-Main-Ar?ch=10&share=66bc3219
  • But even if you go by controversy, it was her Swaymber and she had right to reject anyone. This was not regarded as insult, as in every Swaymber all but one is rejected. Again there is no mention of Karna's rejection by anyone after this incident either by Karna or any one else, proving it is baseless.
  • If you trust nothing, trust Krishna, and let me ask you Why Krishna remained silent? If you don't trust Krishna there is nothing I can say would matter.

Why was Karna silence in Draupadi's vastraharan Adharma?

Simple. Because Karna was not silent. He was loudest —

  • He called Draupadi a whore (वैश्या). Some defend unchaste, but sanskrit clearly says वैश्या (whore)
  • He rebuked Vikarna's plea for adhering to Dharma.
  • He instigated Dussassana to strip her.
  • While some try to justify even these misdeeds Karna himself didn't during his final moments when Krishna cornered him.

Finally both Krishna and Karna agreed, it was Adharma. Even then Karna never asked why was he insulted by Draupadi. Why? Because he wasn't.
