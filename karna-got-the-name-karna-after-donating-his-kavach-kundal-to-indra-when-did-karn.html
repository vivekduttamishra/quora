Karna got the name "Karna" after donating his Kavach-Kundal to Indra. When did Karna exactly donate his earrings and Kavach to Indra. What was he referred to before that by everyone?

📅 Jun 06, 2021 11:25 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

The question’s basic premise is wrong!

Karna didn’t get his name Karna after donating his Kavacha. He is called Karna everywhere. He also had other names as Vasusena and Vrisha. But he has always be popularly referred as Karna or Radheya. Here is his origin story —

The boy was clad in expensive armour and wore golden earrings. On seeing this, the brahmanas gave him the name of Vasushena.32 Thus the infinitely valorous one came to be the son of a suta and was known by the name of Vasushena. The lord was also known as Vrisha.

—BORI, Kundala Harana Parava

He has been referred as Karna throughout the epic.

It may be an interesting point to add that the name Karna got after donating his Kavacha was Vaikartana. Here is a direct reference —

Then he sliced off and gave the earrings. Because of this deed, Karna came to be known as Vaikartana. 39

—BORI, Kunda Harana Parva

This line proves that Karna was a name prior to the time when he gave his Kavacha and Kundala to Indra.
