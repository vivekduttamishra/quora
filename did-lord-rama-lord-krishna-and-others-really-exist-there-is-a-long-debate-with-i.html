Did Lord Rama, Lord Krishna, and others really exist? There is a long debate with it. What is the truth?

📅 Apr 21, 2020 05:31 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

You can’t ever reach a conclusion by debating whether they existed. Hindu is the only philosophy which encapsulates contradiction such nicely when it says —

Ekam sat vipra bahudha vadanti

Same truth is expressed differently by wise men

We believe both in Sakara (form) and Nirakara (formless). Adwayat (One God) and many forms (33 crores). So people who don’t understand the common truth in this contradiction may use it to debate against Rama, Krishna or any other aspect of our belief system.

Those who can see the common cord in the contradiction don’t need a convincing weather, Ram and Krishna existed or not. They just know it.

Now coming to historic aspect of Ram and Krishna.

  1. India didn’t write History. The incident of Ramayana and Mahabharat predates history and notion of historic by centuries.
  2. No written work could survive this long. Not even modern techniques, Harrddisk, SSD or cloud. So how do we expect written evidence? And does non-existance of evidence a conclusive proof of non existence?
  3. What Indians did is use a technique called shruti (श्रुति) and passed on the knowledge by creating a human chain and transferring the knowledge by retelling from one generation to another.
  4. The story existed with very minor deviation across not just India but extended to other countries like Thailand, Malaysia, Burma and Indonesia etc.
  5. Ram and Krishna are the source of our social, moral and cultural inspiration. If you understand Nirakara, you will realize that they exist within you, within our social circle, within our cultural identity.
