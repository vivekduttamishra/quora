How did Lord Krishna stop Barbarik from using his 3 arrows? How did his head get cut off? What’s the whole story?

📅 Nov 08, 2020 10:46 PM PST  |  🌐 English

Hi, Thanks for the A2A.

The whole story is the fact that Barbarik didn’t exist. It doesn’t find a mention in Original Vyas Mahabharata. Not once. This is an interpolation created to narrate the story of how Krishna was the only participant of the war, and the story undermines the greatest teaching of Krishna. No one who knows Krishna has any doubt that he is all prevailing and his teaching confirms everything is the handiwork of the allmighty. But to say he actually acted in the battle undermines his greatness.

Having setup the facts straight, let us come to the fable part how Krishna stopped Barbarika —

The fabled character Barbarik is the son of Ghatotkatch who possess three arrows which could destroy the world in three steps —

  1. Mark Everything that need to be destroyed.
  2. Mark everything that need to be preserved.
  3. Finally, the third arrow will destroy the whole universe.

Before continuing further, let us take a pause and look at the illogic of the whole process —

  1. Isn’t Step 1 and 2, mutually exclusive?
  2. Imagine what happens if you fire the 1st arrow and the 3rd one and not the second one?
    1. Would only those marked for kill be destroyed? Then what’s the point of arrow number 2?
    2. Would everything be destroyed? Then what’s the point of arrow #1?
  3. What do you think other warriors would be doing while Barbarik fires first two arrows? Sitting idle and waiting to be killed?
  4. Was this said weapon by an obscure godess more powerful than Naraynastra, Vaishnavastra and Brahmastras that were in the play?

Ok let us move on. Now Krishna asks Barbarik to show case his powers. He marks all the leaves of the tree. When he fires his weapon, as suggested it destroys all the leaves of the tree and waits. Waits for one leaf which was under Krishna’s foot and destroys it as soon as Krishna moves his foot away. Story exclaims! Imagine! How powerful was that great weapon.

Let us pause Again and ponder a bit more —

  • Why did the weapon fail to destroy the last leaf? Because It was under Krishna’s protection. Now ask yourself, what chance that poor weapon had against those warriors who were under Krishna’s protection. To add to that those warriors were not tree leaves, but those with divine weapons and proven warrior.

But wait, wasn’t Barbarik supposed to destroy Kauravas?

That’s the topmost reason Krishna ensured Barbarik is out of equation. Barbarik made a promise to his mother that he would side with the weaker. While it may sound a lofty ideal, it is one of the most flawed and skewd aim — You don’t fight for right or wrong, you don’t fight for loyalty either. You just fight for the weak even if weak tend to be evils! Imagine, where it will take our society —

  • What would society gain if someone ‘weak’ wins? A weaker society?
  • A society where weakness is a previledge? You don’t win because you are good. You don’t win because you worked hard and made yourself strong. You win because you are weak. Who would like to work hard in such a society?
  • If your goal is to be considered weak to get alms, will such society every grow strong to complete with other stronger societies of the world.

Krishna realized the flaw in the argument and decided to put an end to it. At least for that era. He let Barbarik kill himself. And that is a metaphor for another great lesson — Such illogic die at their own hands. They must die on their own hands. Krishna tricked Barbarik into killing himself.

Barbarik was a fable in Mahabharata era. But I am not sure if he is a fable today. As I see Barbarik lives today. It lives today in the mindset. It lives today fighting for the weaker — not just trying to kill the hard-working strong but also killing any ambition in the weaker to grow stronger, inturn killing the weaker also. When someone else feeds you, they rob you of the incentive to word and steal your dignity.

Only one could have survived — Krishna or Barbarik. Krishna is the teacher, who doesn’t make your life easy. He teaches you to be strong. He doesn’t fight your battle, but guides you to fight your own battle. Barbarik is anti Krishna. It doesn’t want you to be strong. It wants to show its own power. Keep you weak and destroy the society in process.

We are blessed Krishna won then. But who would win today?
