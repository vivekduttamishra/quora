Why did Krishna let the Draupadi vastaharan sequence happen when he knew what happened everywhere?

📅 Apr 17, 2020 04:50 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Krishna knew everything and yet he let things happen. Why? Because he was Krishna!

Krishna was the almighty who could have stopped it, but he didn't. Think this way he could have prevented its root cause itself — he could have manipulated Shakuni and his dices and Yudhisthir would win. Why didn't he?

Think again, if Krishna could have stopped it, who actually allowed it? In hindu philosophy God is adwyat (अद्वैत) one without an equal counterpart such as devil. And the greatest role he could play is not to act but watch. Your actions are guided by your guna Sato, rajo, tamo. He intervenes only when human sense of justice fails.

Had he intervened earlier, how would the heinous wolves be unmasked? How would have be uncovered the true devil of the Era?

Krishna never spoon fed them. He let even Pandavas suffer for their faults. He didn't participate in war despite being an unparalleled warrior.

Why do we expect them to intervene sooner and forgo an opportunity to establish knew teaching for the world — that makes him God and not another ordinary hero.
