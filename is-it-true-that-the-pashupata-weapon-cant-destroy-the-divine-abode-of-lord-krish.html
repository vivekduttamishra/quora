The Gita verses 15.6 doesn’t talk about Pashupatastra and it doesn’t talk about destruction of the the eternal abode.

न तद्भासयते सूर्यो न शशाङ्को न पावक: |
यद्गत्वा न निवर्तन्ते तद्धाम परमं मम || 6||

—Chapter 15

Neither the sun nor the moon, nor fire can illumine that Supreme Abode of Mine. Having gone There, one does not return to this material world again.

But before you reach another erroneous conclusion based on my answer, let us checkout a few more references. Krishna doesn’t only mention just about his eternal abode, he also says that the soul in indestructible —

न जायते म्रियते वा कदाचि
नायं भूत्वा भविता वा न भूय: |
अजो नित्य: शाश्वतोऽयं पुराणो
न हन्यते हन्यमाने शरीरे || 20||

The soul is neither born, nor does it ever die; nor having once existed, does it ever cease to be. The soul is without birth, eternal, immortal, and ageless. It is not destroyed when the body is destroyed.

नैनं छिन्दन्ति शस्त्राणि नैनं दहति पावक: |
न चैनं क्लेदयन्त्यापो न शोषयति मारुत: || 23||

Weapons cannot shred the soul, nor can fire burn it. Water cannot wet it, nor can the wind dry it.

So, does that mean it says Pashupatastra is weak enough to destroy it?

Actually you miss two important considerations.

THE FRAME OF REFERENCE

Shiva uses Pashupatastra at the end of the kalpa. Krishna talks about that abode that exists in the time frame of the kalpa. Since the time frames are different the question as no locus standi

WRONG ASSUMPTION ABOUT SHIVA

Contrary to popular misconception Shiva is not the god of death, he doesn’t destroy. He absorves the entire world with all Gods and abode within himself as a precondition to recreation. That is known as pro-creation. Shiva’s artifect in that process is Pashupatastra. The Gods, the soul and eternal abodes are not destroyed the submerge in Shiva as one. Here is a reference from Skand Puran

आकाशं लिंगमित्याहु: पृथ्वी तस्य पीठिका।
आलय: सर्व देवानां लयनार्लिंगमुच्यते ॥
(स्कन्द पुराण)

The endless sky (that great void which contains the entire universe) is the Linga, the Earth is its base. At the end of time the entire universe and all the Gods finally emerge in the Linga itself.

WRONG NOTION ABOUT SHIVA AND KRISHNA

If you read the highlighted text you would realize that everything merges in one being Shiva at the end of the Kalpa. Krishna says the same thing in Gita chapter 10, shloka 23 —

रुद्राणां शङ्करश्चास्मि वित्तेशो यक्षरक्षसाम् |
वसूनां पावकश्चास्मि मेरु: शिखरिणामहम् || 23||

Amongst the Rudras know me to be Shankar; amongst the demons I am Kuber. I am Agni amongst the Vasus and Meru amongst the mountains.

Show in their eternal form there is no difference between Shiva and Krishna. Only fools go finding the difference and try to wedge a war of supremacy here. And it is they who doubt the statement of Shiva when he says that the Pashupatastra is infallible.

Hope that clears the confusion.
