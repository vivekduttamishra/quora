Why Dronachrya take the thumb of Eklavya and did unjustice?

📅 Mar 19, 2021 11:58 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

This question refuses to die down. I answered this question so many times, the most recent being —

Vivek Dutta Mishra (विवेक दत्त मिश्रा)'s answer to Some people are saying Dronacharya did right by taking Ekalavya's finger, and Eklavya is adharmi because he had stolen knowledge. How to refute this misconception?

LET US TRY TO UNDERSTAND THE SCENARIO FROM MODERN PERSPECTIVE

  • There is a person who self-studied medicine after getting rejected in entrance examination. Now he claims he is a pass out from AIIMS. Do you think AIIMS should prosecute it? What does the current law say? He would go to jail for losing more than his thumb — many years of his life. And he would be barred from practicing medicine, no matter skilled he is in medicine. Eklavya had no right to mis-use the name of Drona after he explicitly forbid him from learning archary.
  • What is the difference between a NDA cadet and a young nuxal of the same age? Both learn to fire guns. One learns under a weapon expert who not only teaches how to fire the gun, but also when not to fire it. A young nuxal or terroritst may also learn firing the gun at the same age and may even get better skilled in firing the gun. What should the government do? Should he be allowed to wield the gun and fire at his will? What is the current law of the land? Do you need a license or not? Should we allow terrorist camps to flourish? What is the key difference? Ethics or the lack of it. What happened to Eklavya? He moved to the path of Adharma, sided with evils and finally killed by Krishna.

Now the question is —

  • Was Dronacharya wrong in refusing Eklavya as his student? Having being killed by Krishna, certainly vindicates Drona’s position.
  • Was Drona unjustified in trying to stop Eklavaya from misusing his name?
  • Was Drona wrong in trying to stop Eklavya from his chosen path of self destruction?
  • Are we justified in curxifying a learned Acharya in favour of an evil man who was finally killed by Krishna on his way to uphold Dharma?
