If Karna failed in Draupadi Swayamvara then why didn't Bhishm, Drona, Arjun target and teased Karna for that? They always targetted Karna for his failure.

📅 Sep 05, 2021 12:06 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A.

This question stems from the doubt that Karna failed in Draupadi’s swyamvar and has been an attempt to refute the authenticity of not only BORI but entire ancient epic text, for all of them categorically mention Karna failure in Draupadi’s swyamvara. Everyone including Neelkantha (the verse that was translated as KMG), southern recitation, original Bengali recitations and Sharda edition. So no one targeting Karna for failure can’t be synonym for Karna not failing.

Now, having refuted the indirect rationale of the question, let us try to understand why Karna’s failure at the swyamver wasn’t looked down as his other spectacular failures.

First, Swyamvara was by no margin considered as a war. Failing a swaymavara wasn’t considered as a humiliations. Had this been the case, few Kings would attend a swyamver for fear of making their mockery. This doesn’t appear to be a case.

Second and an important point to note is, not only Karna, but no other King was ever condemned for failing the swyamver. There was a heated exchange of abuse between Karna and Shalya. Here even Karna doesn’t refer to the failure of Shalya in the Swyamvara. This should give a clear picture.

Some may be eager to point that Karna was particularly targeted by Bhishma, Drona, Kripa and Ashwatthama. But they miss out one important point — They never targetted Karna unless he was boasting of his powers. Kripacharya and Ashwatthama reminded Karna’s battle failures when he was boasting before the Matasya battle, Bhishma when he was boasting his powers in the Udyoga Parva and again Drona and Ashwatthama during Ghatotkatch Vadha Parva. Now we don’t see Karna being boastful during the Swyamver.

I think, this should clear the reasoning of this question clearly.

Another important point to note is — Arjuna never teased Karna. He let the scared Karna retreat just after the Swyamver battle. He asked Karna to stay put but Karna was too scared. Here Arjuna doesn’t abuse him for fleeing or being coward.

There next encounter was during Matasya battle. Here Arjuna reminds Karna of his past misdeeds and his boastful nature. Karna taunts back saying deeds are superior to the words. And Karna was right, at least at this point. Karna retreats three time! He flees seeing his own brother slaughtered.

Next face-off was on the day 14 of the battle after Karna has humiliates Bheema. Arjuna chides Karna for his abusive words and for the death of Abhimany and announces that he would avenge by killing his son in front of his eyes — something he fulfilled on the day 17.

So, I don’t see Arjuna teasing Karna for his failure. Karna proved himself a laughing stock.
