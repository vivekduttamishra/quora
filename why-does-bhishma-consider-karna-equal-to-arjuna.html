Why does Bhishma consider Karna equal to Arjuna?

📅 May 18, 2021 09:55 AM PDT  |  🌐 English

Thanks for the A2A

Short Answer: Bhishma never considered Karna as Arjuna’s equal.

I saw a few answers here who tried to deviate from the answer by also including what Krishna or other considered. While I can respond to those elements too, that would be digressing from the topic. So, I would keep my answer limited to what Bhishma considered.

But before explaining Bhishma’s views which are scattered through out the epic, let us establish Bhishma as a credible authority on this subject.

Bhishma was a great Army commander.

Bhishma apart from being a great warrior, was also a astute Army general and knew how to avoid dissident in his army. To this effect I would like to take you all back to Matasya battle where we find Karna boasting how he would kill Arjuna and bring down his celestial ape flag to dust.

His attitude was unacceptable to wise Kripacharya, who advised Karna to take Arjuna seriously and insisted on joint effort against him.

This caused a rift between Kripacharya, Dronacharya, Ashwatthama and Karna. But wise Bhishma intervened with these words —

‘Bhishma said, “Drona’s son sees it well. Kripa sees it well. Karna wishes to fight, in accordance with the dharma of kshatriyas. No learned man can find fault with the preceptor.

…

What Karna spoke, was for the purpose of inciting enterprise in us. The preceptor’s son121 should pardon us, because a great task has to be undertaken. Kounteya has arrived and this is not the time for dissension. You, 122 the preceptor and Kripa must pardon everything.

—Goharna Parva, BORI

Note:

  • Bhishma supports Karna, despite later being boastful and arrogant.
  • He seeks forgiveness for Karna.

This proves Bhishma wasn’t against Karna without a valid reason.

WHEN DID BHISMA CONSIDERED KARNA EQUAL TO ARJUNA?

As I said, short answer is NEVER. But what Karna’s fan would like to call the closest thing to praising Karna would be in Kurukshetra battle. Bhishma had fallen on the tenth day and it was time for Karna to enter the battle. He goes to meet Bhishma and this is what Bhishma said, which many consider more like final statement of Bhishma negating everything he said earlier. I would later prove that is not the case. But let us see the statement first —

You are not Radheya. I have known this from Narada and from Krishna Dvaipayana474 and Keshava. There is no doubt about this. O son!475 I do not hate you. I am telling you this truthfully. I have spoken harsh words towards you for the sake of reducing your energy. It is my view that you hated the Pandavas without any reason. O descendant of the sun! That is the reason I have spoken many harsh words to you. I know your valour in battle and that the enemy cannot withstand you. I know your devotion towards brahmanas, your valour and your supreme attachment to generosity. There is no man like you and you are like an immortal. I spoke harsh words towards you to prevent dissension in the lineage. In the use of arrows, in aiming at a target, in dexterity and in the strength of your weapons, there is no one like you, with the exception of Phalguna and the great-souled Krishna. O Karna! You went to the king’s capital and as a single archer, defeated all the kings in battle, for the sake of the king of the Kurus. The powerful King Jarasandha was invincible in an encounter and prided himself in battle. But even he wasn’t your equal.

— Bhishma Parva, BORI CE

NOTE:

  • Bhishma said, I don’t hate you and speaking this truthfully. Now I have already provided reference to this fact. Bhishma had no personal grudge against him.
  • Bhishma said since Karna hated Pandavas without any reason, he spoke harsh words to him. He said it was to sap your energy. But he doesn’t say, what he spoke earlier was wrong!

Now comes the most important part that is in favor of Karna — He calls Karna a incomparable to any one, with two exceptions — Krishna and Arjuna. Does that make Karna equal to them?

Consider this there is a class of students. The topper gets 99 marks, The second topper 90 marks. The third gets 50 marks. Rest get less than 50. Now the teacher tells the third student, who obtained 50 marks that you are better than everyone except the top two. Is the teacher lying? NO. Is he considering the third student equal to the other two? NO.

This is all that Bhishma said — Karna is a great archer who is better than everyone except Krishna and Arjuna.

So how can we sure if Bhishma considered them equal or not. We have to take the reference from the entire epic. So let us travel back in time —

1.Yaan Sandhi Parva

Vaishampayana said, ‘On hearing Karna’s words, Shantanu’s son, Bhishma, again spoke to the great king Dhritarashtra. “He always speaks about killing the Pandavas. But he is not a sixteenth part59 of the great-souled Pandavas. Know that the calamity that is about to confront your evil-souled sons is the work of this evil-minded son of a suta. Your evil-minded son Suyodhana depends on him. He ignores those brave sons of the gods, the destroyers of enemies.60 What extremely difficult task has he61 accomplished in the past that can rival any one deed accomplished by any one of the Pandavas earlier? In Virata’s city, he saw his own beloved brother killed through Dhananjaya’s valour.62 What did he do then? Dhananjaya attacked all the Kurus together, defeated them and won back the cattle. Was he not there then? In the expedition over the cattle, your son was captured by the gandharvas. 63 The son of a suta is behaving like a bull now. Where was he then? Wasn’t it Partha and the great-souled Bhima, together with the twins, who defeated the gandharvas then? O bull among the Bharata lineage! O fortunate one! He is deficient in dharma and artha and has always spoken many such falsehoods.”
—Yaan Sandhi, BORI

NOTE:

  • He mentions Karna not even 16th of Arjuna. Is there any contradiction between what he said here and later at Kurukshetra? If you see, there is none. Karna could be better archer than other, yet not even sixteenth of Arjuna, who was already and exception in his last speech.
  • He also reminds that Karna did nothing even when he saw his brother killed.
  • What is more important is, unlike in Kurukshetra, here Bhishma relies on hard evidence. He talks about Matasya battle and Gandharvas battle which are well known.
  • On a side note he also calls him deficient in Dharma and one who speaks falsehood. That is also a known fact.

2. Udyoga Parva

Here Bhishma once again clarifies what he thinks about Arjuna —

Kiriti Partha is powerful. He is immensely strong and is skilled in the use of weapons. Who is there who can withstand Pandu’s son, Dhananjaya, in battle? Even the wielder of the vajra cannot, how can other archers? It is my view that he can stand up against the three worlds.”

Do you think Bhishma forgot about Karna here? If there is a confusion here is the clarification

Bhishma responded, “O Radheya! How can you speak? You should remember your own deeds, when Partha alone vanquished the six charioteers in battle.136 If we do not act as this brahmana has spoken, we will certainly be killed on the field of battle and will have to eat dust.”

3. Ratha Atiratha Samkhya Parva

Here Bhishma’s compares the comparative strength of the two armies. As we already discussed, Bhishma was very objective as a general and can be trusted when he finally said about Arjuna —

O brave one! There is no ratha who is equal to him in either army, nor among gods or earlier danavas, serpents, rakshasas and yakshas, not to speak of men. I have not heard of any ratha who has been, or will be, as accomplished as the intelligent Partha. O great king! Vasudeva is the charioteer and Dhananjaya is the warrior. The bow is the divine Gandiva and the horses have the speed of the wind. The divine armour is impenetrable. The great quivers are inexhaustible. There are the weapons of the great Indra, Rudra, Kubera, Yama and Varuna. His clubs are terrible to see. He has many weapons, with the vajra as the foremost. There were thousands of danavas who lived in Hiranyapura. He slew them on a single chariot. Where is there a ratha like him? He is enraged. He is strong. Truth is his valour. That mighty-armed one can destroy your army, while protecting his own. I can stand up to Dhananjaya, and so can the preceptor. O Indra among kings! But there is no third person, in either army.

Note:

In the first line, Bhishma declares that no one can be compared to Arjuna in either army. That includes Karna. He also says that only He or Drona can stand against him but no third person.

There are many more references of what Bhishma thought, but I think this is sufficient to give a clear picture of Bhishma’s view.


S U M M A R Y

  • Bhishma no where in the entire epic says that Karna is equal to Arjuna. The closest he said is Karna is better than everyone except Krishna and Arjuna. He never said Karna is better or equal. And he doesn’t give any evidence to his statement.
  • He categorically and with sufficient evidence explained why Arjuna was better than Karna. He reminded Karna of Matasya battle and Gandharva’s battle and explained Arjuna to be much greater than Arjuna. It is not just an opinion, but an evidence backed fact.
  • He clearly mentions that no one other than Bhishma and Drona can withstand Arjuna and same was adequately proved during the war.
  • Bhishma statement is in line with what Drona, Parshurama, Krishna and Shiva himself said. It also matches the actual incidents as they happened.

I would like to end my answer with a question which Bhishma himself asked in Yaan Sandhi Parva — What extremely difficult task has he61 accomplished in the past that can rival any one deed accomplished by any one of the Pandavas earlier?
