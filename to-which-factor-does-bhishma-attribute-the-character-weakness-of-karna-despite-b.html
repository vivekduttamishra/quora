To which factor does Bhishma attribute the character weakness of Karna despite being the son of Surya, the God and the virtuous woman Kunthi as per Mahabharatam?

📅 May 10, 2022 10:15 PM PDT  |  🌐 English

Sometimes reading a question is more fun than answering it. If we just read the question, you will find a subtle but clear hint to the answer just like the seed contains the tree. So I would suggest, read the question and if you see the answer, you don't have to credit me for the same.

Ok, if you want to credit me for the answer, here it is. Let's start with the factors that Bhishma didn't held up against Karna — he didn't hold grudges against Karna for being son of Surya, the God and virtuous woman Kunti. Why, because it didn't matter. He was evaluating Karna as an individual, his character, his trustworthiness. And it wasn't a testimony to his illustrious parent.

A parent, the genes or a guru have significant role in a person's life. But then, they together can't define a person completely. Person himself does. His conduct defines his character. Do we think parent of a terrorist would necessarily be a terrorist or a rapist gets inspiration from their parent or son of an illustrious parent would always be equally illustrious? Haven't we heard the stories of drug addicted and wasted sons of illustrious parent? If genes is everything no mighty empire would have ever failed for under every great and mighty emperor came not so trust worthy sons.

Now coming back, Bhishma pointed out the weak character of Karna that was not because but despite of the fact that he was the son of Surya and Kunti and the disciple of Bhagwan Parshurama! Consider this, Dronacharya had had many disciples who were denied of Divyastra. How many of them deserted their guru and moved on to cheat another? Parshurama too had many disciples over centuries. How many did he curse? Karna had unique credentials. I don’t recall from our epic narratives even a single incident where even demons used cheated on their gurus. Should Bhishma have called such a person as a man of strong character? What man of great character would try to strip a woman in public or seek greater pleasure in seeing their rivals tormented than own prosperity or having a son?

But if we were to debate only on the warrior credentials of Karna, that too amply showed the weakness of his character. His greed of weapons made him cheat his guru. And despite of having mastery over archery, we find him continuously fleeing the battle zones in search of safety and during those trying period it become immaterial for him, if his best friend gets imprisoned or his own brother dies in the process. There is indeed a difference between an Olympic gold medalist shooter and a soldier, former may have better aim, but it is the later who can be trusted in trying times. Tell me how Bhishma was supposed to trust such a fighter (not warrior) under his command?

And Bhishma didn’t just called him out as a man of weak character. He amply justified his position. He called him ardharatha because he was harsh, boastful and inferior. There was a long list of failures which were well known. Even Drona points out that he is distracted and keeps running from the battle field.

But most interesting, as a testimony to Bhishma’s word was Karna’s action. Bhishma states facts. But even if we consider them as a provocation, for a brief period, it still exposes Karna’s character weakness. Which man of character would abandon his duty, loyalty and promises because of his hurt ego? Karna refuses to fight the battle under Bhishma’s command because he felt, he won’t get the credit of his achievements if Bhishma were to lead the camp. He also ties to sabotage by trying to persuade Duryodhana to fire Bhishma from the commandership and abused Bhishma without any ground.

What would you call a person whose selfish interest surpasses his love for his friend and family, whose ego surpasses his sense of responsibility? Can we call him a man of character? Nah! Interesting so sure was Bhishma of his caliber that he says —

Be a man. Fight with Partha in battle, whom you seek to challenge. O extremely evil-minded one! I will watch you escape from that fight.’”

—BORI CE

And behold, Karna never disappointed Bhishma. He proved him right. Always.

On a side note, sons of Surya in their raw power were never any match for the sons of Indra. We had another pair in Surgriv (Son of Surya) and Bali (son of Indra). Bali was more powerful that Sugriv but lost. Do you know why? Because epic says — Good should win!
