This question has two parts and it’s rare that both part can be so wrong at once! Let me start by answering the second part first.

If Draupadi never insulted Duryodhana, then why Duryodhana asked Dushashan for Draupadi’s Vastra Harana?

First thing first — Duryodhana didn’t ask Duhshahshana for vastra harana. Karna did. Now the question arises, why did Karna asked Duhshashana to strip Draupadi? This question has been answered multiple times in Mahabharata by Droana, Ashwatthama and Karna himself. And all answers agree — He wanted to please Duryodhana.

But does that mean Duryodhana was innocent? Not at all. But what makes you think Duryodhana’s action was a retribution to some of the Draupadi’s misdeeds? Let me ask a a simple question — Why did Duryodhana plot Lakshagriha conspiracy? Druapadi hadn’t insulted Duryodhana at that point. Did she? So why did he try to burn an entire family?

The answer is simple. Duryodhana was ambitious and greedy for the throne. He also knew he couldn’t win against Pandavas by any fair means and plotting conspiracies was the only option.

The dice game was not the first, but yet another conspiracy by a greedy jealous person. If you care to read the epic and the dialog between Duryodhana and Shakuni, you would find that Duryodhana was tormented by the prosperity of Pandavas and Indraprastha and wanted to snatch them. Shakuni proposed the dice game. In the entire dialog Draupadi doesn’t find a single mention.

Why do People say Draupadi insulted Duryodhana?

Well people haven’t read the epic. None of the authentic recitation include this incident. The idea, most probably, is derived from a 1960 play called Andha Yug by Dhramaveer Bharti and was made highy popular by BR Chopra Mahabharata.
